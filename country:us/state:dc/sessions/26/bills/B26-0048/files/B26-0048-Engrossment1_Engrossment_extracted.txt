12 To amend the District of Columbia Administrative Procedure Act to codify agency deference as
13 it existed prior to the Supreme Court’s decision in Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo
14 while permitting the continued development of the law of deference, and to clarify that a
15 reviewing court or tribunal shall defer to an agency’s reasonable interpretation of a
16 statute or regulation it administers when the statute or regulation is silent or ambiguous
17 with respect to a specific issue so long as that interpretation is not plainly wrong, or
18 inconsistent with the statutory or regulatory language or legislature’s intent.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 act may be cited as the “Review of Agency Action Clarification Amendment Act of 2025”.
22 Sec. 2. Section 110 of the District of Columbia Administrative Procedure Act, approved
23 October 21, 1968 (82 Stat. 1209; D.C. Official Code § 2-510), is amended as follows:
24 (a) Subsection (a)(1) is amended by striking the phrase “So far as necessary” and
25 inserting the phrase “Subject to subsections (c), (d), and (e) of this section and so far as
26 necessary” in its place.
27 (b) New subsections (c), (d), and (e) are added to read as follows:
28 “(c) In reviewing an order or decision of an agency in any court or administrative
29 proceeding, including but not limited to proceedings under subsection (a) of this section, the

30 reviewing tribunal shall defer to the agency’s reasonable interpretation of a statute or regulation
31 it administers, when the statute or regulation is silent or ambiguous with respect to a specific
32 issue, provided that the interpretation is not plainly wrong, or inconsistent with the statutory or
33 regulatory language or the legislature’s intent.
34 “(d) In reviewing a rule adopted by an agency, the reviewing tribunal shall defer to the
35 agency’s reasonable interpretation of a statute it administers, when the statute or regulation is
36 silent or ambiguous with respect to a specific issue, provided that the interpretation is not plainly
37 wrong, or inconsistent with the statutory language or the legislature’s intent.
38 “(e) In determining whether an interpretation by an agency is reasonable under subsection
39 (c) or (d), the reviewing tribunal may consider, among other factors:
40 “(1) Whether the interpretation provided by an agency has been consistent and
41 longstanding;
42 “(2) Whether the specific issue is within the scope of the agency’s subject matter-
43 expertise; and
44 “(3) The thoroughness and formality of the agency’s consideration.”.
