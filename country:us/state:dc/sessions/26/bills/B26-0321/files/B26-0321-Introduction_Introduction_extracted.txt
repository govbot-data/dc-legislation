15 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Language Access Act of 2004 to include the DC Water and
16 Sewer Authority as a covered entity with major public contact; to amend the District of
17 Columbia Public Works Act of 1954 to require that the DC Water and Sewer Authority
18 submit information regarding disconnections to the Council, the Office of the Attorney
19 General, and the Office of the Tenant Advocate on a monthly basis; to amend the Water
20 and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of Public Works Reorganization Act
21 of 1996 to clarify the imposition of charges and penalties for late payment of water service
22 charges and bills; and to amend section 431 of Title 21 of the District of Columbia
23 Municipal Regulations to modify the notice requirements to building occupants prior to a
24 water service disconnection.
25
26 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
27 act may be cited as the “DC Water and Sewer Authority Billing and Disconnection Clarification
28 Emergency Amendment Act of 2025”.
29 Sec. 2. Section 2(3)(B) of the Language Access Act of 2004, effective June 19, 2004 (D.C.
30 Law 15-167; D.C. Official Code § 2-1931(3)(B)), is amended as follows:
31 (a) Sub-subparagraph (xxviii) is amended by striking the phrase “; and,” and inserting a
32 semicolon in its place.
33 (b) Sub-subparagraph (xxix) is amended by striking the period and inserting the phrase “;
34 and” in its place.
35 (c) A new sub-subparagraph (xxx) is added to read as follows:
36 “(xxx) The District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority.”.

37 Sec. 3. Section 216(d) of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of
38 Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996, effective April 18, 1996 (D.C. Law 11-111; D.C.
39 Official Code § 34-2202.16(d)), is amended to read as follows:
40 “(d) The Authority may impose additional charges and penalties for late payment of bills
41 not exceeding a charge of 10% for any charges or bills remaining unpaid for more than 30 days,
42 and a penalty at the rate of 1% per month compounded monthly for any charges or bills that remain
43 unpaid for more than 60 days.”.
44 Sec. 4. Section 103 of the District of Columbia Public Works Act of 1954, approved May
45 18, 1954 (68 Stat. 1012 D.C. Official Code § 34-2407.01), is amended to read as follows:
46 “Sec. 103. Discontinuance of water service for failure to pay water charges.
47 “(a)(1) The District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority (“Authority”), established
48 pursuant to section 202(a) of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of
49 Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996, effective April 18, 1996 (D.C. Law 11-111; D.C.
50 Official Code § 34-2202.02(a)), may provide for the collection of water charges, in advance or
51 otherwise, from the owner or occupant of any building, property, or other location furnished water
52 or water service by the District.
53 “(2) The Authority may shut off the water supply to any such building, property, or
54 other location upon failure of the owner or occupant thereof to pay such water charges within 30
55 days from the date of rendition of the bill therefor, regardless of any change in ownership or
56 occupancy of such building, property, or other location.
57 “(b)(1) If the Authority has shut off the water supply to any property for failure to pay
58 District water and sanitary sewer service charges, it shall be unlawful for any person to restore the
59 water supply without the express authorization of the Authority.
2

60 “(2) The Authority shall impose a fine in an amount not less than 20% of the
61 delinquent charges or more than $100, whichever is greater, upon the owner or occupant of the
62 property, unless the Authority determines that the owner or occupant did not restore or solicit a
63 person to restore the water.
64 “(c) The Authority shall submit on the 7th day of each month, the following information
65 to the Council, the Office of the Attorney General, and the Office of the Tenant Advocate for the
66 preceding month:
67 “(1) How many properties have received a notice of disconnection;
68 “(2) Of those properties that were issued a notice, how many experienced a
69 disconnection;
70 “(3) Of those properties that experienced a disconnection, how many have had
71 service restored;
72 “(4) How many properties have entered into a payment plan;
73 “(5) How many properties have had bill payment assumed by the tenants through a
74 tenant association; and
75 “(6) How many properties have been placed in receivership.”.
