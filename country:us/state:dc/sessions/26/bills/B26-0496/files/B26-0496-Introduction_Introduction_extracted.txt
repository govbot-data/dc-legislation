14 To amend, on an emergency basis, due to congressional review, the District of Columbia
15 Health Occupations Revision Act of 1985 and the Consent for Vaccination of
16 Minors Amendment Act of 2022 to authorize healthcare providers in the District
17 to order and administer immunizations recommended by competent medical and
18 public health organizations designated by the Director of the Department of
19 Health.
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA,
22 that this Act may be cited as the “Community Health Congressional Review Emergency
23 Amendment Act of 2025”.
24 Sec. 2. The District of Columbia Health Occupations Revision Act of 1985,
25 effective March 25, 1986 (D.C. Law 6-99; D.C. Official Code § 3-1201.01 et seq.), is
26 amended as follows:
27 (a) Section 102(11)(A) (D.C. Official Code § 3-1201.02(11)(A)) is amended by
28 striking the phrase “in accordance with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s
29 published guidelines and recommended immunization schedules” and inserting the
30 phrase “in accordance with the published guidelines and recommended immunization
31 schedules of the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices of the Centers for
32 Disease Control and Prevention or a competent medical or public health organization

1 designated by the Director of the Department of Health through a public notice” in its
2 place.
3 (b) Section 755(a-1) (D.C. Official Code § 3-1207.55(a-1)) is amended by striking
4 the phrase “in accordance with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s
5 published guidelines and recommended immunization schedules” and inserting the
6 phrase “in accordance with the published guidelines and recommended immunization
7 schedules of the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices of the Centers for
8 Disease Control and Prevention or a competent medical or public health organization
9 designated by the Director of the Department of Health through a public notice” in its
10 place.
11 Sec. 3. Section 2 of the Consent for Vaccinations of Minors Amendment Act of
12 2022, effective March 10, 2023 (D.C. Law 24-312; D.C. Official Code § 7-1653.01) is
13 amended as follows:
14 (a) Subsection (a) is amended by striking the phrase “recommended by the United
15 States Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices ("ACIP"), and where receipt of
16 the vaccine is in accordance with ACIP's recommended immunization schedule.” and
17 inserting the phrase “recommended by the Advisory Committee on Immunization
18 Practices of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (“ACIP”) or a competent
19 medical or public health organization designated by the Director of the Department of
20 Health (“Director”) through a public notice, and where receipt of the vaccine is in
21 accordance with the recommended immunization schedule of ACIP or the competent
22 medical or public health organization designated by the Director.” in its place.
2

1 (b) Subsection (b)(1) is amended by striking the phrase “recommended by ACIP
2 in accordance with ACIP’s recommended immunization schedule” and inserting the
3 phrase “recommended by ACIP or the competent medical or public health organization
4 designated by the Director, in accordance with the recommended immunization schedule
5 of ACIP or the competent medical or public health organization designated by the
6 Director” in its place.
7 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
8 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the
9 fiscal impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act
10 of 1975, approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
11 Sec. 5. Effective date.
12 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto
13 by the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), and shall remain in effect for
14 no longer than 90 days, as provided for emergency acts of the Council of the District of
15 Columbia in section 412(a) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved
16 December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 788; D.C. Official Code § 1-204.12(a)).
