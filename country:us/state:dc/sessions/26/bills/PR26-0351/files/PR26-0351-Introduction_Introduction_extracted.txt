Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Pursuant to D.C. Official Code § 2-352.02 and 6-227(f)(2), I have enclosed, for consideration by the Council of the
District of Columbia, the (a) Council Contract Summary for the Assignment, Assumption and Amendment of Long
Term Subsidy Contract (“Agreement”) by and among the District of Columbia Housing Authority, Jubilee Housing
Limited Partnership, and Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP, and (b) proposed Assignment, Assumption and Amendment of
Long Term Contract Subsidy Contract for the Ritz Apartments by and among the District of Columbia Housing
Authority, Jubilee Housing Limited Partnership, and Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP Approval Resolution of 2025.
The District of Columbia Housing Authority proposes to consent to an assignment, assumption and amendment of
the current Long Term Subsidy Contract between DCHA and Jubilee Housing Limited Partnership (LTSC Contract
Number 0076-2007-0018L) (the "LTSC"). The LTSC will be assigned by Jubilee Housing Limited Partnership to
Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP and will be assumed by Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP. The LTSC will be extended for
twenty (20) years. With the LTSC extension, Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP will obtain long term financing to conduct
moderate rehabilitation on the Ritz Apartments.
As always, I am available to discuss any questions you may have regarding the proposed Agreement. In order to
facilitate a response to any questions concerning this Agreement, please have your staff contact Hammere Gebreyes,
Director of External Affairs and Senior Advisor, at (202) 207-4694.
I look forward to your favorable consideration of this proposed Agreement.
Sincerely,
Keith Pettigrew
Executive Director, District of Columbia Housing Authority

31 Sec. 2. (a) In 2007, the Council of the District of Columbia ("Council") passed Title II of
32 the Fiscal Year 2007 Budget Support Act of 2006 ("BSA") to provide funding for affordable
33 housing for extremely low-income households in the District of Columbia ("District"). The
34 passage of the BSA created the Local Rent Supplement Program ("LRSP"), a program designed
35 to provide affordable housing and supportive services to extremely low-income District
36 residents, including those who are homeless or in need of supportive services, such as elderly
{0030893 -} 1

1 individuals or those with disabilities, through project-based, tenant-based, and sponsored-based
2 LRSP affordable housing units. The BSA provided for the District of Columbia Housing
3 Authority (“DCHA”) to administer the LRSP on behalf of the District.
4 (b) On February 5, 2007, DCHA issued a “Request for Proposals Number 0076A-
5 2007, Local Rent Supplement Program – Project-Based Program (“2007 RFP”)” seeking
6 proposals from property owners, developers, and service providers for participation in DCHA’s
7 LRSP. In the 2007 RFP, DCHA sought to provide LRSP project-based and sponsor-based
8 funding for units that would combine any LRSP award with public housing subsidy received by
9 DCHA from the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development. The LRSP
10 would be funded with District governmental funds. The LRSP housing units were for extremely
11 low-income families making zero to thirty percent (30%) of the area’s median income, as well as
12 the chronically homeless and individuals with mental or physical disabilities.
13 (c) Under the 2007 RFP, DCHA chose Jubilee Housing Limited Partnership (“Jubilee
14 Housing”), a non-profit housing provider, to receive sponsor-based LRSP funds to subsidize
15 eighteen (18) units at the Ritz Apartments, an affordable housing project located at 1613 Euclid
16 Street, NW, Washington, D.C. On May 13, 2010, DCHA and Jubilee Housing entered into a
17 sponsor-based LTSC for the eighteen (18) LRSP units at the Ritz Apartments.
18 (d) Jubilee Housing desires to obtain long term financing to conduct a rehabilitation
19 of the Ritz Apartments. Two of the conditions to obtain the long-term financing are to transfer
20 the Ritz Apartments to Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP (“Jubilee ADMO”) and to extend the term
21 of the LTSC for the Ritz Apartments by twenty (20) years to February 28, 2046.
22 (e) Under D.C. Code §6-227(f)(2), an existing LTSC may be extended without the
23 need for DCHA to issue another RFP for the LTSC if the owner, when the LTSC was executed,
{0030893 -}2

1 or the owner’s successor-in-interest, will be the owner under the extended LTSC. The current
2 owner, Jubilee Housing, will assign the LTSC to Jubilee ADMO, an affiliate of Jubilee Housing,
3 who will assume the LTSC as the successor-in-interest to Jubilee Housing.
4 (f) After approval by the Council, DCHA will enter into the Assignment,
5 Assumption, and Amendment of Long-Term Subsidy Contract by and among DCHA, Jubilee
6 Housing, and Jubilee ADMO (the “Agreement”) which extends the LTSC for twenty (20) years
7 and assigns the LTSC from Jubilee Housing to Jubilee ADMO.
8 (g) There exists an immediate need to approve the Agreement in order to continue to
9 provide long-term affordable housing units for extremely low-income households for the Ritz
10 Apartments LRSP units located at 1631 Euclid Street, NW.
11 (h) The legislation to approve the Agreement will authorize the assignment of the
12 Ritz LTSC from Jubilee Housing to Jubilee ADMO, the assumption by Jubilee ADMO of the
13 Ritz Apartments LTSC, and the extension of the LTSC for twenty (20) years.
14 Sec. 3. Pursuant to section 451 of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved
15 December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 803; D.C. Official Code § 1-204.51), and section 202 of the
16 Procurement Practices Reform Act of 2010, effective April 8, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-371; D.C.
17 Official Code § 2-352.02), the Council approves the Agreement by and among Jubilee
18 Housing, Jubilee ADMO and DCHA to assign the Ritz Apartments LTSC to Jubilee ADMO,
19 to have Jubilee ADMO assume the Ritz Apartments LTSC, and to extend the Ritz
20 Apartments LTSC for twenty (20) years to continue to provide an operating subsidy in
21 support of twenty-five (25) affordable housing units at Ritz Apartments.
