10 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA

12 ____________
13
14 To recognize and celebrate Ms. Ruby L. Powell on the occasion of her 100th birthday.
15
16 WHEREAS, Ms. Ruby L. Powell was born on September 6, 1926 in Atlanta, Georgia;
17
18 WHEREAS, in 1945, Ms. Powell moved to the District of Columbia where she first resided
19 in Ward 1;
20 WHEREAS, in 1948, she and her Uncle George opened a dry-cleaning business on 22nd
21 Street NW in Georgetown. Their business also included a shoe repair shop, laundry, and barber
22 shop, all of which served the local community;
23 WHEREAS, she married David A. Powell in 1949, and they shared a loving marriage until
24 his passing in 2002;
25 WHEREAS, since 1950, Ms. Powell has proudly called Ward 4 home and has resided on
26 Second Street NW;
27 WHEREAS, Ms. Powell retired in 1982 after 30 years of dedicated service with C&P
28 Telephone Company;
29 WHEREAS, in her younger years, Ms. Powell was an avid tennis player, and she remains
30 an enthusiastic card player who enjoys a spirited game of bid whist; and
31 WHEREAS, Ms. Powell is a faithful member of Turner Memorial AME Church, where
32 she has maintained her membership for approximately 80 years. She continues to participate in
33 Bible study and attends worship services whenever she is able.
34 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
35 resolution may be cited as the “Ruby L. Powell 100th Birthday Ceremonial Recognition
36 Resolution of 2026”.
37
38 Sec. 2. The Council joins the family and friends of Ms. Ruby L. Powell in wishing her a
39 happy 100th birthday on September 6, 2026, celebrating and acknowledging this milestone and

40 the robust legacy she has created, and showing appreciation for her commitment to and love of
41 the District of Columbia.
42
43 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
