10 To amend the Construction Codes Approval and Amendments Act of 1986 to direct the
11 Department of Buildings to promulgate rules to allow for residential buildings of a
12 certain height to be served by a single entrance or egress under certain conditions.
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
15 act may be cited as the “One Front Door Amendment Act of 2026”.
16 Sec. 2. The Construction Codes Approval and Amendments Act of 1986, effective March
17 21, 1987 (D.C. Law 6-216; D.C. Official Code § 6-1401 et seq.) is amended as follows:
18 (a) Section 2 (D.C. Official Code § 6-1401) is amended by adding a new paragraph (9A)
19 to read as follows:
20 “(9A) “Multifamily residential building” means a building containing more than 2
21 dwelling units where the occupants are primarily permanent in nature.”.
22 (b) A new section 10f is added to read as follows:
23 “Sec. 10f. Single staircase multifamily residential buildings.
24 “(a)(1) No later than July 1, 2027, the Department of Buildings shall promulgate rules
25 amending the Construction Codes to allow for a single entrance and egress stairway to serve
1

26 multifamily residential buildings up to six stories above grade plane, or a high-rise building.
27 (2) For purposes of subparagraph (1) of this paragraph, the term “high-rise
28 building” means a building with an occupied floor located more than 75 feet above the lowest
29 level of fire department vehicle access.
30 “(b) In drafting rules required pursuant to subsection (a)(1) of this section, the
31 Department shall consider:
32 “(1) Requirements that prevent the spread of fires and smoke in such multifamily
33 residential buildings, including requiring the use of fire-resistive construction, and requiring the
34 provision of adequate automatic fire sprinklers throughout a building;
35 “(2) Requirements that enable occupants to safely and quickly exit such a
36 multifamily residential building in emergency situations, including establishing a maximum
37 travel distance from the exit or entry door of any dwelling unit to the exit stairway, setting square
38 footage limits for any floor of the building, and prohibiting dwelling unit doors from opening
39 directly into an interior exit stairway; and
40 “(3) Any best practices in state, local, and international laws and codes that allow
41 for the construction of multifamily residential buildings with a single entrance and egress
42 stairway.”.
