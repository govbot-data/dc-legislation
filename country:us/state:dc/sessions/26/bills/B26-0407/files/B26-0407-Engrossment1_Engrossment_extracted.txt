12 To amend the Department of Youth Rehabilitation Services Establishment Act of 2004 to require
13 the Mayor to publish within 120 days a plan to address persistent overcrowding of the
14 Youth Services Center and to require the Department of Youth Rehabilitation Services to
15 publish publicly certain population data pertaining to youth at Youth Services Center and
16 New Beginnings Youth Development Center.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
19 act may be cited as the “Strengthening Capacity and Transparency at DYRS Amendment Act of
20 2025”.
21 Sec. 2. The Department of Youth Rehabilitation Services Establishment Act of 2004,
22 effective April 12, 2005 (D.C. Law 15-335; D.C. Official Code § 2-1515.01 et seq.), is amended
23 as follows:
24 Section 104b (D.C. Official Code § 2–1515.04b) is amended by adding new subsections
25 (c-d) to read as follows:
26 “(c) Notwithstanding juvenile confidentiality laws pursuant to D.C. Official Code § 2–
27 1515.06, the Department shall publicly report on their website population data for youth at the

28 Youth Services Center and New Beginnings Youth Development Center. The reported
29 information shall include:
30 “(1) Daily population counts by status at entry, race, and age;
31 “(2) Average length of stay for all youth within the facility; and
32 “(3) The monthly number of youth who:
33 “(A) Are newly admitted to the YSC already with committed status; or
34 “(B) Convert to committed status from another legal status while at YSC
35 “(C) Are placed in instances of improper room confinement pursuant to §
36 24–912, including instances in which they are confined to their room for a period exceeding 6
37 hours.”
38 “(4) The average number of days confined for committed youth at release from
39 the YSC, by month of admission or conversion to committed status; and
40 “(5) Monthly critical incident and assault data by incident type, facility, and
41 month.”
42 “(6) Recidivism data which shall be measured using the following metrics and
43 publicly reported at 12-month, 24-month, and 36-month intervals:
44 “(A) The percentage of youth who are adjudicated delinquent or convicted
45 of a new offense within 12 months of release from an out of home committed placement.
46 “(B) The percentage of youth who are charged with a new offense within
47 12 months of release from an out of home committed placement.

48 “(C) The percentage of youth who are committed or incarcerated as a
49 result of a new offense within 12 months of release from an out of home committed placement.
50 “(d) Within 120 days after the effective date of Strengthening Capacity and Transparency
51 of DYRS Amendment Act of 2025, the Mayor shall submit to the Council a one-year action plan
52 to address overcrowding and the awaiting placement population at the Youth Services Center.
53 The plan shall specify how the Department will permanently reduce the juvenile population at
54 the Youth Services Center to at or below its maximum capacity and avoid future instances of
55 overcrowding.
56 Sec. 3. Applicability
57 (a) This Act shall apply upon the date of inclusion of its fiscal effect in an approved
58 budget and financial plan.
59 (b) The Chief Financial Officer shall certify the date of the inclusion of the fiscal effect in
60 an approved budget and financial plan, and provide notice to the Budget Director of the Council
61 of the certification.
62 (c)(1) The Budget Director shall cause the notice of the certification to be published in
63 the District of Columbia Register.
64 (2) The date of publication of the notice of the certification shall not affect the
65 applicability of this Act.
