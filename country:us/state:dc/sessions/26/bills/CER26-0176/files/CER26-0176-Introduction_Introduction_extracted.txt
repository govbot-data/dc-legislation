8 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
9
10 _________________________
11
12 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
13
14 _________________________
15
16
17 To recognize honoree Kenya Hutton during the 2026 celebration of DC Black Pride.
18
19 WHEREAS, as a social justice, equity, HIV prevention, and sexual health advocate,
20 Kenya Hutton has worked to address a number of determinants of health among communities
21 heavily affected by HIV and other health disparities for over 20 years;
22
23 WHEREAS, throughout his career, Kenya Hutton has advocated for HIV prevention on
24 the local and national level and brought awareness to LGBTQIA+ young adults as well as
25 members of the House/Ballroom community;
26
27 WHEREAS, as a result of his service to these communities, Kenya Hutton has been
28 honored with several accolades, including the Dorian Corey Wisdom Award, the Michael J.
29 Harrington Humanitarian Award, the Award of Excellence from the Washington DC Ballroom
30 Coalition, and the DC Black Pride Presidents Award;
31
32 WHEREAS, Kenya Hutton collaborated with local nonprofits and the DC Health
33 Department in 2019 to develop an educational program focused on providing potential
34 employment opportunities for ballroom community members interested in becoming certified
35 Community Health Workers;
36
37 WHEREAS, Kenya Hutton spearheaded the development of the Capitol Ballroom
38 Council during this period – which allowed ballroom leaders to develop programming and
39 advocacy for the House/Ballroom community in addition to providing valuable programming
40 insight – resulting in him becoming one of the co-founders of the Capitol Ballroom Council, Inc.,
41 a non-profit organization based in Washington, DC;
42
1

43 WHEREAS, Kenya Hutton continues to push the envelope to make sure ballroom culture
44 is recognized as a vibrant, resilient community worthy of praise and support by non-ballroom
45 mainstream organizations;
46
47 WHEREAS, in recent years, Kenya Hutton has been a leading voice in the discussion
48 urging research and interventions to address substance use within the ballroom community,
49 especially Crystal Meth use, as well as developing systems to provide relief and support for the
50 ballroom community during the COVID-19 pandemic and the mpox outbreak;
51
52 WHEREAS, the work of Kenya Hutton is dedicated to the enhancement of the health,
53 economic, and social well-being of marginalized and disenfranchised communities;
54
55 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is an annual event to increase awareness of and showcase
56 pride in the diversity of the LGBTQIA+ populations in the African American community, and to
57 support organizations that focus on health disparities, education, youth, and families;
58
59 WHEREAS, as the very first Black Pride festival in the world, DC Black Pride fostered
60 the beginning of the Center for Black Equity and the “Black Pride Movement”;
61
62 WHEREAS, since the birth of DC Black Pride, more than 50 other Black Pride
63 celebrations now take place throughout the world and on 4 continents, with many using DC
64 Black Pride as a model;
65
66 WHEREAS, May 22, 2026, through May 25, 2026, marks the 35th annual DC Black
67 Pride celebration;
68
69 WHEREAS, the theme for this year’s celebration is “New Black Renaissance,” which
70 centers Black LGBTQIA+ “artists, leaders, healers, and organizers while confronting what still
71 makes Black Pride necessary, including HIV, inequity, homophobia, and transphobia;”
72
73 WHEREAS, for more than 30 years, DC Black Pride has been an opportunity for the
74 Black LGBTQIA+ community to gather and celebrate being both African American and
75 members of LGBTQIA+ communities;
76
77 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is the oldest and one of the largest Black Pride events in the
78 world, drawing thousands of residents and visitors from around the globe; and
79
80 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride brings more than 500,000 people to the nation’s capital
81 from across the United States, Canada, the Caribbean, South Africa, Great Britain, France,
82 Germany, and the Netherlands and will carry that tradition this year and into the future to
83 celebrate their shared community, raise awareness and funding for HIV/AIDS prevention, and
84 stand against homophobia inspired by violence and bigotry.
85
86 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That
87 this resolution be cited as the “Kenya Hutton Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
2

88
89 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates Kenya Hutton
90 during the 2026 celebration of DC Black Pride.
91
92 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
