Dear Secretary Howard:
Today, I am introducing the “Lenora Baker Way Designation Act of 2025”. Please find enclosed a
signed copy of the legislation.
Lenora Baker was a native Washingtonian who symbolized community leadership, endurance, and
resilience in the Southwest community. Having grown up and raised a family during the 1980s and
1990s, Lenora Baker experienced a community ravaged by the crack epidemic, gun and gender-based
violence, premature deaths, and systemic racial and economic inequality. Despite these difficult
odds, Lenora Baker devoted herself to serving her community, volunteering at Amidon-Bowen
Elementary School, Jefferson Middle School Academy, and Richard Wright Public Charter School, in
addition to serving as a deaconess and cook at Westminster Presbyterian Church. She became known
for her cooking and role at the church, taking steps to preserve African American culinary traditions
at the church’s “Friday Night Jazz Nights” and other events. Lenora Baker also worked extensively
with the Ward 6 office to put on events at the Southwest Branch Library and community health fairs.
This legislation would symbolically designate 3rd Street, SW, between I Street, SW, and M Street, SW,
as “Lenora Baker Way”. I would note that Advisory Neighborhood Commission 6D voted unanimously
in support of this symbolic designation on May 19, 2025.
Please feel free to reach out to me or my Legislative Director, Antonio Nunes, with any questions or
for additional information.
Sincerely,
Councilmember Charles Allen, Ward 6
Chairperson, Committee on Transportation & the Environment
