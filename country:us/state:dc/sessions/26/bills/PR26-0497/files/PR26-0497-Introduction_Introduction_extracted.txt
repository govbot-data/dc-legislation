Councilmember Brooke Pinto
A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
______________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
__________________
1 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Criminal Justice
2 Coordinating Council for the District of Columbia Establishment Act of 2001 to clarify
3 certain reporting requirements.
4
5 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
6 resolution may be cited as the “Pretrial Detention Reporting Emergency Declaration Resolution
7 of 2026”.
8 Sec. 2. (a) The Secure DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2024, effective June 8, 2024
9 (D.C. Law 25-175; 71 DCR 2732) (“Secure DC”), established a rebuttable presumption for
10 pretrial detention in certain cases in our criminal justice and juvenile justice systems. These
11 measures are set to expire on December 31, 2026. The Council also established a requirement
12 that the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council (“CJCC”) submit to the Mayor and the Council
13 reports on the efficacy of the criminal justice and juvenile justice pretrial detention provisions.
14 Modifications are needed to Secure DC to ensure the CJCC report covers the appropriate data for
15 effective analysis of these policies. The Council determined earlier this year that additional time
16 is needed for the CJCC to complete the report.
17 (b) Emergency legislation is necessary to amend section 1505 of the Criminal Justice
18 Coordinating Council for the District of Columbia Establishment Act of 2001, effective October

19 3, 2001 (D.C. Law 14-28; D.C. Official Code § 22-4234), to clarify certain reporting
20 requirements.
21 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
22 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Pretrial Detention
23 Reporting Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted after a single reading.
24 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
