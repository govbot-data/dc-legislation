Dear Secretary Howard,
Today, along with Councilmember Christina Henderson, I am introducing the Youth Links App Act of
2025. Please find enclosed a signed copy of the legislation.
Employment access remains a critical challenge for youth in DC. Throughout numerous conversations over
the last several years, I have heard from young people who are trying to stay out of trouble and build a
positive future for themselves that one of the biggest challenges they face is needing to find ways to earn
money. Despite numerous programs available for in-school and out-of-school youth, navigating the web of
opportunities as a young person can be onerous. Many young people express that they struggle to find
productive work opportunities that are flexible with their school and family responsibilities. Meanwhile,
many employers indicate that they are ready and willing to provide opportunities for young people, but do
not know how to find and connect with those young people.
This bill requires the Deputy Mayor for Education (“DME”) to create the DC Youth Links app and website,
a coordinated communication platform to bridge the opportunity and equity gap by streamlining
connections to low-barrier, tailored job opportunities for young people. The bill requires DME to vet
employers before they post opportunities on the app, prioritize recruiting employers from across all eight
wards, work with young people to ensure they are ready for the workforce, and collect data to evaluate the
program.
Should you have any questions about this legislation, please contact my Committee and Legislative
Director, Linn Groft, at lgroft@dccouncil.gov.
Thank you,
Brooke Pinto
Councilmember, Ward 2

19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
20 be cited as the “DC Youth Links App Act of 2025”.
21 Sec. 2. Definitions.
22 For the purposes of this title, the term:
23 (1) “Economic hardship” means circumstances that significantly hinder an
24 individual's ability to access or sustain employment opportunities and shall include the following
25 conditions: homelessness or housing instability; lack of a high school diploma or general
26 education development test or not being on track to complete a high school diploma or general
27 education development test; pregnancy or parenting youth; current or prior involvement in the
28 juvenile or criminal legal systems; living with a disability; or being within or aging out of the
29 foster care system.
30 (2) “Smartphone” means an electronic device capable of Internet access, data
31 storage, and communication features through text, email, and similar digital functionalities.
1

32 (3) “Smartphone application” means a software program developed to operate on
33 a smartphone with specific functionalities or services, including data processing, communication,
34 Internet access, and access to online resources.
35 (4) “Youth” means an individual who is at least 14 years of age and under 24
36 years of age.
37 (5) “Youth participant” means any youth who has provided informed consent and
38 has registered and created an account on the smartphone application.
39 Sec. 3. Establishment of a smartphone application and website for youth employment.
40 (a) The Deputy Mayor for Education (“DME”) shall hire a contractor to establish a
41 centralized platform to directly connect youth to paid employment opportunities called DC
42 Youth Links, which shall be administered by the DME. DME shall oversee the development and
43 operation of DC Youth Links and streamline employment pathways and reduce barriers to
44 workforce entry for District youth.
45 (b) DME shall have the authority to issue grants or enter into contracts or partnership
46 agreements for the development of DC Youth Links.
47 (c) DC Youth Links shall be accessible through a smartphone application and through a
48 publicly accessible website. The smartphone application and website shall:
49 (1) Maintain a centralized platform of paid employment opportunities for District
50 youth;
51 (2) Provide users the ability to filter employment opportunities by:
52 (A) Skills required;
53 (B) Hourly wage;
54 (C) Location; and
2

55 (D) Hours available.
56 (3) Provide users the ability to apply for employment opportunities through the
57 app and website;
58 (4) Feature a feedback tool for youth and employers to rate their experience;
59 (5) Collect and report data to DME pursuant to section 5 of this Act; and
60 (6) Comply with federal and District data privacy standards.
61 Sec. 4. Requirements and eligibility.
62 (a) DME shall:
63 (1) Require employers to complete training on applicable federal and District
64 labor laws, including the Fair Labor Standards Act, the Minimum Wage Act Revision Act of
65 1992, effective March 25, 1993 (D.C. Law 9-248; D.C. Official Code § 32-1003 et al), the Child
66 Labor Amendments Act of 1976 (D.C. Law 1-68; D.C. Official Code § 32-201 et al), workplace
67 safety standards outlined in the Occupational Safety and Health Act, and relevant reporting
68 requirements, prior to accessing DC Youth Links;
69 (2) Develop terms and conditions of use for employers, employer onboarding, and
70 job postings;
71 (3) Create an agreement for all participating employers to adhere to the terms and
72 conditions, including paragraph (1) of this subsection;
73 (4) Implement a vetting process to evaluate and approve new employers;
74 (5) Recruit employers located in all eight wards, offering opportunities that are
75 suitable for youth employment and prioritizing opportunities that are public transit accessible;
76 and
77 (6) Develop and implement a public engagement campaign to promote youth

78 participation, including strategies to engage youth experiencing economic hardship.
79 (b) Employers shall:
80 (1) Be licensed to do business in the District;
81 (2) Respond to workplace abuse reports within 5 business days, unless more
82 immediate attention is warranted;
83 (3) Provide information on youth labor laws and training on workplace
84 expectations to youth participants; and
85 (4) Comply with any other requirements deemed appropriate by DME.
86 (c) Eligible youth participants shall be District residents.
