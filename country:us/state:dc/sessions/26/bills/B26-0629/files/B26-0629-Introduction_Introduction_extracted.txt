SECTION C: SPECIFICATIONS/WORK STATEMENT
C.1 BACKGROUND
From August 2023 to August 2024, the District’s DPW conducted a year-long pilot of
organic waste (food waste) collection from approximately 9,000 single-family
(residential) households located throughout the District. The purpose of the pilot program
was to test operations and gather key data and information to determine the viability of a
District-wide residential food waste collection program in support of the District’s goal to
divert 80% of its waste from landfills and incineration.
During this year’s long pilot, 9,000 households located across the District’s eight (8)
Wards opted in to receive free, once-a-week food waste collection services provided by a
third-party contractor on behalf of the District. As of December 2024, approximately
1,400 tons of food waste collected from 9,000 homes.
The District is currently transitioning from the pilot and aims to expand this residential
food waste collection service to a broader group of single-family households located in
the District of Columbia. The program is now known as the Compost DC: Food Waste
Collection program.
