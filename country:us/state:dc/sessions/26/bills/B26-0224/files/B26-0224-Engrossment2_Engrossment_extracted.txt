11 To amend Title 28 of the District of Columbia Official Code to protect artists, venues, and
12 consumers in the District from unfair and deceptive practices in live event ticket purchases
13 by clarifying a ticket issuer, reseller, or secondary ticket exchange’s existing obligation
14 under the Consumer Protection Procedures Act, like in all industries, to clearly and
15 conspicuously disclose upfront certain information regarding ticket pricing, to prescribe
16 procedures governing the provision of refunds, to prohibit the sale of speculative tickets,
17 to prohibit a ticket issuer from restricting the transferability of a ticket unless the terms
18 and conditions on transferability are clearly disclosed to the consumer prior to purchase, to
19 permit ticket issuers to maintain and enforce policies and conditions or requirements for
20 ticket purchase with respect to conduct, behavior, public health and safety, or age at the
21 venue or event, and establish limits on the amount of tickets that can be purchased, to
22 require a ticket issuer or secondary ticket exchange to maintain reasonable technology to
23 prevent any machine, device, computer program, or computer software, with or without
24 human assistance, from bypassing security measures or access control systems designed to
25 limit the number of tickets that can be purchased by a person on a marketplace for initial
26 purchase or secondary ticket exchange, to require the Mayor to issue rules, to authorize
27 the Mayor to establish regulations governing the price and fee structure for the primary
28 and secondary ticket markets, and to provide for civil penalties for violations of this
29 chapter; and to amend Chapter 28 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code to
30 require a reseller that resells or offers to resell 50 or more tickets a year to obtain a license
31 and maintain a surety bond of $25,000, to clarify that secondary ticket exchanges shall
32 obtain a basic business license, and to outline licensing and reporting requirements for the
33 Department of Licensing and Consumer Protection, resellers, and secondary ticket
34 exchanges.

56 “(1) “Department” means the Department of Licensing and Consumer Protection.
57 “(2) “Resale” means the second or subsequent sale of a ticket by any method,
58 including in-person transactions, telephone, mail, e-mail, facsimile, or electronic means through
59 a website or mobile application.
60 “(3) “Reseller” means a person engaged in the resale of tickets.
61 “(4) “Secondary ticket exchange” means an electronic marketplace that enables a
62 person to sell, purchase, and resell tickets.
63 “(5) “Speculative ticket” means a ticket that is not in the actual or constructive
64 possession of a reseller at the time of listing, sale, resale, or advertisement, including tickets that
65 are not owned by the reseller and ticket that are under contract to be transferred to the reseller.
66 “(6) “Ticket” means any form of physical, electronic, or other evidence that grants
67 the possessor of the evidence license to enter a place of entertainment for one or more events,
68 including live concerts, theatrical performances, shows, or similarly scheduled live activities, at a
69 specified date and time; provided, that the event is not a sporting event organized by a
70 professional or intercollegiate sports team located in the District or professional athletic
71 association with an event located in the District; provided further, that the event is not a motion
72 picture screening.
73 “(7) “Ticket issuer” means a person or their agent who issues tickets for initial
74 sale, including musicians, venues, promoters, theater companies, and marketplaces for initial
75 purchases.

76 “§ 28-5502. Ticket disclosure and refund requirements.
77 “(a) The listing for a ticket and each step of a transaction to purchase a ticket shall clearly
78 and conspicuously disclose:
79 “(1) The total price of the ticket, including all fees and taxes other than shipping
80 costs that are not determinable at a step in the transaction;
81 “(2) An itemized listing of all charges that comprise the total price of the ticket,
82 including all fees and taxes; and
83 “(3) The seat number, zone, and section, to the extent applicable to the seat and
84 venue.
85 “(b)(1) The total price of a ticket disclosed pursuant to subsection (a) of this section may
86 be increased in a noninitial step of a transaction by the amount of reasonable shipping costs for
87 physically delivered tickets.
88 “(2) The shipping costs allowed under paragraph (1) of this subsection may vary
89 with the consumer’s location relative to the shipment’s location of origin and the delivery
90 method selected by the consumer.
91 “(3) The total price of the ticket, including all fees, taxes, and shipping costs, shall
92 be clearly and conspicuously disclosed prior to final purchase of the ticket.
93 “(c)(1) A ticket issuer, secondary ticket exchange, or reseller directly engaged in a
94 transaction with a consumer shall provide a full refund, including all fees and taxes, if:
95 “(A) The ticket is counterfeit;

104 “§ 28-5503. Prohibition on the sale or resale of speculative tickets.
105 “A reseller or a secondary ticket exchange shall not sell, resell, or offer to sell or offer to
106 resell speculative tickets.
107 “§ 28-5504. Transferability of tickets.
108 “(a) A ticket issuer shall not restrict the transferability of a ticket sold unless the terms
109 and conditions on transferability are clearly and conspicuously disclosed to the consumer prior to
110 purchase and the consumer acknowledges receipt of such disclosure prior to purchase.
111 “(b) A ticket issuer may, subject to applicable District or federal law:
112 “(1) Maintain and enforce policies and conditions or requirements for ticket
113 purchase with respect to conduct, behavior, public health and safety, or age at the venue or event;

115 “(2) Establish limits on the quantity of tickets that may be purchased.

116 “§ 28-5505. Requirement of reasonable technology for a ticket issuer and secondary
117 ticket exchange.
118 “A ticket issuer and secondary ticket exchange, to the extent applicable and under the
119 totality of the circumstances, shall implement and maintain reasonable technology to prevent any
120 machine, device, computer program, or computer software, with or without human assistance,
121 from bypassing security measures or access control systems designed to limit the number of
122 tickets that can be purchased by a person on a primary or secondary ticket exchange.
123 “§ 28-5506. Requirements for resale of tickets.
124 “(a) A secondary ticket exchange shall, prior to a ticket being resold or offered to be
125 resold:
126 “(1) Verify that the reseller meets all licensure and bonding requirements
127 established in § 47-2890.02; and
128 “(2) Disclose the original purchase price of the ticket, including all fees and taxes
129 in connection with the initial purchase price of the ticket.2
130 “(b) Except as provided in subsection (c) of this section, a reseller or secondary
131 ticket exchange shall not sell or offer to sell a ticket that exceeds the total price of the initial
132 ticket, including all fees and taxes in connection with the initial ticket, plus up to 10% of
133 the total price of the initial ticket, including all fees and taxes in connection with the initial
134 ticket.

175 “§ 28-5508. Penalties and enforcement.
176 “(a) A violation of this chapter shall be a Class 2 civil infraction and subject to fines
177 pursuant to section 3201 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations (16
178 DCMR § 3201).
179 “(b) Failure to comply with the requirements of this chapter shall be an unlawful trade
180 practice under § 28-3904.”.
181 “(c) The civil penalty imposed pursuant to subsection (a) of this section shall be in
182 addition to the remedies available to the Attorney General pursuant to § 28-3909.
183 “§ 28-5509. Prohibition on surveillance-based price discrimination.
184 “(a) A ticket issuer, reseller, or secondary ticket exchange shall not use personally
185 identifiable information collected through electronic surveillance technology, technological
186 methods, systems, or tools, including sensors, cameras, device tracking, biometric
187 monitoring, or other forms of observation or data collection capable of gathering
188 personally identifiable information about a consumer’s behavior, characteristics, location,
189 or other personal attributes, whether in physical or digital environments, to set the price
190 for a ticket covered under this chapter.
191 “(b) For the purposes of this section, the term “personally identifiable information”
192 means information that can be used to distinguish or trace an individual’s identity, either
193 alone or when combined with other personal or identifying information that is linked or
194 linkable to a specific individual.”.
