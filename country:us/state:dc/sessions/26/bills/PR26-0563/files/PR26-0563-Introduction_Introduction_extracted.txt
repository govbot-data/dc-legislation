20 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Body-Worn
21 Camera Regulation and Reporting Requirements Act of 2015 and Chapter 39 of Title 24
22 of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations to require release of body-worn
23 camera recordings of Metropolitan Police Department Officers present in the event of an
24 officer-involved death or serious use of force by a law enforcement officer other than a
25 Metropolitan Police Department officer.
26
27 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
28 resolution may be cited as the “Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency
29 Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
30 Sec. 2. (a) District residents deserve transparency when there is a serious use of force or
31 officer-involved death involving federal agencies. Current law requires that the Mayor release
32 body-worn camera recordings of Metropolitan Police Department (“MPD”) officers present in
33 the event of an officer-involved death or serious use of force by an MPD officer. However, there
34 is no such requirement for release of MPD body-worn camera recordings when the officer-
35 involved death or serious use of force involves a law enforcement officer from another agency,
36 including federal officers.

37 (b) Emergency legislation is necessary to require the release of MPD body-worn
38 camera recordings and incident details when there is a serious use of force or officer-involved
39 death involving other law enforcement agencies while an MPD officer is present.
40 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
41 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making it necessary that the Body-Worn Camera
42 Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted after a single
43 reading.
44 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
