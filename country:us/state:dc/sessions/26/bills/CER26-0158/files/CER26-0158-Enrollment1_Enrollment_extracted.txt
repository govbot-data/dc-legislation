ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
26-158
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
May 5, 2026
To acknowledge and express gratitude to David C. Tseng for his years of service to the District
of Columbia on the occasion of his retirement from public service.
WHEREAS, David C. Tseng received a bachelor’s degree, with honors, from Columbia
University in English Literature and a Juris Doctor from the University of California at Los
Angeles;
WHEREAS, Mr. Tseng was appointed General Counsel for the Office of the Chief
Financial Officer (OCFO) in July of 2007 and served under four Chief Financial Officers (CFO).
Mr. Tseng served as the principal legal advisor to the CFO, directing the legal resources of the
OCFO with respect to federal and District appropriations, public finance, contracting and
procurement, tax accounting, and economic development. Mr. Tseng also represented the CFO in
other legal matters in which the CFO was a party or had a legal interest, supervised and managed
outside counsel in all legal matters, and ensured the legal sufficiency of the OCFO's programs and
policies;
WHEREAS, Prior to his current position, Mr. Tseng served as the OCFO’s Associate
General Counsel for Legislative Affairs from 2006 to 2007, and Deputy Associate General
Counsel for the Office of Tax and Revenue from 2004 to 2006. Before joining the District of
Columbia government, Mr. Tseng was a White House Senior Policy Advisor and Staff Director
for the National Economic Council under President Bill Clinton from 2000 to 2001. He also served
as Chief Assistant Treasurer for the City and County of San Francisco from 1998 to 2000. Mr.
Tseng worked as a Special Assistant to the Assistant Secretary of Labor for Pension and Welfare
Benefits at the U.S. Department of Labor from 1993 to 1998;
WHEREAS, Mr. Tseng represents the OCFO’s ideal of a public servant who strives every
day to provide the highest standard of service to residents, businesses, visitors, and the government
of the District of Columbia by being Service Driven with a Strong Work Ethic; Motivated and
Mission Focused; Accountable; Respectful of his Customers and Co-Workers; Trustworthy and
Team-Focused; Empowered to Achieve Excellence; and Results Oriented, or SMARTER; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Tseng will be remembered, with gratitude, by countless colleagues who
were better able to perform their jobs because of his willingness to share his extensive knowledge
