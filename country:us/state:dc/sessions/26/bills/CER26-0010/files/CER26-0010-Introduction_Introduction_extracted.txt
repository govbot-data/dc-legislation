13 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
14 __________
15
16
17 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
18
19 ____________________
20
21 To honor and recognize the life of Derrick Ward and celebrate his profound contributions
22 to journalism, his unwavering dedication to the Washington, D.C., community, and his enduring
23 legacy as a loving father and inspiring role model.
24
25 WHEREAS, Derrick Ward was born and raised in Washington, D.C. and grew up in the
26 Marshall Heights and H Street Corridor neighborhoods, attended H.D. Woodson High School,
27 and graduated from the University of Maryland, reflecting his deep-rooted connection to the
28 community he served;
29
30 WHEREAS, Derrick began his illustrious journalism career in radio, contributing to
31 esteemed stations such as WPFW, WAMU, and WTOP, where he covered significant events
32 including the Iran-Contra hearings, September 11th, and the Washington-area sniper shootings;
33
34 WHEREAS, throughout his career, Ward served as a mentor to many aspiring journalists,
35 offering guidance and support to those entering the field. His leadership and dedication to
36 journalism left a lasting impact on his colleagues and the broader media community.
37
38 WHEREAS, in 2006, Derrick joined News4 Washington as a reporter, bringing his
39 profound knowledge of the city’s history and his exceptional storytelling skills to television
40 audiences, earning the reputation of News4’s “resident D.C. historian and poet”;
41
42 WHEREAS, beyond his journalistic endeavors, Derrick was a talented musician, known
43 for his proficiency with the guitar, and was celebrated for his warmth, humor, and unwavering
44 dedication to his craft;
45

46 WHEREAS, Derrick Ward passed away on January 7, 2025, at the age of 62;
47
48 WHEREAS, Derrick is survived by his three children—Derrick Jr., Ian, and Marisa—
49 who continue to honor his legacy;
50
51 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, this resolution may be
52 cited as the “Derrick Ward Posthumous Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
53
54 The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the accomplishments of
55 the Derrick Ward and congratulates him on a great season.
56
57 This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
58 District of Columbia Register.
