15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Energy Efficiency
16 Financing Act of 2010 to remove the limitation on the principal amount of bonds that the
17 District may issue.
18
19 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
20 resolution may be cited as “Energy Efficiency Financing Debt Cap Emergency Declaration
21 Resolution of 2025”.
22 Sec. 2. (a) The Energy Efficiency Financing Act of 2010, effective May 27, 2010 (D.C.
23 Law 18-183; D.C. Official Code § 8–1778.01 et seq.) (“Act”) established the Commercial Property
24 Assessed Clean Energy (“C-PACE”) Program in the District. The C-PACE Program facilitates
25 private financing for energy efficiency, renewable energy, and water efficiency improvements,
26 which borrowers repay through a special assessment on the property’s tax bill.
27 (b) When initially established, the C-PACE Program operated as a bonding program,
28 through which the District would issue bonds to support energy efficiency upgrades. The Act
29 established a $250 million aggregate bond cap for C-PACE projects, which remains in place today.
30 (c) In 2012, the District amended the original program to enable direct investment in C-
31 PACE projects from private capital providers. Under this model, the District still issues a note for
32 that private investment.

33 (d) In 2021, the DC Green Finance Authority (“DC Green Bank”) assumed administration
34 of the C-PACE Program. Even though the District is not obligated in any privately financed C-
35 PACE deal, the Office of the Attorney General has advised the DC Green Bank that the bond cap
36 still applies.
37 (e) Currently, the C-PACE Program is utilizing $184 million of the $250 million cap,
38 leaving only $66 million for additional financing capacity. The DC Green Bank has determined
39 that the C-PACE Program market demand exceeds its capacity given the bond cap.
40 (f) More specifically, the DC Green Bank has identified an opportunity to issue bonds in
41 the principal amount of $470 million in partnership with Nuveen to finance the installation of
42 energy efficiency improvements at The Geneva (1825—1875 Connecticut Avenue NW) before
43 the end of the Calendar Year 2025. The Geneva is an office building comprised of two towers that
44 will be converted into a 14-story, 532-unit residential building.
45 (g) However, the issuance of bonds for this project would exceed the Act’s statutory limit
46 on the principal amount of bonds that may be issued. Additionally, the DC Green Bank anticipates
47 an increasing number of larger C-PACE deals in the near future with the development of the RFK
48 site and surrounding areas.
49 (h) Furthermore, after a review of its peers in other jurisdictions, the DC Green Bank has
50 determined that placing a statutory cap on the principal amount of bonds that may be issued is not
51 a common practice.
52 (i) Emergency legislation is therefore necessary to remove the limitation on the principal
53 amount of bonds that may be issued under the Energy Efficiency Act.
2

54 Sec. 3. The Council finds the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency
55 circumstances making it necessary that the “Energy Efficiency Financing Debt Cap Emergency
56 Amendment Act of 2025” be adopted after a single reading.
57 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
