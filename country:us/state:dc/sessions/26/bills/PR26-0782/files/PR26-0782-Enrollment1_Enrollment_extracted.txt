ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A RESOLUTION
26-490
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
July 14, 2026
To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Restoration of
Covenanted Roads and Alleys by the District Government Act of 2024 to further clarify
the District’s authority to repair or restore certain private rights-of-way to a condition
appropriate for the passage of motor vehicles owned or operated by the District
government.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
resolution may be cited as the “Restoration of Covenanted Roads and Alleys by the District
Government Clarification Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
Sec. 2. (a) There exists an immediate need to further clarify the District’s authority to
repair certain roads and alleys under the Restoration of Covenanted Roads and Alleys by the
District Government Act of 2024, effective March 28, 2025 (D.C. Law 25-320; D.C. Official
Code § 9-401.19) (“Restoration Act”).
(b) In 2024, the Council passed the Restoration Act to authorize the Mayor to repair and
restore certain covenanted roadways in the District of Columbia with or without the consent of
the property owner. The law also authorized the Mayor to seek appropriate releases and
indemnification from adjacent property owners, and to recover expenses from private property
owners on behalf of the District, if applicable.
(c) The need for the law arose from the outreach of several members of the Hillcrest
community in Southeast Washington. In 2021, a group of residents requested that a private
alleyway off W Street, SE, be repaired as if a public alley so that the residents could continue to
have the ability to use the driveway to access the rear of their homes. In that case, the owner of
record in the 1960s, Johnson Brothers, had established the alleyway as a condition of obtaining
city approval of its housing development, and the alleyway continues to serve several houses. In
July 2019, the alleyway (Lot 811) was sold at a tax sale to a new owner, who filed a foreclosure
action in the Superior Court to extinguish Johnson Brothers’ right to redeem. Several of the
neighbors filed a motion to intervene in the matter attempting to stop the sale. The efforts of the
neighbors, coupled with other government intervention, did result in the tax sale being reversed.
(d) There are similar situations regarding alleys in other places around the city. There is
an unpaved alley off Morris Road, SE, that is badly in need of repair. The alley is on the south
or west side of Morris Road, on the block between Mount View Place and High Street.
1

Residents have reported that a Department of Public Works trash truck got stuck in snow and ice
in previous winters and badly damaged the surface of the alley. However, the alley was never
recorded in the Office of the Surveyor as a public alley. Nevertheless, the alley is necessary for
trash collection and other city services. Moreover, just as was the case in the Hillcrest situation,
the alley is “L” shaped and is the only way residents along this portion of Morris Road can park
or put out their trash.
(e) Emergency legislation is necessary to provide the Executive with sufficient authority
to repair the Morris Road, SE, alley and other alleys and roadways that are similarly situated,
right now, prior to what may be another harsh winter in the District. Permanent legislation also
will be introduced to ensure that this authority is available to the Executive on a lasting basis.
Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
Restoration of Covenanted Roads and Alleys by the District Government Clarification
Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted after a single reading.
Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
