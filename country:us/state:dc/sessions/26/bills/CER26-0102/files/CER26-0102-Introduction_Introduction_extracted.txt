15 To honor Rev. Dr. A. Knighton Stanley and his legacy as pastor of Peoples Congregational
16 United Church of Christ for nearly 40 years, for the occasion of the designation of Iowa
17 Avenue N.W., between Arkansas Avenue, N.W., and 13th Street, N.W., in Ward 4, as
18 “Rev. A. Knighton Stanley Way”.
19
20 WHEREAS, Rev. Dr. A. Knighton Stanley, a minister, civil rights leader, educator, and
21 community leader, served the Washington, D.C. community for nearly 40 years;
22 WHEREAS, Rev. Dr. A. Knighton Stanley graduated with top honors from Talladega
23 College in 1959, earned a Master of Divinity Degree from Yale University and a Doctor of
24 Ministry degree from Howard University, as well as serving as a Woodrow Wilson and
25 Rockefeller Fellow;
26 WHEREAS, Rev. Dr. A. Knighton Stanley was active in the Greensboro, North Carolina
27 Civil Rights Movement, served as advisor to the local chapter of the Congress of Racial Equality
28 (“CORE”), a Field Representative for CORE in eastern North Carolina and was appointed to the
29 first Human Rights Commission of the city of Greensboro, North Carolina, in 1962-63;
30 WHEREAS, Dr. A. Knighton Stanley joined the faculty and administration of Bennett
31 College as an instructor in the Humanities and Social Sciences Department, and Director of
32 Religious life in 1964;
1

33 WHEREAS, Rev. Dr. A. Knighton Stanley was first called to Washington in 1968 as the
34 eighth senior pastor of Peoples’ Congregational United Church of Christ in the Petworth
35 community;
36 WHEREAS, Under his leadership, the church became the keystone institution in the
37 Petworth Community, the congregation’s membership grew three-fold, it was home to the largest
38 scouting troops in the city, a food pantry that served hundreds of families, a youth group that
39 included dozens of youth from the neighborhood and beyond, a credit union, drama and liturgical
40 dance groups and other community programs for children and adults;
41 WHEREAS, During his tenure, Peoples Congregational United Church was a cultural
42 hub for black scholars and activists including Maya Angelou, Desmond Tutu, Andrew Young,
43 James Baldwin, Shirley Chisolm, and Dorothy Irene Height who donned the pulpit on special
44 occasions;
45 WHEREAS, During his tenure, Peoples Church became known for its cultural arts
46 programs; its scholarship program, which serves church and community; its openness to all as a
47 community facility, and its efforts to forge new alliances across religious and cultural lines;
48 WHEREAS, During his tenure, Peoples Church spear-headed conversations between
49 African Americans and African immigrants and with the Jewish, Islamic and Ethiopian Orthodox
50 communities;
51 WHEREAS, Rev. Dr. A. Knighton Stanley served his Denomination in numerous
52 capacities, including a member of the Committee on Theological Education; a member of the
53 Council for Christian Social Action; President of Ministers for Racial and Social Justice; a
54 member of the Board of the Office of Communications, a member of the New Century Hymnal
55 Committee; a member of the Nominating Committee of the General Synod; a member of the
2

56 Large Gifts Committee of the Denomination’s Capital Funds Campaign; a member on the
57 Church and Ministry Committee of the Potomac Association and as a member of the Board of
58 Directors of the Central Atlantic Conference of the United Church of Christ and served as a
59 consultant for the revision of the Book of Worship for the United Church of Christ;
60 WHEREAS, Dr. A. Knighton Stanley was a teacher and administrator on the post-
61 secondary level, served as Adjunct Faculty at Howard University School of Divinity, and Chair
62 of the Board of Trustees of the University of the District of Columbia, and was a member of the
63 Board of Governors of the Washington Latin School and the Board of Advisors of the Yale
64 University Divinity School;
65 WHEREAS, Dr. A. Knighton Stanley was the writer of many articles, former publisher
66 of “The New American Missionary,” and the author of The Children is Crying, and traveled
67 extensively in Asia, Africa, Europe, the Caribbean and the Middle East;
68 WHEREAS, Rev. Dr. A. Knighton Stanley served on the Board of Directors and as Vice
69 President of Greater Washington Council of Churches, chairman of the District of Columbia
70 Chapter of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference, appointed to the Educational
71 Institution Licensure Commission by Mayor Walter E. Washington, special advisor to Mayor
72 Walter Washington on the DC Bicentennial Commission, and served on the Advisory Board of
73 Industrial Bank of Washington;
74 WHEREAS, Dr. A. Knighton Stanley served as a member of the Judiciary Nominating
75 Committee for the District of Columbia Superior Court and Court of Appeals; was the founder
76 and General Secretary of the Petworth Assembly and a member of the Board of Directors of the
77 National Interfaith Alliance, as well as the founding President of the Faith Based Community
78 Action Partnership, an interfaith organization which, in conjunction with the Metropolitan Police
3

79 Department and area schools, addresses the needs of children and youth, also serving on the
80 panel of Religious Advisors to Mayor Anthony Williams; and
81 WHEREAS, the Council passed the “Rev. A. Knighton Stanley Way Designation Act of
82 2025”, symbolically designating Iowa Avenue N.W., between Arkansas Avenue, N.W., and 13th
83 Street, N.W., in Ward 4, as “Rev. A. Knighton Stanley Way”.
84 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
85 resolution may be cited as the “Rev. A. Knighton Stanley Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
86 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia honors Rev. Dr. A. Knighton Stanley and
87 his legacy as pastor of Peoples Congregational United Church of Christ for nearly 40 years, for
88 the occasion of the designation of Iowa Avenue N.W., between Arkansas Avenue, N.W., and
89 13th Street, N.W., in Ward 4, as “Rev. A. Knighton Stanley Way”.
90 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
91 the District of Columbia Register.
