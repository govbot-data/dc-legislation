Dear Secretary Howard:
Today, along with Chairman Phil Mendelson and Councilmembers Brooke Pinto, Janeese Lewis
George, Brianne K. Nadeau, and Robert C. White, Jr., I am introducing the “Architect and
Engineer Good Samaritan Amendment Act of 2025.” Please find enclosed a signed copy of the
legislation.
In response to natural and human-made disasters and emergencies, we rely on licensed
professionals to voluntarily render aid. To make sure that these licensed professionals are not
discouraged from providing that critical assistance, current law protects some types of
professionals against civil liability when rendering aid to those in need. This type of law is known
as a “Good Samaritan” law, and it is frequently invoked in situations involving help provided by
physicians in medical or life-threatening emergencies.
Architects and registered civil, geotechnical, and structural engineers are more than willing to
volunteer their skills and time to help communities rebuild and safely respond to natural or
human-made disasters. For example, these professionals may be called on by state or local
emergency management agencies to provide immediate post-crisis safety and habitability
evaluations of homes, buildings, or other infrastructure in the event of a fire, building collapse,
earthquake, or severe weather. Forty-one states across the country – including Maryland and
Virginia -- have Good Samaritan laws in place to protect architects and engineers who provide
disaster assistance from civil liability.
This legislation would have the District join the supermajority of states across the country by
limiting civil liability, absent gross negligence or willful misconduct, for licensed architects or
professional engineers who, in good faith and without compensation, render assistance at the
scene of or in connection with a life-threatening emergency in the built environment, at the
direction or request of or with the approval of a federal or District official, during a period of a
1

public emergency declared by the Mayor or within 90 days following the end of the period for
the life-threatening emergency in the built environment.
Please feel free to reach out to me or my Legislative Director, Antonio Nunes, with any questions
or for additional information.
Sincerely,
Charles Allen, Ward 6 Councilmember
Chairperson, Committee on Transportation & the Environment

25 To amend Subchapter I-B of Chapter 28 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code to
26 provide limited civil liability, absent gross negligence or willful misconduct, to licensed
27 architects and professional engineers who, in good faith and without compensation,
28 render assistance at the scene of or in connection with a life-threatening emergency in the
29 built environment, at the direction or request of or with the approval of a federal or
30 District official, during a period of a public emergency declared by the Mayor or within
31 90 days following the end of the period for the life-threatening emergency in the built
32 environment.
33
34 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
35 act may be cited as the “Architect and Engineer Good Samaritan Amendment Act of 2025”.
36 Sec. 2. Subchapter I-B of Chapter 28 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official
37 Code is amended as follows:
38 (a) Part C is amended as follows:
39 (1) The table of contents is amended by adding a new section designation as
40 follows:
1

41 “§ 47-2853.69. Limited civil liability for voluntary practice of architecture at scene of
42 life-threatening emergency in the built environment.”.
43 (2) A new section 47-2853.69 is added to read as follows:
44 “§ 47-2853.69. Limited civil liability for voluntary practice of architecture at scene of
45 life-threatening emergency in the built environment.
46 “Any person with a valid license as an architect shall not be liable, in the absence of gross
47 negligence or willful misconduct, for any civil damages for acts or omissions resulting from the
48 rendering of assistance at the scene of or in connection with a life-threatening emergency in the
49 built environment; provided, that the person acted:
50 “(1) During a public emergency declared by the Mayor pursuant to section 5(a) of
51 the District of Columbia Public Emergency Act of 1980, effective March 5, 1981 (D.C. Law 3-
52 149; D.C. Official Code § 7-2304(a)), or within 90 days following the end of the period for the
53 life-threatening emergency in the built environment;
54 “(2) In good faith and without compensation;
55 “(3) At the direction or request of or with the approval of a federal or District
56 official reasonably believed by the person to be acting in an official capacity; and
57 “(4) After having completed a training program that uses the California Office of
58 Emergency Management’s Safety Assessment Program to provide licensed architects the ability
59 to assist local governments in safety evaluation of their built environment in the aftermath of a
60 life-threatening emergency in the built environment.”.
61 (b) Part J is amended as follows:
62 (1) The table of contents is amended by adding a new section designation as
63 follows:
2

64 “§ 47-2853.134. Limited civil liability for voluntary practice of engineering at scene of
65 life-threatening emergency in the built environment.”.
66 (2) A new section 47-2853.134 is added to read as follows:
67 “§47-2853.134. Limited civil liability for voluntary practice of engineering at scene of
68 life-threatening emergency in the built environment.
69 “Any person with a valid license as a professional engineer shall not be liable, in the
70 absence of gross negligence or willful misconduct, for any civil damages for acts or omissions
71 resulting from the rendering of assistance at the scene of or in connection with a life-threatening
72 emergency in the built environment; provided, that the person acted:
73 “(1) During a public emergency declared by the Mayor pursuant to section 5(a) of
74 the District of Columbia Public Emergency Act of 1980, effective March 5, 1981 (D.C. Law 3-
75 149; D.C. Official Code § 7-2304(a)), or within 90 days following the end of the period for the
76 life-threatening emergency in the built environment;
77 “(2) In good faith and without compensation;
78 “(3) At the direction or request of or with the approval of a federal or District
79 official reasonably believed by the person to be acting in an official capacity; and
80 “(4) After having completed a training program that uses the California Office of
81 Emergency Management’s Safety Assessment Program to provide licensed professional
82 engineers the ability to assist local governments in safety evaluation of their built environment in
83 the aftermath of a life-threatening emergency in the built environment.”.
