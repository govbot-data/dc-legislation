(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
1

The contractor shall immediately provide the heavy equipment, management, personnel, and
transportation needed to remove heavy accumulations of snow and ice across primary and
secondary roadways, bridges, intersections, and critical access routes within the District.
(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:
The District solicited quotes from multiple firms that demonstrated experience in the required
services and the ability to perform the work immediately. The District received a quote from Fort
Myer. Fort Myer has the availability and capacity to begin the work immediately. Therefore, Fort
Myer’s quote was determined to be the most advantageous to the District, given the nature of the
emergency.

Fort Myer is a leading infrastructure construction company with extensive experience in providing
a wide range of street and roadway construction services for local, state, and federal government
agencies in the District of Columbia, Virginia, and Maryland. Fort Myer is currently performing:
DCKA-2024-C-0037 – Construction of Citywide Local Sidewalk Restoration;

DCKA-2023-C-0006 – Metropolitan Branch Trail;

DCKA-2021-C-0010 – Asphalt Materials.
(G) The background and qualifications of the proposed contractor, including its organization,
financial stability, personnel, and performance on past or current government or private
sector contracts with requirements similar to those of the proposed contract:
Fort Myer is an infrastructure construction firm that provides a wide range of street and roadway
maintenance and construction services. Fort Myer has been performing under contracts for local,
state, and federal government agencies, as well as private clients, for over 50 years. Fort Myer has a
record of satisfactory performance on previous District Department of Transportation (DDOT)
projects. Furthermore, Fort Myer has the capacity, personnel, and equipment necessary to begin the
work immediately.

This is to Certify that this Office has reviewed the above-referenced Contract. To the extent
OAG is being requested to review the contents of the contract action the contents are found to
be legally sufficient. The process used to enter in this contract violated the requirements of
DC Code § 2–352.02. (Criteria for Council review of multiyear contracts and contracts in
excess of $1 million) because the parties agreed the value of the base period of the contract
would potentially be more than $1,000,000 during a twelve-month period, before receiving
Council approval. Further, the letter contract violated 27 DCMR 2425.7 since the District’s
maximum liability under the letter contract exceeded fifty percent (50%) of the overall price
ceiling for the term of the anticipated definitive contract. The legislation included with this
contract action was written to remedy these deficiencies.
