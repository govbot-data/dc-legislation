48 Sec. 3. Section 501(r) of the Rental Housing Act of 1985, effective July 17, 1985 (D.C.
49 Law 6-10; D.C. Official Code § 42-3505.01(r)), is amended to read as follows:
50 “(r)(1) The court may stay any proceedings for a claim brought by a housing provider to
51 recover possession of a rental unit for non-payment of rent if a tenant submits documentation to
52 the court demonstrating that he or she has a pending Emergency Rental Assistance Program
53 application; provided, that the court may stay proceedings pursuant to this subsection only once
54 during the pendency of the case.
55 “(2) The proviso in paragraph (1) of this subsection shall not be construed to limit
56 a court’s discretion to extend or continue a stay.
2

57 “(3) When an eviction that involves non-payment of rent has been authorized by
58 the court and a tenant notifies the housing provider that he or she has an approved Emergency
59 Rental Assistance Program application that would pay the full amount of unpaid rent owed by
60 the tenant no later than 48 hours prior to the scheduled date and time of the eviction, the housing
61 provider shall reschedule the eviction for a date no earlier than 3 weeks from the current
62 scheduled eviction date to allow for the application to be processed, a determination of funding
63 to be made, and, if the application is approved, funding to be distributed to the housing
64 provider.”.
