Dear Secretary Smith,
Today I, along with Councilmembers Brianne Nadeau, Matthew Frumin, Robert C. White, Janeese
Lewis George, Charles Allen, and Wendell Felder, am introducing the “Expanding Access to
Public Restrooms Act of 2025.” Please find enclosed a signed copy of the legislation. I introduced
an identical version of this legislation in Council Period 25, B25-102.
This legislation would dramatically increase the availability of public restrooms by providing grant
funding to facilitate the provision and maintenance of public bathrooms and by requiring that all
new and substantially renovated parks that are one acre or more in size have a public restroom.
This legislation follows my previously introduced legislation to expand restroom accessibility –
the Medical Necessity Restroom Access Act of 2021––which provides an individual with an
eligible medical condition the right to use a public or employee toilet facility at retail
establishments.
Currently, public restrooms are difficult to access in the District; this disproportionately impacts
people experiencing homelessness, pregnant people, people with gastrointestinal diseases and a
variety of other medical conditions, and young children. A lack of public restrooms contributes to
public urination and defecation that lead to serious public health issues.
Access to safe and clean restrooms is a basic necessity and thus it is imperative that the Council
take action to address the scarcity of public bathrooms across the District. This legislation will
provide dignity to people experiencing homelessness, pregnant people, people with medical
conditions, and young children who rely most consistently on access to restrooms. By expanding
public restroom access, the District will be a safer, more welcoming and inclusive place for all
residents and visitors.
The “Expanding Access to Public Restrooms Act of 2025” will increase the availability of public
restrooms by:
 Dramatically increasing the number of public restrooms in all eight wards in high-use
locations,
 Establishing a grant fund to support the efforts of BIDs and Main Streets to provide and
maintain public restroom facilities,

 Requiring the Department of Parks and Recreation (DPR) to include public restroom
facilities in all new and substantially renovated parks that are at least one acre or more in
size, and
 Ensuring public restrooms are well-maintained by providing BIDs, Main Streets, and
DPR the flexibility to determine whether to contract with a third-party provider or hire
internal staff to maintain restroom facilities.
Should you have any questions about this legislation, please contact my Legislative and Committee
Director, Linn Groft, at lgroft@dccouncil.gov.
Thank you,
Brooke Pinto
Ward 2 Councilmember

5 To establish a Business Improvement District and Main Street Public Restroom Facility Fund to
6 be administered by the Mayor to support efforts to provide and maintain public restroom
7 facilities; to require the Department of Parks and Recreation to include public restroom
8 facilities in all new and substantially renovated parks that are one acre or more in size.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
10 act may be cited as the “Expanding Access to Public Restrooms Act of 2025”.
11 Sec. 1. Definitions.
12 (a) “Business Improvement District” or “BID” has the same meaning as defined in § 2–
13 1215.02.
14 (b) “DPR” means Department of Parks and Recreation.
15 (c) “Public Restroom Facility” means a gender-neutral restroom maintained by the
16 District or a third-party and accessible to the public free of charge, 7 days a week, from at least 8
1

17 a.m. to 8 p.m. This may include a utility-free restroom but shall not include a portable restroom.
18 Sec. 2. Business Improvement District and Main Street Public Restroom Facility Fund.
19 (a) There is established as a nonlapsing fund the Business Improvement District and
20 Main Street Public Restroom Facility Fund (“Fund”), which shall be used solely for the purposes
21 set forth in subsection (c) of this section, and administered by the Deputy Mayor for Planning
22 and Economic Development.
23 (b)(1) All funds deposited into the Fund shall not revert to the unrestricted fund balance
24 of the General Fund of the District at the end of a fiscal year, or at any other time, but shall be
25 continually available for the uses and purposes set forth in subsection (c) of this section without
26 regard to the fiscal year limitation, subject to authorization by Congress.
27 (2) Any funds that are transferred through intra-District funding and are not expended
28 in any fiscal year shall revert to the Fund and be available for use in the subsequent fiscal year.
29 (c) The Fund shall be distributed to every BID and Main Street solely to support efforts to
30 provide and maintain public restroom facilities within their respective BID or Main Street areas.
31 Sec. 3. Public Restrooms in DC Parks
32 DPR shall include at least one gender neutral public restroom facility in all new and
33 substantially renovated parks that are one acre or more in size.
