14 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to continue existing
15 requirements for providers of commercial or residential rental property to abide by agreed
16 upon rent payment plans with eligible tenants.
17
18 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this resolution
19 may be cited as the “Tenant Payment Plan Phasing Emergency Declaration Resolution of
20 2025”.
21 Sec. 2. (a) As part of its response to the COVID-19 public health emergency, the
22 Council passed emergency and temporary legislation allowing commercial and residential
23 tenants demonstrating financial hardship in connection with the emergency to apply for
24 payment plans with their landlords.
25 (b)The Council has kept the tenant payment plan framework in place through
26 subsequent emergency and temporary measures. The current instance is the Tenant Payment
27 Plan Phasing Continuation Temporary Act of 2024, enacted July 26, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-211;
28 D.C. Official Code § 42-3331). That measure will expire on May 1, 2025.
29 (c)Although eligibility to apply for payment plans pursuant to such legislation ended in
30 July 2022, the legislation contained tenant protections and administrative provisions, including
31 a 3-year application record retention provision that applies to landlords. The record-keeping
1

32 obligation was therefore intended to run through July of 2025.
33 (d) Emergency legislation is necessary to avoid a gap in the law prior to the end of the
34 originally contemplated 3-year implementation timeframe.
35 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
36 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Tenant
37 Payment Plan Phasing Emergency Act of 2025 must be adopted after a single reading.
38 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
