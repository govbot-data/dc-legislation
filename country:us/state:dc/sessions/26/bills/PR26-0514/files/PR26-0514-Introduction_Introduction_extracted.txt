17 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to provide grant authority to
18 the District Department of Transportation to Issue a Grant to Improve Water Access at
19 Wangari Gardens.
20
21 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 resolution may be cited as the “Friends of Wangari Gardens Grant Emergency Declaration
23 Resolution of 2026.”
24 Sec. 2. (a) In the Report and Recommendations of the Committee on Transportation and
25 the Environment on the Fiscal Year 2026 Budget for Agencies Under Its Purview (Report),1 the
26 Committee on Transportation and the Environment (Committee) identified $75,000 in the capital
27 budget of the District Department of Transportation (DDOT) for the “the assessment, design, and
28 installation of a water meter and potable water source” at Wangari Gardens, a community
29 garden-park located on DDOT property bordered by Kenyon St. NW, Park Pl. NW, and Irving
30 St. NW.

31 (b) Friends of Wangari Gardens is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization that has a
32 memorandum of understanding with DDOT regarding the use of Wangari Gardens. As detailed
33 in the Report, Friends of Wangari Gardens has been using a fire hydrant permit from DC Water
34 as its water source, but the cost of this arrangement has increased dramatically in recent years.
35 As the Report details,
36 The treasurer for the Friends of Wangari Gardens, in communications with the Ward 1
37 and Ward 5 Council offices, shared with the Committee, notes that the 2025 bill for the
38 meter and backflow preventer rental totaled $5,525, which is considered “commercial
39 use.” This means Wangari Gardens pays the same rate as a developer demolishing a
40 building. The largest portion of this bill is the refundable deposit for the meter ($2,750)
41 and the daily rental fee ($15/day totaling $2,700 for the season), while the actual water
42 usage amounts to less than $1,000. This fee structure has led to the depletion of the
43 Friends of Wangari Gardens' bank account in recent years, requiring personal funds to
44 cover the bill in 2025.2
45
46 (c) Funds were allocated to DDOT because that agency is the custodian of the property
47 where Wangari Gardens is located. Funds could not be appropriated to D.C. Water because D.C.
48 Water is an independent self-financing authority
49 (d) The Council adopted the Committee’s recommendation and included $75,000 in
50 capital funds for a water source at Wangari Gardens in the FY 2026 Local Budget Act of 2025
51 and FY 2026 Local Budget Emergency Act of 2025.
52 (e) In conversations between DDOT staff, officers of Friends of Wangari Gardens, and
53 council staff, it has become clear that the best path to expeditious completion of this project is for
54 Friends of Wangari Gardens to manage the project directly and for DDOT to transfer the funds to
55 them. While DDOT is the custodian of the property, it does not have staff capacity or expertise
56 to engage with D.C. Water on the unique needs of Wangari Gardens.

57 (f) Emergency legislation is needed to provide DDOT with authority to make this grant to
58 Friends of Wangari Gardens. While the Department of Transportation Establishment Act of
59 2022, effective May 21, 2002 (D.C. Law 14-137; D.C. Official Code § 50-921.02(c)(1)) provides
60 DDOT with the authority to make grants under $1 million dollars, that authority is constrained
61 by language requiring that those grants “achieve the District’s transportation goals, including
62 safety objectives and to support streateries and the streatery program.” The emergency
63 legislation clarifies that DDOT has the authority to make a grant to establish a water source at
64 Wangari Gardens even though such a grant is not related to achieving the District’s
65 transportation goals.
66 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
67 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making it necessary that the Fidelity in
68 Compliance of Contracting and Procurement Emergency Amendment Act of 2021 be adopted
69 after a single reading.
70 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
