16 To amend, on an emergency basis, An Act Making appropriations to provide for the expenses of
17 the government of the District of Columbia for the fiscal year ending June thirtieth,
18 nineteen hundred and fourteen and for other purposes, to extend the holdover period for
19 commissioners of the Public Service Commission.
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 act may be cited as the “Public Service Commission Holdover Period Extension Emergency
23 Amendment Act of 2026”.
24 Sec. 2. Paragraph 97(a) of section 8 of An Act Making appropriations to provide for the
25 expenses of the government of the District of Columbia for the fiscal year ending June thirtieth,
26 nineteen hundred and fourteen and for other purposes, approved March 4, 1913 (37 Stat. 995; D.C.
27 Official Code § 34-801), is amended by adding a new subsection (c) to read as follows:
28 “(c) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this paragraph, and section 2(c) of the Confirmation
29 Act of 1978, effective March 3, 1979 (D.C. Law 2-142: D.C. Official Code § 1-523.01(c)), a
30 commissioner may serve in a holdover capacity for no longer than 270 days after expiration of the
31 term to which he or she was appointed.”.
32 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
1

33 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
34 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
35 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
36 Sec. 4. Effective date.
37 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
38 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), and shall remain in effect for no longer than
39 90 days, as provided for emergency acts of the Council of the District of Columbia in section
40 412(a) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 788;
41 D.C. Official Code § 1-204.12(a)).
