15 To authorize and provide for the issuance, sale, and delivery in an aggregate principal amount not to

23 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 resolution may be cited as the "Methodist Home of the District of Columbia d/b/a Forest Hills of
25 DC by Goodwin Living Revenue Bonds Project Approval Resolution of 2026".
26 Sec. 2. Definitions.
27 For the purposes of this resolution, the term:
28 (1) "Authorized Delegate" means the Mayor or the Deputy Mayor for Planning and
29 Economic Development, or any officer or employee of the Executive Office of the Mayor to whom
30 the Mayor has delegated or to whom the foregoing individuals have subdelegated any of the
31 Mayor's functions under this resolution pursuant to section 422(6) of the Home Rule Act.
32 (2) "Bond Counsel" means a finn or firms of attorneys designated as bond counsel from
33 time to time by the Mayor.
1

34 (3) “Bonds” means the District of Columbia revenue bonds, notes, or other obligations
35 (including refunding bonds, notes, and other obligations), in one or more series, authorized to be
36 issued pursuant to this resolution.
37 (4) “Borrower” means the owner of the assets financed, refinanced, or reimbursed with
38 proceeds from the Bonds, which shall be The Methodist Home of the District of Columbia d/b/a
39 Forest Hills of DC by Goodwin Living, a not-for-profit corporation organized and existing under
40 the laws of the District of Columbia, qualified to do business in the District of Columbia, and
41 exempt from federal income taxes under 26 U.S.C § 501(a) as an organization described in 26
42 U.S.C. § 501(c)(3).
43 (5) “Closing Documents” means all documents and agreements other than Financing
44 Documents that may be necessary and appropriate to issue, sell, and deliver the Bonds and to make
45 the Loan, and includes agreements, certificates, letters, opinions, forms, receipts, and other similar
46 instruments.
47 (6) “District” means the District of Columbia.
48 (7) “Financing Documents” means the documents other than Closing Documents that relate
49 to the financing, refinancing or reimbursement of transactions to be effected through the issuance,
50 sale, and delivery of the Bonds and the making of the Loan, including any offering document, and
51 any required supplements to any such documents.
52 (8) “Home Rule Act” means the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
53 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 774; D.C. Official Code § 1-201.01 et seq.).
54 (9) “Issuance Costs” means all fees, costs, charges, and expenses paid or incurred in

56 and the making of the Loan, including, but not limited to, underwriting, legal, accounting, rating

57 agency, and all other fees, costs, charges, and expenses incurred in connection with the development
58 and implementation of the Financing Documents, the Closing Documents, and those other
59 documents necessary or appropriate in connection with the authorization, preparation, printing,
60 issuance, sale, marketing, and delivery of the Bonds and the making of the Loan, together with
61 financing fees, costs, and expenses, including program fees and administrative fees charged by the
62 District, fees paid to financial institutions and insurance companies, initial letter of credit fees (if
63 any), compensation to financial advisors and other persons (other than full-time employees of the
64 District) and entities performing services on behalf of or as agents for the District.
65 (10) “Loan” means the District’s lending of proceeds from the sale, in one or more series, of
66 the Bonds to the Borrower.
67 (11) “Project” means the financing, refinancing or reimbursing of the Borrower for all or a
68 portion of the Borrower’s costs incurred in connection with:
69 (A) Refunding all of the outstanding District of Columbia Revenue Bonds (The
70 Methodist Home of the District of Columbia Issue) Series 1999, originally issued in the principal
71 amount of $14.485 million, and reissued in 2015 in the principal amount of $9.94 million, the
72 proceeds of which were used to finance or refinance a portion of the costs of the construction of an
73 addition and renovations to the Borrower’s nursing, and independent and assisted living facilities
74 located at 4901 Connecticut Avenue, NW, Washington, DC;
75 (B) Refunding all of the outstanding District of Columbia Fixed Rate Revenue

77 the principal amount of $16.63 million, and reissued in 2015 in the principal amount of $15.435
78 million, the proceeds of which were used to finance or refinance a portion of the cost of the
79 acquisition, renovation, furnishing and equipping of a facility for residents suffering from

81 DC.
82 (C) Certain capital expenditures of the Borrower, including but not limited to, the
83 renovation of and improvements to the facilities located at 4901 Connecticut Avenue, NW,
84 Washington, D.C., and at 2701 Military Road, NW, Washington, DC;
85 (D) Funding certain working capital costs, to the extent financeable, relating to
86 the Bonds;
87 (E) Funding interest on the Bonds and any credit enhancement costs, liquidity
88 costs or a debt service reserve fund relating to the Bonds; and
89 (F) Paying allowable Issuance Costs.
90 Sec. 3. Findings.
91 The Council finds that:
92 (1) Section 490 of the Home Rule Act provides that the Council may by resolution authorize
93 the issuance of District revenue bonds, notes, or other obligations (including refunding bonds, notes,
94 or other obligations) to borrow money to finance, refinance, or reimburse costs, and to assist in the
95 financing, refinancing, or reimbursing of, the costs of undertakings in certain areas designated in
96 section 490 and may effect the financing, refinancing, or reimbursement by loans made directly or
97 indirectly to any individual or legal entity, by the purchase of any mortgage, note, or other security,
98 or by the purchase, lease, or sale of any property.
99 (2) The Borrower has requested the District to issue, sell, and deliver revenue bonds, in one
100 or more series, in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed $13.7 million, and to make the Loan
101 for the purpose of financing, refinancing, or reimbursing costs of the Project.
4

102 (3) The Project is located in the District and will contribute to the health, education, safety,
103 or welfare of, or the creation or preservation of jobs for, residents of the District, or to economic
104 development of the District.
105 (4) The Project is an undertaking in the area of health facilities within the meaning of
106 section 490 of the Home Rule Act.
107 (5) The authorization, issuance, sale, and delivery of the Bonds and the Loan to the
108 Borrower are desirable, are in the public interest, will promote the purpose and intent of section 490
109 of the Home Rule Act, and will assist the Project.
110 Sec. 4. Bond authorization.
111 (a) The Mayor is authorized pursuant to the Home Rule Act and this resolution to assist in
112 financing, refinancing, or reimbursing the costs of the Project by:
113 (1) The issuance, sale, and delivery of the Bonds, in one or more series, in an
114 aggregate principal amount not to exceed $13.7 million; and
115 (2) The making of the Loan.
116 (b) The Mayor is authorized to make the Loan to the Borrower for the purpose of financing,
117 refinancing, or reimbursing the costs of the Project and establishing any fund with respect to the
118 Bonds as required by the Financing Documents.
119 (c) The Mayor may charge a program fee to the Borrower, including, but not limited to, an
120 amount sufficient to cover costs and expenses incurred by the District in connection with the
121 issuance, sale, and delivery of each series of the Bonds, the District’s participation in the monitoring
122 of the use of the Bond proceeds and compliance with any public benefit agreements with the
123 District, and maintaining official records of each bond transaction and assisting in the redemption,
124 repurchase, and remarketing of the Bonds.
5

125 (d) The Bond authorization set forth in this resolution includes the authorization to issue
126 refunding Bonds to refinance any Bonds previously issued under this resolution to finance the
127 Project; provided that the maximum principal amount of Bonds outstanding at any time does not
128 exceed the maximum principal amount of Bonds authorized hereunder.
129 Sec. 5. Bond details.
130 (a) The Mayor and each Authorized Delegate is authorized to take any action reasonably
131 necessary or appropriate in accordance with this resolution in connection with the preparation,
132 execution, issuance, sale, delivery, security for, and payment of the Bonds of each series, including,
133 but not limited to, determinations of:
134 (1) The final form, content, designation, and terms of the Bonds, including a
135 determination that the Bonds may be issued in certificated or book-entry form;
136 (2) The principal amount of the Bonds to be issued and denominations of the Bonds;
137 (3) The rate or rates of interest or the method for determining the rate or rates of
138 interest on the Bonds;
139 (4) The date or dates of issuance, sale, and delivery of, and the payment of interest
140 on the Bonds, and the maturity date or dates of the Bonds;
141 (5) The terms under which the Bonds may be paid, optionally or mandatorily
142 redeemed, accelerated, tendered, called, or put for redemption, repurchase, or remarketing before
143 their respective stated maturities;
144 (6) Provisions for the registration, transfer, and exchange of the Bonds and the
145 replacement of mutilated, lost, stolen, or destroyed Bonds;
146 (7) The creation of any reserve fund, sinking fund, or other fund with respect to the
147 Bonds;
6

148 (8) The time and place of payment of the Bonds;
149 (9) Procedures for monitoring the use of the proceeds received from the sale of the
150 Bonds to ensure that the proceeds are properly applied to the Project and used to accomplish the
151 purposes of the Home Rule Act and this resolution;
152 (10) Actions necessary to qualify the Bonds under blue sky laws of any jurisdiction
153 where the Bonds are marketed; and
154 (11) The terms and types of credit enhancement, if any, under which the Bonds may
155 be secured.
156 (b) The Bonds shall contain a legend, which shall provide that the Bonds are special
157 obligations of the District, are without recourse to the District, are not a pledge of, and do not
158 involve the faith and credit or the taxing power of the District, do not constitute a debt of the
159 District, and do not constitute lending of the public credit for private undertakings as prohibited in
160 section 602(a)(2) of the Home Rule Act.
161 (c) The Bonds shall be executed in the name of the District and on its behalf by the manual
162 or facsimile signature of the Mayor, and attested by the Secretary of the District of Columbia by the
163 Secretary of the District of Columbia’s manual or facsimile signature. The Mayor’s execution and
164 delivery of the Bonds shall constitute conclusive evidence of the Mayor’s approval, on behalf of the
165 District, of the final form and content of the Bonds.
166 (d) The official seal of the District, or a facsimile of it, shall be impressed, printed, or
167 otherwise reproduced on the Bonds.
168 (e) The Bonds of any series may be issued in accordance with the terms of a trust instrument
169 to be entered into by the District and a trustee to be selected by the Borrower subject to the approval
7

170 of the Mayor, and may be subject to the terms of one or more agreements entered into by the Mayor
171 pursuant to section 490(a)(4) of the Home Rule Act.
172 (f) The Bonds may be issued at any time or from time to time in one or more issues and in
173 one or more series.
174 Sec. 6. Sale of the Bonds.
175 (a) The Bonds of any series may be sold at negotiated or competitive sale at, above, or
176 below par, to one or more persons or entities, and upon terms that the Mayor considers to be in the
177 best interest of the District.
178 (b) The Mayor or an Authorized Delegate may execute, in connection with each sale of the
179 Bonds, offering documents on behalf of the District, may deem final any such offering document on
180 behalf of the District for purposes of compliance with federal laws and regulations governing such
181 matters and may authorize the distribution of the documents in connection with the sale of the
182 Bonds.
183 (c) The Mayor is authorized to deliver the executed and sealed Bonds, on behalf of the
184 District, for authentication, and, after the Bonds have been authenticated, to deliver the Bonds to the
185 original purchasers of the Bonds upon payment of the purchase price.
186 (d) The Bonds of any series shall not be issued until the Mayor receives an approving
187 opinion from Bond Counsel as to the validity of the Bonds of such series and, if the interest on the
188 Bonds of such series is expected to be exempt from federal income taxation, the treatment of the
189 interest on the Bonds of such series for purposes of federal income taxation.
190 Sec. 7. Payment and security.
191 (a) The principal of, premium, if any, and interest on, the Bonds shall be payable solely from
192 proceeds received from the sale of the Bonds, income realized from the temporary investment of
8

193 those proceeds, receipts and revenues realized by the District from the Loan, income realized from
194 the temporary investment of those receipts and revenues prior to payment to the Bond owners, other
195 moneys that, as provided in the Financing Documents, may be made available to the District for the
196 payment of the Bonds, and other sources of payment (other than from the District), all as provided
197 for in the Financing Documents.
198 (b) Payment of the Bonds shall be secured as provided in the Financing Documents and by
199 an assignment by the District for the benefit of the Bond owners of certain of its rights under the
200 Financing Documents and Closing Documents, including a security interest in certain collateral, if
201 any, to the trustee for the Bonds pursuant to the Financing Documents.
202 (c) The trustee is authorized to deposit, invest, and disburse the proceeds received from the
203 sale of the Bonds pursuant to the Financing Documents.
204 Sec. 8. Financing and Closing Documents.
205 (a) The Mayor is authorized to prescribe the final form and content of all Financing
206 Documents and all Closing Documents to which the District is a party that may be necessary or
207 appropriate to issue, sell, and deliver the Bonds and to make the Loan to the Borrower. Each of
208 the Financing Documents and each of the Closing Documents to which the District is not a party
209 shall be approved, as to form and content, by the Mayor.
210 (b) The Mayor is authorized to execute, in the name of the District and on its behalf, the
211 Financing Documents and any Closing Documents to which the District is a party by the Mayor’s
212 manual or facsimile signature.
213 (c) If required, the official seal of the District, or a facsimile of it, shall be impressed,
214 printed, or otherwise reproduced on the Financing Documents and the Closing Documents to which
215 the District is a party.

217 Documents to which the District is a party shall constitute conclusive evidence of the Mayor’s
218 approval, on behalf of the District, of the final form and content of the executed Financing
219 Documents and the executed Closing Documents.
220 (e) The Mayor is authorized to deliver the executed and sealed Financing Documents and
221 Closing Documents, on behalf of the District, prior to or simultaneously with the issuance, sale, and
222 delivery of the Bonds, and to ensure the due performance of the obligations of the District contained
223 in the executed, sealed, and delivered Financing Documents and Closing Documents.
224 Sec. 9. Authorized delegation of authority.
225 To the extent permitted by District and federal laws, the Mayor may delegate to any
226 Authorized Delegate the performance of any function authorized to be performed by the Mayor
227 under this resolution.
228 Sec. 10. Limited liability.
229 (a) The Bonds shall be special obligations of the District. The Bonds shall be without
230 recourse to the District. The Bonds shall not be general obligations of the District, shall not be a
231 pledge of, or involve the faith and credit or the taxing power of, the District, shall not constitute a
232 debt of the District, and shall not constitute lending of the public credit for private undertakings as
233 prohibited in section 602(a)(2) of the Home Rule Act.
234 (b) The Bonds shall not give rise to any pecuniary liability of the District and the District
235 shall have no obligation with respect to the purchase of the Bonds.
236 (c) Nothing contained in the Bonds, in the Financing Documents, or in the Closing
237 Documents shall create an obligation on the part of the District to make payments with respect to
238 the Bonds from sources other than those listed for that purpose in section 7.
10

239 (d) The District shall have no liability for the payment of any Issuance Costs or for any
240 transaction or event to be effected by the Financing Documents.
241 (e) All covenants, obligations, and agreements of the District contained in this resolution,
242 the Bonds, and the executed, sealed, and delivered Financing Documents and Closing Documents to
243 which the District is a party, shall be considered to be the covenants, obligations, and agreements of
244 the District to the fullest extent authorized by law, and each of those covenants, obligations, and
245 agreements shall be binding upon the District, subject to the limitations set forth in this resolution.
246 (f) No person, including, but not limited to, the Borrower and any Bond owner, shall have
247 any claims against the District or any of its elected or appointed officials, officers, employees, or
248 agents for monetary damages suffered as a result of the failure of the District or any of its elected or
249 appointed officials, officers, employees or agents to perform any covenant, undertaking, or
250 obligation under this resolution, the Bonds, the Financing Documents, or the Closing Documents, as
251 a result of the incorrectness of any representation in or omission from the Financing Documents or
252 the Closing Documents, unless the District or its elected or appointed officials, officers, employees,
253 or agents have acted in a willful and fraudulent manner.
254 Sec. 11. District officials.
255 (a) Except as otherwise provided in section 10(f), the elected or appointed officials, officers,
256 employees, or agents of the District shall not be liable personally for the payment of the Bonds or be
257 subject to any personal liability by reason of the issuance, sale or delivery of the Bonds, or for any
258 representations, warranties, covenants, obligations, or agreements of the District contained in this
259 resolution, the Bonds, the Financing Documents, or the Closing Documents.
260 (b) The signature, countersignature, facsimile signature, or facsimile countersignature of
261 any official appearing on the Bonds, the Financing Documents, or the Closing Documents shall
11

262 be valid and sufficient for all purposes notwithstanding the fact that the individual signatory
263 ceases to hold that office before delivery of the Bonds, the Financing Documents, or the Closing
264 Documents.
265 Sec.12. Maintenance of documents.
266 Copies of the specimen Bonds and of the final Financing Documents and Closing
267 Documents shall be filed in the Office of the Secretary of the District of Columbia.
268 Sec.13. Information reporting.
269 Within 3 days after the Mayor’s receipt of the transcript of proceedings relating to the
270 issuance of the Bonds, the Mayor shall transmit a copy of the transcript to the Secretary to the
271 Council.
272 Sec. 14. Disclaimer.
273 (a) The issuance of Bonds is in the discretion of the District. Nothing contained in this
274 resolution, the Bonds, the Financing Documents, or the Closing Documents shall be construed as
275 obligating the District to issue any Bonds for the benefit of the Borrower or to participate in or assist
276 the Borrower in any way with financing, refinancing, or reimbursing the costs of the Project. The
277 Borrower shall have no claims for damages or for any other legal or equitable relief against the
278 District, its elected or appointed officials, officers, employees, or agents as a consequence of any
279 failure to issue any Bonds for the benefit of the Borrower.
280 (b) The District reserves the right to issue the Bonds in the order or priority it determines in
281 its sole and absolute discretion. The District gives no assurance and makes no representations that
282 any portion of any limited amount of bonds or other obligations, the interest on which is excludable
283 from gross income for federal income tax purposes, will be reserved or will be available at the time
284 of the proposed issuance of the Bonds.
12

285 (c) The District, by adopting this resolution or by taking any other action in connection with
286 financing, refinancing, or reimbursing costs of the Project, does not provide any assurance that the
287 Project is viable or sound, that the Borrower is financially sound, or that amounts owing on the
288 Bonds or pursuant to the Loan will be paid. Neither the Borrower, any purchaser of the Bonds, nor
289 any other person shall rely upon the District with respect to these matters.
290 Sec. 15. Expiration.
291 If any Bonds are not issued, sold, and delivered to the original purchaser within 3 years of
292 the date of this resolution, the authorization provided in this resolution with respect to the issuance,
293 sale, and delivery of the Bonds shall expire.
294 Sec. 16. Severability.
295 If any particular provision of this resolution, or the application thereof to any person or
296 circumstance is held invalid, the remainder of this resolution and the application of such provision
297 to other persons or circumstances shall not be affected thereby. If any action or inaction
298 contemplated under this resolution is determined to be contrary to the requirements of applicable
299 law, such action or inaction shall not be necessary for the purpose of issuing of the Bonds, and the
300 validity of the Bonds shall not be adversely affected.
301 Sec. 17. Compliance with public approval requirement.
302 This approval shall constitute the approval of the Council as required in section 147(f) of
303 the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the “Code”), and section 490(k) of the Home
304 Rule Act, for the Project to be financed, refinanced, or reimbursed with the proceeds of the
305 Bonds. This resolution approving the issuance of the Bonds for the Project has been adopted by
306 the Council after a public hearing held in accordance with section 147(f) of the Code, as such

307 section may be amended, and the corresponding regulations promulgated by the United States
