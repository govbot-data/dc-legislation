16 To remember, honor, and celebrate the life of Olivia Cadaval.
17 WHEREAS, Olivia Cadaval was born and raised in Mexico City, immigrating to the
18 United Stated in 1961 to attend MacMurray College and later settling in Washington, D.C. in
19 1968, and;
20 WHEREAS, Ms. Cadaval documented the history of D.C.’s Latino community and
21 advocated for spaces for Latino and other marginalized communities, and;
22 WHEREAS, Ms. Cadaval served as the Director for El Centro de Arte, an arts
23 organization and community space for Latin-American artistic expression that operated for
24 twenty years, and;
25 WHEREAS, Ms. Cadaval earned a Ph.D. in American Studies and Folklife from the
26 George Washington University, and;
27 WHEREAS, Ms. Cadaval served as a cultural liaison for the Smithsonian Folklife
28 Festival beginning in 1976, she went on to serve as the Curator and Chair of Cultural Research
29 and Education for the Center for Folklife and Cultural Heritage, where she curated numerous
30 Smithsonian Folklife Festival programs, and;
1

31 WHEREAS, Ms. Cadaval mentored many young students, curators, artists and folklorists
32 throughout her career and retirement, especially those from underrepresented communities and;
33 WHEREAS, Ms. Cadaval continued her work in the community in her retirement,
34 including curating La Esquina, a photo exhibition documenting the Latino community of Mount
35 Pleasant, and;
36 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
37 resolution may be cited as the “Olivia Cadaval Memorial Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
38 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes, honors, and remembers and
39 the life of Ms. Olivia Cadaval.
40 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
41 the District of Columbia Register.
