15 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Act to Regulate Public Conduct on Public Passenger
16 Vehicles to provide that the adjudication of fare evasion citations shall be pursuant to the
17 Department of Consumer, and Regulatory Affairs Civil Infractions Act of 1985; to amend
18 the Omnibus Police Reform Amendment Act of 2000 to allow applicants for sworn
19 officers of the Metropolitan Police Department to count certain credit hours for
20 completion of the initial training program towards the requirement that certain applicants
21 complete 60-hours of post-secondary education at an accredited college or university; to
22 amend Title 23 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify the authority of
23 Metropolitan Police Department warrantless arrest requirements, and to create an
24 exception to the rebuttable presumption for pretrial detention for robbery where the
25 victim does not sustain a physical injury and burglary in the second degree; to amend
26 Chapter 8 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify that offenses
27 eligible in the future for automatic sealing are currently eligible for sealing by motion;
28 and to amend the Secure DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2024 to repeal the sunset date
29 of certain provisions regarding pretrial detention.
30
31 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
32 act may be cited as the “Peace DC Omnibus Temporary Amendment Act of 2025”.
