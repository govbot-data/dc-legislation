Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is the
Subcontracting Requirements Exemption for Contracts for Health Benefits for District Employees
Emergency Amendment Act of 2025, along with the accompanying emergency declaration
resolution, the Subcontracting Requirements Exemption for Contracts for Health Benefits for
District Employees Temporary Amendment Act of 2025, and the Subcontracting Requirements
Exemption for Contracts for Health Benefits for District Employees Amendment Act of 2025.
The legislation adds a new subsection (d)(4) to D.C. Official Code§ 2-218.46 to extend the time
in which compliant subcontracting plans for contracts for health benefits to District employees and
their families shall be provided.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action at your earliest convenience.
Sincerely,

16 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Small and Certified Business Enterprise Development and
17 Assistance Act of 2005 to extend the time in which compliant subcontracting plans for
18 contracts for health benefits for District employees and their families shall be provided.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 act may be cited as the "Subcontracting Requirements Exemption for Contracts for Health
22 Benefits for District Employees Emergency Amendment Act of 2025".
23 Sec. 2. Section 2346( d) of the Small and Certified Business Enterprise Development and
24 Assistance Act of 2005, effective October 20, 2005 (D.C. Law 16-33; D.C. Official Code§ 2-
25 218.46(d)), is amended by adding a new paragraph (4) to read as follows:
26 "(4) Notwithstanding the foregoing paragraphs (1) and (3) of this subsection, the
27 subcontracting plan for contracts to provide health benefits to District government employees
28 and their families shall be provided prior to commencement of performance of the plan year, and
29 a bid or proposal that does not include a subcontracting plan may be deemed responsive if it is
30 submitted in response to a solicitation to provide health benefits to District government
31 employees and their families."
