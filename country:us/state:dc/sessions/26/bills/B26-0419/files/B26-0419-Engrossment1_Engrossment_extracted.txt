9 To amend the Criminal Background Checks for the Protection of Children Act of 2004 to allow
10 authorized covered youth services providers to perform background checks on behalf of
11 their applicants, employees, and volunteers, and to clarify that an applicant, employee, or
12 volunteer may consent to having their criminal background check records released so
13 another jurisdiction can perform a criminal history or traffic records check.
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
16 act may be cited as the “Background Check Adjustments Amendment Act of 2026”.
17 Sec. 2. The Criminal Background Checks for the Protection of Children Act of 2004,
18 effective April 13, 2005 (D.C Law 15-353; D.C. Official Code § 4-1501.01 et seq.), is amended
19 as follows:
20 (a) Section 205(c) (D.C. Official Code § 4-1501.05(c)) is amended as follows:
21 (1) Paragraph (2) is amended by striking the phrase “for the Mayor to conduct”
22 and inserting the phrase “to conduct” in its place.
23 (2) Paragraph (3) is amended by striking the phrase “Mayor of the” and inserting
24 the phrase “Mayor or the” in its place.
25 (b) A new section 206b is added to read as follows:
26 “Sec. 206b. Private entities authorized to conduct background checks.
1

ENGROSSED ORIGINAL
27 “(a) The Mayor may authorize a non-governmental entity that is a covered youth services
28 provider to perform the background check functions and procedures described in sections 203(a),
29 204, and 205 for the covered youth services provider’s applicants, employees, and volunteers.
30 “(b) A covered youth services provider authorized to perform criminal background
31 checks pursuant to this section shall submit all records obtained pursuant to such authorization to
32 the Mayor for uses consistent with this act.”.
33 (c) Section 208 (D.C. Official Code § 4-1501.08) is amended as follows:
34 (1) The lead-in language is amended by striking the phrase “received by the
35 Mayor shall” and inserting the phrase “received, pursuant to this act, by the Mayor or a private
36 entity that is a covered child or youth services provider shall” in its place.
37 (2) Paragraph (4) is amended by striking the semicolon and inserting the phrase “,
38 including to satisfy a criminal history or traffic records check required by another jurisdiction;”
39 in its place.
40 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
41 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
42 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
43 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code§ 1-301.47a).
44 Sec. 4. Effective date.
45 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
46 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto) and a 30-day period of congressional review
