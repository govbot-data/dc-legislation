12 To amend the Residential Tranquility Act of 2010 to prohibit persons targeting a residence for
13 purposes of demonstration from using sound amplifying devices in a residential zone
14 between 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m., to prohibit a person from launching or throwing a
15 projectile onto the residential property of another with the intent to cause fear, intimidate,
16 or harass any person living or working at that residence, and to prohibit the use of
17 unattended sound amplifying devices emitting sound while targeting a residence; and to
18 amend the National Capital Revitalization and Self-Government Improvement Act of
19 1997 to provide that the term expiration date for a Council appointment to the
20 Corrections Information Council shall be December 7th of the year in which the term
21 expires.
22
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 act may be cited as the “Residential Tranquility Amendment Act of 2025”.
25 Sec. 2. The Residential Tranquility Act of 2010, effective May 26, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-
26 374; D.C. Official Code § 22-2751 et seq.), is amended as follows:
27 (a) Section 2 (D.C. Official Code § 22-2751) is amended as follows:
28 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended by striking the phrase “for the purposes of
29 persuading one or more individuals, or the public, or to protest some action, attitude, or belief.”
30 and inserting a period in its place.

31 (2) Paragraph (3) is amended by striking the phrase “human beings” and inserting
32 the word “individuals” in its place.
33 (3) A new paragraph (4) is added to read as follows:
34 “(4) "Sound amplifying device" means any machine, device, or loudspeaker that
35 amplifies sound of the human voice, music, or any other sound. The term "sound amplifying
36 device" shall not include automobile audio systems when used and heard only by the occupants of
37 the vehicle in which the automobile audio system is installed.”.
38 (b) Section 3 (D.C. Official Code § 22-2752) is amended by adding new subsections (a-
39 1), (a-2), (a-3), and (a-4) to read as follows:
40 “(a-1) It is unlawful for a person to target a residence for purposes of a demonstration by
41 using a sound amplifying device between 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m. where the targeted residence
42 is, in fact, located in Residential Zones, Residential Flat Zones, or Residential Apartment Zones,
43 as those terms and Zones are defined at the time of the of the conduct giving rise to the violation
44 in the District of Columbia Zoning Regulations.
45 “(a-2) It is unlawful to leave an unattended sound amplifying device emitting sound to
46 target a residence for purposes of a demonstration where the targeted residence is, in fact, located
47 in Residential Zones, Residential Flat Zones, or Residential Apartment Zones, as those terms and
48 Zones are defined at the time of the of the conduct giving rise to the violation in the District of
49 Columbia Zoning Regulations.
50 “(a-3) It is unlawful to continue or resume targeting a residence in violation of subsection

51 (a-1) or (a-2) of this section after being instructed by a law enforcement officer to cease targeting
52 a residence in violation of this law.
53 “(a-4) The Mayor and Metropolitan Police Department may seize and impound any
54 unattended sound amplifying devices targeting a residence for purposes of a demonstration in
55 violation of subsection (a-2) of this section.”.
56 (c) A new section 3a is added to read as follows:
57 “Sec. 3a. Throwing objects at residential property.
58 “(a) It is unlawful for any person to knowingly launch or throw an object onto the
59 residential property of another with the intent to cause fear, intimidate, or harass any person
60 living or working at that residence.
61 “(b) A person who violates this section, upon conviction, shall be fined no more than the
62 amount set forth in section 101 of the Criminal Fine Proportionality Amendment Act of 2012,
63 effective June 11, 2013 (D.C. Law 19-317; D.C. Official Code § 22-3571.01), or incarcerated for
64 no more than 90 days, or both.”.
65 Sec. 3. Section 11201a(b)(2) of the National Capital Revitalization and Self-Government
66 Improvement Act of 1997, effective October 2, 2010 (D.C. Law 18-233; D.C. Official Code §
67 24-101.01(b)(2)), is amended by adding a new subparagraph (B-1) to read as follows:
68 “(B-1) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the term expiration
69 date for a Council appointment to the Corrections Information Council shall be December 7th of
70 the year in which the term expires.”.
