Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is the
“Unlicensed Establishment Enforcement Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2025,”
along with accompanying an accompanying emergency declaration resolution, and the
“Unlicensed Establishment Enforcement Clarification Temporary Amendment Act of 2025.”
To date, the Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis Administration (ABCA), along with the
Metropolitan Police Department, have been able to take action to close down thirty-one
unlicensed cannabis establishments operating in the District illegally. Despite this progress,
additional enforcement authority will better equip ABCA to move forward with the closure of the
remaining operating unlicensed establishments. These emergency measures would therefore
expand ABCA’s authority to take summary action and padlock businesses engaged in illegal
commerce in cannabis and Schedule I substances by expanding the agency’s padlocking authority
to medical cannabis businesses and non-licensed businesses engaged in the illegal sale of
cannabis or Schedule I substances and ending the grace period that prevented enforcement against
certain unlicensed businesses that applied for a license with ABCA but have not obtained a
license by April 1, 2025.
For these reasons discussed above, I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the
enclosed proposed bill.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser
Mayor

19 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Legalization of Marijuana for Medical Treatment
20 Initiative of 1999 to amend the definition of an unlicensed establishment to include
21 establishments that sell Schedule I substances or products that contain Schedule I
22 substances; to authorize enforcement actions to be taken against all unlicensed
23 establishments beginning on April 1, 2025, regardless of whether the unlicensed
24 establishments have an application pending application before the Alcoholic Beverage
25 and Control Board ("ABC Board"); to authorize certain enforcement actions before April
26 1, 2025, against an unlicensed establishment that has an application pending before the
27 ABC Board if the unlicensed establishment sells a Schedule I substance or a product that
28 contains a Schedule I substance; to authorize summary closures and related enforcement
29 actions to continue for as long as the imminent danger to the public persists and is likely
30 to recur; and to authorize the ABC Board to issue a notice of summary closure to and
31 order the padlocking of, and the Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis Administration and
32 Metropolitan Police Department to take related enforcement actions against, a licensed
33 medical cannabis establishment that engages in certain unlawful activity.
34
35 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
36 act may be cited as the "Unlicensed Establishment Enforcement Clarification Emergency
37 Amendment Act of 2025".
38 Sec. 2. The Legalization of Marijuana for Medical Treatment Initiative of 1999, effective
39 July 27, 2010 (D.C. Law 18-210; D.C. Official Code§ 7-1671.01 et seq.), is amended as follows:
40 (a) Section 2 (D.C. Official Code§ 7-1671.01) is amended as follows:

41 (1) A new paragraph (2B) is added to read as follows:
42 “(2B) “Cannabis product” means a product derived from or composed of
43 cannabis, in part or in whole.”.
44 (2) A new paragraph (20B-i) is added to read as follows:
45 “(20B-i) “Schedule I substance” means a controlled substance listed in section
46 204 of the District of Columbia Uniform Controlled Substances Act of 1981, effective August 5,
47 1981 (D.C. Law 4-29; D.C. Official Code § 48-902.04), or rules implementing that section.”.
48 (3) Paragraph (22) is amended to read as follows:
49 “(22) “Unlicensed establishment" means:
50 “(A) A sole proprietorship, partnership, or other business entity that:
51 “(i) Sells, exchanges as part of a commercial transaction, or
52 delivers cannabis and cannabis products;
53 “(ii) Operates at or delivers from a specific location in the District;
54 and
55 “(iii) Is not licensed by ABCA as a cultivation center, retailer,
56 internet retailer, manufacturer, courier, or testing laboratory; or
57 “(B) For the purposes of section 9(g), (h), and (i), a sole proprietorship,
58 partnership, or other business entity that:
59 “(i) Sells, exchanges as part of a commercial transaction, or
60 delivers Schedule I substances or products that contain Schedule I substances; and
61 “(ii) Operates at or delivers from a specific location in the
62 District.”.

63 (b) Section 7b (D.C. Official Code § 7-1671.06b) is amended by adding a new subsection
64 (a-1) to read as follows:
65 “(a-1) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this section:
66 “(1) Regardless of whether an unlicensed establishment has a retailer or internet
67 retailer application pending review with the ABC Board, the ABCA may inspect the entire
68 premises, inventory, and business records of the unlicensed establishment pursuant to section
69 9(g), ABCA investigators from testing products of the unlicensed establishment under section
70 9(i), and the ABC Board from summarily closing and ordering the padlocking of the unlicensed
71 establishment pursuant to section 9(g), ABCA or MPD from padlocking the unlicensed
72 establishment pursuant to section 9(g), ABCA or MPD from seizing cannabis, cannabis products,
73 Schedule I substances, and products that contain Schedule I substances found at the premises of
74 the unlicensed establishment pursuant to section 9(g), or the ABC Board, ABCA, or MPD from
75 taking action under section 9(h) if the unlicensed establishment distributes, attempts to distribute,
76 or makes available for sale or exchange a Schedule I substance or a product that contains a
77 Schedule I substance.
78 “(2) After April 1, 2025, an unlicensed establishment shall be subject to
79 compliance with this act regardless of whether the unlicensed establishment has a retailer or
80 internet retailer application pending review with the ABC Board.”.
81 (c) Section 9 (D.C. Official Code § 7-1671.08) is amended as follows:
82 (1) Subsection (g) is amended as follows:
83 (A) Paragraph (1) is amended as follows:

84 (i) Strike the phrase “this title” and insert the phrase “this act or is
85 selling, exchanging as part of a commercial transaction, or delivering a Schedule I substance or
86 products that contains a Schedule I substance” in its place.
87 (ii) Strike the phrase “cannabis products” and insert the phrase
88 “cannabis products, Schedule I substances, or products that contain Schedule I substances” in its
89 place.
90 (B) A new paragraph (6) is added to read as follows:
91 “(6) The Board may continue the summary closure and order of padlocking, and
92 the padlocking of the premises and seizure of cannabis, cannabis products, Schedule I
93 substances, and products containing Schedule I substances may continue, until the imminent
94 danger that triggered the summary closure is abated. Even if the imminent danger that triggered
95 the summary closure is abated, the Board may continue the summary closure, and the padlocking
96 of the premises and seizure of cannabis, cannabis products, Schedule I substances, and products
97 containing Schedule I substances may continue, if the Board determines, in its reasonable
98 judgment, the imminent danger is likely to recur.”.
99 (2) Subsection (i)(1) is amended to read as follows:
100 “(i)(1) An ABCA investigator may test substances and products found at a licensed
101 establishment or unlicensed establishment for the presence and quantity of THC or a Schedule I
102 substance.”.
103 (3) A new subsection (j) is added to read as follows:
104 “(j)(1) The ABC Board may summarily close and order the padlocking, by ABCA or
105 MPD without a prior hearing, of a licensed establishment, and ABCA or MPD may seize all
106 cannabis, cannabis products, Schedule I substances, and products containing Schedule I

107 substances found at the premises, if, after an inspection, ABCA determines that the licensed
108 establishment that presents an imminent danger to the health and safety of the public for the
109 reasons set forth in subsection (g)(C) through (G) of this section.
110 “(2) ABCA shall provide the licensed establishment’s owner and the property
111 owner with written notice of the summary closure and the right to request a hearing.
112 “(3) The owner of the licensed establishment shall have 5 business days after
113 service of the notice of summary closure to request a hearing with the ABC Board, which shall
114 hold a hearing within 5 business days of a timely request. The ABC Board shall issue a written
115 decision within 5 business days after the hearing, or if no hearing is requested, within 10
116 business days after the service of the notice.
117 “(4)(A) ABCA or the Metropolitan Police Department may post signage at the
118 site of the licensed establishment indicating that unlawful activity has been found to have
119 occurred for violations of this chapter.
120 “(B) If the ABC Board orders the closure of a licensed establishment, the
121 ABC Board shall post 2 notices in conspicuous places at or near the main street entrance on the
122 outside of the establishment.
123 “(C) The posted notice shall state that the closure is ordered because of a
124 violation of this chapter or of the regulations promulgated pursuant to this chapter.
125 “(D) Any person willfully removing, obliterating, or defacing the notice
126 shall be guilty of a violation of this chapter.”.
