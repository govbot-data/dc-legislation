16 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Office of Citizen
17 Complaint Review Establishment Act of 1998 to expand the membership of the Police
18 Complaints Board, and to allow the Office of Police Complaints’ Executive Director to
19 initiate their own complaint if they discover evidence of abuse or misuse of police powers
20 that was not alleged in the original complaint, including the failure to intervene or report
21 to a supervisor when another officer used excessive force, engaged in other forms of
22 misconduct, or violated a rule or regulation; and to amend Chapter 3 of Title 14 of the
23 District of Columbia Official Code to clarify that certain provisions only apply to a victim
24 who is under the age of 18.
25
26 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
27 resolution may be cited as the “Comprehensive Policing and Justice Reform Technical Emergency
28 Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
29 Sec. 2. (a) On December 20, 2022, the Council passed the Comprehensive Policing and
30 Justice Reform Amendment Act of 2022, effective April 21, 2023 (D.C. Law 24-345; 70 DCR
31 953) (“permanent law”). While most of the permanent law went into effect on April 21, 2023,
32 section 105 of the permanent law remains unfunded and has not yet taken effect. Instead,
33 substantially similar provisions have been maintained on an emergency and temporary basis.
1

34 (b) There also existed a need to clarify the scope of reporting requirements amended by the
35 Expanding Supports for Crime Victims Amendment Act of 2022, effective April 6, 2023 (D.C.
36 Law 24-341; 69 DCR 14698). While section 4 of the Leading Education Access for Reentry and
37 Necessary Success (“LEARNS”) Amendment Act of 2026, passed on 2nd reading on June 2, 2026
38 (Enrolled version of Bill 26-526), clarifies the scope of reporting requirements moving forward, it
39 has not been signed by the Mayor or completed congressional review.
40 (c) In response to these issues, the Council passed the Comprehensive Policing and Justice
41 Reform Technical Emergency Amendment Act of 2025, effective October 30, 2025 (D.C. Law
42 26-177; 72 DCR 12393) (“emergency legislation”). The emergency legislation expired on January
43 28, 2026. The Council also passed the Comprehensive Policing and Justice Reform Technical
44 Temporary Amendment Act of 2025, effective December 31, 2025 (D.C. Law 26-73; 73 DCR
45 606) (“temporary legislation”). The temporary legislation is set to expire on August 13, 2026, prior
46 to the Council’s return from recess.
47 (d) This new round of emergency and temporary legislation is now necessary to prevent a
48 gap in the law between the expiration of the temporary legislation and the applicability date of
49 section 105 of the permanent legislation. This round of emergency and temporary legislation is
50 also necessary to clarify the scope of reporting requirements amended by the Expanding Supports
51 for Crime Victims Amendment Act of 2022 until the Leading Education Access for Reentry and
52 Necessary Success (“LEARNS”) Amendment Act of 2026 takes effect.
53 Sec. 3. The Council determines that the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute
54 emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Comprehensive Policing and Justice
55 Reform Technical Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted after a single reading.
2

56 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
