Enclosed for consideration and approval by the Council of the District of Columbia
(“Council”) is “Modification Nos. 1, 2, and 3 to Contract NFPHC-OPS-23-C-00059
between the Not-for-Profit Hospital Corporation and Morrison Management Specialists,
Inc. Approval and Payment Authorization Emergency Act of 2025”, and “Modification
Nos. 1, 2, and 3 to Contract NFPHC-OPS-23-C-00059 between the Not-for-Profit
Hospital Corporation and Morrison Management Specialists, Inc. Approval and Payment
Authorization Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”, for the provision of Food and
Nutrition Management services (“Contract”).
Council action is necessary to approve proposed Modifications Nos. 1, 2, and 3 to Option
Year 1, because when combined, the revised total retroactive value would be
$1,144,733.00. Modification 1 partially exercised Option Year 1 of this contract
($997,649.00; August 1, 2024 – March 31, 2025). When it was announced that Cedar
Hill’s opening would be delayed by fifteen days, the Not-for-Profit Hospital Corporation
prepared Modification 2 for $53,618.00, to extend the contract from April 1, 2025 to
April 15, 2025, which if approved would have increased the Option Year 1 value to
$1,051,267.00. Unfortunately, United Medical Center was unable to seek Council
approval prior to recess because the vendor did not obtain its updated Clean Hands
Certification until July 8, 2025.
As United Medical Center continued its closure and dissolution preparation activities
during recess, Contract NFPHC-OPS-23-C-00059 was found to be trending over budget
due to the impact of inflation on the cost of food and higher than estimated utilization.
These higher costs, which totaled $93,466.00, are captured in Proposed Modification 3
and if approved along with Proposed ModificUation 2, would brinMg the total Option YearC 1
contract amount to $1,144,733.00.
United Medical Center is submitting this legislation to Council for approval at its first
legislative meeting after its recess. Emergency approval is required to approve these
modifications at Council’s September 17, 2025 meeting, because the Not-for-Profit
Hospital Corporation will statutorily dissolve at the end of the day on September 30,
