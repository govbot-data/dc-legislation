Statement of Introduction
DC Student Loan Authority Establishment Act of 2026
January 23, 2026
Today, alongside Councilmembers Pinto, Nadeau, and R. White, I am introducing the DC
Student Loan Authority Establishment Act of 2026, a bill to create a public lending institution in
Washington, D.C. to issue student loans and help D.C. residents refinance existing education
debt. This statement is written for the public and designed to be clear for the average reader.
The District has expanded borrower protections, but many residents still depend on private
student loans that can include high interest, surprise fees, and limited flexibility when financial
circumstances change. Those costs make it harder to complete school, switch careers, or work in
public service roles without fear of falling behind. This bill creates a public alternative built
around fairness and affordability. This bill builds on work first advanced by former
Councilmember Elissa Silverman, who championed the creation of a public, mission-driven
alternative to private student lending in the District.
The bill establishes the District of Columbia Student Loan Authority as an independent part of
the District government, overseen by a 7-member board, including 5 voting members appointed
by the Mayor and approved by the Council, plus 2 ex-officio members. The Authority can
provide new loans for accredited college or vocational training programs and refinance education
debt for current D.C. residents.
The bill improves loan terms in a way most residents have never had access to. The Authority
may not charge application or origination fees. The first $5,000 of any loan will carry 0%
interest, adjusted annually for inflation. Monthly payments are capped at 10% of a borrower’s
discretionary income, keeping payments tied to what someone can afford. Repayment begins 6
months after completion or withdrawal and may extend up to 30 years. Borrowers who complete
10 cumulative years of full-time public service work, including education sector roles, may be
eligible for discharge of remaining loan balances, and qualifying hours may include paid leave
and Family and Medical Leave Act time. This bill is about expanding opportunity, giving
residents financial breathing room to learn, work, build businesses, and explore new industries
without being weighed down by fast-growing interest or unexpected fees.
The Authority must publish regular financial and activity reports, and a certified public
accountant will audit its finances each year, sending those audits to the Mayor and Council. Loan
servicing must remain in-house and may not be outsourced, ensuring accountability stays public.
I look forward to working with my colleagues, advocates, and borrowers to move this proposal
through the legislative process.

19 To establish a finance authority in the District of Columbia to provide new student loans and
20 refinance existing student loans.
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 act may be cited as the “DC Student Loan Authority Establishment Act of 2026”.
23 Sec. 2. Definitions.
24 For the purposes of this act the term:
25 (1) “Authority” means the District of Columbia Student Loan Authority established by
26 section 3.
27 (2) “Board” means the executive board of the Authority.
28 (3) “Discretionary income” means the difference between a participant’s annual adjusted
29 gross income and 150% of the poverty guideline based on family size.
30 (4) “Loan” means money borrowed and evidenced by a loan agreement between the
31 Authority and a borrower, including any terms related to repayment, deferment, or interest.
1

32 (5) “Loan agreement” means a contract between the participant and the Authority which
33 specifies the promises made by each party. The Loan Agreement shall reflect the total amount
34 borrowed by the Participant as well as the terms of Repayment.
35 (6) “Interest rate” means the money paid regularly for the use of money lent or for
36 delaying repayment of the debt. The Interest Rate shall be specified in the Loan Agreement.
37 (7) “Qualifying program” means any regionally accredited post-secondary educational or
38 vocational training program.
39 (8) “Participant” means any person who executes a Loan Agreement with the Authority
40 pursuant to the requirement of the financial assistance.
41 Sec. 3. Authority establishment and purpose.
42 (a)(1) There is established, as an independent authority of the District government, the
43 District of Columbia Student Loan Authority. The Authority shall be a corporate body, created to
44 effectuate the purposes of this act, and with a separate legal existence within the District
45 government.
46 (b) The purpose of the Authority shall be to:
47 (1) Provide new student loans and refinance existing student loans to individuals
48 residing in the District at the time of application and to students of post-secondary educational
49 programs located in the District of Columbia; and
50 (2) Engage in any other activities that may help students attending higher
51 education institutions.
52 Sec. 4. Executive board establishment and membership.
53 (a) There is established an executive board to govern the Authority, which shall consist of
54 5 voting members appointed by the Mayor, with the advice and consent of the Council, pursuant

57 District.

60 Metropolitan Area or a similar organization;

153 Sec. 8. Loan programs.
154 (a)(1) The Authority shall provide financial assistance to eligible applicants seeking to
155 enroll in a qualifying program in furtherance of an educational or vocational goal.
156 (2) The financial assistance shall be made in the form of deferred payment loans
157 secured by a loan agreement that is executed by the applicant and the Authority. The amount of
158 the financial assistance shall be limited to the estimated cost of attendance.
159 (3) To be eligible, the applicant shall be:
160 (A) A resident of the District enrolled in a post-secondary educational or
161 vocational program;
162 (B) A student:
163 (i) Who is an individual who claims a student as a dependent, a
164 resident of the District, and
165 (ii) Who is enrolled in a qualifying post-secondary educational or
166 vocational training program, or
7

167 (C) A student who is not a resident of the District but who is enrolled in a
168 qualifying post-secondary educational or vocational program that is based in the District of
169 Columbia.
170 (b)(1) The Authority shall provide financial assistance to eligible applicants seeking to
171 refinance existing debt incurred for the purpose of seeking a post-secondary educational,
172 vocational, advanced, or professional education.
173 (2) The financial assistance shall be made in the form of deferred payment loans
174 secured by a Loan Agreement that is executed by the applicant and the Authority.
175 (3) An applicant shall be eligible for the financial assistance under this subsection
176 if the applicant is:
177 (A) A resident of the District of Columbia; and
178 (B) Provides evidence that the existing debt is the result of previous
179 enrollment in a qualifying program.
180 (c)(1) The Authority shall provide financial assistance to eligible applicants who are the
181 parent or other caretaker of a student who seeks to enroll or is enrolled in a qualifying
182 postsecondary educational or vocational program and who is claimed as a dependent by the
183 applicant for federal income tax purposes.
184 (2) The financial assistance shall be made in the form of deferred payment loans
185 secured by a loan agreement executed by the applicant and the Authority.
186 (3) An applicant shall be eligible for financial assistance under this subsection if
187 the applicant:
188 (A) Is a current resident of the District; and
8

189 (B) Provides documentation demonstrating that the student is claimed as
190 the applicant’s dependent for federal income tax purposes.
191 (d)(1) The Authority shall provide financial assistance to eligible participants seeking to
192 enroll in a postgraduate degree program.
193 (2) The financial assistance shall be made in the form of deferred payment loans
194 secured by a Loan Agreement that is executed by the Participant and the Authority.
195 (3) An applicant shall be eligible for the assistance under this subsection if the
196 applicant is:
197 (A) A resident of the District of Columbia;
198 (B) Enrolled in a qualifying advanced or professional degree program, or
199 (C) A student who is not a resident of the District of Columbia but who is
200 enrolled in a qualifying advanced or professional degree program that originates in the District of
201 Columbia.
202 Sec. 9. Loan terms.
203 Financial assistance provided pursuant to section 8 shall be subject to the following
204 conditions:
205 (1) The Authority shall not charge any applicant or participant origination,
206 application, or other associated fees for applying for or receiving a loan.
207 (2)(A) The first $5,000 of any loan made shall not be subject to interest charges.
208 (B) For any loan amount in excess of $5,000, the Board shall establish an
209 interest rate by rule, provided that the rate shall not exceed a maximum rate established by the
210 Board.
9

211 (C) Beginning October 1 of the first fiscal year following the applicable
212 date of this act, and annually thereafter, the $5,000 amount specified in subparagraph (A) of this
213 paragraph shall be adjusted by the Authority to reflect any increase in the Consumer Price Index
214 for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U), or a successor index, as published by the United States
215 Bureau of Labor Statistics.
216 (3)(A) Repayment of a loan shall be deferred, and no interest shall accrue, during
217 any period in which the student for whom the loan was made is enrolled in a qualifying
218 postsecondary educational or vocational program.
219 (B) Repayment shall begin 6 months after the student completes or
220 withdraws from the qualifying program.
221 (C) Repayment shall be made on a scheduled monthly basis for a term not
222 to exceed 360 months.
223 (D) Monthly repayment amounts shall not exceed 10% of the participant’s
224 annual discretionary income, divided by 12, as determined by the Authority by rule.
225 (4)(A) A participant who has entered repayment under a loan agreement may have
226 any outstanding loan balance discharged after completing 10 cumulative years of full-time
227 employment in public service.
228 (B) To qualify as public service employment, the employment shall:
229 (1) Be with any local, state, or federal government entity or non-
230 profit organization;
231 (ii) Be for 10 cumulative years; provided, that the employment
232 may be with more than one qualifying employer, must begin upon completion of or withdrawal
233 from a qualifying program, and do not have to be consecutive; and
10

234 (iii) May include work performed either as an employee or
235 contractor for the qualifying employer.
236 (C) For purposes of this paragraph, the term “full time” means:
237 (i) A minimum average of 30 hours per week during the period
238 being certified;
239 (ii) A minimum of 30 hours per week throughout a contractual or
240 employment period of at least 8 months in a 12-month period, such as elementary and secondary
241 school teachers and professors and instructors, in higher education, in which case the participant
242 is deemed to have worked full time; or
243 (iii) The equivalent of 30 hours per week as determined by
244 multiplying each credit or contact hour taught per week by at least 3.35 in non-tenure track
245 employment at an institution of higher education.
246 (D) Routine paid vacation or paid leave time hours provided by the
247 employer, and leave taken under the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 (29 U.S.C.
248 2612(a)(1)) shall count towards the hours for determining full time status.
