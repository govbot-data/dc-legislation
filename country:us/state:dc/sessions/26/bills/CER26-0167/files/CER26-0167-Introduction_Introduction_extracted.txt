20 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
21 __________
22
23 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
24 __________
25
26
27 To recognize and honor the life and legacy of Dr. Thornell Kenly Page and commend his
28 dedication to scholarship, public service, expanding educational access, historic preservation,
29 and community empowerment.
30
31 WHEREAS, Dr. Thornell Kenly Page was a distinguished educator, administrator,
32 community advocate, economic development leader, and devoted public servant whose lifelong
33 commitment to the people of the District of Columbia helped shape generations of residents and
34 strengthen communities across the city;
35
36 WHEREAS, born and educated in Dillon, South Carolina, Dr. Page pursued higher
37 education with distinction, earning degrees from Johnson C. Smith University, the University of
38 the District of Columbia, and Virginia Tech, becoming a respected scholar and advocate for
39 educational opportunity and advancement;
40
41 WHEREAS, Dr. Page dedicated much of his professional life to expanding educational
42 access, economic opportunity, and social services for communities throughout the District,
43 particularly low-income families, seniors, and young Black students pursuing higher education;
44
45 WHEREAS, as the Director of the Cooperative Extension Service at the University of the
46 District of Columbia, Dr. Page helped establish innovative community-based programs in all

47 eight wards of the city, including initiatives focused on public health, urban agricultures,
48 education and neighborhood empowerment;
49
50 WHEREAS, Dr. Page also served the nation with distinction as the Deputy Director of
51 the Office of Community Services at the United States Department of Health and Human
52 Services during the Clinton administration, overseeing programs dedicated to community
53 development, poverty reduction, and support for historically underserved communities;
54
55 WHEREAS, through his leadership of Page Research Associates, Inc., and his
56 involvement in numerous civic, educational, and economic development initiatives, Dr. Page
57 played a meaningful role in strengthening the civic and economic fabric of the District and
58 advancing opportunities for minority-owned businesses and neighborhood revitalization;
59
60 WHEREAS, Dr. Page was a steadfast supporter of education and youth achievement,
61 including through his service as President and Chief Executive Officer of the Washington
62 Teachers’ Union Scholarship Fund Trust, supporting students pursuing careers in education and
63 public service;
64
65 WHEREAS, deeply devoted to preserving African American history and heritage in the
66 District of Columbia, Dr. Page worked tirelessly to protect and preserve the historic Mt. Zion
67 Cemetery and the Female Union Band Society Cemetery in Georgetown, safeguarding their
68 historic significance for future generations;
69
70 WHEREAS, beyond his many professional accomplishments, Dr. Page will be
71 remembered as a devoted husband, father, grandfather, mentor, church leader, political
72 supporter, and friend, whose wisdom, dignity, intellect, generosity, and unwavering commitment
73 to his community leaves an enduring impact on all who knew him; and
74
75 WHEREAS, the life and legacy of Dr. Thornell Kenly Page reflect a profound dedication
76 to public service, educational excellence, historic preservation, and the advancement of all of
77 those making the District of Columbia their home.
78
79 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that the Council of
80 the District of Columbia recognizes Dr. Thornell Kenly Page for his extraordinary contributions
81 to the District of Columbia and highly commend his legacy of leadership, scholarship, service,
82 and community empowerment with deep gratitude and appreciation for a life well lived and
83 faithfully dedicated to others.
84
85 Sec. 2. The resolution may be cited as the “Dr. Thornell Kenly Page Recognition
86 Resolution of 2026.”
87
88 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
89 the District of Columbia Register.
