18 To amend, on a temporary basis, compulsory school enrollment requirements to ensure
19 minors attending pre-kindergarten programs are not considered truant or victims
20 of educational neglect; to ensure that if a minor living in another state turns five
21 after that state's kindergarten entrance-age date cutoff, but before September 30,
22 and then the family moves into the District during the DC school year, that the
23 child will not be considered truant; to ensure students enrolled in private or
24 parochial pre-k programs and students moving into the District of Columbia shall
25 be permitted to be enrolled in kindergarten at the request of the minor's parent or
26 guardian; and to ensure that students who relied upon prior communications by
27 DC Public Schools related to feeder pattern advancement are not disadvantaged
28 by that reliance since the school lottery has passed.
29
30 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA,
31 That this act may be cited as the "Let Students Attend Kindergarten Temporary
32 Amendment Act of 2025".
33 Sec. 2. Section 1 of Article II of an Act to provide for compulsory school
34 attendance, for the taking of a school census in the District of Columbia, and for other
35 purposes, approved February 4, 1925 (43 Stat. 806; D.C. Official Code§ 38-202 et seq.),
36 is amended by inserting a new subsection (b) to read as follows:

37 “(b) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this section, for School Years 2025-2026
38 and 2026-2027, a minor shall be exempt from the requirements of subsection (a) of this
39 section if:
40 “(1)(A) The minor was living in another state, and did not meet that state’s
41 age requirement to enroll in kindergarten at the date of that state’s enrollment deadline;
42 and
43 “(B) The minor moved to the District after the beginning of the
44 DCPS academic year; or
45 “(2)(A) The minor is younger than the age of 6; and
46 (B) The minor is enrolled in a public, private, or parochial five-
47 day-per-week pre-kindergarten program.”.
48 Sec. 3. Section 203 of the Student Promotion Act of 2013, effective February 22,
49 2014 (D.C. Law 20-84; D.C. Official Code § 38-781.02), is amended by adding new
50 subsections (e), (f), and (g) to read as follows:
51 “(e)(1) For School Years 2025-2026 and 2026-2027, any minor who has not
52 attended kindergarten, and who has not reached the age of 6 on or before May 31 shall be
53 permitted to be enrolled in kindergarten at the request of the minor’s parent or guardian.
54 “(2) DCPS shall provide written notice by July 31, 2025, or as soon as
55 possible, to the family or guardian of any child who has been told they must skip DCPS
56 kindergarten, or who has enrolled in DCPS as a first grader without having attended
57 kindergarten, that the child is eligible for kindergarten in School Year 2025-2026.
58 “(f) Any student completing their terminal year at a feeder school in School Year
59 2024-2025, may advance to the next middle or high school of that feeder pattern in
2

60 School Year 2025-2026, regardless of whether the student moved out of the boundary at
61 the current school.
62 “(g) Any student completing their terminal year at Hardy Middle School in
63 School Year 2024-2025, may choose whether they advance to MacArthur High School or
64 Jackson-Reed High School if they have a sibling attending Jackson-Reed High School for
65 School Year 2025-2026.”.
66 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
67 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Chief Financial Officer as
68 the fiscal impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures
69 Act of 1975, approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-
70 301.47a).
71 Sec. 5. Effective date.
72 (a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or, in the event of
73 veto by the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto) and a 30-day period of
74 congressional review as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home
75 Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-
76 206.02(c)(1)).
77 (b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.
