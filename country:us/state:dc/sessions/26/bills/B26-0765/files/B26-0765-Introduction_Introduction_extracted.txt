16 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Restoration of Covenanted Roads and Alleys by the
17 District Government Act of 2024 to further clarify the District’s authority to repair or
18 restore certain private rights-of-way to a condition appropriate for the passage of motor
19 vehicles owned or operated by the District government.
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 act may be cited as the “Restoration of Covenanted Roads and Alleys by the District
23 Government Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2026”.
24 Sec. 2. Section 2 of the Restoration of Covenanted Roads and Alleys by the District
25 Government Act of 2024, effective March 28, 2025 (D.C. Law 25-320; D.C. Official Code § 9-
26 401.19), is amended as follows:
27 (a) Subsection (c) is amended as follows:
28 (1) The lead-in language is amended by striking the phrase “this section” and
29 inserting the phrase “subsection (a) of this section” in its place.
30 (2) Paragraph (2) is amended by striking the phrase “, and impose such other
31 conditions as the Mayor deems appropriate.” and inserting the phrase “as the Mayor deems
32 appropriate.” in its place.
33 (b) New subsections (e) and (f) are added to read as follows:
34 “(e)(1) The Mayor may also repair or restore a private right-of-way to such condition as
35 the Mayor deems appropriate for the passage of motor vehicles owned or operated by the District
1

36 government, and the Mayor may enter such private right-of-way for the purpose of such repair or
37 restoration, if:
38 “(A) The private right-of-way is used by the District government to
39 provide regular trash removal service;
40 “(B) The private right-of-way is not in a state of good repair, as
41 determined by the Mayor, for the passing of motor vehicles by the District government for
42 regular trash removal service;
43 “(C) The owner of the private right-of-way has not covenanted for the
44 benefit of the District government, and is not otherwise legally obligated for the benefit of the
45 District government, to maintain the private right-of-way in a state of good repair for regular
46 trash removal service; and
47 “(D) The owner of the private right-of-way has consented to the Mayor’s
48 repair or restoration of the private right-of-way and has provided a release of liability and
49 indemnification related to the repair or restoration.
50 “(2) Before repairing or restoring a private right-of-way under this subsection, the
51 Mayor shall attempt to:
52 “(A) Obtain from each owner of property likely to be impacted by the
53 repair or restoration of the private right-of-way a release of liability and indemnification related
54 to the repair or restoration; and
55 “(B) Obtain such other releases and indemnifications as the Mayor deems
56 appropriate.
57 “(f) As a condition of repairing or restoring a private right-of-way under this section, the
58 Mayor may impose such terms and conditions as the Mayor deems appropriate.”.
