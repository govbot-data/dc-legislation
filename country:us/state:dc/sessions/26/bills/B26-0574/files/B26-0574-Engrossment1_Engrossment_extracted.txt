11 To amend, on an temporary basis, the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016 to prohibit
12 private disability insurance providers from reducing short-term disability benefits based
13 on actual or estimated paid leave benefits to which an eligible individual may be entitled
14 to from the District, regardless of the jurisdiction in which the insurance policy was
15 issued or written; and to amend the Insurance Trade and Economic Development
16 Amendment Act of 2000 to make the prohibition on offsetting or reducing benefits under
17 a private market short-term disability insurance policy based on estimated or actual
18 benefits received under the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016 enforceable
19 under that law, regardless of the jurisdiction in which the insurance policy was issued or
20 written.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
23 act may be cited as the “Short-Term Disability Insurance Benefit Protection Clarification
24 Temporary Amendment Act of 2026”.
25 Sec. 2. Section 107(j)(1) of the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016, effective
26 April 7, 2017 (D.C. Law 21-264; D.C. Official Code § 32-541.07(j)(1)), is amended to read as
27 follows:
28 “(j)(1) No insurer may offset or reduce benefits or income available to an eligible
29 individual under an individual or group policy for temporary or short-term disability insurance

32 Sec. 3. Section 120a the Insurance Trade and Economic Development Amendment Act of
33 2000, effective November 13, 2021 (D.C. Law 24-45; D.C. Official Code § 31-2231.20a), is
34 amended as follows:
35 (a) Subsection (a) is amended to read as follows:
36 “(a) No insurer may offset or reduce benefits or income available to an eligible individual
37 under a temporary or short-term disability insurance policy, based on estimated or actual benefits
38 the eligible individual may or does receive under the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of
39 2016, effective April 7, 2017 (D.C. Law 21-264; D.C. Official Code § 32-541.01 et seq.),
40 regardless of in which jurisdiction such policy was executed, written, or delivered.”.
41 (b) Subsection (c) is amended to read as follows:
42 “(c) For the purposes of this section, the term:
43 “(1) “Eligible individual” shall have the same meaning as provided in section
44 101(6) of the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016, effective April 7, 2017 (D.C. Law
45 21-264; D.C. Official Code § 32-541.01(6)).
46 “(2) “Self-insured employer” shall have the same meaning as provided in section
47 101(19A) of the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016, effective April 7, 2017 (D.C.
48 Law 21-264; D.C. Official Code § 32-541.01(19A)).”.
