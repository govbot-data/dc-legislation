26 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
27
28 __________
29
30 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
31
32 ____________________
33
34 To recognize the Transgender Day of Remembrance, to celebrate the resilience of transgender
35 individuals and communities, and to declare Thursday, November 20, 2025, as
36 Transgender Day of Remembrance in the District of Columbia.
37
38 WHEREAS, the government of the District of Columbia has affirmatively and officially
39 recognized the right of transgender people to live openly and freely as their authentic selves,
40 without fear of discrimination or violence;
41
42 WHEREAS, examples of the District’s official commitment to the legal rights of
43 transgender residents include the prohibition of discrimination on the basis of gender identity or
44 expression since 2005, and the refusal to facilitate cross-border interference in gender-affirming
45 health care under the Human Rights Sanctuary Amendment Act of 2022;
46
1

47 WHEREAS, the District of Columbia nevertheless has a particularly alarming history of
48 violence against transgender individuals including the murders of A’Nee Roberson, Jasmine
49 Parker, Zoe Spears, Ashanti Carmon, Deeniquia Dodds, Deoni Jones, Lashai Mclean, Tyli’a Mack,
50 Elexius Woodland, Bella Evangelista, Emonie Spaulding, Stephanie Thomas, Ukea Davis, and too
51 many others;
52
53 WHEREAS, bias, violence and systemic oppression against transgender people remain
54 unacceptably prevalent, including in the District of Columbia;
55
56 WHEREAS, incarceration, homelessness, poverty and addiction disproportionately harm
57 transgender people, especially transgender women of color, and continue to claim lives;
58
59 WHEREAS, transgender people have the same human rights as all people to be free from
60 violence and discrimination, and to live their lives with joy, dignity, and hopes for the future,
61 respected and loved as any other person;
62
63 WHEREAS, transgender people in the District of Columbia have long exhibited resilience
64 and bravery, including by organizing in response to the 1997 death of Tyra Hunter, a Black
65 transgender woman whom District employees refused to treat for life-threatening injuries after
66 determining that she was transgender;
67
68 WHEREAS, since the 2024 Transgender Day of Remembrance, transgender people in the
69 surrounding area have continued to be murdered, including Dream Johnson, while misgendering
70 by media and police may have obscured additional instances of violence against transgender
71 people in the region;
72
73 WHEREAS, the people and government of the District will continue to fight for
74 transgender people’s well-being, even in the face of escalating, cruel, politically motivated
75 attempts to discriminate against, stigmatize, and criminalize transgender people in state
76 legislatures and national political campaigns; and
77
78 WHEREAS, the transgender community and allies in the District of Columbia have
79 commemorated Transgender Day of Remembrance since 2001 and communities and organizations
80 here and around the world will observe the Transgender Day of Remembrance on November 20,
81 2025.
82
83 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
84 resolution may be cited as the “Transgender Day of Remembrance Recognition Resolution of
85 2025”.
86
87 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia declares November 20, 2025, as the
88 Transgender Day of Remembrance in the District of Columbia.
89
90 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
91 the District of Columbia Register.
