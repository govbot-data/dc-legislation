16 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
17
18 _________________________
19
20 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
21 _________________________
22
23
24 To recognize and celebrate Georgetown on the 275th anniversary of its founding.
25 WHEREAS, Georgetown was established in 1751 as a port town in the colony of
26 Maryland on land known as the Tahoga Village belonging to the people of Nacotchtank;
27 WHEREAS, Georgetown remained a separate and often self-governing entity with
28 growing commercial traffic and a bustling business sector until it was fully incorporated into the
29 District of Columbia in 1895;
30 WHEREAS, Black Americans and those brought to this country as enslaved people have
31 lived and worked in Georgetown since its founding, at times making up 30-40% of the
32 population, and continue to shape the area’s history and culture including through institutions
33 like Mt. Zion United Methodist Church, the oldest African American congregation in DC.;

34 WHEREAS, the Georgetown Historic District was created in 1950 as the first historic
35 district in Washington and the sixth in the United States;
36 WHEREAS, in 1962, Georgetown was designated as a National Historic Landmark and
37 has since been included in the DC Inventory of Historic Sites and National Register of Historic
38 Places;
39 WHEREAS, Georgetown remains a hub of residential, retail, restaurant, and tourist sites
40 that draws residents and visitors all over the world to experience the neighborhood’s unique
41 charm and vibrant offerings;
42 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
43 resolution may be cited as the “Georgetown 275th Anniversary Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
44 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates Georgetown
45 for its long history and its social, cultural and economic contributions to the District.
46 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
47 the District of Columbia Register.
