10 To establish the Youth Advisory Council on Climate Change and Environmental Conservation to
11 provide an organized youth perspective on various issues, including the impacts of the
12 changing climate, adverse weather events, and ways the District can mitigate, prepare, and
13 adapt to climate change and create and sustain environmental educational opportunities for
14 youth; and to amend the District of Columbia Government Comprehensive Merit Personnel
15 Act of 1979 to authorize a stipend for members of the Youth Advisory Council on Climate
16 Change and Environmental Conservation.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
19 act may be cited as the “Youth Advisory Council on Climate Change and Environmental
20 Conservation Establishment Act of 2025”.
21 Sec. 2. Youth Advisory Council on Climate Change and Environmental Conservation.
22 (a)(1) There is established the Youth Advisory Council on Climate Change and
23 Environmental Conservation (“Youth Climate Council”), the mission of which is to consult with
24 and advise the Mayor, the Council, the Department of Energy and Environment (“DOEE”),
25 Commission on Climate Change and Resiliency (“Commission”), and other entities on the effects
26 of, and recommend improvements to, the District’s laws, policies, practices, programs, and
27 budgets regarding the following topics in the District:

29 change;
30 “(B) Environmental conservation; and
31 “(C) Green workforce development, including apprenticeships, certification
32 programs, and other educational or work-based opportunities for youth.
33 (2) Pursuant to its mission, the Youth Climate Council may, with respect to the
34 topics listed in paragraph (1) of this subsection:
35 (A) Provide comments and recommendations on laws and regulations;
36 (B) Develop and propose its own plans, programs, or initiatives;
37 (C) Collaborate with other youth organizations and entities on programs,
38 events, or initiatives that educate and engage youth and adults;
39 (D) Respond to requests for comments and recommendations from District
40 officials, agencies, boards, commissions, and advisory committees;
41 (E) Present its views to the Council, the Mayor, or District agencies; and
42 (F) Issue or publish reports on its findings, recommendations, activities,
43 goals, and accomplishments.

115 Sec. 3. Functions and procedures of the Youth Climate Council.
116 (a)(1) The Youth Climate Council shall elect from among its membership a chairperson,
117 vice-chairperson, and secretary, each for a term of one year.
118 (2) These officers shall be re-elected annually or in the event of a vacancy, but shall
119 serve no more than 2 terms in any combination of offices.
120 (3) The Youth Climate Council may vote to create other leadership positions from
121 within its membership.
6

ENGROSSED ORIGINAL
122 (4) The Youth Climate Council may form committees or working groups from its
123 membership to consider specific subtopics and invite participation from outside individuals and
124 entities, including the public and subject-matter experts.
125 (b)(1) The Youth Climate Council shall meet at least once per month.
126 (2) The chairperson of the Youth Climate Council, or vice-chairperson in the
127 chairperson’s absence, shall, in consultation with and with the assistance of DOEE, convene all
128 Youth Climate Council meetings.
129 (3) A majority of the members appointed to the Youth Climate Council at any given
130 time shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of official business.
131 (c)(1) The Youth Climate Council may provide updates to the Commission at each of the
132 Commission’s quarterly meetings, and may seek the advice of the Commission as needed.
133 (2) The Youth Climate Council shall meet jointly with the Commission at least once
134 each year to exchange information and feedback related to common issues and concerns.
135 (d) All actions of the Youth Climate Council shall be considered advisory and shall be
136 approved by a majority vote of the members present and voting at a meeting.
137 (e)(1) DOEE shall provide principal staff to support the activities of the Youth Climate
138 Council to include:
139 “(A) Convening and managing logistics for meetings;
140 “(B) Delivering and coordinating education and training;

142 colleges; and

144 reports to the Council and Mayor.
145 (2) The Youth Climate Council may use space and supplies owned or rented by the
146 District government for purposes consistent with this act.
147 (3) The Council shall provide staff assistance to the Youth Climate Council for the

150 (4) The chairpersons of the committees with jurisdiction over the environment,
151 energy, and natural resources at the Council shall provide an opportunity for the Youth Climate
152 Council to report in person on its findings and recommendations at least once each year.
