Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Pursuant to D.C. Official Code § 2-352.02, I have enclosed, for consideration by the Council of the District of Columbia,
the (a) Council Contract Summary for the Agreement to Enter into a Long-Term Subsidy Contract (“ALTSC”) between
the District of Columbia Housing Authority (“DCHA”) and 10Q NW LLC, and (b) proposed Local Rent Supplement
Program Contract No. 2025-LRSP-05A with 10Q NW LLC Resolution of 2025. DCHA proposes to provide a housing
subsidy to the Owner in the initial amount of $187,308.00 annually for a multi-year term of twenty (20) years, in
support of the District’s Local Rent Supplement Program (“LRSP”) to provide five (5) affordable housing units at The
Barnett Aden (Florida & Q) located at 10 Q Street, NW.
As always, I am available to discuss any questions you may have regarding the proposed LTSC. In order to facilitate a
response to any questions concerning this ALTSC, please have your staff contact Hammere Gebreyes, Director of
External Affairs and Senior Advisor, at (202) 207-4694.
I look forward to your favorable consideration of this proposed ALTSC agreement.
Sincerely,
Keith Pettigrew
Executive Director, District of Columbia Housing Authority

26 Sec. 2. (a) In 2007, the Council of the District of Columbia ("Council") passed Title II of
27 the Fiscal Year 2007 Budget Support Act of 2006 ("BSA") to provide funding for affordable
28 housing for extremely low-income households in the District of Columbia ("District"). The
29 passage of the BSA created the Local Rent Supplement Program ("LRSP"), a program designed
30 to provide affordable housing and supportive services to extremely low-income District
31 residents, including those who are homeless or in need of supportive services, such as elderly
32 individuals or those with disabilities, through project-based, tenant-based, and sponsored-based
33 LRSP affordable housing units. The BSA provided for the District of Columbia Housing
34 Authority ("DCHA") to administer the LRSP on behalf of the District.

1 (b) In June of 2019, the District’s Department of Housing and Community Development
2 (“DHCD”), previously issued a Request for Proposals (“RFP”) regarding the availability of
3 funds from multiple district agencies, including DCHA, DHCD, the Department of Behavioral
4 Health, the Department of Human Services, and the District of Columbia Housing Finance
5 Agency. Of the twenty-one (21) total proposals received, eleven (11) developers were chosen to

7 supportive housing units for extremely low-income families making zero to thirty percent (30%)
8 of the area’s median income, as well as the chronically homeless and individuals with mental or
9 physical disabilities. Upon approval of the contract by the Council, DCHA will enter into an
10 agreement to enter into a long-term subsidy contract (“ALTSC”) with the selected housing
11 providers under the LRSP for housing services.
12 (c) There exists an immediate need to approve the long-term subsidy contract with 10Q
13 NW LLC under the LRSP in order to provide long-term affordable housing units for extremely
14 low-income households for units located at 10 Q Street, NW.
15 (d) The legislation to approve the contract will authorize an ALTSC between DCHA and
16 10Q NW LLC with respect to the payment of a rental subsidy and allows the owner to lease the
17 rehabilitated units at 10 Q Street NW and house extremely low-income households with incomes
18 at thirty percent (30%) or less of the area median income.
