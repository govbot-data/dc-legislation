18 To amend, on an emergency basis due to congressional review, section 8f of the Homeless Services
19 Reform Act of 2005 to require specific documentation from tenants establishing eligibility
20 for Emergency Rental Assistance Funds, to clarify the definition of a qualifying emergency
21 situation, and to require the Mayor to issue regulations implementing the amended
22 provisions of that section; and to amend section 501 of the Rental Housing Act of 1985 to
23 permit a court to enter a stay, rather than require a court to enter a stay, when a tenant
24 submits documentation to the court demonstrating that the tenant has a pending Emergency
25 Rental Assistance Program application, to require housing providers to reschedule
26 evictions in certain circumstances involving approved Emergency Rental Assistance
27 Program applications, and to create an affirmative defense in eviction cases on the basis of
28 housing providers’ noncompliance with the Emergency Rental Assistance Program
29 application process.
30
31 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
32 act may be cited as the “Emergency Rental Assistance Program Reform Congressional Review
33 Emergency Amendment Act of 2025”.

35 (D.C. Law 24-287; D.C. Official Code § 4-753.08), is amended as follows:
36 (a) Subsection (a) is amended as follows:
37 (1) Paragraph (2)(A) is amended as follows:
38 (A) The lead-in language is amended to read as follows:

57 the loss of a job or high medical costs, that impacts the applicant unit’s ability to pay rent and that
58 cannot be resolved without financial assistance.”.

66 Sec. 3. The Rental Housing Act of 1985, effective July 17, 1985 (D.C. Law 6-10; D.C.
67 Official Code § 42-3505.01 et seq.), is amended as follows:
68 (a) Section 103 (D.C. Official Code § 42-3501.03) is amended as follows:
69 (1) A new paragraph (12A) is added to read as follows:
70 “(12A) “ERAP provider” shall have the same meaning as provided in section 8f(d)(3A) of
71 the Homeless Services Reform Act of 2005, passed on first reading on April 1, 2025 (Engrossed
72 version of Bill 26-46).”.
73 (2) A new paragraph (23A) is added to read as follows:
74 “(23A) “Payment information” means documents or information that identify the owner or
75 manager of a rental unit, identify the balance of rent owed by a tenant, or facilitate the payment of
76 the owner or manager of a rental unit and that are necessary to perfect an application for Emergency
77 Rental Assistance Funds, which may include personal identification, a W-9 form, a basic business
78 license, proof of a Social Security Number, a property management agreement, or a rent ledger,
79 but which shall not include the content of, or information associated with, a payment plan.”.
80 (b) Section 501 (D.C. Official Code § 42–3505.01) is amended to read as follows:
81 (1) Subsection (r) is amended to read as follows:
82 “(r)(1) The court may stay any proceeding adjudicating a claim brought by a housing
83 provider to recover possession of a rental unit for nonpayment of rent if a tenant submits
84 documentation to the court demonstrating that he or she has a pending Emergency Rental
85 Assistance Program (“ERAP”) application; provided, that the court may stay a proceeding under
86 this subsection only once during the pendency of the case.
3

87 “(2) Paragraph (1) of this subsection shall not be construed to limit a court’s
88 discretion to extend a stay.
89 “(3) When an eviction based solely on a claim for nonpayment of rent has been
90 authorized by the court and the tenant notifies the housing provider, no later than 48 hours prior to
91 the scheduled date and time of the eviction, that the tenant has an approved ERAP application that
92 would pay the full amount necessary to redeem the tenancy, the housing provider shall cancel the
93 eviction. If the housing provider then reschedules the eviction, the housing provider shall
94 reschedule the eviction for a date no sooner than 3 weeks from the earlier-scheduled eviction date.
95 “(4) A housing provider that cancels an eviction under paragraphs (3) of this section
96 shall have leave to file an alias writ (“writ”), and no further leave of court shall be required for the
97 next-requested issuance of a writ following the cancellation of the originally scheduled eviction.
98 This paragraph shall not apply if a judgment is permanently stayed by a tenant’s redemption of a
99 tenancy.”.
100 (2) New subsections (s) and (t) are added to read as follows:
101 “(s) In any matter adjudicating a claim brought by a housing provider to recover possession
102 of a rental unit for nonpayment of rent in which the tenant alleges, at or before an initial hearing,
103 that the housing provider is withholding payment information requested by an ERAP provider, the
104 court may order the housing provider to comply with any pending requests for the payment
105 information from an ERAP provider.
106 “(t)(1) In any matter adjudicating a claim brought by a housing provider to recover
107 possession of a rental unit for nonpayment of rent in which the court finds, at trial or upon the
108 resolution of a dispositive motion resolving the entire matter, that the housing provider failed to
109 provide payment information requested by an ERAP provider for more than 45 days after receiving
110 a request for the payment information from an ERAP provider, the court may order that the amount
4

111 of rent that would have been redeemed by a successful ERAP application be deemed waived and
112 shall not be recoverable by the housing provider.
113 “(2) The court shall not waive rent under paragraph (1) of this subsection if the
114 housing provider shows that:
115 “(A) Compliance with a request for payment information necessary to
116 perfect an ERAP application was delayed by a third party not under the control of the housing
117 provider;
118 “(B) Good cause otherwise exists for the housing provider’s non-
119 compliance; or
120 “(C) The housing provider has, since the date of the relevant request for
121 payment information, received payment from ERAP on behalf of the tenant.
122 “(3) A waiver of rent under paragraph (1) of this subsection shall apply to any future
123 proceeding in which the housing provider seeks from the tenant:
124 “(A) The recovery of possession of the rental unit the rent for which was
125 waived under paragraph (1) of this subsection; or
126 “(B) A money judgment for the waived rent.
127 “(4) Nothing in this subsection shall limit a tenant’s ability to assert any defense to
128 an outstanding rent balance sought by a housing provider.”.
