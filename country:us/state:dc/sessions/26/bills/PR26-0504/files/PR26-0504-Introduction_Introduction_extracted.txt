Dr. Roger Kahn
Dr. Roger Kahn began his forensic laboratory career in 1988 with the
Miami-Dade Police Department Crime Laboratory in Miami, Florida,
where he established one ofthe first forensic DNA labs in the nation, In
1997 he was named Laboratory System Director for the Ohio Bureau of
Criminal Identification and Investigation, in charge of Ohio'sthree state-
level crime laboratories. Afteradecade atOhio BCI, hejoined the Harris
County Institute of Forensic Sciences in Houston, Texas, serving as the
Forensic Genetics Lab director and then as the Institute’s Crime
Laboratory Director.
Dr. Kahnis a pastpresidentof the American SocietyofCrime Laboratory
Directors, afellow ofthe AmericanAcademy ofForensic Sciences, anda
formerand founding memberofthe FBI’s Scientific Working Group on DNA Analysis Methods.
He is the author ofpeer-reviewed publications in basic DNA research and forensic DNA and is
certified by the American Board of Criminalistics in Molecular Biology. He holds a doctoral
degree in human genetics fromYale University.
Dr. Kahn completed two competitive NIJ research grants, one to develop methods to identify
human proteins of abuse, such as the human growth hormone, and another for the use of Next-
Generation DNA sequencing to determine the cause ofsudden death ofinfants andyoung people
with mutations associated with cardiac channelopathies,
‘A California resident, Dr. Khan eared his Bachelor of Arts in Biology from the University of
California Santa Barbara and his DoctorofPhilosophy from Yale University.
