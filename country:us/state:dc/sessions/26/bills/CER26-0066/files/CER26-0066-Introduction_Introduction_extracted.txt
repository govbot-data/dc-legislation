25 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
26
27 _________________________
28
29 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
30
31 _________________________
32
33
34 To recognize the importance of kinship families in the District of Columbia and the many
35 benefits kinship caregivers bestow on the children in their care as well as the many
36 challenges they face when raising these children, and to declare the month of September
37 2025 as “Kinship Care Month” in the District of Columbia.
38
39 WHEREAS, the D.C. Council seeks to proclaim September 2025 as Kinship Care Month
40 in the District of Columbia; and
41
42 WHEREAS, Kinship Care Month is observed to recognize children in kinship families,
43 and the grandparents, other relatives, and family friends who raise them, ensuring their safety,
44 promoting their well-being, and establishing a safe and stable home for these young people; and
1

45
46 WHEREAS, in kinship families, a caregiver cares for a child or children who were
47 biologically born to another family member or close friend because the biological parents are
48 unable to raise them; and
49
50 WHEREAS, nationally, approximately 2.7 million children are living with grandparents
51 and other relatives or fictive kin in kinship care; more than 22,000 of these young people reside
52 in the District of Columbia, with fewer than 125 of them in foster care; and
53
54 WHEREAS, one in 11 of all children, and one in five Black children, will live within a
55 kinship family sometime during their childhood; and
56
57 WHEREAS, kinship caregivers report tremendous satisfaction in caring for and raising
58 children, but face substantial challenges in accessing the resources they need to care for their
59 families; and
60
61 WHEREAS, many kinship caregivers live at or below the margins of financial stability
62 themselves even before they agree to take a child into their homes, while others may give up
63 their retirement years to assume parenting duties for these children, and
64
65 WHEREAS, many children come to live with kinship caregivers after allegations of
66 parental abuse or neglect; and
67
68 WHEREAS, children in kinship care frequently have mental health conditions from
69 trauma experienced in their homes and communities; and
70
71 WHEREAS, kinship caregivers offer a refuge, as well as love, nurturing and care, for
72 these children; and
73
74 WHEREAS, research shows that kinship care helps maintain family, sibling and
75 community ties, improves behavioral and mental health outcomes, and promotes permanency for
76 children who cannot live with their parents; and
77
78 WHEREAS, it is vitally important for the public to grow in awareness of the challenges
79 faced by children, grandparents, and other relatives in kinship families; so as to ensure there are
80 services and supports available and accessible to enable kinship youth to grow and thrive; and
81
82 WHEREAS, the D.C. Council expresses its wholehearted thanks and support for kinship
83 caregivers for stepping up in times of crisis to provide loving homes for D.C. children; and
84
85 WHEREAS, Kinship Care Month honors the priceless role these grandparents and other
86 kinship caregivers play in the lives of the children they love.
87
88 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
89 resolution may be cited as the “Kinship Care Month Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
90
2

91 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes the contributions of kinship
92 families raising children in the District of Columbia, and declares the month of September 2025
93 as “Kinship Care Month”, as a confirmation of the District’s continued support of kinship
94 caregivers for stepping up in times of crisis to provide loving homes for children in the District.
95
96 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
