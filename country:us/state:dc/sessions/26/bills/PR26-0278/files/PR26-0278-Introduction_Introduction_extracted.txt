15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Urban Forest
16 Preservation Act of 2002 to require that the Mayor issue a permit for the removal of a
17 Heritage Tree abutting or within the site of the Long Bridge Project under certain
18 conditions.
19
20 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 resolution may be cited as the “Long Bridge Project Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
22 Sec. 2. (a) The Long Bridge crosses the Potomac River to connect the District to Arlington,
23 Virginia. The Long Bridge is used by CSX Transportation freight trains, Amtrak intercity trains,
24 and Virginia Railway Express commuter trains.
25 (b) The Long Bridge Project will add a new two-track railroad bridge next to the existing
26 bridge, eliminating a major bottleneck for rail transport (both passenger and freight) through the
27 mid-Atlantic region. The Virginia Passenger Rail Authority (“VPRA”) is currently managing the
28 project, though the project’s development was original led by the District Department of
29 Transportation (“DDOT”) from 2011 through 2020. The project will also add a dedicated bicycle
30 and pedestrian bridge over the Potomac River, linking Arlington’s Long Bridge Park, the District’s
31 East and West Potomac Parks, and the Mount Vernon Trail. In sum, the project will increase
32 regional rail capacity, improve passenger rail service, enhance pedestrian and bicycle connectivity
33 between the District and Virginia, and boost the regional economy.

34 (c) Unfortunately, the project has encountered an obstacle with respect to an American Elm
35 tree that is located within the footprint of the project. Specifically, there is an American Elm tree
36 that sits between Maine Avenue, SW, the Washington Marina, and the Maine Avenue pedestrian
37 bridge that must be either protected in place, relocated, or removed for the project to move forward,
38 since there is no reasonable design alternative that could avoid this location.
39 (d) Given the tree’s size (its circumference is 126.3 inches), it qualifies as a “Heritage Tree”
40 under District law. It, therefore, cannot be completely removed unless it is demonstrated to be a
41 Hazardous Tree, or of a species that DDOT has determined is appropriate for removal. The
42 American Elm tree impacting the Long Bridge Project is neither a Hazardous Tree nor of a species
43 that is appropriate for removal. Removal is, therefore, not an option under current law.
44 (e) DDOT’s Urban Forestry Division (“UFD”) has examined the tree and does not believe
45 protecting the tree in place while construction moves forward is feasible. Much of the tree’s critical
46 root system is within the footprint of the new rail bridge abutment. The construction will likely
47 damage the root system and irreparably harm the tree. Given that attempts to protect the tree in
48 place will likely result in its destruction, DDOT is unlikely to issue a permit under that condition.
49 (f) District law allows for the relocation of a Heritage Tree if the tree can be moved
50 “without significant harm to the tree.” However, if the tree dies within 3 years of replanting, the
51 person or entity who relocated the tree is subject to a fine.
52 (g) Unfortunately, relocating the tree presents another series of issues. First, because the
53 tree is located between two bridges on Maine Avenue SW with low clearance, DDOT is limited to
54 one potential relocation site—a traffic median 700 feet east of its current location. DDOT recently
55 planted approximately 40 trees at this site, and those newly planted trees would need to be removed
56 prior to the relocation of the American Elm. Additionally, the relocation will require a closure of

57 Maine Avenue, SW, for up to 48 hours, closures of the Maine Avenue Pedestrian Bridge, sidewalk,
58 and Washington Marina parking spaces while the tree is being transported and replanted in the new
59 location. Relocation will still require that VPRA coordinate with UFD to monitor the tree’s health
60 after relocation, including monthly arborist inspections and periodic, single-lane closures of Maine
61 Avenue. VPRA estimates that the total cost for relocation is approximately $3 million. All told,
62 there will be significant costs and inconveniences associated with relocating the tree, including the
63 destruction of dozens of younger trees.
64 (h) The fine for illegally removing a tree, or relocating a tree that dies within 3 years, is
65 $300 for each inch of the tree’s circumference. Here, the fine for illegal removal or relocation
66 would be approximately $37,890. While those costs are not insurmountable for the Long Bridge
67 Project, a violation of the Heritage Tree protection laws also results in the denial of construction
68 permits for 3 years, which would essentially halt work on the project.
69 (i) VPRA is willing to pay an increased fee to remove the tree, so long as it would allow
70 the Long Bridge Project to move forward.
71 (j) This emergency legislation would allow for the removal of a Heritage Tree if the tree
72 abuts or lies within the site of the Long Bridge Project. An applicant for a removal permit would
73 also need to demonstrate that the tree cannot reasonably be protected in place and that relocation
74 would either cost more than $1 million or unreasonably burden the public (e.g., road and sidewalk
75 closures). Finally, the applicant must agree to pay $1,200 per each inch of circumference of the
76 Heritage Tree to be removed.
77 (k) This emergency legislation is necessary to allow the Long Bridge Project to proceed
78 without unreasonable delays or significant additional costs.

79 Sec. 3. The Council finds the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency
80 circumstances making it necessary that the Long Bridge Project Emergency Amendment Act of
81 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
82 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
