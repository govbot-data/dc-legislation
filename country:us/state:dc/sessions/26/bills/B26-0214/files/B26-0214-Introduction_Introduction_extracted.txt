Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is an emergency
bill, the “Modification Nos. 15, 16, 17, 17a, 17b, 17c, and 18 to Contract No. CW98887 with Great

contact Marc Scott, Chief Operating Officer, Office of Contracting and Procurement, at (202) 724-8759.

(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The Department of Aging and Community Living (“DACL”) is seeking to fully exercise option year
two of the contract with Great American Corporation to continue to provide hot/cold ready-to-eat,
frozen boxed meals and supplements for the DACL’s meal delivery program participants.
These services are critical and urgent because they provide the District's senior residents aged 60
years and older with nutritious meals containing one-third of the Dietary Reference Intakes as
established by the Food and Nutrition Board and they meet the current Dietary Guidelines for
Americans.
In addition, Great American Corporation provides meals with the flexibility to accommodate daily
changes in delivery location and meal quantities. Participants in the DACL program receive hot and
freshly prepared, ready-to-eat meals delivered to community dining sites daily. They also provide
meals for special senior events hosted by the District (DACL and Mayor).
(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:
The solicitation, DOC590203, was advertised in the Washington Times newspaper and published in
PASS to DC Supply Schedule holders on November 30, 2021, with a closing date of January 14,
2022. A virtual pre-proposal conference was held on December 7, 2021. Three CBE vendors
submitted proposals in response to the solicitation: Run Veggie, Ocean Pro, and Great American
Corporation, and each was qualified by the Technical Evaluation Panel (TEP). Each of the offerors
submitted a Contractor Qualification Record (CQR).
In accordance with 27 DCMR Section 1905.6, the primary basis for selection for the Human Care
Agreement provider was the pre-qualifying of the Contractor Qualification Record of the applicant.
The contracting officer (CO) and the TEP reviewed and evaluated the provider’s technical
2

competence, menus and nutrition of meals offered, prices, resumes, professional qualifications, the
relevant professional and business licenses, capacity to accomplish the required work in the required
time; experience with similar scope of work, the contractor’s past performance and other
documentation submitted by the provider. In addition, the contracting officer reviewed through other
readily available sources compliance verifying mechanisms and determined that each of the offerors
has satisfied the requirements specified in the Request for Qualifications.
The decision to award the three offerors was based on the evaluation criteria outlined in 27 DCMR
Section 1905.6 “Selection of Human Care Agreement Contractor. All three offerors were awarded a
letter contract.
Prior to definitizing the HCA, DACL decided to continue receiving services from only two of the
offerors, Great American Corporation and Run Veggie. As a result, the letter contract with OceanPro
Industries (OceanPro), was terminated for convenience and the capacity of services distributed to the
remaining two offerors. OceanPro remained a qualified vendor so that we can provide an HCA at a
later date, for up to 3 years. In 2024, an HCA was entered into with OceanPro and services from Run
Veggie were discontinued.
The CO conducted an independent assessment of each proposal submission. As a result of the CO’s
assessment, the CO has decided it’s in the best interest of the District to award the Human Care
Agreement to the three Offerors that submitted bids.
