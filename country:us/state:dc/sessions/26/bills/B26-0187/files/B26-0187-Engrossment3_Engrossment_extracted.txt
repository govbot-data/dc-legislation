12 To amend Title 6-A1101 of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations to expand the
13 authority of Special Police Officers to include the issuance of notice of violations for
14 individuals engaging in fare evasion; to amend the Act to Regulate Public Conduct on
15 Public Passenger Vehicles to provide that the adjudication of fare evasion citations shall
16 be pursuant to the Department of Consumer, and Regulatory Affairs Civil Infractions Act
17 of 1985; to amend the Omnibus Police Reform Amendment Act of 2000 to allow
18 applicants for sworn officers of the Metropolitan Police Department to count certain
19 credit hours for completion of the initial training program towards the requirement that
20 certain applicants complete 60-hours of post-secondary education at an accredited college
21 or university; to amend the Policemen and Firemen's Retirement and Disability Act to
22 clarify certain benefits for Fire and Emergency Medical Services’ survivors; to amend
23 Title 23 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify the authority of Metropolitan
24 Police Department warrantless arrest requirements, and to create an exception to the
25 rebuttable presumption for pretrial detention for robbery where the victim does not
26 sustain a physical injury and burglary in the second degree; to amend Chapter 8 of Title
27 16 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify that offenses eligible in the future
28 for automatic sealing are currently eligible for sealing by motion; and to amend the
29 Secure DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2024 to repeal the sunset date of certain
30 provisions regarding pretrial detention.
31
32 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
33 act may be cited as the “Peace DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2025”.
