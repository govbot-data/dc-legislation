8 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
9
10 _________________________
11
12 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
13
14 _________________________
15
16
17 To recognize, honor, and thank the first responders who demonstrated extraordinary courage,
18 dedication, and professionalism in their response to the tragic midair collision of
19 American Eagle Flight No. 5342 and a U.S. army Black Hawk helicopter over the
20 Potomac River on January 29, 2025.
21
22 WHEREAS, on January 29, 2025, at approximately 8:47 PM, a devastating midair
23 collision occurred between American Eagle Flight No. 5342 and a U.S. Army Black Hawk
24 helicopter over the Potomac River near Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport;
25 WHEREAS, this tragic incident resulted in the loss of 67 precious lives, including 60
26 passengers and four crew members aboard the commercial aircraft and three U.S. Army service
27 members aboard the helicopter;
28 WHEREAS, the victims of this tragedy represented a diverse group of individuals from
29 the District of Columbia, Maryland, Virginia, Massachusetts, Delaware, Ohio, North Carolina,
30 Kansas, as well as Russia, including accomplished figure skaters, coaches, and dedicated military
31 service members;
32 WHEREAS, first responders from across the District of Columbia, Virginia, Maryland,
33 and federal agencies demonstrated exemplary coordination and swift action in their immediate
34 response to this emergency;
1

35 WHEREAS, the District of Columbia first responders, including the Metropolitan Police
36 Department (MPD), Fire and Emergency Medical Services (Fire and EMS), Office of Unified
37 Communications (OUC), Homeland Security and Emergency Management Agency (HSEMA),
38 as well as the Office of the Chief Medical Examiner (OCME) and Department of Forensic
39 Sciences (DFS), worked tirelessly to respond to the tragedy;
40 WHEREAS, the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority (WMATA) provided
41 crucial support by deploying warming buses and extending Silver Line service to assist with
42 diverted flights and emergency operations;
43 WHEREAS, Virginia first responders, including the Virginia State Police and Virginia
44 Emergency Management teams, and Maryland first responders, including divers from Maryland
45 State Police, Prince George's County, Baltimore City, Anne Arundel County, and the Maryland
46 Department of Natural Resources, played a vital role in the search and recovery operations;
47 WHEREAS, federal agencies, including the National Transportation Safety Board
48 (NTSB), U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, Federal Bureau of Investigation's National Capital
49 Response Squad, and the Department of Defense, provided essential support and expertise;
50 WHEREAS, the nature of the operation was highly complex and responders faced
51 extremely challenging conditions, including dangerously cold temperatures and an extraordinary
52 crash scene;
53 WHEREAS, these brave first responders worked diligently to conduct search and
54 recovery operations, demonstrating extraordinary dedication to their mission;
55 WHEREAS, the coordinated efforts of local, state, and federal agencies exemplified the
56 strength of our region's emergency response capabilities and the dedication of our public
57 servants; and
2

58 WHEREAS, these first responders and supporting agencies deserve our deepest gratitude
59 and recognition for their service during and following this tragic event as well as their ongoing
60 commitment to serving and protecting the nation’s capital and the surrounding region.
61 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
62 resolution may be cited as the “January 29, 2025 Midair Collision Response Recognition
63 Resolution of 2025”.
64 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and commends the
65 extraordinary courage, professionalism, and dedication demonstrated by all first responders and
66 supporting agencies which participated in the response to the January 29, 2025, midair collision,
67 and extends its deepest gratitude for their service during this tragic event.
68 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
69 the District of Columbia Register.
