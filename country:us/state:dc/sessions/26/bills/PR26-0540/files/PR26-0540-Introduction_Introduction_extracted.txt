LeRoy Clay III
Mr. LeRoy Clay III is the Associate CFO for the Economic Development
and Regulation Cluster within the Office of the Chief Financial Officer of
the District of Columbia. In this role, he manages the development and
execution of a combined $1.5 billion operating and capital budget for 21
District agencies within the cluster including the Office of the Deputy
Mayor for Planning and Economic Development, the Department of
Licensing and Consumer Protection, the Department of Buildings, and the
Department of Housing and Community Development.
Prior to his current position, Mr. Clay served as the Agency Fiscal Officer
for the Metropolitan Police Department, where he managed the agency’s
$500 million budget. He began his career at Arthur Andersen as an
auditor, where he primarily served clients in the real estate, hospitality, and financial services
industries. His engagements included financial statement audits, audits of internal controls and
compliance, due-diligence projects, IPOs, annual and quarterly SEC filings, and ad hoc consulting
projects. With more than 25 years of progressive experience in finance and accounting, Mr. Clay
has developed expertise in the areas of strategic leadership, financial modeling, financial planning
and analysis, internal and external auditing, business process reengineering, internal control
evaluation and development, and financial accounting.
A Ward 8 resident, Mr. Clay earned his Bachelor of Business Administration in Accounting from
Howard University.
