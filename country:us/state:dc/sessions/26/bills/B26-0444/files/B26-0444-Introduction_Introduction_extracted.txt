Dear Secretary Smith,
Today, I am introducing the Food Policy Council Procurement Amendment Act of 2025 along
with my colleagues Councilmember Christina Henderson, Councilmember Janeese Lewis
George, Councilmember Matthew Frumin, and Councilmember Brooke Pinto. This legislation
would change the reporting timeline for the Food Policy Council to every three years and expand
their authority to evaluate food procurement practices across the District government.
The District spends more than $62 million on food every year across multiple agencies.
Currently, there is no single place in the District to support all food-buying agencies with
complying with and meeting all environmental, nutrition, and economic requirements and goals.
The Food Policy Council currently compiles all food-related legislation and District-wide
commitments made by the Mayor in national or international pacts. They already have federal
funding to begin the work of evaluating our food procurement practices, so this legislation gives
them the local authority to continue this work in the future.
This legislation simplifies the reporting scope and schedule for the Food Policy Council. Most
state and local governments complete a food system assessment every three years, and this
legislation would align District law with that standard in lieu of the current annual report.
Additionally, this legislation will ensure that the Food Policy Council has authority to report on
food jobs, urban agriculture area, and food insecurity—metrics that they are already responsible
for in the Sustainable DC data dashboard.
I look forward to working with my colleagues on the Council and in the Executive to improve
our food procurement strategies to align with our environmental, social, health, and economic
values. Please contact my Deputy Chief of Staff at cshaw@dccouncil.gov if you have any
questions about this legislation.

Sincerely,
Zachary Parker
Ward 5 Councilmember
Chair, Committee on Youth Affairs

26 To amend the Food Policy Council and Director Establishment Act of 2014 to change the
27 reporting timeline and expand the authority of the Food Policy Council to evaluate food
28 procurement practices across the District government.
29
30 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this act may
31 be cited as the “Food Policy Council Procurement Amendment Act of 2025”.
32 Sec. 2. The Food Policy Council and Director Establishment Act of 2014, effective
33 March 10, 2015 (D.C. Law 20-191; D.C. Official Code § 48-311 et seq.), is amended as follows:
34 (a) Section 3(c) is amended to read as follows:
35 “(c) On September 30, 2026, and triennially thereafter, the Food Policy Council shall
36 provide a report to the Council of the District of Columbia on the state of the local food system,
37 including the food economy and food access across the District. The report shall identify national

38 best practices in food policy, assess District food access, including an identification of low food
39 access areas (“food deserts”), assess District food assets, recommend revisions to regulations and
40 policies that affect the local food economy and food access, and identify funding priorities.”.
41 (b) Section 5 is amended as follows:
42 (1) Subsection (b) is amended as follows:
43 (A) Paragraph (5) is amended by striking the phrase “; and” and inserting
44 a semicolon in its place;
45 (B) Paragraph (6) is amended by striking the period and inserting the
46 phrase “; and” in its place.
47 (C) A new paragraph (7) is added to read as follows:
48 “(7) Oversee efforts to improve the quality of institutional food purchased with
49 District local funds, including in schools, jails, and shelters, to make food procured for District
50 institutions more nutritious and sustainable while supporting the local economy.”.
51 (2) A new subsection (c) is added to read as follows:
52 “(c) To achieve the objectives under subsection (b)(7), the Director shall:
53 “(1) Advise food-procuring agencies on developing solicitations and contracts
54 that:
55 “(A) incorporate best practices for nutrition, sustainability, and local food
56 procurement;
57 “(B) include all applicable District laws and standards; and
58 “(C) require vendors to regularly report on purchased food products to
59 enable assessment of compliance with purchasing goals;

60 “(2) Collaborate with the Office of Contracting and Procurement to ensure that
61 food service solicitations reach a wide audience of high-quality prospective vendors;
62 “(3) Serve on evaluation panels to contribute industry and food sector subject
63 matter expertise in procurement determinations;
64 “(4) Consolidate food purchasing data from all food-procuring agencies to track
65 District-wide performance on food procurement goals, including local procurement,
66 sustainability, and nutrition; and
67 “(5) Develop and maintain food procurement standards, practices, and templates
68 to be used in all procurements, in collaboration with DC Health, Department of Small and Local
69 Business Development, the Department of Energy and the Environment, and the Office of
70 Contracting and Procurement.”.
71 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
72 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
73 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
74 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
75 Sec. 4. Effective date.
76 This act shall take effect after approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
77 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of congressional review as
78 provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
79 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)), and publication in the District of
80 Columbia Register.
