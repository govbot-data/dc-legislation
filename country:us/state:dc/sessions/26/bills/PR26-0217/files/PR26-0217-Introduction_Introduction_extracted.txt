13 A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
14 _________________
15
16 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
17 _________________________
18
19 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Minimum Wage
20 Act Revision Act of 1992 to pause the date on which the next increase to the tipped
21 minimum wage takes effect on July 1, 2025.
22
23 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 resolution may be cited as the “Tipped Minimum Wage Increase Clarification Emergency
25 Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
26 Sec. 2. (a) On November 30, 2022, the District of Columbia Board of Elections certified
27 the election results for Initiative Measure No. 82 (“Initiative 82”), the District of Columbia Tip
28 Credit Elimination Act of 2022, (“Act”) effective February 23, 2023 (D.C. Law 24-281; 70 DCR
29 2953).
30 (b) This law gradually increases the tipped minimum wage until tipped wage employees
31 earn the minimum hourly wage by July 1, 2027. As a result, the tipped minimum wage is
32 required to be at least $12.00 per hour beginning on July 1, 2025.
33 (c) On May 5, 2025, the Mayor announced that her Fiscal Year 2026 budget proposal will
34 include the repeal of Initiative 82. Since that time, the Committee on Business and Economic
1

35 Development has received questions and comments from several restaurant operators who
36 expressed confusion about whether the next statutory increase to the tipped minimum wage on
37 July 1, 2025, would still take effect given the news of the proposed repeal.
38 (d) Additionally, on May 20, 2025, the Senate unanimously passed the “No Tax on Tips
39 Act”, which among other things, would establish a new tax deduction of up to $25,000 for cash
40 tips of employees who make under $160,000 in 2025 and adjusted annually for inflation. On
41 May 22, 2025, a time-limited “No Tax on Tips” provision was included in the reconciliation bill
42 passed by the House.
43 (e) To provide clarity to our local businesses, give the Council time to fully consider the
44 Executive’s budget proposal, and time to evaluate a seemingly imminent change in the tax
45 treatment of tips at the federal level, it is imperative that legislation pausing the July 1, 2025
46 tipped minimum wage increase take effect immediately.
47 Sec. 3. The Council determines that the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute
48 emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Tipped Minimum Wage Increase
49 Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
50 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
