21 To amend compulsory school enrollment requirements to permit minors to defer
22 kindergarten enrollment for one year without being considered truant if they meet
23 specified criteria; and to permit parents with a child born June 16 through
24 September 30 with flexibility in applications and enrollment for public school
25 Pre-K3, Pre-K4, and Kindergarten.
26
27 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA,
28 That this act may be cited as the "Kindergarten and Pre-Kindergarten Enrollment
29 Flexibility Act of 2026".
30 TITLE I. COMPULSORY EDUCATION REQUIREMENT AMENDMENTS.
31 Sec. 101. Short title.
32 This title may be cited as the "Parental Choice in Kindergarten Deferral
33 Amendment Act of 2026".
34 Sec. 102. An Act to provide for compulsory school attendance, for the taking of a
35 school census in the District of Columbia, and for other purposes, approved February 4,
36 1925 (43 Stat. 806; D.C. Official Code§ 38-201 et seq.), is amended as follows:

37 (a) Paragraph (4) of Section 1 of Article I is amended by striking the word
38 “September” and inserting the word “August” in its place.
39 (b) Section 1 of Article II is amended as follows
40 (1) Subsection (a) shall be renumbered as paragraph (a)(1).
41 (2) A new paragraph (a)(2) shall be added to read as follows:
42 “(2) Notwithstanding the requirements of paragraph (1) of this subsection,
43 a child who reached the age of 5 years or who will become 5 years of age, on or between
44 the dates of June 16th of the previous school year and September 30th of the current
45 school year, shall be exempt from the compulsory school attendance requirements,
46 provided that:
47 “(A) The parent, guardian, or other person, who resides
48 permanently or temporarily in the District and who has custody or control of the minor:
49 “(i) Notifies DCPS, in writing, of their decision to defer
50 kindergarten enrollment for one year and provides documentation verifying that the child
51 is enrolled:
52 “(I) Full-time in a childcare program licensed by the
53 District of Columbia, Maryland, or Virginia; or
54 “(II) Full-time in a pre-kindergarten class at a public
55 school, public charter school, or licensed private school in the District of Columbia,
56 Maryland, or Virginia; and
57 “(ii) The minor maintains enrollment in an institution
58 described in sub-subparagraph (i) of this subparagraph.
2

59 “(B) The minor established residency in the District of Columbia
60 after the start date of the school year for kindergarten at DCPS.”.
61 TITLE II. KINDERGARTEN AND PRE-KINDERGARTEN APPLICATION
62 PROCESS AMENDMENTS.
63 Sec. 101. Short title.
64 This title may be cited as the "Flexibility in Kindergarten and Pre-Kindergarten
65 Applications Act of 2026".
66 Sec. 102. Notwithstanding D.C. Official Code § 38-781.02(b)(1) any parent,
67 guardian, or other person, who resides permanently or temporarily in the District and who
68 has custody or control of the minor, shall have the following rights in public school
69 enrollment and applications:
70 (a) A child who reached the age of 4 years or who will become 4 years of age, on
71 or between the dates of June 16th of the previous school year and September 30th of the
72 current school year, shall be allowed to enroll in either Pre-K3 or Pre-K4 at DCPS or DC
73 public charter schools;
74 (b A child who reached the age of 5 years or who will become 5 years of age, on
75 or between the dates of June 16th of the previous school year and September 30th of the
76 current school year, shall be allowed to enroll in either Pre-K4 or kindergarten at DCPS
77 or DC public charter schools; and
78 (c) A child who reached the age of 6 years or who will become 6 years of age, on
79 or between the dates of June 16th of the previous school year and September 30th of the
80 current school year, shall be allowed to enroll in either kindergarten or first grade at
81 DCPS or DC public charter schools.
3

82 Sec. 103. The District shall update the public school lottery application process to
83 permit parents the ability to apply for enrollment in accordance with Section 102 of this
84 Act.
