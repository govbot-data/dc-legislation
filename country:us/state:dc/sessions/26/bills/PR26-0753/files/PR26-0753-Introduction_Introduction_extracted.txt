15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend An Act Making
16 appropriations to provide for the expenses of the government of the District of Columbia
17 for the fiscal year ending June thirtieth, nineteen hundred and fourteen and for other
18 purposes, to extend the holdover period for commissioners of the Public Service
19 Commission.
20
21 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 resolution may be cited as the “Public Service Commission Holdover Period Extension Emergency
23 Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
24 Sec. 2. (a) The Public Service Commission of the District of Columbia (“PSC”) is the
25 District’s energy and telecommunications regulator. The PSC is led by three commissioners who
26 are appointed by the Mayor with the advice and consent of the Council. The current commissioners
27 are Chairman Emile C. Thompson, and commissioners Ted Trabue, Jr. and Richard A. Beverly.
28 Charmain Thompson and Commissioner Trabue’s terms are set to expire on June 30, 2026.
29 (b) On January 6, 2026, PR26-0500, the Public Service Commission Emile Thompson
30 Confirmation Resolution of 2026, and PR26-0501, the Public Service Commission Ted Trabue, Jr.
31 Confirmation Resolution of 2026, were introduced by the Chairman at the request of the Mayor.
32 The measures would have reappointed Chairman Thompson and Commissioner Trabue to new
33 four-year terms to end on June 30, 2030. The measures were initially referred to the Committee of
1

34 the Whole on January 20, 2026, but were ultimately re-referred to the Committee on Transportation
35 and the Environment on February 17, 2026. The Committee on Transportation and the
36 Environment did not hold a hearing on or approve the measures. On April 22, 2026, the Mayor
37 withdrew the measures from consideration before they were deemed disapproved.
38 (c) PR26-0668, the Public Service Commission Emile Thompson Confirmation Resolution
39 of 2026, and PR26-0669, the Public Service Commission Ted Trabue, Jr. Confirmation Resolution
40 of 2026, were introduced by the Chairman at the request of the Mayor on April 22, 2026. Like the
41 previously introduced resolutions, these measures would reappoint Chairman Thompson and
42 Commissioner Trabue to new four-year terms to end on June 30, 2030. The measures were referred
43 to the Committee on Transportation and the Environment on May 5, 2026, and will be deemed
44 disapproved on October 6, 2026, unless the Council approves the resolution.
45 (d) Section 2(c) of the Confirmation Act of 1978, effective March 3, 1979 (D.C. Law 2-
46 142: D.C. Official Code § 1-523.01(c)) provides that “[n]o person shall serve in a hold-over
47 capacity for longer than 180 days after the expiration of the term to which he or she was appointed”
48 for offices that are appointed by the Mayor with the advice and consent of the Council. Given that
49 their current terms are set to expire on June 30, 2026, Chairman Thompson and Commissioner
50 Trabue’s holdover period under current law would end on December 27, 2026.
51 (e) Over the last year, energy costs in the District have risen more than any other state in
52 the country. As the District’s energy utility regulator, the PSC has presided over the very cases that
53 have allowed these rate increases to occur. The PSC needs new leadership to navigate the utility
54 affordability crisis taking place in the District. A new Mayor will be inaugurated in January 2027,
55 and they should determine who is best qualified to lead the PSC for the next four years.
2

56 (f) This emergency legislation is now necessary to extend the holdover period from 180
57 days to 270 days to allow the current commissioners to continue to serve while the next Mayor
58 determines their successors.
59 Sec. 3. The Council finds the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency
60 circumstances making it necessary that the “Public Service Commission Holdover Period
61 Extension Emergency Amendment Act of 2026” be adopted after a single reading.
62 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
