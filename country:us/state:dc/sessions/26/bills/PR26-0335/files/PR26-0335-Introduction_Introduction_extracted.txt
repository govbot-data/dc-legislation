16 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the District of
17 Columbia Public Works Act of 1954 to expressly provide that an owner or occupant of
18 property in the District shall pay an impervious area charge that DC Water assesses; and to
19 amend the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of Public Works
20 Reorganization Act of 1996 to clarify that DC Water may assess an impervious area charge
21 on any property in the District.
22
23 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 resolution may be cited as the “CRIAC Clarification Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
25 Sec. 2. (a) In 2009, the Council passed the Water and Sewer Authority Equitable
26 Ratemaking Amendment Act of 2008, effective March 25, 2009 (D.C. Law 17-30; 56 DCR 1350),
27 which authorized the District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority (DC Water) to use a billing
28 methodology that takes into account the amount of impervious surface on a property. Since then,
29 DC Water has been assessing the Clean Rivers impervious area charge (“CRIAC”) against
30 property owners in the District.
31 (b) The CRIAC funds upgrades the District’s combined sewer system, as mandated by a
32 consent order between the District and the federal government, to prevent overflows during heavy
1

33 rains that impair the region’s water quality and health. This federally-mandated project is known
34 as the Clean Rivers Project.
35 (b) Two lawsuits before the D.C. Court of Appeals challenge DC Water’s authority to levy
36 the CRIAC against property owners who are not connected to the District’s sewer system.
37 However, the Council did not intend to exempt property owners not connected to the sewer system
38 from the CRIAC. Such property owners – like parking lot owners – still contribute substantially
39 to stormwater runoff and the overflow of the District’s combined sewer system. Charging such
40 property owners the CRIAC for their contribution to stormwater runoff equitably spreads the
41 burden of upgrading and maintaining the District’s sewer systems. Briefing in one of these appeals
42 is scheduled to close in early November.
43 (c) To resolve any ambiguities regarding the Council’s intent, the Council passed the
44 CRIAC Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2024, effective October 25, 2024 (D.C. Act
45 25-602; 71 DCR 13489) (“emergency legislation”). The emergency legislation expired on January
46 23, 2025. On October 15, 2024, the Council also passed the CRIAC Clarification Temporary
47 Amendment Act of 2024, effective March 7, 2025 (D.C. Law 25-285; 72 DCR 3096) (“temporary
48 legislation”). The temporary legislation is set expire on October 18, 2025.
49 (d) This emergency legislation is therefore necessary to continue clarifying the Council’s
50 following the expiration of the temporary legislation.
51 Sec. 3. The Council determines that the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute
52 emergency circumstances making it necessary that the CRIAC Clarification Emergency
53 Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
54 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
