Statement of Introduction
Closing of a Public Alley in Square 5624, S.O. 17-21054, Act of 2025
February 14, 2025
Today, I am introducing the Closing of a Public Alley in Square 5624, S.O. 17-21054, Act of
2025. This bill would dispose of an unimproved, or “paper”, alley that falls within the boundaries
of the Lee Montessori East End campus in Ward 8, with the goal of ultimately allowing the
school community to create a play space for its students on the unused land.
The Lee Montessori school community moved into the school building located at 2345 R Street
SE at the start of the 2020-2021 school year. At the time, the school building contained only 6
classrooms, limiting student enrollment. School leadership began planning for a campus
expansion, which was completed in the summer of 2024. The addition will allow the student
population to grow by 80, to approximately 305 eventually, and created a “U” shaped school
building. In the open space in the center of the “U” is a paper alley, which leads to nowhere, and
limits the potential uses for the open space in the center of the Lee Montessori East End campus.
Lee Montessori school leadership envisions turning the open and currently unused space in the
center of the campus, including the paper alley, into a play area for students. To facilitate
planning and eventual construction, Lee Montessori would need to acquire the land on which the
paper alley sits, to create the largest possible play space and achieve the best and highest use of
all available land. To support this vision of additional outdoor spaces in which students can play
and learn, I propose disposing of the paper alley through this measure. After passage of this
measure, the school could then acquire the land and develop it.
I am honored to play this part in realizing the school community’s vision for a new play space,
which will promote young scholars’ healthy bodies and minds, and I look forward to working
with my colleagues to advance this measure.
