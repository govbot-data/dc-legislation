13 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
14 ______________
15
16
17 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
18
19 ____________________________
20
21 To recognize, honor, and celebrate the late Felix Sambuchino, Jr. for his contributions to the
22 Glover Park community and the District of Columbia.
23
24 WHEREAS, Felix Sambuchino, though born in Erie, Pennsylvania on July 23, 1945, to
25 Mary Mancini and Felix Sambuchino, became a beloved neighborhood icon in his adopted home
26 of Glover Park in Washington, D.C.;
27
28 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino’s commitment to community began at an early age,
29 helping younger students with their homework and science projects while attending high school;
30
31 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino attended Gannon University and received the Innovative
32 Engineering Award every year while there and many years later passed on his love of science to
33 his grandsons;
34
35 WHEREAS, following graduation, Mr. Sambuchino joined the United States Air Force
36 and was deployed to investigate the sinking of the USS Thresher nuclear submarine;
37
38 WHEREAS, upon leaving the Air Force, Mr. Sambuchino was awarded the Outstanding
39 Achievement Award;
40
41 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino moved to Washington, D.C. in 1969 to join UNIVAC as a
42 computer consultant;

43
44 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino fell in love with his new home and in particular the Glover
45 Park community where he lived for the remainder of his life;
46
47 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino served as the President of the Glover Park Citizens'
48 Association and organized the annual Glover Park Day in June, for which he served as emcee
49 and as a willing sponsor and participant in the dunk tank;
50
51 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino played in the Glover Park Coed Softball League from 1988
52 to 1997, winning three championships;
53
54 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino served as the tech expert for Glover Park Village and
55 computer teacher to senior citizens in the community;
56
57 WHEREAS, every December Mr. Sambuchino served as one of the set-up and operating
58 engineers for the trains that encircled the National Christmas Tree on The Ellipse to the delight
59 of hundreds of children;
60
61 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino traveled around Glover Park and the rest of the city on a
62 green bicycle he called Forrest Green;
63
64 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino passed away on January 18, 2025;
65
66 WHEREAS, Mr. Sambuchino is survived by his wife Brenda, daughter Kiersten, two
67 grandsons, Bradley and Brandon, sister Eve Grimaldi, two younger brothers, Charles and Roy
68 Sambuchino, and many nieces and nephews;
69
70 WHEREAS, with Mr. Sambuchino’s passing, Glover Park and the District of Columbia
71 have lost a community leader who brought joy and learning to and improved the lives of
72 thousands;
73
74 IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF
75 COLUMBIA, that this resolution may be cited as the “Felix Sambuchino, Jr. Posthumous
76 Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
77
78 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes, honors, and celebrates Felix
79 Sambuchino, Jr. for his considerable contributions to the District.
80
81 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
82 the District of Columbia Register.
