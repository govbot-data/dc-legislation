SASHA-GAYE ANGUS
PROFILE
• Real estate development executive with experience in community development, managing and executing residential and
mixed-use real estate development projects with a focus on affordable/mixed income, and multi-layered LIHTC
transactions.
• Demonstrative experience in project management, and the life cycle of mixed income rental projects including master
planning, land development, team assemblage, securing entitlements, managing stakeholder relationships with community
and government agencies, managing third party consultants, negotiating with lenders, investors, nonprofit clients, and
public- private partner relationships.
• Managed, structured, and led multiple LIHTC projects valued over $350MM in development costs and totaling
approximately 1,600 dwelling units. Oversee master planning efforts of over 2000+ dwelling units including Planned Unit
Developments, HOPE VI, and Choice Neighborhood Initiatives (CNI). Deep knowledge of various financing programs
used to develop affordable housing.
• Strong leadership skills, policy analysis, advocacy, and a passion for responsible equitable development.

Sasha-Gaye Angus
Ms. Sasha-Gaye Angus is a leader of affordable housing and community
development with over 23 years of experience in rebuilding communities
for low-income individuals, families, and seniors. She brings executive
experience in leading teams, directing projects, risk management and
building public-private partnerships. She has led multiple housing
projects resulting in over a quarter of a billion dollars invested in
disadvantaged communities.
In, 2020, she joined MANNA, Inc. as President & CEO, from
McCormack Baron Salazar, Inc. where she served as Senior Vice
President. There she managed a diverse team and oversaw the planning
and implementation of large multi-phased neighborhood transformation
projects in Pittsburgh, PA and Baltimore, MD totaling 2,000+ affordable/mixed-income units
combined. Throughout her career, she has held leadership roles with national developers of
affordable housing and has served on numerous boards including the Community Design Center
of Pittsburgh, and Housing Association of Nonprofit Developers. She currently serves as a Board
of Director with the National NeighborWorks Association.
A Ward 5 resident, Ms. Angus earned her Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science and Public
Administration from Fisk University and her Master’s degree in International Affairs from the
University of Pittsburgh.
