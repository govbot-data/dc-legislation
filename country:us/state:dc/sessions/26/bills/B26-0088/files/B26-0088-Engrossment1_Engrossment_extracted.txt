12 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Open Meetings Act of 2010 to provide that a meeting
13 shall be deemed open to the public if the public body takes steps reasonably calculated to
14 allow the public to view or hear the meeting while the meeting is taking place, or, if
15 doing so is not technologically feasible, as soon thereafter as reasonably practicable.
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
18 act may be cited as the “Virtual Open Meetings Authority Extension Temporary Amendment Act
19 of 2025”.
20 Sec. 2. The Open Meetings Act of 2010, effective March 31, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-350;
21 D.C. Official Code § 2-571 et seq.), is amended as follows:
22 (a) Section 405(a) (D.C. Official Code § 2-575(a)) is amended as follows:
23 (1) Paragraph (2) is amended by striking the phrase “; or” and inserting a
24 semicolon in its place.
25 (2) Paragraph (3) is amended by striking the period and inserting the phrase “; or”
26 in its place.
27 (3) A new paragraph (4) is added to read as follows:
28 “(4) The public body takes steps reasonably calculated to allow the public to view

37 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact

41 (a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by
42 the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), and a 30-day period of congressional
43 review as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved
44 December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813, D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)).
45 (b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.
