Justine Kreso
Ms. Justine Kreso has been the Firearms Section supervisor at the
Onondaga County Center for Forensic Sciences in Syracuse, New York
since 2012 and began working as a Firearms Examiner in 2004.
Ms. Kreso has served as a member of the Association of Firearm and Tool
Mark Examiners (AFTE) since 2006 and a Distinguished Member since
2010. To date, she has served on ten committees, which currently include
the Scientific Advancement Committee, the Mentoring Ad-Hoc
Committee, the 2025 Training Seminar Host Committee, and the Social
Media Subcommittee. She also spent six years as a member of the AFTE
Board of Directors, has published papers in the AFTE Journal, taught
workshops, and presented at annual training seminars.
In addition, Ms. Kreso has served on the Firearms and Toolmarks Subcommittee of the
Organization of Scientific Area Committees (OSAC) since 2022 and is currently an OSAC Firearm
and Toolmarks Subcommittee Officer. Moreover, she is a member of the Executive Board for the
3D Tool Mark Technologies Technical Working Group. Ms. Kreso serves on the New York State
Technical Working Group for Firearms and formerly served on New York State’s Backlog
Reduction Technical Working Group. Previously, she was a member of the National Integrated
Ballistics Information Network (NIBIN) Executive Board and acted as a Regional User’s Congress
Representative. Ms. Kreso is also a current technical assessor for the ANSI National Accreditation
Board (ANAB).
A resident of New York, Ms. Kreso earned her Bachelor of Science in Biology from the University
of New Mexico.
