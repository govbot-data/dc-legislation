Dear Secretary Smith,
We are jointly introducing “Eric’s ID Amendment Act of 2026.” Please see enclosed a signed
copy of the legislation.
As we recognize the beginning of Developmental Disabilities Awareness Month, this legislation
is especially important as it serves to create more equity for our disabled neighbors. This
legislation will provide protections to our residents who have hidden disabilities, and ensure that
our police department engages in interactions appropriate for these residents. This bill is the
result of meaningful collaboration and support from the family behind Eric’s ID Law1 in
Maryland – Eric and Linda Carpenter-Grantham – and the District Department of Motor
Vehicles. We are proud to introduce this legislation with our colleagues Councilmember Charles
Allen, Councilmember Brooke Pinto, Councilmember Brianne K. Nadeau, Councilmember Doni
Crawford, Councilmember Christina Henderson, Councilmember Robert C. White, Jr., and
Councilmember Anita Bonds.
This legislation serves to protect individuals with nonapparent disabilities by:
• Allowing individuals to choose to indicate that they have a nonapparent disability on
their license, permit, or identification card.
• Defining a nonapparent disability as a disability or health condition that is not
immediately obvious or visible, including developmental disabilities, intellectual
disabilities, and immunodeficiency conditions.
• Requiring the District Department of Transportation to coordinate with the Office of
Disability Rights to conduct public outreach efforts.
• Prohibiting the District Department of Transportation from disclosure of information
related to an individual’s nonapparent disability except in certain circumstances.

• Prohibiting the District Department of Transportation from charging fees to add or

• Requiring the Metropolitan Police Department to implement training concerning

The National Association of Councils on Developmental Disabilities estimates that, as of 2023,
the total estimated disability population in the District was 11.5 %, or approximately 77,200
residents.2 Of those residents, about 4.5% had developmental disabilities.3 Interactions with
disabled people can take place anywhere, and these interactions are not unique to any particular
city, state, or country. This legislation will expand the reach of Eric’s ID law, which was enacted
in Maryland in September 2025. Similar versions of this bill have since been introduced in
Delaware and Georgia. We must continue to champion the rights of all of our residents to dignity
and respect, and this legislation continues that journey.

36 To amend section 7 of the District of Columbia Traffic Act, 1925, to allow applicants for a
37 license, permit, or identification card to choose to indicate on the document the
38 applicant’s nonapparent disability, to designate a butterfly as the nonapparent disability
39 notation, to establish public outreach efforts, to authorize an applicant to delete their
40 nonapparent disability notation, to prohibit requiring proof of a nonapparent disability,
41 to prohibit denial of an application due to certain circumstances related to information
42 about a nonapparent disability, to prohibit charging additional fees for applicants who
43 choose to include or remove their nonapparent disability on an initial or renewal

44 application, to prohibit disclosure of certain information, and to require the
45 Metropolitan Police Department to implement training concerning interactions with
46 individuals with nonapparent disabilities.
47
48 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
49 be cited as the “Eric’s ID Amendment Act of 2026”.
50 Sec. 2. Section 7 of the District of Columbia Traffic Act, 1925, approved March 3,
51 1925 (43 Stat. 1121; D.C. Official Code § 50-1401.01), is amended by adding a new
52 subsection (i-1) to read as follows:
53 “(i-1)(1) On any initial or renewal application for a license, permit, or identification card
54 issued pursuant to this section, section 8a, or section 8c, an applicant may choose to indicate that
55 the applicant has a nonapparent disability.
56 “(A) For any applicant that indicates that the applicant has a nonapparent
57 disability, a nonapparent disability notation shall be placed on the front of the license, permit, or
58 identification card.
59 “(B) Corresponding text indicating a nonapparent disability shall be
60 placed on the back of the license, permit, or identification card.
61 “(C) The Department shall not require an applicant to identify the
62 applicant’s nonapparent disability.
63 “(2) This indication shall not affect an applicant’s ability to add a special
64 indicator, as authorized by the Department, to the back of a license, permit, or identification card.
65 “(3) The Department shall consult with the Office of Disability Rights to establish
66 public outreach efforts on the availability of a nonapparent disability notation.
67 “(4) The Department shall designate a butterfly symbol as the nonapparent
68 disability notation.

69 “(5) The Department shall delete records of an individual’s nonapparent disability
70 and the notation from the individual’s license, permit, or identification card upon request from
71 the individual.
72 “(6) The Department shall not:
73 “(A) Require proof of the nonapparent disability before approving the
74 nonapparent disability notation;
75 “(B) Deny an application for a license, permit, or identification card
76 because the nonapparent disability selected by the applicant does not match the nonapparent
77 disability indicated on another document associated with the applicant;
78 “(C) During the processing of an initial or renewal application, collect an
79 additional fee to include or remove a notation of a nonapparent disability on a license, permit, or
80 identification or to delete records of an individual’s nonapparent disability notation;
81 “(D) Use information disclosed by an individual under this section as a
82 basis to require the individual to undergo a driver license medical review process; or
83 “(E) Disclose records pertaining to an individual’s election of having a
84 nonapparent disability, except to:
85 “(i) A court;
86 “(ii) A criminal justice agency upon a court order;
87 “(iii) A driver licensing authority;
88 “(iv) The applicant;
89 “(v) The applicant’s attorney;
90 “(vi) Any third party designated by the applicant; or
91 “(vii) The Child and Family Servies Agency.

92 “(7) Within the fiscal year following the effective date of this act, the
93 Metropolitan Police Department shall implement training concerning interactions with
94 individuals who have a nonapparent disability notation on their license, permit, or identification
95 card.
96 “(8) For the purposes of this subsection, the term “nonapparent disability” means
97 a disability or health condition that is not immediately obvious or visible, including
98 developmental disabilities and intellectual disabilities.”.
