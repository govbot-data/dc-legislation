Dear Secretary Howard,
Today, I am introducing the “Henry E. Baker Alley Designation Act of 2025”, which designates the alley within
Square 2857, bounded by Harvard Street N.W., Sherman Avenue N.W., and Girard Street N.W., and 11th Street,
N.W. in Ward 1, as Henry E. Baker Alley to recognize Henry Edwin Baker for his pioneering efforts in recording
and publicizing the achievements of Black inventors and innovators.
In 1874, Henry Baker became the third African American to enter the United States Naval Academy. While at
the Naval Academy, Baker faced racial discrimination and property damage, leading to his departure from the
Academy. He completed his education at the Ben-Hyde Benton School of Technology in Washington D.C. and later
graduated from the Howard University School of Law in 1881.
While working at the U.S. Patent Office as a copyist, Baker noticed a significant gap in the records regarding
Black inventors. This motivated Baker to dedicate himself to documenting and promoting their contributions. He
gained success at the Patent Office and was promoted to Second Assistant Examiner, remaining committed
throughout his career to highlighting Black inventors. Baker sent letters to patent attorneys, company executives,
newspaper editors, and prominent Black leaders to gather patent information, ultimately identifying more than 1,200
Black innovators. Of these, only 800 of them agreed to have their identities disclosed, as many feared that exposure
would negatively affect their businesses.
Henry Baker authored several publications showcasing the work of these inventors, including a pamphlet
published in 1913 to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the Emancipation Proclamation. Thanks to Henry
Baker’s dedication, the names of many remarkable Black innovators have been preserved, allowing us to properly
recognize their contributions today.
As a resident of Columbia Heights, neighbors and the community are proud to honor the resiliency of Henry
Baker and his crucial role in chronicling Black innovation. Attached to this letter is a copy of a resolution from
Advisory Neighborhood Commission 1A in support of this naming.
Sincerely,
Brianne K. Nadeau
Councilmember, Ward 1
