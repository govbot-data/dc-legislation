Antonio Williams
Mr. Antonio Williams currently serves as Vice President of Local
Government Affairs and Advocacy for Comcast NBCUniversal. In this
role he helps county and municipal governments find commonsense
solutions to complex problems.
Previously, Mr. Williams led External Affairs for Comcast NBCU where
he partnered with civil rights, consumer, and community organizations to
expand free speech, civil rights, and economic inclusion within
Technology and Media.
He is also a creator of Comcast’s Internet Essentials program, the largest
non-government broadband adoption program in the world, connecting
over 10 million low-income Americans to high-speed Internet.
Mr. Williams serves as the board chair of DC Public Libraries Board of Library Trustees, the
Washington Leadership Academy, and Black Tech Futures. He is also a member of the board of
Joint Center for Political and Economic Studies, America’s Black think tank.
Prior to his work with Comcast, Mr. Williams worked for Senator Norm Coleman and the U.S.
Department of Veterans Affairs. He is a 2016 Presidential Leadership Scholar, a 2020 Seeding
Disruption Fellow, and a graduate of the NAMIC Executive Leadership Development Program at
the University of Virginia’s Darden School of Business.
A Ward 4 resident, Mr. Williams earned his Bachelor of Arts in International Studies, History and
Philosophy from Macalester College and his Master of Arts in Communications from American
University.
