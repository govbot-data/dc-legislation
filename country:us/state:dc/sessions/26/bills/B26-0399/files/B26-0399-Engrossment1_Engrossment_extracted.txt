11 To amend Chapter 23 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia Official Code to establish an
12 additional permanency pathway that includes a circle of caring adults who provide
13 support and legal relationships for young people ages 16 and older as they transition from
14 foster care to adulthood.

17 act may be cited as the “Support, Opportunity, Unity, Legal Relationships (SOUL) Amendment
18 Act of 2025”.

42 “§ 16-2399.21. Definitions.
43 “For purposes of this subchapter, the term:
44 “(1) “SOUL Caregiver” means an adult who is not the youth’s birth parent and who is
45 committed to providing housing for the youth, meeting the basic needs of the youth, and holding

48 “(2) “Parent and child relationship” is as defined in §16–2352(a-1)
49 “(3) “SOUL” means a permanency plan for a youth in the care and custody of the Agency
50 that allows the youth to establish a legal relationship with a SOUL family, without terminating
51 existing familial legal relationships.
52 “(4) “SOUL Supporter” means an adult who is committed to holding rights and
53 responsibilities to care for the youth as specified in the SOUL family Agreement.
54 “(5) SOUL family” means a legal relationship between a youth, a caregiver and either a
55 caregiver or SOUL supporter. The SOUL family may include additional caregivers and SOUL

58 “(A) Is sixteen years of age or older;
59 “(B) Has been adjudicated to be neglected pursuant to § 16-2317; and

96 “§ 16.2399.24. Allocation of Rights and Responsibilities.
97 “(a) Unless the court specifies otherwise, one or more SOUL caregivers shall maintain
98 physical custody of the youth until they are 18 years of age.
99 “(b) The SOUL family shall determine the allocation of the following legal custody rights
100 amongst the members of the SOUL family until the youth is 18 years of age:

102 “(2) The right to make educational decisions when a decision by a parent is

104 “(3) Access to information related to the youth’s physical or mental health;
105 “(4) Consent to health care when consent of a parent is required by law,

118 rights or capacity to make education, medical, or financial decisions for themselves.

208 “§ 16-2399.29. Adjudicatory Hearings.
209 “(a) An adjudicatory hearing on the motion to establish a SOUL family shall be
210 scheduled to occur within 90 days following the filing of the motion.
211 “(b) The Family Court shall begin the adjudicatory hearing by determining whether all
212 parties are present and whether proper notice of the hearing has been given.

213 “(1) If a parent has been given proper notice but fails to appear, the Family Court
214 may proceed in the parent’s absence.
215 “(2) The Family Court may not proceed without the youth or any of the members
216 of the proposed SOUL family.
217 “(c) Each party shall have the right to present evidence, to be heard on his or her own
218 behalf, and to cross-examine witnesses called by another party. The Family Court may direct the
219 Agency to submit a summary report or provide testimony establishing that the Agency
220 completed background and safety checks and addressed any safety-related concerns.
221 “(d) The moving party, the youth, shall have the burden of proving by a preponderance of
222 the evidence that the proposed SOUL family is in the youth’s best interest.
223 “(e) In considering whether to grant the motion to establish the proposed SOUL family,
224 the Family Court shall take into account all relevant, material, and competent evidence presented
225 at the adjudicatory hearing. Neither the spouse or domestic partner privilege nor the physician-
226 client or mental health professional-client privilege shall be a ground for excluding evidence.
227 “(f) In its determination of whether to grant the motion to establish the proposed SOUL
228 family, the Family Court shall consider whether the proposed SOUL family is in the youth’s best
229 interest, based on the following factors:
230 “(1) The youth’s need for a stable and permanent home, adequate care that meets
231 their specific needs, and consistent and reliable caregivers;

232 “(2) The physical, mental, and emotional health of all individuals involved to the
233 degree that each affects the welfare of the youth, the decisive consideration being the physical,
234 mental, and emotional needs of the youth;
235 “(3) The quality of interaction and interrelationship of the youth with their
236 parent(s), siblings, relatives, caregivers, and the proposed SOUL family members;
237 “(4) The terms of the proposed SOUL family agreement, including whether:
238 “(A) The allocation of rights and responsibilities amongst the members of

245 “(5) The youth’s opinion of their own best interests in the matter; and
246 “(6) The impact on the youth of remaining in the care and custody of the Agency.
