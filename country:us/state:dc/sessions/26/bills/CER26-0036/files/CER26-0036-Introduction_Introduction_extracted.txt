7 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
8
9 _________________________
10
11
12 To remember, acknowledge and condemn the Holocaust and to declare April 24, 2025 as Yom
13 Ha’Shoah or Holocaust Remembrance Day.
14 WHEREAS, the Holocaust was a dark period in human history between 1933 and 1945
15 marked by the systematic persecution and annihilation by Nazi Germany and their collaborators
1

16 of six million Jews and another six million individuals targeted and victimized for their ethnicity,
17 religious beliefs, disabilities, sexual orientation, political ideology, or nationality;
18 WHEREAS, it is our moral responsibility to educate future generations about the Holocaust
19 and its devastating impact on the world, and to work towards creating a society that is free from
20 prejudice, bigotry, and discrimination;
21 WHEREAS, April 24, 2025 marks Yom Ha’Shoah or Holocaust Remembrance Day, a
22 solemn day of reflection and remembrance for the victims of the Holocaust and an opportunity for
23 us to reaffirm our commitment to human rights and dignity;
24 WHEREAS, the District of Columbia stands united in its condemnation of all forms of
25 hatred, prejudice, and discrimination, and remains committed to promoting a culture of respect,
26 understanding, and acceptance; and
27 WHEREAS, the Jewish Community Relations Council of Greater Washington and 3GDC
28 are hosting Yom Ha’Shoah Commemorations to honor the lives of those affected and to remember
29 their courage and strength.
30 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
31 resolution may be cited as the “Holocaust Remembrance Day Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
32 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes Holocaust Remembrance Day
33 and its significance in remembering the victims of the Holocaust and promoting the values of
34 justice, equality, and human dignity.
35 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
