16 To designate the District of Columbia as the “Official Sports Capital of the United
17 States” and to require the Washington Convention and Sports Authority (Events DC) to develop
18 and implement a plan to promote, support, and enhance the District’s status as the nation’s
19 premier destination for sports events, teams, and activities.
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
22 act may be cited as the “Official Sports Capital of the United States Designation Act of 2025”.
23 Sec. 2. Findings.
24 (a) The District of Columbia is home to numerous professional, collegiate, and amateur
25 sports teams, including but not limited to the Washington Wizards, Washington Mystics,
26 Washington Nationals, Washington Capitals, D.C. United, Washington Spirit, D.C. Defenders,
27 Old Glory DC and the D.C. Breeze.
28 (b) The District has hosted premier national and international sporting events, including
29 the MLB All-Star Game, NBA All-Star Game, NHL Winter Classic, WNBA All-Star Game,
30 World Cup qualifiers, and the Army-Navy Game, showcasing its capacity as a global sports hub.
1

31 (c) The District’s unique status as the nation’s capital makes it the most fitting city to
32 serve as the “Official Sports Capital of the United States,” given its accessibility, infrastructure,
33 and sports culture.
34 (d) The District continues to invest in world-class sports venues such as Capital One
35 Arena, Nationals Park, Audi Field, Robert F. Kennedy Memorial Stadium Campus and the
36 CareFirst Arena, further enhancing its reputation as a premier sports destination.
37 (e) The District plays a critical role in advancing equity and inclusivity in sports,
38 fostering youth participation through programs like the Department of Park’s and Recreation’s
39 community athletics initiatives, and supporting diversity in sports leadership and opportunities.
40 (f) Official designation as the “Official Sports Capital of the United States” will boost
41 tourism, economic development, and national recognition of the District’s rich sports culture and
42 history.
43 Sec. 3. Designation.
44 (a) The District of Columbia is hereby designated as the only “Official Sports Capital of
45 the United States.”
46 (b) Events DC shall develop and implement a plan to promote and enhance the District’s
47 status as the Official Sports Capital of the United States, which shall include:
48 (1) Initiatives to attract and retain major sporting events, conferences,
49 conventions, and tournaments;
50 (2) Strategies to increase investment in youth sports and community athletic
51 programs;
2

52 (3) Partnerships with professional, collegiate, and amateur teams to engage
53 residents and visitors; and
54 (4) Marketing and branding efforts to highlight the District’s role as the nation’s
55 premier sports hub.
56 (c) No later than 120 days after the effective date of this act, Washington Convention and
57 Sports Authority (Events DC) shall submit to the Council a report detailing the implementation
58 of the plan required under subsection (b).
59 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
60 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
61 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
62 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
63 Sec. 5. Effective date.
64 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
65 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of Congressional review as
66 provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
67 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Code § 1-206(c)(1)), and publication in the District of Columbia
68 Register.
