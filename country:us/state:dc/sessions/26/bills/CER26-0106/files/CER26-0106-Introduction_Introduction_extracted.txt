34 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
35
36 _________________________
37
38 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
39
40 _________________________
41
42
43 To celebrate the 20th anniversary of the District of Columbia Access to Justice Commission and
44 recognize its contributions to accessible civil justice for District residents and beyond.
45
1

46 WHEREAS, the District of Columbia Access to Justice Commission was created by the
47 D.C. Court of Appeals and “charged with responsibility of assuring high quality access for low-
48 and moderate-income residents and others in the District of Columbia who suffer disparate access
49 barriers to the civil justice system, and with raising the profile in our community of the need for
50 equal access to justice”;
51 WHEREAS, the Commission is celebrating the 20th anniversary of the appointment of the
52 first Commissioners in February 2005;
53 WHEREAS, the Commission has published two seminal studies on the state of civil access
54 to justice in the District of Columbia;
55 WHEREAS, the Commission's work has led to marked improvements in the justice
56 experience of low- and moderate-income residents by offering recommendations that have
57 strengthened the process and administration of civil justice;
58 WHEREAS, the Commission successfully advocated for the creation in fiscal year 2007
59 and maintenance and growth of the Access to Justice Initiative that has led to the District
60 government's investment in free civil legal services for low-income D.C. residents in a wide range
61 of legal areas;
62 WHEREAS, the Access to Justice Initiative has in past years served upwards of 40,000
63 District residents’ civil legal needs in a single year alone, thereby playing a vital role in protecting
64 the District’s most vulnerable populations by funding civil legal service projects, fortifying
65 community stability, and achieving the Council’s long-term public safety goals while reducing
66 reliance on other, more costly governmental programs;
2

67 WHEREAS, the Commission has made clear that where residents are unable to access civil
68 legal aid services, they are more likely to suffer long-term financial harms and to be unable to
69 vindicate individual legal rights when those have been unfairly or unduly infringed upon;
70 WHEREAS, the Commission has championed efforts to promote private investment in free
71 civil legal services through its Raising the Bar in D.C. campaign and efforts to promote pro bono
72 service at District law firms;
73 WHEREAS, the Commission continues to play a unique and critical role in bringing the
74 civil legal justice community together to promote collaboration, coordination, and innovation;
75 WHEREAS, through its Justice for All priorities, the Commission is setting a path for
76 ensuring that all District residents facing a civil legal problem get 100% access to some form of
77 legal help; and
78 WHEREAS, the Council is especially grateful to Professor Peter B. Edelman, who has
79 served as Chair of the Commission since its inception.
80 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
81 resolution may be cited as the “District of Columbia Access to Justice Commission 20th
82 Anniversary Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
83 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates the District of
84 Columbia Access to Justice Commission for its 20 years of commitment and public service to
85 ensure access to justice for District residents and beyond.
86 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
87 the District of Columbia Register.
