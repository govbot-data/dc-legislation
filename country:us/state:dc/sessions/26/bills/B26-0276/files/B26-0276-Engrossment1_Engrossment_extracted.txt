12 To amend, on a temporary basis, Article II of An Act To provide for compulsory school
13 attendance, for the taking of a school census in the District of Columbia, and for other
14 purposes to direct the Mayor to establish a truancy pilot for the purpose of referring
15 students with unexcused absences to the Department of Human Services and
16 documenting attendance and academic outcomes of students receiving interventions from
17 the Department of Human Services.
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
20 act may be cited as the “Pilot Truancy Reduction Temporary Amendment Act of 2025.”
21 Sec. 2. Section 7 of Article II of An Act To provide for compulsory school attendance, for
22 the taking of a school census in the District of Columbia, and for other purposes, effective
23 September 19, 2013 (D.C. Law 20-17; D.C. Official Code § 38-208), is amended by adding a
24 new subsection (c-1) to read as follows:
25 “(c-1)(1)(A) By August 12, 2024, the Mayor shall identify 5 secondary educational
26 institutions in the District that had a truancy rate greater than 50% in the 2023-2024 school year
27 to participate in a truancy pilot with the Department of Human Services (“DHS”) during School
28 Year 2024-2025.

29 “(B) The Mayor shall notify the identified educational institutions of their
30 participation in the truancy pilot at least 10 business days before the first day of instruction in
31 School Year 2024-2025. The notice shall include appropriate agency contacts, timelines, and
32 procedures for complying with paragraph (2) of this subsection.
33 “(2) The 5 secondary educational institutions identified pursuant to paragraph (1)
34 of this subsection shall refer each student who is 14 years of age through 17 years of age to DHS
35 no later than 2 school days after the accrual of 15 unexcused full school day absences within a
36 school year.
37 “(3) By March 31, 2025, DHS shall publish a preliminary report, and by August
38 15, 2025, DHS shall publish a final report that:
39 “(A) Describes the interventions and services provided through the
40 truancy pilot;
41 “(B) Provides the 5 most common reasons for unexcused absences for the
42 students referred to DHS, such as housing instability, transportation issues, or medical
43 emergencies;
44 “(C) Presents aggregate data on the 5 most common truancy intervention
45 services or programs that students referred to DHS utilized; and
46 “(D) Provides an analysis that compares:

47 “(i) The attendance outcomes, academic performance, and
48 delinquency status of students referred to DHS to his or her attendance outcomes, academic
49 performance, and delinquency status during the same time period in the prior school year;
50 “(ii) The attendance outcomes, academic performance, and
51 delinquency status of students referred to DHS before and after the DHS referral during the
52 applicable time period for School Year 2024-2025; and
53 “(iii) The attendance outcomes and academic performance of
54 educational institutions participating in the truancy pilot with the attendance and academic
55 performance during School Year 2024-2025 of non-participating secondary educational
56 institutions that are socio-demographically similar to participating educational institutions.
57 “(4) For purposes of paragraph (3) of this subsection, the term “delinquency
58 status” means whether a minor student was arrested by a law enforcement official during the
59 time period analyzed in the report.
60 “(5) Educational institutions participating in the truancy pilot shall be exempt
61 from the requirements of subsection (c) of this section for minor students who are 14 through 17
62 years of age.”.
