33 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
34 __________
35
36 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
37 __________
38
39
40 To officially declare May 1st, 2025, as DC Statehood Day in the District of Columbia.
41
42 WHEREAS, the District of Columbia’s more than 700,000 citizens pay more federal income
43 taxes than 22 states, and more federal income taxes per capita than any state;
44
45 WHEREAS, the United States Constitution, as ratified in 1788, provided the right to vote for
46 representation in Congress to the “People of the several States” of the United States, which included
47 citizens living on the land that would later be designated by the federal government for the nation’s
48 capital as ceded by Maryland and Virginia to become the District of Columbia;

49
50 WHEREAS, despite the provision of the right of vote in the Constitution, through the enactment
51 of the “District of Columbia Organic Act of 1801”, DC residents were disenfranchised from voting for
52 representative Members of Congress more than 200 years ago;
53
54 WHEREAS, the District of Columbia has more citizens than the States of Vermont and
55 Wyoming, and almost as many as Alaska and North Dakota;
56
57 WHEREAS, the citizens of the District of Columbia continue to express their determination to
58 achieve full representation by electing two United States Senators and a United States Representative to
59 advocate for statehood and full democracy;
60
61 WHEREAS, citizens of the District of Columbia bear all the responsibilities of citizenship,
62 including fighting in wars and serving in the military, paying federal income taxes, and serving on federal
63 juries;
64
65 WHEREAS, no other democratic nation denies basic rights of self-government, including
66 participation in the national legislature, to residents of its capital;
67
68 WHEREAS, the United Nations Human Rights Committee has released statements expressing
69 concern over this denial of representation to citizens in DC;
70
71 WHEREAS, the Organization of American States (OAS), to which the United States is a
72 signatory, has described the disenfranchisement of DC residents as a violation of its charter agreement;
73
74 WHEREAS, Congress often interferes in legislation passed by DC’s locally elected officials by
75 enacting spending restrictions on DC revenue, violating the fundamental principle that state and local
76 governments are best suited to follow the will of their citizens;
77
78 WHEREAS, despite nearly three decades of balanced budgets, DC faces the possibility each year
79 of being shut down because of congressional interference, threatening both DC and national security;
80
81 WHEREAS, it has been more than 60 years since the passage of the Civil Rights Act, and more
82 than 50 years since the District of Columbia was granted limited home rule, and the residents of the
83 District of Columbia have yet to receive the same rights as the residents of the 50 states.
84
85 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this resolution
86 may be cited as the “DC Statehood Day Recognition Resolution of 2025.”
87
88 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia hereby declares May 1st, 2025 to be DC
89 Statehood Day and encourages leaders and residents to discuss statehood for the District of Columbia in
90 high-profile settings to educate Americans regarding the lack of congressional voting representation and
91 full budget autonomy for the residents of the District.
92
93 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
94 District of Columbia Register.
