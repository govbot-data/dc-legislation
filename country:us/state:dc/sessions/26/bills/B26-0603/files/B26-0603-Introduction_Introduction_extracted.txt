Dear Secretary Howard,
Today, I, along with Chairman Mendelson, am introducing the “Body-Worn Camera Transparency
for Use of Force Amendment Act of 2026.” This bill would enhance transparency with use of force
incidents involving federal agencies.
District residents deserve transparency when there is a serious use of force or officer-involved
death involving federal agencies. Current law requires that the Mayor release body-worn camera
recordings of Metropolitan Police Department (“MPD”) officers present in the event of an officer-
involved death or serious use of force by an MPD officer. However, there is no such requirement
for release of MPD body-worn camera recordings when the officer-involved death or serious use
of force involves a law enforcement officer from another agency.
Additionally, on December 18, 2025, Councilmember Pinto sent a letter to MPD requesting data
about how federal law enforcement officers are being deployed in joint patrols with MPD officers.
While MPD’s initial response to the letter did not address these specific questions, MPD
subsequently provided responses to performance oversight pre-hearing questions from the
Committee on the Judiciary and Public Safety and indicated that such information was not able to
be shared.
To enhance transparency around federal law enforcement serious uses of force as well as patrols
with MPD, the Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Amendment Act of 2026
would:
• Require the public release of MPD body-worn camera recordings of all officer-involved
deaths or serious use of force incidents as well as the names of all officers involved and a
description of the incident. For body-worn camera recordings involving an officer-
involved death, the decedent’s next of kin must provide consent for the footage to be
released. For recordings of a serious use of force incident, the alleged victim or guardian if
the victim is a minor must consent to its release.
• Require that MPD’s public database of MPD body-worn camera recordings and names of
all officers directly involved with an officer-involved death since January 1,
2026, include names of directly involved federal law enforcement officers, as available.

• Require a roll call record of all federal agents in attendance at daily MPD roll call to provide
transparency with MPD joint patrols with federal agencies.
Should you have any questions about this legislation, please contact my Committee and Legislative
Director, Linn Groft, at lgroft@dccouncil.gov.
Thank you.
Best,
Brooke Pinto

17 To amend the Body-Worn Camera Regulation and Reporting Requirements Act of 2015 to
18 require release of body-worn camera recordings of Metropolitan Police Department
19 Officers present in the event of an officer-involved death or serious use of force by a law
20 enforcement officer other than a Metropolitan Police Department officer; and to require
21 that the Metropolitan Police Department record and share information about all law
22 enforcement officers present at roll calls.
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
25 be cited as the “Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Amendment Act of
26 2026”.
27 Sec. 2. Section 3004 of the Body-Worn Camera Regulation and Reporting Requirements
28 Act of 2015, effective October 22, 2015 (D.C. Law 21-36; D.C. Official Code § 5-116.33), is
29 amended as follows:
30 (a) Subsection c(2)(A) is amended as follows:
31 (1) Sub-subparagraph (i)(I) is amended to read as follows:
32 “(I) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all
33 Metropolitan Police Department officers as well as the names, as available, of all other law
34 enforcement officers directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force; and”.

35 (2) Sub-subparagraph (ii) is amended by striking the phrase “; and” and inserting
36 the phrase “, provided that for all officer-involved deaths since January 1, 2026, the information
37 reported pursuant to this sub-subparagraph shall include the names of all Metropolitan Police
38 Department officers, as well as the names of other law enforcement officers, as available,
39 directly involved in the officer-involved death; and” in its place.
40 (b) Subsection (e)(1) is amended by striking the phrase “serious use of force” and
41 inserting the phrase “serious use of force directly involving an MPD officer” in its place.
42 (c) Subsection (g)(3) is amended as follows:
43 (1) Subparagraph (A) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan Police
44 Department officer” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department officer, or any
45 other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
46 (2) Subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan Police
47 Department officer” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department officer, or any
48 other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
49 (3) Subparagraph (C) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan Police
50 Department officer” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department officer, or any
51 other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
52 (4) Subparagraph (D) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan Police
53 Department canine” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department canine, or canine
54 of any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
55 Sec. 3. (a) Beginning on the effective date of this Act, the Metropolitan Police
56 Department shall record the following information for all law enforcement officers, other than
57 MPD officers, present at roll call:

58 (1) Full name;
59 (2) Badge identification number or other identifying number;
60 (3) Law enforcement agency represented; and
61 (4) Assignment for the shift.
62 (b) The Department shall make the information recorded pursuant to subsection (a) of
63 this section available to the Chairperson of the Council Committee with jurisdiction over the
64 Metropolitan Police Department (“Chairperson”) upon request within 5 business days of such
65 request.
66 (c) The Department shall provide aggregate information regarding paragraphs (3) and (4)
67 of subsection (a) of this section available to the public upon request by the Chairperson within 5
68 business days of such request.
69 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
70 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
71 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
