PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE
Council of the District of Columbia | Washington, D.C.
Office of At-Large Councilmember Kenyan R. McDuffie March 2022 – January 2026
Committee Director (January 2025 – January 2026); Legislative Director (May 2022 – December 2024); and Senior
Policy Advisor (March 2022 – May 2022)
• Spearheaded the Committee on Business and Economic Development’s Fiscal Year 2026 budget review by leading a
team to hold agency oversight hearings with the public and government, rigorously vetting 27 Budget Support Act
subtitles and making detailed recommendations on changes, and ultimately identifying millions of dollars to invest in
small businesses, commercial corridors, economic development, and an inclusive economy despite significant fiscal
constraints.
• Served as a member of the small Council team that negotiated with the Washington Commanders football team to
facilitate the redevelopment of the Robert F. Kennedy Campus including strengthening the $50 million Community
Benefits Agreement, and local business procurement and hiring requirements; planning two Council hearings with public,
government, and team witnesses; and co-drafting the committee report and final legislation.
• Drafted and advanced legislation to address longstanding racial and economic inequities in the District including the
Historic Burial Grounds Preservation Program Amendment Act of 2025, Insurance Database Amendment Act of 2024,
Insurance Regulation Amendment Act of 2024, and the Medical Cannabis Amendment Act of 2022.
• Conducted oversight of agencies that shape the District’s business and economic climate, including a focus on the Office
of the Chief Financial Officer, Office of the Deputy Mayor for Planning and Economic Development, Alcoholic Beverage
and Cannabis Administration, and the Department of Insurance, Securities and Banking.
• Engaged with Executive branch officials, Council offices, constituents, lobbyists, and community groups on economic
development roundtables, confirmation resolutions, and pending legislation related to taxation and revenue; revenue
bonds; land dispositions; the regulation of banks and insurance providers; sports and entertainment; and economic
development.
• Managed legislative staff and supported their professional development, ensuring the delivery of high-quality work.
• Prepared and reviewed legislation, written remarks, talking points, hearing and press statements, and legislative
memorandums for the Councilmember.

DC Fiscal Policy Institute | Washington, D.C. January 2019 – March 2022
Senior Policy Analyst (April 2021 – March 2022) and Policy Analyst (January 2019 – April 2021)
• Developed and published research reports to influence public policy in DC, including authoring the award-winning
report, Black Workers Matter: How the District’s History of Exploitation & Discrimination Continues to Harm
Black Workers, a two-part series on the Black burden of COVID-19, and First In Line: Why the District Must Take a
Reparative Approach to Recreational Cannabis Policy for Black and Brown Communities.
• Co-led a coalition of excluded workers and published a compelling brief that helped secure $41 million in 2021 for workers
who were ineligible for unemployment insurance during the COVID-19 pandemic.
• Partnered with the DC Initiative on Racial Equity to strengthen and pass landmark legislation to incorporate racial
equity as a key focus of DC government - Racial Equity Achieves Results (REACH) Amendment Act of 2020.
• Cultivated and maintained relationships with DC government officials to ensure budget and policy proposals address DC’s
longstanding racial and economic inequities, by delivering public testimonies and meeting with members of the DC
Council to strengthen legislation.
• Analyzed the District of Columbia’s budget each fiscal year and produced comprehensive budget toolkits for residents on
investments in an inclusive economy and affordable housing.
• Engaged with the media to influence policymakers, including being featured in Time Magazine, CNN, The Washington
Post, The Kojo Nnamdi Show, The Outlaw Report, DCist, Greater Greater Washington, Washington City Paper,
WAMU, and a host of other media outlets.
• Supervised a team of fellows and interns and ensured that they had an enriching experience at the organization.
Neighborhood Allies | Pittsburgh, PA August 2014 – January 2019
Program Manager for Research, Evaluation & Policy (January 2017 – January 2019); Program Coordinator for Research,
Evaluation & Policy (September 2015 – December 2016); and Community Data and Policy Fellow (August 2014 –
August 2015)
• Partnered with PolicyLink to develop and release Equitable Development: The Path to an All-In Pittsburgh,
engaging the community, developers, public officials, funders and corporate institutions on an equitable
development action plan for Pittsburgh.
• Served as a core project team member of Pittsburgh’s Equitable Development Collaborative and identified policies
to increase the production of affordable housing and eliminate barriers to employment.
• Researched and deployed national best practices including planning, developing and launching the PGH Mobile
Toolbox – a community resource to connect residents and organizations with the landscaping tools needed for
neighborhood cleanups, vacant lot beautification, maintenance, and greenspace enhancement.
• Co-managed the Catalytic Grants Program, deployed over $4.2M in grants to support community revitalization
projects, and managed evaluation, including enhancing the organization's capacity to collect, aggregate, analyze,
and report data around performance, outcomes and impact through a metrics and indicators evaluative
accountability system.
• Led internal strategic planning for the organization including developing a Healthy Neighborhoods Framework
which became the unifying foundation for organizational decision-making and investments.
• Acquired over $1.35M in grants to support programming and operations throughout tenure, built out the online
grants management system, and aligned the system with our Healthy Neighborhoods Framework.
• Analyzed local data, studied neighborhood plans, and partnered with the community and Center for Community
Progress to develop neighborhood level strategies that directed investment and resource deployment at the
neighborhood level.
