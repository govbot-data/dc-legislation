11 To amend the Rental Housing Act of 1985 to prohibit housing providers from separately
12 charging tenants for the utility charges accrued by the housing accommodation for its
13 common spaces or vacant units, to require that housing providers give notification to a
14 tenant of any unpaid amounts owed to the housing provider within 45 days after vacating
15 a rental unit and to obtain evidence that the tenant was served with the notification at
16 least 60 days prior to sending the unpaid amount to a debt collector; and to clarify that
17 housing providers cannot charge tenants for services required under the implied warranty
18 of habitability.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 act may be cited as the “Fair Housing Practices Amendment Act of 2025”.
22 Sec. 2. The Rental Housing Act of 1985, effective July 17, 1985 (D.C. Law 6-10; D.C.
23 Official Code § 42-3501.01 et seq.), is amended as follows:
24 (a) Section 217 (D.C. Official Code § 42–3502.17) is amended by adding a new
25 subsection (e) to read as follows:
26 "(e)(1) Upon termination of a tenancy, the housing provider shall request a forwarding
27 mailing address or email address from the tenant for purposes of providing any notices required
28 pursuant to this section.
29 "(2)(A) Within 45 days after termination of the tenancy, the housing provider
30 shall notify the tenant in writing, either personally or by certified mail at the tenant's last known

52 (b) Section 510(b-2) is amended to read as follows:
53 "(b-2)(1)(A) A housing provider shall not charge a fee to a prospective tenant
54 before move-in, during a tenancy, or after move-out for services required of the housing provider
55 to maintain a housing accommodation or rental unit in a condition consistent with the implied
56 warranty of habitability and with Titles 12 and 14 of the District of Columbia Municipal
57 Regulations, or substantially similar subsequent regulations, including any fee charged to tenants
58 for services or facilities related to utilities, trash, locks, administrative fees for third-party billing,
59 or other services or facilities required to be provided.
60 "(B) Notwithstanding sub-paragraph (A) of this paragraph, nothing in this
61 subsection prohibits a housing provider from withholding a tenant's security deposit to replace
62 damaged items if the tenant has caused damage to the unit beyond the standard of ordinary wear
63 and tear as defined in section 217(c)(3).".
64 (c) A new section 511 is added to read as follows:
65 “Sec 511. Prohibition on separately billing a housing accommodation’s utility charges to
66 tenants.

67 “(a) Beginning on January 1, 2027, a housing provider, or any third party contracted by
68 the housing provider, shall not separately charge tenants, other than through monthly rent, for the
69 utility charges accrued by the housing accommodation for its common spaces or vacant units.
70 “(b) Nothing in this subsection shall prevent a housing provider from using a Ratio
71 Utility Billing System to allocate master-metered utility charges to tenants, subject to the
72 limitations of this section.
73 "(c) For the purposes of this section, the term:
74 "(1) "Common space" means all portions of the premises used in common by the
75 occupants of a building or structure not under the exclusive control of a single tenant, including:
76 "(A) Lobbies;
77 "(B) Leasing offices;
78 "(C) Business centers;
79 "(D) Pools; and
80 "(E) Fitness centers.
81 "(2) "Ratio Utility Billing System" includes a program that utilizes a
82 mathematical formula for allocating, among the tenants and property ownership in a residential
83 building, their share of the actual or anticipated water, sewer, electrical, oil, or natural gas
84 charges billed to the housing provider from a direct or third-party provider of any such utility
85 service. Permitted allocation methods may include formulas based on square footage, occupancy,
86 or number of bedrooms.
