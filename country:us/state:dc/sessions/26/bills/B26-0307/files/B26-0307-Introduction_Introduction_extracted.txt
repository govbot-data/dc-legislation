16 To amend, on an emergency basis due to congressional review, the Open Meetings Act to clarify
17 the definition of meeting, to provide for a public body’s ability to be briefed about
18 potential terrorist or public health threats so long as no official action is taken, to exempt
19 from the act meetings between the Council and the Mayor provided that no official action
20 is taken at such meetings, and to provide that a meeting shall be deemed open to the
21 public if the public body takes steps reasonably calculated to allow the public to view or
22 hear the meeting while the meeting is taking place, or, if doing so is not technologically
23 feasible, as soon thereafter as reasonably practicable.
24
25 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
26 act may be cited as the “Open Meetings Clarification Congressional Review Emergency
27 Amendment Act of 2025”.
28 Sec. 2. The Open Meetings Act, effective March 31, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-350; D.C.
29 Official Code § 2-571 et seq.), is amended as follows:
30 (a) Section 404 (D.C. Official Code § 2-574) is amended as follows:
31 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended to read as follows:
32 “(1)(A) “Meeting” means any gathering of a quorum of the members of a public
33 body, including hearings and roundtables, whether formal or informal, regular, special, or
34 emergency, at which the members consider, conduct, or advise on public business, including
35 gathering information, taking testimony, discussing, deliberating, recommending, and voting,
36 regardless of whether held in person, by telephone, electronically, or by other means of
37 communication.
1

38 “(B) The term “meeting” shall not include:
39 “(i) A chance or social gathering; provided, that it is not held to
40 avoid the provisions of this paragraph; or
41 “(ii) A press conference.
42 “(C) For the purposes of the Council of the District of Columbia, the term
43 “meeting” means a regular or additional legislative meeting, and committee meetings where
44 votes are taken.”.
45 (2) Paragraph (3) is amended as follows:
46 (A) Subparagraph (E) is amended by striking the phrase “; or” and
47 inserting a semicolon in its place.
48 (B) Subparagraph (F) is amended by striking the period at the end and
49 inserting the phrase “; or” in its place.
50 (C) A new subparagraph (G) is added to read as follows:
51 “(G) The Criminal Justice Coordinating Council.”.
52 (b) Section 405 (D.C. Official Code § 2-575) is amended as follows:
53 (1) Subsection (a) is amended as follows:
54 (A) Paragraph (2) is amended by striking the phrase “; or” and inserting a
55 semicolon in its place.
56 (B) Paragraph (3) is amended by striking the phrase “televised.” and
57 inserting the phrase “televised; or” in its place.
58 (C) A new paragraph (4) is added to read as follows:
59 “(4) The public body takes steps reasonably calculated to allow the public to view
60 or hear the meeting while the meeting is taking place, or, if doing so is not technologically
61 feasible, as soon thereafter as reasonably practicable.”.
62 (2) Subsection (b)(8) is amended to read as follows:

63 “(8) To discuss and take action regarding specific methods and procedures to

65 dangers to public health and safety, or to receive briefings by staff members, legal counsel, law
66 enforcement officials, or emergency service officials concerning these methods and procedures;

68 (3) Subsection (f) is amended to read as follows:
