Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is legislation
entitled the "Georgia Avenue Acquisition Authority Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025"
and the "Georgia Avenue Acquisition Authority Emergency Act of 2025."
This emergency legislation will authorize the acquisition of Lots 809, 840, 841, 842, 843, and
848 in Square 2937 , located on Georgia Av enue. These six properties are currently underutilized,
facing foreclosure, and are at risk of becoming blighted. It is in the best interest of the
redevelopment of these properties for the District to acquire them and ensure a cohesive
development process, especially considering the adjacent nature of the properties to a District­
owned site, Engine 22, located at 5760 Georgia Avenue N.W.
In light of the pending foreclosure action on these properties, emergency legislation is necessary
to gain control of these properties immediately for the benefit of the Georgia Av enue corridor
and to ensure that they are put to an economically advantageous purpose.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed bill.
Sincerely,

MEMORANDUM

FROM: Glen Lee

DATE: May 16, 2025
SUBJECT: Fiscal Impact Statement – Georgia Avenue Acquisition Authority
Emergency Act of 2025
REFERENCE: Draft bill as provided to the Office of Revenue Analysis on May 5, 2025
Conclusion
Funds are sufficient in the fiscal year 2025 through fiscal year 2028 budget and financial plan to
implement the bill.
Background
The bill authorizes the Mayor to acquire six lots1 near 5760 Georgia Avenue, N.W., which are facing
foreclosure. The expected purchase price is $2.8 million, and the Mayor has requested2 use of the
District’s Contingency Cash Reserve Fund3 for the purchase. The funding has been loaded in the
budget for the Deputy Mayor for Planning and Economic Development (DMPED).
Financial Plan Impact
Funds are sufficient in the fiscal year 2025 through fiscal year 2028 budget and financial plan to
implement the bill.
DMPED has been allocated $2.8 million from the Contingency Cash Reserve Fund for the purchase
and closing costs only. If additional funding is required for the future development of the properties,
it will need to be identified.
