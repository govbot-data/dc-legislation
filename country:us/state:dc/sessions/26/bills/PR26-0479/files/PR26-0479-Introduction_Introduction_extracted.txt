______________________________
Councilmember Trayon White, Sr.
A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
_____________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
_____________
1 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Green Building
2 Act of 2006 to exempt District-built recreation centers in Ward 8 with indoor pools and
3 indoor gyms from being required to maintain net zero energy compliance standards with
4 respect to the pools and gyms, and to require that Ward 8 recreation center indoor pools be
5 built to at least LEED Silver standards.
6
7 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
8 resolution may be cited as the “Ward 8 Recreation Facility Emergency Declaration Resolution of
9 2025”.
10 Sec. 2. (a) There exists an immediate need to amend the Green Building Act of 2006 to
11 exempt indoor pools and indoor gyms built in Ward 8 from the requirement to build to net zero
12 energy standards, the adherence to which will mean that residents in Ward 8 will not have an
13 indoor pool built with the modernization of the Congress Heights Recreation Center nor an
14 indoor gym built as part of the modernization of the Douglass Community Center.
15 (b) The Congress Heights Recreation Center and the Douglass Community Center in
16 Ward 8 are in the process of being modernized.
1

17 (c) For years, community members have been firm in meetings with the Department of
18 Parks and Recreation (DPR) and the Department of General Services (DGS) that the modernized
19 Congress Heights Recreation Center must include an indoor pool and the modernized Douglass
20 Community Center has an indoor gym.
21 (d) In 2023, the Council passed the Greener Government Buildings Amendment Act
22 which amended the Green Building Act of 2006 to require buildings built with District funds be
23 built to net zero standards, meaning that the facility creates as much energy as is needed for
24 operations or more.
25 (e) The law became effective while DGS was in the last stages of design for the Congress
26 Heights Recreation Center, and DGS had created a design working with the community that
27 included an indoor pool.
28 (f) For projects that do not meet the net zero standard, design plans must be submitted to
29 the Green Building Advisory Committee (GBAC) for its review and recommendation to
30 determine if an exemption should be granted by the Department of Energy and Environment
31 (DOEE).
32 (g) For the Congress Heights Recreation Center, DGS applied for an exemption to the net
33 zero requirements from GBAC and the GBAC denied the application, and DOEE formalized the
34 denial.
35 (h) DGS redesigned the Congress Heights Recreation Center without a pool, however,
36 when the community learned of the new design, they insisted on a pool which will require a
37 revised design and resubmission to GBAC.
2

38 (i) There are two net zero pools in the country. The indoor pool in Los Angeles has the
39 benefit of temperate weather and a large campus. The other pool is in the Plainfield Park
40 District, Illinois, a suburban/rural community with more land for solar and geothermal wells for
41 heating the water and running the high energy components of an indoor pool and aquatic facility.
42 (j) There is no net zero pool built in the country facing the space constraints of a dense
43 urban setting and with the need to heat the pool due to our zone 7 temperature parameters.
44 (k) The site for the Congress Heights Recreation Center is very challenging because a
45 large portion of it is covered by heritage trees, which cannot be disturbed to install the
46 geothermal wells underneath them to support heating the building in accordance with net zero
47 standards.
48 (l) While the GBAC required the design plans to include the installation of net zero
49 infrastructure, the available land at the site not covered by heritage trees is insufficient space for
50 the geothermal wells required to heat the indoor pool and support the energy demands of an
51 aquatic center according to net zero standards, particularly since a portion of the available land
52 will be used to support the net zero modernization efforts at the adjacent Martin Luther King, Jr.
53 Elementary School.
54 (m) DGS asserts that the installation costs of the geothermal wells are beyond the budget
55 provided for this project and the agency cannot provide a pool to satisfy the requirements of the
56 GBAC without further delays to this project.
57 (n) Similarly, the indoor gym has been removed from the design for the modernized
58 Douglass Community Center since the executive had to fund net-zero requirements and thus
59 reduce the project scope.
3

60 (o) For years, the Congress Heights community has worked with DPR and DGS to design
61 a modernized recreation center with an indoor pool, and any further delay will cause significant
62 disruption to the government’s ability to deliver a modernized Congress Heights Recreation
63 Center with an indoor pool and negatively impact the health and wellbeing of residents who seek
64 to use the modernized recreation center and pool.
65 (p) The community has worked with DPR and DGS to design a modernized Douglass
66 Community Center with an indoor gym, the lack of inclusion of which will negatively impact the
67 health and wellbeing of residents, particularly our youth, who seek to use safe spaces like the
68 modernized community center with a gym.
69 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
70 enumerated in Section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the “Ward
71 8 Recreation Facility Emergency Amendment Act of 2025” be adopted after a single reading.
72 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
