18 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
19
20 _________________________
21
22 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
23
24 _________________________
25
26
27 To recognize, honor, and celebrate the life, contributions, and accomplishments of civil rights
28 and equal employment leader Pierpont Mobley.
29
30 WHEREAS, Mr. Pierpont Mobley was born on December 20, 1937, in Washington,
31 D.C.;
32
33 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley received his education in the District, and became interested in
34 civil rights, politics, and government after experiencing his own challenges of discrimination
35 while growing up in Washington, D.C.;
36
37 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley attended Antioch School of Law, now known as the University
38 of the District of Columbia’s David A. Clarke School of Law;
39
40 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley graduated from Antioch with a Master of Arts and Master of
41 Legal Studies in equal employment opportunity and employment law, which steered the course
42 of his future pursuits;
43
1

44 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley established himself as a family man early on, marrying
45 Jeannette Mobley, raising two children Anthony and Patricia, and spending the next 58 years
46 building a life together rooted in care for one another as well as their community;
47
48 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley’s faith was central to his life as well, as he was an active
49 member of the Second New St. Paul Baptist Church in Ward 5 for 50 years;
50
51 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley began his public service with the National Security Agency,
52 and went on to the White House to serve under President Johnson, Nixon, Ford, and Carter’s
53 Administrations, where he spent more than 25 years as an equal employment opportunity
54 specialist across government agencies;
55
56 WHEREAS, one of Mr. Mobley’s proudest moments was when he was appointed the
57 Equal Employment Opportunity Manager in the White House Personnel Office, where he
58 oversaw personnel relations, and had the distinction of drafting the first affirmative action plan
59 for the White House and the Executive Offices of the President during the Carter Administration;
60
61 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley also worked for the Department of the Army with the DC
62 National Guard as a military personnel and race relations specialist for 22 years;
63
64 WHEREAS, before retiring from federal service, Mr. Mobley served in the Department
65 of the Interior as Chief of Complaints and Adjudication for the Bureau of Mines;
66
67 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley also served as the Branch Chief of Equal Employment
68 Opportunity for the Department of Agriculture’s Soil Conservation Service, where his work
69 helped to create national awareness of the plight of Black farmers and was the impetus for their
70 receipt of a major financial settlement under President Obama’s Administration;
71
72 WHEREAS, upon Mr. Mobley’s retirement, he and his wife founded The JPM Group,
73 LLC, a consulting firm specializing in human resources and management, providing services to
74 business, government and non-profits;
75
76 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley remained active in local politics as well, serving on
77 Congresswoman Eleanor Holmes Norton’s Service Academy Board, and was a Mayoral
78 Appointee on the D.C. Office of Human Rights Commission and the Office of Administrative
79 Appeals;
80
81 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley was also the campaign manager for former Councilmember
82 Vincent Orange in 1998, who successfully won the Ward 5 seat;
83
84 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley’s long-standing commitment to his community and activism
85 garnered him the respect of many in both business and political circles;
86
87 WHEREAS, in 2012, Mr. Mobley was inducted into the National HistoryMakers for his
88 outstanding government service and commitment to civil and human rights;
89
2

90 WHEREAS, in 2014, Congresswoman Eleanor Holmes Norton presented Mr. Mobley
91 with a Certificate of Special Congressional Recognition for outstanding and invaluable service;
92
93 WHEREAS, in 2020, Mr. Mobley and his wife were honored by a DC Council
94 Ceremonial Resolution, which celebrated their induction into the Washington, D.C. Hall of
95 Fame, and recognized them for their many years of leadership and service to the city and
96 residents of the District of Columbia;
97
98 WHEREAS, in 2021, Mr. Mobley received the Man-Made Award for mentoring and
99 training young men on job readiness and manhood issues;
100
101 WHEREAS, in 2022, Mr. Mobley received President Biden’s Presidential Lifetime
102 Achievement Award for Volunteer Services;
103
104 WHEREAS, in 2024, Mr. Mobley authored a memoir called Black Side of the White
105 House: A Memoir For Generations to Come, where he shared his experiences as the first Black
106 person to work in the White House Personnel Office under four Administrations;
107
108 WHEREAS, in 2025, Mr. Mobley and his wife received the Global Couples Award for
109 Community Service; and
110
111 WHEREAS, Mr. Mobley passed on January 22, 2026, at the age of 88, having fulfilled
112 his commitment to himself that his education and life journey would be focused on helping
113 others in the areas of employment and civil rights, both on the job and in his community.
114
115 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
116 resolution may be cited as the “Pierpont Mobley Posthumous Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
117
118 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors Pierpont Mobley
119 for his unwavering dedication and leadership in the United States, the District of Columbia, and
120 his local community.
121
122 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
