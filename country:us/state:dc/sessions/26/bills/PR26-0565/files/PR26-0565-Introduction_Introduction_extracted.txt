HOLLY FLOOD

Holly Flood
Ms. Holly Flood is a native Washingtonian and the current Grants and
Corporate Relations Specialist at Christ House with 30+ years of
experience as a senior program manager and administrator who has led,
designed, managed, and supported a variety of humanitarian response,
international development, and social services programs of all sizes and
levels of complexity, in the United States and abroad. Previously, she
served as Director of Proposal Writing at Blumont. She has experience
managing U.S. government (USG) grants and contracts, as well as proven
technical writing and business development capabilities.
Ms. Flood’s career in international development has included long-term
assignments leading and managing programs in Angola, Kenya,
Paraguay, Sierra Leone, Sudan, and Türkiye, as well as short-term assignments in 30+ countries
in Asia, Africa, Latin America, and the Middle East. She also served as a Peace Corps’ volunteer
in Paraguay. Earlier in her career, she spent several years resettling newly arriving refugees in the
DC area and elsewhere in the United States. She is an avid reader and DC library user from an
early age.
A Ward 1 resident, Ms. Flood earned her Bachelor of Arts in English from The Catholic University
of America and her Master of Arts in International Affairs from The George Washington
University.
