14 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Department of
15 For-Hire Vehicles Establishment Act of 1985 to clarify the definition of a private vehicle-
16 for-hire operator.
17
18 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
19 resolution may be cited as the “Private Vehicle-for-Hire Operator Clarification Emergency
20 Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
21 Sec. 2. (a) The District’s for-hire vehicle laws are designed to authorize only properly
22 licensed, insured, and identifiable vehicles and operators to transport passengers for
23 compensation. Specifically, private vehicle-for-hire operators are required to be registered with a
24 private vehicle-for-hire company that is registered to operate in the District and must provide
25 service only through the registered digital dispatch of such a company. These legal requirements
26 are carefully tailored to safeguard public safety, accountability, and passenger trust.
27 (b) For several years, the Department of For-Hire Vehicles (“DFHV”) and the
28 Metropolitan Police Department (“MPD”) have identified an increase in illegal vehicle-for-hire
29 services wherein certain operators are not registered with a private vehicle-for-hire company that
30 is legally authorized to operate in the District. In these instances, vehicle-for-hire service is being
1

31 provided without confirmation that the operator holds required commercial insurance, without
32 internal processes to screen out dangerous or predatory drivers, and without proper trade dress as
33 required by District law.
34 (c) Failure of such vehicle-for-hire services to comply with insurance and registration
35 requirements poses an immediate and irreparable risk of harm to passengers, operators, and the
36 public. In the event of a crash, unregistered or uninsured operators cannot provide coverage or
37 recourse for injuries or damages.
38 (d) Further, for illegal operations, there is no guarantee of processes to appropriately
39 identify and screen out dangerous operators, both in terms of operators who have demonstrated a
40 record of unsafe driving or those who pose a meaningful risk of harm to passengers. Illegal
41 operations may also lack a process for passengers to file complaints or otherwise seek reasonable
42 redress following an incident involving operator misconduct.
43 (e) Where vehicles used to provide illegal service lack proper trade dress, passengers are
44 prevented from verifying the vehicles as legitimate. This practice impedes enforcement officers’
45 efforts to determine whether a particular operator is providing service legally.
46 (f) This illegal activity has a harmful effect on the broader for-hire vehicle industry,
47 eroding public confidence in legitimate for-hire services and undermining safety, fair
48 competition, and the District’s consumer protection framework.
49 (g) Proceedings in Superior Court have challenged DFHV’s authority to enforce illegal
50 for-hire-vehicle operations, as described in subsections (a) through (f) of this section, with an
51 unregistered private vehicle-for-hire company attempting to take advantage of ambiguity in the
2

52 Department of For-Hire Vehicles Establishment Act of 1985, effective March 25, 1986 (D.C.
53 Law 6-97; D.C. Official Code § 50-301.01 et seq.).
54 (h) Recent emergency and temporary legislation has clarified that DFHV has had and
55 continues to be empowered with the authority to continue enforcing registration, insurance, and
56 trade dress requirements.
57 (i) The Private Vehicle-for-Hire Operator Clarification Temporary Amendment Act of
58 2025, effective February 12, 2026 (D.C. Law 26-94; 73 DCR 2683), will expire on September
59 25, 2026. Emergency legislation is needed to address the range of threats to passenger and driver
60 safety caused by illegal for-hire vehicle operators and companies.
61 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
62 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making it necessary that the Private
63 Vehicle-for-Hire Operator Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted after a
64 single reading.
65 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
