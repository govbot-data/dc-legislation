Summary
Leighton Ku, PhD, MPH, is a professor of health policy and management at the George Washington
University (GW), with a secondary appointment in public policy and administration. He is a nationally
known health policy and health services research scholar with more than 30 years of experience. He has
examined topics such as national and state health reforms, access to care for low-income populations,
Medicaid, preventive services, the health care safety net, cost and benefits of health services, and
immigrant health. He has authored or co-authored more than 100 peer-reviewed articles and 200 policy
briefs and other translational reports. While he is primarily a quantitative researcher, he had used diverse
methods including policy analysis, legal research, cost-benefit analysis, mixed methods research, case
studies, focus groups, survey research, multivariate analyses, randomized experiments, sibling studies,
propensity score methods and difference-in-difference studies. He directs the Center for Health Policy
Research, a transdisciplinary research center, which includes economists, attorneys, physicians, health
management and policy experts and others, with more than 20 faculty and staff; with a research portfolio
in excess of $20 million. He has been principal investigator for a large number of studies with support
from the National Institutes of Health, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, Centers for Medicare
and Medicaid Services, the Commonwealth Fund and Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, and other
sources. In the course of his career at GW, the Center on Budget and Policy Priorities and the Urban
Institute, he has worked with federal and state executive and legislative agencies, health care
organizations, advocates and others in research, technical assistance, strategic advice and advocacy. As a
faculty, he has taught research methods, econometrics and policy analysis at the graduate level for more
than 25 years and guided dozens of students through dissertations and other research. As a member of his
community, he helped establish and guide the District of Columbia’s Health Benefits Exchange Authority
as a founding member of its Executive Board.

Dr. Leighton Ku
Dr. Leighton Ku is the Director and a Professor at The Center for Health
Policy Research at The George Washington University (GWU). He is also
a current member of the Health Benefit Exchange Authority Executive
Board in the District.
Dr. Ku is a nationally known health policy and health services scholar
with more than 25 years of experience. He has examined topics such as
national and state health reforms; access to care for low-income
populations; Medicaid; preventive services; the health care safety net;
cost and benefits of health services; and immigrant health. Dr. Ku joined
the Milken Institute School of Public Health at The George Washington
University as a full-time faculty member in 2008. However, he has held
adjunct positions at George Washington's School of Public Policy and Public Administration since
the early 1990s.
Currently, Dr. Ku directs the multidisciplinary research Center for Health Policy at GWU. The
Center for Health Policy has a research portfolio of more than $25 million, with its members
including physicians, attorneys, economists, and health management experts. Prior to this, Dr. Ku
served as a senior fellow at the Washington based Center on Budget and Policy Priorities. He was
the principal researcher at the Urban Institute and a policy and budget analyst for the federal
nutrition program WIC (Women, Infants, and Children). Here, he focused on improving policies
for low- and moderate-income Americans. In addition, Dr. Ku has been a principal investigator
for many studies from the National Institutes of Health; Centers for Disease Control and
Prevention; Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services; and the Commonwealth Fund and
Robert Wood Johnson Foundation.
A Ward 1 resident, Dr. Ku earned his Bachelor of Arts in Biochemistry from Harvard University,
his Master of Science in Nutritional Sciences and Master of Public Health from the University of
California Berkeley, and his Doctorate in Health Policy from Boston University.
