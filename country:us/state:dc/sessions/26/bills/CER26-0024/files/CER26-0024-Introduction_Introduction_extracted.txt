19 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
20 __________
21
22 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
23 __________
24
25
26 To officially recognize the 100th anniversary of the National Capital Planning Commission.
27
28 WHEREAS, the National Capital Planning Commission (NCPC) was established by Congress in
29 1924 as the federal government’s central planning agency for the National Capital Region, tasked with
30 guiding long-term development while preserving the unique character, cultural heritage, and historical
31 significance of the nation’s capital;
32
33 WHEREAS, for 100+ years, NCPC has played a vital role in shaping Washington, DC, and the
34 surrounding region through comprehensive planning, policymaking, and project reviews, ensuring that
35 federal interests align with the city’s growth and well-being;
36
37 WHEREAS, NCPC has worked collaboratively with the District of Columbia Office of Planning
38 to enhance Washington as a great National capital, fostering equitable development through inspiring
39 architecture, public spaces, environmental stewardship, and thoughtful land-use managements;
40
41 WHEREAS, the passage of the Home Rule Act of 1973 redefined NCPC’s role to focus primarily
42 on federal properties within Washington, DC and the National Capital Region, establishing a shared
43 planning framework with the District of Columbia government;
44
45 WHEREAS, NCPC has provided expert guidance on critical planning initiatives, including the
46 Comprehensive Plan for the National Capital, the design and location of national memorials and
47 embassies, infrastructure projects and federal capital improvements that shape the monumental core and
48 public spaces of the nation’s capital;
49

50 WHEREAS, the twelve-member Commission of the NCPC represents both federal and local
51 governments, including appointees by the President of the United States and the Mayor and Chair of the
52 District Council of the District of Columbia, ensuring a diverse and collaborative approach to national and
53 local planning decisions;
54
55 WHEREAS, over the past century, NCPC has been instrumental in preserving Washington, DC’s
56 historic skyline through oversight of the Height of Building Act and has assured that the District’s voice
57 is heard during deliberation of new projects;
58
59 WHEREAS, as NCPC celebrated its centennial year in 2024, reflected on its history,
60 acknowledged past challenges, and reaffirmed its commitment to inclusive and forward-thinking planning
61 that benefits both the residents and the visitors of our city;
62
63 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this resolution
64 may be cited as the “National Capital Planning Commission 100th Anniversary Recognition Resolution of
65 2025.”
66
67 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia hereby commends NCPC for a century of
68 dedicated service and looks forward to its continued leadership in collaboration with the Government of
69 the District of Columbia in shaping a vibrant, resilient, and equitable future for our city and the
70 surrounding region.
71
72 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
73 District of Columbia Register.
