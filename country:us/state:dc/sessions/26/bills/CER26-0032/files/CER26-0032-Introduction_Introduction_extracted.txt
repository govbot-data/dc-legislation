6 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
7 __________
8
9
10 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
11
12 ____________________
13
14 To recognize and honor Dr. Perétte Arrington, Head Coach of the Jackson-Reed High
15 School Volleyball Team, for achieving her 500th career coaching victory and for her outstanding
16 contributions to student-athletes and the broader community.
17
18 WHEREAS, Dr. Perétte Arrington has served as the Head Coach of the Jackson-Reed
19 High School Varsity Volleyball Team, demonstrating an unwavering commitment to excellence,
20 leadership, and mentorship in high school athletics;
21
22 WHEREAS, under her exemplary leadership, the Jackson-Reed Tigers have secured 23
23 DCIAA Championships, one DCSAA State Volleyball Championship in 2021, and two DCSAA
24 runner-up titles, setting a high standard for volleyball in the District of Columbia;
25
26 WHEREAS, Dr. Arrington has been recognized as the 2021 DCIAA Coach of the Year,
27 the 2021 DCSAA Coach of the Year, and the 2021 Washington Post All-Met Co-Coach of the
28 Year, reflecting her extraordinary ability to develop talent and cultivate a culture of teamwork,
29 perseverance, and sportsmanship;
30
31 WHEREAS, under her leadership, the Jackson-Reed Volleyball Team has been ranked as
32 high as #10 in The Washington Post rankings, showcasing their dominance in the region;
33
34 WHEREAS, through her guidance, countless student-athletes have excelled both on and
35 off the court, earning opportunities to compete at the collegiate level and developing invaluable
36 life skills;
37
38 WHEREAS, Dr. Arrington has achieved the remarkable milestone of 506 career coaching
39 victories, an extraordinary accomplishment that speaks to her dedication, strategic expertise, and
40 the lasting impact she has had on the Jackson-Reed community;
41
42 WHEREAS, her contributions extend beyond the volleyball court, as Dr. Arrington is a
43 clinical psychologist with the Department of Behavioral Health, serving as a role model, mentor,
44 and advocate for young people in the District while emphasizing the values of education,
45 discipline, and leadership;
46

47 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, this resolution
48 may be cited as the “Dr. Perétte Arrington Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
49
50 The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors Dr. Perétte Arrington on
51 achieving her 500th career win, commends her lasting contributions to high school athletics, and
52 extends its deepest appreciation for her dedication to empowering student athletes across the
53 District.
54
55 This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
56 District of Columbia Register.
