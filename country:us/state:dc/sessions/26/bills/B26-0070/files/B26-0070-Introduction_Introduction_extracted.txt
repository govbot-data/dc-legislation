34 To amend the Rental Housing Act of 1985 to remove the exemption from the Rent Stabilization
35 Program for units rented to tenants using a tenant-based subsidy, to prohibit the
36 administrator of any tenant-based subsidy from paying an increased rent without
37 receiving proper notice that the increase is compliant with the Rent Stabilization
38 Program, to prohibit agreements to waive the requirements of the Rent Stabilization
39 Program, to empower the subsidy administrator to challenge any inappropriate rent and to
40 prohibit it from denying any reasonable rent increase, and to provide for reasonable
41 increases to unit rent after a tenant voluntarily vacates the unit.
42
43 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
44 act may be cited as the “Rent Stabilization Protection Amendment Act of 2025”.

45 Sec. 2. The Rental Housing Act of 1985, effective July 17, 1985 (D.C. Law 6-10; D.C.
46 Official Code § 42-3502.05 et seq.), is amended as follows:
47 (a) Section 103 (D.C. Official Code § 42-3501.03) is amended as follows:
48 (1) Paragraph (29B) is amended by striking the phrase “title II of this act” and
49 inserting the phrase “sections 205(g) through (h), 206 through 216, and 224 of this act” in its
50 place.
51 (2) A new paragraph (33B) is added to read as follows:
52 “(33B) “Subsidy administrator” means a federal or District agency or instrumentality or
53 other organization that makes rent payments to a housing provider as part of a tenant-based
54 subsidy program.”
55 (3) A new paragraph (36B) is added to read as follows:
56 “(36B) “Tenant-based subsidy program” means a federally or District-funded program
57 through which money is paid by a subsidy administrator to a housing provider as partial or total
58 rent, including the Housing Choice Voucher Program, the Local Rent Supplement Program, the
59 Family Re-housing Stabilization Program.”

65 section, exempt from the requirements of the Rent Stabilization Program:
