ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A RESOLUTION
26-85
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
April 1, 2025
To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Department of
For-Hire Vehicles Establishment Act of 1985 to authorize the Department of For-Hire
Vehicles to enforce traffic laws against carrier-for-hire vehicles.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
resolution may be cited as the “Department of For-Hire Vehicles Delivery Vehicle Traffic
Enforcement Expansion Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
Sec. 2. (a) On July 8, 2024, the Council passed the Department of For-Hire Vehicles
Delivery Vehicle Traffic Enforcement Expansion Temporary Act of 2022, effective September
4, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-205; 71 DCR 9325), which grants the Department of For-Hire Vehicles
(“DFHV”) the authority to enforce traffic laws and issue citations to delivery vehicles, or
“carriers-for-hire”, defined as “a class of transportation service by which a network of private
operators, couriers, or carriers provide delivery of parcels, food, or beverages in the District for
compensation”.
(b) The authority granted to DFHV by D.C. Law 25-025 is a continuation of emergency
and temporary measures passed by the Council in 2022 and 2023. DFHV has exercised this
enforcement authority to promote safety for carrier-for-hire operators and customers, and for
other road users. In Fiscal Year 2024, a total of 1,297 warnings or notices of infraction were
issued by DFHV to carriers-for-hire.
(c) D.C. Law 25-205 is set to expire on April 17, 2025.
(d) On December 17, 2024, the Council passed the Carrier-for-Hire Oversight and
Enforcement Amendment Act of 2024, effective March 21, 2025 (D.C. Act 25-693; 72 DCR
742), which includes provisions substantively similar to D.C. Law 25-205. That permanent act is
subject to appropriations, which indicates a likely applicability date of no earlier than October 1,
2025.
(e) Additional emergency and temporary measures are necessary to prevent a gap in
DFHV’s authority to conduct street enforcement of carrier-for-hire operators.
Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
