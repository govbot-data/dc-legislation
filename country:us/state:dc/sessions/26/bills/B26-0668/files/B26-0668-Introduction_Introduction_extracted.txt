(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The District of Columbia (“District”), Child and Family Services Agency (“CFSA”) is
entering into a Sole Source Contract with The National Center for Children and Families, Inc.,
(NCCF) to provide foster care placement and case management services for children and
youth in the District’s foster care system who will be placed, by the Contractor with foster families
in the State Maryland while their families reside in the District. The Contractor shall possess a
Child Placement Agency (CPA) and Adoption license issued by the State of Maryland’s
Department of Human Resources (“DHR”), Office of Licensing and Monitoring (“OLM”).
The Contractor’s social workers shall be dually licensed in the District and Maryland.
(D) The date on which a competitive procurement for these goods or services was last conducted,
the date of the resulting award, and a detailed explanation of why a competitive procurement
is not feasible:
The contract was competitively solicited on March 27, 2017, and subsequently awarded for a three-
year base period to NCCF on September 28, 2017. NCCF currently serves as the incumbent
contractor for these services. CFSA is currently soliciting via RFP for the stated services via the
District’s e-Sourcing, system as Doc799448. The extension will also allow for no disruption in
services while giving adequate time for the council to review complex multiyear base year projects.
(E) A description of any bid protest related to the award of the contract, including whether the
protest was resolved through litigation, withdrawal of the protest by the protestor, or
voluntary corrective action by the District. Include the identity of the protestor, the grounds
alleged in the protest, and any deficiencies identified by the District as a result of the protest:
CFSA did not receive a protest or litigation against the awarded contract.
(F) A description of any other contracts the proposed contractor is currently seeking or holds
with the District:
NCCF is the incumbent contractor for the stated services. NCCF may submit a response to the
pending solicitation Doc799448.
(G) The background and qualifications of the proposed contractor, including its organization,
financial stability, personnel, and performance on past or current government or private
sector contracts with requirements similar to those of the proposed contract:
2

NCCF was incorporated in Washington DC in 1915 and is the largest private child and family
welfare agency in the city. NCCF has partnered with CFSA for over 22 years providing case
management and foster care services. NCCF is an organization with a solid reputation and is
currently providing these foster care placement and case management services to CFSA. The
Contractor is uniquely qualified to serve the diverse interests and needs of the communities of the
District where they perform services.
An independent audit was conducted by RSM US LLP, on NCCF’s fiscal years 2019 and 2018
consolidated financial statements and opinioned that their financial statements presented fairly in all
material respects. Changes in their net assets and their cash flow for the years 2024 and 2025 ended
in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted in the US.
Staff members represent the diversity of the city, and they bring together clinical, community
building, and logistical skills to form a high-powered planning and implementation team. The
Contractor shall ensure that the staff composition, capacity and qualifications comply with
standards for licensed Child Placement Agencies in Maryland and that direct practice social
workers, supervisors and managers Columbia and Maryland.
This organization has provided satisfactory services during the period of performance under their
current contract and on previous contracts for the same services for the District.

NCCF did not submit a subcontracting plan. CFSA applied for a waiver of the sub-contracting

Development (DSLBD) on January 17, 2024, and received an approved waiver memorandum (WR-
5144) on February 6, 2024, for option periods one and two.
(I) Performance standards and the expected outcome of the proposed contract:
The performance standards for the contract are set forth in the contract. The Contractor shall
provide all the necessary labor, personnel, equipment, materials, facility(s), functional and technical
expertise and management strategies to perform foster care placement and case management
services in accordance with the provisions contained in the contract. The Contractor shall provide
these services in accordance with all existing federal and District of Columbia laws, rules, and
regulations, including appropriate District licensure requirements, and consistent with policies,
procedures and standards promulgated by CFSA. The contractor shall meet all expected outcomes
of the proposed contract to direct and CFSA referral services to foster and adoptive parents,
children/youth achieving positive permanency, placing children/youth in stable placement foster
homes, and working collaboratively with CFSA to identify barriers and solutions to service
provision.

governing child welfare, including those established by the Agency and under the Adoptions and
Safe Families Act. The Contractor shall also comply with all HIPAA requirements.
