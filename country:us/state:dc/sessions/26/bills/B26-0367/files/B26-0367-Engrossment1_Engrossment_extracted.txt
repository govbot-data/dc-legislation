10 To require the Department of Human Services to issue electronic benefit transfer cards with theft
11 prevention technology and to restore stolen public benefits; and to extend the operation of
12 the Flexible Rent Subsidy Pilot Program by one year, through Fiscal Year 2027.
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
15 act may be cited as the “Public Benefits Security Amendment Act of 2025”.
16 Sec. 2. The District of Columbia Public Assistance Act of 1982, effective April 6, 1982
17 (D.C. Law 4-101; D.C. Official Code § 4-201.01 et seq.), is amended as follows:
18 (a) Section 101 (D.C. Official Code § 4–201.01) is amended as follows:
19 (1) A new paragraph (1E) is added to read as follows:
20 “(1E) “Cloning” means using data acquired by unlawful means to create a duplicate
21 copy of a recipient’s EBT card.”.
22 (2) Paragraph (3A) is renumbered as paragraph (3C).
23 (3) New paragraphs (3A) and (3B) are added to read as follows:

24 “(3A) “Electronic benefit transfer card,” or “EBT card,” means a method to access
25 public assistance issued to a recipient or a recipient’s authorized representative through an
26 electronic payments system.
27 “(3B) “EMV technology” means the integration in a method of payment of a
28 microchip that generates a dynamic, one-time-use code for each payment transaction.”.

32 as EBT card information, through a solicitation in which the solicitor purports to be other than
33 who the solicitor is.”.

35 “(8A) “Skimming” means using technology unlawfully installed on an automated
36 teller machine, point-of-sale terminal, or similar device to capture data associated with a recipient’s
37 EBT card.”.
38 (7) A new paragraph (10A) is added to read as follows:
39 “(10A) “Theft” shall have the same meaning as in section 111 of the District of
40 Columbia Theft and White Collar Crimes Act of 1982, effective December 1, 1982 (D.C. Law 4-
41 164; D.C. Official Code § 22-3211).”.
42 (b) New sections 582 and 583 are added to read as follows:
43 “Sec. 582. Prevention of theft of electronically transferred benefits.

44 “(a) By October 1, 2027, the Department shall issue, to all recipients receiving public
45 assistance on electronic benefit transfer cards, updated EBT cards that incorporate EMV
46 technology.
47 “(b) The Department shall seek reimbursement for the costs of transitioning to the use of
48 EMV technology from the Food and Nutrition Service of the Department of Agriculture to the
49 fullest extent permissible under federal law.
50 “Sec. 583. Reimbursement for theft of electronically transferred benefits.
51 “(a)(1) If a recipient reports the theft of public assistance by fraudulent means to the
52 Department within 45 days of the discovery of the theft and within six months of the theft, and if
53 an investigation by the Department shows that a recipient’s correctly issued public assistance was
54 lost due to theft by fraudulent means, the Department shall restore up to two months of correctly
55 issued public assistance lost due to theft by fraudulent means.
56 “(2) For the purposes of this subsection:
57 “(A) Public assistance is “correctly issued” if the amount of, and the
58 recipient’s eligibility for, public assistance were accurately calculated under applicable law;
59 “(B) Theft is “by fraudulent means” if it is done by phishing, skimming,
60 cloning, or otherwise with the intent to obtain the property of another by means of a false or
61 fraudulent pretense, representation, or promise;
62 “(C) If incidents of theft that occurred in multiple months are reported
63 simultaneously, the value of “two months of correctly issued public assistance” subject to
