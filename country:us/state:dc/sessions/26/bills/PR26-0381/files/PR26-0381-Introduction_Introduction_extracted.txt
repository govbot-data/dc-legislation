Councilmember Brooke Pinto
A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
______________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
__________________
1 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Juvenile Curfew
2 Act of 1995 to authorize the Mayor to extend the juvenile curfew hours in the District
3 when appropriate to protect public safety or public or private property, to authorize the
4 Chief of Police to establish extended juvenile curfew hour zones in specified
5 circumstances, to start the juvenile curfew hours at 11 p.m. every day, and to extend the
6 applicability of the juvenile curfew to 17-year olds; and to amend the Volunteer Services
7 Clarification Temporary Amendment Act of 2025 to make conforming changes.
8
9
10 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
11 resolution may be cited as the “Juvenile Curfew Second Emergency Declaration Resolution of
12 2025”.
13 Sec. 2. (a) The Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995, effective September 20, 1995 (D.C. Law
14 11-48; D.C. Official Code § 2-1541 et seq.) (“Juvenile Curfew Act”), was enacted for the
15 protection of minors to prevent violence and crime in the interest of public health, safety, and
16 general welfare.
17 (b) The Juvenile Curfew Act has subsequently been amended multiple times to extend the
18 curfew period in the Fiscal Year 2003 Budget Support Emergency Act of 2002, effective July 23,
19 2002 (D.C. Act 14-453; 49 Stat. 8026), the Enhanced Crime Prevention and Abatement
20 Emergency Amendment Act of 2006, effective July 21, 2006 (D.C. Act 16-446; 53 DCR 6477),

21 and the Juvenile Curfew Emergency Amendment Act of 2025, effective July 7, 2025 (D.C. Act
22 26-104; 72 DCR 7691).
23 (c) Since 2023, the District has seen a rise in concerning behavior by some
24 unaccompanied youth. Already this spring, the District has had several instances of large groups
25 of youth engaging in harmful, and often times criminal, conduct in multiple areas around the
26 city, including the Wharf, Navy Yard, and U Street. Multiple fights have broken out and
27 robberies, assaults, and shootings have occurred. While MPD has used various methods to
28 address this conduct, including enhanced enforcement of the disturbance of the peace offenses,
29 working with local businesses on signage related to trespassing by unaccompanied minors, and
30 requests for a restricted curfew in the Wharf, the tools available are limited. After seeing the
31 success of the temporary juvenile curfew zones throughout the summer months when incidents
32 of violence tend to tick upward, the District is seeking continued flexibility in our juvenile
33 curfew to allow MPD to ensure the safety of both young people and the public in order to more
34 nimbly address significant safety concerns. Over the summer, during which several Juvenile
35 Curfew Zones were declared, MPD reports that the zones successfully kept groups of youth from
36 gathering in those specific zones during the extended times and MPD found zero curfew
37 violations during the extended hours from 8pm-11pm.
38 (d) Unfortunately, since the Juvenile Curfew Emergency Amendment Act of 2025
39 expired on October 5, 2025, there have already been several instances this month of large
40 gatherings of youth––in Navy Yard, Gallery Place, Rhode Island Avenue, and elsewhere––that
41 resulted in fights and robberies. According to MPD, these types of gatherings have become
42 increasingly predictable, with young people passing out flyers physically and online indicating
43 that there will be a large gathering on a particular day. When the emergency curfew legislation

44 was in place, MPD was able to use this kind of information to determine where to designate
45 juvenile curfew zones in an effort to prevent such gatherings from resulting in violence. Without
46 this legislative mechanism, MPD has been unable to be as proactive as before, putting a strain on
47 resources when they must respond.
48 (e) Therefore, emergency legislation is needed to continue maintaining the safety of
49 young people and the public in the District.
50 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
51 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Juvenile Curfew
52 Second Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
53 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
