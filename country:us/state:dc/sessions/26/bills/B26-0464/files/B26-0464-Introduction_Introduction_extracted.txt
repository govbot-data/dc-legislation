Dear Secretary Howard:
Today, alongside Councilmembers Janeese Lewis George and Brooke Pinto, I am introducing the
“Small and Local Business Credit Enhancement Amendment Act of 2025”. Please find enclosed a
signed copy of the legislation.
The District’s many small and local businesses are the engines of our economy. According to data
published by the U.S. Small Business Administration, more than 98.1% of DC businesses are small
businesses. As the District continues to welcome and pursue development of our commercial
corridors, it is paramount that our small and local businesses can compete with national chains for
new and prime commercial space.
Many developers prefer to secure financing for a project by signing long-term leases with tenants
that can afford to do so. These tenants are more likely to be big national chains and not small and
local businesses, whose owners must typically rely on their own personal savings and financing from
credit cards to get the business going.
This legislation would create a Small and Local Business Credit Enhancement Program within the
Department of Small and Local Business Development to provide qualifying businesses rent
guarantees and other credit enhancement services. The program provides for a rent guarantee for
up to three years that would be 100% of the rent owed in year one, 50% in year two, and 25% in year
three. The program will enhance DC small and local businesses’ competitiveness with big national
chains and also incentivize developers across the District to work and support DC’s entrepreneurs
and small and local business owners.
Please feel free to reach out to me or my Legislative Director, Antonio Nunes, with any questions or
for additional information.

26 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
27 act may be cited as the “Small and Local Business Credit Enhancement Amendment Act of
28 2025”.
29 Sec. 2. The Small, Local, and Disadvantaged Business Enterprise Development and
30 Assistance Act of 2005, effective October 20, 2005 (D.C. Law 16-33; D.C. Official Code § 2-
31 218.01 et seq.), is amended by adding a new section 2316 to read as follows:
32 “§ 2316. Small and local business credit enhancement program.
33 “(a) There is established within the Department a Small and Local Business Credit
34 Enhancement Program (“Program”), to provide to qualifying businesses rent guarantees and
35 other credit enhancement services as determined by the Department.
1

36 “(b) The Department shall maintain a Small and Local Business Underwriting Registry
37 (“Registry”), listing local business enterprises and small business enterprises that are eligible to
38 receive credit enhancement services from the Department.
39 “(c) To be placed on the Registry, a local business enterprise or small business enterprise
40 shall provide to the Department, on an annual basis and in a form determined by the Department,
41 information sufficient to determine creditworthiness, including:
42 “(1) For a local business enterprise or small business enterprise that has been
43 operating for at least 2 years:
44 “(A) Corporate tax returns for the previous 2 years;
45 “(B) The local business enterprise or small business enterprise owner’s
46 individual tax returns for the previous 2 years;
47 “(C) The local business enterprise or small business enterprise owner’s
48 personal financial statement, including a listing of all assets and liabilities; and
49 “(D) A financial statement for the local business enterprise or small
50 business enterprise, including:
51 “(i) A profit and loss statement;
52 “(ii) Balance sheet; and
53 “(iii) A listing of accounts receivable and accounts payable; or
54 “(2) For a local business enterprise or small business enterprise that has been
55 operating for fewer than 2 years:
56 “(A) All corporate tax returns;
57 “(B) The local business enterprise or small business enterprise owner’s
58 individual tax returns for the previous 2 years;
2

59 “(C) The local business enterprise or small business enterprise owner’s
60 personal financial statement, including a listing of all assets and liabilities;
61 “(D) A financial statement for the local business enterprise or small
62 business enterprise, including:
63 “(i) A profit and loss statement;
64 “(ii) Balance sheet; and
65 “(iii) A listing of accounts receivable and accounts payable;
66 “(E) A business plan; and
67 “(F) Evidence of the business owner’s management experience or other
68 experience relevant to the business plan.
69 “(d) If the Department determines, pursuant to publicly available criteria adopted by
70 rulemaking, that the information provided by a local business enterprise or small business
71 enterprise pursuant to subsection (c) of this section demonstrates an acceptable level of risk, the
72 Department shall place the local business enterprise or small business enterprise on the Registry.
73 “(e) A local business enterprise or small business enterprise listed on the Registry shall be
74 eligible to apply for rent guarantees, as follows:
75 “(1) The Department may provide a certification to a property owner that, in the
76 event a local business enterprise or small business enterprise listed on the Registry is unable to
77 pay rent and the property owner is unable to mitigate the lost rent, the Department will provide
78 payments, pursuant to the terms of a lease between the property owner and the local business
79 enterprise or small business enterprise listed on the Registry, to the property owner for up to 3
80 years as follows:
81 “(A) In the first year of the lease, 100% of the rent owed;
3

82 “(B) In the second year of the lease, 50% of the rent owed; and
83 “(C) In the third year of the lease, 25% of the rent owed.
84 “(2) The Department shall not provide a certification to a property owner pursuant
85 to paragraph (1) of this subsection after the qualifying business is obligated to make payments
86 pursuant to a lease with the property owner, or if the local business enterprise or small business
87 enterprise listed on the Registry has at any other time been obligated to make payments pursuant
88 to a lease between the qualifying local business and the local business enterprise or small
89 business enterprise listed on the Registry.
90 “(3) Before receiving a certification pursuant to paragraph (1) of this subsection, a
91 property owner shall provide the Department a certification, in a form determined by the
92 Department, that but for the rent guarantee, the property owner would not enter into a lease
93 agreement with the local business enterprise or small business enterprise listed on the Registry.
94 “(4) The Department shall not make a payment pursuant to paragraph (1) of this
95 subsection if the property owner has not made a good faith effort to replace the local business
96 enterprise or small business enterprise listed on the Registry.
97 “(5) The payment made pursuant to paragraph (1) of this subsection shall not
98 exceed 110% of the average commercial rent per square foot for the census tract where the
99 qualifying business is located.
100 “(f) Within 90 days after the effective date of the Small and Local Business Credit
101 Enhancement Amendment Act of 2025, introduced November 3, 2025, and annually thereafter,
102 the Department shall publish on its website a list of the average commercial rent per square foot
103 for each census tract.
104 “(g) The Department may provide other credit enhancement services.”.
