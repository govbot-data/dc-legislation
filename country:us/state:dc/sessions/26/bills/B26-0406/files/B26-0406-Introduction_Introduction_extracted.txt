15 To amend the Prevention of Child Abuse and Neglect Act of 1977 to establish a District Office
16 for Children and an Interagency Council for Children.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
19 act may be cited as the “Streamlining Services for Children Amendment Act of 2025”.
20 Sec. 2. The Prevention of Child Abuse and Neglect Act of 1977 is amended as follows:
21 (a) A new section 302b is added to read as follows:
22 “Sec. 302b. Establishment of the District Office for Children
23 “(a) There is established within the Child and Family Services Agency a District Office
24 for Children.
25 “(b) The District Office for Children shall be administered by a Deputy Director of the
26 Child and Family Services Agency who reports directly to the Director of the Child and Family
27 Services Agency.
28 “(c) The District Office for Children shall:
29 “(1) Convene, organize, and staff an Interagency Council for Children;
30 “(2) Improve engagement and communication with District youth and families
31 internal and external to the welfare system, including by developing a coordinated, centralized

32 website and other communications platforms for District families with children;
33 “(3) Advise the Mayor on policies related to the well-being of children and
34 families, including economic mobility;
35 “(4) Promote values, policies and practices that continually advance the well-
36 being of the District’s children and families;
37 “(5) Leverage federal initiatives and programs to support the District’s children
38 and families;
39 “(6) Submit by March 1 an annual report to the Council and the Mayor that
40 includes:
41 “(A) Recommendations for streamlining youth services, reducing
42 inefficiencies and redundances, and improving the delivery of services to youth and families; and
43 “(B) An assessment of progress towards goals identified by the
44 Interagency Council for Children, including communication, direct services, programming,
45 employment, truancy reduction, and system involvement.
46 “(7) Engage with the District Youth Council.”
47 (b) A new section 302c is added to read as follows:
48 “Sec. 302c. Establishment of the Interagency Council for Children
49 “(a) There is established in the District an Interagency Council for Children for the
50 purpose of facilitating interagency, cabinet-level leadership in planning, policymaking, program
51 development, and alignment of District services provided for the wellbeing and positive
52 outcomes of youth.
53 “(b) The Interagency Council is composed of:
54 “(1) The Deputy Mayor for Health and Human Services;

55 “(2) The Deputy Mayor for Education;
56 “(3) The Deputy Mayor for Public Safety and Justice;
57 “(4) The Deputy Mayor for Economic Development;
58 “(5) The administrative head of each of the following entities or divisions
59 thereof, or their designee, provided that the designee reports directly to the administrative
60 head of the agency or division:
61 “(A) The Department of Child and Family Services;
62 “(B) The Department of Behavioral Health;
63 “(C) The Department of Health;
64 “(D) The Department of Human Services;
65 “(E) The Department of Human Services;
66 “(F) The Department of Parks and Recreation;
67 “(G) The District of Columbia Public Library;
68 “(H) The Office of the State Superintendent for Education;
69 “(I) The Department of Employment Services;
70
71 “(J) The Department of Youth Rehabilitation Services;
72 “(K) The Attorney General of the District of Columbia;
73 “(L) The Metropolitan Police Department;
74 “(6) The Chairperson or Chairpersons of the D.C. Council committee or
75 committees with jurisdiction over the Child and Family Services Agency and the Department of
76 Youth Rehabilitation Services, or their designees;
77 “(7) The Ombudsperson for Children; and

78 “(8) One representative each from a minimum of 3 and a maximum of 5
79 organizations that provide services to or advocate for the needs of youth in the District.
80 “(c) All non-government members of the Interagency Council described in subsubsection
81 (b)(8) of this section shall be nominated for appointment by the Mayor and approved by the
82 Council. The Mayor shall transmit to the Council nominations of each non-government member
83 of the Interagency Council for a 60-day period of review, excluding days of Council recess. If the
84 Council does not approve or disapprove a nomination by resolution within the 60-day review
85 period, the nomination shall be deemed approved.
86 “(e) The Interagency Council for Children shall:
87 “(1) Coordinate District policies and programs to reduce truancy;
88 “(2) Coordinate District policies and programs targeting child poverty;
89 “(3) Establish and maintain trauma and resilience informed practices across
90 District government;
91 “(4) Align and coordinate youth workforce development, internship, and training
92 programs;
93 “(5) Ensure District agencies provide aligned support for students with
94 disabilities, and justice and welfare system-involved youth;
95 “(6) Establish best practices to leverage federal initiatives and programs to
96 support the District’s children and families;
97 “(7) Establish measurable targets for the District’s youth facing programs,
98 including communication, direct services, programming, employment, truancy reduction, and
99 system involvement; and
100 “(8) Coordinate, establish, and monitor other policies and practices to support

101 effective and supportive engagement with District youth across District government.”.
102 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
103 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
104 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
105 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
106 Sec. 4. Effective date.
107 This act shall take effect after approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
108 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of congressional review as
109 provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
110 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)), and publication in the District of
111 Columbia Register.
