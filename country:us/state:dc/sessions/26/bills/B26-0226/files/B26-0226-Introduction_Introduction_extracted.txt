Statement of Introduction
Place-Based Substance Use Disorder Outreach Amendment Act of 2025
April 11, 2025
Today, I am joined by Councilmembers Henderson, Pinto, Allen, Lewis George, Parker,
and White in introducing the “Place-Based Substance Use Disorder Outreach Amendment Act of
2025”, which establishes a targeted outreach program within the Department of Behavioral
Health to provide direct support, relationship development, and resource brokering to individuals
in need of substance use disorder (SUD) treatment and behavioral health services in areas of high
need.
This measure would make permanent a successful pilot program that Council has funded
since 2021, the Substance Abuse and Behavioral Health Services Targeted Outreach Grant,
which began at Columbia Heights Civic Plaza and has since expanded to locations in Wards 1, 5,
6, 7, and 8. Despite subsisting on one-time funding each year, this program has shown
considerable early successes – for example, a 51% decrease in nonfatal overdoses in the 500
block of T Street NW in less than a year.
That grant program was created in response to inadequate efforts to in address long-
standing SUD challenges in select areas throughout the city, especially in prominent public
spaces. Establishing a permanent program ensures that this important and successful work can
continue without service interruptions. The success of this pilot highlights the impact of place-
based strategies that provide consistent and intensive outreach and support to individuals.
DBH plans to open a second Stabilization Center in Columbia Heights in the Summer of
2026. The first DC Stabilization Center, located at 35 K Street NE, has been critical for adults
experiencing a substance use crisis, overdoses, and injuries. Enhancing the current SUD outreach
infrastructure in the city will be critical to the continued success of both centers.

7 Sec. 2. Title V, Subtitle J, Fiscal Year 2014 Budget Support Act of 2013 (D.C. Law 20-
8 61, D.C. Official Code § 7-1141.01 et seq.) is amended as follows:
9 (a) Section 5112 (D.C. Official Code § 7-1141.02) is amended as follows:
10 (1) New paragraphs (5a) and (5b) are added to read as follows:
1

11 “(5a) “Direct support” means providing behavioral health crises assessment and
12 interventions.
13 “(5b) "Placed-based outreach” means focused on a specific geographic area, such
14 as a neighborhoods or corridor.
15 (2) New paragraphs (7a) and (7b) are added to read as follows:
16 “(7a) “Relationship development” means establishing a trusting relationship
17 through regular interaction and incorporation of peer supports when possible that fosters open
18 communication and encourages active engagement with services for individuals using
19 substances.
20 “(7b) “Resource brokering” means providing support to help individuals access
21 supportive services and public benefits, including housing, food, Medicaid, SSI or SSDI, and
22 transportation services needed to access primary and behavioral health care.
23 (b) A new section 5117d is added to read as follows:
24 “Sec. 5117d. Place-Based Substance Use Disorder Outreach.
25 “(a) There is established, within the Department of Behavioral Health, the Place-Based
26 Substance Use Disorder Outreach Program (“Program”) to provide direct support, relationship
27 development, and resource brokering to individuals in need of substance use treatment and
28 behavioral health services in high-need areas, as designated by the Department.
29 “(b) The Department shall designate a specific location as a high-need area for no less
30 than 12 months, which may be renewed. In assessing and designating high-need areas, the
31 Department shall consider:
32 “(1) Within the preceding 6-month period, the occurrence of a disproportionately
33 high rate of:
2

34 “(A) Substantiated reports of substance use in public spaces; or
35 “(B) Hospitalizations, overdoses, and deaths from substance use;
36 “(2) High reoccurrences of the same individuals using substances in the same
37 vicinity;
38 “(3) Areas with a high concentration high pedestrian and business interaction with
39 people experiencing SUD.
40 “(c) The Department may issue grants to experienced non-profit organizations to provide
41 services pursuant to this section.
42 “(d) Beginning 90 days after the applicability date of this section, and every 90 days
43 thereafter, the Department shall publish program performance data on a publicly-accessible
44 website, which shall be broken down by location and include the following information:
45 “(1) The number of individuals the program engaged through outreach efforts;
46 “(2) The number of individuals connected to substance use disorder treatment
47 programs, primary healthcare, mental health services, housing assistance, employment support,
48 or other services;
49 “(3) The number of overdose reversals or interventions performed using overdose
50 reversal medications;
51 “(4) The amount of harm reduction supplies distributed;
52 “(5) The number of educational sessions, workshops or prevention activities
53 delivered to target populations, and other performance data as determined by the Director.”.
