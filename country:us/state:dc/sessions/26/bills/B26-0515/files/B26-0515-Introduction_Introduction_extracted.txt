14 To Amend the District of Columbia Election Code of 1955 to require the Board of Elections to
15 conduct a comprehensive needs assessment to ensure the District’s institutional readiness
16 for implementing ranked choice voting, to analyze voter awareness and public
17 perceptions of the ranked choice voting system, to assess the equity and access
18 implications of ranked choice voting for historically marginalized communities, to review
19 best practices and implementation experiences from other jurisdictions, and to require the
20 Board to submit a report to the Mayor and the Council that includes a RCV
21 implementation plan, a full cost model, an operational timeline with critical milestones
22 and a comprehensive voter outreach and education plan.
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
25 act may be cited as the “Board of Elections Ranked Choice Voting Needs Assessment
26 Amendment Act of 2025.”
27 Sec. 2. Section 8a of the District of Columbia Election Code of 1955, effective March 21,
28 2025 (D.C. Law 25-295), D.C. Official Code § 1-1001.08a), is amended by adding a new
29 subsection (n) to read as follows:
30 “(n) No later than January 1, 2027, the Board Shall conduct a comprehensive needs
31 assessment to support the effective and efficient implementation of Ranked Choice Voting.
32 “(1) The assessment shall include:
33 “(A) An institutional readiness review, including:
34 “(i) An evaluation of Board’s capacity to implement ranked choice
35 voting, including ballot design, vote tabulation system and associated staff training; and

36 “(ii) Identification of necessary systems upgrades, operational
37 process, and logistical requirements for successful implementation;
38 “(iii) Projected costs of implementation, including equipment,
39 staffing, technology, translation, accessibility services, and public education.
40 “(B) Development of proposed regulations governing the implementation
41 of RCV that includes at a minimum:
42 “(i) ballot structure, ranking limits, tabulation sequencing, tie-
43 breaking procedures, ballot adjudication, recount triggers, certification and public reporting
44 “(ii) a timeline for public notice, comment, final rulemaking and
45 Council review.
46 “(C) A public knowledge and perception evaluation to measure:
47 “(i) General voter awareness and understanding of rank choice
48 voting; and
49 “(ii) Targeted analysis of awareness among populations with
50 limited English proficiency, seniors, and communities with historically low civic engagement;
51 “(D) An equity and access impact analysis to assess the potential effects of
52 ranked choice voting on historically underrepresented communities, including Black, Latino,
53 low-income, and retuning citizen populations; and
54 “(E) A best practices review, including:
55 “(i) A summary of implementation strategies from other
56 jurisdiction; and
57 “(ii) Identification of success factors and common implementation
58 challenges.
59 “(2) Upon completion of the assessment, the Board Shall submit to the Mayor and
60 the Council a report that includes:

61 “(A) A proposed plan regarding ranked choice voting implementation;
62 “(B) A timeline for phased implementation that includes critical
63 milestones; and
64 “(C) A robust outreach and education plan.”
65 “(3) The Board of Elections shall not implement or administer any new voting
66 policies, procedures, or election system modifications, including but not limited to ranked choice
67 voting, unless it has first conducted a comprehensive needs assessment addressing operational
68 capacity, budgetary requirements, staffing, technology, public education needs, and equity
69 impacts and submitted to the Mayor and the Council a detailed implementation plan, finalized
70 regulations, a budget analysis, and operational timelines with key milestones.
71 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
72 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
73 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
74 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
75 Sec. 4. Effective date.
76 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
77 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto) and a 30-day period of congressional review
78 as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
79 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Code § 1-206(c)(1)).
