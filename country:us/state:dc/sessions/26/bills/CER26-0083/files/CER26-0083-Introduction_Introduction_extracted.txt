9 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
10 _____________
11
12 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
13 _________________
14
15 To honor Alma Woodsey Thomas, an African American artist, art teacher, and influential
16 modernist painter of colorful abstraction who, in 1972, became the first African American
17 woman to have a solo exhibition at the Whitney Museum of American Art.
18
19 WHEREAS, Alma Woodsey Thomas was born on September 22nd, 1891, in Columbus,
20 Georgia, the eldest of four daughters born to John Harris Thomas, a businessman, and Amelia
21 Cantley Thomas Nanjemoy, a dress designer;
22
23 WHEREAS, she enrolled at Howard University in 1921, originally studying home
24 economics before switching to fine art under mentorship of James V. Herring, who was also her
25 professor, and, in 1924, became the first graduate of Howard’s fine arts program;
26
27 WHEREAS, upon graduation, Thomas taught art at Shaw Junior High School in
28 Washington, D.C. for thirty-five years, during which she established community arts
29 programming and encouraged cultural enrichment among her students;
30
31 WHEREAS, she earned a Master’s in Art Education from Columbia University in 1934
32 and pursued further artistic training at American University in the 1950s;
33
34 WHEREAS, after retiring in 1960, Thomas devoted herself fully to her art, pioneering a
35 vivid abstract style characterized by rhythmic patterns joyful color, influenced by Pointillism,
36 Matisse, the Washington Color School, and her own observations of nature;
37
38 WHEREAS, in 1966, she held her first retrospective exhibition at Howard University
39 debuting what would be later called “Alma Stripes” style—dense, mosaic-like patterns using
40 small daubs of vibrant acrylic color;
41
42 WHEREAS, in 1972, at age 80, she received the distinction of being her first African
43 American woman to hold a solo exhibition at the Whitney Museum of American Art, and
44 simultaneously exhibited at the Corcoran Gallery of Art;
45
46 WHEREAS, in 2015, her painting Resurrection (1966) became the first artwork by Black
47 woman to enter the White House permanent collection;
1

48
49 WHEREAS, Thomas’ lifelong home at 1530 15th Street, NW, in Logan Circle, is now
50 recognized for its historical importance and remains listed on the National Register of Historic
51 Places;
52
53 WHEREAS, through her late-blooming and vibrant art, she broke barriers for women,
54 older artists, and African American creators— and remains a celebrated figure in American
55 Modernism.
56
57 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
58 resolution may be cited as the “Alma Woodsey Thomas Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
59
60 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes the groundbreaking career of
61 Alam Woodsey Thomas and commemorates her life, legacy and contributions to the District of
62 Columbia on the 134th anniversary of her birth.
63
64 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
65 the District of Columbia Register.
