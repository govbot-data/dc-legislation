10 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Legalization of Marijuana for Medical Treatment Initiative
11 of 1999 to extend the expiration dates of conditional licenses for medical cannabis
12 cultivation centers, retailers, internet retailers, manufacturers, couriers, and testing
13 laboratories for an additional year.
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
16 act may be cited as the “Medical Cannabis Conditional Licensure Extension Temporary
17 Amendment Act of 2025”.
18 Sec. 2. Section 7 of the Legalization of Marijuana for Medical Treatment Initiative of
19 1999, effective July 27, 2010 (D.C. Law 18-210; D.C. Official Code § 7-1671.06), is amended as
20 follows:
21 (a) Subsection (k) is amended as follows:
22 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended by striking the phrase “a 2-year conditional license”
23 and inserting the phrase “a conditional license valid for up to 3 years” in its place.
24 (2) Paragraph (2) is amended by striking the phrase “shall have 2 years from the
25 date of ABC Board approval” and inserting the phrase “shall have until the end of the term of the
26 conditional license” in its place.

27 (3) Paragraph (4) is amended by striking the phrase “after a period of 2 years” and
28 inserting the phrase “before the end of the term of the conditional licenses” in its place.
29 (b) Subsection (k-1) is amended to read as follows:
30 “(k-1) A one-year conditional license that was in effect as of December 17, 2024, and
31 that automatically converted to a 2-year conditional license, shall automatically convert to a 3-
32 year conditional license, expiring 2 years after the date the original conditional license was set to
33 expire, at no additional cost and without additional ABC Board approval.”.
34 (c) Subsection (l) is amended by striking the phrase “A one-year conditional license” and
35 inserting the phrase “A conditional license” in its place.
36 Sec. 3. Applicability.
37 This act shall apply as of June 28, 2025.
38 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
39 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Chief Financial Officer as the fiscal
40 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
41 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
