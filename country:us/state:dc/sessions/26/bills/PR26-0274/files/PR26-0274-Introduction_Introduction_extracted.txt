14 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Urban Forest
15 Preservation Amendment Act of 2002 to exempt Heritage Trees located in Lots 865, 866,
16 867, 868, and 869 in Square 5056 of the Parkside mixed-use economic development
17 project from Heritage Tree removal requirements
18
19 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
20 resolution may be cited as the “Parkside Canopy Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
21 Sec. 2. (a). The Urban Forest Preservation Act of 2002 was enacted to establish an urban
22 forest preservation program, to require a Special Tree removal permit and community
23 notification prior to the removal of a replacement tree with a circumference of 55 inches or more,
24 to establish a Tree Fund to be used to plant trees, and to revise penalties for injuring trees on
25 public space and private property.
26 (b) Urban Forest Preservation has subsequently been amended by the Tree Canopy
27 Protection Amendment Act of 2016 to increase the permit fees for Special Tree removal, to
28 increase the fines for unlawful removal of a Special Tree, to protect trees with a circumference of
29 100 inches or more, to expand the permissible uses of the Tree Fund, and to establish the Urban
30 Forestry Advisory Council.
1

31 (c) Exempting the removal of the Heritage Trees in Lots 865, 866, 867, 868, and 869 of
32 Square 5056 from permit requirements established by the Urban Forest Preservation Act of 2002
33 is a critical component of the ongoing transit-oriented economic development initiatives within
34 the Parkside community in Ward 7. This project will introduce approximately 209 units of much-
35 needed affordable housing and ground floor retail to include a grocery store and was approved
36 through a planned unit development prior to the enactment of the Tree Canopy Protection
37 Amendment Act of 2016. The Parkside development has already created a new tree-lined buffer
38 and community park that includes green space and other community amenities that would offset
39 the loss of these Heritage Trees.
40 Therefore, emergency legislation is needed to support the ongoing economic
41 development initiatives including the construction of new affordable housing units and retail in
42 the Parkside community in Ward 7.
43
44 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
45 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Parkside Canopy
46 Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
47
48 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
