10 To prohibit the District of Columbia Public Library from entering into or renewing licensing
11 agreements with publishers of electronic literary materials that restrict District residents’
12 access to books.
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
15 act may be cited as the “Library E-Book Licensing Fairness Amendment Act of 2026”.
16 Sec. 2. An Act To establish and provide for the maintenance of a free public library and
17 reading room in the District of Columbia, approved June 3, 1896 (29 Stat. 244; D.C. Official Code
18 § 39-101 et seq.), is amended by adding a new section 18 to read as follows:
19 “Sec. 18. Restrictions on licensing agreements for electronic materials.
20 “(a) For the purposes of this section, the term:
21 “(1) “Aggregator” means any person in the business of licensing access to
22 electronic literary material collections that include electronic literary material from multiple
23 publishers.

24 “(2) “All access collection” mean a pre-packaged selection of electronic literary
25 materials, including but not limited to, electronic books, digital audiobooks, magazines, comics,
26 and graphic novels available simultaneously for patron usage for one flat fee.
27 “(3) “Borrower” means a person or organization, including another library, to
28 whom the library loans media of any sort.
29 “(4) “Digital audiobook” means a sound recording of a reading of any literary
30 material that has been converted into or published in a digital audio file that may be listened to on
31 an electronic device.
32 “(5) “Electronic book” means a text document that has been converted into or
33 published in a digital format that may be read on an electronic device.
34 “(6) “Electronic literary material” means any digital audiobook or electronic book.
35 “(7) “Library” means the District of Columbia Public Library.
36 “(8) “Loan” means the creation and transmission by a library to a borrower of a
37 copy of any electronic literary material and the library’s removal of the borrower’s access to that
38 copy upon the expiration of the loan period.
39 “(9) “Loan period” means the period of time commencing with the library’s
40 creation and transmission of a copy of any electronic literary material to a borrower and concluding
41 with the library’s deletion of that copy,
42 “(10) “Publisher” means any person in the business of the manufacture,
43 promulgation, license or sale of books, audiobooks, journals, magazines, newspapers or other

44 literary materials, including those in the form of electronic literary materials, and includes any
45 aggregator who enters into a contract with any library for the purpose of providing materials for
46 purchase or license.
47 “(11) “Subscription-based literary platform” means any platform which charges
48 users a recurring fee to gain ongoing access to a collection of literary content, including, but not
49 limited to, educational materials, electronic books, digital audiobooks, and serialized stories.
50 “(12) “Technological protection measure” means any technology that enhances the
51 security of loaning or circulating electronic literary materials by a library.
52 “(b) Once per quarter, the Executive Director of the Library (“Director”) shall report to the
53 Board of Library Trustees (“Board”) whether any states have enacted laws substantially similar to
54 this section. If the Board determines that a law substantially similar to the provisions of this section
55 has been enacted in ten or more states, not including the District of Columbia, and the aggregate
56 population of such states equals at least 50,000,000 people, as enumerated in the most recent
57 United States decennial census, the Board shall direct the Director to publish notice of the Board’s
58 determination in the District of Columbia Register (“Register”).
59 “(c) Not later than thirty days after the publication in the Register of the Board’s
60 determination under subsection (b), the Director shall electronically notify the Council of such
61 determination and the date the requirements of subsection (d) are projected to take effect.
62 “(d) Beginning on the sixtieth day after publication in the Register of the Board’s
63 determination under subsection (b), the Library shall not enter into or renew any contract with a
