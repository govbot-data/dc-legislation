Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and approval by the Council of the District of Columbia is the "Health
Occupations Revision Exemption Emergency Amendment Act of 2025" and the "Health
Occupations Revision Exemption Temporary Amendment Act of 2025"and the accompanying
emergency declaration resolution. This legislation enhances the District of Columbia's status as a
world-class sports venue for international competitions.
The Federation Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) is hosting the upcoming Club World Cup
2025 which will feature three games played by five clubs from foreign countries in the District in June
2025. FIFA has therefore requested that the physicians who accompany these teams as an integral part of
the staff, who are not licensed in the United States but are licensed in good standing in foreign countries,
be able provide continued care to the members of their teams and staff while in the District. These clubs,
and other sports organizations that come to the District from foreign countries, often travel with their own
medical professionals who treat the team members and staff in training and during competitions.
Currently there is no statutory authority to allow healthcare professionals licensed in other countries who
accompany the foreign sports teams to the District to continue in their roles while in the District of
Columbia. This proposed exemption is limited to the healthcare professionals who accompany the foreign
teams, who are licensed in good standing in another foreign country, and who are only able to provide
care to the team members and staff. This legislation is necessary to continue attracting the world-level
competitions that the District of Columbia is well-positioned to host.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action at your earliest convenience.
If you have any questions regarding this matter, please contact J. Sam Hurley, Senior Deputy
Director at the Department of Health on (202) 671-0487.

15 To amend, on a temporary basis, the District of Columbia Health Occupations Revision Act of
16 1985 to allow an exemption from licensure for health professionals licensed in foreign
17 countries who accompany foreign sports teams for athletic competitions in the District of
18 Columbia.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 act may be cited as the "Health Occupations Revision Exemption Temporary Amendment Act of
22 2025".
23 Sec. 2. Section 502(a) of the District of Columbia Health Occupations Revision Act of
24 1985, effective March 25, 1986 (D.C. Law 6-99; D.C. Official Code§ 3-1205.02(a)), is amended
25 by adding a new paragraph (3B) to read as follows:
26 "(3B) To an individual licensed, registered or certified in good standing to
27 practice a health occupation in another country who is in the District to attend to the healthcare
28 needs of an athletic team from another country that is competing in a an organized sporting
29 event, as defined by section 3(3A) of the District of Columbia Smoking Restriction Act of 1979,
30 effective September 28, 1979 (D.C. Law 3-22; D.C. Code§ 7-1702(3A)); provided that,:
31 "(A) The time period during which the provisions of this act prohibiting
32 the practice of a health occupation without a District of Columbia license, registration, or

33 certification shall not apply to the individual (the “exemption”) shall not exceed 30 consecutive
34 days;
35 “(B) The exemption shall be limited to healthcare provided to the athletes,
36 support personnel, and coaching staff of the athletic team at the team basecamp, lodging, and
37 training sites and at the site of any matches within the District while the team is competing in
38 those matches; and
39 “(C) The exemption shall not provide the authority to the individual to
40 practice at a healthcare clinic or facility in the District.”.
41 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
