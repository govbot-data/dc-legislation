16 To amend, on a temporary basis, An Act To create a Department of Corrections in the District of
17 Columbia and the Corrections Oversight Improvement Omnibus Amendment Act of 2022
18 to designate the Department of Corrections as the District agency to provide a free
19 appropriate public education under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act and
20 District law to certain individuals in its custody and detained in its secure facilities.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
23 act may be cited as the “Special Education for Young Adults in the Custody of the Department of
24 Corrections Temporary Amendment Act of 2026”.
25 Sec. 2. Section 2(b) of An Act To create a Department of Corrections in the District of
26 Columbia, approved June 27, 1946 (60 Stat. 320; D.C. Official Code § 24-211.02(b)), is
27 amended as follows:
28 (a) Paragraph (9) is amended by striking the phrase “; and” and inserting a semicolon in
29 its place.
30 (b) The first paragraph (10) is amended by striking the period and inserting a semicolon
31 in its place.
32 (c) The second paragraph is redesignated as paragraph (11).
33 (d) The newly redesignated paragraph (11) is amended by striking the period and
34 inserting the phrase “; and” in its place.

35 (e) A new paragraph (12) is added to read as follows:
36 “(12) During school years 2025-2026 and 2026-2027, provide a free appropriate
37 public education (“FAPE”) under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, approved
38 April 13, 1970 (84 Stat. 175; 20 U.S.C. § 1400 et seq.) (“IDEA”), and District law, to individuals
39 with disabilities who are in its custody and detained in its secure facilities from the age of 18
40 through the end of the individual’s eligibility for FAPE under the IDEA and District law.”.
41 Sec. 3. Amendatory section 2 within section 10(a) of the Corrections Oversight
42 Improvement Omnibus Amendment Act of 2022, effective April 21, 2023 (D.C. Law 24-344; 70
43 DCR 635), is amended as follows:
44 (a) Paragraph (12) is amended by striking the phrase “; and” and inserting a semicolon in
45 its place.
46 (b) Paragraph (13) is amended by striking the period and inserting the phrase “; and” in
47 its place.
48 (c) A new paragraph (14) is added to read as follows:
49 “(14) During school years 2025-2026 and 2026-2027, provide a free appropriate
50 public education (“FAPE”) under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, approved
51 April 13, 1970 (84 Stat. 175; 20 U.S.C. § 1400 et seq.) (“IDEA”), and District law, to individuals
52 with disabilities who are in its custody and detained in its secure facilities from the age of 18
53 through the end of the individual’s eligibility for FAPE under the IDEA and District law.”.
54 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
55 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
56 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
57 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
2

58 Sec. 5. Effective date.
59 (a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
60 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto) and a 60-day period of congressional review as
61 provided in section 602(c)(2) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24,
62 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(2)).
63 (b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.
