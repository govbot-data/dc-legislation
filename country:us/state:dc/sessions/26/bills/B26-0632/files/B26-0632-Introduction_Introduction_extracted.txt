13 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Open Meetings Act to clarify the definition of meeting; to
14 provide for a public body’s ability to be briefed about potential terrorist or public health
15 threats so long as no official action is taken; to exempt from the act meetings between the
16 Council and the Mayor provided that no official action is taken at such meetings; and to
17 provide that a meeting shall be deemed open to the public if the public body takes steps
18 reasonably calculated to allow the public to view or hear the meeting while the meeting is
19 taking place, or, if doing so is not technologically feasible, as soon thereafter as
20 reasonably practicable.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
23 act may be cited as the “Open Meetings Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2026”.
24 Sec. 2. The Open Meetings Act, effective March 31, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-350; D.C.
25 Official Code § 2-571 et seq.), is amended as follows:
26 (a) Section 404 (D.C. Official Code § 2-574) is amended as follows:
27 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended to read as follows:
28 “(1)(A) “Meeting” means any gathering of a quorum of the members of a public
29 body, including hearings and roundtables, whether formal or informal, regular, special, or
30 emergency, at which the members consider, conduct, or advise on public business, including
31 gathering information, taking testimony, discussing, deliberating, recommending, and voting,
32 regardless whether held in person, by telephone, electronically, or by other means of
33 communication.
1

34 “(B) The term “meeting” shall not include:
35 “(i) A chance or social gathering; provided, that it is not held to
36 avoid the provisions of this paragraph; or
37 “(ii) A press conference.
38 “(C) For the purposes of the Council of the District of Columbia, the term
39 “meeting” means a regular or additional legislative meeting, and committee meetings where
40 votes are taken.”
41 (2) Paragraph (3) is amended as follows:
42 (A) Subparagraph (E) is amended by striking the phrase “: or” and
43 inserting a semicolon in its place;
44 (B) Subparagraph (F) is amended by striking the period at the end and
45 inserting the phrase “; or” in its place.
46 (C) A new subparagraph (G) is added to read as follows:
47 “(G) the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council.”.
48 (b) Section 405 (D.C. Official Code § 2-575) is amended as follows:
49 (1) Subsection (a) is amended as follows:
50 (A) Paragraph (2) is amended by striking the phrase “; or” and inserting a
51 semicolon in its place.
52 (B) Paragraph (3) is amended by striking the phrase “televised.” and inserting
53 the phrase "televised; or” in its place.
54 (C) A new paragraph (4) is added to read as follows:
2

55 “(4) The public body takes steps reasonably calculated to allow the public to view
56 or hear the meeting while the meeting is taking place, or, if doing so is not technologically
57 feasible, as soon thereafter as reasonably practicable.”.
58 (2) Subsection (b)(8) is amended to read as follows:
59 “(8) To discuss and take action regarding specific methods and procedures to
60 protect the public or a public body from existing or potential terrorist activity or other substantial
61 dangers to public health and safety, or to receive briefings by staff members, legal counsel, law
62 enforcement officials, or emergency service officials concerning these methods and procedures;
63 provided, that disclosure could endanger the public or the public body;”
64 (3) Subsection (f) is amended to read as follows:
65 “(f) Notwithstanding any provision of this act, the Council may adopt its own rules to
66 ensure the District’s open meetings policy, as established in section 402, is met with respect to
67 gatherings of councilmembers; provided that unless the Council adopts rules pursuant to this
68 subsection, this title shall apply to the Council.”
69 (4) A new subsection (h) is added to read as follows:
70 “(h) This act shall not apply to a meeting between members of the Council and the
71 Mayor, provided, that no official action is decided at the meeting.”.
72 (c) Section 406 (D.C. Official Code § 2-576) is amended as follows:
73 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended by striking the phrase "Except for emergency

75 provide” in its place.
