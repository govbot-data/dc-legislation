Statement of Introduction
Judith Heumann Memorial Workers with Disabilities Act of 2025
November 3, 2025
Today, I am proud to introduce the Judith Heumann Memorial Workers with Disabilities Act of
2025, alongside Councilmembers Matthew Frumin, Charles Allen, Brianne K. Nadeau, Anita
Bonds, Brooke Pinto, Janeese Lewis George, and Robert White. This bill is named in honor of
Judith Heumann, a disability rights pioneer who helped deliver landmark federal disability
protections, served as the first director of the Department of Disability Services, lived in the
District for over three decades, and advocated for this legislation. It establishes the District
Workers with Disabilities Program, which would allow individuals with disabilities in the District
to enroll in Medicaid even while earning income above the District’s Medicaid eligibility limits
This type of program, commonly called “Medicaid Buy-In”, are public healthcare options where
recipients with disabilities can continue receiving healthcare services as long as they pay modest
monthly premiums. Currently, aged, blind, and disabled individuals who earn more than $1,305
per month, or two-person households earning more than $1,763 per month, no longer qualify for
Medicaid and may face a choice between working and maintaining access to health care. ‘Buying
in’ to Medicaid allows people with disabilities to be employed without losing access to essential
health services.
Currently, 47 states operate Medicaid Buy-In programs.1 The District should strengthen its
commitment to equity and inclusion and recognize that workers with disabilities should not have
to choose between advancing in their career and maintaining health coverage.
The bill establishes guidelines regarding the application process, eligibility, premiums, coverage,
and program administration. Under the program:
• Applicants and recipients must be DC residents, aged 16–64, have a disability as defined
by federal law, and be employed. A temporary unemployment grace period is allowed. The
Department of Health Care Finance (DHCF) will determine eligibility.
• Monthly premiums are based on countable net income, tied to the Federal Poverty Level.
A hardship claim process allows temporary relief from premium payments for
participants.
• Recipients are eligible for health services covered under the District’s Medicaid program
through participating providers. Participants are not enrolled in Medicaid managed care,
specialty waiver programs, or long-term care exceeding 30 consecutive days.

29 To establish a District Workers with Disabilities program for employed individuals with
30 disabilities who meet specific eligibility requirements to enroll in Medicaid, to authorize
31 the Department of Health Care Finance to administer the program, to establish eligibility
32 requirements for applicants and recipients under the program, and to authorize applicants
33 and recipients of the program to appeal an eligibility denial from the Department of
34 Health Care Finance.

36 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
37 act may be cited as the “Judith Heumann Memorial Workers with Disabilities Act of 2025”.
38 Sec 2. Definitions.
39 For the purposes of this act, the term:
1

40 (1) “Assistance unit” means an applicant or recipient and the applicant’s or
41 recipient's spouse, when living together, whose eligibility for the program benefits is determined
42 in conjunction with each other.
43 (2) “Certification period” means the continuous period of enrollment for the
44 program, beginning with the date of approval and ending on the date by which eligibility must be
45 redetermined.
46 (3) “Department” means the Department of Health Care Finance.
47 (4) “Disability” shall have the same meaning as provided in section 1614(a)(3) of
48 the Social Security Act, approved October 30, 1972 (86 Stat. 1471; 42 U.S.C. § 1614(a)(3)),
49 except for consideration of substantial gainful activity.
50 (5) “Employment” means engagement in paid work, including self-employment,
51 subject to verification of compliance with applicable District and federal employment and tax
52 requirements, including the Federal Insurance Contributions Act (26 U.S.C. § 3101 et seq.) and
53 income tax obligations.
54 (6) “Hardship claim” means a request submitted by an applicant or recipient for
55 relief from payment of a required premium under the program, based on the individual’s inability
56 to pay due to financial or other extenuating circumstances, as determined by the Department.
57 (7) “Independence account” means a savings account, owned by a participant,
58 into which income may be deposited and used for expenses that promote independence and self-
59 sufficiency, which shall be disregarded for purposes of determining eligibility under this act.
60 (8) “Premium” means the monthly contribution required from a participant as a
61 condition of continued eligibility under the program.
2

62 (9) “Program” means the District Workers with Disabilities Program established
63 by this act.
64 (10) “Recipient” means an individual who has been determined eligible and
65 enrolled in the program.
66 (11) “Unemployment grace period” means a period up to 12 months, during
67 which an individual who loses employment involuntarily or for medical reasons may remain
68 eligible for the program; provided, that the individual continues to pay any required premium.
69 Sec. 3. District Workers with Disabilities Program.
70 (a) There is established the District Workers with Disabilities Program to provide certain
71 employed individuals with disabilities with Medicaid coverage, as authorized by section 1148 of
72 the Social Security Act, approved December 17, 1999 (113 Stat. 1863; 42 U.S.C. § 1320b-19).
73 (b) An individual is eligible for the program if they:
74 (1) Are between the ages of 16 years old and 64 years old;
75 (2) Are a resident of the District of Columbia;
76 (3) Provide documentation verifying employment, which includes one or more of
77 the following:
78 (A) Recent pay stubs;
79 (B) A letter from the employer verifying employment;
80 (C) Bank statements showing deposits of earnings;
81 (D) Recent federal and District income tax returns;
82 (E) Evidence of payment of estimated taxes, if self-employed; or
83 (F) Business ledgers, if self-employed;
3

84 (4) Has a disability, except for consideration of substantial gainful activity, as
85 determined by:
86 (A) The Department, if no Social Security Administration disability
87 determination has been made within the required time period; or
88 (B) The Social Security Administration, if the applicant or recipient:
89 (i) Receives Social Security Disability Insurance (“SSDI”)
90 benefits; or
91 (ii) Has lost eligibility for SSDI or Supplemental Security Income
92 (“SSI”) for any reason other than medical improvement;
93 (5) Is not:
94 (A) Residing in a medical institution or long-term care facility, such as a
95 nursing facility or intermediate care facility for individuals with intellectual disabilities;
96 (B) An inmate of a public institution; or
97 (C) An inpatient in an institution for mental illness; and
98 (6) Is not currently enrolled in:
99 (A) Medicaid;
100 (B) DC Healthcare Alliance;
101 (C) DC Healthy Families; or
102 (D) The Child and Adolescent Supplemental Security Income Program.
103 (c) The Department shall periodically review a recipient’s disability status and shall
104 establish the intervals for such reviews by rulemaking; provided, that the intervals are reasonable
105 and consistent with federal law.
106 Sec. 4. Financial eligibility.
4

107 (a) For purposes of determining eligibility under the program, an applicant’s or

109 (1) The applicant or recipient; and
110 (2) The applicant’s or recipient’s spouse, if residing in the same household,
111 regardless of whether the spouse is otherwise eligible for benefits under this program.
112 (b) To determine the countable net income of an assistance unit, the Department shall
113 subtract from the assistance unit’s total gross income:

118 (2) Any documented cost-sharing for current employer-sponsored insurance
119 coverage for the applicant or recipient.
120 (c) Applicants and recipients shall take all reasonable steps to obtain annuities, pensions,
121 retirement, and disability benefits to which they are entitled, including:
122 (1) Veterans’ compensation and pensions;
123 (2) Social Security Disability Insurance and other Social Security benefits;
124 (3) Railroad retirement benefits; and
125 (4) Unemployment compensation.
126 (d) Income shall only be used to determine premium levels for the program, and shall not
127 be used to determine program eligibility, except as necessary to comply with federal law.
128 (e) The Department shall subtract from the total countable resources the aggregate current
129 cash value of the applicant’s or recipient’s ownership interest in retirement accounts, including:

130 (1) 401(k), 403(b), Keogh, IRA, and pension accounts; and
131 (2) Any other retirement or independence accounts recognized by the Internal
132 Revenue Service.

134 shall register the account with the Department. The applicant or recipient shall provide a timely

137 (2) The total contributions to all independence accounts by an applicant or

140 (3) The Department shall assess and verify all contributions to an independence
141 account as part of eligibility determinations and redeterminations.
142 (g) An applicant or recipient may establish an independence account prior to applying for

144 date of eligibility shall be considered excluded resources for purposes of this program.

198 Sec. 6. Premiums.
199 (a) Participants in the program shall pay a monthly premium as follows:
200 (1) No premium shall be required for an individual with income at or below 138%
201 of the federal poverty level.
202 (2) An applicant or recipient with income above 100% and at or below 200% of
203 the federal poverty level shall pay $25 per month.
204 (3) An applicant or recipient with income above 200% and at or below 250% of
205 the federal poverty level shall pay $40 per month.
206 (4) An applicant or recipient with income above 250% and at or below 300% of
207 the federal poverty level shall pay $55 per month.
208 (5) An applicant or recipient with income above 300% and at or below 450% of
209 the federal poverty level shall pay 4% of net countable monthly income.
210 (6) An applicant or recipient with income above 450% and at or below 600% of
211 the federal poverty level shall pay 5% of net countable monthly income.
212 (7) An applicant or recipient with income above 600% of the federal poverty level
213 shall pay 7.5% of net countable monthly income.
214 (b) The Department may adjust the monthly premium amounts annually for inflation
215 through rulemaking.
216 (c) An applicant or recipient shall submit the premium within the time allowed, except
217 when a claim of hardship is pending. If a hardship claim is denied, the premium must be
218 submitted within 10 days of notice.
9

219 (d) The Department shall allow a grace period of up to 6 months within a 12-month
220 period for individuals who lose employment involuntarily or for medical reasons, provided the
221 individual continues to pay the applicable premium.
222 (e) An applicant who fails to pay the required premium within 30 days of notice shall be
223 terminated from the program.
224 (f) An individual terminated for nonpayment may not reenroll until all outstanding
225 premiums have been paid.
226 (g)(1) An applicant or recipient may submit a claim of hardship if payment of the
227 premium would compromise the ability of the individual or assistance unit to obtain basic food,
228 shelter, or clothing.
229 (2) The Department shall evaluate hardship claims within 30 days of receipt and
230 issue a written decision.
231 (3) If the Department determines the hardship claim is without merit, the
232 applicant or recipient shall pay the outstanding premium within 10 days of notice.
233 Sec. 6. Determination, notice, certification, and recertification period.
234 (a) When the Department determines that an applicant is eligible either through an initial
235 application or recertification application, the Department shall issue written notice specifying the
236 beginning and ending dates of coverage.
237 (b) If the Department finds an applicant is not eligible, it shall issue a written denial
238 notice specifying the finding, reason for denial, applicable regulation, and the right to appeal.
239 (c) If, during the recertification process, the Department finds a recipient is no longer
240 eligible, it shall issue a written notice of termination specifying the finding, reason for
241 termination, applicable regulation, any refund of enrollment fees, and the right to appeal.
10

242 (d) Grounds for denial or termination include that an applicant or recipient:
243 (1) Does not meet nonfinancial or financial eligibility criteria;
244 (2) Fails to submit a complete application or requested documentation within
245 required timeframes;
246 (3) Fails to cooperate with the Department’s eligibility determination process;
247 (4) Fails to pay the required premium or does not have an approved hardship
248 claim;
249 (5) Fails to comply with program participating requirements defined by the
250 Department through rulemaking; or
251 (6) Has died.
252 (e) Unless terminated earlier, a certification period shall end on the last day of the 12-
253 month eligibility period.
254 (f) The Department may extend a certification period if additional time is required to
255 complete redetermination of eligibility.
256 Sec. 7. Responsibilities of the recipients.
257 (a) If the Department makes a determination of ineligibility under this act, the
258 Department shall:
259 (1) Inform the applicant or recipient of their rights and obligations under this
260 Program; and
261 (2) Provide written notice of its decision, including findings, rights, and
262 obligations, consistent with the notice requirements applicable to the District’s Medicaid
263 program.
264 (b) A recipient or the recipient’s authorized representative, shall:
11

265 (1) Notify the Department within 30 days of any changes that may affect
266 eligibility for the program;
267 (2) Limit use of the program benefits to the individual whose name appears on a
268 health insurance card;
269 (3) Cooperate with the Department in providing forms and information necessary
270 for eligibility, program integrity, and third-party reimbursement;
271 (4) Provide timely notice to the Department of any accident or incident giving rise
272 to potential third-party liability, including the identities of parties involved, attorneys, and
273 insurers;
274 (5) Discontinue use of program benefits upon receipt of written notice of
275 ineligibility; and
276 (6) Cooperate with program integrity and quality control procedures.
277 (c) A recipient’s failure to comply with subsection (b) of this section may result in
278 termination of benefits under this act, recovery of improperly paid benefits, and other remedies
279 available under District or federal law.
280 (d) A recipient of benefits under the program shall:
281 (1) Be deemed to have authorized the Department to access records and
282 information necessary to pursue third-party reimbursement;
283 (1) Assign to the Department the right to collect available benefits from any liable
284 third party; and
285 (2) Assist and cooperate with the Department in recovering available third-party
286 benefits and payments.
287 Sec. 8. Scope of coverage.
12

288 (a) A recipient of the program shall be eligible for medically necessary services if the
289 services are:
290 (1) Furnished by a provider who is approved by the Department to participate in
291 the District’s Medicaid program;
292 (2) Covered under the District’s State Medicaid Plan;
293 (3) Not covered under employer-sponsored insurance or any other health
294 insurance available to the recipient; and
295 (4) Not subject to third party liability.
296 (b) The District shall not cover copayments, deductibles, or coinsurance applicable to
297 employee sponsored insurance coverage held by a Program recipient.
298 (c) A Program recipient shall not be entitled to:
299 (1) Enrollment in Medicaid managed care programs administered by the District;
300 (2) Enrollment in the Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly (PACE); or
301 (3) Coverage of long-term care facility services exceeding 30 consecutive days.

306 (1) The general Medicaid provider participation criteria established by the

308 (2) The service-specific participation and reimbursement requirements set forth in
309 Title 29, Chapter 9 of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations (Medicaid Program),
13

310 including provisions applicable to physician services, hospital services, home health care,

312 Sec. 10. Fraud, liens, recovery, and third-party liability.
313 (a) An applicant or recipient of the program shall be subject to the same prohibitions,
314 penalties, and procedures applicable to fraud and misrepresentation as specified under the section
315 3 of the Medicaid Provider Fraud Prevention Amendments Act of 1984, effective March 16,
316 1985 (D.C. Law 5-193; Official Code § 4-802).
317 (b) The Department may recover the cost of medical assistance improperly paid on behalf
318 of an individual under this program, including:
319 (1) Benefits paid for any period during which the individual was not eligible;
320 (2) Benefits paid as a result of misrepresentation, failure to report, or failure to
321 cooperate with program requirements; and
322 (3) Benefits paid pending the outcome of a hearing where the Department’s
323 adverse action is upheld, provided that recovery shall not be sought if the individual reasonably
324 believed the action of the Department was erroneous.
325 (c) The Department may impose liens and seek reimbursement against property or assets
326 of a current or former recipient to the extent permitted by federal law and District Medicaid
327 provisions governing estate recovery and liens.
328 (d) The Department may accept voluntary repayment from a current or former recipient,
329 or from a person acting on behalf of such recipient, for the cost of benefits improperly paid under
330 this program.
14

331 (e) A provider furnishing services to a recipient under this program shall seek payment
332 from any liable third party, including an insurer, before billing the Department, consistent with
333 federal Medicaid law.
334 (f) An applicant or recipient shall report to the Department any known third-party health
335 insurance coverage or other party that may be liable for the cost of care, and shall cooperate in
336 the Department’s efforts to recover such costs.
