Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia are the Medical
Cannabis Conditional Licensure Extension Amendment Act of 2025, the Medical Cannabis Conditional
Licensure Extension Temporary Amendment Act of 2025, the Medical Cannabis Conditional Licensure
Extension Emergency Amendment Act of 2025, and an accompanying emergency declaration resolution..
The legislation extends the expiration date on conditional licenses for medical cannabis cultivation centers,
retailers, internet retailers, manufacturers, couriers, and testing laboratories from 2 years to 4 years. This
will provide current conditional license holders additional time to find a permanent location and encourage
continued participation in the District’s legal medical cannabis market.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

14 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Legalization of
15 Marijuana for Medical Treatment Initiative of 1999 to extend the expiration dates of
16 conditional licenses for medical cannabis cultivation centers, retailers, internet retailers,
17 manufacturers, couriers, and testing laboratories for an additional year.
18
19 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
20 resolution may be cited as the "Medical Cannabis Conditional Licensure Extension Emergency
21 Declaration Resolution of 2025".
22 Sec. 2. (a) As of September 10, 2025, the Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis
23 Administration ("ABCA") and Metropolitan Police Department had summarily closed and
24 padlocked 71 unlicensed cannabis establishments. These summary closures resulted in record
25 breaking sales increases for the legal medical cannabis market with over $5.4 million in retail
26 sales to medical cannabis patients during the month of April 2025.
27 (b) Since January 1, 2024, the number of operating licensed medical cannabis retailers
28 has increased from 7 to 64, helping address this increased demand.
29 (c) As September 10, 2025, there were 8 operating cultivation centers, as well as 10
30 cultivation centers approved to operate that ABCA anticipates are likely to open in the near
31 future. Nevertheless, to address the inventory needs of new retailers and to discourage
1

32 participation in the illegal market where the legal market cannot meet demand, there is a need for
33 an increased supply of legal medical cannabis and medical cannabis products.
34 (d) As of September 10, 2025, there are approximately 51 conditional cultivation center
35 license applicants attempting to convert their licenses into permanent cultivation center licenses,
36 which would help to alleviate the current limited availability of certain medical cannabis
37 products. However, several of the conditional licenses have begun to expire.
38 (e) Making conditional licenses issued by ABCA valid for an additional year, for up to a
39 total of 3 years, would facilitate an increase supply of legal medical cannabis and medical
40 cannabis products.
41 (f) It is also important to extend the conditional licenses for medical cannabis
42 manufacturers, retailers, internet retailers, couriers, and testing laboratories on an expedited
43 basis, since these licenses are currently expiring. ABCA has received feedback from applicants
44 that the requirement to open and operate within the current term of the licenses is too difficult
45 due to challenges businesses face raising capital, finding an appropriate facility for their
46 business, and addressing other challenges related to opening the business including hiring staff
47 and building out their facility. As a result, an extension of the term of the licenses is necessary to
48 ensure that license holders have sufficient time to raise capital, find an appropriate location, and
49 open their business. The extension further encourages applicants to remain and participate in the
50 District’s legal medical cannabis market because licenses may only be applied for when made
51 available by the Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis Board.
52 (g) This legislation shall apply retroactively as of June 28, 2025, which is the first day
53 conditional licenses began expiring. This retroactive applicability date will ensure that all
54 licenses impacted by expiration benefit from the legislation and have their licenses reactivated.
2

55 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
56 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
57 Medical Cannabis Conditional Licensure Extension Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be
58 adopted after a single reading.
59 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
