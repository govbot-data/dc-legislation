Statement of Introduction
One Front Door Act of 2025
April 11, 2025
Today, I am joined by Councilmembers Pinto and White in introducing the “One Front Door Act
of 2025”, which directs the Construction Codes Coordinating Board, in its next code revision cycle, to
promulgate amendments to the building code that would allow for a single entrance/egress stairway for
multifamily residential buildings up to six stories. Similar proposals are often referred to as “single stair”
building code reform, though it does not preclude buildings with elevators.
Most building codes in the United States require two stairwells in multifamily buildings, “a holdover
from early fire codes, which focused on evacuating occupants; since then, material and building science
advances have improved our ability to isolate and suppress fires, and firefighting equipment has improved.”
This egress requirement does not exist in many global cities, as well as some within the US: Seattle,
Honolulu, and New York have allowed for single-stair buildings of 6+ stories for many years. The District’s
second-egress requirement, on the other hand, means that more of a building’s square footage is taken up
by non-residential space, which drives up construction costs and future rents.
Further, the efficiency of “point-access” buildings with a single stairwell makes it easier to enhance
the livability of individual units; it becomes much easier to build larger (3 - 4 bedroom) units and improve
access to light and ventilation.
Other housing units may simply never get built due to the District’s building code requirement.
Mandating a second stairwell means some projects are spatially infeasible, especially for smaller lots and
infill development.
In advancing this reform, the District would align with efforts in Virginia and Maryland. Similar
measures have been proposed or recently passed in California (AB835), New York State (S6573), Oregon
(HB3395), Washington (SB 5491), and Nashville (BL2024-181).

9 modifications to the international building code that would allow for a single entrance/egress
10 stairway to serve multifamily residential structures up to six stories above grade plane. In
11 drafting such rules, the Board shall consider:
12 (1) Ensuring adequate and available building water supply and fire suppression;
13 (2) The presence and response time of a fire department;
14 (3) Best practices in state, local, and international laws and codes that allow for
15 the construction of single-staircase buildings;
