16 To amend, on an emergency basis, section 47-813 of the District of Columbia Official Code to
17 provide for more timely classification changes for commercial properties that are to be
18 put to residential use, to provide for an application process to make classification
19 changes, to provide for appeal rights if such application is denied, and to provide for a
20 claw back in the event the real property is not timely put to residential use.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
23 act may be cited as the “Residential Building Permit Classification Emergency Amendment Act
24 of 2026”.
25 Sec. 2. Section 47-813 of the District of Columbia Official Code is amended to read as
26 follows:
27 (a) Subsection (c-9)(2) is amended as follows:
28 (1) Subparagraph (A) is amended to read as follows:
29 “(2)(A) Except as otherwise provided in this paragraph and subject to
30 paragraphs (4) and (5) of this subsection, Class 1A Property shall be comprised of:
31 “(i) Residential real property that is improved and its legal use is
32 for nontransient residential dwelling purposes, and that is not Class 1B Property; provided, that

33 such property may be used to host transient guests pursuant to an unexpired short-term rental
34 license endorsement issued pursuant to § 30-201.04; or
35 “(ii) Real property or a portion of real property for which a
36 building permit has been issued:
37 “(I) To construct a new improvement for predominantly
38 nontransient residential dwelling purpose; or
39 “(II) To substantially rehabilitate that portion or all of an
40 existing improvement for exclusively nontransient residential dwelling purposes.”.
41 (2) Subparagraph (C) is amended by striking the phrase “designated as” and
42 inserting the phrase “designated solely as” in its place.
43 (b) The lead-in language of subsection (d-2) is amended by striking the phrase “Class 3
44 Property or Class 4 Property,” and inserting the phrase “Class 2 Property, Class 3 Property, or
45 Class 4 Property,” in its place.
46 (c) A new subsection (d-3) is added to read as follows:
47 “(d-3)(1) If real property changes classification to Class 1A Property as defined in
48 subsection (c-9)(2)(A)(ii) during the period:
49 “(A) October 1 through March 31 of the tax year, the newly reclassified
50 Class 1A Property, or the portion thereof used exclusively for residential purposes if already
51 improved or assessed under § 47-829, shall be taxed at the Class 1A Property tax rate for the
52 entire tax year; or
53 “(B) April 1 through September 30 of the tax year, the newly reclassified
54 Class 1A Property, or the portion thereof used exclusively for residential purposes if already
2

55 improved or assessed under § 47-829, shall be taxed at the Class 1A Property tax rate for the
56 second installment only.
57 “(2) The owner shall apply for a change of classification to Class 1A Property as
58 defined in subsection (c-9)(2)(A)(ii) of this section and shall promptly provide complete
59 documentation in a manner prescribed by the Chief Financial Officer to substantiate any such
60 classification change before it shall be effective.
61 “(3) A change in classification to Class 1A Property as defined in subsection (c-
62 9)(2)(A)(ii) of this section shall be effective beginning in the half tax year for which application
63 is made under paragraph (2) of this subsection.
64 “(4) If change in classification to Class 1A Property as defined in subsection (c-
65 9)(2)(A)(ii) of this section is determined to be erroneous because the real property was not put to
66 predominantly residential use by the earlier of either the issuance of any new or amended
67 certificate of occupancy (temporary or permanent) for any part of the improvement thereon, or 3
68 years from issuance of the building permit, or the building permit expired and was not renewed
69 within one year, the Chief Financial Officer shall reclassify and tax the real property without
70 limitation for each tax year or half tax year in which the real property was improperly classified
71 as Class 1A Property, subject only to subsection (d-1)(5)(A-i) of this section, if the property is
72 determined to be Class 3 Property or Class 4 Property, or subsection (d-2) of this section. In the
73 event of such reclassification, penalty and interest shall be added beginning from the day the
74 correct amount of tax should have been due but not paid if the property had been properly
75 classified.
76 “(5) Within 45 days from the date of the notice of denial of Class 1A Property
77 reclassification under this subsection, the owner may petition for an administrative review of the

78 rescission or denial and appeal from a final determination thereof to the same extent as if the
79 appeal were filed under § 47-825.01a(d)(2).
80 “(6) Notwithstanding the time limitations in paragraphs (4) and (5) of this
81 subsection, the Chief Financial Officer, in his or her discretion, may grant an extension of time to
82 comply or waive penalty and interest assessed pursuant to paragraph (4) of this subsection to

84 “(7) The provisions of this subsection, subsection (c-9)(2)(A)(ii) of this section,

86 the upcoming tax year pursuant to subsection (f) of this section, when the annual assessment for

88 temporary) was issued.”.
89 Sec. 3. Applicability.
90 This act shall apply as of January 28, 2026.
