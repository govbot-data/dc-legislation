Jayme Menier
Ms. Jayme Menier has spent over 20 years of clinical practice in
swallowing, voice and head and neck cancer rehabilitation in the
Washington, DC area. She holds survivorship training, along with
certifications for MBS Impairment Profile. Her areas ofexpertise include
voice restoration including tracheo-esophageal placement for total
laryngectomy; objective assessments for swallowing disorders including
Fiberoptic Endoscopic Examination Testsand Modified Barium Swallow
Studies; swallow rehabilitation; voice analysis and rehabilitation.
Ms. Menier is the outpatient clinical manager of the Physical Medicine
and Rehabilitation department at MedStar Georgetown University
Hospital. She oversees the operations of the outpatient physical,
occupational, and speech therapy teams while maintaining clinical responsibilities.
Throughout her career, she has mentored graduate clinicians, co-authored a book chapter on the
aging voice, and served as a guest lecturer at area universities, including Georgetown University
School ofMedicine. Her certifications and specialized training include MBS Impairment Profile
certification, Fiberoptic Endoscopic Examination ofSwallow trained, and Survivorship Training
and Rehabilitation Certified.
A Ward 2 resident, Ms. Menier earned her Bachelor ofScience in Communication Sciences and
Disorders from Marywood University and her Master ofScience in Speech-Language Pathology
from the University ofthe District ofColumbia
