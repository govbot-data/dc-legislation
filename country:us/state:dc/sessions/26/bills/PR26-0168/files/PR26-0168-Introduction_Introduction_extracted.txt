Michael Dannenberg
Mr. Michael Dannenberg is the Massachusetts Department of Higher
Education’s Deputy Commissioner for Policy. Most recently, he served
as a Senior Fellow with the College Promise Campaign. Michael is a well-
known, long-time senior education advisor who has worked for various
public officials, including former Obama Administration U.S. Secretary
of Education Arne Duncan, Under Secretary of Education Martha Kanter,
the late Senator Edward M. Kennedy (D-MA) and Senator Claiborne Pell
(D-RI), multiple past Democratic Party nominees for President, and a
series of gubernatorial candidates. While a senior aide at the U.S.
Department of Education, Michael led work on various student aid,
college completion, and teacher education initiatives. While with
Senators Kennedy and Pell, he played a lead role in developing and drafting two Elementary and
Secondary Education Act reauthorization laws, the Education Finance Incentive Grant Program,
Taxpayer-Teacher Protection Act, and numerous elements of federal higher education and
appropriations law.
In between government stints, Mr. Dannenberg has run education budget and policy programs at
advocacy groups and think tanks, including the Education Trust where he was Director of Higher
Education Policy and the New America Foundation where he founded and managed the PK-16
Education Policy Program and Federal Education Budget Project. Michael and his work have
appeared in The Washington Post, The New York Times, Chicago Tribune, Boston Globe, Politico
Magazine, and The American Prospect. He has testified before Congress and multiple state
legislatures as well as made broadcast appearances on PBS, WNYC, C-SPAN, CNBC, MSNBC,
and the CBS news program, 60 Minutes.
A Ward 3 resident, Mr. Dannenberg earned his Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from Boston
University, his Master of Arts in Education Policy Analysis and Administration from Stanford
University, and his Juris Doctor from Yale Law School.
