_____________________________
Councilmember Brianne K. Nadeau
A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
_________________________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
_________________________
1 To remember, honor, and celebrate the life of Arturo J. Griffiths Jr., a long-time community
2 organizer and activist for the Latino community.
3 WHEREAS, Mr. Griffiths immigrated with his family to the United States from Panama
4 in 1964, settling in Washington, D.C., and;
5 WHEREAS, as a teenager, Mr. Griffiths co-founded the Latin American Youth Center,
6 which empowers youth to make a successful transition to adulthood via educational and
7 vocational opportunities and today serves more than 4,000 youth and families each year, and;
8 WHEREAS, Mr. Griffiths was a labor organizer with SEIU International Local 500,
9 Local 25 and in 2014 founded the Trabajadores Unidos de Washington D.C. (TUWDC) to
10 advocate for day laborers, many of whom were Central and South American immigrants, and;
11 WHEREAS, Mr. Griffiths created the Afro-Latino Institute, the city-wide Multicultural
12 Leadership Summit, the Save Our Cities, Save Our Children! March, and mobilized the “Jobs for
13 Youth” campaign to advocate for the community;
1

14 WHEREAS, Mr. Griffiths worked to pass a piece of key DC immigrant rights legislation,
15 the 2013 Driver’s Safety Amendment Act, which allowed undocumented District residents to
16 obtain a driver’s license, an economic lifeline for workers and families, and;
17 WHEREAS, Mr. Griffiths ran the Amigos Park Coalition for many years, advocating for
18 public space at the corner of Kenyon and Mount Pleasants Streets, NW for the Mount Pleasant
19 community, and;
20 WHEREAS, Mr. Griffiths dedicated his life to uplifting youth, fighting for the working
21 class, and supporting D.C.’s immigrant community, and;
22 WHEREAS, Mr. Griffiths was a beloved partner, father, grandfather, neighbor and
23 friend, and;
24 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
25 resolution may be cited as the “Arturo Griffiths Memorial Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
26 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia remembers, honors, and celebrates the life
27 of Mr. Arturo Griffiths and his significant contributions to the community.
28 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
29 the District of Columbia Register.
