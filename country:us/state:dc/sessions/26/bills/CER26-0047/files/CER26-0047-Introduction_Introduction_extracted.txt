20 To recognize and honor the life, accomplishments, and legacy of Ronald R. Collins.
21
22 WHEREAS, Ronald R. Collins was born in Washington, DC, on May 30, 1954, to Shirley
23 Hughes Brown and the late Ronald C. Collins;
24 WHEREAS, Mr. Collins attended St. Anthony’s High School, where he notably
25 represented the school on the student quiz show, It's Academic, and began to nurture his deep
26 Christian faith as a Catholic;
27 WHEREAS, he continued his education at the University of Scranton, earning his
28 bachelor’s degree summa cum laude, and went on to attend the Penn State Dickinson Law School,
29 earning his Juris Doctor;
30 WHEREAS, Mr. Collins dedicated his life and career to public service and the betterment
31 of the District and the communities he called home, starting his career in politics by holding
32 leadership roles with the Ward 2 Democrats;
33 WHEREAS, following redistricting, which placed Mr. Collins in Ward 6, he would go on
34 to serve, from 2006 to 2018, as the Ward’s Democratic State Committeeman, and chair the
1

35 Constitution and By-Laws Committee;
36 WHEREAS, Mr. Collins served in elected office as Commissioner for Advisory
37 Neighborhood Commission 6D representing Southwest, including serving as the commission’s
38 treasurer;
39 WHEREAS, Mr. Collins began distinguished government service at the Council of the
40 District of Columbia as legal counsel and later Chief of Staff to the late H. R. Crawford, Ward 7
41 Councilmember, from 1980 to 1992, and years later, from 2007 to 2011, would continue his
42 Council service as Deputy Assistant Secretary to the Council;
43 WHEREAS, Mr. Collins served in the executive branch under several mayoral
44 administrations, including, under Mayor Sharon Pratt, as legal counsel and Chief of Staff to the
45 Department of Human Services Director Vincent C. Gray, under Mayor Anthony Williams, as
46 Director of the Office of Boards and Commissions from 2003-2007, and in 2011, under Mayor
47 Vincent C. Gray, was again appointed to head the Office of Boards and Commissions, before
48 retiring from District government service in 2012;
49 WHEREAS, Mr. Collins was known for his passion for English football, notably the
50 Manchester City Football Club, and was best recognized proudly sporting a University of Scranton
51 sweatshirt, a symbol of deep devotion to his alma mater;
52 WHEREAS, Mr. Collins's work and presence had a positive impact on Wards 2 and 6, and
53 the government of the District across several mayoral administrations, leaving a lasting legacy of
54 leadership, integrity, and dedication;
55 WHEREAS, residents of the District that knew him best recount that, “Ron was the type
56 of person you were proud to call a friend. Although the years have passed since our paths have
57 crossed, I will always consider Ron a true friend,” and “Ron was a true spirit…He was independent
2

58 but always ready to lend a helping hand”;
59 WHEREAS, Mr. Collins’ presence and impact on the Ward 6 Southwest community is
60 shared by a resident’s reflection: “We are grateful for his service to the people of Southwest and
61 for the wisdom and decorum he brought to ANC 6D deliberations”;
62 WHEREAS, Mr. Collins's legacy will continue to be felt through his former Council
63 colleagues and their fond memories of his diligent work ethic and yet humorous nature, former
64 ANC 6D colleagues who will tell the story of his wisdom and decorum during deliberations, and
65 countless residents and constituents who will recount how he helped them solve a problem or get
66 connected to the right services; and
67 WHEREAS, Mr. Collin’s memory will be a blessing to all who knew him, as he was a
68 kind, generous soul full of commitment, compassion, and an unwavering faith, who always greeted
69 you with a warm smile and was able to bring joy to those around him.
70 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
71 resolution may be cited as the "Ronald Collins Posthumous Recognition Resolution of 2025".
72 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the life and
73 accomplishments of Ronald R. Collins.
74 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
