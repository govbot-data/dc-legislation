Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is an
emergency bill, the “Modification Nos. M02, M03, M03a, and M04 to Contract No. CW117619
with RCM of Washington, Inc. Approval and Payment Authorization Emergency Act of 2026,”
and the accompanying emergency declaration resolution.
The legislation will approve Modification Nos. M02, M03, M03a, and M04 to Contract No.
CW117619 with RCM of Washington, Inc., to provide residential services to citizens with
intellectual and developmental disabilities, and to increase the not-to-exceed amount for option
year one of the contract in the amount of $1,028,121. In addition, the legislation will approve
payment for the goods and services received and to be received under the modifications.
My administration is available to discuss any questions you may have regarding the proposed
contract modification. In order to facilitate a response to any questions you may have, please
have your staff contact Marc Scott, Chief Operating Officer, Office of Contracting and
Procurement, at (202) 724-8759.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The service provider shall provide residential services in the base year to citizens with intellectual
and developmental disabilities. The residential expenses will be funded by local appropriations in
conjunction with residential habilitation or supported living occupancy-related residential expenses
funded by the Medicaid 1915(c) Home and Community Based Services Waiver. Except for
individuals not eligible for Medicaid, the HCA will cover room, board and other occupancy-related
expenses, while Medicaid covers direct care services including nursing services, basic meal
preparation, nutritional services, assessments, psychiatric services, psychological services, care and
maintenance of adaptive equipment, transportation to and from day programs, medical and court
appointments.
(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:
A request for qualifications was issued under PASS/ARIBA Doc702201 on March 18, 2024. The
closing date for applications was set for March 25, 2024. Thirty-two Medicaid Waiver providers
submitted applications in response to the FY 2024 request for qualifications.
The contracting officer certified the financial and professional responsibility of the service provider
for the option year based upon the criteria set forth in 27 DCMR §1905.4. Based upon the criteria
outlined in §1905.4, the ability to maintain the approval of its Medicaid waiver application by the
Department on Disability Services, Developmental Disabilities Administration, Office of Provider
Resource Management and the Department of Health Care Finance, and satisfactory completion of

(G) The background and qualifications of the proposed contractor, including its organization,
financial stability, personnel, and performance on past or current government or private
sector contracts with requirements similar to those of the proposed contract:
(1) The service provider is a women-owned, certified small disadvantaged, for-profit business
providing services and supports in the District of Columbia (District) to people with
intellectual and developmental disabilities. Incorporated in the District on January 8, 1998,
RCM of Washington, Inc. services and supports afford those served with a conduit toward
empowering independence and revitalized membership and integration to the communities.
The provider utilizes an approach that offers services needed to set attainable goals and
develop action plans toward maintaining stable, safe and affordable housing, gaining
sustainable employment and living the lives of people that are served by DDS want, within
their community.
(2) The Department of Health Care Finance has determined the service provider qualified to
receive the Medicaid Waiver payments to provide residential habilitation and supported living
services based on the qualifications of staff identified as critical to organizational performance.
The Medicaid Waiver certification is a prerequisite to qualifying for the HCA that covers
occupancy-related expenses.
(3) The service provider has the necessary financial resources to perform the required supports
according to a review of its Dun and Bradstreet’s Risk Analytics report; dated March 27, 2026.
The report assigns a “high risk” rating to the “Data Depth Indicator,” which is one of three
categories that comprise the Viability Rating. The category represents the quantity and quality
of information Dun & Bradstreet has in its database regarding the Contractor. Despite the
assigned rating, the Contractor has been providing services for the agency since 2008 without
defaulting on any of its obligations. It continues to maintain its Medicaid waiver provider
agreement and receives satisfactory provider certification reviews that specifically examine
fiscal stability. Any risk associated with the aforementioned ratings is mitigated by the fact
that DDS has 72 additional active agreements to provide the same service.
