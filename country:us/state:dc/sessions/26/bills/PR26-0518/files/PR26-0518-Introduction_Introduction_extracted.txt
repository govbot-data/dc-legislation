15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend, on an emergency basis,
16 the Strengthening Traffic Enforcement, Education, and Responsibility (“STEER”)
17 Amendment Act of 2024 to modify its applicability clause to correctly reflect its funding
18 status with respect to the District’s Intelligent Speed Assistance Program.
19
20 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 resolution may be cited as “STEER Clarification Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
22 Sec. 2. (a) On February 6, 2024, the Council passed the Strengthening Traffic Enforcement,
23 Education, and Responsibility Amendment Act of 2024, effective April 20, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-
24 161; 71 DCR 5020) (“STEER Act”).
25 (b) The STEER Act, among other things, established an Intelligent Speed Assistance
26 Program (“ISAP”) in the District. The ISAP, to be administered by the Department of Motor
27 Vehicles (“DMV”), will install and monitor a driver’s compliance with intelligent speed assistance
28 systems if the driver was convicted of certain driving offenses. The STEER Act also modernized
29 the District’s existing Ignition Interlock Device Program.
30 (c) More specifically, the STEER Act amended sections 37 and 38 of the Motor Vehicle
31 Safety Responsibility Act (D.C. Official Code §§ 50-1301.37 and 50-1301.38) to modify the
32 conditions under which the DMV may suspend or reinstate a driver’s license or vehicle registration,

33 respectively. Sections 37 and 38, as amended by the STEER Act, work together to establish
34 predicate offenses that require enrollment in the newly established ISAP as a condition of
35 reinstatement.
36 (d) The ISAP was funded during the Fiscal Year 2024 budget process, and the DMV began
37 working on regulations to implement the program. The updates to the Ignition Interlock Device
38 Program were funded during the Fiscal Year 2026 budget process, though funding for
39 implementation is not actually available to the DMV until Fiscal Year 2027.
40 (e) When updating the STEER Act’s applicability clause to reflect its funding status, the
41 Fiscal Year 2026 Budget Support Act of 2025 left sections 37 and 38 of the Motor Vehicle Safety
42 Responsibility Act subject to appropriations, since funding for the modernized Ignition Interlock
43 Device Program was pending. However, since the DMV relies on sections 37 and 38 to enroll
44 drivers into ISAP, and those key provisions are currently considered to be unfunded, the DMV has
45 paused the launch of the ISAP until those provisions are funded or substantially similar provisions
46 go into effect.
47 (f) To allow ISAP implementation to move forward, this emergency legislation modifies
48 the applicability clause for the STEER Act to reflect that certain provisions are, in fact, fully funded
49 with respect to the implementation of the ISAP (or other purposes), but are still subject to
50 appropriations with respect to the District’s current Ignition Interlock Device Program.
51 Sec. 3. The Council finds the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency
52 circumstances making it necessary that the “STEER Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of
53 2026” be adopted after a single reading.
54 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
