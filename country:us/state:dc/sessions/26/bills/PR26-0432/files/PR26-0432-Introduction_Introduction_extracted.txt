Amy Laitinen
Ms. Amy Laitinen is Director for Higher Education at New America,
where she leads a nationally recognized team of researchers, writers, and
advocates dedicated to creating a higher education system that is
accessible, affordable, equitable, and accountable for helping students
lead fulfilling and economically secure lives.
Ms. Laitinen previously served as Senior Advisor for Postsecondary
Policy and Finance at the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, where she
designed federal and state strategy and oversaw grantmaking to advance
equitable postsecondary finance reform. She also held senior policy roles
in the Obama administration, serving as Senior Advisor to the
Undersecretary at the U.S. Department of Education and as Advisor to the
Second Lady in the Office of the Vice President.
For over a decade, Ms. Laitinen has served on the Finalist Selection Committee for the Aspen
Prize for Community College Excellence, a biennial award that recognizes exceptional community
colleges that achieve high and improving levels of student success.
A Ward 4 resident, Ms. Laitinen earned her Associate of Arts from Miami Dade Community
College, her Bachelor of Arts from New College of Florida, and her Master of Public Policy from
the University of California, Berkeley.
