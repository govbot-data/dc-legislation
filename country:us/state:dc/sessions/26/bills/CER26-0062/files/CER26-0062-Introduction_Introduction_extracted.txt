34 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
35
36 _________________________
37
38 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA

42
1

43 To recognize the history and the importance of DC Black Pride, and to celebrate the
44 contributions of the first Black Pride celebration in the District of Columbia to the
45 national and international recognition of the Black LGBTQIA+ community, including the
46 establishment of the Center for Black Equity.
47
48 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is an annual event to increase awareness of and showcase
49 pride in the diversity of the LGBTQIA+ populations in the African American community, and to
50 support organizations that focus on health disparities, education, youth, and families;
51
52 WHEREAS, from 1975 until 1990, the Club House was a premiere community gathering
53 space for the African-American lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender community of
54 Washington, DC and was notable for its annual Memorial Day weekend celebration, called the
55 Children’s Hour;
56
57 WHEREAS, Wilmore Cook, Theodore Kirkland, and Ernest Hopkins envisioned creating
58 an event that would continue the tradition of the Children’s Hour as well as raise much-needed
59 funds for the HIV/AIDS organizations that served the African American community after the
60 Club House closed its doors in 1990;
61
62 WHEREAS, with the Children’s Hour as inspiration, the inaugural DC Black Pride was
63 held on May 25, 1991, in cooperation with the DC Coalition of Black Lesbians and Gay Men
64 and the Inner-City AIDS Network on the grounds of Banneker Field;
65
66 WHEREAS, as the very first Black Pride festival in the world, DC Black Pride fostered
67 the beginning of the Center for Black Equity (formerly known as the International Federation of
68 Black Prides, Inc. (IFBP)) and the “Black Pride Movement”;
69
70 WHEREAS, as DC Black Pride grew in popularity and in programmatic offerings,
71 organizers created a board of directors, created a 501(c)3 nonprofit organization, and officially
72 established the Black Lesbian & Gay Pride Day, Inc.;
73
74 WHEREAS, during the 1998 festival, Black Lesbian & Gay Pride Day, Inc., and
75 organizers of Black Prides from New York City, Detroit, Atlanta and several other cities formed
76 the International Federation of Black Prides, Inc.;
77
78 WHEREAS, the International Federation of Black Prides later became the Center for
79 Black Equity, and today the organization has more than 50 members and serves as the principal
80 body through which organizers find support, raise funds, and share best practices;
81
82 WHEREAS, since the birth of DC Black Pride, more than 50 other Black Pride
83 celebrations now take place throughout the world and on 4 continents, with many using DC
84 Black Pride as a model;
85
86 WHEREAS, May 23, 2025, through May 26, 2025, marks the 34th annual DC Black
87 Pride celebration;
2

88
89 WHEREAS, the theme for this year’s celebration is “BLACK PRIDE IS FREEDOM”;
90
91 WHEREAS, for more than 30 years, DC Black Pride has been an opportunity for the
92 Black LGBTQIA+ community to gather and celebrate being both African American and
93 members of LGBTQIA+ communities;
94
95 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is the oldest and one of the largest Black Pride events in the
96 world, drawing thousands of residents and visitors from around the globe; and
97
98 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride brings more than 500,000 people to the nation’s capital
99 from across the United States, Canada, the Caribbean, South Africa, Great Britain, France,
100 Germany, and the Netherlands and will carry that tradition this year and into the future to
101 celebrate their shared community, raise awareness and funding for HIV/AIDS prevention, and
102 stand against homophobia inspired by violence and bigotry.
103
104 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That
105 this resolution be cited as the “DC Black Pride Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
106
107 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the hard work of
108 everyone involved in organizing the 34th annual DC Black Pride and the opportunity to celebrate
109 the Black LGBTQIA+ community.
110
111 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
