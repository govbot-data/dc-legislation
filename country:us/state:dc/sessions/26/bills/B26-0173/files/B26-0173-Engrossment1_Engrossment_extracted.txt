11 To amend Subchapter I-B of Chapter 28 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code to
12 provide limited civil liability, absent gross negligence or willful misconduct, to licensed
13 architects and professional engineers who, in good faith and without compensation,
14 render assistance at the scene of or in connection with a life-threatening emergency in the
15 built environment, at the direction or request of or with the approval of a federal or
16 District official, during a period of a public emergency declared by the Mayor or within
17 90 days following the end of such period at the scene of the life-threatening emergency in
18 the built environment.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 act may be cited as the “Architect and Engineer Good Samaritan Amendment Act of 2026”.
22 Sec. 2. Subchapter I-B of Chapter 28 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official
23 Code is amended as follows:
24 (a) Section 47-2853.01 is amended by adding a new paragraph (1A) is added to read as
25 follows:
26 “(1A) “Built environment” means the human-constructed surroundings that
27 support human activities, including buildings, transport systems, and landscapes.”.
28 (b) Part C is amended as follows:
1

29 (1) The table of contents is amended by adding a new section designation to read
30 as follows:
31 “§ 47-2853.69. Limited civil liability for voluntary practice of architecture at scene of
32 life-threatening emergency in the built environment.”.
33 (2) A new section 47-2853.69 is added to read as follows:
34 “§ 47-2853.69. Limited civil liability for voluntary practice of architecture at scene of
35 life-threatening emergency in the built environment.
36 “Any person with a valid license as an architect shall not be liable, in the absence of gross
37 negligence or willful misconduct, for any civil damages for acts or omissions resulting from
38 rendering assistance through the practice of architecture at the scene of or in connection with a
39 life-threatening emergency in the built environment; provided, that the person acted:
40 “(1) During a public emergency declared by the Mayor pursuant to § 7-2304(a),
41 or within 90 days following the end of the public emergency declared by the Mayor pursuant to §
42 7-2304(a) at the scene of the life-threatening emergency in the built environment;
43 “(2) In good faith and without compensation;
44 “(3) At the direction or request of or with the approval of a federal or District
45 official reasonably believed by the person to be acting in an official capacity; and
46 “(4) After having completed a training program that uses the California
47 Governor’s Office Of Emergency Services Safety Assessment Program to provide licensed
48 architects the ability to assist local governments in safety evaluation of their built environment in
49 the aftermath of a life-threatening emergency in the built environment.”.
