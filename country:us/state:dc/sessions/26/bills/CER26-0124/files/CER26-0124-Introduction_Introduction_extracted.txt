A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
______________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
_____________________
1 To recognize the life, service and legacy of Terry Lamont Bennett.
2
3 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett was raised in Southeast Washington, D.C., where his
4 roots, values, and purpose were firmly planted;
5
1

6 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett attended D.C. public schools throughout his entire
7 childhood—elementary, middle, and high school—and was shaped by the very community he
8 would one day dedicate his life to serving;
9
10 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett began playing football at the Boys & Girls Club 11
11 and continued competing in sports throughout high school;
12
13 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett was a natural leader and role model and earned
14 numerous accolades as a student at Ballou High School, where he excelled in multiple sports,
15 including baseball, track, basketball, and his favorite, football;
16
17 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett graduated from Ballou High School as valedictorian
18 in 2011, a testament to both his discipline and determination, and that same year, he made
19 history as the first student from Ballou High School to receive a Posse Scholarship, earning a
20 full-tuition award to attend Bucknell University;
21
22 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett continued to lead with purpose at Bucknell
23 University as president of the Black Student Union, an active participant in the Bucknell Student
24 Union, and a proud member of the football team;
25
26 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett was also known for his joy and creativity beyond
27 academics and athletics, earning campus-wide recognition as the University’s go-to DJ,
28 affectionately known as DJ TRaww or BabyShoes;
29
30 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett was committed to giving back to the community that
31 raised him and returned home to Southeast D.C. after graduating from Bucknell University in
32 2015;
33
34 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett worked at Ballou High School as a behavior
35 technician for two years while also beginning his journey as a football coach;
36
37 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett, driven by a deep desire to make a difference, made
38 the decision to join the Metropolitan Police Department in 2017, often saying he wanted to “be
39 the change” in his community;
40
41 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett graduated from the police academy in 2018 and
42 proudly served his entire career in the First District, earning recognition for his dedication and
43 service by being named Officer of the Month in 2024;
44
45 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett remained deeply committed to youth mentorship and
46 athletics outside of his law enforcement career, coaching football at Ballou High School for nine
47 years, leading the team to numerous victories and two Turkey Bowl appearances, and over the
48 years also serving as defensive coordinator, offensive coordinator, and most recently, assistant
49 head coach;
50
2

51 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett played competitive flag football for 11 years,
52 participating in both state and local championships;
53
54 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett held family at the heart of everything he did, with his
55 mom and dad as his greatest motivation and his loudest supporters who were present at nearly
56 every game, ceremony, and extracurricular milestone, always cheering him on; himself serving
57 as a devoted husband to his wife, Nadia, whom he met in 2014 and married in 2023; a loving,
58 hands-on father to their three-year-old son, Elijah; a proud and supportive uncle to his niece,
59 Janyia, celebrating her academic excellence; a loving uncle to his nephews; a devoted brother to
60 his sister, Keyonna; and as one who carried the memory of his late brother, Tracy, close to his
61 heart;
62
63 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett balanced life as a police officer for the city, a coach
64 for the community, and a family man at home with intention, pride, and unwavering
65 commitment;
66
67 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett often said, “Watch me shine,” and “I’m just a kid
68 from Southeast,” and he lived those words fully, proving that where you start does not limit how
69 brightly you can shine; and
70
71 WHEREAS, Terry Lamont Bennett’s legacy lives on through the lives he touched, the
72 community he served, and the family he loved so deeply.
73
74 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
75 resolution may be cited as the “Terry Lamont Bennett Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
76
77 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia honors and celebrates the life, service and
78 legacy of Terry Bennett, and declares July 21, 2026, the anniversary of his birth, as “Terry
79 Bennett Day” in the District of Columbia.
80
81 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
82 the District of Columbia Register.
