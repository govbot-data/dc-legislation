Statement of Introduction
The Jewels Way Designation Act of 2025
(DATE), 2025
Today, I am introducing The Jewels Way Designation Act of 2025, which proposes to designate
the street in front of Roosevelt High School (the segment of 13th Street NW between Upshur St
NW and Allison St NW) The Jewels Way in honor of Jewels, a trailblazing all-female vocal
group formed by students at Roosevelt High School in the early 1960s.
Originally known as The Impalas, the group—Sandra Bears, Grace Ruffin, Margie Clarke, and
Carrie Owens—began singing together at Trinity AME Zion Church while attending Roosevelt.
They gained early support from legendary guitarist Bo Diddley, who recorded their first single in
his home studio on Rhode Island Avenue NE. Renamed The Four Jewels and later simply The
Jewels, the group recorded several singles on local and national labels, including the Billboard-
charting "Opportunity" in 1964.
That same year, they were discovered by James Brown at the Apollo Theater and became his
first all-female backing group in the famed James Brown Revue. After a year touring nationally,
the group returned to D.C., who they worked with to produce two additional singles prior to their
disbanding in 1968. The Jewels reunited briefly in 1985 and released an album of their singles
entitled Loaded with Goodies.
The Jewels have been recognized for their contributions to D.C.’s rich musical heritage and are
featured in the award-winning PBS documentary The Jewels: The Divas of DC Doo-Wop. This
street naming honors their legacy and enduring influence on American music.
To ensure this tribute is both visible and meaningful, the placement of a sign at the walkway
located at 4264 13th St NW would serve as a lasting reminder to Roosevelt High School students
and the surrounding community of the powerful legacy and cultural impact of The Jewels.
