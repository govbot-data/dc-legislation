15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Personal Delivery
16 Device Act of 2018 to increase the weight limit for personal delivery devices.
17
18 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
19 resolution may be cited as the “Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Emergency Declaration
20 Resolution of 2026”.
21 Sec. 2. (a) The Personal Delivery Act of 2018, effective July 17, 2018 (D.C. Law 22-137;
22 D.C. Code § 50-1101 et seq.) created a permitting process for eligible entities to operate personal
23 delivery devices (“PDDs”) that do not exceed 90 pounds in weight.
24 (b) Since the adoption of the Personal Delivery Act of 2018, several companies have
25 developed new PDD models that exceed the existing weight limit. The weight limit adopted seven
26 years ago now presents a barrier to the introduction and use of newer PPD models, some of which
27 with enhanced hardware features.
28 (c) Many states already allow for the operating of PDDs exceeding 90 pounds in weight.
29 At least 12 states allow PDDs to exceed 500 pounds in weight, and another seven states that allow
30 for PDD operations have not established a weight limit in statute.
1

31 (d) On May 6, 2025, Councilmembers Charles Allen, Zachary Parker, and Christina
32 Henderson introduced Bill 26-0245, the “Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Amendment Act
33 of 2025 (“permanent bill”). The permanent bill would allow the operation of PDDs weighing up
34 to 275 pounds. The Committee held a hearing on the bill on October 16, 2025.
35 (f) To allow for the deployment of PDDs that exceed the current weight limit, the Council
36 passed the Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Emergency Amendment Act of 2025, effective
37 August 12, 2025 (D.C. Act 26-125; 72 DCR 8603) (“emergency act”). The Council also passed
38 the Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Temporary Amendment Act of 2025, effective October
39 1, 2025 (D.C. Law 26-48; 72 DCR 8945) (“temporary law”). The emergency act expired on
40 November 10, 2025. The temporary law is set to expire on May 14, 2026.
41 (e) This new round of emergency and temporary legislation is needed to prevent a gap in
42 the law between the expiration of the temporary law and the passage of a permanent bill.
43 Sec. 3. The Council finds the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency
44 circumstances making it necessary that the “Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Emergency
45 Amendment Act of 2026” be adopted after a single reading.
46 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
