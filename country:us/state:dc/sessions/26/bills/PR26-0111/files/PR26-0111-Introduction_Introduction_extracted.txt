Dr. Nancy Aboulmouna
Dr. Nancy Aboulmouna is a naturopathic integrative medicine
professional who previously practiced at the George Washington Center
of Integrative Medicine. She also began teaching graduate and
undergraduate nutrition at American University with a focus on health
inequities.
During the COVID-19 pandemic, Dr. Aboulmouna worked with the CDC
as a Quarantine Medical Officer in the Division of Global Migration and
Quarantine, screening and triaging cases of infectious disease at our
nation’s ports of entry. However, the most rewarding position of her
career involves Dr. Aboulmouna’s active civic engagement in community
health disparities as a board member of AIM Health Institute, a non-profit
dedicated to making Integrative medicine accessible to the most vulnerable populations of our DC
community.
A Ward 3 resident, Dr. Aboulmouna earned her Bachelor of Science in Global and Environmental
Change from George Mason University, and both her Master of Science in Human Nutrition and
her Doctorate in Naturopathic Medicine from the University of Bridgeport.
