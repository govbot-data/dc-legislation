19 A CEREMONIAL
20 __________
21
22 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
23
24
25 ____________________
26
27 To recognize and honor Dr. Maurice Jackson for his outstanding contributions to scholarship,
28 music, and public service in the District of Columbia and beyond, and for his lifelong
29 dedication to preserving and amplifying the history and cultural legacy of Black
30 Washingtonians.
31
32 WHEREAS, Dr. Maurice Jackson is a tenured professor in the History Department at
33 Georgetown University and an associate professor in the Black Studies and Music Departments,
34 with a particular focus on jazz;
35
36 WHEREAS, before entering academia, Dr. Jackson worked as a longshoreman, shipyard
37 rigger, construction worker, and community organizer, experiences that deeply informed his
38 scholarship and commitment to social justice;
39
40 WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson is the author of Rhythms of Resistance and Resilience: How
41 Black Washingtonians Used Music and Sports in the Fight for Equality (2025), Let This Voice Be
42 Heard: Anthony Benezet, Father of Atlantic Abolitionism, and co-editor of DC Jazz: Stories of
43 Jazz Music in Washington, DC, African-Americans and the Haitian Revolution, and Quakers and
44 Their Allies in the Abolitionist Cause, 1754-1808;
45
46 WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson has contributed extensively to historical scholarship through
47 numerous articles, including “Washington, D.C.: From the Founding of a Slaveholding Capital to

48 a Center of Abolitionism,” and has co-edited a special issue on jazz in Washington, D.C. for
49 Washington History;
50
51 WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson has made significant contributions to jazz history, writing liner
52 notes for albums by Charlie Haden and Hank Jones, including Steal Away: Spirituals, Folk
53 Songs, and Hymns and Come Sunday;
54
55 WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson has shared his expertise internationally, delivering lectures in
56 France, the Dominican Republic, Turkey, Egypt, Italy, Puerto Rico, and Qatar;
57
58 WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson has demonstrated an unwavering commitment to civic
59 engagement, serving as the first Chair of the DC Commission on African American Affairs
60 (2013–2016), a member of the Georgetown University Slavery Working Group, and Special
61 Assistant for DC Affairs to Georgetown University President Jack DeGioia;
62
63 WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson has been recognized for his public service, including induction
64 into the Washington, D.C. Hall of Fame in 2009, and has presented critical analyses on African
65 American employment, population, and housing trends to DC government;
66
67 WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson has preserved and shared vital historical materials by donating a
68 3,000-item movement pamphlet collection to Georgetown University, archives on the 1919 and
69 1968 Washington, D.C. riots, and a 300-item Black pamphlet collection to the Smithsonian
70 National Museum of African American History and Culture; rare Cuban jazz records to the Felix
71 Grant Jazz Archives at the University of the District of Columbia; and materials to the African
72 American Civil Rights Museum and the Rosenwald School Library in Gloucester, Virginia;
73
74 WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson’s forthcoming works, Halfway to Freedom: The Struggles and
75 Strivings of African Americans in Washington, D.C. and Michael Shiner: A Black Man in a 19th
76 Century White City: Washington, DC, will further illuminate the rich history of Black
77 Washingtonians and their fight for equality; and
78
79 WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson is not only an esteemed scholar and historian but also a devoted
80 husband to Laura Ginsburg and a proud father to Lena, a graduate of Georgetown University and
81 the National Cathedral School for Girls, and Miles, a graduate of the University of Michigan and
82 St. Albans School for Boys.
83
84 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
85 resolution may be cited as the “Dr. Maurice Jackson Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
86
87 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors Dr. Maurice
88 Jackson for his extraordinary contributions to history, music, and public service, his dedication to
89 preserving Black history in Washington, D.C., and his lifelong commitment to scholarship and
90 community engagement.
91
92 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
93 the District of Columbia Register.
