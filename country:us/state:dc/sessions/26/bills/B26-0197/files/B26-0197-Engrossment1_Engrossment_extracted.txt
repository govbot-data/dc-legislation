12 To require, on a temporary basis, that providers of commercial or residential rental property
13 offer and maintain any rent payment plan agreed upon between March 11, 2020, and
14 July 25, 2022, with eligible tenants, and to maintain the minimum requirements for such
15 a plan.
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
18 be cited as the “Tenant Payment Plan Phasing Temporary Act of 2025”.
19 Sec. 2 Tenant payment plans.
20 (a) From March 11, 2020, through July 25, 2022 ("program period"), a provider shall
21 offer a rent-payment-plan program ("program") for eligible tenants. Under its program, a
22 provider shall:
23 (1) Make a payment plan available to an eligible tenant for the payment of gross
24 rent, contractual increases agreed to in a commercial lease's rent escalation tables, and any other
25 amounts that come due under the lease during the program period and prior to the cessation of
26 tenancy ("covered time period"), with a minimum term length of one year unless a shorter
27 payment plan term length is requested by the eligible tenant;

28 (2) Waive any fee, interest, or penalty that arises out of an eligible tenant entering
29 into a payment plan;
30 (3) Not report to a credit reporting agency as delinquent the rent subject to the
31 payment plan;
32 (4) Provide that an eligible tenant does not lose any rights under the lease by
33 entering into the payment plan; and
34 (5) Notify all tenants of the availability, terms, and application process for its
35 program.
36 (b)(l) Tenants entering into a payment plan shall be required to make payments in equal
37 monthly installments for the duration of the payment plan unless a different payment schedule is
38 requested by the tenant.
39 (2) A provider shall permit a tenant that has entered into a payment plan to pay an
40 amount greater than the monthly amount provided for in the payment plan.
41 (3) A provider shall not require or request a tenant to provide a lump-sum
42 payment under a payment plan.
43 (4) A provider shall agree in writing to the terms of a payment plan.
44 (c) A provider shall utilize existing procedures or, if necessary, establish new procedures
45 to provide a process by which an eligible tenant may apply for a payment plan, which may
46 include requiring the tenant to submit supporting documentation. A provider shall permit an
47 application for a payment plan to occur online or by telephone.

48 (d) A provider shall approve each application for a payment plan submitted during a
49 covered time period in which an eligible tenant:
50 (1) Demonstrates to the provider evidence of a financial hardship resulting
51 directly or indirectly from the COVID-19 public health emergency, regardless of an existing
52 delinquency or a future inability to make rental payments established prior to the start of the
53 COVID-19 public health emergency; and
54 (2) Agrees in writing to make payments in accordance with the payment plan.
55 (e)(1) A provider who receives an application for a payment plan shall retain the
56 application, whether approved or denied, for at least 3 years.
57 (2) Upon request of the tenant, a provider shall make an application for a payment
58 plan available to:
59 (A) For residential tenants, the Rent Administrator and the Office of the
60 Tenant Advocate; and
61 (B) For commercial tenants, the Department of Licensing and Consumer
62 Protection.
63 (f)(1) A residential tenant whose application for a payment plan is denied may file a
64 written complaint with the Rent Administrator. The Rent Administrator shall forward the
65 complaint to the Office of Administrative Hearings for adjudication.
66 (2) A commercial tenant whose application for a payment plan is denied may file
67 a written complaint with the Department of Licensing and Consumer Protection.

68 (g) Tenant payment plans may not contain any waiver of the tenant's rights under the
69 tenant's lease or District of Columbia law. A tenant entering into a tenant payment plan retains
70 the right to contest the amount of rent due unless this is agreed to in writing by both parties.
71 (h) During the program period, unless the provider has offered a rent payment plan
72 pursuant to this section and approved a rent payment plan pursuant to subsection (d) of this
73 section, that provider shall be prohibited from filing any collection lawsuit or eviction for
74 nonpayment of rent; provided, that the tenant does not default on the terms of the payment plan.
75 (i) For the purposes of this act, the term:
76 (1) "COVID-19 public health emergency" means the emergencies declared in the
77 Declaration of Public Emergency (Mayor's Order 2020-045) together with the Declaration of
78 Public Health Emergency (Mayor's Order 2020-046), declared on March 11, 2020, including any
79 extension of those declared emergencies.
80 (2) “Eligible tenant” means a tenant that:
81 (A) Has notified a provider of an inability to pay all or a portion of the rent
82 due as a result of the COVID-19 public health emergency;
83 (B) Is not a franchisee unless the franchise is owned by a District resident;
84 and
85 (C) Has leased from a provider:
86 (i) A residential property;
87 (ii) Commercial retail space; or

88 (iii) Commercial space that is less than 6,500 square feet in size
89 and that comprises all or part of a commercial building.
90 (3) ''Housing provider" means a person or entity who is a residential landlord,
91 residential owner, residential lessor, residential sublessor, residential assignee, or the agent of
92 any of the foregoing or any other person receiving or entitled to receive the rents or benefits for
93 the use or occupancy of any residential rental unit within a housing accommodation within the
94 District.

96 landlord, non-residential owner, non-residential lessor, non-residential sublessor, non-residential
97 assignee, a non-residential agent of a landlord, owner, lessor, sublessor, or assignee, or any other
98 person receiving or entitled to receive rents or benefits for the use or occupancy of a commercial
99 unit.
100 (5) "Provider" means a housing provider or a non-housing provider.
