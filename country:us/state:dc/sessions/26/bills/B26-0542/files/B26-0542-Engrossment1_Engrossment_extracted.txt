11 To provide affordable housing developers seeking utility provider clearance in the building
12 permitting process with expert consultation, administrative coordination, and inter-
13 agency data transparency.
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
16 act may be cited as the “Powering Local Utility Guidance in Housing Act of 2026”.
17 Sec. 2. Definitions.
18 For the purposes of this act, the term:
19 (1) “DHCD” means the Department of Housing and Community Development.
20 (2) “DOB” means the Department of Buildings.
21 (3) “OCTO” means the Office of the Chief Technology Officer.
22 (4) “Qualifying affordable housing proposal” means an affordable housing
23 construction or rehabilitation project proposal for which the developer submits at least one of the
24 following to DHCD:
25 (A) Documentation demonstrating that the applicant has secured financing
26 or funds from the District or federal government that is conditioned on the applicant
27 providing income-restricted affordable housing units for a period of at least 15 years;

29 or funds from the District or federal government that would be conditioned on the applicant
30 providing income-restricted affordable housing units for a period of at least 15 years; or

39 (5) “Utility provider” means a business or government instrumentality that provides
40 electric, water, sewer, or fuel line service to residential buildings in the District and from which
41 DOB requires authorization before issuing building certificates of occupancy.
42 Sec. 3. Technical consulting for affordable housing utility permitting, administrative
43 assistance, and data transparency.
44 (a)(1) DHCD shall offer qualifying affordable housing proposals the services of one or
45 more utility design consultants, who shall be experienced in designing projects in compliance
46 with the residential design requirements of the various utility providers.
47 (2) DHCD shall clearly communicate to each applicant using the services of a
2

48 DHCD utility design consultant that such services are a courtesy, are used at the applicant’s sole
49 risk, and will not supersede the utility provider’s determination in the event of an error or
50 disagreement on the part of the utility design consultant. Failure to communicate the limitations
51 of the utility design consultant shall not constitute grounds for liability to any applicant on the
52 part of the District or any utility design consultant.
53 (b) DHCD shall offer qualifying affordable housing proposals the services of one or more
54 utility permitting liaisons, who shall assist developers with the administrative requirements
55 involved in obtaining utility provider construction approval for qualifying affordable housing
56 proposals, including by coordinating pre-construction utility provider disconnections,
57 coordinating utility provider inspections, tracking corrective actions necessary for obtaining final
58 utility provider approval, and maintaining communication with key utility provider personnel.
59 (c) Within 60 days after the applicability date of this act DHCD, DOB, and OCTO shall
60 execute a Memorandum of Understanding and data-sharing agreement among themselves and
61 with utility providers to implement subsections (a) and (b) of this section. These agreements
62 shall ensure that consultants have the access and prioritization necessary to fulfill their statutory
63 mandate and that residential developers can view timely utility provider inspection and review
64 updates through DOB’s system of record or another centralized permitting database. DHCD shall
65 consult annually with residential building industry stakeholders to update these agreements as
66 necessary.
