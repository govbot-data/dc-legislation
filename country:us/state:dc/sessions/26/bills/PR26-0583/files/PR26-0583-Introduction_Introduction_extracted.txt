16 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Retail Electric
17 Competition and Consumer Protection Act of 1999 to disallow electricity service
18 disconnection in a period of time after an order approved by the Public Service
19 Commission is vacated and remanded.
20
21 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 resolution may be cited as the “Rate Plan Vacatur Interim Protections Emergency Declaration
23 Resolution of 2026”.
24 Sec. 2. (a) In January 2026, District residents experienced an extreme hike in electricity
25 rates leading to a spike in shutoff notices and reports of residents’ utilities bills doubling or
26 raising hundreds of dollars in a single month. (more info on reports from residents)
27 (b) On March 5, 2026, the District of Columbia Court of Appeals vacated and remanded
28 Orders Nos. 22328 and 22358 of the Public Service Commission which approved Potomac
29 Electric Power Company’s (Pepco) multi-year rate plan and $123.4 million rate increase over
30 two years (Order No. 22328) and denied the Office of the People’s Counsel’s request to the
31 Commission for reconsideration (Order No. 22358).
32 (c) Given this, the Commission should restore Pepco distribution rates to those
33 established prior to Order No. 22328, last in effect in December 2024, as these were the last rates
34 that maintain legal standing, per a recent filing by the Office of the People’s Counsel.
1

35 (d) Until the Commission takes action to reduce rates to their previous amount and bring
36 immediate relief to ratepayers, District residents should not experience instability caused by
37 having their electricity service disconnected, which many are at substantially higher risk of
38 because of the inaction of the Commission. It is necessary to pause disconnection of electricity to
39 ensure that District residents are not unnecessarily harmed while the Commission and Council
40 weigh what actions should be taken to ensure electricity affordability in the longer term.
41 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
42 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making it necessary that the Rate Plan Vacatur
43 Interim Protections Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026 be adopted after a single reading.
44 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
