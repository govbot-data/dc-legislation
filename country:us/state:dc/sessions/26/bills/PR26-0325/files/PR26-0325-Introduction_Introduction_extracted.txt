5 A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
6 __________
7
8 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
9 ____________________
10
11 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Housing Production Trust Fund Act of 1988 to insert net
12 zero energy requirements for large residential and mixed-use construction projects
13 receiving Fund assistance; the Clean Energy DC Building Code Amendment Act of 2022
14 to require periodic reporting on the development of universal net zero energy building
15 code regulations that will apply to residential and mixed-use construction; and the Green
16 Building Act of 2006 to remove those net zero energy compliance provisions inserted by
17 the Greener Government Buildings Amendment Act of 2022 that apply to residential and
18 mixed-use projects.
19
20 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 resolution may be cited as the “Green Housing Coordination Emergency Declaration Resolution
22 of 2025”.
23 Sec. 2. (a) Under the Clean Energy DC Building Code Amendment Act of 2022, the
24 Mayor is required to promulgate rules by the end of calendar year 2026 that will impose a net
25 zero energy standard, including a prohibition on onsite fossil fuel combustion, on most new
26 construction and major renovation projects.
27 (b) On January 12, 2023, Mayor Bowser signed into law the Greener Government
28 Buildings Act of 2022 (GGBA), which, among other things, compels District government
29 agencies to achieve net zero energy compliance in most new or substantially improved
30 government-owned and government-funded buildings.
31 (c) The Council funded the GGBA as part of the Fiscal Year 2024 budget process.
32 (d)In light of earlier legislation, the effect of the net zero energy compliance provisions
33 of the GGBA was to accelerate the existing net zero mandate specifically for government-owned
34 and government-funded buildings.

35 (e) The Council, in consultation with the executive, affordable housing providers, and
36 local environmental advocates, has determined that the timing and scope of net zero standards
37 for residential and mixed-use construction projects should be adjusted.
38 (f) Large new-construction residential and mixed-use projects that receive funding
39 through the Department of Housing and Community Development’s Consolidated Request for
40 Proposals process should still be held to strong environmental standards, but these standards
41 should be applied through the solicitation and award process rather than through the building
42 permit process.
43 (g) The DC Housing Authority (“DCHA”) is also pursuing comprehensive energy
44 improvements to DCHA properties whenever fiscally possible via the Enterprise Green
45 Communities certification and on-site renewable energy systems. These improvements will
46 largely be implemented via rehabilitation projects, with smaller repairs being made to properties
47 within stabilization and preservation phases. The Agency is focused on delivering repairs that
48 prioritize resident health and well-being, in a timely manner. Additional time added to project
49 timelines, that may derive from the net zero energy exemption review process, could cause
50 delays in providing residents with the necessary capital improvements for their homes.
51 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
52 enumerated in section 2 constitute an emergency, making it necessary that the Green Housing
53 Coordination Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
54 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
