12 To require the Department of Housing and Community Development to create a tangled title
13 information sheet and to require funeral service providers to give the information sheet to
14 survivors of a deceased District resident; to amend Title 18 of the District of Columbia
15 Official Code to allow for the creation of a will registry; to establish a single family home
16 rehabilitation program; to amend Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code to
17 establish payment plans for the payment of delinquent real property taxes; to amend An
18 Act To provide for the abatement of nuisances in the District of Columbia by the
19 Commissioners of said District, and for other purposes, to amend definitions, factors for
20 establishing vacancy or blight, and exemptions from registration, to require the
21 submission of vacant building maintenance plans, and to allow the Mayor to petition the
22 Superior Court of the District of Columbia for vacant property receivership actions; to
23 amend the Abatement and Condemnation of Nuisance Properties Omnibus Amendment
24 Act of 2000 to provide a definition for the term “blighted area”; to amend the
25 Construction Codes Approval and Amendments Act of 1986 to require the Department of
26 Buildings to establish an expedited permit review process for certain construction and
27 building permits for Class 3 and 4 properties; to amend Title 47 of the District of
28 Columbia Official Code to establish new tax rates for Class 3 and 4 properties, to
29 establish a new process for tax sales of Class 3 and 4 properties, to establish a tax credit
30 for the renovation or rehabilitation of Class 3 or 4 properties, and to establish a tax
31 abatement for the renovation or rehabilitation of Class 3 or 4 properties that are used for
32 commercial purposes.
33
34 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
35 act may be cited as the “Vacant to Vibrant Amendment Act of 2025”.

62 Section 101. Definitions.
63 For purposes of this subtitle, the term:
64 (1) “Department” means the Department of Housing and Community
65 Development.
66 (2) “Funeral services establishment” shall have the same meaning as set forth in
67 section 3(11) of the District of Columbia Funeral Services Regulatory Act of 1984, effective May
68 22, 1984 (D.C. Law 5-84; D.C. Official Code § 3–402(11)).
69 (3) “Heir” shall have the same meaning as set forth in section 101(c) of the
70 District of Columbia Probate Reform Act of 1980, effective June 24, 1980 (D.C. Law 3-72; D.C.
71 Official Code § 20-101(c)).
72 (4) “Legatee” shall have the same meaning as set forth in section 101(g) of the
73 District of Columbia Probate Reform Act of 1980, effective June 24, 1980 (D.C. Law 3-72; D.C.
74 Official Code § 20-101(g)).
75 (5) “Survivor of the deceased” means any individual with whom funeral services
76 are being arranged.

77 Sec. 102. Tangled Title disclosure.
78 (a) No later than 180 days after the effective date of the Vacant to Vibrant Amendment
79 Act of 2024, as introduced on January 13, 2025 (Bill 26-53), the Department shall, in
80 consultation with the Superior Court of the District of Columbia and legal service providers,
81 create and make publicly available a tangled title information sheet detailing the steps necessary,
82 after the death of a real property owner, to legally transfer the real property to a decedent’s heirs
83 or legatees. The information sheet also shall include information on legal service providers who
84 offer low-cost or no-cost representation in estate administration cases.
85 (b)(1) No later than 5 days after the final disposition of the deceased, or at the time of
86 providing to survivors of the deceased any death certificate or death certificates ordered by the
87 funeral services establishment from the Department of Health, whichever occurs first, a funeral
88 service establishment shall provide the tangled title information sheet made available by the
89 Department to survivors of the deceased.
90 (2) A funeral service establishment that fails to provide a tangled title information
91 sheet pursuant to paragraph (1) of this subsection may be subject to a civil penalty not to exceed
92 $100 for each offense.

151 residence for at least 3 years;

160 in D.C. Official Code § 47–803(6).
161 Sec. 105. Single-family home rehabilitation program.
162 (a) The Department may offer single-family home rehabilitation grants to eligible
163 homeowners to pay for the following:
164 (1) Accessibility modifications to adjust physical barriers within the property for

168 reduction and replacing or repairing drywall or damaged flooring;
169 (4) Energy-related improvements;
170 (5) Repairs to the foundation or to address other significant structural damage; or
171 (6) Repairs to or replacement of electrical, plumbing, or heating and cooling
