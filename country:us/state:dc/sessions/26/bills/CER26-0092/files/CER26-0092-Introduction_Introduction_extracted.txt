21 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
22
23
24
25 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
26
27
28
29
30 To recognize and celebrate Higher Achievement on its 50th anniversary of service to middle
31 school students, to commend its founders, leadership, staff, mentors, and community partners for
32 their steadfast commitment and contributions the District of Columbia and beyond.
33
34 WHEREAS, Higher Achievement was founded in 1975 by educator Greg Gannon in
35 Washington, D.C., originally known as the Higher Achievement Program (HAP), with a mission
36 to expand rigorous academic opportunity to underserved youth;
37
38 WHEREAS, Higher Achievement has expanded its reach beyond Washington, D.C. to
39 include affiliates in the DC Metro area, Baltimore, and Richmond;
40
41 WHEREAS, Higher Achievement’s model includes afterschool programming, academic
42 enrichment, homework support, social-emotional skill development, and high school placement
43 guidance, all centered on rigorous expectations, caring mentorship, and scholar‐centric values;

44
45 WHEREAS, Higher Achievement is grounded in five core values. Scholar centricity,
46 racial equity, voice and inclusion, the power of community, and striving for greatness;
47
48 WHEREAS, in recent years, Higher Achievement’s impact has been affirmed through
49 rigorous evaluation: two randomized controlled trials have demonstrated statistically significant
50 academic gains for its scholars, including improvements in overall GPA and core subjects;
51
52 WHEREAS, today, approximately 95 percent of Higher Achievement scholars graduate
53 high school on time and 90 percent report a sense of belonging within the program and graduates
54 are 3x more likely than their peers to graduate from a four-year college;
55
56 WHEREAS, over more than five decades, tens of thousands of young people have
57 benefited from Higher Achievement’s educational support and mentorship, many of whom have
58 gone on to make lasting contributions in education, public service, business, law, medicine, and
59 the arts;
60
61 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
62 resolution may be cited as the “Higher Achievement 50th Anniversary Recognition Resolution of
63 2025.”
64
65 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors Higher
66 Achievement on its 50th anniversary of service to middle school scholars and for their
67 outstanding commitment and transformative impact in empowering young people across the
68 District of Columbia, Prince Georges County, Baltimore, and Richmond.
69
70 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
