14 To amend the Private Contractor and Subcontractor Prompt Payment Act of 2013 to prohibit clauses in
15 construction contracts that preclude contractors or subcontractors from stopping work if they
16 have not been paid for completed work within a reasonable timeframe, to establish a
17 procedure and minimum timeline for contractors and subcontractors to provide notice of
18 nonpayment and intent to suspend work, to establish notification requirements and timelines
19 for owners, contractors, and subcontractors to withhold payment for deficient items, to require
20 non-deficient items to be paid according to the District's prompt pay statute, and to require
21 owners, contractors, and subcontractors to notify other contractors or subcontractors of errors
22 in an invoice and to provide an explanation of the errors.
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act
25 may be cited as the "Contractor's Right to Stop Work for Nonpayment Amendment Act of 2025".
26 Sec. 2. The Private Contractor and Subcontractor Prompt Payment Act of 2013, effective
27 November 5, 2013 (D.C. Law 20-34; D.C. Official Code§ 27A-101 et seq.), is amended as follows:
28 (a) Section 2 (D.C. Official Code§ 27A-101) is amended as follows:
29 (1) Paragraph (1) is redesignated as paragraph (1A)
30 (2) A new paragraph (1) is added to read as follows:
31 “(1) Billing period” means a payment cycle agreed to by the parties or, in the absence of
32 an agreement, the calendar month within which work is performed.”.
33 (3) A new paragraph (2A) is added to read as follows:
34 “(2A) “Deficiency item” means work performed but which an owner, contractor, or
35 subcontractor, or any inspector authorized by an owner, contractor, or subcontractor, will not certify as
36 being completed according to the specifications of the contract.”.

37 (b) Section 3 (D.C. Official Code§ 27A-102) is amended as follows:
38 (1) A new subsection (d) is added to read as follows:
39 "(d) A contract provision that creates a default by the contractor for suspending work
40 when the contractor has not received payment pursuant to subsections(a), (b), and (c) of this section, or
41 has the effect of prohibiting a contractor from suspending work for a violation of this act is against
42 public policy and is void and unenforceable.".
43 (c) Section 4 (D.C. Official Code§ 27A-103) is amended to read as follows:
44 (1) The lead-in language is redesignated as (a).
45 (2) New subsections (b), (c), and (d) are added to read as follows:
46 "(b)(l) If payment is not received by a contractor within the deadlines established by §
47 27A-102 subsections(a), and (c), the contractor shall have the right to suspend performance of any
48 work, without penalty, until payment is received according to the terms of the contract.
49 "(2) A contractor may suspend performance for a violation of this act only if:
50 "(A) The contractor provides written notice to the owner or the owner's
51 authorized agent, via electronic mail or postal service, stating that the payment is at least 15 days
52 delinquent according to the deadlines established by subsections (a) or (c) of this section;
53 "(B) At least 15 calendar days have passed since the written notice in
54 subparagraph (A) of this paragraph was sent; and
55 "(C) The contractor has provided at least 10 calendar days' written notice, via
56 certified mail, of the contractor's intent to suspend performance to the owner or the owner's authorized
57 agent.
58 "(c)(1) An owner may withhold payment for deficiency items or disputed amounts
59 according to the terms of a construction contract. Notwithstanding the above, the owner shall pay the
60 contractor according to the provisions of this act for any items that appear on the invoice or payment
61 request and have been satisfactorily completed or are undisputed.

62 "(2) If an owner withholds payment from a construction contractor for a
63 deficiency item, the amount withheld shall be reasonable and the owner shall notify the contractor of
64 the deficiency item by a written explanation of its good faith reason within 14 calendar days of the date
65 that the invoice is received.
66 "(A) Failure to comply with paragraph (2) shall constitute a waiver of the
67 basis to withhold payment and necessitate payment of the contractor in full for the invoice.
68 "(d)(1) If an invoice is filled out incorrectly or incompletely by the contractor or if there

71 plainly list any deficiencies contained in the contractor's invoice or payment request.

73 apply until such time as the contractor resubmits an invoice or payment request that cures all
74 deficiencies listed in the notice required by paragraph (1) of this subsection.
