9 To amend Chapter 28 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code to establish
10 licensing standards and certification requirements for glazier and glazing contractors, and to add
11 2 members representing glaziers and glazing contractors to the Board of Industrial Trades.

13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
14 act may be cited as the “Glazier Licensing Standards and Certification Act of 2026”.

46 “§ 47-2853.100. Definitions.
47 “For the purposes of this part, the term:
48 “(1) “Glazier” means any person who erects, constructs, installs, alters, services,
49 or repairs aluminum windows, curtain walls, interior glass partitions, glass handrails, aluminum
50 entrances, skylights, storefronts, and general architectural glass and metal installation.
51 “(2) “Glazing contractor” means a sole proprietor, firm, or corporation who, for
52 compensation, engages in erecting, constructing, installing, altering, servicing, and repairing
53 aluminum windows, curtain walls, interior glass partitions, glass handrails, aluminum entrances,
54 skylights, storefronts, and general architectural glass and metal installation.
55 “§ 47-2853.100a. Eligibility requirements.
56 “(a) The Board of Industrial Trades (“Board”) shall regulate and license glaziers and
57 glazing contractors to ensure that persons engaged in the occupation conform to applicable
58 national safety and performance standards.”
59 “(b)(1) An applicant for licensure as a glazing contractor shall establish to the satisfaction
60 of the Board that the applicant:
61 “(A) Has under their employ no less than 3 licensed or temporarily
62 licensed glaziers who perform the work described by the applicant in the application;
63 “(B) Has complied with the bonding and insurance requirements
64 established by rulemaking; and
65 “(C) Meets any other requirements established by the Mayor through
