8
9 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
10 ________________
11
12 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the District of
13 Columbia Health Occupations Revision Act of 1985 and the Consent for Vaccinations of
14 Minors Amendment Act of 2022 to authorize healthcare providers in the District to order
15 and administer immunizations recommended by competent medical and public health
16 organizations designated by the Director of the Department of Health.
17
18 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
19 resolution may be cited as the “Community Health Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
20 Sec. 2. (a) The 2025-2026 respiratory disease season begins in the fall and will likely
21 cause an increase in COVID-19, influenza, and RSV cases in the District of Columbia. These
22 respiratory diseases can each be mitigated through safe and effective immunizations.
23 (b) The Department of Health ensures that District of Columbia residents and visitors
24 have comprehensive access to immunizations. A critical access point for immunizations are local
25 pharmacies, which operate without prescribers such as physicians or nurse practitioners on site.
26 (c) In recent months, federal actions have created uncertainty about whether pharmacists
27 may continue to administer certain immunizations without a prescription from an authorized
28 prescriber. Following these changes, several local pharmacies have announced they will not be
29 able to administer certain immunizations this fall without such a prescription. This is due to
30 current District law and regulations that tie a pharmacists’ ability to independently administer a
31 vaccine to federal immunization recommendations.
1

32 (d) Enactment of emergency legislation is necessary to ensure that pharmacists can order
33 and administer, and pharmacy technicians can administer, without a prescription, the
34 immunizations needed to protect the public health of District of Columbia residents, visitors, and
35 workers. Additionally, emergency legislation will allow people under the age of 18 to access
36 these critical immunizations as appropriate. Emergency legislation aligns District practice and
37 policy with previous respiratory illness seasons when pharmacists and pharmacy technicians
38 could administer key immunizations without a prescription.
39 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
40 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
41 Immunizations Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
42 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
