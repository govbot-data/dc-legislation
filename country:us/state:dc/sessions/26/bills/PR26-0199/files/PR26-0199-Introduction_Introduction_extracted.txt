Dr. Gillian Rai
Dr. Gillian Rai, originally from South Africa, is owner and founder of
SubLime Kidz, LLC, and teaches part-time in the Department of Public
Health Biology and Department of Exercise and Nutrition Sciences at
George Washington University (GWU), Milken Institute of Public
Health. She is also an online course designer at Rehab Essentials, Inc., an
affiliate and community partner of the GWU School of Medicine, and
Health Sciences, Rodham Institute, and the GW Autism and
Neurodevelopmental Institute (ANDI).
Dr. Rai possess over 22+ years of combined experience in academia, in
both professorship and administrative leadership roles and clinical
occupational therapy practice in the U.S. and Africa, working with
children, adults and families, teaching and overseeing educational activities for learners in diverse
and multicultural settings. Dr. Rai’s experience includes working as a member of interdisciplinary
teams, in poorer communities in high-, low- and middle-income countries such as the U.S., Ghana
and South Africa respectively, and with organizations such as Jirah Projects, Liv Village, Sisters
Mentors, Dipethogo Preschool, the Occupational Therapy African Regional Congress, the
American Occupational Therapy Association, the World Federation of Occupational Therapy, the
GW Rodham Institute and the GW Autism and Neurological Disorders Institute.
Dr. Rai’s research interests include:
• The impact of the environment and its effects on participation and quality of life.
• Family-centered care and community-centered occupational therapy practice.
• Disability in a global context.
• The impact of adversity (poverty-related stress) on learning in children ages 0-5 and
elementary school -aged children.
• Occupational justice (social determinants of health, global health, health equity).
Dr. Rai is an ambassador with Sisters Mentors, a non-profit organization where women and girls
of color are mentored to achieve academic and personal success through higher education resulting
in economically independent and meaningful lives.
A Ward 2 resident, Dr. Rai earned her Bachelor of Science in in Occupation Therapy from the
University of KwaZulu-Natal, her Master of Education in Early Childhood Special Education/
Learning Disabilities from Virginia Tech, and her Doctorate in Occupational Therapy from
Temple University.
