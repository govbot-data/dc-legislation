COMMITTEE ON HOUSING
ROBERT C. WHITE, JR., CHAIR
COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
Statement of Introduction
LIHTC Rent Stabilization Exemption Clarification Amendment Act of 2025
October 10, 2025
Today, alongside Chairman Phil Mendelson, Councilmember Anita Bonds, and Councilmember
Brianne Nadeau, I am introducing the LIHTC Rent Stabilization Exemption Clarification
Amendment Act of 2025. This legislation restores and clarifies a long-standing exemption under
the District’s Rental Housing Act of 1985 to ensure that Low-Income Housing Tax Credit
(LIHTC) properties remain exempt from the District’s rent stabilization requirements.
Since 1985, the rent stabilization law has included an exemption for “[a]ny rental unit in any
federally or District-owned housing accommodation or in any housing accommodation with
respect to which the mortgage or rent is federally or District-subsidized.” Ever since the LIHTC
program began a few years later, the District government and stakeholders have consistently
interpreted this exemption to allow LIHTC be exempt from additional local rent restrictions in
order to serve low- and moderate-income families under strict federal affordability guidelines.
However, a recent Superior Court ruling — Milestone v. Daniels (2024-LTB-7847) —
overturned this interpretation by distinguishing between direct and indirect subsidies. The
decision has introduced serious uncertainty into the affordable housing market. Without
legislative clarification, nearly 100 LIHTC-assisted properties — representing more than 11,000
affordable units — would be at risk of default. If we don’t act, we undermine one of our most
important tools for affordable housing preservation. LIHTC helps us keep older buildings
habitable and affordable. It’s how we bring in private capital to support affordable housing
without new local tax dollars.
The LIHTC Rent Stabilization Exemption Clarification Amendment Act restores the intent of the
law, protects tenants, keeps affordable housing financially viable, and preserves confidence in
the District’s housing programs.
