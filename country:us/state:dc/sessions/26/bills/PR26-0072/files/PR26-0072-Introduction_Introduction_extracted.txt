14 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Clemency Board
15 Establishment Act of 2018 to authorize the Clemency Board to, for applicants seeking a
16 pardon, grant a waiver of the 5-year waiting period in certain circumstances, and to
17 amend the Second Chance Amendment Act of 2022 to update the applicability date to
18 October 1, 2027.
19
20 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 resolution may be cited as the “Safety Cluster Resource Alignment and Clarification Emergency
22 Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
23 Sec. 2. (a) The Clemency Board (the "Board") was established in 2018 to determine
24 whether, upon application, to recommend that the President of the United States grant clemency
25 to applicants convicted of D.C. Code offenses.
26 (b) The Clemency Board Establishment Act of 2018 (the "Act"), the legislation that
27 established both the Board and the process by which applicants may seek a letter of
28 recommendation from the Board, was deliberately structured by the Council to mirror the
29 eligibility requirements and generally the process used by the Department of Justice, Office of
30 the Pardon Attorney ("DOJ-OPA") for accepting and reviewing applications for clemency.
31 Having the language of the Act mirror the federal process helps the Board avoid inadvertently
32 deeming an application ineligible that is in fact eligible under DOJ-OPA’s rules.

33 (c) Currently, under D.C. Official Code § 24-481.05(c)(1), to be eligible for a letter of
34 recommendation from the Board, an applicant must wait 5 years after the date of their release
35 from confinement or, in cases where no prison sentence was imposed, 5 years after the date of
36 the conviction. This statutory language mirrors DOJ-OPA’s eligibility criteria, which also sets a
37 5-year waiting period for pardon applicants.
38 (d) Board staff has recently learned, however, that the DOJ-OPA is authorized to grant
39 waivers of this 5-year waiting period, pursuant to § 9-140.112 of the Department of Justice’s
40 Justice Manual. The Act, as passed by Council in 2018, however, did not provide the Board with
41 similar authority to grant a waiver of this requirement.
42 (e) The Board recently received applications from applicants who have received a waiver
43 of the 5-year waiting period from DOJ-OPA. However, absent legislation amending the Act, the
44 Board lacks the authority to grant its own waiver and these and other applications will be deemed
45 ineligible for a letter of recommendation, despite being actively considered by the DOJ-OPA and
46 the President.
47 (f) Thus, this emergency legislation would amend the Act to authorize the Board to
48 consider an applicant’s request for a waiver of the 5-year waiting period where the DOJ-OPA
49 has already granted a similar waiver. Absent these changes, several applications before the Board
50 that could be considered by the President in the coming months will be ineligible for a letter of
51 recommendation from the Board, potentially negatively impacting those applicants’ applications
52 for clemency.
53 (g) Additionally, certain provisions of the Second Chance Amendment Act of 2022 are
54 set to go into effect on March 1, 2025, with the remaining provisions going into effect on
55 October 1, 2027. Multiple agencies, including the Superior Court of the District of Columbia, the

56 United States Attorney’s Office for the District of Columbia, the Office of Attorney General for
57 the District of Columbia, and the Metropolitan Police Department, would require additional time
58 to prepare for implementation as well as additional resources to implement the administrative
59 requirements of the Second Chance Amendment Act of 2022. Emergency legislation is therefore
60 necessary to delay the applicability date of the Second Chance Amendment Act of 2022 to
61 October 1, 2027.
62 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
63 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Safety Cluster
64 Resource Alignment and Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a
65 single reading.
66 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
