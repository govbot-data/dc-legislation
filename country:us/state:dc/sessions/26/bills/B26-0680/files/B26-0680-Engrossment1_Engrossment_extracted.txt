10 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Language Access Act of 2004 to include the District of
11 Columbia Water and Sewer Authority as a covered entity with major public contact; to
12 amend the District of Columbia Public Works Act of 1954 to require that the District of
13 Columbia Water and Sewer Authority submit information regarding disconnections to the
14 Council, the Office of the People’s Counsel, the Office of the Attorney General, and the
15 Office of the Tenant Advocate on a monthly basis; to amend the Water and Sewer
16 Authority Establishment and Department of Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996 to
17 clarify the imposition of charges and penalties for late payment of water service charges
18 and bills; and to amend section 431 of Title 21 of the District of Columbia Municipal
19 Regulations to modify the notice requirements to building occupants prior to a water
20 service disconnection.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
23 act may be cited as the “DC Water and Sewer Authority Billing and Disconnection Clarification
24 Temporary Amendment Act of 2026”.
25 Sec. 2. Section 2(3)(B) of the Language Access Act of 2004, effective June 19, 2004 (D.C.
26 Law 15-167; D.C. Official Code § 2-1931(3)(B)), is amended by adding a new sub-subparagraph
27 (vii-I) to read as follows:
28 “(vii-I) The District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority;”.
29 Sec. 3. Section 216(d) of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of
30 Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996, effective April 18, 1996 (D.C. Law 11-111; D.C.
31 Official Code § 34-2202.16(d)), is amended to read as follows:

32 “(d) The Authority may impose additional charges and penalties for late payment of bills
33 not exceeding a charge of 10% for any charges or bills remaining unpaid for more than 30 days,
34 and a penalty at the rate of 1% per month compounded monthly for any charges or bills that remain
35 unpaid for more than 60 days”.”
36 Sec. 4. Section 103 of the District of Columbia Public Works Act of 1954, approved May
37 18, 1954 (68 Stat. 102; D.C. Official Code § 34-2407.01), is amended to read as follows:
38 “Sec. 103. Discontinuance of water service for failure to pay water charges.
39 “(a)(1) The District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority (“Authority”), established
40 pursuant to section 202(a) of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of
41 Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996, effective April 18, 1996 (D.C. Law 11-111; D.C.
42 Official Code § 34-2202.02(a)), may provide for the collection of water charges, in advance or
43 otherwise, from the owner or occupant of any building, property, or other location furnished water
44 or water service by the District.
45 “(2) The Authority may shut off the water supply to any such building,
46 property, or other location upon failure of the owner or occupant thereof to pay such water charges
47 within 30 days from the date of rendition of the bill therefor, regardless of any change in ownership
48 or occupancy of such building, property, or other location.
49 “(b)(1) If the Authority has shut off the water supply to any property for failure to pay
50 District water and sanitary sewer service charges, it shall be unlawful for any person to restore the
51 water supply without the express authorization of the Authority.
2

52 “(2) The Authority shall impose a fine in an amount not less than 20% of the
53 delinquent charges or more than $100, whichever is greater, upon the owner or occupant of the
54 property, unless the Authority determines that the owner or occupant did not restore or solicit a
55 person to restore the water.
56 “(c) The Authority shall submit on the 7th day of each month, the following information
57 to the Council, the Office of the Attorney General, the Office of the People’s Counsel, and the
58 Office of the Tenant Advocate for the preceding month:
59 “(1) The number of properties that received a notice of disconnection;
60 “(2) Of those properties that were issued a notice, the number of properties that
61 experienced a disconnection;
62 “(3) Of those properties that experienced a disconnection, the number of properties
63 that had service restored;
64 “(4) The number of properties that entered into a payment plan;
65 “(5) The number of properties that have had bill payment assumed by the tenants
66 through a tenant association; and
67 “(6) The number of properties that have been placed in receivership.”.

69 DCMR § 431), is amended as follows:

71 the phrase “TO OCCUPANTS” in its place.
