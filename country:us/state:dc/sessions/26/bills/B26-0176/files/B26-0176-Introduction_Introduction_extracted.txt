17 To amend the Day Care Policy Act of 1979 to update the minimum salaries child development
18 facilities must pay assistant and lead teachers beginning in January 2025 to participate in
19 the Early Childhood Educator Pay Equity Program.
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 act may be cited as the “Early Childhood Educator Pay Scales Amendment Act of 2025”.

155 (e) A new section 15c is added to read as follows:
156 “Sec. 15c. Early Childhood Educator Equitable Compensation Task Force establishment.
157 “(a)(1) There is established by the Council an Early Childhood Educator Equitable
158 Compensation Task Force (“Task Force”) to provide recommendations on how to implement an
159 employee compensation scale for child development facilities.
160 “(2) Following the submission of the report required pursuant to subsection (c)(3)
161 of this section, the Task Force shall reconvene every 4th calendar year, or as deemed necessary
162 by the Chairman of the Council.
9

163 “(b)(1) The Task Force shall be comprised of the Chairman of the Council, or his or her
164 designee, the State Superintendent of Education, or his or her designee, and 12 District residents,
165 appointed by the Chairman, representing the following entities or groups:
166 “(A) Families whose children are receiving or have received childcare
167 services from an early childhood development provider in the District;
168 “(B) Community-based organizations;
169 “(C) Early childhood advocacy organizations;
170 “(D) Operators of child development facilities who participate in the
171 childcare subsidy program;
172 “(E) Operators of child development facilities who do not currently
173 participate in the childcare subsidy program;
174 “(F) Operators of home-based child development facilities;
175 “(G) Employees of child development facilities; and
176 “(H) An individual with an expertise in economics or policy, who has an
177 understanding of the District’s early childhood development and education sector.
178 “(3) At least 2 members of the Task Force shall be employees of child
179 development facilities.
180 “(4) The Chairman, or his or her designee, shall serve as the Chairperson of the
181 Task Force.
182 “(c) The Task Force shall:
10

183 “(1) Review the findings and recommendations of the Early Childhood Educator
184 Compensation in the Washington Region study completed by the Urban Institute and any
185 completed employee compensation scale and other relevant materials provided by the Office of
186 the State Superintendent of Education;
187 “(2) Submit a report to the Mayor and Council by January 15, 2022 that:
188 “(A) Assesses the potential impact of implementing an employee
189 compensation scale on early childhood development providers that:
190 “(i) Do not provide childcare services to children eligible for
191 subsidy; or
192 “(ii) Serve a minimum number of children who receive subsidy;
193 “(B) Proposes an employee compensation scale for early childhood
194 development providers that accounts for employee role, credentials, and experience; and
195 “(C) Provides recommendations for implementing the employee
196 compensation scale, which at a minimum considers:
197 “(i) Equitable implementation that accounts for different staffing
198 models, types, and sizes of early childhood development facilities;
199 “(ii) Long-term implications of the District providing funding to
200 early childhood providers to implement the pay scale, including how to allocate funds for new
201 early childhood development facilities that open after legislation is enacted; provided, that
202 recommendations do not exceed the $70 million appropriated in the Early Childhood Educator
203 Pay Equity Fund, plus any amounts adjusted for inflation in years beyond Fiscal Year 2023; and
11

204 “(iii) Oversight, reporting, and accountability mechanisms for the
205 use of funds allocated to early childhood development providers from the Early Childhood
206 Educator Pay Equity Fund; and
207 “(3) Following the adoption of the Fiscal Year 2025 budget and financial plan,
208 submit a report to the Mayor and Council by September 30, 2024, that:
209 “(A) Recommends changes to the Early Childhood Educator Pay Equity
210 Program established pursuant to section 3(b), including recommendations for limiting fiscal
211 pressures on the Early Childhood Educator Pay Equity Program through Fiscal Year 2028;
212 “(B) Proposes a new compensation scale for employees of childhood
213 development facilities that takes into account the compensation and benefits of individuals
214 employed by the District of Columbia Public Schools and District public charter schools who
215 teach pre-kindergarten and kindergarten; and
216 “(C) Provides additional recommendations for the allocation of monies
217 available in the Early Childhood Educator Pay Equity Fund.
218 “(d) The composition of the Task Force shall be the same as the composition of the Early
219 Childhood Educator Equitable Compensation Task Force established pursuant to the Early
220 Childhood Educator Equitable Compensation Task Force Act of 2021, effective November 13,
221 2021 (D.C. Law 24-45, D.C. Official Code § 38-2241 et seq.).”.
