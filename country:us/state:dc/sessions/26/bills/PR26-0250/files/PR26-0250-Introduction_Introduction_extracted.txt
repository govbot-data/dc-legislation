Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is a proposed
resolution titled "National Community Reinvestment Coalition, Inc., Revenue Bonds Project Emergency
Approval Resolution of 2025" and the accompanying emergency declaration resolution.
The resolution authorizes the issuance, sale, and delivery of tax-exempt revenue bonds, notes, or other
obligations in aggregate principal amount not to exceed $88,000,000. The bonds will be used for
refinancing the outstanding tax-exempt and taxable debt of the borrower that was incurred by the
borrower to finance: the acquisition, construction, furnishing, and equipping of 740 15th Street, N.W.,
Washington, D.C. 20005, an 11-floor, 22-unit office building with a total area of approximately 175,508
square feet, located in Ward 2.
In accordance with section 490 of the Home Rule Act, it has been determined that the bonds, when, as,
and if issued, shall be without recourse to the District. The bonds shall not be general obligations of the
District; shall not be a pledge of or involve the full faith and credit or the taxing power of the District;
shall not constitute a debt of the District; and shall not constitute a lending of public credit for a private
undertaking as prohibited in section 602(a)(2) of the Home Rule Act. The bonds shall not give rise to any
pecuniary liability of the District, and the District shall have no obligation with respect to the purchase of
the bonds.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed measure.

29 (1) Refinancing the District's Revenue Bonds, Series 2018A (National Community
30 Reinvestment Coalition Inc. Issue) and Revenue Bonds, Series 2018B (National Reinvestment

32 acquisition, construction, furnishing, and equipping of 740 15th Street, N.W., Washington, D.C.
33 20005 (Lot 37, Square 221), an 11-floor, 22-unit office building with a total area of approximately
34 175,508 square feet;

35 (2) Funding any credit enhancement costs, liquidity costs, capitalized interest or
36 debt service reserve fund relating to the Bonds; and
37 (3) Paying Issuance Costs and other related costs to the extent permissible. (the
38 “Project”).
39 (b) The planned financing will make available funds critically needed to finance, refinance,
40 or reimburse the Borrower for costs of the Project.
41 (c) Due to the current economic uncertainty in the financial markets, it is important for the
42 Council to expedite the process for the issuance of the Bonds and avoid any delay that may
43 adversely affect the ability of the Borrower to market the Bonds to investors and/or to obtain an
44 interest rate within the range contemplated by the Project budget.
45 (d) Council approval of the bond resolution authorizing the issuance of up to $88 million
46 of District of Columbia revenue bonds would permit the revenue bonds to be issued promptly to
