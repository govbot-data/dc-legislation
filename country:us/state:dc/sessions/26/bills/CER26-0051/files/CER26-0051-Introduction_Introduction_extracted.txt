8 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
9
10 _________________________
11
12 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
13
14 _________________________
15
16
17 To recognize, honor, and celebrate the contributions of passionate leader and public advocate
18 David Catania on his service on the DC Council from December 1997 to January 2015,
19 and recognize him as being the first openly gay member to serve.
20
21 WHEREAS, David Catania began his career in DC government as Chair of the Sheridan-
22 Kalorama Advisory Neighborhood Commission from 1996-1997.
23
24 WHEREAS, David Catania made history when he was elected as the first openly gay DC
25 Councilmember in a December 1997 special election.
26
27 WHEREAS, David Catania was elected to a full term as an At-Large Councilmember in
28 1998, and re-elected in 2002 and 2006.
29
30 WHEREAS, beginning in 2005, David Catania served as Chairman of the Health
31 Committee for eight years, where he helped to lead the charge against the HIV/AIDS epidemic in
32 the District.
33
34 WHEREAS, David Catania pushed for the extension of health insurance to every District
35 resident, increased funding for mental health services, and launched a school nurse program that
36 saw a full-time nurse placed on staff in 96 percent of the District’s public schools.
37
38 WHEREAS, David Catania established the HomeStart Initiative in 2000, creating an
39 advisory committee to identify ways to strengthen affordable housing, and created the Office of
40 Vocational Education and Skills Training to enhance job opportunities for District residents.
41
42 WHEREAS, David Catania became a staunch public supporter of same-sex marriage.
43
1

44 WHEREAS, in December 2009, twelve years after his swearing in, David Catania saw
45 DC Council provide final approval to his bill, the “Religious Freedom and Civil Marriage
46 Equality Amendment Act of 2009,” to legalize same-sex marriage.
47
48 WHEREAS, on March 9, 2010, due to David Catania’s advocacy, the District became the
49 sixth state or jurisdiction to permit same-sex marriage.
50
51 WHEREAS, David Catania left his DC Council seat to pursue an ultimately unsuccessful
52 bid for Mayor of the District in 2014.
53
54 WHEREAS, David Catania went on to pursue work in various areas of public affairs,
55 including lobbying and campaign management, and co-founded Georgetown Public Affairs,
56 LLC, in 2018.
57
58 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
59 resolution may be cited as the “David Catania Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
60
61 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates David Catania
62 for his steadfast leadership and dedication to the District of Columbia and its LGBTQIA+
63 community.
64
65 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
