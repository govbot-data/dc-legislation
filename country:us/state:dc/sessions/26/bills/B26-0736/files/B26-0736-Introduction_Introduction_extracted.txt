15 To amend, on an emergency basis, due to congressional review, Chapter 3 of Title 20 of the
16 District of Columbia Official Code to amend the definition of abbreviated probate, to
17 authorize the Register of Wills to refer proceedings to the Court to determine if one or more
18 personal representatives should be appointed and the will be admitted to probate, and to
19 strike the reference to will admission in transfers by affidavit; and to amend the
20 Strengthening Probate Amendment Act of 2024 to clarify that the act applies to estates for
21 whom the decedent died on or after March 21, 2025.
22
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 act may be cited as the “Strengthening Probate Administration Congressional Review Emergency
25 Amendment Act of 2026”.
26 Sec. 2. Chapter 3 of Title 20 of the District of Columbia Official Code is amended as
27 follows:
28 (a) Section 20-311 is amended to read as follows:
29 “§ 20-311. Nature of proceeding.
30 “(a) An abbreviated probate proceeding is a proceeding for probate of a will or a
31 determination of a decedent's intestacy and for the appointment of a personal representative. This
32 proceeding is instituted when an interested person files a petition for an abbreviated probate
33 proceeding with the Court in accordance with the provisions of § 20-304. This proceeding may be
34 conducted without the prior notice required for formal probate under § 20-323.
1

35 “(b) The finality of abbreviated probate shall be governed by § 20-331.”.
36 (b) Section 20-312 is amended to read as follows:
37 “§ 20-312. Action on petition.
38 “(a) General. — Upon a request for abbreviated probate filed in accordance § 20-304, the
39 Court or the Register of Wills shall appoint one or more personal representatives, except as
40 provided in § 20-322. The appointment of a personal representative shall constitute an Order for
41 unsupervised administration, unless the Order specifically provides for supervised administration
42 as provided in § 20-402. In no event, however, shall the appointment of a personal representative
43 be delayed pending the Court's decision with regard to whether the administration will be
44 supervised or unsupervised.
45 “(b) Wills. — In the case of a petition to admit a will to abbreviated probate, due execution
46 of the will shall be presumed and the Court or the Register of Wills may admit a will to probate
47 either: (1) if the will appears to have been duly executed and contains a recital by attesting
48 witnesses of facts constituting due execution; or (2) upon the verified statement of any person with
49 personal knowledge of the circumstances of execution, whether or not the person was in fact an
50 attesting witness, reciting facts showing due execution of the will.
51 “(c) Additional Proof. — The Register of Wills may require additional verified proof,
52 which shall be filed in the proceeding. If the Register of Wills requires additional proof, the matter
53 may be referred to the Court to determine if one or more personal representatives shall be appointed
54 and whether the will should be admitted to probate.”.
55 (c) Section 20-323(a) is amended by striking the phrase “legal periodical of general
56 circulation in the District” and inserting the phrase “legal periodical or newspaper of general
57 circulation in the District” in its place.
2

58 (d) Section 20-343(a) is amended by striking the phrase “legal periodical of general
59 circulation in the District” and inserting the phrase “legal periodical or newspaper of general
60 circulation in the District” in its place.
61 (e) Section 20-361(a)(4) is repealed.
62 (f) Section 20-704(a) is amended by striking the phrase “legal periodical of general
63 circulation in the District” and inserting the phrase “legal periodical or newspaper of general
64 circulation in the District” in its place.
65 Sec. 3. Section 7 of the Strengthening Probate Administration Amendment Act of 2024,
66 effective March 21, 2025 (D.C. Law 25-302; 72 DCR 780), is amended by adding a new subsection
67 (d) to read as follows:
68 “(d) This act shall apply to estates of decedents who died on or after March 21, 2025.”.
69 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
70 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
71 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
72 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
73 Sec. 5. Effective date.
74 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
75 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), and shall remain in effect for no longer than
76 90 days, as provided for emergency acts of the Council of the District of Columbia in section
77 412(a) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 788;
78 D.C. Official Code § 1-204.12(a)).
