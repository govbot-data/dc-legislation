12 To amend, on a temporary basis, Chapter 3 of Title 20 of the District of Columbia Official Code
13 to amend the definition of abbreviated probate, to authorize the Register of Wills to refer
14 proceedings to the Court to determine if one or more personal representatives should be
15 appointed and the will be admitted to probate, and to strike the reference to will admission
16 in transfers by affidavit; and to amend the Strengthening Probate Amendment Act of 2024
17 to clarify that the act applies to estates for whom the decedent died on or after March 21,
18 2025.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 act may be cited as the “Strengthening Probate Administration Temporary Amendment Act of
22 2025”.
23 Sec. 2. Chapter 3 of Title 20 of the District of Columbia Official Code is amended as
24 follows:
25 (a) Section 20-311 is amended to read as follows:
26 “§ 20-311. Nature of proceeding.
27 “(a) An abbreviated probate proceeding is a proceeding for probate of a will or a
28 determination of a decedent's intestacy and for the appointment of a personal representative. This
29 proceeding is instituted when an interested person files a petition for an abbreviated probate

30 proceeding with the Court in accordance with the provisions of § 20-304. This proceeding may be
31 conducted without the prior notice required for formal probate under § 20-323.
32 “(b) The finality of abbreviated probate shall be governed by § 20-331.”.
33 (b) Section 20-312 is amended to read as follows:
34 “§ 20-312. Action on petition.
35 “(a) General. — Upon a request for abbreviated probate filed in accordance § 20-304, the
36 Court or the Register of Wills shall appoint one or more personal representatives, except as
37 provided in § 20-322. The appointment of a personal representative shall constitute an Order for
38 unsupervised administration, unless the Order specifically provides for supervised administration
39 as provided in § 20-402. In no event, however, shall the appointment of a personal representative
40 be delayed pending the Court's decision with regard to whether the administration will be
41 supervised or unsupervised.
42 “(b) Wills. — In the case of a petition to admit a will to abbreviated probate, due execution
43 of the will shall be presumed and the Court or the Register of Wills may admit a will to probate
44 either: (1) if the will appears to have been duly executed and contains a recital by attesting
45 witnesses of facts constituting due execution; or (2) upon the verified statement of any person with
46 personal knowledge of the circumstances of execution, whether or not the person was in fact an
47 attesting witness, reciting facts showing due execution of the will.
48 “(c) Additional Proof. — The Register of Wills may require additional verified proof,
49 which shall be filed in the proceeding. If the Register of Wills requires additional proof, the matter

52 (c) Section 20-361(a)(4) is repealed.
53 Sec. 3. Section 7 of the Strengthening Probate Administration Amendment Act of 2024,
54 effective March 21, 2025 (D.C. Law 25-302; 72 DCR 780), is amended by adding a new subsection
55 (d) to read as follows:
56 “(d) This act shall apply to estates of decedents who died on or after March 21, 2025.”.
57 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
58 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
59 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
60 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
61 Sec. 5. Effective date.
62 (a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by
63 the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of congressional review
64 as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
65 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)).
66 (b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.
