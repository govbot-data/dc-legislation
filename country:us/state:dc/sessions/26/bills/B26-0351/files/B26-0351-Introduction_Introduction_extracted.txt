14 To amend, on an temporary basis, the District of Columbia Health Occupations Revision
15 Act of 1985 and the Consent for Vaccination of Minors Amendment Act of 2022
16 to authorize healthcare providers in the District to order and administer
17 immunizations recommended by competent medical and public health
18 organizations designated by the Director of the Department of Health.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA,
21 that this Act may be cited as the “Community Health Temporary Amendment Act of
22 2025”.
23 Sec. 2. The District of Columbia Health Occupations Revision Act of 1985,
24 effective March 25, 1986 (D.C. Law 6-99; D.C. Official Code § 3-1201.01 et seq.), is
25 amended as follows:
26 (a) Section 102(11)(A) (D.C. Official Code § 3-1201.02(11)(A)) is amended by
27 striking the phrase “in accordance with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s
28 published guidelines and recommended immunization schedules” and inserting the
29 phrase “in accordance with the published guidelines and recommended immunization
30 schedules of the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices of the Centers for
31 Disease Control and Prevention or a competent medical or public health organization
32 designated by the Director of the Department of Health through a public notice” in its
33 place.

1 (b) Section 755(a-1) (D.C. Official Code § 3-1207.55(a-1)) is amended by striking
2 the phrase “in accordance with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s
3 published guidelines and recommended immunization schedules” and inserting the
4 phrase “in accordance with the published guidelines and recommended immunization
5 schedules of the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices of the Centers for
6 Disease Control and Prevention or a competent medical or public health organization
7 designated by the Director of the Department of Health through a public notice” in its
8 place.
9 Sec. 3. Section 2 of the Consent for Vaccinations of Minors Amendment Act of
10 2022, effective March 10, 2023 (D.C. Law 24-312; D.C. Official Code § 7-1653.01) is
11 amended as follows:
12 (a) Subsection (a) is amended by striking the phrase “recommended by the United
13 States Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices ("ACIP"), and where receipt of
14 the vaccine is in accordance with ACIP's recommended immunization schedule.” and
15 inserting the phrase “recommended by the Advisory Committee on Immunization
16 Practices of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (“ACIP”) or a competent
17 medical or public health organization designated by the Director of the Department of
18 Health (“Director”) through a public notice, and where receipt of the vaccine is in
19 accordance with the recommended immunization schedule of ACIP or the competent
20 medical or public health organization designated by the Director.” in its place.
21 (b) Subsection (b)(1) is amended by striking the phrase “recommended by ACIP
22 in accordance with ACIP’s recommended immunization schedule” and inserting the
23 phrase “recommended by ACIP or the competent medical or public health organization
2

1 designated by the Director, in accordance with the recommended immunization schedule
2 of ACIP or the competent medical or public health organization designated by the

4 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
5 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the
6 fiscal impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act
7 of 1975, approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
8 Sec. 5. Effective date.
9 (a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of
10 veto by the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of
11 congressional review as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home
12 Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-
13 206.02(c)(1)), and publication in the District of Columbia Register.
14 (b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.
