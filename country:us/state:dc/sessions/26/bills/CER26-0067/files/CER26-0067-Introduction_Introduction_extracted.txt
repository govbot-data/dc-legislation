11 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
12 __________
13
14 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
15 __________
16
17
18 To recognize and commemorate the life of Washingtonian and public activist Carroll “Skeezy”
19 Rayburg Payne.
20
21 WHEREAS, Carroll “Skeezy” Rayburg Payne, born October 25, 1941, and gave selflessly to our
22 community before departing this life on June 13, 2025 at the age of 83;
23
24 WHEREAS, Mr. Payne was a proud graduate of Spingarn High School and, in 1968, became the
25 first official student at Federal City College—now the University of the District of Columbia—where he
26 emerged as a student leader and lifelong advocate and activist for educational equity, youth opportunity,
27 and civic empowerment;
28
29 WHEREAS, over decades of distinguished public service, Mr. Payne served in roles within the
30 DC Department of Housing, the Department of Parks and Recreation, the Mayor’s Youth Leadership
31 Institute, the Office of Congressman Walter Fauntroy, the Department of Family and Child Services, and
32 the DC Housing Authority, always championing housing with dignity, youth with promise, and
33 communities with power;
34
35 WHEREAS, “Skeezy” was a decorated basketball coach at Roosevelt High School, leading his
36 teams to two championship titles, and an influential youth mentor in Barry Farm, Potomac Gardens, and
37 across the city through the Kiddie Football League and countless grassroots efforts;
38
39 WHEREAS, Mr. Payne was celebrated for his Afrocentric elegance, radiant smile, and gift of
40 giving meaningful nicknames to his many friends as symbols of his appreciation and caring of their
41 friendship;
42
43 WHEREAS, Mr. Payne was a formidable presence in civic and cultural circles, including the
44 Langston Golf Course Fellowship, Men of Matthews Way, and alumni associations at Spingarn and
45 Federal City College, and was honored during John “Peterbug” Matthews Day for his community service;
46
47 WHEREAS, Mr. Payne’s personal creed—love and dedication to family—defined his every
48 endeavor, reflected in his 22-year partnership with Katrina Bellamy, and in the lives of the countless
49 number of youth and neighbors he uplifted with grace, humor, and unwavering belief in their potential;
50

51 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this resolution may be
52 cited as the “Carroll “Skeezy” Payne Memorial Recognition Resolution of 2025.”
53
54 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and commemorates the life and
55 legacy of Carroll “Skeezy” Rayburg Payne and urges all residents to celebrate the life of this remarkable
56 Washingtonian whose legacy of service, mentorship, and cultural pride will forever inspire the spirit of
57 our great city.
58
59 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
60 District of Columbia Register.
