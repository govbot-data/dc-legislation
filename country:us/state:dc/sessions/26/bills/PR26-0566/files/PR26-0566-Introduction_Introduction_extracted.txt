Kristi Whitfield
Ms. Kristi C. Whitfield is the Chief Impact Officer and Senior Director of
Racial Justice at the Nathan Cummings Foundation. Here, Ms. Whitfield
helps guide and measure strategic efforts to leverage all the foundation’s
assets in ways that advance impact toward racial, economic, and
environmental justice. She also oversees the racial justice grantmaking
portfolio.
Ms. Whitfield began her career in the nonprofit arena of housing and
community economic development. At the local level in Boston and
Washington, DC, Ms. Whitfield created programs designed to undermine
systemic inequality. She developed affordable housing, taught economic
literacy classes, and designed community programming. Ms. Whitfield
gained a national perspective on community economic development as the coordinator of the $65
million National Community Development Initiative for the Enterprise Foundation.
Ms. Whitfield took a detour into the for-profit world when she founded Washington DC’s first
mobile cupcake company, Curbside Cupcakes. She grew into a professional baker, and her
business grew to 3 trucks and a brick-and-mortar location. As the Founding Chairperson of the
DC Food Truck Association, Ms. Whitfield successfully advocated for groundbreaking vending
law reform.
Most recently, she served in local government as the Director of the DC Department of Small and
Local Business Development. She led the agency to address barriers to the marketplace through
access to capital, programming, and grants. In this role, she oversaw the allocation of over
$1billion to the small business community, the majority of whom were people of color.
A Ward 5 resident, Ms. Whitfield earned her Bachelor of Arts from Swarthmore College and
earned her Master of City Planning from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
