RE: Request for introduction of the Uniform College Athlete Name, Image or Likeness
Amendment Act of 2025.
Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Pursuant to Rule 401(b)(1) of the Rules of Organization and Procedure for the
Council, this is to request, on behalf of the District of Columbia Uniform Law
Commission, that you introduce the proposed “Uniform College Athlete Name, Image or
Likeness Act Amendment of 2025.” The act would amend Section 215 of D.C. Law 24-
245, Title II, the Omnibus Uniform Athlete Agent and College Athlete Name, Image, or
Likeness Act of 2022, D.C. Code § 38-1631.05, to conform the Act to the recent change
in the Bylaws of the National College Athletic Association (“NCAA”), and the settlement
in In re: College Athlete NIL Litigation (House v. NCAA), No. 4:20-CV-03919 (N.D.
Cal.). On April 18, 2024, the NCCA amended its Bylaws regarding the role of institutions
in student-athlete name, image, and likeness activities. The hearing for final approval of
the House settlement is scheduled for April 7, 2025. These actions expand the scope of
what an educational institution is permitted to do to assist college athletes as they seek to
monetize their name, image, and likeness (“NIL”).
The amended NCAA Bylaws by adding Article 22, which provides that
educational institutions may provide certain assistance and services to a college athlete
pursuing NIL opportunities that are currently prohibited under District law. These
activities include assisting a college athlete with selecting, arranging for, or providing
payment to a NIL agent and selecting, arranging for, or collecting payment from a third
party engaged in specific NIL agreements with a college athlete. In addition, the House
settlement will allow educational institutions to provide direct payments to college
athletes for the use of an athlete’s name, image, and likeness.
The Uniform College Athlete Name, Image, or Likeness Act, upon which District
law is based, was drafted in accordance with the existing NCAA Bylaws and finalized
just before the recent addition of Article 22 and before the litigation resulting in the

House Settlement began. The proposed amendment would ensure that universities and
college athletes in the District are not at a disadvantage.
We discussed the proposed amendment with Mondi Kumbula-Fraser, Vice
President of Government Relations & General Counsel of the Consortium of Universities
of the Washington Metropolitan Area Office and she supports its adoption, as
representative of the Consortium.
A proposed Uniform College Athlete Name, Image or Likeness Amendment Act
of 2025 is being filed at the same time as this letter.
We would be pleased to answer any questions.
Sincerely,

18 To amend section 215 of the Uniform College Athlete, Name, Image, or Likeness Act of 2022, to
19 permit an institution, conference, or athletic association to assist a college athlete in
20 selecting, arranging for, or providing payment to a name, image, or likeness agent and in
21 selecting, arranging for, or collecting payment from a third party engaged in specific
22 name, image, or likeness agreements with college athletes, and to remove the prohibition
23 prohibiting institutions or conferences from providing compensation to a college athlete
24 for the athlete's name, image, or likeness.
25
26 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
27 act may be cited as the "Uniform College Athlete Name, Image, or Likeness Act Amendment
28 Act of 2025."
