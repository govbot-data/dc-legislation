10 To designate the District of Columbia as the Official Sports Capital of the United States and to
11 require Events DC to develop and implement a plan to promote, support, and enhance the
12 District’s status as the nation’s premier destination for sports events, teams, and
13 activities.

15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
16 act may be cited as the “Official Sports Capital of the United States Designation Amendment Act
17 of 2025”.
18 Title I. Designation of the District as the Official Sports Capital of the United States.
19 Sec. 101. This title may be cited as the “Sense of the Council on the Designation of the
20 District of Columbia as the Official Sports Capital of the United States Act of 2025”.
21 Sec. 102. The Council finds that:
22 (1) The District of Columbia is home to numerous professional, collegiate, and
23 amateur sports teams, including but not limited to the Washington Wizards, Washington
24 Mystics, Washington Nationals, Washington Capitals, D.C. United, Washington Spirit, D.C.
25 Defenders, Old Glory DC, and the D.C. Breeze.
26 (2) The District has hosted premier national and international sporting events,
27 including the MLB All-Star Game, NBA All-Star Game, NHL Winter Classic, WNBA All-Star

28 Game, the Mubadala Citi DC Open, World Cup qualifiers, and the Army-Navy Game,
29 showcasing its capacity as a global sports hub.
30 (3) The District’s unique status as the nation’s capital, and its accessibility,
31 infrastructure, and sports culture make it the most fitting city to serve as the Official Sports
32 Capital of our nation.
33 (4) The District continues to invest in world-class sports venues such as Capital
34 One Arena, Nationals Park, Audi Field, Robert F. Kennedy Memorial Stadium Campus and the
35 CareFirst Arena, further enhancing its reputation as a premier sports destination.
36 (5) The District plays a critical role in advancing equity and inclusivity in sports,
37 fostering youth participation through programs like the Department of Park’s and Recreation’s
38 community athletics initiatives, and supporting diversity in sports leadership and opportunities.
39 (6) Designation of the District as the “Official Sports Capital of the United States”
40 will boost tourism, economic development, and national recognition of the District’s rich sports
41 culture and history.
42 Sec. 103. It is the sense of the Council that the District of Columbia shall be designated
43 as the only “Official Sports Capital of the United States.”
44 Title II. Plan to promote the District as the Official Sports Capital of the United States.
45 Sec. 201. This title may be cited as the “Sports Incentives and Marketing Amendment
46 Act of 2025”.

47 Sec. 202. The Washington Convention Center Authority Act of 1994, effective
48 September 28, 1994 (D.C. Law 10-188; D.C. Official Code § 10-1201.01 et seq.), is amended as
49 follows:
50 (a) Section 203 (D.C. Official Code § 10-1202.03) is amended by adding a new
51 paragraph (10N) is added to read as follows:
52 “(10N) To direct no less than $500,000 per fiscal year to support the solicitation
53 and selection of a qualified organization to host marquee sports related events designed to attract
54 sports business professionals, including sports media executives, league commissioners, and
55 sports agency professionals.”.
56 (b) A new section 203b is added to read as follows:
57 “Sec. 203b. Plan to promote the District as the Official Sports Capital of the United
58 States.
59 “(a) Events DC shall develop and implement a plan to promote and enhance the District’s
60 status as the Official Sports Capital of the United States, which shall include:
61 “(1) Initiatives to attract and retain major sporting events, conferences,
62 conventions, and tournaments;
63 “(2) Strategies to increase investment in youth sports and community athletic
64 programs;
65 “(3) Partnerships with professional, collegiate, and amateur teams to engage
66 residents and visitors;
