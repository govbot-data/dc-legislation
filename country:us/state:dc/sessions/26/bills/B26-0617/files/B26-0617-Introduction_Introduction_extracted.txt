16 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Body-Worn Camera Regulation and Reporting
17 Requirements Act of 2015 and Chapter 39 of Title 24 of the District of Columbia
18 Municipal Regulations to require release of body-worn camera recordings of
19 Metropolitan Police Department Officers present in the event of an officer-involved death
20 or serious use of force by a law enforcement officer other than a Metropolitan Police
21 Department officer.
22
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 act may be cited as the “Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Temporary
25 Amendment Act of 2026”.
26 Sec. 2. Section 3004 of the Body-Worn Camera Regulation and Reporting Requirements
27 Act of 2015, effective October 22, 2015 (D.C. Law 21-36; D.C. Official Code § 5-116.33), is
28 amended as follows:
29 (a) Subsection (c)(2)(A) is amended as follows:
30 (1) Sub-subparagraph (i) is amended as follows:
31 (A) Sub-sub-subparagraph (I) is amended to read as follows:

32 “(I) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all
33 Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law
34 enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force;
35 and”.
36 (B) Sub-sub-subparagraph (II) is amended by striking the phrase “; and”
37 and inserting a semicolon in its place.
38 (2) A new sub-subparagraph (i-I) is added to read as follows:
39 “(i-I) Notwithstanding sub-subparagraph (i) of this subparagraph,
40 for incidents occurring between August 1, 2025, and the effective date of the Body-Worn
41 Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, passed on
42 emergency basis on March 3, 2026 (Enrolled version of Bill 26-XXX), within 10 business days
43 after the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency
44 Amendment Act of 2026, passed on emergency basis on March 3, 2026 (Enrolled version of Bill
45 26-XXX), publicly release:
46 “(I) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all
47 Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law
48 enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force; and
49 “(II) A description of the incident; and”.
50 (3) Sub-subparagraph (ii) is amended by striking the phrase “; and” and inserting
51 the phrase “; provided, that for all officer-involved deaths since August 1, 2025, the information
52 reported pursuant to this sub-subparagraph shall include the names of all Metropolitan Police
53 Department officers as well as other law enforcement officers directly involved in the officer-
54 involved death; and” in its place.
2

55 (b) Subsection (e)(1) is amended by striking the phrase “officer-involved death or serious
56 use of force” and inserting the phrase “officer-involved death directly involving an MPD officer
57 or a serious use of force directly involving an MPD officer” in its place.
58 (c) Subsection (g) is amended as follows:
59 (1) A new paragraph (1A) is added to read as follows:
60 “(1A) “Law enforcement officer” means any officer, agent, or employee of the
61 United States or any state or local government authorized by law or by a government agency to
62 engage in or supervise the prevention, detection, or investigation of any violation of criminal or
63 civil law.”.
64 (2) Paragraph (3) is amended as follows:
65 (A) Subparagraph (A) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan
66 Police Department officer” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department officer, or
67 any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
68 (B) Subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan
69 Police Department officer” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department officer, or
70 any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
71 (C) Subparagraph (C) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan
72 Police Department officer” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department officer, or
73 any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
74 (D) Subparagraph (D) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan
75 Police Department canine” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department canine, or
76 canine of any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.

77 Sec. 3. Chapter 39 of Title 24 of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations is
78 amended as follows:
79 (a) Section 3900 is amended as follows:
80 (1) Subsection 3900.9 is amended to read as follows:
81 “(a) For any incident involving an officer-involved death directly involving an MPD
82 member or a serious use of force directly involving an MPD member, members shall not review
83 any BWC recordings to assist in initial report writing.
84 “(b) For an incident other than those described in paragraph (a) of this subsection,
85 members shall indicate, when writing any initial or subsequent reports, whether the member
86 viewed BWC footage prior to writing the report and specify what BWC footage the member
87 viewed.”.
88 (2) Subsection 3900.10(a)(1) is amended as follows:
89 (A) Sub-subparagraph (A) is amended as follows:
90 (i) Sub-sub-subparagraph (i) is amended to read as follows:
91 “(i) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all
92 Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law
93 enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force;
94 and”.
95 (ii) Sub-sub-subparagraph (ii) is amended by striking the phrase “;
96 and” and inserting a semicolon in its place.
97 (B) A new sub-subparagraph (A-i) is added to read as follows:
4

98 “(A-i) Notwithstanding sub-subparagraph (A) of this subparagraph, for
99 incidents occurring between August 1, 2025, and the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera
100 Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, passed on emergency basis
101 on March 3, 2026 (Enrolled version of Bill 26-XXX), within 10 business days after the effective
102 date of the Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of
103 2026, passed on emergency basis on March 3, 2026 (Enrolled version of Bill 26-XXX), publicly
104 release:
105 “(i) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all
106 Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law
107 enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force; and
108 “(ii) A description of the incident; and”.
109 (C) Sub-subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase “; and” and
110 inserting the phrase “; provided, that for all officer-involved deaths since August 1, 2025, the
111 information reported pursuant to this sub-subparagraph shall include the names of all
112 Metropolitan Police Department officers as well as other law enforcement officers directly
113 involved in the officer-involved death; and” in its place.
114 (b) Section 3999.1 is amended by adding a new definition between the definitions of
115 “FOIA” and “metadata” to read as follows:
116 ““Law enforcement officer” - means any officer, agent, or employee of the United States
117 or any state or local government authorized by law or by a government agency to engage in or
118 supervise the prevention, detection, or investigation of any violation of criminal or civil law.”.
119 Sec. 4. Applicability.
120 This act shall apply as of August 1, 2025.

121 Sec. 5. Fiscal impact statement.
122 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
123 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved

125 Sec. 6. Effective date.
126 (a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by
127 the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of congressional review
128 as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
129 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)), and publication in the District of
130 Columbia Register.
131 (b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.
