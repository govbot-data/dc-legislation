7 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
8 __________
9
10
11 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
12
13 ____________________
14
15 To recognize and honor the life, service, and legacy of Joan A.T. Kelley and to commend her for
16 her decades of dedication to education, faith, leadership, and family in the District of Columbia.
17
18 WHEREAS, Joan A.T. Kelley was born on November 8, 1938, in Washington, D.C., to
19 the late Bennie Frank and Thomasine Scott Adams;
20
21 WHEREAS, Joan A.T. Kelley received her early education in the District of Columbia
22 Public Schools before graduating from Paul Laurence Dunbar High School and later earning
23 degrees from D.C. Teachers College and Georgetown University, reflecting her lifelong
24 commitment to learning and excellence;
25
26 WHEREAS, Joan A.T. Kelley devoted 43 years to the District of Columbia Public
27 Schools, beginning her teaching career in 1961 and later serving in staff development and
28 leadership roles, ultimately spending 17 years as Principal of John Tyler Elementary School,
29 where her dedication, professionalism, and compassion shaped the lives of generations of
30 students and educators;
31
32 WHEREAS, Joan A.T. Kelley was an active member of several professional educational
33 organizations and, even after retiring in 2004, remained engaged in supporting the educational
34 community through the D.C. Retired Educators Association and regular fellowship with fellow
35 retired principals;
36
37 WHEREAS, Joan A.T. Kelley was a proud member of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.
38 for nearly five decades, exemplifying its values of sisterhood, scholarship, and service
39 throughout her personal and professional life;
40
41 WHEREAS, Joan A.T. Kelley was a deeply committed church leader who served
42 faithfully in multiple ministries, including the Lay Council, Christian Education, music, youth
43 programming, missionary work, and community outreach, and also contributed her leadership to
44 Church Women United at both the District and regional levels;
45

46 WHEREAS, Joan A.T. Kelley cherished her family, raising her daughters Kathy and
47 Dawn, and embraced her extended family of grandchildren, siblings, and loved ones who were
48 central to her life;
49
50 WHEREAS, Joan A.T. Kelley will be remembered for her warmth, generosity, and
51 radiant personality, as well as for her love of dancing, travel, and the joy she brought to every
52 community she touched;
53
54 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
55 resolution may be cited as the “Joan A.T. Kelley Posthumous Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
56
57 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the life of Joan
58 A.T. Kelley for her extraordinary contributions to public education, her steadfast commitment to
59 faith and service, and the enduring love and inspiration she shared with her family, students,
60 colleagues, and community.
61
62 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
63 the District of Columbia Register.
