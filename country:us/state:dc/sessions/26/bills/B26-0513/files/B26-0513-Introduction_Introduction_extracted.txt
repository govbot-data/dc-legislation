(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The Government of the District of Columbia, Department of Human Services, is seeking to exercise
the remaining option in year three for Friendship Place to continue to provide permanent supportive
housing III (PSH3) program case management services, utility assistance, and financial assistance to
chronically homeless and other highly vulnerable individuals and families experiencing homelessness.
The purpose of this program is to assist residents with histories of chronic homelessness in
transitioning from the street or temporary living situation into stable and permanent housing,
maintaining long-term housing, and achieving self-sufficiency. The success of the aforementioned
initiative will be achieved by referring and connecting participants to supportive services that will
address their barriers to obtaining and maintaining permanent housing and achieving an optimal level
of self-sufficiency.
(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:
The solicitation was issued in the Washington Times on April 8, 2022. An electronic RFQ
DOC575024 was issued in the open market on April 11, 2022, with a closing date of May 17, 2022.
The District received a total of 28 submissions. Twenty-three of the 28 responses were deemed
qualified and the vendors were notified on June 23, 2022.
The responses were evaluated in accordance with the qualification criteria outlined in the solicitation.
The qualification evaluation factors were PSH3 design, case management, organizational capacity, and
past performance. Friendship Place met the qualifications indicated in the RFQ and has a satisfactory
rating, providing similar services through current and prior HCAs with the District.
The contracting officer awarded an HCA after determining that Friendship Place was qualified and
responsible, and the proposed price was determined to be fair and reasonable. A definitive HCA was
awarded to Friendship Place.

14. Description of Amendment/Modification (Organized by UCF Section headings, including solicitation/contract subject matter where feasible.)
The purpose of this modification to the subject Human Care Agreement (HCA) identified in block 10A is as follows:
1. Exercise the contract terms for the remainder of option period three. The total not-to-exceed amount of
option period three is hereby increased from $862,441.76 by $1,002,913.34 to $1,865,355.10. The
period of performance is from July 1, 2025 through June 30, 2026.
2. Delete in its entirety 29 DCMR Chapter 25 and 77 and Replace with 29 DCMR Chapters 25 and 74.
3. In accordance with HCA Section F.8 “Department of Labor Wage Determinations” the US Department
of Labor Wage Determination No. 2015-4281, Revision No. 34, dated July 8, 2025, is incorporated by
reference and available at: https://sam.gov/wage-determination/2015-4281/34. Section F.10 “Way to
Work Amendment Act of 2006”, the District of Columbia 2024 Living Wage rate is adjusted to $17.50
per hour effective January 1, 2025 until June 30, 2025. Effective July 1, 2025, the District’s Minimum
Wage and Living Wage will increase to $17.95 per hour. The 2025 Living Wage Notice and Fact Sheet
are hereby incorporated by reference:2025 Living Wage Notice | (dc.gov) and 2025 Living Wage Fact
Sheet | (dc.gov); Mayor’s Order 2023-131; Notice of Prohibition of Sexual Harassment in the Workplace:
https://ocp.dc.gov/modules/file/icons/application-pdf.png.
