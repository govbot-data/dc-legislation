7 A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
8

11 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
12
13 To declare an emergency with respect to the need to direct the Mayor to establish a truancy pilot
14 for the purpose of referring students with unexcused absences to the Department of
15 Human Services and documenting attendance and academic outcomes of students
16 receiving interventions from the Department of Human Services.
17 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
18 resolution may be cited as the “Pilot Truancy Reduction Emergency Declaration Resolution of
19 2025.”
20 Sec. 2. (a) Funding of $3.38 million was included in the Fiscal Year 2025 Local Budget
21 Act of 2024, effective September 18, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-218; 71 DCR 8366), for the expansion
22 of truancy prevention services at the Department of Human Services (“DHS”). On July 9, 2024,
23 the Council passed the Pilot Truancy Reduction Emergency Amendment Act of 2024
24 (“emergency act”) followed by passage of the Pilot Truancy Reduction Temporary Amendment
25 Act of 2024 on September 7, 2024 (“temporary act”).
26 (b) The emergency and temporary act sought to test the effectiveness of the Mayor’s
27 proposal to redirect truancy referrals in our schools to DHS through a one-year, 5-school pilot
28 program.
1

29 (c) The final report on the outcomes of the truancy pilot is due August 15, 2025, but the
30 temporary act is set to expire on July 10, 2025. Emergency legislation is therefore necessary to
31 extend the requirements of the temporary act until the final report is furnished.
32 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
33 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
34 “Truancy Reduction Emergency Amendment Act of 2025” be adopted after a single reading.
35 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
