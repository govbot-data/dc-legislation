Dear Secretary Howard:
Today, I am introducing the “Marvin “Slush” Gross Way Designation Act of 2025” to
symbolically designate Sumner Road, SE, in the Barry Farm neighborhood of Ward 8 as Marvin
“Slush” Gross Way. I have enclosed a signed version of the legislation.
Marvin Gross, affectionately known as Slush, was a native son of Washington, DC, and of the
Barry Farm community. Born on June 13, 1982, and raised on Stevenson Road, SE, Mr. Gross
brought unmatched energy and creativity to every space he entered, from neighborhood block
parties to the Anacostia High School hallways where he attended school. Mr. Gross was a
visionary artist and the founder of Beat Ya Feet, a uniquely DC dance that evolved out of a DC
dance called the Hee Haw and quickly became a defining expression of DC’s Go-Go music
culture starting in the late 1990s. Mr. Gross taught his friends, peers, and younger dancers how to
move with pride and power. His signature style helped fuel a cultural identity that young people
still carry forward today on stages, in street battles, and in their everyday lives. Mr. Slush lost his
life in 2002, but his creative spark lives on. Dancers like Crazy Legz and crews like the Beat Ya
Feet Kings continue to spread his dance style internationally, keeping his vision and spirit alive.
Mr. Gross’s family and neighbors organized to advance this symbolic street naming, in
recognition of his tremendous impact as a neighbor and cultural leader. In March 2025, Advisory
Neighborhood Commission 8C, in which this street is located, voted unanimously to rename
Sumner Road, SE, as Marvin “Slush” Gross Way in his honor. And in May 2025, the Council
will consider the “Marvin “Slush” Gross Posthumous Recognition Resolution of 2025.”
In a city that values the artists who shape its identity and the homegrown brilliance that echoes
far beyond its borders, I am proud to introduce this legislation in support of Mr. Gross’s legacy.
