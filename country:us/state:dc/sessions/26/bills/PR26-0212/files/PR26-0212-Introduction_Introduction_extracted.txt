15 To declare the existence of an emergency, due to congressional review, with respect to
16 the need to amend An Act To create a Department of Corrections in the District of
17 Columbia and the Corrections Oversight Improvement Omnibus Amendment Act of
18 2022 to designate the Department of Corrections as the District agency to provide
19 a free appropriate public education under the Individuals with Disabilities
20 Education Act and District law to certain individuals in its custody and detained
21 in its secure facilities.
22
23 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That
24 this resolution may be cited as the “Special Education for Young Adults in the Custody
25 of the Department of Corrections Congressional Review Emergency Declaration
26 Resolution of 2025”.
27 Sec. 2. (a) On April 1, 2025, the Council passed the Special Education for Young
28 Adults in the Custody of the Department of Corrections Emergency Amendment Act of
29 2025, effective April 11, 2025 (D.C. Act 26-42; 72 DCR 4548) (“emergency act”), which
30 expires on July 10, 2025.
31 (b) On May 6, 2025, the Council passed the Special Education for Young Adults
32 in the Custody of the Department of Corrections Temporary Amendment Act of 2025,
1

33 passed on 2nd reading on May 6, 2025 (Enrolled version of Bill 26-211) (“temporary
34 act”), which does not yet have a projected law date.
35 (c) This congressional review emergency legislation is necessary to prevent a gap
36 in the law between the expiration of the emergency act and the effective date of the
37 temporary act.
38 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
39 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
40 Special Education for Young Adults in the Custody of the Department of Corrections
41 Congressional Review Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single
42 reading.
43 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
