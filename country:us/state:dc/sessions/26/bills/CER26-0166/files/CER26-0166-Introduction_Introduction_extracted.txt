10 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA

15 To honor and recognize DC Central Kitchen on the occasion of celebrating a total of 2,500
16 graduates from its Culinary Job Training Program.
17
18 WHEREAS, DC Central Kitchen began operations in 1989 with the belief that food alone
19 would never end hunger, but that food could be a powerful tool for strengthening bodies,
20 empowering minds, and building communities;
21
22 WHEREAS, DC Central Kitchen’s Culinary Job Training Program was launched in 1990
23 as a root-cause solution to the challenges of hunger and poverty by preparing District residents
24 with histories of homelessness, incarceration, and addiction for culinary careers;
25
26 WHEREAS, the Culinary Job Training Program now offers 14 weeks of tuition-free “knife
27 skills and life skills” training, including internships and externships, social services, job placement
28 support, and alumni services, thanks to the efforts and commitment of all 313 DC Central Kitchen
29 employees;
30
31 WHEREAS, the Culinary Job Training Program has partnered with hundreds of District of
32 Columbia restaurants, hotels, event venues, foodservice companies, and professional associations
33 like the Restaurant Association of Metropolitan Washington and Les Dames d'Escoffier
34 Washington, DC to create meaningful career opportunities and reduce barriers to employment;
35
36 WHEREAS, the Culinary Job Training Program has produced 181 graduating classes in its
37 history and doubled the number of annual graduating classes since it moved into its new
38 headquarters, the Michael R. Klein Center for Jobs and Justice, in 2023;
39
40 WHEREAS, 160 graduates of the Culinary Job Training Program now work for DC Central
41 Kitchen in a variety of culinary, instructional, and managerial roles, serving as mentors and guides
42 to incoming students;
43
44 WHEREAS, the Culinary Job Training Program has worked to shift narratives and expand
45 pathways for returning citizens through decades of collaboration with partners like the Court
1

46 Services and Offender Supervision Agency, Mayor’s Office on Returning Citizen Affairs, and
47 Council for Court Excellence;
48
49 WHEREAS, the Culinary Job Training Program has earned national acclaim as a best
50 practice program and social innovation from the Washington Post, New York Times, Wall Street
51 Journal, National Geographic, PBS NewsHour, Aspen Institute, the National Restaurant
52 Association, the US Chamber of Commerce, and White House Champion of Change Awards; and
53
54 WHEREAS, on June 26, 2026, the Culinary Job Training Program will mark its 2,500th
55 graduate, which represents a remarkable milestone worthy of recognition.
56
57 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
58 resolution may be cited as the “DC Central Kitchen Culinary Job Training Program Recognition
59 Resolution of 2026”.
60
61 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors DC Central Kitchen
62 for their efforts to train and empower a total of 2,500 Culinary Job Training Program graduates
63 since the inception of its program.
64
65 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
66 the District of Columbia Register.
