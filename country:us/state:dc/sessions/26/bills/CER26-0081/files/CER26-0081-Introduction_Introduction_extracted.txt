9 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
10 _____________
11
12 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
13 _________________
14
15 To honor Rohulamin Quander – affectionately known as “Rohul” by family and admirers
16 – who dedicated his life to public service, scholarship, and the preservation of African American
17 history.
18
19 WHEREAS, as a proud native of Washington, DC and alumnus of Howard University
20 (BA 1966, JD 1969), he served with distinction as a Senior Administrative Law Judge for the
21 District of Columbia;
22
23 WHEREAS, Judge Quander’s leadership on the bench reflected his sharp legal mind and
24 steadfast commitment to truth, establishing him as a pillar of the District’s judicial system;
25
26 WHEREAS, he founded the Quander Historical and Educational Society in 1985 to
27 document, preserve, and share the enduring legacy of the Quander Family, one of the oldest
28 African American family lines in the nation dating back to the 1670s;
29
30 WHEREAS, through his scholarship - including significant books such as “The
31 Quanders” and biographies of family members- he enriched community understanding of
32 heritage, resilience, and African American contributions to American life;
33
34 WHEREAS, he was instrumental in founding The League of Enslaved Mount Vernon
35 Descendants, ensuring that the stories of the lives of those held in bondage be remembered and
36 recorded;
37
38 WHEREAS, as a public lecturer, archivist, advisor, and licensed DC tour guide, he
39 deepened public knowledge and appreciation for African American contributions to our local and
40 national history;
41
42 WHEREAS, his family devotion - to his wife Carmen Torruella Quander, their three
43 children, and a grandchild - mirrored the love, humility, and dedication he carried into all areas
44 of his life;
45
46 WHEREAS, his passing in August 2025 represents a profound loss to his family, the
47 District of Columbia, and all who benefitted from his guidance and follow in his footsteps.
1

48
49 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
50 resolution may be cited as the “Rohulamin “Rohul” Quander Memorial Recognition Resolution
51 of 2025”.
52
53 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia honors the life and legacy of Judge
54 Rohulamin Quander, express our deepest condolences to his family and loved ones, and offer
55 appreciation for his commitment to advancing the principles of historical preservation,
56 education, and public service.
57
58 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
59 the District of Columbia Register.
