15 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Language Access Act of 2004 to include the District of
16 Columbia Water and Sewer Authority as a covered entity with major public contact; to
17 amend the District of Columbia Public Works Act of 1954 to require that the District of
18 Columbia Water and Sewer Authority submit information regarding disconnections to the
19 Council, the Office of the People’s Counsel, the Office of the Attorney General, and the
20 Office of the Tenant Advocate on a monthly basis; to amend the Water and Sewer
21 Authority Establishment and Department of Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996 to
22 clarify the imposition of charges and penalties for late payment of water service charges
23 and bills; and to amend section 431 of Title 21 of the District of Columbia Municipal
24 Regulations to modify the notice requirements to building occupants prior to a water
25 service disconnection.
26
27 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
28 act may be cited as the “DC Water and Sewer Authority Billing and Disconnection Clarification
29 Temporary Amendment Act of 2026”.
30 Sec. 2. Section 2(3)(B) of the Language Access Act of 2004, effective June 19, 2004 (D.C.
31 Law 15-167; D.C. Official Code § 2-1931(3)(B)), is amended by adding a new sub-subparagraph
32 (vii-I) to read as follows:
33 “(vii-I) The District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority;”.
34 Sec. 3. Section 216(d) of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of
1

35 Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996, effective April 18, 1996 (D.C. Law 11-111; D.C.
36 Official Code § 34-2202.16(d)), is amended to read as follows:
37 “(d) The Authority may impose additional charges and penalties for late payment of bills
38 not exceeding a charge of 10% for any charges or bills remaining unpaid for more than 30 days,
39 and a penalty at the rate of 1% per month compounded monthly for any charges or bills that remain
40 unpaid for more than 60 days”.”
41 Sec. 4. Section 103 of the District of Columbia Public Works Act of 1954, approved May
42 18, 1954 (68 Stat. 102; D.C. Official Code § 34-2407.01), is amended to read as follows:
43 “Sec. 103. Discontinuance of water service for failure to pay water charges.
44 “(a)(1) The District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority (“Authority”), established
45 pursuant to section 202(a) of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of
46 Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996, effective April 18, 1996 (D.C. Law 11-111; D.C.
47 Official Code § 34-2202.02(a)), may provide for the collection of water charges, in advance or
48 otherwise, from the owner or occupant of any building, property, or other location furnished water
49 or water service by the District.
50 “(2) The Authority may shut off the water supply to any such building,
51 property, or other location upon failure of the owner or occupant thereof to pay such water charges
52 within 30 days from the date of rendition of the bill therefor, regardless of any change in ownership
53 or occupancy of such building, property, or other location.
54 “(b)(1) If the Authority has shut off the water supply to any property for failure to pay
55 District water and sanitary sewer service charges, it shall be unlawful for any person to restore the
56 water supply without the express authorization of the Authority.
2

57 “(2) The Authority shall impose a fine in an amount not less than 20% of the
58 delinquent charges or more than $100, whichever is greater, upon the owner or occupant of the
59 property, unless the Authority determines that the owner or occupant did not restore or solicit a
60 person to restore the water.
61 “(c) The Authority shall submit on the 7th day of each month, the following information
62 to the Council, the Office of the Attorney General, the Office of the People’s Counsel, and the
63 Office of the Tenant Advocate for the preceding month:
64 “(1) The number of properties that received a notice of disconnection;
65 “(2) Of those properties that were issued a notice, the number of properties that
66 experienced a disconnection;
67 “(3) Of those properties that experienced a disconnection, the number of properties
68 that had service restored;
69 “(4) The number of properties that entered into a payment plan;
70 “(5) The number of properties that have had bill payment assumed by the tenants
71 through a tenant association; and
72 “(6) The number of properties that have been placed in receivership.”.
73 Sec. 5. Section 431 of Title 21 of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations (21
74 DCMR § 431), is amended as follows:
75 (a) The section heading is amended by striking the phrase “TO TENANTS” and inserting
76 the phrase “TO OCCUPANTS” in its place.
77 (b) Subsection 431.1 is amended to read as follows:
3

78 “431.1 At least thirty (30) working days prior to terminating water and sewer services to
79 the premises, the General Manager shall post notice outside of the premises, and at each unit if
80 publicly accessible and where practicable, in English, Spanish, or another language that the
81 Authority reasonably determines may be spoken by occupants at the premises.”.
82 (b) Subsection 431.2 is amended as follows:
83 (1) The lead-in language is amended to read as follows:
84 “431.2 The notice shall be developed in consultation with the Office of the Tenant
85 Advocate and provide the occupants, whether tenants or owners, with the following information:”.
86 (2) Paragraph (a) is amended to read as follows
87 “(a) That the owner, condominium association, community association, or other
88 common interest development is delinquent in the payment of water and sewer service charges,
89 including the current amount owed and the number of days the account is past due;”.
90 (3) Paragraph (c) is amended by striking the phrase “may permit” and inserting the
91 phrase “shall permit” in its place.
92 (4) Paragraph (d) is amended by striking the phrase “receivership;” and inserting
93 the phrase “receivership, as provided in D.C. Official Code § 34-2304(a)(1);” in its place.
94 (c) Subsection 431.5 is amended by striking the word “Director” and inserting the phrase
95 “General Manager” in its place.
