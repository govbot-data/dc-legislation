Councilmember Brooke Pinto
A BILL
________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
________________
1 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995 to authorize the Mayor to
2 extend the juvenile curfew hours in the District when appropriate to protect public safety
3 or public or private property, to authorize the Chief of Police to establish extended
4 juvenile curfew hour zones in specified circumstances, to start the juvenile curfew hours
5 at 11 p.m. every day, and to extend the applicability of the juvenile curfew to 17-year
6 olds; and to amend the Volunteer Services Clarification Temporary Amendment Act of
7 2025 to make conforming changes.
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
10 act may be cited as the “Juvenile Curfew Second Emergency Amendment Act of 2025”.
11 Sec. 2. The Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995, effective September 20, 1995 (D.C. Law 11-48;
12 D.C. Official Code § 2-1541 et seq.), is amended as follows:
13 (a) Section 3 (D.C. Official Code § 2-1542) is amended as follows:
14 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended to read as follows:
15 “(1) “Chief of Police” means the Chief of the Metropolitan Police Department.”.
16 (2) A new paragraph (1A) is added to read as follows:
17 “(1A) “Curfew hours” means:
18 “(A) From 11:00 p.m. on every dayuntil 6:00 a.m. on the following day;
19 “(B) Extended curfew hours established by the Mayor pursuant to section
20 3a; and
1

21 “(C) Extended curfew hours in extended juvenile curfew zones established
22 by the Chief of Police pursuant to section 3b.”.
23 (3) Paragraph (5) is amended by striking the phrase “under the age of 17 years”
24 and inserting the phrase “under the age of 18 years” in its place.
25 (4) A new paragraph (6A) is added to read as follows:
26 “(6A) “Notice of violation of curfew” means a verbal warning, authorized by an official
27 that informs a groups of juveniles that they are violating the terms of a curfew, that they may be
28 arrested if they continue to violate the curfew, and identifies reasonable steps that they can take
29 to leave the area to avoid arrest.”.
30 (b) New sections 3a and 3b are added to read as follows:
31 “Sec. 3a. Temporary extensions of juvenile curfew hours.
32 “(a) The Mayor may, by issuance of an administrative order pursuant to section 422(12)
33 of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, effective December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 890; D.C.
34 Official Code § 1-204.22(12), extend the juvenile curfew hours in the District, or any part of the
35 District, beyond the hours established in section 3(1A)(A), (B), and (C), if the Mayor determines
36 that such an extension is appropriate to protect public safety or public or private property.
37 “(b) An administrative order issued by the Mayor under subsection (a) of this section
38 shall:
39 “(1) State the basis for the extension of the juvenile curfew hours;
40 “(2) State the locations in the District where the extended juvenile curfew hours
41 shall apply or state that the extended juvenile curfew hours shall apply throughout the District;
42 and
2

43 “(3) State the days and hours during which the extended juvenile curfew hours
44 shall be in effect; provided, that the extended juvenile curfew hours:
45 “(A) Shall not begin earlier than 8:00 p.m., nor extend past 6:00 a.m., on
46 any day;
47 “(B) Shall not remain in effect for more than 4 consecutive days, except as
48 provided in subsection (e) of this section; and
49 “(C) Shall not apply to groups of 8 or fewer individuals.
50 “(c) Except where emergency circumstances, as determined by the Mayor and described
51 in the administrative order issued under subsection (a) of this section, warrant otherwise,
52 extended juvenile curfew hours imposed under this section shall not take effect until at least 24
53 hours after the issuance of the administrative order.
54 “(d) Notice of the issuance of each administrative order issued under this section shall be
55 promptly posted on the main website of the District government and on the website of the
56 Metropolitan Police Department.
57 “(e) The Mayor may extend the time period during which extended juvenile curfew hours
58 imposed by this section shall remain in effect by issuing subsequent Mayor’s orders extending
59 the time period; provided, that each such extension of the time period shall not exceed 30 days.
60 “(f) If members of the Metropolitan Police Department discover juveniles in the
61 designated extended juvenile curfew hours zone during the hours of curfew, the Metropolitan
62 Police Department shall:
63 “(1) Where there:
64 “(A) Is not an imminent danger of bodily injury of significant damage to
65 property, issue at lest 2 clearly audible and understandable notices of violation of curfew; or
3

66 “(B) Is imminent danger of bodily injury or significant damage to
67 property, issue at least one clearly audible and understandable notice of violation of curfew;
68 “(2) Provide juveniles a reasonable and adequate time to disperse; and
69 “(3) Capture on body-worn camera each component of the order to return home
70 described in paragraph (1) of this subsection.
71 “Sec. 3b. Temporary designations of extended juvenile curfew hour zones.
72 “(a) The Chief of Police may designate any area of the District as an extended juvenile
73 curfew hours zone, in which extended juvenile curfew hours determined by the Chief of Police
74 shall apply, if the Chief of Police determines that the designation of the extended juvenile curfew
75 hours zone is appropriate to protect public safety or public or private property in the extended
76 juvenile curfew hours zone.
77 “(b) To designate an extended juvenile curfew hours zone, the Chief of Police shall issue
78 an order that includes:
79 “(1) The basis for the designation of the extended juvenile curfew hours zone;
80 “(2) The boundaries of the extended juvenile curfew hours zone; and
81 “(3) The days and hours of the extended juvenile curfew hours in the extended
82 juvenile curfew hours zone; provided, that the extended juvenile curfew hours in the extended
83 juvenile curfew hours zone:
84 “(A) Shall not begin earlier than 8:00 p.m., nor extend past 6:00 a.m., on
85 any day;
86 “(B) Shall not remain in effect for more than 4 consecutive days, except as
87 provided in subsection (e) of this section; and
88 “(C) Shall not apply to groups of 8 or fewer individuals.
4

89 “(c) In determining whether to designate an extended juvenile curfew hours zone, the
90 Chief of Police shall consider the following:
91 “(1) Within the preceding 30 days, the occurrence of:
92 “(A) Juvenile curfew violations in the proposed extended juvenile curfew
93 hours zone;
94 “(B) Police reports of disturbances of the public peace described in section
95 1 of An Act To confer concurrent jurisdiction on the police court of the District of Columbia in
96 certain cases, approved July 16, 1912 (37 Stat. 192; D.C. Official Code§ 22-1301), sections 6,
97 3,9, or 16of An act for the preservation of the public peace and the protection of property within
98 the District of Columbia, approved July 29, 1892 (27 Stat. 323; D.C. Official Code § 22-1307, §
99 22-1309, § 22-1312, or § 22-1318), section 211 of the District of Columbia Law Enforcement
100 Act of 1953, approved June 29, 1953 (67 Stat. 98; D.C. Official Code § 22-1321), or section 901
101 of An Act relating to crime and criminal procedure in the District of Columbia, approved
102 December 27, 1967 (81 Stat. 742; D.C. Official Code § 22-1322), in the proposed extended
103 juvenile curfew hours zone;
104 “(C) Police reports of dangerous crimes, as that term is defined in D.C.
105 Official Code § 23-1331(3), in the proposed extended juvenile curfew hours zone;
106 “(D) Police reports of crimes of violence, as that term is defined in D.C.
107 Official Code § 23-1331(4), in the proposed extended juvenile curfew hours zone; or
108 “(E) Police reports of destruction of or damage to public or private
109 property in the proposed extended juvenile curfew hours zone;
110 “(2) Intelligence or verifiable information indicative of an intent of 9 or more
111 juveniles to gather in the proposed extended juvenile curfew hours zone in such a manner that is
5

112 likely to result in substantial harm to the public safety, or the safety of those gathering, or
113 substantial damage to public or private property; and
114 “(3) Any other verifiable information from which the Chief of Police may
115 ascertain that public safety or public or private property in the proposed extended juvenile
116 curfew hours zone is likely to be endangered by the presence of unaccompanied juveniles in the
117 extended juvenile curfew hours zones.
118 “(d) Upon the designation of an extended juvenile curfew hours zone the Chief of Police
119 shall post notice of the designation on the website of the Metropolitan Police Department and
120 shall post signs in the immediate area of the extended juvenile curfew hours zone.
121 “(e) The Chief of Police may extend the duration of the designation of an extended
122 juvenile curfew hours zone to 30 days by issuing a subsequent order extending the duration of
123 the designation.
124 “(f) If members of the Metropolitan Police Department discover juveniles in the
125 designated extended juvenile curfew zone during the hours of curfew, the Metropolitan Police
126 Department shall:
127 “(1) Where there:
128 “(A) Is not an imminent danger of bodily injury or significant damage to
129 property, issue at least 2 clearly audible and understandable notices of violation of curfew; or
130 “(B) Is imminent danger of bodily injury or significant damage to
131 property, issue at least one clearly audible and understandable notice of violation of curfew;
132 “(2) Provide juveniles a reasonable and adequate time to disperse; and
133 “(3) Capture on body-worn camera each component of the order to return home
134 described in paragraph (1) of this subsection.

135 “(g)(1) An Advisory Neighborhood Commission, business improvement district, or Main
136 Street organization may petition the Chief of Police to establish an extended juvenile curfew
137 hours zone in an area covered by, or adjacent to the area covered by, such entity. Such petition
138 shall be approved by a formal vote of the members of the Advisory Neighborhood Commission,
139 the members of the board of the business improvement district, or the members of the board of
140 the Main Street organization.
141 “(2) A petition submitted pursuant to this subsection shall include the following
142 information:
143 “(A) The proposed boundaries of the extended juvenile curfew hours zone;
144 “(B) The proposed hours of the extended juvenile curfew;
145 “(C) The proposed duration, in days, of the proposed extended juvenile
146 curfew hours;
147 “(D) The reasons why the Advisory Neighborhood Commission, business
148 improvement district, or Main Street organization is requesting the designation of an extended
149 juvenile curfew hours zone; and
150 “(E) Such other information as the Chief of Police may require.
151 “(3) Consideration of a petition submitted under this section, and the
152 determination of whether to designate an extended juvenile curfew hours zone in response to the
153 petition, shall be at the sole discretion of the Chief of Police.”.
