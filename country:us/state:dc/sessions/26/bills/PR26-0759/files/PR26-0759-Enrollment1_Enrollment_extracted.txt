ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A RESOLUTION
26-476
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
July 14, 2026
To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to approve Modification Nos.
M02, M03, M03a, and M04 to Contract No. CW117619 with RCM of Washington, Inc.,
and to authorize payment for the goods and services received and to be received under
the modifications.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
resolution may be cited as the “Modification Nos. M02, M03, M03a, and M04 to Contract No.
CW117619 with RCM of Washington, Inc. Approval and Payment Authorization Emergency
Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
Sec. 2. (a) There exists an immediate need to approve Modification Nos. M02, M03,
M03a, and M04 to Contract No. CW117619 with RCM of Washington, Inc. to provide
residential services to citizens with intellectual and developmental disabilities, and to authorize
payment for the goods and services received and to be received under the modifications.
(b) By Modification No. M02, the Office of Contracting and Procurement, on behalf of
the Department on Disability Services, exercised a partial option of option year one in the not-to-
exceed amount of $259,142.83 for the period from March 1, 2026, through May 31, 2026.
(c) Modification No. M03 exercised another partial option for the period from May 31,
2026, through June 30, 2026, at no additional cost.
(d) Modification No. 03a exercised another partial option for the period from June 30,
2026, through July 30, 2026, at no additional cost.
(e) Proposed Modification No. 04 will exercise the remainder of option year one and
increase the not-to-exceed amount by $768,978.17 to a total not-to-exceed amount of
$1,028,121.
(f) Council approval is necessary because the modifications will increase the value of the
contract by more than $1 million during a 12-month period.
(g) Council approval is necessary to allow the continuation of these vital services.
Without Council approval, RCM of Washington, Inc. cannot be paid for goods and services
provided in excess of $1 million for the period from March 1, 2026, through February 28, 2027.
Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
