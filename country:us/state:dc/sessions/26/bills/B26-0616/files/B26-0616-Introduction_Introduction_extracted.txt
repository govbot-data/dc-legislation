17 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Body-Worn Camera Regulation and Reporting
18 Requirements Act of 2015 and Chapter 39 of Title 24 of the District of Columbia
19 Municipal Regulations to require release of body-worn camera recordings of
20 Metropolitan Police Department Officers present in the event of an officer-involved death
21 or serious use of force by a law enforcement officer other than a Metropolitan Police
22 Department officer.
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
25 act may be cited as the “Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency
26 Amendment Act of 2026”.
27 Sec. 2. Section 3004 of the Body-Worn Camera Regulation and Reporting Requirements
28 Act of 2015, effective October 22, 2015 (D.C. Law 21-36; D.C. Official Code § 5-116.33), is
29 amended as follows:
30 (a) Subsection (c)(2)(A) is amended as follows:
31 (1) Sub-subparagraph (i) is amended as follows:
32 (A) Sub-sub-subparagraph (I) is amended to read as follows:

33 “(I) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all
34 Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law
35 enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force;
36 and”.
37 (B) Sub-sub-subparagraph (II) is amended by striking the phrase “; and”
38 and inserting a semicolon in its place.
39 (2) A new sub-subparagraph (i-I) is added to read as follows:
40 “(i-I) Notwithstanding sub-subparagraph (i) of this subparagraph,
41 for incidents occurring between August 1, 2025, and the effective date of the Body-Worn
42 Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, passed on
43 emergency basis on March 3, 2026 (Enrolled version of Bill 26-XXX), within 10 business days
44 after the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency
45 Amendment Act of 2026, passed on emergency basis on March 3, 2026 (Enrolled version of Bill
46 26-XXX), publicly release:
47 “(I) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all
48 Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law
49 enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force; and
50 “(II) A description of the incident; and”.
51 (3) Sub-subparagraph (ii) is amended by striking the phrase “; and” and inserting
52 the phrase “; provided, that for all officer-involved deaths since August 1, 2025, the information
53 reported pursuant to this sub-subparagraph shall include the names of all Metropolitan Police
54 Department officers as well as other law enforcement officers directly involved in the officer-
55 involved death; and” in its place.
2

56 (b) Subsection (e)(1) is amended by striking the phrase “officer-involved death or serious
57 use of force” and inserting the phrase “officer-involved death directly involving an MPD officer
58 or a serious use of force directly involving an MPD officer” in its place.
59 (c) Subsection (g) is amended as follows:
60 (1) A new paragraph (1A) is added to read as follows:
61 “(1A) “Law enforcement officer” means any officer, agent, or employee of the
62 United States or any state or local government authorized by law or by a government agency to
63 engage in or supervise the prevention, detection, or investigation of any violation of criminal or
64 civil law.”.
65 (2) Paragraph (3) is amended as follows:
66 (A) Subparagraph (A) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan
67 Police Department officer” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department officer, or
68 any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
69 (B) Subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan
70 Police Department officer” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department officer, or
71 any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
72 (C) Subparagraph (C) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan
73 Police Department officer” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department officer, or
74 any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.
75 (D) Subparagraph (D) is amended by striking the phrase “Metropolitan
76 Police Department canine” and inserting the phrase “Metropolitan Police Department canine, or
77 canine of any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present” in its place.

78 Sec. 3. Chapter 39 of Title 24 of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations is
79 amended as follows:
80 (a) Section 3900 is amended as follows:
81 (1) Subsection 3900.9 is amended to read as follows:
82 “(a) For any incident involving an officer-involved death directly involving an MPD
83 member or a serious use of force directly involving an MPD member, members shall not review
84 any BWC recordings to assist in initial report writing.
85 “(b) For an incident other than those described in paragraph (a) of this subsection,
86 members shall indicate, when writing any initial or subsequent reports, whether the member
87 viewed BWC footage prior to writing the report and specify what BWC footage the member
88 viewed.”.
89 (2) Subsection 3900.10(a)(1) is amended as follows:
90 (A) Sub-subparagraph (A) is amended as follows:
91 (i) Sub-sub-subparagraph (i) is amended to read as follows:
92 “(i) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all
93 Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law
94 enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force;
95 and”.
96 (ii) Sub-sub-subparagraph (ii) is amended by striking the phrase “;
97 and” and inserting a semicolon in its place.
98 (B) A new sub-subparagraph (A-i) is added to read as follows:
4

99 “(A-i) Notwithstanding sub-subparagraph (A) of this subparagraph, for
100 incidents occurring between August 1, 2025, and the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera
101 Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, passed on emergency basis
102 on March 3, 2026 (Enrolled version of Bill 26-XXX), within 10 business days after the effective
103 date of the Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of
104 2026, passed on emergency basis on March 3, 2026 (Enrolled version of Bill 26-XXX), publicly
105 release:
106 “(i) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all
107 Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law
108 enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force; and
109 “(ii) A description of the incident; and”.
110 (C) Sub-subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase “; and” and
111 inserting the phrase “; provided, that for all officer-involved deaths since August 1, 2025, the
112 information reported pursuant to this sub-subparagraph shall include the names of all
113 Metropolitan Police Department officers as well as other law enforcement officers directly
114 involved in the officer-involved death; and” in its place.
115 (b) Section 3999.1 is amended by adding a new definition between the definitions of
116 “FOIA” and “metadata” to read as follows:
117 ““Law enforcement officer” - means any officer, agent, or employee of the United States
118 or any state or local government authorized by law or by a government agency to engage in or
119 supervise the prevention, detection, or investigation of any violation of criminal or civil law.”.
120 Sec. 4. Applicability.
121 This act shall apply as of August 1, 2025.
