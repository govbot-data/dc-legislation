Dear Secretary Smith,
Today, I am introducing Small Retailer Property Tax Credit Expansion Amendment Act of 2025
along with my colleagues Councilmember Janeese Lewis George, Councilmember Brooke Pinto,
and Councilmember Robert White. This legislation would expand the small retailer property tax
credit (from $10,000 to $20,000) that allows retail businesses to offset their property taxes or
rent paid to their landlord for property taxes.
This year, due to a confluence of systemic factors, far too many local restaurants have closed and
many more small businesses are struggling to make ends meet. According to the Restaurant
Association of Metropolitan Washington (RAMW), on average, two restaurants are closing
every week with 53 closing in the first half of 2025 (nearly double 2024’s pace)1. Food and
beverage sales are forecast to drop 4.2% in FY2026 ($569M to $545M)2, and a majority of
restaurants are reporting declines in both sales and customer traffic.
District retailers—including neighborhood-facing restaurants—are facing significant headwinds,
including inflationary pressures, high rents, impacts on demand from federal activities, and more
recently a government shutdown. International tourist arrivals have dropped significantly, with
hotel occupancy down 7.9% year-over-year and room rates falling 6.4%3 —directly impacting
restaurant traffic. Tariffs also have driven up operational costs for ingredients and equipment
while immigration-related workforce disruptions compound staffing challenges.
These compounding factors threaten the viability of small, independently owned restaurants that
serve as neighborhood anchors and provide employment pathways for thousands of District

21 To amend Chapter 18 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code to expand the small
22 retailer property tax credit that allows retail businesses to reduce their property taxes or
23 rent paid to property taxes.
24
25 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this act may
26 be cited as the “Small Retailer Property Tax Credit Expansion Amendment Act of 2025”.
27 Sec. 2. Chapter 18 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code is amended as
28 follows:
29 (a) Section 47-1807.14(a) is amended as follows:
30 (1) Paragraph (5) is amended to read as follows:
31 “(5) “Maximum credit amount” means:
32 “(A) For tax years beginning after December 31, 2017, and before January
33 1, 2024, $5,000;
34 “(B) For the tax year ending December 31, 2024, $10,000;
35 “(C) For tax year ending December 31, 2025, $10,000, increased annually

36 pursuant to the cost-of-living adjustment (if the adjustment does not result in a multiple of $100,
37 rounded down to the next multiple of $100);
38 “(D) For the tax year ending December 31, 2026, $20,000;
39 “(E) For tax years beginning after December 31, 2026, $20,000, increased
40 annually pursuant to the cost-of-living adjustment (if the adjustment does not result in a multiple
41 of $100, rounded down to the next multiple of $100).”.
42 (b) Section 47-1808.14(a)(5) is amended to read as follows:
43 “(5) “Maximum credit amount” means:
44 “(A) For tax years beginning after December 31, 2017, and before January
45 1, 2024, $5,000;
46 “(B) For the tax year ending December 31, 2024, $10,000;
47 “(C) For tax year ending December 31, 2025, $10,000, increased annually
48 pursuant to the cost-of-living adjustment (if the adjustment does not result in a multiple of $100,
49 rounded down to the next multiple of $100);
50 “(D) For the tax year ending December 31, 2026, $20,000;
51 “(E) For tax years beginning after December 31, 2026, $20,000, increased
52 annually pursuant to the cost-of-living adjustment (if the adjustment does not result in a multiple
53 of $100, rounded down to the next multiple of $100).”.
