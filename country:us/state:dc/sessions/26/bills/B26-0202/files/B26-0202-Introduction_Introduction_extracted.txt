21 To establish an Office of Social Housing Developments to foster the construction, maintenance,
22 and growth of District-owned residential properties designed to be mixed-income housing
23 with not less than two-thirds of a building developed as family units and not less than
24 two-thirds of units rented as permanently affordable units for extremely-, very-, and low-
25 income households, and to establish a framework for tenant governance, environmentally
26 conscious building standards, and street-level amenities that serve a public purpose at
27 social housing developments; to amend the District of Columbia Government
28 Comprehensive Merit Personnel Act of 1978 to provide for the appointment of the
29 Director of the Office; to amend the Green Finance Authority Establishment Act of 2018
30 to allow investments in District-owned social housing developments; to amend an Act
31 Authorizing the sale of certain real estate in the District of Columbia no longer required
32 for public purposes to require that the Mayor evaluate such properties for conversion into
33 social housing developments before disposing of them; to amend the Housing Production
34 Trust Fund Act of 1988 to make social housing developments eligible to receive loans
35 and grants; and to amend The Rental Housing Conversion and Sale Act of 1980 to allow
36 the District to purchase residential property for conversion into social housing
37 developments.
38
39 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
40 act may be cited as the “Housing Development Growth Amendment Act of 2025”.

43 Sec. 101. DEFINITIONS.
44 For the purposes of this chapter, the term:
45 (1) “Area Median Income” shall have the same meaning as provided in section 2(1) of
46 the Housing Production Trust Fund Act of 1988, effective March 16, 1989 (D.C. Law 7-202;
47 D.C. Official Code § 42-2801(1)).
48 (2) “Bonds” means any bond, note, debenture, interim certificate, or other evidence of
49 financial indebtedness of the Office authorized to be issued under the provisions of this chapter.
50 (3) “Extremely low-income” means a household income equal to, or less than, 30% of the
51 area median income.
52 (4) “Fair market value” means the average, unsubsidized rental rate a reasonable person
53 could expect to pay for a comparable dwelling unit when considering the age, condition, and
54 location of similar properties.
55 (5) “Low-income” means a household income equal to between 51% and 80% of the area
56 median income.
57 (6) “Mixed-income” means a property that includes tenants with a range of household
58 incomes including extremely low-, very low-, low-, and above low-income levels.
59 (7) “Mixed-use” shall mean a multi-family residential development that includes space
60 for commercial or institutional purposes in accordance with zoning regulations set forth by the
61 Office of Zoning.
62 (8) “Net-zero emissions” means all energy is produced on-site, and to the degree that off-
63 site energy production is necessary, it is provided via contracts for electricity produced from
64 renewable sources and no energy may be produced from combustion or other sources that emit
65 greenhouse gases.
2

66 (9) “Operating costs” means any costs associated with maintaining a Social Housing
67 Development including management expenses, maintenance, utilities, and associated services.
68 (10) “Permanently affordable” means a tenant’s rent, in perpetuity, will not exceed 30%
69 of tenant household income if, at the time a tenant signs an initial lease, they qualify as
70 extremely low-, very low-, or low-income relative to area median income.
71 (11) “Personal mobility device” shall have the same meaning as provided in § 50-
72 2201.02(13).
73 (12) “Real property” means land titled in the District or in which the District has a
74 controlling interest and includes all structures of a permanent character erected thereon or affixed
75 thereto, any natural resources located thereon or thereunder, all riparian rights attached thereto,
76 or any air space located above or below the property or any street or alley under the jurisdiction
77 of the Mayor.
78 (13) “Rent” means money owed to occupy a unit in a social housing development,
79 including:
80 (A) Money to be paid directly by a tenant, and
81 (B) Funding associated with housing voucher programs operated by the District or
82 Federal government.
83 (14) “Restorative justice” means a process of allowing tenants or property managers, or
84 management sub-contractors, who are causing harm in the community to take responsibility,
85 reconcile with impacted parties, and address root causes of the harm with the goal of restoring all
86 parties to the level of trust and cooperation prior to the harm.
87 (15) “Social housing development” means real property that:
3

88 (A) Is owned by the District of Columbia and operated by the Office of Social
89 Housing Development;
90 (B) Provides mixed-income apartment housing that is permanently affordable for
91 lower income tenants; and
92 (C) Uses the revenue generated from rent to further the purpose and duties of The
93 Office of Social Housing Development pursuant to section 103 of this Act.
94 (16) “Street-level” means the portion of a property that sits on or most proximately opens
95 to the same level as the street or sidewalk. “Street-level” may also be considered ground floor or
96 ground level.
97 (17) “Tenant” means a tenant, subtenant, lessee, sublessee, or other person entitled to the
98 possession, occupancy or benefits of a rental unit within a social housing development. The
99 singular term “tenant” includes the plural.
100 (18) “Tenant association” means:
101 (A) A group of tenants organized to represent their collective interests as residents
102 of social housing developments;
103 (B) An association that represent a minimum of 51% of the households in a
104 building, as determined by rules established by the tenant association pursuant to section 106(b);
105 and
106 (C) the term “tenant association” may also include a tenant union, tenant
107 organization, tenant council, or similar term reflecting the chosen name of a duly organized
108 group of tenants of a social housing development.
109 (19) “Universal design” means creating a built environment to be accessible and usable to
110 the greatest number of people regardless of ability, age, or other characteristics.
4

111 (20) “Utility provider” means a provider of services for electricity, water, and
112 telecommunications and internet.
113 (21) “Very low income” means a household income equal to between 31% and 50% of
114 the area median income.
115 Sec. 102. Office of Social Housing Development Establishment.
116 (a)(1) Pursuant to section 404(b) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved
117 December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 787; D.C. Official Code § 1-204.04(b)), the Council establishes an
118 Office of Social Housing Development (“The Office”) as a subordinate office within the
119 executive branch of the District government.
120 (2) The Office shall be under the direction and agency cluster of the Deputy
121 Mayor for Planning and Economic Development.
122 (b) The purpose of The Office is:
123 (1) The development, construction, conversion, retention, operation, and
124 maintenance of District-owned real property to generate permanently affordable, well-
125 maintained mixed-income rental housing accommodations (“social housing developments”) that
126 meet high environmental standards for District residents;
127 (2) The conversion of District-owned land into social housing developments; and
128 (3) The cultivation of street-level commercial tenant amenities that serve a public
129 purpose at all properties developed by The Office pursuant to paragraphs (1) and (2) of this
130 subsection where properties are zoned for mixed-use.
131 (c) The Office shall be headed by a Director, who shall be appointed by the Mayor with
132 the advice and consent of the Council pursuant to section 2(a) of the Confirmation Act of 1978,
133 effective March 3, 1979 (D.C. Law 2-142; D.C. Official Code § 1-523.01(a)).
5

134 (d)(1) The Mayor shall establish a board (“Board”) to govern the operations of The
135 Office which shall consist of 9 residents of the District of Columbia with the following
136 qualifications:
137 (A) Two members shall have expertise in the financing or development of
138 affordable housing for extremely low-income households;
139 (B) One member shall have expertise in the management or operation of
140 affordable housing for extremely low-income households;
141 (C) Two members shall have expertise in housing law, legal services, or
142 tenant organizing; and
143 (D) Four members shall be elected by tenants of social housing
144 developments to serve as tenant-representatives to the Board.
145 (2) Board members shall serve three-year terms and vacancies shall be filled
146 within 90 days of a board member departure.
147 (3) The Board shall establish bylaws to govern their operations, leadership, and
148 meeting procedures.
149 (4) With input from the Director, The Office and the social housing coordinating
150 council established in section 103(b), The Board shall create key performance indicators and
151 development goals for new social housing developments which shall be shared with the Council
152 of the District of Columbia and with tenant association leadership board on an annual basis.
153 (5) Board members shall participate in annual trainings related to:
154 (A) The role of the governing body of a District office;
155 (B) District government ethics;

156 (C) Background on major District and federal housing programs and
157 financing opportunities; and
158 (D) Fair housing, reasonable accommodations, and non-discrimination.

275 Sec. 104. Social Housing Development Fund.

277 ("Fund"), which shall be administered by The Office of Social Housing Development. The
278 purpose of the Fund is to:

285 (4) Pay for services related to tenant governance and property management
286 pursuant to sections 106 and 107 of this Act;

12

290 (6) Establish and manage capital and operating reserves to carry out fiduciary

296 Sec. 105. Residential Characteristics of Social Housing Developments.
297 (a) Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, when the Office of Social
298 Housing Development develops real property, the building’s residential development shall
299 include the following:
300 (1) Not less than 30% of apartment units constructed with three, or more, bedrooms;
301 (2) Not less than 30% of apartment units constructed with two bedrooms;
302 (3) Universal design standards; and
303 (4) When street-level commercial office space is not available, one, or more, apartment
304 units shall be designed and dedicated as office space for providers of case management or counseling
305 services associated with District housing and economic security programs.
306 (b)(1) When the Office of Social Housing Development acquires real property through a
307 TOPA or DOPA process pursuant to The Rental Housing Conversion and Sale Act of 1980,
308 effective September 10, 1980 (D.C. Law 3-86; D.C. Official Code § 42-3401.01 et seq.), at such
309 time when the building requires a substantial renovation, the Office shall produce a renovation
310 plan to generate redevelopment of multi-bedroom units in an amount equal to or greater than the
311 number of multi-bedroom units in the existing building.
312 (2) The Office shall guarantee a right to return to all tenants who wish to move
313 back to a renovated property.
13

314 (3) The Office shall engage the tenant association leadership board in
315 redevelopment planning processes to ensure the right to return and equitable development of
316 multi-bedroom units is achieved through renovations.
317 (c) Except as provided in subsection (d) of this section, when the Office of Social
318 Housing Development develops real property, each social housing development shall initially
319 rent apartment units according to the following distribution:
320 (1) Not less than 30% of tenant households shall, at the time of application, be
321 considered extremely low-income;
322 (2) Not less than 30% of tenant households shall, at the time of application, be
323 considered very low or low income; and
324 (3) Not more than 40% of units shall be leased to households who shall agree to
325 pay a fair market value in monthly rent.
326 (d) When the Office of Social Housing Development acquires real property through a
327 TOPA or DOPA process pursuant to The Rental Housing Conversion and Sale Act of 1980,
328 effective September 10, 1980 (D.C. Law 3-86; D.C. Official Code § 42-3401.01 et seq.), new
329 rents shall be set according to annual household income such that no tenant household is paying
330 more than 30% of their income in rent.
331 (e) When a tenant moves out of a social housing development, the Office shall fill the
332 vacant unit according to the distribution requirements and housing preference bonus
333 considerations set forth in subsections (c)(1)-(3) and (f) of this section.
334 (f) In evaluating tenant applications for social housing units, the Office shall assign a
335 25% housing preference bonus to prospective tenants who, at the time of application, are
336 employed or are beginning employment in one of the following professional sectors:
14

337 (1) Education, including early childhood education, Pre-kindergarten to 12th grade
338 instruction, higher education, out-of-school time programming, and educational administration;
339 (2) Health, including nursing, mental health care, home health assistance, and
340 emergency medical services; or
341 (3) Case management or direct service professions.
342 (g)(1) A tenant of a social housing development property who, at the time of their initial
343 lease agreement, is considered extremely-, very-, or low-income, shall never pay more than 30%
344 of their monthly income in rent.
345 (2) the Office of Social Housing Development shall certify a tenant’s household
346 income on an annual basis to determine their monthly rent rate;
347 (3) If a tenant’s income is reduced or disrupted mid-year, a tenant may present
348 their situation to The Office to request a temporary reduction in rent to match 30% of their new
349 income level until the time of annual recertification, provided that a rent adjustment shall not be
350 granted below $250 per month.
351 (h) The Office of Social Housing Development shall not increase residential rent:
352 (1) More than once annually;
353 (2) Without 60-days advance written notice; or
354 (3) More than the average of the Consumer Price Index for the Washington-
355 Baltimore Metropolitan Statistical Area for all-urban consumers published by the Department of
356 Labor, or any successor index, as of the close of the 12-month period ending on November 30 of
357 such year.

435 Sec. 107. Responsibilities of Social Housing Property Management.

533 Sec. 109. Environmental Standards at Social Housing Developments.
534 (a) When constructed or renovated, social housing developments shall meet high
535 environmental standards, including:
536 (1) Net-zero emissions, including no energy from combustion or any sources that
537 emit greenhouse gases;
538 (2) High-efficiency, all-electric heating and cooling systems;
539 (3) Energy-efficient or high-efficiency appliances, windows, and lights in all
540 apartment units and common spaces;
23

541 (4) On-site solar energy production to the maximum extent practicable, including
542 using the District’s solar installation program and employing labor through the District’s solar
543 installation workforce training programs;
544 (5) Eco-friendly landscape architecture, including green walls and green roofs, to
545 maximize natural cooling;
546 (6) Low-flow toilets, smart shower and washing machine technologies, and rain
547 capture and recycling techniques to reduce water waste;
548 (7) On-site composting services;
549 (8) On-site electric vehicle and electric bike charging ports that are accessible to
550 tenants and to the public;
551 (9) Indoor facilities to lock and store bicycles and other personal mobility devices;
552 and
553 (10) To the extent that on-site parking is developed or available at a social
554 housing development, not less than 20% of parking spaces shall be reserved for agreements with
555 car-sharing services.
556 (b) Social housing developments shall not include the following:
557 (1) Natural gas for heating, hot water, or cooking; or
558 (2) Off-street parking in excess of zoning regulations.
