12 To amend the Homeless Services Reform Act to establish grounds for extensions to participation
13 in the Family Re-Housing Stabilization Program (FRSP) that require the Department of
14 Human Services or its designee to consider the totality of the participant's circumstances,
15 including the participant’s progress in case management and eligibility for affordable
16 housing.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
19 Act may be cited as the “Family Re-Housing Stabilization Program Protection Temporary
20 Amendment Act of 2025”.
21 Sec. 2. Section 7(b)(4)(B) of the Homeless Services Reform Act, effective October 22,
22 2005 (D.C. Law 16-35; D.C. Official Code § 4–753.01(b)(4)(B)), is amended as follows:
23 (a) The existing text is designated as sub-subparagraph (i).
24 (b) New sub-subparagraphs (ii), (iii), and (iv) are added to read as follows:
25 “(ii) Notwithstanding any other law; provided, that funding is
26 available within the Family Re-Housing Stabilization Program (“FRSP”) to implement this sub-
27 subparagraph and sub-subparagraphs (iii) and (iv) of this subparagraph, the Department, or the
28 Department’s designee, shall consider requests for FRSP assistance extending past 12 months if:

29 “(I) The participant has requested an extension in
30 writing;
31 “(II) The participant has made a good faith effort
32 towards the achievement of goals set forth in an individualized plan with the aim of a targeted
33 progression toward exit from the supports of FRSP, as observed by a service provider at consistent
34 intervals, but cannot yet sustain housing stability independently of FRSP; and
35 “(III) The participant has not yet been approved for
36 permanently affordable housing.
37 “(iii) When making a determination of whether to grant a
38 participant in FRSP an extension beyond 12 months, the Department, or the Department's
39 designee, shall:
40 “(I) Consider the totality of the circumstances; and
41 “(II) Grant extensions of time in increments not
42 greater than 6 months, with formal reviews every 3 months to ensure that participants are given
43 the support necessary to exit FRSP with stable housing.
44 “(iv) If a requested extension of FRSP assistance by a
45 participant is denied, the participant shall be given 30 days written notice prior to the final subsidy
46 payment explicitly setting forth the reason for the denial of additional assistance and informing the
47 participant that the participant has a right to:

48 “(I) Appeal the determination through a fair
49 hearing and administrative review, including deadlines for requesting an appeal; and
50 “(II) The continuation of FRSP services
51 pending the outcome of any fair hearing requested within 15 days of receipt of a written notice of
52 a termination.”.
53 Sec. 3. Applicability.
54 This act shall apply as of July 10, 2025.
55 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
56 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
57 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
58 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
59 Sec. 5. Effective date.
60 (a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by
61 the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of congressional review as
62 provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December24,
63 1973 (87 Stat. 788; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)), and publication in the District of
64 Columbia Register.
65 (b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.
