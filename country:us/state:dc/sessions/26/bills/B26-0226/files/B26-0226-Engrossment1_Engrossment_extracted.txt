1 To amend the Department of Behavioral Health Establishment Act of 2013 to establish a
2 program for place-based substance use disorder outreach.
3
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
5 act may be cited as the “Place-Based Substance Use Disorder Outreach Amendment Act of
6 2026”.
7 Sec 2. The Department of Behavioral Health Establishment Act of 2013, effective
8 December 24, 2013 (D.C. Law 20-61; D.C. Official Code § 7-1141.01 et seq.), is amended by
9 adding a new Part C to read as follows:
10 “PART C.
11 “Sec. 5120d. Definitions.
12 “For the purposes of this part, the term:
13 “(1) “Direct support” means behavioral health crises assessment and intervention
14 services.
15 “(2) “High-need area” means a defined geographic area, which may include a
16 neighborhood, corridor, or cluster of blocks, designated by the Department as experiencing a

17 disproportionately high concentration of substance use disorder activity, including opioid use,
18 overdoses, or related behavioral health service needs, based on available public health data,
19 service utilization data, or other relevant indicators.
20 “(3) “Relationship development” means regular interactions, and the
21 incorporation of peer supports, when possible, that foster open communications and encourage
22 active engagement with behavioral health services for individuals using substances.
23 “(4) “Resource brokering” means assistance with accessing supportive services
24 and public benefits, including housing, food, Medicaid, Social Security Income or Social
25 Security Disability Insurance, and transportation services needed to access primary and
26 behavioral health care.
27 “Sec. 5120e. Establishment of the Place-Based Substance Use Disorder Outreach
28 Program.
29 “(a) There is established, within the Department, the Place-Based Substance Use
30 Disorder Outreach Program (“Program”), which shall issue grants to 501(c)(3) not-for-profit
31 organizations with experience in providing direct supports, relationship development, and
32 resource brokering to individuals in need of substance use treatment and behavioral health
33 services in high-need areas designated by the Department.
34 “(b)(1) The Department, in consultation with the grantees, may designate a high-need
35 area for a period of time to be determined by the Department based on programmatic needs, but
36 not to exceed 180 days.

37 “(2) The Department may designate a high-need area for a short-term period of
38 not more than 90 days if the Department determines that emergent circumstances warrant
39 immediate intervention.
40 “(3) The Department may modify the geographic boundaries of a high-need area
41 if data indicates that substance use activity has shifted to nearby locations.
42 “(c) In designating a high-need area, the Department shall consider whether, within the
43 preceding 6-month period, there was a disproportionately high rate of:
44 “(1) Substantiated reports of substance use in public spaces;
45 “(2) Hospitalizations, overdoses, and deaths from substance use;
46 “(3) High reoccurrences of the same individuals using substances in the same
47 vicinity; or
48 “(4) Areas with a high concentration of pedestrian and business interaction with
49 people experiencing substance use disorder.
50 “(d) For each designated high-need area, the Department shall within 45 days of the
51 designation:
52 “(1) Notify local businesses, business improvement districts, and relevant
53 community-based organizations of the high-need area designation and provide information on:.
54 “(A) The purpose and scope of the Program, including the services and
55 supports available;
56 “(B) How to contact the grantee assigned to the area; and
