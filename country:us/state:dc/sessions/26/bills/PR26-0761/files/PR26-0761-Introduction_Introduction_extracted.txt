15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Advisory
16 Neighborhood Commissions Act of 1975 to modify the notice requirement for certain
17 business licenses; and to amend Chapter 28 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia
18 Official Code to exempt motor clubs that perform private tow services from towing
19 service storage lot requirements, to authorize 4-year basic business licenses, to extend
20 license renewal permission beyond 9 months after expiration, to authorize the Mayor to
21 issue refunds for erroneously paid business license fees, to revise the eligibility
22 requirements for accountancy licensure by authorizing a holder of a baccalaureate degree
23 with 2 years of experience and who has passed an examination in accounting and auditing
24 to obtain a license, to allow the Mayor to add additional licensure requirements by
25 rulemaking, and to amend mobility requirements for those who are licensed in another
26 jurisdiction and want to practice in the District.
27
28 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
29 resolution may be cited as the “Business Licensing Reform and Accountancy Practice
30 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
31 Sec. 2. (a) The Business and Entrepreneurship Support to Thrive Amendment Act of
32 2022, effective March 22, 2023 (D.C. Law 24-333; 70 DCR 1524) (“BEST Act”) streamlined
33 and modernized certain aspects of the District’s business licensing system. The BEST Act was
34 fully funded in subsequent budget legislation, and full implementation began October 1, 2025.
1

35 (b) In the course of implementation, the Department of Licensing and Consumer
36 Protection (DLCP) identified licensing process concerns and recommended adjustments.
37 Accordingly, the Council unanimously passed Bill 26-362, the Revised Business Licensing
38 Reform Emergency Amendment Act of 2025, effective October 23, 2025 (D.C. Act 26-165), and
39 Bill 26-363, the Revised Business Licensing Reform Temporary Amendment Act of 2025,
40 effective December 31, 2025 (D.C. Law 26-78; 73 DCR 611).
41 (c) The Council recently unanimously approved the Business Licensing Reform and
42 Accountancy Practice Amendment Act of 2026, passed on 1st reading on June 30, 2026
43 (Engrossed version of Bill 26-494), and final Council approval is anticipated on July 14, 2026.
44 This permanent legislation incorporates the same licensing reform provisions as Bill 26-362, but
45 is not expected to complete congressional review and take effect until after Bill 26-362 expires
46 on August 13, 2026.
47 (d) For the reasons noted in the Revised Business Licensing Reform Emergency
48 Declaration Resolution of 2025, effective October 7, 2025 (Res. 26-219; 72 DCR 11722), failure
49 to keep these provisions in place could have significant adverse impacts on businesses and the
50 economic growth of the District.
51 (e) The District is experiencing a shortage of licensed accounting professionals. The
52 pending permanent Business Licensing Reform and Accountancy Practice Amendment Act of
53 2026 will also provide additional licensure opportunities to would-be accounting professionals.
54 Failure to institute these changes promptly would deprive the most recent class of graduates from
55 accounting programs of access to licensure.
2

56 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
57 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
58 Business Licensing Reform and Accountancy Practice Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be
59 adopted after a single reading.
60 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
