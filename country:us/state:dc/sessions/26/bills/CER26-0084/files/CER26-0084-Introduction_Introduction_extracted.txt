7 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
8 __________
9
10 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
11 __________
12
13
14 To recognize and honor DC native and longtime Washington Post columnist Colbert I. King on
15 the occasion of his upcoming retirement in September 2025.
16
17 WHEREAS, Colbert “Colby” I. King was born in September 1939 and grew up in the old Foggy
18 Bottom section of Washington, DC;
19
20 WHEREAS, Mr. King attended Thaddeus Stevens Elementary School, Francis Jr. High School,
21 and graduated from Paul Laurence Dunbar High School in 1957, where he was a member of the ROTC
22 and Dunbar’s championship drill team and earned his BA degree in government from Howard University
23 in 1961;
24
25 WHEREAS, prior to his distinguished career as a journalist with the Washington Post, Mr. King
26 played a pivotal role in shaping the future of the District of Columbia through his involvement as a Senate
27 staffer in the development of the Home Rule Act, helping lay the foundation for self-governance and
28 greater civic autonomy for the residents of Washington, DC;
29
30 WHEREAS, Mr. King joined the Washington Post in September 1990 and remains, until his
31 pending retirement in September 2025, a beloved and insightful contributor to the publication;
32
33 WHEREAS, through his decades of sharp political commentary, Mr. King has consistently held
34 elected officials accountable, lent moral weight to critical policy debates, and offered feared and fearless
35 endorsements of local candidates for political office and critiques that shaped public understanding of
36 local, national and international issues;
37
38 WHEREAS, Mr. King’s columns, rooted in both historical knowledge and personal integrity,
39 have examined the character of political leadership in the District and addressed movements for social
40 and economic justice;
41
42 WHEREAS, Mr. King received many accolades for his work, including a Pulitzer Prize in 2003
43 for Commentary, with the Columbia University Graduate School for Journalism citing his “against-the-
44 grain columns that speak to people in power with ferocity and wisdom”;
45
46 WHEREAS, his retirement after 35 years as a Post journalist marks the conclusion of an
47 extraordinary chapter in American journalism;
48
49 WHEREAS, his willingness to ask tough questions and confront uncomfortable truths leaves a
50 journalistic legacy that will be last well beyond his retirement.

51
52 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this resolution
53 may be cited as the “Colbert I. King Recognition Resolution of 2025.”
54
55 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors DC native Colbert I. King
56 for his invaluable contributions to public life, to journalism and to the civic and moral conscience of the
57 United States and the District of Columbia and extend to him our deepest gratitude and abiding respect
58 for his work.
59
60 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
61 District of Columbia Register.
