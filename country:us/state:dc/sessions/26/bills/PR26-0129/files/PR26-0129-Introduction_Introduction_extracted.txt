Matthew Bell
Mr. Matthew Bell, FAIA, currently serves as an architect memberofthe
Historic Preservation Review Board. He is ProfessorofArchitecture at
the University of Maryland and Principal at Perkins Eastman in
Washington, DC. He has been active throughout the Washington and
Baltimore regions with projects ranging from waterfronts,newtowns, and
neighborhoods to residential projects, civic and mixed-use buildings, and
schools. He has served as presidentof the Neighborhood Design Center
in Baltimore and Prince George’s County, Maryland, and from 1994 to
1999 was the Directorof the Northeast Mayor’s Institute forCity Design.
Mr. Bell’s work hasbeen exhibited atthe Triennaledi Milanoand he has
served as ajuror forthe Biennale ofVenice (Italy). Projects with Perkins
Eastman include: master plans for the new town of Crown, MD; the Bareroft community in
Norther Virginia; and the MeMillan site in Washington, DC; Dunbar High School (DC); the new
Dining Commons at Catholic University; Cleveland Park Library (DC); Collection 14 (DC); and
campus master plans for George Washington University and American University, both in
Washington, DC.
Mr. Bell isa Fellowofthe American Institute of Architects(“AIA”) and a Fellowof theCongress
for the New Urbanism (“CNU”). His work has received national and local recognition from the
AIA and awards as well from the AssociationofCollegiate SchoolsofArchitecture, CNU, the
U.S. Green Buil ding Council, the American Society ofLandscape Arch itects, the Committee of
100forthe Federal City, andthe Urban Land Institute, In 2020 he was appointed by Mayor Muriel
Bowser tothe Historic Preservation Review Board.
‘A Ward 3 resident, Mr. Bell earned his Bachelor ofArchitecture from the University ofNotre
Dame and his MasterofArchitecture in Urban Design from Cornell University.
