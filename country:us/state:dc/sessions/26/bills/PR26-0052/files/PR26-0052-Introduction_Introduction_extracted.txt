13 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Open Meetings
14 Act of 2010 to provide that a meeting shall be deemed open to the public if the public
15 body takes steps reasonably calculated to allow the public to view or hear the meeting
16 while the meeting is taking place, or, if doing so is not technologically feasible, as soon
17 thereafter as reasonably practicable.
18
19 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
20 resolution may be cited as the “Virtual Open Meetings Authority Extension Emergency
21 Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
22 Sec. 2. (a) In March 2020, in response to the COVID-19 pandemic, the Council adopted
23 emergency legislation to ensure continuity of government operations during the ongoing public
24 health emergency. That emergency legislation included among other provisions legislative
25 language clarifying that a meeting shall be deemed open to the public, and therefore satisfy
26 requirements of the District’s Open Meetings Act, where the public body holding the meeting
27 takes steps reasonably calculated to allow the public to view or hear the meeting while the
28 meeting is taking place, or, if doing so is not technologically feasible, as soon thereafter as
29 reasonably practicable. By authorizing public meetings to be held virtually, that legislation
30 facilitated access to these meetings both for members of the public and the public body while
31 residents were advised to minimize travel outside the home to reduce the risk of transmitting the
32 COVID-19 virus.
1

33 (b) Since 2020, the Council has extended authorization for public bodies to hold meetings
34 in a virtual or remote format on several occasions. Although the public health emergency has
35 concluded, this authorization has provided public bodies with the flexibility to determine the
36 most efficient format for their meetings without reducing access for residents. In fact, largely,
37 providing a virtual option to attend meetings has increased access for many residents, including
38 seniors, individuals with mobility issues, care-givers, individuals who are immunocompromised,
39 and those who are ill. Although many public bodies have resumed in-person meetings, most
40 continue to stream their meetings online or otherwise make the meeting available in a virtual
41 format to allow residents to participate remotely.
42 (c) On April 2, 2024, the Council passed emergency legislation that included
43 authorization for public bodies to hold virtual meetings. That legislation, the Virtual Open
44 Meetings Authority Extension Emergency Amendment Act of 2024, effective April 15, 2024
45 (D.C. Act 25-442; 71 DCR 4621) (“emergency act”), expired on July 14, 2024.
46 (d) On May 7, 2024, the Council passed the Virtual Open Meetings Authority Extension
47 Temporary Amendment Act of 2024, effective July 19, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-194; 71 DCR
48 6752) (“temporary act”), which will expire on March 1, 2025.
49 (e) This emergency legislation is necessary to prevent a gap in the law following the
50 expiration of the cuurent temporary act.
51 Sec. 3. The Council determines that the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute
52 emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Virtual Open Meetings Authority
53 Extension Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
54 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately
