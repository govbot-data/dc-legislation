8 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
9
10 _________________________
11
12 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
13
14 _________________________
15
16
17 To recognize honoree Arlen Herrell during the 2026 celebration of DC Black Pride.
18
19 WHEREAS, Arlen Herrell is a public servant, entrepreneur, and community leader whose
20 work has focused on expanding opportunity, equity, and access for communities too often left
21 out of the room;
22
23 WHEREAS, Arlen Herrell currently serves as Senior Deputy Director at the D.C.
24 Department of Employment Services, where he helps lead workforce development initiatives
25 connecting District residents to training, career pathways, and economic mobility;
26
27 WHEREAS, Arlen Herrell’s career has been shaped by service at every level of
28 government, from the local and state to national levels;
29
30 WHEREAS, Arlen Herrell previously served in the Obama Administration and has been
31 a strong advocate for the My Brother’s Keeper initiative, championing mentorship and stronger
32 systems of support for boys and young men of color;
33
34 WHEREAS, before coming to Washington, D.C., Arlen Herrell was deeply engaged in
35 civic leadership in Cincinnati, where his passion for public service, political advocacy, and
36 building pathways for Black communities was first ignited;
37
38 WHEREAS, as part of his entrepreneurial ventures, Arlen Herrell is the founder of
39 aStatesman Consulting Group, a strategy and public affairs firm focused on workforce
40 development, government relations, program design, and community impact;
41
42 WHEREAS, Arlen Herrell owns ASC Properties, where he works to expand quality
43 housing opportunities and build long-term economic stability through real estate investments for
44 those in need;
1

45
46 WHEREAS, as a proud Black gay man and member of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc.,
47 Arlen Herrell moves with intention, using his voice and platform to speak boldly on politics,
48 culture, equity, and the lived experience and power of the Black and LGBTQIA+ communities;
49
50 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is an annual event to increase awareness of and showcase
51 pride in the diversity of the LGBTQIA+ populations in the African American community, and to
52 support organizations that focus on health disparities, education, youth, and families;
53
54 WHEREAS, as the very first Black Pride festival in the world, DC Black Pride fostered
55 the beginning of the Center for Black Equity and the “Black Pride Movement”;
56
57 WHEREAS, since the birth of DC Black Pride, more than 50 other Black Pride
58 celebrations now take place throughout the world and on 4 continents, with many using DC
59 Black Pride as a model;
60
61 WHEREAS, May 22, 2026, through May 25, 2026, marks the 35th annual DC Black
62 Pride celebration;
63
64 WHEREAS, the theme for this year’s celebration is “New Black Renaissance,” which
65 centers Black LGBTQIA+ “artists, leaders, healers, and organizers while confronting what still
66 makes Black Pride necessary, including HIV, inequity, homophobia, and transphobia;”
67
68 WHEREAS, for more than 30 years, DC Black Pride has been an opportunity for the
69 Black LGBTQIA+ community to gather and celebrate being both African American and
70 members of LGBTQIA+ communities;
71
72 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is the oldest and one of the largest Black Pride events in the
73 world, drawing thousands of residents and visitors from around the globe; and
74
75 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride brings more than 500,000 people to the nation’s capital
76 from across the United States, Canada, the Caribbean, South Africa, Great Britain, France,
77 Germany, and the Netherlands and will carry that tradition this year and into the future to
78 celebrate their shared community, raise awareness and funding for HIV/AIDS prevention, and
79 stand against homophobia inspired by violence and bigotry.
80
81 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That
82 this resolution be cited as the “Arlen Herrell Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
83
84 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates Arlen Herrell
85 during the 2026 celebration of DC Black Pride.
86
87 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
