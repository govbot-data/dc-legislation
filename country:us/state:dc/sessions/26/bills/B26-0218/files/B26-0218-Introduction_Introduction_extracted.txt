Dear Chairman Mendelson:
The “Furthering Admissions Inclusion and Representation (FAIR) Act of 2025” is enclosed for
consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia.
The State Board of Education approved this proposed bill for transmittal to the District of
Columbia Council by a vote of 8-1 on February 19, 2025.
The D.C. State Board of Education believes that the D.C. Council should take up this legislation
to dissuade higher education institutions from utilizing a donor or legacy preference in
admissions and to prohibit the Mayor from issuing or reissuing a license, permit, award for a
government-issued project, contract, or a grant to a higher education institution if it administers
a legacy or donor preference in admissions. The FAIR Act requires higher education institutions
to submit, on an annual basis, a report of District residents and graduates of local education
agencies who applied, were admitted, and enrolled in a higher education institution in the
District.
If you or your staff have any questions about this legislation, please contact me at (202) 741-
0888 or bernice.butler@dc.gov.
On behalf of the State Board of Education, I respectfully request that the Council take prompt and
favorable action on the enclosed legislation.

24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
25 act may be cited as "Furthering Admissions Inclusion and Representation (FAIR) Act of 2025".
26 TITLE I. DONOR AND LEGACY PREFERENCES IN HIGHER EDUCATION
27 ADMISSIONS.
28 Sec. 101. Definitions.
29 For the purposes of this act, the term:
30 (1) "Donor preference in admissions" means considering an applicant's relation to
31 a donor of, or a donation to, the higher education institution as a factor in the admissions process,
32 including asking an applicant to indicate their family's donor status and including that information
33 among the documents that the higher education institution uses to consider an applicant for
34 admission.

35 (2) “Higher Education Institution” means a college or university located in the
36 District of Columbia providing higher education leading to the award of a diploma, degree, or the
37 equivalent thereof.
38 (3)(A) “Legacy preference in admissions” means considering an applicant’s
39 relation to an alumni of the higher education institution as a factor in the admissions process,
40 including asking an applicant to indicate where their relatives attended college and including that
41 information among the documents that the higher education institution uses to consider an
42 applicant for admission.
43 (B) “Legacy preference in admissions” does not include collecting data on
44 an applicant’s relation to an alumni or donors for purposes other than admissions decisions.
45 (4) “Local education agency” means the District of Columbia Public Schools
46 system or any individual or group of public charter schools operating under a single charter.
47 Sec. 102. Limitations on Higher Education Institutions with Legacy and Donor
48 Preferences in Admissions.
49 (a) Higher education institutions that offer a legacy or donor preference in admissions
50 shall be prohibited from being any entities as described in subsection (d) of this section, except
51 in scenarios as provided in subsection (b) of this section.
52 (b)(1) The higher education institution may waive such admission limits, as described in
53 section (4)(a) of this section, for applicants who are descendants of people enslaved for the
54 benefit of that higher education institution if the higher education institution provides preferential
55 treatment in the admission process for those applicants who are actual descendants of people
56 enslaved for the benefit of that higher education institution.
2

57 (2)(A) A higher education institution shall be exempt from the provisions of this
58 section if it submits records to the Mayor documenting that 30% of the institution’s student body
59 receives Federal Pell Grants.
60 (B) The Mayor shall create a process for an institution to submit records
61 documenting that it has met the requirements of sub-paragraph (A) of this paragraph.
62 (c) The Mayor shall not issue or reissue a license or permit or award a government-
63 assisted project, contract, or grant requested by a higher education institution if the applicant
64 administers a legacy or donor preference in admissions.
65 (d) Higher education institutions that offer legacy or donor preferences in admission are
66 prohibited from being the following entities:
67 (1) The signatory to a contract or grant executed by the Mayor which involves any
68 District of Columbia government funds, or funds which, in accordance with a federal grant or
69 otherwise, the District government administers; and
70 (2) A recipient of a District government economic development action including
71 contracts, grants, loans, tax abatements, land transfers for redevelopment, or tax increment
72 financing that results in a financial benefit from an agency, commission, instrumentality, or other
73 entity of the District government.
74 TITLE II. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS.
75 Sec. 201. Section 202(b-1) of the Department of Education Establishment Act of 2007,
76 effective June 12, 2007 (D.C. Law 17-9; D.C. Official Code § 38-191), is amended as follows:
77 (1) Subsection (3)(A) is amended by adding a new subsection (vi) to read as
78 follows:
3

79 “(vi) Data on where students graduated from high school and their home
80 jurisdiction.”.
81 (2) A new subsection (4) is added to read as follows:
82 “(4)(A) Higher education institutions in the District shall submit, on an annual
83 basis to the Deputy Mayor for Education for the purposes of including in a centralized data
84 system, a report disaggregated by race or ethnicity, gender or non-binary from the previous five
85 academic years that includes the following information:
86 “(i) The number of first-time, full-time District residents who
87 applied, were admitted, enrolled, recipient of a Federal Pell Grant, recipient of a subsidized
88 Stafford loan who did not receive a Pell grant, received neither a Pell Grant or a subsidized
89 Stafford Loan, first generation, enrolled with credits, program of study, received remedial
90 academic supports, and received a certificate, diploma, degree, or the equivalent thereof within
91 five years;
92 “(ii) The number of first-time, full-time graduates from a local
93 education agency that applied, were admitted, enrolled, recipient of a Federal Pell Grant,
94 recipient of a subsidized Stafford loan who did not receive a Pell grant, received neither a Pell
95 Grant or a subsidized Stafford Loan, first generation, enrolled with credits, program of study,
96 received remedial academic supports, and received a certificate, diploma, degree, or the
97 equivalent thereof within five years;
98 “(iii) The number of Pell Grant-eligible students who applied,
99 were admitted, and enrolled;
4

100 “(iv) The number of first-time, full-time District residents that
101 applied, were admitted, and enrolled; and
102 “(v) The number of first-time, full-time non-District residents that
103 applied, were admitted, and enrolled.”.
104 (B) Data in the report submitted pursuant to clause (i) shall only be
105 publicly provided in the aggregate and in a manner that prevents the identification of any
106 individual.
107 (3) A new subsection (5) is added to read as follows:
108 “(5) Higher education institutions in the District shall submit, on an annual basis,
109 to the Deputy Mayor for Education for the purposes of including in a centralized data system, a
110 report from the previous five academic years that includes the following information:
111 “(A) A list of high schools that the higher education institution recruits
112 from for admission as first-year students;
113 “(B) An accounting of the value of the institutions' tax exemption in the
114 District; and
115 “(C) A listing of all public contracts and grants the institution receives
116 from the District or Federal Governments.”.
