ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A RESOLUTION
26-188
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
September 17, 2025
To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to approve Contract No.
DCRL-2024-C-0051, and to authorize payment for the goods and services received and
to be received under the contract.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
resolution may be cited as the “Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051 with The Hospital for Sick
Children Pediatric Center Approval and Payment Authorization Emergency Declaration
Resolution of 2025”.
Sec. 2. (a) There exists an immediate need to approve Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051
and to authorize payment for the goods and services received and to be received under the
contract.
(b) On August 20, 2024, the Child and Family Services Agency (“CFSA”), executed an
emergency Letter Contract with The Hospital for Sick Children Pediatric Center to provide in-
patient respite care services for medically fragile CFSA youth in the not-to-exceed amount of
$900,277.20.
(c) Modification No. M001 extended the period of performance through November 16,
2024, and decreased the not-to-exceed amount of the contract by $7,504.62 to a total not-to-
exceed amount of $892,772.58.
(d) Modification No. M002 extended the period of performance through December 9,
2024, and increased the contract by $69,519.25 to a total not-to-exceed amount of 955,291.83.
(e) On December 9, 2024, Letter Contract DCRL-2025-H-0022 was issued in a total not-
to-exceed amount of $987,804.15.
(f) On February 26, 2025, Letter Contract DCRL-2025-H-0040 was issued in a total not-
to-exceed amount of $990,304.92.
(g) Contract Nos. DCRL-2024-C-0051, DCRL-2025-H-0022, and DCRL-2025-C-0040
were combined into a single definitized contract, designated Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051,
which incorporated and superseded the previous letter contracts, extended the period of
performance through August 19, 2025, and increased the not-to-exceed amount by $730,224.84
to a total not-to-exceed amount of $3,663,625.74.

(h) Council approval is necessary because the value of the contract is more than $1
million during a 12-month period.
(i) Council approval is necessary to allow the continuation of these vital services.
Without Council approval, The Hospital for Sick Children Pediatric Center cannot be paid for
goods and services provided in excess of $1 million for the period August 20, 2024, through
August 19, 2025.
Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051 with The Hospital for Sick Children Pediatric Center
Approval and Payment Authorization Emergency Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
