17 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Block Party Act
18 of 2012 to enable residents hosting a musical performance on their property as part of a
19 Porchfest or other distributed musical festival to apply for block party permits.
20
21 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 resolution may be cited as the “Porchfest Permitting Emergency Declaration Resolution of
23 2025.”
24 Sec. 2. (a) Petworth PorchFest and Adams Morgan PorchFest are currently in the
25 planning phases for their spring events, and emergency legislation is needed to ensure they are
26 not negatively impacted by block party permit denials. A permanent bill to address this issue has
27 already been introduced and is expected to advance next year, but action is needed now to
28 protect the upcoming spring events.
29 (b) Porchfest events are volunteer-led community gatherings featuring small musical
30 performances hosted on porches and lawns throughout a neighborhood. These popular events
31 bring neighbors together and boost economic activity for local small businesses.
1

32 (c) The Block Party Act of 2012, effective October 23, 2012 (D.C. Law 19-190, D.C.
33 Official Code § 9-631 et seq.), was enacted to create a simple, accessible process for residents to
34 obtain permission to host neighborhood block parties.
35 (d) Residents who applied for block party permits to host PorchFest musical
36 performances have been denied because the events were considered part of a larger PorchFest
37 festival. They were told they must instead apply for a special event permit, which is used for
38 major, professionally organized events and is significantly more costly and complex.
39 (e) Volunteer hosts receive no funding or support for the performances and cannot afford the
40 costs associated with a special event permit that is designed for much larger events.
41 (f) An amendment to the Block Party Act of 2012 is needed to clarify that PorchFest
42 performances hosted by individual residents are a permissible use of the block party permitting
43 process and to ensure that the future of PorchFest events this spring is not in jeopardy.
44 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
45 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
46 Porchfest Permitting Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025 should be adopted after a single
47 reading.
48 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
