Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Pursuant to D.C. Official Code § 2-352.02, I have enclosed, for consideration by the Council of the District of
Columbia, the (a) Council Contract Summary to provide an increased amount in Local Rent Supplement Program
(“LRSP”) funds to be disbursed pursuant to that Agreement to Enter into a Long-Term Subsidy Contract (“ALTSC”)
between the District of Columbia Housing Authority (“DCHA”) and Flats at South Capitol LLC that was previously
approved by the DC Council on March 4, 2025 as Resolution Number 26-47 (see attached), and (b) proposed Local
Rent Supplement Program Contract No. 2025-LRSP-02A with Flats at South Capitol LLC LRSP Funding Increase
Approval Resolution of 2026. DCHA requests an increase in the currently DC Council approved initial annual LRSP
housing subsidy from $486,960 to an increased initial annual amount of $845,490.67 for a multi-year term of twenty
(20) years, in support of the District’s Local Rent Supplement Program (“LRSP”) to provide twenty-two (22)
affordable housing units at Flats at South Capitol Apartments located at 3838 South Capitol Street, SE.
As always, I am available to discuss any questions you may have regarding the proposed LTSC. In order to facilitate a
response to any questions concerning this ALTSC, please have your staff contact Hammere Gebreyes, Director of
External Affairs and Senior Advisor, at (202) 207-4694.
I look forward to your favorable consideration of this proposed ALTSC agreement.
Sincerely,
Nicole Wickliffe
Interim Executive Director, District of Columbia Housing Authority

27 Sec. 2. (a) In 2007, the District passed Title II of the Fiscal Year 2007 Budget Support
28 Act of 2006 ("BSA") to provide funding for affordable housing for extremely low-income
29 households in the District. The passage of the BSA created the LRSP, a program designed to
30 provide affordable housing and supportive services to extremely low-income District residents,
31 including those who are homeless or in need of supportive services, such as elderly individuals
32 or those with disabilities, through project-based, tenant-based, and sponsored-based LRSP
33 affordable housing units. The BSA provided for the District of Columbia Housing Authority
34 ("DCHA") to administer the LRSP on behalf of the District.

1 (b) On May 4, 2025, the Council of the District of Columbia approved Resolution
2 Number 26-47 that allowed DCHA to enter into the Agreement to Enter into a Long-Term
3 Subsidy Contract (“ALTSC”) with the Flats at South Capitol, LLC (the “Owner”) to provide
4 LRSP funding in the initial annual amount of no more than Four Hundred Eighty-Six Thousand
5 Nine Hundred Sixty Dollars ($486,960) for twenty-two (22) units in the project known as the
6 Flats at South Capitol Apartments located at 3848 South Capitol Street, SE (“The Flats”).
7 (c) There exists an immediate need to approve the increased LRSP funding for the
8 ALTSC in order to provide long-term affordable housing units for extremely low-income
9 households for units located at The Flats.
10 (d) The legislation to approve the increase in the LRSP funding will authorize the
11 currently approved LRSP funding of Four Hundred Eighty-Six Thousand Nine Hundred Sixty
12 Dollars ($486,960) for The Flats to be increased to Eight Hundred Forty-Five Thousand Four
13 Hundred Ninety and 67/00 Dollars ($845,490.67) under the ALTSC. The increase in LRSP
14 funding will allow the Owner to lease the rehabilitated units at The Flats and house extremely
15 low-income households with incomes at 30% or less of the area median income.
16 Sec. 3. Pursuant to section 451 of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved
17 December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 803; D.C. Official Code § 1-204.51), and section 202 of the
18 Procurement Practices Reform Act of 2010, effective April 8, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-371; D.C.
19 Official Code § 2-352.02), the Council approves the increase in the LRSP operating subsidy
20 in support twenty-two (22) affordable housing units at The Flats in an initial annual amount
21 not to exceed Eight Hundred Forty-Five Thousand Four Hundred Ninety and 67/00 Dollars
22 ($845,490.67).
