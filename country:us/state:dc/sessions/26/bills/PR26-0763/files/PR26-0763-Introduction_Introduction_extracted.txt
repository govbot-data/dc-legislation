18 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend, on an emergency
19 basis, section 1507 of the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council for the District of
20 Columbia Establishment Act of 2001 to change the date by which the Prearrest Diversion
21 Task Force shall issue initial recommendations for prearrest diversion of certain
22 misdemeanor offenses and categories of persons to July 2026; and to amend Title 20 of
23 the District of Columbia Official Code to allow for notice of request for formal probate,
24 notice from a foreign personal representative of a decedent who owned any property
25 located in the District of Columbia, and notice of appointment to interested persons,
26 creditors and unknown heirs in a legal periodical or newspaper of general circulation in
27 the District.
28
29 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
30 resolution may be cited as the “Prearrest Diversion Task Force Recommendations Emergency
31 Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
32 Sec. 2. (a) Section 1507 of the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council for the District of
33 Columbia Establishment Act of 2001, effective June 8, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-175; D.C. Official
34 Code § 22-4237) (“Secure DC”), requires the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council (“CJCC”)
35 to establish a Prearrest Diversion Task Force (“Task Force”) to review best practices and make
36 recommendations to implement prearrest diversion of certain misdemeanor offenses and for
37 certain categories of persons identified by the Task Force as being appropriate for diversion. The
38 Task Force was initially required to begin meeting within 3 months after the applicability date of

39 Secure DC, and issue its initial recommendations within one year after the applicability date of
40 Secure DC. However, due to CJCC staffing constraints, the Task Force was not able to begin
41 meeting until June 2025.
42 (b) On December 17, 2024, the Council passed on final reading the Strengthening
43 Probate Administration Amendment Act of 2024, effective March 21, 2025 (D.C. Law 25-302;
44 72 DCR 780) (“Strengthening Probate Act”), which proposed to modernize and update the legal
45 and administrative processes and procedures around probate for District residents and estate
46 lawyers, consistent with the recommendations of the Working Group’s 2022 report.
47 Subsequently, the Council adopted emergency legislation on July 14, 2025, to make technical
48 amendments in order to be implemented as some of the language had proven inadministrable and
49 unclear, leading to delays in appointing personal representatives and precluding access to
50 decedents’ estate assets. Probate proceedings require a publication of a notice. Currently the law
51 says that various notices must be published in a legal periodical, which can limit access to
52 residents and has also interfered with the law’s implementation.
53 (c) Emergency legislation is necessary to update the deadlines in Secure DC to accurately
54 reflect feasible timelines for the Task Force and to amend our probate laws to expand
55 permissible publications to include newspapers in order to ensure access to publications and
56 implementability of the original law.
57 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
58 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Prearrest Diversion
59 Task Force Recommendations Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted after a single
60 reading.
61 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
