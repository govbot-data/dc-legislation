Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is an
emergency bill, the “Contract No. CW117574 with Compost Crew Inc. A Benefit Corporation
Approval and Payment Authorization Emergency Act of 2024,” and the accompanying
emergency declaration resolution for Mods 01 through 05. The contract is in the estimated
amount of $4,524,736 for the period of July 19, 2024, through July 18, 2025.
The legislation will approve Contract No. CW117574 with Compost Crew Inc. A Benefit
Corporation to support organic waste collection from residential households in the District. In
addition, the legislation will approve payment for the goods and services received and to be
received under the contract.
My administration is available to discuss any questions you may have regarding the proposed
contract modification. In order to facilitate a response to any questions you may have, please have
your staff contact Marc Scott, Chief Operating Officer, Office of Contracting and Procurement, at
(202)724-8759.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The contractor shall support organic waste (food waste) collection from single-family (residential)
households in the District. The contract shall assemble, bundle, and distribute sets of curbside
compost “starter kits”, and collect and haul curbside organics to a fully permitted, licensed, and
compliant processing facility five days a week for a total of 52 weeks.

(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:
Sole Source
I certify that the notice of intent to award a sole source was published in accordance with the 27
DCMR 1304.1 with no responses; therefore, it was recommended that the Chief Procurement
Officer approve the use of the sole source procurement method. In July 2023, DPW launched a
year-long Curbside Composting pilot Program with Compost Crew as the contractor and with a
subcontractor. DPW needed services without disruption for the existing program while the District
recompeted the requirement.

the Department of Public Works’ (DPW) Office of Waste Diversion (OWD).

C3 DEFINITIONS
. Certifiedcompostable-Compostable fibersorresinsthatarecertified by BPI
(Biodegradable ProductsInstitute)and shall notcontain BPAor PFAS(polyfluoroalkyl
substances).
Compostable~ Any itemcomprised solely ofmaterials that breakdowninto, or
otherwisebecome part of, usablecompost inasafe and timely manner. The term
“compostable” when referring to fibersor resins shall be defined as aforementionedand
shall becertified by BPI (BiodegradableProducts Institute) andshall notcontain BPAor
PFAS (polyfluoroalkyl substances).
‘Composting~ a managed process whichutilizes microorganisms naturallypresent in
organic matter and soil to decomposeorganic material. Thesemicroorganisms require
basic nutrients, oxygen, and waterfordecompositiontooccurat an accelerated pace. The
end-product, compost, is a darkbrown, humus-likematerial whichcan be easily and
safelyhandled,stored, and used as avaluablesoil conditioner.The compostingprocess is
dependent upon several factors, including: thepopulationofmicroorganisms, carbonto
nitrogen ratio, oxygen level, temperature, moisture, surfacearea,pH, and time.
Compostablebin liners— Bagsusedto line compost bins madeofnatural plantmatter
and certified by BPI (Biodegradable Products Institute) that do notcontain any toxic
materials such as BPAor PFAS (polyfluoroalkyl substances)and breakdown readily ina
compostingsystem withnocontamination.
Composted —toconvert decayingorganicmaterial into a soil amendment.
Curbside compost bins—Receptacles supplied by theDistrict tobeused forthe
