14 To amend, on a temporary basis, the District of Columbia Housing Authority Act of 1999 to
15 establish a temporary Stabilization and Reform Board to govern the District of Columbia
16 Housing Authority (“DCHA”) and to require that the Board and the Executive Director of
17 DCHA take specific actions to reform and revitalize the operations of DCHA.
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
20 act may be cited as the “District of Columbia Housing Authority Stabilization and Reform
21 Temporary Amendment Act of 2025”.

130 “Sec. 11b. Reform activities.
131 “(a) Within 15 days after the end of each calendar quarter, the Executive Director shall,
132 after submission to the Stabilization and Reform Board and the City-Wide Resident Advisory
133 Board, submit a report to the Mayor and the Council that describes the progress of the Authority
134 in:
135 “(1) Addressing and remediating the issues identified by the U.S. Department of
136 Housing and Urban Development in its 2022 assessment of the Authority (“HUD DC001
137 Assessment”);
138 “(2) Developing and implementing a plan to expedite the leasing of dwelling units
139 owned, operated, or managed by the Authority;
140 “(3) Identifying individual dwelling units within Housing Properties of the
141 Authority that are in a substandard condition and improving the condition of such units to a state
142 of good repair;
143 “(4) Developing and implementing a plan for the maintenance, in an ongoing state
144 of good repair, of Housing Properties of the Authority and individual dwellings units within
145 those Housing Properties;
6

146 “(5) Improving the management of the wait list for dwelling units within Housing
147 Properties of the Authority;
148 “(6)(A) Reviewing and developing recommendations for improvements of the
149 Authority’s:
150 “(i) Capital and operating budgets;
151 “(ii) Capital and operating expenditures;
152 “(iii) Accounting and fiscal management systems, controls; and
153 procedures; and
154 “(iv) Contracting and procurement systems, controls, and
155 procedures.
156 “(B) Requirements of this paragraph are subject to review by the Chief
157 Financial Officer measured against industry and government standards and best practices; and
158 “(7) Meeting the training requirements established by sections 12(h) and 14(d).
159 “(b) In addition to the requirements of subsection (a) of this section, the Executive
160 Director shall submit to the Council any updated policies, procedures, and reports provided to the
161 United States Department of Housing and Urban Development as outlined in the Authority’s
162 “Response to the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development’s March 2022 On-Site
163 Assessment Report,” published on November 29, 2022. Such policies, procedures and reports
164 shall be submitted no later than 48 hours after submission to the United States Department of
165 Housing and Urban Development.
166 “(c) The Stabilization and Reform Board shall:
167 “(1) Review the progress of the Authority in addressing the findings and
168 recommendations in the HUD DC001 Assessment each month;
7

169 “(2) Reserved.
170 “(3) At least once a quarter, invite the City-Wide Resident Advisory Board to
171 report to the Board on any topics of interest or concerns, and respond to the concerns of the City-
172 Wide Resident Advisory Board in writing, no later than 45 days following the quarterly meeting;
173 and
174 “(4) Reserved.
175 “(d) If requested by the Council, a quorum of members of the Stabilization and Reform
176 Board shall attend a Council oversight hearing on the Authority and provide testimony.
177 “(e) A quorum of members of the Stabilization and Reform Board shall be present at each
178 listening session held pursuant to subsection (c)(2) of this section.
179 “Sec. 11c. City-Wide Resident Advisory Board.
180 “(a) The Authority shall establish and implement a comprehensive training program for
181 members of the City-Wide Resident Advisory Board with the goal of enabling tenant members to
182 participate fully in the oversight of the housing authority’s operation and capital planning. The
183 Authority shall develop the training program in consultation with public housing residents and
184 public housing industry professional organizations.
185 “(b)(1) The City-Wide Resident Advisory Board shall be provided access to trainings
186 referenced in section 12(h)(1) and (2).
187 “(2) Additionally, the Authority shall provide training on relevant federal and
188 District laws, leadership development, communication, and negotiations.
189 “(c) The Authority shall provide a copy of resolutions on the agenda for consideration by
190 the Stabilization and Reform Board to the City-Wide Resident Advisory Board at least 24 hours
8

192 resolution will be considered.

194 Board when a policy or program change affects residents.”.
