Dear Secretary Smith,
Today, I am introducing the 151 Q Street Residential Economic Development Real Property Tax
Exemption Amendment Act of 2026. This legislation would provide much-needed financial
stability to the Gale Eckington, a 603-unit apartment complex located at 151 Q Street N.E. A
copy of the legislation as introduced is attached.
The Gale is a mid-life apartment building that provides affordable and workforce housing to
residents in a transit and amenity rich neighborhood. Unfortunately, in recent years, the Gale has
experienced challenges receiving rent from tenants, which has in turn complicated efforts to
reinvest in the building, which in turn has made it hard to retain and attract tenants.
The proposed tax abatement would provide the building ownership and tenants a way out of a
potential downward spiral by providing ownership with additional resources to invest in the
property, cement affordability guarantees, and guarantee that tenants enjoy much-needed repairs
to facilities that are starting to show their age. The legislation accomplishes this by requiring the
resolution of notices of infraction issued by the Department of Building prior to ownership
receiving a tax abatement.
I look forward to working with my colleagues on the Council and in the executive to advance
this legislation. Please contact my Deputy Chief of Staff, Conor Shaw, at cshaw@dccouncil.gov
if you have any questions about this legislation.
Sincerely,
Zachary Parker
Ward 5 Councilmember
Chair, Committee on Youth Affairs

25 (b) Section § 47-4618 is amended to read as follows:
26 “(a) For the purposes of this section, the term:
27 “(1) “Owner” means 151 Q Street Preservation LLC, its successors, affiliates, and
28 assigns.
29 “(2) “151 Q Street Residential Project” means the operations, including the
30 financing, refinancing, or reimbursing of costs incurred, of the mixed-use, multi-family
31 residential and ground-floor retail project located on the 151 Q Street Residential Property,

32 consisting of:
33 “(A) Approximately 603 units of residential condominium/apartment
34 house use totaling approximately 560,000 square feet of floor area and housed in 3 buildings;
35 “(B) Approximately 1,200 square feet of ground-floor retail space;
36 “(C) Below-grade parking garages;
37 “(D) Other ancillary improvements, including extension of Q Street, N.E.,
38 from Eckington Place to Harry Thomas Way;
39 “(E) Approximately 350 units designated affordable housing;
40 “(F) Designating no less than 302 of the 350 affordable units to be leased
41 to individuals and families with annual incomes not in excess of 80% of the Area Median
42 Income, as determined from time to time by the United Stated Department of Housing and Urban
43 Development and such households will pay rents that are no more than 30% of 80% of the
44 adjusted Area Median Income; and
45 “(G) Designating no less than 48 of the 350 affordable units to be leased to
46 individuals and families with annual incomes not in excess of 60% of the Area Median Income,
47 as determined from time to time by the United Stated Department of Housing and Urban
48 Development and such households will pay rents that are no more than 30% of 60% of the
49 adjusted Area Median Income.
50 “(3) “151 Q Street Residential Property” means the real property, including any
51 improvements constructed thereon, located in Lots 816, 817, 818, 819, and 820, Square 3576 (or
52 as the land for such lots may be subdivided into a record lot or lots or assessment and taxation
53 lots, condominium lots, air rights lots, or any combination in the future).
54 “(b)(1) Beginning in Tax Year 2031, 100% of the tax imposed by Chapter 8 of this title
2

55 on the 151 Q Street Residential Property shall be abated.
56 “(2) The real property tax abatement granted by paragraph (1) of this
57 subsection shall apply for 10 consecutive real property tax years.
58 “(3) The real property tax abatements granted by paragraphs (1) and (2) of
59 this subsection shall not exceed, in the aggregate, $21 million.
60 “(4) Prior to receiving the tax abatement granted under this subsection, the
61 Owner shall remedy any notice of infraction and pay any outstanding fine issued prior to October

65 improve living conditions at the Property. Any building code infraction issued after October
