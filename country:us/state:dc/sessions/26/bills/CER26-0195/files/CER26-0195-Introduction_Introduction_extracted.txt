6 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
7
8 ____________
9
10 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
11
12 ____________
13
14 To recognize, honor, and express gratitude to Mr. Christopher A. Hart for his years of service to
15 the District of Columbia as a member and chair of the Washington Metrorail Safety
16 Commission (WMSC).
17
18
19 WHEREAS, Mr. Hart holds a law degree from Harvard University and Master's and
20 Bachelor's degrees (magna cum laude) in Aerospace Engineering from Princeton University. He
21 is a member of the District of Columbia Bar and the Lawyer-Pilots Bar Association;
22
23 WHEREAS, over the course of his career, Mr. Hart has served in a variety of positions
24 including Deputy Director, Air Traffic Safety Oversight Service Federal Aviation Administration
25 (FAA); Assistant Administrator for System Safety, FAA; Deputy Administrator for the National
26 Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA); Deputy Assistant General Counsel to the
27 Department of Transportation; managing partner of Hart & Chavers, a Washington, D.C., law firm;
28 and attorney with the Air Transport Association, as well as an initial stint as a member of the
29 National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) in 1990, having been nominated by President
30 George H.W. Bush.;
31
32 WHEREAS, Mr. Hart also is the founder of Hart Solutions LLP, which specializes in
33 improving safety in a variety of contexts, including the safety of automation in motor vehicles,
34 workplace safety, and process safety in potentially hazardous industries;
35
36 WHEREAS, on August 12, 2009, Mr. Hart was sworn in as a member of the NTSB (for a
37 second time) and designated by President Obama for a two-year term as vice chairman of the board
38 on August 18, 2009. Four years later, in August 2013, President Obama nominated Mr. Hart for a
39 second term as board member and after Senate confirmation of his nomination, designated him for
40 a third term as vice chairman. Mr. Hart also served as acting NTSB chairman beginning in April
41 2014;
42
43 WHEREAS, in July 2014, Mr. Hart was nominated by President Obama to serve as
44 chairman of the NTSB. He served as chairman until August 2017;
45

46 WHEREAS, after his tenure with the NTSB, Mr. Hart was appointed by the Council in
47 2018 to the Washington Metrorail Safety Commission, a three-jurisdictional agency (MD, VA,
48 DC) that was created to oversee the safety of the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Agency’s
49 (WMATA) subway system, better known as Metrorail.
50
51 WHEREAS, shortly after his appointment to the WMSC, Mr. Hart was elected Board
52 Chairman, a position he maintained until the end of his tenure on the Commission in 2026.
53
54 WHEREAS, in 2019 Mr. Hart was asked by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)
55 to lead the Joint Authorities Technical Review that was created to bring together the certification
56 authorities of 10 countries, as well as NASA, to review the robustness of the FAA certification of
57 the flight control systems of the Boeing 737 MAX and make recommendations as needed to
58 improve the certification process. He also joined the Board of the Joint Commission on
59 Accreditation of Healthcare Organizations, the organization responsible for accrediting hospitals,
60 to help improve healthcare safety; and
61
62 WHEREAS, Christopher A. Hart is a distinguished attorney, engineer, pilot, and
63 administrator with exceptional professional credentials who has dedicated his career to improving
64 the safety and effectiveness of all modes of transportation in the United States.
65
66 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
67 resolution may be cited as the “Christopher A. Hart Ceremonial Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
68
69 Sec. 2. The Council recognizes and thanks Christopher A. Hart for the distinguished service
70 and valuable contributions he has made to the District of Columbia and its citizens, both before
71 and while as a member and the chair of the Washington Metrorail Safety Commission. The Council
72 commends Mr. Hart for devoting his career and professional endeavors to making transportation
73 safer and more efficient for District residents and all users across the United States.
74
75 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
