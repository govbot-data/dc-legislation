9 To amend the Relief for River East at Grandview Condominium Owners Act of 2024 to provide
10 housing relief to certain owners and former owners of the River East at Grandview
11 Condominiums by establishing eligibility for Home Purchase Assistance Program grants,
12 converting existing Home Purchase Assistance Program loans to grants, and limiting the
13 affordability covenant period for former owners Inclusionary Zoning and Accessory
14 Dwelling ownership units to 15 years; and to address related tax considerations and
15 reporting requirements.
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA,
18 That this act may be cited as the “River East at Grandview Homeownership Relief and
19 Restoration Amendment Act of 2026”.
20 Sec. 2. The Relief for River East at Grandview Condominium Owners Act of 2024,
21 effective September 18, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-217; D.C. Official Code § 42–2081 et seq.) is
22 amended as follows:
23 (a) Section 2132 (D.C. Official Code § 42–2081) is amended as follows:
24 (1) A new subsection (c-1) is added to read as follows:
25 “(c-1) “Former Property Owner” means an individual who purchased a condominium
26 unit at the River East at Grandview Condominiums between July 2017 and March 2019 and who
27 no longer owns that unit.

28 (2) Paragraph (l) is amended by striking the period and inserting the phrase “and purchased
29 the condominium unit between July 2017 and March 2019.” in its place.
30 (b) A new Section 2135 is added to read as follows:
31 “Sec. 2135 HPAP loan conversion to grant and shortening the length of IZ or ADU
32 affordability covenant for Property Owners and Former Property Owners
33 “(a) DHCD shall provide relief under this section to Property Owners or Former Property
34 Owners as follows:
35 (1) DHCD shall make Property Owners or Former Property Owners who have
36 not purchased another primary residence since leaving the Property eligible for HPAP assistance,
37 pursuant to the Home Purchase Assistance Fund Act of 1978, effective September 20, 1978
38 (D.C. Law 2-135; D.C. Official Code § 42–2601 et seq.), in the form of a grant toward the
39 purchase of a primary residence in the District of Columbia. The Property Owner or Former
40 Property Owner shall be excluded from credit requirements for the HPAP program.
41 (2) DHCD shall convert outstanding HPAP loans for Property Owners or Former
42 Property Owners who previously received HPAP assistance to purchase a primary residence in
43 the District of Columbia into grants. This provision shall not apply to any subsequent purchase
44 beyond the first primary residence.
45 (A) DHCD and the Office of Tax and Revenue shall record any release,
46 modification, or other instrument necessary to reflect the conversion in the land records.

47 (3) DHCD shall award HPAP grants of up to $50,000 to be used towards the
48 payment of a primary residence mortgage principal balance, in the form of a recast or refinance
49 of the current mortgage, for Property Owners or Former Property Owners who purchased a
50 primary residence in the District of Columbia without HPAP assistance. This provision shall not
51 apply to any subsequent purchase beyond the first primary residence.
52 (4) A Property Owner or Former Property Owner who has purchased, or will
53 purchase, an IZ or ADU unit as their next primary residence under 14 DCMR § 2200 et seq. shall
54 receive priority when applying for an IZ or ADU ownership unit. The affordability covenant on
55 that unit shall terminate 15 years after the date the Property Owner or Former Property Owner
56 closed on a condominium unit at the Property. This provision applies only to the first qualifying
57 unit and does not apply to any later purchases. DHCD shall record and make publicly available
58 the termination of any covenant under this paragraph.
59 “(b) Notwithstanding any other provision of law:
60 (1) Any amount received under this section shall be excluded from District gross
61 income for purposes of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code; and
62 (2) Any amount realized by reason of the conversion or forgiveness of an HPAP
63 loan under subsection (a)(2) shall also be excluded from District gross income for purposes of
64 Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code. The Office of Tax and Revenue shall issue
65 guidance to implement this subsection.

66 “(c) By September 1, 2027, and annually thereafter through Fiscal Year 2032, DHCD
67 shall submit to the Council a report identifying:
68 (1) The number of individuals receiving relief and the total amount of relief
69 received under each paragraph of subsection (a);
70 (2) The ward in which each grant recipient purchased a property; and
