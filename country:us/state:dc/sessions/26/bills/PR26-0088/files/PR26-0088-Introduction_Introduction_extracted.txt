Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Pursuant to D.C. Official Code § 2-352.02, I have enclosed, for consideration by the Council of the District of Columbia,
the (a) Council Contract Summary for the Agreement to Enter into a Long-Term Subsidy Contract (“ALTSC”) between
the District of Columbia Housing Authority (“DCHA”) and Flats at South Capitol LLC, and (b) proposed Local Rent
Supplement Program Contract No. 2025-LRSP-02A with Flats at South Capitol LLC Approval Resolution of 2025. DCHA
proposes to provide a housing subsidy to the Owner in the initial amount of $486,960.00 annually for a multi-year
term of fifteen (15) years, in support of the District’s Local Rent Supplement Program (“LRSP”) to provide twenty-two
(22) affordable housing units at Flats at South Capitol Apartments located at 3838 South Capitol Street, SE.
As always, I am available to discuss any questions you may have regarding the proposed LTSC. In order to facilitate a
response to any questions concerning this ALTSC, please have your staff contact Hammere Gebreyes, Director of
External Affairs and Senior Advisor, at (202) 207-4694.
I look forward to your favorable consideration of this proposed ALTSC agreement.
Sincerely,
Keith Pettigrew
Executive Director, District of Columbia Housing Authority

28 Sec. 2. (a) In 2007, the District passed Title II of the Fiscal Year 2007 Budget Support
29 Act of 2006 ("BSA") to provide funding for affordable housing for extremely low-income
30 households in the District. The passage of the BSA created the Local Rent Supplement Program
31 ("LRSP"), a program designed to provide affordable housing and supportive services to
32 extremely low-income District residents, including those who are homeless or in need of
33 supportive services, such as elderly individuals or those with disabilities, through project-based,
34 tenant-based, and sponsored-based LRSP affordable housing units. The BSA provided for the
35 District of Columbia Housing Authority ("DCHA") to administer the LRSP on behalf of the
36 District.

1 (b) In June 2019, the District of Columbia’s Department of Housing and Community
2 Development (“DHCD”) issued a Request for Proposals (“RFP”) regarding the availability of
3 funds from multiple district agencies, including DCHA, DHCD, the Department of Behavioral
4 Health, the Department of Human Services, and the District of Columbia Housing Finance
5 Agency. Of the total proposals received, (25) developers were chosen to work with DCHA and
6 others to develop affordable housing and permanent supportive housing units for extremely low
7 income families making zero to thirty percent (30%) of the area’s median income, as well as the
8 chronically homeless and individuals with mental or physical disabilities throughout
9 Washington, DC.
10 After meeting the requirements for DHCD review and LRSP subsidy eligibility,
11 proposals were evaluated on various underwriting and prioritization criteria including financial
12 feasibility, project sustainability, development team capacity, amount of capital requested,
13 nonprofit participation, and furtherance of the development of affordable housing. Additionally,
14 DCHA has determined that the proposed project meets the District of Columbia’s Consolidated
15 Plan and the Mayor’s goal for providing affordable housing opportunities in neighborhoods.
16 Upon approval of the contract by the Council, DCHA will enter into an agreement to enter into a
17 long-term subsidy contract (“ALTSC”) with the selected housing providers under the LRSP for
18 housing services.
19 (c) There exists an immediate need to approve the long-term subsidy contract with Flats
20 at South Capitol LLC under the LRSP in order to provide long-term affordable housing units for
21 extremely low-income households for units located at 3838 South Capitol Street SE.
22 (d) The legislation to approve the contract will authorize an ALTSC between the District
