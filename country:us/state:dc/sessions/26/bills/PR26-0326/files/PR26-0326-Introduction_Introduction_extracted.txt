11 To declare an emergency with respect to the need to amend, on an emergency basis, the District
12 of Columbia Rental Housing Act of 1985 to clarify that any rental unit participating in
13 the Low-Income Housing Tax Credit program is exempt from the Rent Stabilization
14 Program.
15
16 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
17 resolution may be cited as the “LIHTC Rent Stabilization Exemption Clarification Emergency
18 Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
19 Sec. 2. (a) Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) units in the District of Columbia
20 have been exempt from rent stabilization since the inception of the District of Columbia Rental
21 Housing Act of 1985 based on the exemption language: “[a]ny rental unit in any federally or
22 District-owned housing accommodation or in any housing accommodation with respect to which
23 the mortgage or rent is federally or District-subsidized.” D.C. Code § 42-3502.05(a)(1).
24 (b)This exemption of LIHTC units from rent stabilization was overturned by Judge Julie
25 H.Becker’s decision in Milestone v. Daniels, Case No. 2024-LTB-7847, based on the definition
26 of direct vs indirect government subsidy.
27 (c) Ninety-nine (99) buildings with more than 11,000 units are LIHTC assisted and would
28 be subject to rent stabilization without this exemption. Without a large infusion of new capital,
29 these properties will likely default on their financial commitments due to loss of rental income.
30 (d)The LIHTC program has its own rent increase limitations, defined by the U.S.
31 Department of Housing & Urban Development (HUD) as “rents can increase annually by 5% or

32 two times the percentage change in national median income, whichever is higher.”1 Furthermore,
33 HUD capped that increase at 10% in 2024. These limitations ensure that even if LIHTC units are
34 exempt from the District’s rent stabilization laws, rents will be affordable and align with tenants’
35 incomes.
36 (e) Statute must be clarified to state that LIHTC units are exempt from further rent
37 stabilization as defined in the District of Columbia Rental Housing Act of 1985 to ensure that
38 LIHTC properties remain subject to the rent structures agreed to at financing. This clarification
39 also aligns the Rental Housing Act of 1985 with the District of Columbia Housing Finance
40 Agency Act which exempts HFA LIHTC units from rent control or stabilization.
41 (f) LIHTC provides critical preservation funding for affordable housing in the District
42 and attracts essential reinvestment capital for older housing stock in need of repairs.2 If the
43 District does not act, LIHTC preservation properties will be at risk of defaulting on their
44 financing, stymying the LIHTC investment market and slowing down affordable housing
45 production.
46 (g) Exempting LIHTC units from rent control or stabilization does not raise rents but
47 preserves the rents and rent increases agreed to by tenants at the time they signed their leases.
48 (h) Emergency legislation is necessary to ensure that LIHTC preservation properties
49 avoid foreclosure, the LIHTC market remains strong, and tenants are able to remain in their
50 homes.

52 enumerated in section 2 constitute an emergency making it necessary that the LIHTC Rent
