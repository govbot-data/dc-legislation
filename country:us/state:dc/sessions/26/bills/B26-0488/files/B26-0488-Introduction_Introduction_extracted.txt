Dear Secretary Howard,
Today, I am introducing the Apprenticeships and Credentials as Pathways to Prosperity Act of 2025. Please find
enclosed a signed copy of the legislation.
As we plan for the future and seek to diversify the District’s economy, we must focus on developing and
supporting District residents for our future workforce. This bill will create more opportunities for residents to gain
the training and education necessary to find lasting, stable, and well-paid careers. As we transform our workforce
and continue to make the District even more attractive to high-growth industries––including technology,
healthcare, education, hospitality, and construction––we will in turn transform our tax base, as residents earn
higher incomes and more businesses are attracted to the District. Many residents face financial barriers to
obtaining the job and career training they need to access these industries—not only finding a way to pay for the
cost of tuition, but also meeting their basic living expenses while in school or receiving training. Apprenticeships
in particular are a strong but currently underutilized tool in the District. Construction trades make the most use of
this training pathway, but apprenticeships can be more widely implemented in many other industries.
This bill creates local talent pathways, through apprenticeships and support to earn degrees and other credentials,
to high-growth and high-demand sectors in the District for D.C. high school graduates and other residents. The
program targets the following high-growth, high-demand industries: computer science and information
technology, early childhood education, healthcare, hospitality, and construction management. Participants will
receive zero-interest forgivable loans to cover tuition or costs of related instruction, supplemental support for
participants with dependent children, payment for on-the-job training, job placement support, and supportive
programming including job skills training, mentorship, and financial counseling.
Should you have any questions about this legislation, please contact my Committee and Legislative Director, Linn
Groft, at lgroft@dccouncil.gov.
Thank you,

211 Sec. 7. Degree pathway.
212 (a)(1) The Program shall provide a tuition loan to each student. The funds for the tuition
213 loan shall be drawn from the Fund and disbursed by the DME. The tuition loan shall be
214 forgivable upon completion of the conditions in section 10 of this Act.
215 (2) Since the Program loans shall cover the full cost of tuition and living
216 expenses, participants shall not apply for other student loans.
217 (3) The tuition loan shall not exceed the lesser of:
218 (A) The published full-time undergraduate tuition, books, and mandatory
219 fees charged by the University of the District of Columbia and
220 (B) The published tuition and mandatory fees of the participating
221 institution, whichever amount is lower.
222 (4) For students attending a participating institution other than UDC, the Program
223 may cover tuition, books and mandatory fees above UDC’s published tuition only if:
224 (A) Federal Pell Grants, DC TAG, institutional aid, and private
225 scholarships have been applied first; and
226 (B) The remaining amount to be covered does not exceed 20% above the
227 equivalent full-time tuition at UDC, unless waived by DME based on program necessity.
228
10

229 (b) The Program shall provide a living loan to each student. The funds for the living loan
230 shall be drawn from the Fund and disbursed by the DME.
231 (1)(A) The living loan shall be equal to the difference between the tuition loan
232 and the participating institution’s published cost of attendance.
233 (B) The DME shall establish annual maximum living-expense caps based
234 on UDC’s standard student budget.
235 (2) The living loan shall be increased by $5,000 per year for each dependent child
236 in the participant’s household to assist students who are parents or guardians of dependent
237 children. Eligibility for District public benefits shall not be affected by the receipt of this
238 funding.
239 (3) The living loan shall be forgivable upon completion of the conditions in
240 section 10 of this Act.
241 (c) Tuition loans and living loans shall be counted as the last dollar after all other
242 scholarships and grants, including DC TAG, and before a recipient’s private or federal student
243 loans.
244 (d) UDC and UDC-CC are approved as participating institutions and shall:
245 (1) Provide a list of existing majors, minors, or certificates qualifying as target

247 (2) Develop and maintain employer partnerships;
248 (3) Administer tuition loans and living loans according to subsections (b) and (c)
249 of this section;

251 this Act; and

258 Sec. 8. Apprenticeship pathway.
259 (a) The Program shall facilitate the creation of apprenticeships in target industries and
260 provide apprentices with industry-recognized credentials upon completion of the apprenticeship.
261 Apprenticeships shall meet federal and DC Apprenticeship Council standards.
262 (b) Participating employers in the apprenticeship pathway shall:
263 (1) Develop related instruction curriculum with UDC, WDLL, and other
264 participating institutions;
265 (2) Register apprenticeships with the Apprenticeship Council;
266 (3) Provide on-the-job training to apprentices;
267 (4) Pay apprentices at least District minimum wage for hours spent both in on-the-
268 job training and in required related instruction settings;
269 (5) Commit to hiring apprentices upon Program completion for at least two years,
270 provided that the individual remains eligible for employment and remains in good standing;
271 (6) Meet progressive wage schedules aligned to federal apprenticeship standards;
272 and
273 (7) Comply with data and reporting requirements of this act.
274 (c) The Program shall provide a related instruction loan to each apprentice. The funds for
12

275 the related instruction loan shall be drawn from the Fund and disbursed by the DME. The related
276 instruction loan shall be forgivable upon completion of the conditions in section 10 of this Act.
277 (1) The related instruction loan shall be increased by $5,000 per year for each
278 dependent child in the participant’s household to assist apprentices who are parents or guardians
279 of dependent children. Eligibility for District public benefits shall not be affected by the receipt
280 of this funding.
281 (2) Related instruction loans shall be counted as the last dollar after all other
282 scholarships and grants, including DC TAG, and before an apprentice’s private or federal student
283 loans.
284 (3) The related instruction loan shall not exceed the lesser of:
285 (A) The published per-credit tuition rate at UDC for Workforce
286 Development/UDC-CC coursework, multiplied by the number of credits required for the related
287 instruction and
288 (B) The published tuition charges of the participating related instruction
289 provider, whichever is lower.
290 (4) For technical apprenticeships requiring specialized instruction (e.g., aviation
291 maintenance, allied health, IT/cybersecurity), DME may authorize payments up to 25% above
292 UDC’s published rate, provided that:
293 (A) No comparable instruction is available at UDC, UDC-CC or the
294 participating institution; and
295 (B) The employer or intermediary demonstrates inability to absorb the
296 cost.
297 (d) Participating institutions shall provide:

346 Sec. 10. Loan forgiveness.
347 (a) For the purpose of this section, “program loans” shall mean the tuition loan, the living
348 loan, and the related instruction loan.
349 (b) Program loans shall accrue zero interest.
350 (c) All program loans issued under this act shall comply with the funding limits
351 established in Sections 7 and 8. The DME shall:
352 (1) Verify that no tuition or related-instruction loan exceeds the UDC-based
353 maximums;
354 (2) Annually update UDC tuition benchmarks and adjust loan limits accordingly;
355 and
356 (3) Publish maximum allowable loan amounts each fiscal year.
357 (d) Participants shall be eligible for forgiveness of program loans upon completion of the
358 following conditions:
359 (1) Successful completion of the academic program or apprenticeship;
360 (2) Living and working in the District as required under section 5(a)(5) of this act;
361 and
362 (3) Fulfillment of the conditions of participation as provided in section 6 of this
363 Act for the duration of participation in the Program.
364 (e) Participants shall be eligible for graduated loan forgiveness.
365 (1) 50% loan forgiveness shall be granted after one year of DC employment and
366 residency.
16

367 (2) 100% loan forgiveness shall be granted after living and working in the District
368 for the full duration required under section 5(a)(5) of this act.
369 (3) Additional hardship-based pro-rated forgiveness may be approved by DME.
370 (f) Participants who do not meet the requirements for loan forgiveness shall:
371 (1) Repay the loan over a period not to exceed 10 years;
372 (2) Receive a standardized, income-sensitive repayment plan;
373 (3) Be eligible for rehabilitation to restore forgiveness eligibility.
374 (g) The DME may waive any the requirements of subsection (c) of this section on an
375 individual basis if a participant demonstrates a personal or familial hardship to the DME such
376 that the DME determines a waiver of the requirement is necessary. If a participant demonstrates
377 a hardship that would permit the student to take a leave of absence from the participant’s
378 academic program under the rules of the participating institution, then the participant may return
379 to the Program upon the participant’s return to the academic program as funding is available.
380 (e) Additional exceptions to the conditions of this section must be approved by the DME.
