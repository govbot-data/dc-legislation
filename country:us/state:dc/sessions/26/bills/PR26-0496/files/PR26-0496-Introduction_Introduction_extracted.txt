Councilmember Trayon White, Sr.
A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
_____________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
_____________
1 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Business
2 Improvement Districts Act of 1996 to authorize the establishment and administration of
3 the Soul of the City Business Improvement District.
4
5 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
6 resolution may be cited as the “Soul of the City Business Improvement District Emergency
7 Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
8 Sec. 2. (a) The Business Improvement Districts Act of 1996, effective May 29, 1996
9 (D.C. Law 11-134; D.C. Official Code § 2-1215.52) authorizes the establishment of a Business
10 Improvement District (“BID”).
11 (b) The Congress Heights neighborhood, generally located between Mississippi Avenue,
12 Southeast and Alabama Avenue, Southeast, within and around which the Soul of the City BID is
13 proposed to be established, is a historically underinvested community that is now experiencing
14 rapid and consequential transformation as a result of significant public and private investment.
15 (c) Recent and planned investments contributing to this transformation include major
16 development at the Saint Elizabeths East and West campuses, including a 4,200-seat sports and
1

17 entertainment arena serving as the home of the Washington Mystics and a practice facility for the
18 Washington Wizards; the General Services Administration’s investment in approximately 4
19 million square feet of office space at Saint Elizabeths West; and private investment resulting in
20 the construction of approximately 1,000 new residential housing units. These investments have
21 created an urgent need for a coordinated, community-based entity to serve as a steward of
22 neighborhood change, ensure effective public space management, and act as a bridge between
23 longtime residents, local businesses, and new stakeholders.
24 (d) The commercial corridors within the proposed Soul of the City Business
25 Improvement District are home to a concentration of small, locally owned businesses, many of
26 which are owned and operated by District residents and long-time members of the surrounding
27 community, that face disproportionate vulnerability during periods of rapid neighborhood
28 change. These businesses are operating amid rising commercial rents, increased competition
29 from new development, heightened public-space management demands, and limited access to
30 coordinated marketing, technical assistance, and capital, and currently lack a formal mechanism
31 to connect to workforce pipelines, visitor-serving opportunities, and procurement pathways
32 associated with major nearby anchor institutions, including large-scale employment centers,
33 entertainment venues, and public facilities. Absent a structured and locally governed Business
34 Improvement District, many of these small businesses lack the collective capacity to adapt to
35 increased foot traffic, visitor demand, and evolving market conditions, placing them at risk of
36 displacement or closure at the precise moment when neighborhood investment is accelerating.
37 The establishment of the Soul of the City BID is necessary to stabilize existing small businesses,
38 improve commercial corridor conditions, support business retention and expansion, and ensure
2

39 that long-standing neighborhood enterprises are positioned to benefit from, rather than be
40 harmed by, ongoing public and private investment.
41 (e) The Soul of the City BID’s goals are to coordinate efforts to ensure that existing
42 residents and businesses benefit from new development and increased prosperity; improve the
43 area’s parks and public spaces, creating more inviting places for neighborhood gathering and
44 community building; diversify and strengthen neighborhood retail and services; bring more job
45 opportunities and neighborhood amenities to existing residents; improve the perception of the
46 Congress Heights commercial area; Increase visitation to area attractions and businesses; and
47 increase sales and occupancy along the neighborhood’s commercial corridors.
48 (f) The BID application has been filed with the Department of Small and Local Business
49 Development, and a certification hearing has been scheduled January 16, 2025.
50 (g) The Soul of the City Business Improvement District Amendment Act of 2025, which
51 has been reported out of the Committee on Business and Economic Development, authorizes the
52 establishment and administration of the Soul of the City Business Improvement District,
53 including the applicable tax rates for eligible properties.
54 (h) Without the timely establishment and operationalization of the Soul of the City BID,
55 residents, businesses, and visitors will be unable to realize the benefits of enhanced services,
56 public realm improvements, economic development programming, and community-driven
57 revitalization during a critical period of neighborhood transition.
58 (i) Absent emergency Council action to allow the BID to submit required materials to the
59 Office of Tax and Revenue in time for the issuance of tax bills in March 2026, the BID would be
60 designated by the Mayor as such but unable to operate, collect assessments, or provide services
3

61 until tax bills are issued, creating a prolonged gap between designation and functionality. Failure
62 to do so would delay the issuance of tax bills until September 2026, resulting in approximately
63 six months during which the BID would be unable to collect revenue or carry out its statutory
64 purpose.
65 (j) Such a delay would significantly hinder the BID’s ability to respond to ongoing
66 development activity, support neighborhood businesses, maintain public spaces, and deliver
67 timely benefits to residents and visitors in Ward 8, while also risking the loss of momentum
68 generated by recent investment and community engagement efforts.
69 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
70 enumerated in Section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the “Soul
71 of the City Business Improvement District Emergency Amendment Act of 2026” be adopted
72 after a single reading.
73 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
