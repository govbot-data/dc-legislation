12 To amend the Department of Health Functions Clarification Act of 2001 to establish a farmers
13 market support program within the Department of Health to provide technical assistance
14 to farmers markets, farm stands, and mobile markets, to recommend improvements to the
15 licensing and permitting processes and regulations, to oversee coordination between
16 agencies regulating farmers markets, and to establish and administer a farmers market
17 grant program; and to amend the Vending Regulation Act of 2009 to require the Mayor to
18 establish a centralized application system for farmers markets, farm stands, and mobile
19 markets, standardize licensing and permitting requirements, and waive the fees for
20 operating in a low food access area.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
23 act may be cited as the “Farmers Market Support Amendment Act of 2025”.
24 Sec. 2. The Department of Health Functions Clarification Act of 2001, effective October
25 3, 2001 (D.C. Law 14-28; D.C. Official Code § 7-731 et seq.), is amended by adding a new
26 subtitle C-ii to read as follows:
27 “Subtitle C-ii. Farmers Markets.
28 “Sec 4938. Definitions.
29 “For the purposes of this subtitle, the term:
1

ENGROSSED ORIGINAL
30 “(1) “Farmer” means a person or entity that raises or produces locally grown farm
31 products on land that the person or entity farms and owns, rents, or leases for direct sale to
32 consumers.
33 “(2) “Farmers market” means a market at a fixed location with 2 or more vendors
34 that includes at least one farmer, is open to the public, is operated by a District or federal
35 government agency, a for-profit or non-profit organization, or one or more farmers or producers,
36 and operates at least 6 times per calendar year, and where at least 50 percent of the vendors are
37 providing direct-to-consumer sales of locally grown farm products or locally processed food
38 products.
39 “(3) “Farm products” means fruits, vegetables, mushrooms, herbs, nuts, shell
40 eggs, honey or other bee products, flowers, nursery stock, fish, and livestock food products,
41 including meat, milk, cheese, and other dairy products.
42 “(4) “Farm stand” means a fixed location with one farmer vendor that is open to
43 the public, operated by a District or federal government agency, a for-profit or non-profit
44 organization, or a farmer, and operates at least 6 times per calendar year, and where at least 50%
45 of sales consist of direct-to-consumer sales of locally grown farm products and value-added farm
46 products.
47 “(5) “Locally grown” shall have the same meaning as in section 101(3) of the
48 Healthy Schools Act of 2010, effective July 27, 2010 (D.C. Law 18-209; D.C. Official Code §
49 38-821.01(3)).

50 “(6) “Locally processed food products” means food that has been processed,
51 including canning, cooking, dehydrating, milling, or the addition of other ingredients, in the
52 jurisdictions listed in section 101(3) of the Healthy Schools Act of 2010, effective July 27, 2010
53 (D.C. Law 18-209; D.C. Official Code § 38-821.01(3)). The term “locally processed food
54 products” includes cooked ready-to-eat foods, bread, baked goods, pickles, jams, and other
55 value-added farm products.
56 “(7) “Low food access area” means a census tract where:
57 “(A) More than 20% of households earn 185% or less of the federal
58 poverty line; and
59 “(B) At least 500 people or 33%, whichever is greater, of the population
60 live more than 0.5 miles or a 10-minute walk from a grocery store.
61 “(8) “Mobile market” means a farm stand that travels to 2 or more fixed locations
62 and operates at least 6 times per calendar year.
63 “(9) “Producer” means a person or entity that produces locally processed food
64 products.
65 “(10) “Value-added farm product” means any product processed by a producer
66 from a locally grown farm product.
67 “Sec. 4939. Farmers market support program.
68 “(a) There is established within the Department of Health (“Department”) a farmers
69 market support program (“program”).

70 “(b) The program shall:
71 “(1) Provide information and technical assistance relating to permitting, licensure,

84 markets in the District, including establishing the centralized application system as required

86 18-71; D.C. Official Code § 37-131.06(b));
87 “(4) Create and update annually a publicly available online map of farmers
88 market, farm stand, and mobile market locations, including their hours of operation;
