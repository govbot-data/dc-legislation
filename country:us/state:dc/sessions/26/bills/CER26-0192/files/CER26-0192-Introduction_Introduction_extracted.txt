16 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
17
18 __________
19
20 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
21
22 __________
23 To recognize ANC Commissioner Harold Cunningham for his extraordinary advocacy on behalf
24 of returning citizens, his unwavering commitment to restoring educational and
25 rehabilitative programming at the District of Columbia Jail, and his exemplary public
26 service to the residents of the District of Columbia.
27 WHEREAS, Commissioner Harold Cunningham was elected by his neighbors to serve as
28 an Advisory Neighborhood Commissioner, where he has represented District residents with
29 integrity, compassion, and an unwavering commitment to justice and community service;
30 WHEREAS, Commissioner Cunningham left school after the eighth grade and entered
31 the District of Columbia correctional system without the ability to read, yet through remarkable
32 determination taught himself to read while incarcerated, transforming adversity into a lifelong
33 commitment to learning and advocacy;
34 WHEREAS, Commissioner Cunningham spent thirty-four years incarcerated, including
35 twenty consecutive years in solitary confinement, during which no educational programming
1

36 was available through the Department of Corrections;
37 WHEREAS, despite those extraordinary circumstances, Commissioner Cunningham
38 refuses to allow his confinement to define him and instead is committed to ensuring that others
39 would have access to education, programming, and opportunities for personal growth while
40 incarcerated;
41 WHEREAS, Commissioner Cunningham independently studied the District’s budget and
42 appropriations process to advocate effectively for the restoration of educational and
43 rehabilitative programming at the District of Columbia Jail;
44 WHEREAS, through diligent research, persistent engagement, and informed advocacy,
45 Commissioner Cunningham played an instrumental role in restoring funding for the Lead Up!
46 Lead Out! initiative and the numerous educational, rehabilitative, and reentry programs
47 operating under its umbrella at the District of Columbia Jail;
48 WHEREAS, Commissioner Cunningham has demonstrated that lived experience, when
49 combined with perseverance, thoughtful research, and civic engagement, can shape public
50 policy and improve government for the benefit of countless District residents;
51 WHEREAS, Commissioner Cunningham continues to advocate for policies that expand
52 opportunity and improve conditions for incarcerated and returning residents of the District of
53 Columbia;
54 WHEREAS, Commissioner Cunningham’s journey to becoming a respected community
55 leader, Advisory Neighborhood Commissioner, and advocate for justice reflects the
56 transformative power of education, resilience, and second chances.
57 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
58 resolution may be cited as the “ANC Commissioner Harold Cunningham Recognition
2

59 Resolution of 2026.”
60 Sec. 2. The Council expresses its profound gratitude for Commissioner Cunningham’s
61 resilience, leadership, and dedication to ensuring that every resident has access to education,
62 opportunity, and the possibility of a second chance.
63 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
