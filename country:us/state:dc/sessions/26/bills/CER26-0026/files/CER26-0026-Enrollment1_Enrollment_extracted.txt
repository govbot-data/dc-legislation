ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
26-26
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
April 1, 2025
To recognize, honor, and celebrate the 50th anniversary of the Mu Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma
Theta Sorority, Incorporated, at The George Washington University.
WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, founded on January 13, 1913, at
Howard University, is an esteemed organization of college-educated women committed to the
constructive development of its members and to public service, with a primary focus on the
Black community;
WHEREAS, on April 20, 1975, nine dynamic women chartered the Mu Beta Chapter of
Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, at The George Washington University, making history
as the first Black Greek-lettered organization on campus;
WHEREAS, the visionary charter members—Francine Burnett, Linda Finch, Tanya
Goodman, Monica Jordan, Carolyn Marks, Helen Quander, Deborah Randolph, Ellen Wells, and
Theresa Weston—established a legacy of excellence that has flourished for 5 decades;
WHEREAS, for 50 years, the Mu Beta Chapter has exemplified Delta Sigma Theta's
commitment to sisterhood, scholarship, and service through numerous community initiatives,
including the Walk to End HIV with the Whitman-Walker Foundation, Voter Registration
Tabling with GWVotes, and participation in The George Washington University's Martin Luther
King, Jr., Day of Service;
WHEREAS, the Mu Beta Chapter has served as a beacon of leadership, academic
excellence, and civic engagement, fostering the development of women leaders who have made
significant contributions to the District of Columbia, the nation, and communities around the
world;
WHEREAS, the Mu Beta Chapter continues to embody the sorority's mission through
dedicated service to The George Washington University campus and the greater Washington
Metropolitan area, creating meaningful impact through thoughtful programming and community
outreach; and
1

WHEREAS, in April 2025, the Mu Beta Chapter will commemorate its 50th Charter Day
with a week-long celebration, bringing together current members, alumnae, and supporters to
honor this historic milestone and reaffirm their commitment to continued service.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
resolution may be cited as the "Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated - Mu Beta Chapter 50th
Anniversary Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates the 50th
anniversary of the Mu Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, at The George
Washington University for 5 decades of exemplary service, leadership, and dedication to the
advancement of the District of Columbia community.
Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
