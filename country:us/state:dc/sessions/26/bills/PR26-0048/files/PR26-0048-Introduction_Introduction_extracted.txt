18 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Secure DC
19 Omnibus Amendment Act of 2024 to remove the sunset date of certain pretrial detention
20 provisions; and to amend the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council for the District of
21 Columbia Establishment Act of 2001 to clarify certain reporting requirements.
22
23 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 resolution may be cited as the “Secure DC Pretrial Detention Extension and Reporting
25 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
26 Sec. 2. (a) The Secure DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2024 (“Secure DC”) established
27 a rebuttable presumption for pretrial detention in certain cases in our criminal justice and
28 juvenile justice systems. These measures are set to expire 225 days after the effective date of
29 Secure DC, on February 18, 2025. The Council also established a requirement that the Criminal
30 Justice Coordinating Council (“CJCC”) submit to the Mayor and the Council reports on the
31 efficacy of the criminal justice and juvenile justice pretrial detention provisions. Modifications
32 are needed to Secure DC to ensure the CJCC report covers the appropriate data for effective
33 analysis of these policies. Additional time is also needed for the CJCC to complete the report.
34 (b) Emergency legislation is necessary to amend Secure DC to remove the sunset date of
35 certain pretrial detention provisions at least until such a point as the report can be completed and
1

36 submitted to Council and to amend the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council for the District of
37 Columbia Establishment Act of 2001 to clarify certain reporting requirements.
38 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
39 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making it necessary that the Secure DC Pretrial
40 Detention Extension and Reporting Emergency Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
41 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
