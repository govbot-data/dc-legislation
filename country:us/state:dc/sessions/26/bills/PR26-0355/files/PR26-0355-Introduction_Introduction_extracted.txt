Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is a proposed
resolution titled "Richard Wright Public Charter School Revenue Bonds Project Approval
Resolution of 2025".
The resolution authorizes the issuance, sale, and delivery of tax-exempt revenue bonds, notes, or
other obligations in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed $22,000,000. The bonds will be
used to finance renovations and building improvements, as well as refinance existing debt
associated with the Richard Wright Public Charter School located at 475 School St., SW, in
Ward 6.
In accordance with section 490 of the Home Rule Act, it has been determined that the bonds,
when, as, and if issued, shall be without recourse to the District. The bonds shall not be general
obligations of the District; shall not be a pledge of or involve the full faith and credit or the
taxing power of the District; shall not constitute a debt of the District; and shall not constitute a
lending of public credit for a private undertaking as prohibited in section 602(a)(2) of the Home
Rule Act. The bonds shall not give rise to any pecuniary liability of the District, and the District
shall have no obligation with respect to the purchase of the bonds.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed measure.

Richard Wright Public Charter School
Revenue Bond
FACT SHEET
The Richard Wright Public Charter School (“Richard Wright PCS”) has requested that the
District issue up to $22,000,000 in revenue bonds to finance current renovations and new
construction and to refinance existing debt associated with their campus located at 475 School
Street, SW, in Ward 6.
The Applicant
Since its founding in 2010, Richard Wright Public Charter School for Journalism and Media Arts
has served students in grades 8–12 in the Ward 6 neighborhood of Washington, D.C. As a non-
profit public charter school, Richard Wright PCS is committed to transforming students into
articulate, media-savvy contributors through a student-centered academic environment. The
school integrates classical studies with modern languages, emphasizing strong writing and
vocabulary development across the curriculum.
Currently serving 310 students and employing 26 teachers, Richard Wright PCS offers a rigorous
and well-rounded educational experience. Beyond academics, the school fosters intellectual,
cultural, social, and physical development through a wide range of extracurricular clubs, athletic
programs, events, and experiential learning opportunities. These offerings help students apply the
concepts, skills, and processes they learn in the classroom to real-world scenarios.
The school has a 100% college acceptance rate and has secured over $100 million in
scholarships and grants for its graduates during its tenure in the District.
Proposed Project
Richard Wright PCS seeks bond financing to support critical facilities projects that will allow the
school to better serve its students and fulfill its mission. The proposed financing will be used for:
• Renovation and modernization of the existing school building located at 475 School
Street SW, in Ward 6
• Expansion of instructional space, including:
Additional classrooms
o
Larger, updated science laboratories
o
A new media newsroom
o
A fully equipped media theater
o
Enhanced collaborative workspaces for students
o
In addition to funding capital improvements, the financing will allow the school to refinance
existing debt, supporting long-term financial sustainability.
Upon completion, Richard Wright PCS will be able to increase its enrollment capacity to 500
students.

Feasibility/Structure/Security of the Bonds
Building Hope has reviewed the financial feasibility of the Richard Wright PCS in connection
with the proposed $22,000,000 revenue bond application. Based on the assessment of Richard
Wright PCS’s financial position and financing needs, Building Hope believes such financing is
feasible.
Building Hope is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization dedicated to increasing educational
opportunities for K-12 students. Building Hope offers direct lending, bridge loans, and other
financial and technical services to support school development and operations in the District,
New York, Florida, Texas, Nevada, and Idaho.
Public Purpose Benefits
The issuance of these bonds will continue to support the mission and development of the Richard
Wright PCS school campus situated in Ward 6. This will enhance career, contracting, and
educational opportunities for District residents and business.
Legal and Regulatory Affairs
The law office of Orrick, Herrington & Sutcliffe is assigned as bond counsel to the Revenue
Bond Program and has preliminarily determined that the applicant is a 501(c)(3) organization,
and the project constitutes a permissible undertaking under Section 490(a)(1) of the District of
Columbia Home Rule Act.
Based upon the information outlined in the application, the Revenue Bond Program staff has
determined that the proposed project complies with the criteria for approval of a proposed
financing transaction through the District’s Revenue Bond Program and will assist the Borrower
in furthering its organizational mission.
