12 To amend the Urban Forest Preservation Act of 2002 to amend the definition of a Special Tree, to
13 require that the Mayor increase the fines and fees associated with the removal of Special
14 Trees or Heritage Trees for inflation, and to modify the permissible uses of the Tree Fund.
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
17 act may be cited as the “Tree Preservation Enhancement Amendment Act of 2025”.
18 Sec. 2. The Urban Forest Preservation Act of 2002, effective June 12, 2003 (D.C. Law 14-
19 309; D.C. Official Code § 8-651.01 et seq.), is amended as follows:
20 (a) Section 102(5) (D.C. Official Code § 8-651.02(5)) is amended by striking the phrase
21 “between 44 inches” and inserting the phrase “between 40 inches” in its place.
22 (b) Section 104(e) (D.C. Official Code § 8-651.04(e)) is amended to read as follows:
23 “(e)(1) The Mayor shall, beginning July 1, 2029, and every 3 years thereafter, increase the
24 fee described in subsection (b)(3) of this section and the fine described in subsection (d) of this
25 section by regulation to adjust for inflation using the Consumer Price Index.
26 “(2) In addition to an increase under paragraph (1) of this subsection, the Mayor
27 may increase the fee described in subsection (b)(3) of this section or the fine described in
28 subsection (d) of this section by regulation.”.

29 (c) Section 104a(d) (D.C. Official Code § 8–651.04a(d)) is amended to read as follows:
30 “(d)(1) The Mayor shall, beginning July 1, 2029, and every 3 years thereafter, increase the
31 fine described in subsection (c) of this section by regulation to adjust for inflation using the
32 Consumer Price Index.
33 “(2) In addition to an increase under paragraph (1) of this subsection, the Mayor
34 may increase the fee described in subsection (c) of this section by regulation.”.
35 (d) Section 107 (D.C. Official Code § 8-651.07) is amended to read as follows:
36 “Sec. 107. Tree Fund.
37 “(a) There is established as a special fund the Tree Fund (“Fund”), which shall be
38 administered by the District Department of Transportation in accordance with subsection (c) of
39 this section.
40 “(b) All funds collected from the following sources shall be deposited into the Fund:
41 “(1) Donations; and
42 “(2) Fees and penalties collected pursuant to this act or any rule established
43 pursuant to this act.
44 “(c) Money in the Fund:
45 “(1) Shall be used for the following purposes:
46 “(A) Tree planting in public space or District-owned land, including parks
47 and school property; and

48 “(B) Providing income-based subsidies to assist District residents with costs
49 related to the removal and replacement of hazardous trees; and
50 “(2) May be used for the following purposes:
51 “(A) Tree planting on private land, in coordination with the Department of
52 Energy and Environment;
53 “(B) Conducting survival checks of trees planted on public or private land
54 pursuant to paragraph (1)(A) of this subsection and subparagraph (A) of this paragraph; and
55 “(C) For any other costs associated with implementing this act, including
56 administrative costs.
57 “(d) The Mayor shall ensure that trees planted pursuant to this section are checked for
58 survival at appropriate intervals to evaluate canopy replacement and inform future planting
59 decisions.
60 “(e) Private voluntary donations to the Fund shall be tax deductible for purposes of District
61 income and franchise taxes.
62 “(f)(1) The money deposited into the Fund but not expended in a fiscal year shall not revert
63 to the unassigned fund balance of the General Fund of the District of Columbia at the end of a
64 fiscal year, or at any other time.
65 “(2) Subject to authorization in an approved budget and financial plan, any funds
66 appropriated in the Fund shall be continually available without regard to fiscal year limitation.”.
