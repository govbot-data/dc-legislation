17 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
18 __________
19
20
21 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
22
23 ____________________
24
25 To honor and recognize Jayden Daniels for being named the 2025 Associated Press (AP)
26 Offensive Rookie of the Year as the starting quarterback of the Washington Commanders.
27
28 WHEREAS, Jayden Daniels, born on December 18, 2000, in San Bernardino, California;
29
30 WHEREAS, Daniels played three seasons of college football at Arizona State University,
31 where he emerged as a bright young star, throwing for 17 touchdowns and rushing for an
32 additional three during his freshman year;
33
34 WHEREAS, in 2022, Daniels transferred to Louisiana State University (LSU), where he
35 continued to excel, culminating in a historic 2023 season in which he won the Heisman Trophy,
36 becoming the third player in LSU history to receive this honor;
37
38 WHEREAS, during the 2023 season at LSU, Daniels achieved remarkable statistics,
39 including 3,812 passing yards, 1,134 rushing yards, and 50 touchdowns, leading the Football
40 Bowl Subdivision (FBS) in total yards;
41
42 WHEREAS, he was selected second overall in the 2024 National Football League Draft
43 by the Washington Commanders and was named the team's starting quarterback near the end of
44 training camp;
45

46 WHEREAS, in his rookie season with the Commanders, Daniels set an NFL rookie and
47 team record with a 91.3% completion percentage on 23 attempts in a game against the Cincinnati
48 Bengals, amassing 293 total yards and three touchdowns, earning him NFC Offensive Player of
49 the Week honors;
50
51 WHEREAS, Daniels was named NFL Offensive Rookie of the Month for September
52 2024, being the first Washington Commander to win it since Robert Griffin III in 2012;
53
54 WHEREAS, during the 2024 regular season, Jayden Daniels completed 69% of his
55 passes for 3,568 yards, threw 25 touchdowns, and rushed for 891 yards and 6 touchdowns,
56 showcasing his dual-threat capabilities;
57
58 WHEREAS, he became the third rookie in NFL history to pass for over 3,000 yards and
59 rush for over 600 yards in a season, as well as the fifth player and first rookie to have four games
60 with a completion percentage of at least 80% in a season;
61
62 WHEREAS, Daniels set the NFL record for most rushing yards in a season by a rookie
63 quarterback and led the Commanders to their first playoff win since 2005 and first NFC
64 Championship Game appearance since 1991;
65
66 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, this resolution
67 may be cited as the “Jayden Daniels Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
68
69 The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors Jayden Daniels for his
70 outstanding accomplishments in the National Football League as a member of the Washington
71 Commanders, and we commend his dedication, perseverance, and contributions to the sport.
72 .
73 This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
74 District of Columbia Register.
