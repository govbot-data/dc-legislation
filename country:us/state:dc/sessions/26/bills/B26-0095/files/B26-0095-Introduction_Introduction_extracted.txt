Council action is necessary to approve proposed Modification #2 to the Option Year One
($3,200,000; January 3, 2024 - January 2, 2025) because when combined with the previous
Council approved Modification #1 to exercise Option Year One of this contract ($2,500,000;
January 3, 2024 -January 2, 2025� CA25-573), the total retroactive value for the Option Year
One would be $5,700,000. Council action is also necessary to approve proposed Modification #3 ,
which seeks to partially exercise Option Year Two for $1,200,000 for the period, January 3, 2025
through March 31, 2025. In recent years UMC has seen costs increase for nurse staffing due to
competing national and regional personnel needs, so the contract was further modified to address
these expenses. Further, with the winding down and closure of the hospital, it has been
increasingly difficult to hire nursing personnel, and thus UMC has needed to rely more on staffing
agencies to maintain required staffing levels. Council approval remains vital to avoid disruption
to these essential services.

winding-down operations in preparation for a tentative March 31, 2025 closure.. Thus, UMC
needed to enter into Modification #2, for an additional $3,200,000.00 for Option Year 1 and for
Option Year 2 to ensure nurse staffing was available.
