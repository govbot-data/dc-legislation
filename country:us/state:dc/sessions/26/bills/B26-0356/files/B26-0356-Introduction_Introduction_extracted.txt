Statement of Introduction on the
Prenatal and Postpartum Remote Patient Monitoring Clarification Amendment Act of
2025
September 16, 2025
Today, I, along with Councilmembers Matthew Frumin, Janeese Lewis George, Brianne K.
Nadeau, Zachary Parker, Brooke Pinto, and Robert C. White Jr., am introducing the Prenatal and
Postpartum Remote Patient Monitoring Clarification Amendment Act of 2025. This bill clarifies
that the Department of Health Care Finance (DHCF) shall cover and reimburse for remote patient
monitoring to improve maternal health outcomes. Specifically, it ensures coverage for remote
monitoring of blood pressure and blood glucose levels during pregnancy and up to 12 months
postpartum.
Research shows that remote monitoring improves maternal health by enabling early detection of
complications. Studies have found it particularly effective in managing hypertension1,2 and
improving postpartum blood pressure control by as much as 43%.3 This is especially critical in the
District, where around 5% of birthing parents experience gestational diabetes and around 8.5%
experience gestational hypertension.4 The District’s Perinatal Health and Infant Mortality Report
identifies a key goal in improving maternal health outcomes: every pregnant woman receives
patient-centered, high quality prenatal care, and remote patient monitoring supports this goal.
This bill clarifies the original intent of the 2019 Postpartum Coverage Act which became law in
2020 and was funded in 2021. The Act was meant to specifically cover gestational diabetes and
hypertension, but the broad language led DHCF to project significantly higher costs than
anticipated. During the FY 2026 Budget Oversight hearing, DHCF stated that remote patient
monitoring was temporarily being provided through managed care organizations but was not
included in their FY 2026 budget. The agency is exploring bundling remote patient monitoring
as a wraparound service under the Transforming Maternal Health Model, but implementation is
not expected until 2030.

pressure and glucose monitoring, this amendment makes funding and implementation more

24 To amend the Telehealth Reimbursement Act of 2013 to cover and reimburse remote patient
25 monitoring of specific health care services and expenses for pregnant patients, including
26 blood pressure and blood glucose levels during pregnancy and up to 12 months
27 postpartum.

29 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
30 act may be cited as the “Prenatal and Postpartum Remote Patient Monitoring Clarification
31 Amendment Act of 2025”.
32 Sec. 2. Section 3a(2) of the Telehealth Reimbursement Act of 2013, effective October 20,
33 2020 (D.C. Law 23-132; DC Official Code § 31-3862.01(2)), is amended to read as follows:
34 “(2) Digital health interventions intended to remotely monitor a patient’s blood
35 pressure and blood glucose levels during a patient’s pregnancy and up to 12 months
36 postpartum.”.
