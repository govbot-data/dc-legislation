15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend An Act To
16 create a Department of Corrections in the District of Columbia and the Corrections
17 Oversight Improvement Omnibus Amendment Act of 2022 to designate the
18 Department of Corrections as the District agency to provide a free appropriate
19 public education under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act and
20 District law to certain individuals in its custody and detained in its secure
21 facilities.
22
23 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That
24 this resolution may be cited as the “Special Education for Young Adults in the Custody
25 of the Department of Corrections Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
26 Sec. 2. (a) There is an immediate need to designate the Department of Corrections
27 as the District agency responsible for providing a free appropriate public education
28 (“FAPE”) under the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (“IDEA”) and
29 District law to eligible individuals in its custody and detained in its secure facilities.
30 (b) Under the IDEA and District law, individuals aged 18 years old through the
31 end of their eligibility for FAPE under the IDEA and District law (hereinafter “eligible
32 individuals”) who are incarcerated in an adult correctional facility are entitled to receive a
33 free appropriate public education through the delivery of special education and related
1

34 services if they were previously identified or found eligible as a student with a disability
35 or had an individualized education program. DOC has eligible individuals in its custody.
36 (c) For school year 2021-2022 and schools year 2022-2023, special education
37 services for eligible individuals in DOC’s custody were provided by Maya Angelou
38 Public Charter Schools under a temporary charter authorization from the Public Charter
39 School Board. The temporary charter authorization expired at the end of school year
40 2022-2023.
41 (d) In order to ensure the continuation of appropriate special education services at
42 DOC in school year 2023-2024 and beyond, the Council passed emergency and
43 temporary legislation to designate DOC as the District agency responsible for providing a
44 FAPE to eligible individuals in DOC’s custody, with the understanding that DOC would
45 contract with Maya Angelou Public Charter Schools to provide the services.
46 (e) A permanent version of this legislation is included in the Leading Education
47 Access for Reentry and Necessary Success (“LEARNS”) Amendment Act of 2026,
48 enacted on July 2, 2026 (D.C. Act 26-368; XX DCR XXX), which still must undergo
49 congressional review.
50 (f) Emergency legislation is necessary to extend the designation of DOC as the
51 District agency responsible for providing a FAPE to eligible individuals in DOC’s
52 custody during school years 2025-2026 and 2026-2027, to ensure that DOC can continue
53 its contract with Maya Angelou Public Charter Schools for the current school year.
54 (g) It is critical to pass this legislation immediately so that there is no disruption in
55 the delivery of required special education and related services under IDEA for eligible
56 individuals in the custody of the Department of Corrections.
2

57 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
58 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
59 Special Education for Young Adults in the Custody of the Department of Corrections
60 Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted after a single reading.
61 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
