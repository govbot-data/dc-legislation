Councilmember Brooke Pinto
A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
______________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
__________________
1 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Retired Police
2 Officer Redeployment Amendment Act of 1992 to expand the Metropolitan Police
3 Department Senior Officer Program to Metropolitan Police Department Lieutenants, and
4 to amend the Policemen and Firemen's Retirement and Disability Act to remove the
5 Metropolitan Police Department and Fire and Emergency Medical Services mandatory
6 retirement age.
7
8 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
9 resolution may be cited as the “First Responder Retention Efforts Emergency Declaration
10 Resolution of 2025”.
11 Sec. 2. (a) Over the last several years, maintaining appropriate staffing levels across the
12 District’s public safety agencies has been a challenge. Both the Fire and Emergency Medical
13 Services Department (“FEMS”) and the Metropolitan Police Department (“MPD”) have
14 struggled with short staffing and significant overtime pressures in recent years. Staffing
15 shortages within fire and police departments has become a major issue across the Metropolitan
16 area. While recruitment and retention levels within MPD have improved to about 3,200 officers
17 as of August of 2025, MPD Chief Smith says an ideal staffing level would be closer to 4,000.
18 (b) This emergency legislation would expand the MPD Senior Officer Program, which
19 permits MPD to rehire retired police officers on annual contracts under specific circumstances, to
20 include lieutenants in addition to the officers who currently qualify for this program. Currently,
21 the MPD Senior Officer Program allows certain officers, detectives, and sergeants who are

22 retired to be rehired at specific pay scales at the discretion of the Chief of Police without
23 jeopardy to their retirement benefits. Those who qualify, however, do not currently include
24 lieutenants, and thus this legislation would expand the MPD Senior Officer Program to allow
25 lieutenants to also be rehired under these criteria. The Senior Officer Program is a useful tool to
26 help ensure that MPD maintains experienced employees while mitigating the retirement bubble.
27 The program assigns the Senior Police Officers to technical, administrative, or special skills
28 positions in accordance with their respective knowledge, skills, and abilities, and thus expanding
29 the program to lieutenants would open up a group of officers who have unique skills that can
30 continue to contribute to the mission of MPD. This emergency legislation would also eliminate
31 the mandatory retirement age of 60 years for both MPD police officers and Fire and EMS
32 workers.
33 (c) These changes will help Fire and EMS and MPD retain their most experienced
34 members for longer, mitigate the retirement bubble, and better anticipate future staffing needs.
35 This is crucial. Continued support and investment from the government is needed to ensure the
36 stability of the Departments and their ability to retain experienced police officers and firefighters,
37 and to allow those individuals to use their experience and expertise to train new officers and
38 firefighters.
39 (d) Therefore, emergency legislation is needed to continue recruiting and retaining key
40 members of our public safety infrastructure in the District.
41 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
42 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the First
43 Responder Retention Efforts Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single
44 reading.
45 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
