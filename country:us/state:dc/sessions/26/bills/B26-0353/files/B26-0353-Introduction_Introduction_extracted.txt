________________________
Councilmember Brooke Pinto
A BILL
________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
________________
1 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Retired Police Officer Redeployment Amendment Act of
2 1992 to expand the Metropolitan Police Department Senior Officer Program to
3 Metropolitan Police Department Lieutenants, and to amend, on an emergency basis, the
4 Policemen and Firemen's Retirement and Disability Act to remove the Metropolitan
5 Police Department and Fire and Emergency Medical Services mandatory retirement age.
6
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
8 act may be cited as the “First Responder Retention Efforts Emergency Amendment Act of
9 2025”.
10 Sec. 2. The Retired Police Officer Redeployment Amendment Act of 1992, effective
11 September 29, 1992 (D.C. Law 9-163; D.C. Official Code § 5-761(d)), is amended to read as
12 follows:
13 “(d) A retired police officer who is rehired under subsection (a) of this section as a:
14 “(1) Senior police officer shall be paid a salary of no more than that equal to the
15 salary paid a Class 1, Step 5 Officer and shall not be eligible for longevity pay; and
16 “(2) Senior police lieutenant shall be paid a salary of no more than that equal to
17 the salary paid a Class 5, Step 3 lieutenant and shall not be eligible for longevity pay.”.
1

18 Sec. 3 Section 12(h)(2) of the Policemen and Firemen's Retirement and Disability Act,
19 approved September 1, 1916 (39 Stat 718; D.C Official Code § 5-712(b)), is amended to read as
20 follows:
21 “(b) Any member of the United States Secret Service Uniformed Division or of the
22 United States Park Police force or of the United States Secret Service Division, having reached
23 the age of 60 years, to whom this subchapter apply shall, in the discretion of the head of the
24 member’s department, be retired from the service and shall be entitled to receive an annuity as
25 computed under subsection (a) of this section.”.
26 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
27 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
28 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
29 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
30 Sec. 5. Effective date.
31 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
32 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto) and shall remain in effect for no longer than
33 90 days, as provided for emergency acts of the Council of the District of Columbia in section
34 412(a) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813;
35 D.C. Official Code § 1-204.12(a)).
