Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and approval by the Council of the District of Columbia are the
“Schedule of Fines Rulemaking Approval and Authority Emergency Declaration Resolution of
2026” and the “Schedule of Fines Rulemaking Approval and Authority Emergency Act of
2026.”The bill and corresponding resolution will, on an emergency basis, approve rules that
clarify the schedule of fines related to speeding.
Council action on an emergency basis is necessary to preserve public safety and welfare because
effective enforcement of the traffic laws creates safer roadways for all road users. Reductions in
speeding have been shown to significantly reduce the risk of death or serious injury in crashes,
and this rulemaking is necessary for effective continued enforcement.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.

20 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 resolution may be cited as the "Schedule of Fines Rulemaking Approval and Authority
22 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026".
23 Sec. 2. (a) Rulemakings to amend the schedule of fines under section 105(a)(l) of the
24 District of Columbia Traffic Adjudication Act of 1978, effective September 12, 1978 (D.C. Law
25 2-104; D.C. Official Code§ 50-2301.05(a)(l)) ("Section 105(a)(l)"), require prior Council
26 approval, and the amendment of the schedule of fines pursuant to an emergency rulemaking is
27 currently restricted by Section 105(a)(l).
28 (b) The Director of the District Department of Transportation recently issued proposed
29 rules to amend the schedule of fines to clarify the intended speed at which each monetary penalty
30 for speeding applies.
31 ( c) Immediate approval of these rules by the Council, and statutory authorization to issue
32 the rules on an emergency basis, is necessary to preserve public safety and welfare because
33 effective enforcement of traffic laws create safer roadways for all road users. Reductions in

34 speeding have been shown to significantly reduce the risk of death or serious injury in crashes,
35 and adoption of the rulemaking is necessary for effective continued enforcement.
36 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
37 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
38 Schedule of Fines Rulemaking Approval and Authority Emergency Act of 2026 be adopted after
39 a single reading.
40 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
