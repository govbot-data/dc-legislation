Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is the
Juvenile Curfew Amendment Act of 2025. This legislation will strengthen and enhance existing
enforcement accountability tools for juveniles, with a focus on establishing a stronger and more
responsive curfew program. The legislation would provide the Mayor with critical flexibility in
setting and enforcing the District’s juvenile curfew, enabling the Mayor and the Metropolitan
Police Department (MPD) to implement curfew policy that will best protect the safety of juveniles
and the public.
In the past, MPD has thoughtfully used its current authority under the Code to address illegal
juvenile conduct, including enhancing enforcement of the disturbance of the peace offenses,
working with local businesses on signage related to trespassing by unaccompanied minors, and
providing support for requests for a restricted curfew in the Wharf. The tools available to MPD,
however, are limited, restricting the District’s ability to comprehensively address these ongoing
concerns.
The emergency version of this legislation was prompted by incidents in some of our neighborhoods
this spring and early summer, with groups of dozens, to more than 100, youths engaged in
disruptive behavior, such as running through stores, private lobbies, and restaurants, jumping on
tables, brawling, and damaging property. Incidents like these happened in Navy Yard, the Wharf,
and the U Street Corridor. The emergency version of this legislation produced the desired outcome:
with narrowly tailored periods of enforcement, it increased community safety without increasing
juvenile arrests. Not only does the curfew safeguard the community, it protects the youth from the
consequences of poor decisions. This legislation provides a flexible tool that can be strategically
used to safeguard both the community and youth.
This legislation will address these concerns by broadening the tools available to the Mayor and
MPD to address this issue, best protecting the safety of juvenile and the public. Specifically, the
legislation will:

• Change the statutorily established juvenile curfew hours to begin at 11 p.m. every day,
including during the summer months and weekends, and extend the juvenile curfew to
apply to 17-year-olds.
• Authorize the Chief of Police to establish extended juvenile curfew zones. This provision
mirrors the parameters of the drug free zones from the Secure DC Omnibus bill that I signed
into law in March 2024. In these extended juvenile zones, the curfew will begin at 8:00
p.m. and remain in effect for up to 4 consecutive days with a possibility of the curfew being
extended where there is an established public safety need. Under the bill, an Advisory
Neighborhood Commission, BID Corporation, or Main Street organization is empowered
to petition the Chief of Police to establish an extended juvenile curfew hours zone.
• Establish a process by which the Mayor may authorize an emergency juvenile curfew.
I urge Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser
Mayor

15 To amend the Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995 to authorize the Mayor to extent the juvenile curfew
16 hours in the District when appropriate to protect public safety of public or private
17 property, to authorize the Chief of Police to establish extended juvenile curfew hour
18 zones in specified circumstances, to start the juvenile curfew hours at 11 p.m. every day,
19 and to extend the applicability of the juvenile curfew to 17 -year olds.
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 act may be cited as the "Juvenile Curfew Amendment Act of 2025".
23 Sec. 2. The Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995, effective September 20, 1995 (D.C. Law 11-48;
24 D.C. Official Code§ 2-1541 et seq.), is amended as follows:
25 (a) Section 3 (D.C. Official Code§ 2-1542) is amended as follows:
26 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended to read as follows:
27 "( 1) "Chief of Police" means the Chief of the Metropolitan Police Department.".
28 (2) A new paragraph (IA) is added to read as follows:
29 "(IA) "Curfew hours" means:
30 "(A) From 11 :00 p.m. until 6:00 a.m. of the following day;
31 "(B) Extended curfew hours established by the Mayor pursuant to section
32 3a; and
33 "(C) Extended curfew hours in extended juvenile curfew zones established

34 by the Chief of Police pursuant to section 3b of this Act.”.
35 (3) Paragraph (5) is amended by striking the phrase “under the age of 17 years”
36 and inserting the phrase “under the age of 18 years” in its place.
37 (4) A new paragraph (6A) is added to read as follows:
38 “(6A) “Notice of violation of curfew” means a verbal warning that informs a
39 juvenile or group of juveniles that they are violating the terms of a curfew, informs the juvenile
40 or group of juveniles that they may be arrested if they continue to violate the curfew, and
41 identifies reasonable steps the juvenile or group of juveniles can take to avoid arrest.”.
42 (b) New sections 3a and 3b are added to read as follows:
43 “Sec. 3a. Temporary extensions of juvenile curfew hours.
44 “(a) The Mayor may, by issuance of an order pursuant to section 422(11) of the District
45 of Columbia Home Rule Act, effective December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 790; D.C. Official Code §
46 1-204.22(11)), extend the juvenile curfew hours in the District or any part of the District
47 (“designated extended juvenile curfew hours zone”) beyond the hours established in section
48 3(1A)(A), (B), and (C) of this Act where the Mayor determines that such an extension is
49 appropriate to protect public safety or public or private property.
50 “(b) An order issued by the Mayor pursuant to subsection (a) of this section shall:
51 “(1) State the basis for the extension of the juvenile curfew hours;
52 “(2) State the locations in the District where the extended juvenile curfew hours
53 shall apply or state that the extended juvenile curfew hours shall apply throughout the District;
54 and
55 “(3) State the days and hours during which the extended juvenile curfew hours
56 shall be in effect; provided, that the extended juvenile curfew hours:

58 any day;
59 “(B) Shall not remain in effect for more than 4 consecutive days, except as
60 provided for in subsection (e) of this section; and
61 “(C) Shall not apply to groups of 8 or fewer juveniles.
62 “(c) Except where emergency circumstances, as determined by the Mayor and described
63 in the order issued under subsection (a) of this section, warrant otherwise, extended juvenile
64 curfew hours imposed under this section shall not take effect until at least 24 hours after the
65 posting of the order pursuant to subsection (c) of this section.
66 “(d) Notice of the issuance of an order issued under this section shall be promptly posted
67 on the main website of the District government and on the website of the Metropolitan Police
68 Department.
69 “(e) The Mayor may extend the time period during which extended juvenile curfew hours
70 imposed by this section shall remain in effect by issuing subsequent orders extending the time
71 period; provided, that each such extension of the time period shall not exceed 30 days.

73 designated extended juvenile curfew hours zone during the hours the curfew is in effect, the

75 “(l) Where there:
