17 To recognize, honor, and celebrate 30 years of Al-Malik Farrakhan and Cease Fire… Don’t
18 Smoke The Brothers & Sisters, Inc. for service to the District of Columbia.
19
20 WHEREAS, Cease Fire… Don’t Smoke The Brothers & Sisters, Inc. has been in
21 existence since 1993, and became official on April 7, 1995;
22
23 WHEREAS, Al-Malik Farrakhan, known as “The Fighter,” was paralyzed by the Los
24 Angeles Sheriffs in 1986, and returned to Washington, D.C. in 1993 as a wheelchair user,
25 devoting his life to service and advocacy;
26
27 WHEREAS, Al-Malik Farrakhan has devoted countless hours to improving the quality of
28 life for the ex-prisoner population, having been appointed to numerous positions that focused on
29 the plight of ex-prisoners, and successfully campaigned to keep the death penalty out of the
30 District’s sentencing structure in at least five high-profile cases;
31
32 WHEREAS, Al-Malik Farrakhan facilitated the first truce meeting between five local
33 rivals to start the peace process, the first time in history that any mayor, through Cease Fire,
34 hosted rival gang members in the office of the Mayor, resulting in a major impact upon the lives
35 of youth at risk and gang members throughout the metropolitan area.

37 WHEREAS, along with Roach Brown, Sidney Davis, Tyrone Parker, Hanif Watkins, and
38 Yango Sawyer, Al-Malik Farrakhan facilitated the first Ex-Prisoner for Mayor Marion Barry
39 Committee, a group that focused on the rights of ex-prisoners and the concerns of prisoners in
40 penal institutions throughout the country, as well as those returning to the District of Columbia
41 following incarceration; assisted the Mayor-elect in recognizing and supporting the concerns of
42 ex-prisoners; organized a voter registration drive that engaged halfway house residents, the D.C.
43 Jail, Lorton Youth Center I–3, and Occoquan, Virginia resulting in over 4,000 new voters were
44 registered and voted for the first time in their lives; and served on the Mayor-Elect’s Transition
45 Team Fact-Finding Task Force on Corrections and Parole;
46
47 WHEREAS, Al-Malik Farrakhan hosted the first Ex-Prisoner and Persons with
48 Disabilities Awards and Appreciation Program for the Honorable Marion Barry;
49
50 WHEREAS, with Roach Brown, Sidney Davis, and Tyrone Parker, Al-Malik Farrakhan
51 assisted Occoquan Correctional Institution Warden John Henderson to reduce tension during a
52 prisoner disturbance in 1995, and Lorton Correctional Institution Warden Vince Givens during a
53 prisoner work stoppage in the summer of 1995;
54
55 WHEREAS, Al-Malik Farrakhan served as Chair for prisoners and ex-prisoners from
56 1995–2000;

60 Million Man March;
61

63 organize and register 5,000 Million Man March participants;
64
65 WHEREAS, with Roach Brown, Al-Malik Farrakhan led the Ex-Offenders March to the
66 Million Man March, which engaged 1,500 ex-prisoners in Washington, D.C.;
