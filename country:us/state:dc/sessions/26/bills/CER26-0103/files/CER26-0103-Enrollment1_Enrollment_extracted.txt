ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
26-103
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
November 4, 2025
To celebrate the annual 17th Street High Heel Race, a unique and beloved LGBTQ+ tradition in
the District of Columbia.
WHEREAS, on Halloween night in 1986, a group of 25 drag performers and enthusiasts
held an informal contest that involved running in high heels from JR’s Bar, up 17th Street, N.W.
to Annie’s Paramount Steakhouse, upstairs to the bar for a shot of schnapps, and back;
WHEREAS, the 17th Street High Heel Race has since become an annual tradition that
brings together participants showcasing the diversity, creativity, and vibrancy of the District’s
lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, and other sexual and gender minority (LGBTQ+)
communities, along with thousands of enthusiastic spectators;
WHEREAS, in 2018, the District of Columbia Office of Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual,
Transgender, and Questioning Affairs assumed responsibility for organizing the annual 17th
Street High Heel Race;
WHEREAS, in the face of nationwide attacks on transgender people and drag artists, the
District of Columbia government still proudly protects and supports gender and sexual diversity
and celebrates the power of drag as a versatile, delightful form of art and play; and
WHEREAS, on Tuesday, October 28, 2025, the beloved tradition will continue, as drag
artists assemble to show off their dazzling drag outfits, and sprint or saunter down a stretch of
historic 17th Street, N.W., which remains a hub of LGBTQ+ nightlife.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
resolution may be cited as the “17th Street High Heel Race Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia celebrates the 17th Street High Heel Race
and wishes the participants a fabulous evening.
Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
