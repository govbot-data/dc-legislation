99 Sec. 3. Findings
4

100 (a) The RFK Campus consists of approximately 180 acres of waterfront property located
101 in the District within Reservation 343F, as shown on the transfer of jurisdiction plat recorded in
102 the Surveyor’s Office on February 5, 2025, on page 13 of subdivision book 223. The District has
103 been unable to redevelop the site over the last decade due to federal restrictions on development
104 at the site, and the site has remained vacant, blighted, and fallow. Over the course of the last
105 decade, the District has worked to gain broader control of the site and remove the federal
106 development restrictions so that a new, multi-use future for the area could be realized.
107 (b) With bipartisan support from Congress, the President signed Public Law 118-274, the
108 D.C. Robert F. Kennedy Memorial Stadium Campus Revitalization Act, into law on January 6,
109 2025. Pursuant to this law, the National Park Service transferred administrative jurisdiction over
110 the RFK Campus to the District. The Council approved the transfer of jurisdiction pursuant to the
111 Transfer of Jurisdiction Over the Robert F. Kennedy Memorial Stadium Campus Emergency
112 Approval Resolution of 2025, effective February 4, 2025 (Res. 26-16; 72 DCR 1436).
113 (c) The District now has a once-in-a-generation opportunity to continue to revitalize the
114 Anacostia Waterfront, catalyze growth, and develop a vibrant, mixed-use development featuring
115 community sports, professional sports, public recreation, public park space, and cultural
116 amenities. The development will provide the District with significant economic, cultural, social,
117 and financial benefits.
118 (d) Since gaining administrative jurisdiction over the RFK Campus, the District
119 government has undertaken a comprehensive public engagement process, considering the
120 specific interests of communities near the RFK Campus; economic development opportunities;
121 the capacity of the campus to generate jobs for District residents and opportunities for District
122 businesses; park, open space, and recreational options; river access options; potential community
5

123 amenities; opportunities to create new housing units; and environmental and historical factors,
124 among other considerations, to determine the best uses for the site.
125 (e) Considering the public input received, the existing infrastructure of the campus, and
126 the possibility of a catalytic investment in the District, it was determined that the construction
127 and operation of a new first-class, state-of-the-art, sports stadium with supporting facilities,
128 including parking, and infrastructure, for use by the professional football franchise owned by
129 Pro-Football LLC, and an adjacent Sportsplex would be of considerable public benefit to the
130 District as the first stage of the redevelopment and revitalization of the entire campus.
131 (f) To ensure delivery of the Stadium, supporting facilities, and Sportsplex by 2030, a
132 limited zoning exemption is necessary for the RFK Campus.
133 (g) Planning for other residential and commercial development at the RFK Campus, in
134 addition to other uses permitted by the transfer of jurisdiction and the Declaration of Covenants,
135 will continue as the Stadium and Sportsplex projects move forward, and will inform the
136 Comprehensive Plan rewrite (DC 2050).
137 (h) The District intends for the comprehensive redevelopment of the RFK Campus to
138 create a vibrant, multi-use community and destination that will create new jobs, business
139 opportunities, housing, community amenities, and neighborhood-serving retail and achieve the 5
140 Anacostia Waterfront Framework Plan themes: (1) a clean and active river, (2) gaining access to,
141 along, and across the river, (3) a great riverfront park system, (4) a riverfront of distinct places
142 and cultural destinations, and (5) building and sustaining strong waterfront neighborhoods.
143 (i) The District and Pro-Football LLC have entered into a term sheet for the development
144 of the Stadium and supporting facilities and the redevelopment of a portion of the RFK Campus
145 that sets forth the terms and conditions upon which the parties are willing to undertake the
146 projects.
6

147 (j) The construction, development, and revitalization of the RFK Campus and the public
148 ownership of the Stadium, for use for athletic and entertainment events, is a municipal use that is
149 in the interest of, and for the benefit of, District residents because such a publicly-owned
150 Stadium will contribute to the social and economic well-being of District residents and
151 significantly enhance economic development and employment opportunities in the District.
152 (k) To further that interest, it is appropriate for the District to take certain actions with
153 respect to the redevelopment of the RFK Campus, as further set forth in this act.
