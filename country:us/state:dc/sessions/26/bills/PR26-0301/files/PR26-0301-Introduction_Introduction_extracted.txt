Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and approval by the Council of the District of Columbia is the
“Compensation and Working Conditions Agreement between the District of Columbia
Government and the Fraternal Order of Police – Metropolitan Police Department Labor Committee
Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025” and the “Compensation and Working Conditions
Agreement between the District of Columbia Government and the Fraternal Order of Police –
Metropolitan Police Department Labor Committee Emergency Approval Resolution of 2025.”
The collective bargaining agreement that will be approved by these resolutions provides the
following wage increases to bargaining unit members of the Fraternal Order of
Police/Metropolitan Police Department Labor Committee:
• 4.5% for Fiscal Year 2024 (effective the first full pay period on or after October 1, 2023);
• 4.25% for Fiscal Year 2025 (effective the first full pay period on or after October 1, 2024);
and
• 4.25% for Fiscal Year 2026 (effective the first full pay period on or after October 1, 2025).
I appreciate your attention to this important agreement that will benefit our police officers. Please
contact me or E. Lindsey Maxwell II, Director, Office of Labor Relations and Collective
Bargaining, at (202) 724-4953, should you have questions concerning this transmittal.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

17 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to approve the negotiated
18 compensation and working conditions collective bargaining agreement submitted by the
19 Mayor for employees of the District of Columbia Metropolitan Police Department who
20 are represented by the Fraternal Order of Police - Metropolitan Police Department Labor
21 Committee.
22
23 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 resolution may be cited as the "Compensation and Working Conditions Agreement between the
25 District of Columbia Government and the Fraternal Order of Police - Metropolitan Police
26 Department Labor Committee Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025".
27 Sec. 2. (a) There exists an immediate need to approve the negotiated compensation and
28 working conditions agreement submitted by the Mayor for employees of the Metropolitan Police
29 Department ("MPD") who are represented by the Fraternal Order of Police - Metropolitan Police
30 Department Labor Committee ("FOP").
31 (b) The District of Columbia negotiated a compensation and working conditions
32 agreement ("negotiated agreement") for certain MPD employees represented by the FOP that
33 includes compensation increases over a period of 3 years.
1

34 (c) The negotiation of this agreement significantly increases compensation for MPD
35 sworn officers as well as addresses working conditions concerns of both MPD and FOP. MPD
36 values a positive work environment and the best resources and opportunities for professional
37 growth for all of its employees. This negotiated agreement will enhance MPD’s strong system of
38 support by providing employees represented by FOP with additional professional compensation
39 and premier working conditions.
40 (d) In order to implement the compensation increases, the Department of Human Resources
41 and the Office of the Chief Financial Officer must carry out a number of lengthy administrative
42 and technological actions.
43 (e) It is important this negotiated agreement be approved as soon as possible so that the
44 Department of Human Resources and the Office of the Chief Financial Officer can promptly
45 begin to carry out and complete these actions and employees represented by the FOP can benefit
46 in a timely manner.
47 Sec.3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
48 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
49 Compensation and Working Conditions Agreement between the District of Columbia
50 Metropolitan Police Department and the Fraternal Order of Police – Metropolitan Police
51 Department Labor Committee Emergency Approval Resolution of 2025 be adopted on an
52 emergency basis.
53 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
