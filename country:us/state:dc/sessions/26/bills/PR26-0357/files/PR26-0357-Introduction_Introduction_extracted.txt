16 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Day Care Policy
17 Act of 1979 to update the minimum salaries child development facilities must pay assistant
18 and lead teachers beginning in January 2026 to participate in the Early Childhood Educator
19 Pay Equity Program, to incorporate the Early Childhood Educator Equitable Compensation
20 Task Force and the Early Childhood Educator Pay Equity Fund into the act, and to make
21 conforming amendments.
22
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 resolution may be cited as the “Early Childhood Educator Pay Scales Emergency Declaration
25 Resolution of 2025”.
26 Sec. 2. (a) Early childhood educators and child development facilities are essential to
27 providing children with foundational mental, emotional, and social skills prior to formal
28 schooling, while supplying a stable and reliable form of childcare for working families and a
29 more robust economy. Despite their importance to families’ and children’s wellbeing, early
30 childhood educators have historically been underpaid, affecting their ability to stay in the
31 profession and provide families with stable and quality childcare. To meet the needs of educators
32 and families alike, the Council adopted the Early Childhood Educator Pay Equity Fund

33 Establishment Act of 2021, effective November 21, 2021 (D.C. Law 24-45; D.C. Official Code §
34 1-325.431).
35 (b) The Early Childhood Educator Pay Equity Fund (“pay equity fund”) was established
36 in the Fiscal Year 2022 budget to provide supplemental payments to child development facilities
37 to implement an Early Childhood Educator salary scale through the Early Childhood Educator
38 Pay Equity Program (“program”). The Fiscal Year 2025 budget necessitated reductions across
39 the District Government. Of the $70,000,000 allocated to the pay equity fund in Fiscal Year
40 2025, and annually over the next 4 years, 5% of the fund is allocated to administrative costs and
41 $12,000,000 is allocated to the DC Health Benefit Exchange Authority.
42 (c) In September 2024, the Early Childhood Educator Equitable Compensation Task
43 Force (“task force”) released a new lead and assistant teacher compensation scale based on the
44 FY25 appropriation. These financial parameters required changes to the program formula and
45 salary scale to keep early childhood educator salaries comparable to D.C. Public Schools and
46 local education agencies in the District. The Council codified the recommended salary scales on
47 October 15, 2024, through emergency and temporary legislation, and they were later made
48 permanent through the Fiscal Year 2026 Budget Support Amendment Act of 2025.
49 (d) Over the course of FY25, it became apparent that the policy measures in the
50 legislation and the compensation scale recommended by the task force were inadequate to
51 alleviate financial pressures, and expenditures for the program totaled $77,342,319. Council
52 appropriated additional funding to the program for Fiscal Year 2026, but it was insufficient to
53 cover the projected discrepancy between program costs and appropriated funds of $9,000,000.
54 Emergency legislation is therefore needed to reduce program costs to offset the gap in funding
55 for the program.
56 (e) Therefore, there exists an immediate need to amend the Day Care Policy Act to
57 update the current compensation scales to ensure the District has the new, financially feasible

58 minimum educator salaries in place by December 31, 2025. This amendment would provide
59 child development facilities in the District, with an updated salary structure that keeps the
60 program viable and within the limits established by the FY26 budget.
61 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
62 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Early
63 Childhood Educator Pay Scales Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single
64 reading.
65 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
