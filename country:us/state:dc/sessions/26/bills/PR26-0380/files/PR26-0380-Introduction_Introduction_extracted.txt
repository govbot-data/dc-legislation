12 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA

16 To declare an emergency with respect to the need to certain provisions of Chapter 18 of Title 47
17 of the District of Columbia Official Code so that they no longer conform to
18 corresponding provisions of the Internal Revenue Code and to make other technical and
19 conforming changes.
20
21 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 resolution may be cited as the “D.C. Income and Franchise Tax Conformity and Revision
23 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
24 Sec. (a) On July 4, 2025, President Trump signed H.R.1, the “One Big Beautiful Bill
25 Act” (OBBBA) into law (Pub. L. 119-21). The law contains approximately 84 tax-related
26 provisions. Since the District is a rolling conformity jurisdiction (i.e., the District’s tax code is
27 coupled with the federal tax code unless specified in law), these tax provisions will automatically
28 go into effect unless the Council takes action.
29 (b) It is necessary to decouple from these (and other) tax provisions in the OBBBA on an
30 emergency basis for three reasons:
31 (1) According to estimates from the Office of the Chief Financial Officer, tax
32 provisions in the OBBBA will reduce District revenues by $94.4 million in Fiscal Year 2025,
33 and $657.8 million from Fiscal Year 2025 through Fiscal Year 2029. With a protracted federal

34 government shutdown, a projected revenue decline of nearly 2% from Fiscal Year 2025 to Fiscal
35 Year 2026, and slow revenue growth projected thereafter, the District must be very thoughtful
36 about how our revenues are expended;
37 (2) The Council have not had adequate time to determine what, if any, tax
38 provisions in the OBBBA should be adopted by the District. Moving this legislation on an
39 emergency basis will give the Council more time to thoughtfully analyze and consider the
40 various tax provisions of the OBBBA; and
41 (3) Several of these tax provisions—such as the no tax on overtime and no tax on
42 tips—are retroactive to tax year 2025 or earlier. The Office of Tax and Revenue needs sufficient
43 time to develop forms, guidance, etc., to effectuate any decoupling of these provisions.
44 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
45 enumerated in Section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
46 “D.C. Income and Franchise Tax Conformity and Revision Emergency Amendment Act of
47 2025” be adopted after a single reading.
48 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
