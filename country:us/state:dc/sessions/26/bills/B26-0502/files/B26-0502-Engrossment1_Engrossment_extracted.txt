10 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Block Party Act of 2012 to enable residents hosting a
11 musical performance on their property as part of a porchfest to apply for block party
12 permits.
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
15 act may be cited as the “Porchfest Permitting Temporary Amendment Act of 2025”.
16 Sec. 2. The Block Party Act of 2012, effective October 23, 2012 (D.C. Law 19-190, D.C.
17 Official Code § 9-631 et seq.), is amended as follows:
18 (a) Section 2 (D.C. Official Code § 9-631) is amended by adding a new paragraph (5) to
19 read as follows:
20 “(5) “Porchfest” means a distributed neighborhood music festival where
21 musicians perform on a resident’s private property, such as their porch or yard, or any abutting
22 public property, and exclusively on a resident’s private property, such as their porch or yard, and
23 no fee is charged to participants or attendees.”.
24 (b) Section 3 (D.C. Official Code § 9-632) is amended by adding a new subsection (b-1)
25 to read as follows:
1

ENGROSSED ORIGINAL
26 “(b-1) A District resident may submit a block party application requesting that a street on
27 which they reside be closed for the purpose of holding a porchfest.”.
28 (c) Section 5(a) (D.C. Official Code § 9-634(a)) is amended to read as follows:
29 “(a) It is the policy of the District to approve block parties, including porchfests.”.
30 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
31 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
32 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
33 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
34 Sec. 4. Effective date.
35 (a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by
36 the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of Congressional review
37 as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
38 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)), and publication in the District of
39 Columbia Register.
40 (b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.
