Statement of Introduction
Charlie McCormick Way Designation Act of 2025
December 8, 2025
Today I am introducing legislation to ceremonially designate the 2500 block of Champlain Street
NW, in Ward 1, as “Charlie McCormick Way”.
This block designation is in honor of Charlie McCormick, a D.C. native for more than 30 years,
who supported the Adams Morgan neighborhood and advocated for bicycling throughout the D.C. area.
He was a bike messenger in D.C. until his leg was broken in a crash. In 1987, McCormick took over an
old, abandoned gasoline station on the corner of Euclid and Champlain Street in Adams Morgan and turned
it into the thriving bike store City Bikes, and ran it for over 35 years until he died in 2023. City Bikes won
the National Retail Advocate Store of the Year award from the League of American Bicyclists in 2003 for
its demonstrated efforts in promoting safe bicycling infrastructure and advocacy for cyclists.
Charlie McCormick was always a champion for the Adams Morgan neighborhood. He cofounded
the DMV region's annual Bike To Work Day and was a board member of the Washington Area Bicycle
Association, headquartered only a block away from City Bikes. Peter Harnick, founder of the Rails to
Trails Movement, talked about Charlie’s support of Rock Creek Park and efforts to close roads in the park
to vehicle traffic. He has always made an effort to improve the lives and conditions for himself and his
neighbors.
Attached is a resolution in support of this street designation, passed by ANC 1C on July 9, 2025.
This resolution fulfills the requirement of D.C. Official Code § 9-204.21(j), making this designation
worthy of Council consideration.
