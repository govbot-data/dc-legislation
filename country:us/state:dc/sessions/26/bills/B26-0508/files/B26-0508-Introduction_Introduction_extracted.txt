(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The Government of the District of Columbia, Department of Human Services, is seeking to exercise
the remaining option in year three for Community of Hope, Inc. to continue to provide permanent
supportive housing III (PSH3) program case management services, utility assistance, and financial
assistance to chronically homeless and other highly vulnerable individuals and families
experiencing homelessness.
The purpose of this program is to assist residents with histories of chronic homelessness in
transitioning from the street or temporary living situation into stable and permanent housing,
maintaining long-term housing, and achieving self-sufficiency. The success of the aforementioned
initiative will be achieved by referring and connecting participants to supportive services that will
address their barriers to obtaining and maintaining permanent housing and achieving an optimal
level of self-sufficiency.
(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:
The solicitation was issued in the Washington Times on April 8, 2022. An electronic RFQ
DOC575024 was issued in the open market on April 11, 2022, with a closing date of May 17, 2022.
The District received a total of 28 submissions. Of the 28 responses, 23 were deemed qualified and
the vendors were notified on June 23, 2022.
The responses were evaluated in accordance with the qualification criteria outlined in the
solicitation. The qualification evaluation factors were PSH3 design, case management,
organizational capacity, and past performance. Community of Hope, Inc. met the qualifications
indicated in the RFQ and has a satisfactory rating, providing similar services through current and
prior HCAs with the District.
The contracting officer awarded an HCA after determining that Community of Hope, Inc. was
qualified and responsible, and the proposed price was determined to be fair and reasonable. A
definitive HCA was awarded to the Community of Hope, Inc.
