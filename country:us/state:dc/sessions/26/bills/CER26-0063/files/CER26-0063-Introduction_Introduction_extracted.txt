9 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
10 __________
11
12
13 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
14
15 ____________________
16
17
18 To recognize and honor the Founding Class of Antioch School of Law on the occasion of
19 their 50th Reunion on September 13, 2025, and to commend their enduring contributions to
20 social justice, public service, and legal education.
21
22 WHEREAS, Antioch School of Law was founded in 1972 by Jean Camper Cahn and
23 Edgar S. Cahn, trailblazing visionaries who designed the blueprint for the Legal Services
24 Program and committed themselves to educating lawyers dedicated to social justice;
25
26 WHEREAS, the founding class of Antioch School of Law graduated in 1975, and
27 embodied the school’s mission to use law as a catalyst for progressive change, challenging
28 traditional legal education and expanding access to justice;
29
30 WHEREAS, members of the founding class came from diverse personal and professional
31 backgrounds, engaging in civil rights and anti-poverty efforts, teaching, serving in the military,
32 and pursuing public interest work, before arriving at Antioch;
33
34 WHEREAS, after graduation, these alumni served as legal aid attorneys, public
35 defenders, government officials, judges, legislators, general counsels, professors, advocates, and
36 writers across the District of Columbia and 27 states, including work in Native American affairs,
37 workers’ rights, juvenile justice, and civil rights;
38
39 WHEREAS, their accomplishments include founding the Juvenile Rights Project,
40 authoring landmark consumer protection laws, securing justice for Guantanamo detainees,

41 winning Fulbright fellowships, training Olympic athletes, and leading legal, civic, and academic
42 institutions nationwide;
43
44 WHEREAS, the founding class's influence reaches beyond the courtroom into
45 communities and institutions where they led efforts to rebuild neighborhoods, expand affordable
46 housing, protect vulnerable populations, and inspire new generations of legal advocates;
47
48 WHEREAS, their personal journeys reflect lives of profound civic engagement, from
49 participating in the March on Washington and the Selma to Montgomery marches, to founding
50 youth development programs, clinics, and charitable initiatives from South Carolina to Tanzania;
51
52 WHEREAS, the Antioch School of Law’s legacy lives on through the University of the
53 District of Columbia David A. Clarke School of Law, which continues its mission of advancing
54 legal education grounded in justice and service;
55
56 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
57 resolution may be cited as the “Antioch School of Law Founding Class Recognition Resolution
58 of 2025”.
59
60 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the Founding
61 Class of Antioch School of Law on the 50th anniversary of their graduation, commends their
62 historic contributions to public interest law, and extends its gratitude for their unwavering
63 commitment to justice, equity, and community empowerment.
64 .
65
66 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
