22 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Department of
23 Transportation Establishment Act of 2002 to reduce the fees for participating in the
24 Streatery Program, to establish a start date for enforcement of the Streatery Guidelines or
25 applicable rules, to declare the District’s policy of promoting streateries, and to authorize
26 the Public Space Committee to grant waivers of certain program requirements, if such
27 waiver would not create an undue risk.
28
29 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
30 resolution may be cited as the “Streatery Program Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
31 Sec. 2. (a) The COVID-19 pandemic was an incredible challenge for restaurants in the
32 District. While social distancing helped slow the spread of the virus, it discouraged residents and
33 visitors from patronizing restaurants and bars.
34 (b) To better support those businesses, in June 2020, the District Department of
35 Transportation (“DDOT”) began piloting a street eatery, or “streatery,” program. The program
36 allowed businesses to set up outdoor dining in public spaces such as parking spaces, vehicle travel
37 lanes, and alleys.

38 (c) The program proved to be a massive success and helped businesses maintain operations
39 through the public health emergency while offering diners a unique outdoor dining experience.
40 Given the popularity of the pilot program, DDOT began to develop a permanent version of the
41 program.
42 (d) On December 5, 2024, the Public Space Committee adopted DDOT’s permanent
43 Streatery Guidelines. The Streatery Guidelines provide more specific rules for streateries,
44 including which businesses qualify for a streatery installation, where streateries can located, and
45 how streateries must be designed.
46 (e) On May 2, 2025, a Proposed Rulemaking issued by DDOT and the Department of
47 Buildings (“DOB”) was published in the D.C. Register (72 DCR 18), beginning a 30-day period
48 during which members of the public could comment on the proposed rules. The Proposed
49 Rulemaking would establish a $260 streatery permit fee and $20 per square foot annual public
50 space rental fee. The public comment period expired in early June 2025, and now the Council is
51 awaiting submission of the final rulemaking from the Executive for approval.
52 (f) Initially, the Streatery Guidelines adopted by the Public Space Committee are scheduled
53 to take effect on November 30, 2025, when all temporary streatery permits expire. After that date,
54 DDOT would be able to take enforcement actions against noncompliant steateries.
55 (g) A significant number of restaurants have expressed concerns about their ability to
56 comply with the new guidelines, which could result in their exclusion from the program.
57 Specifically, some businesses are worried that they will be unable to maintain an existing, or
58 construct a new, streatery due to the new restrictions on the installation of streateries near alleys
59 or trees. Many businesses are unaware of their option to seek an exemption from the Public Space
60 Committee (“PSC”) and, instead, have suggested that they will remove their temporary

61 installations to avoid a potential violation. Another common complaint is that fees for streateries
62 are too expensive.
63 (h) In response to the concerns from the business community, Advisory Neighborhood
64 Commissions, and the general public, the Committee on Transportation and the Environment held
65 a public oversight roundtable on “The District’s Streatery Program” on November 6, 2025.
66 (i) In her testimony before the Committee, DDOT’s director explained that “Businesses
67 that submitted a streatery permit application . . . can continue operating their existing streatery
68 until a determination is made about their permit application, either via the permitting system or the
69 PSC.” She also stated that “In early 2026, DDOT will begin enforcement actions against any non-
70 permitted streatery structure.”
71 (j) While the pause in enforcement was a helpful accommodation for businesses, the
72 restrictions on streatery placement and the permitting costs may still discourage participation in
73 the program, which has been a commercial success for businesses and a popular attraction for
74 diners. Additionally, many businesses are still confused about when DDOT will begin enforcing
75 the permanent Streatery Guidelines.
76 Sec. 3. The Council finds the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency
77 circumstances making it necessary that the “Streatery Program Emergency Amendment Act of
78 2025” be adopted after a single reading.
79 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
