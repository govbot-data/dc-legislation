Councilmember Brooke Pinto
A RESOLUTION
_______
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
_______
1 To declare the existence of an emergency, due to congressional review, with respect to the need
2 to amend the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council for the District of Columbia
3 Establishment Act of 2001 to clarify certain reporting requirements.
4
5 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
6 resolution may be cited as the “Pretrial Detention Reporting Congressional Review Emergency
7 Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
8
9 Sec. 2. (a) On January 6, 2026, the Council adopted the Pretrial Detention Reporting
10 Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, effective January 27, 2026 (D.C. Act 26-254; 73 DCR
11 1078) (“emergency act”), which expires on May 28, 2026.
12 (b) On February 3, 2026, the Council adopted the Pretrial Detention Reporting
13 Temporary Amendment Act of 2026, enacted on March 2, 2026 (D.C. Act 26-274, 73 DCR
14 3134) (“temporary act”), which has not yet completed congressional review.
15 (c) This congressional review emergency legislation is necessary to prevent a gap in the
16 law between the expiration of the emergency act and the effective date of the temporary act.
17 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
18 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Pretrial
1

19 Detention Reporting Congressional Review Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted
20 after a single reading.
21 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
