12 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Language Access Act of 2004 to include the DC Water and
13 Sewer Authority as a covered entity with major public contact; to amend the District of
14 Columbia Public Works Act of 1954 to require that the DC Water and Sewer Authority
15 submit information regarding disconnections to the Council, the Office of the Attorney
16 General, and the Office of the Tenant Advocate on a monthly basis; to amend the Water
17 and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of Public Works Reorganization Act
18 of to clarify the imposition of charges and penalties for late payment of water service
19 charges and bills; and to amend section 431 of Title 21 of the District of Columbia
20 Municipal Regulations to modify the notice requirements to building occupants prior to a
21 water service disconnection.
22
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 act may be cited as the “DC Water and Sewer Authority Billing and Disconnection Clarification
25 Temporary Amendment Act of 2025”.

34 Sec. 3. Section 216(d) of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of

41 Sec. 4. Section 103 of the District of Columbia Public Works Act of 1954, approved May
42 18, 1954 (68 Stat. 102; D.C. Official Code § 34-2407.01), is amended to read as follows:
43 “Sec. 103. Discontinuance of water service for failure to pay water charges.
44 “(a)(1) The District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority (“Authority”), established
45 pursuant to section 202(a) of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of
46 Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996, effective April 18, 1996 (D.C. Law 11-111; D.C.
47 Official Code § 34-2202.02(a)), may provide for the collection of water charges, in advance or
48 otherwise, from the owner or occupant of any building, property, or other location furnished water
49 or water service by the District.
50 “(2) The Authority may shut off the water supply to any such building,
51 property, or other location upon failure of the owner or occupant thereof to pay such water charges

52 within 30 days from the date of rendition of the bill therefor, regardless of any change in ownership
53 or occupancy of such building, property, or other location.
54 “(b)(1) If the Authority has shut off the water supply to any property for failure to pay
55 District water and sanitary sewer service charges, it shall be unlawful for any person to restore the
56 water supply without the express authorization of the Authority.

59 property, unless the Authority determines that the owner or occupant did not restore or solicit a
60 person to restore the water.

65 “(2) Of those properties that were issued a notice, how many experienced a

69 “(4) How many properties have entered into a payment plan;
