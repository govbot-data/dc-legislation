15 To declare the existence of an emergency, due to congressional review, with respect to the need to
16 amend the Personal Delivery Device Act of 2018 to increase the weight limit for personal
17 delivery devices.
18
19 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
20 resolution may be cited as the “Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Congressional Review
21 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
22 Sec. 2. (a) The Personal Delivery Act of 2018, effective July 17, 2018 (D.C. Law 22-137;
23 D.C. Code § 50-1101 et seq.) created a permitting process for eligible entities to operate personal
24 delivery devices (“PDDs”) that do not exceed 90 pounds in weight.
25 (b) Since the adoption of the Personal Delivery Act of 2018, several companies have
26 developed new PDD models that exceed the existing weight limit. The weight limit adopted seven
27 years ago now presents a barrier to the introduction and use of newer PPD models.
28 (c) Many states already allow for the operating of PDDs exceeding 90 pounds in weight.
29 At least 12 states allow PDDs to exceed 500 pounds in weight, and another seven states that allow
30 for PDD operations have not established a weight limit in statute.
31 (d) On May 6, 2025, Councilmembers Charles Allen, Zachary Parker, and Christina
32 Henderson introduced Bill 26-0245, the “Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Amendment Act
1

33 of 2025 (“permanent bill”). The permanent bill would allow the operation of PDDs weighing up
34 to 275 pounds. The Committee held a hearing on the bill on October 16, 2025. The Committee
35 intends to hold an Additional Meeting to consider and vote on the measure prior to recess.
36 (e) To allow for the deployment of PDDs that exceed the current weight limit, the Council
37 passed the Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, effective
38 May 30, 2026 (D.C. Act 26-338; 73 DCR 8205) (“emergency act”). The Council also passed the
39 Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Temporary Amendment Act of 2026 (“temporary act”),
40 which has been transmitted to the Mayor for her signature. The emergency act is set to expire on
41 August 28, 2026. There is no projected law date for the temporary measure.
42 (f) This congressional review emergency legislation is needed to prevent a gap in the law
43 between the expiration of the emergency act and the temporary act.
44 Sec. 3. The Council finds the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency
45 circumstances making it necessary that the “Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Congressional
46 Review Emergency Amendment Act of 2026” be adopted after a single reading.
47 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
