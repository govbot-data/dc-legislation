Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and approval by the Council of the District of Columbia is the
“Transfer of Jurisdiction over a Portion of Lot 1008 in Square 1299, being a Portion of
Reservation 357, S.O. 25-01882, Approval Resolution of 2026.”
The proposed resolution will approve the transfer of jurisdiction over a portion of Lot 1008 in
Square 1299, being a portion of Reservation 357, from the United States Department of the
Interior, National Park Service, to the District, in order to facilitate the private redevelopment of
a vacant office building into a primarily multifamily residential building. The approval resolution
will transfer jurisdiction over a small portion of federally owned land located in the public right
of way to allow for ingress and egress from the building.
My administration is available to discuss any questions you may have regarding this resolution.
To facilitate a response to any questions you may have, please have your staff contact Sharon
Kershbaum, Director, District Department of Transportation, at (202) 671-2317.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed resolution.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

REFERENCE: Draft Approval Resolution as provided to the Office of Revenue

Conclusion
Funds are sufficient in the fiscal year 2026 through fiscal year 2029 budget and financial plan to
implement the proposed resolution.
Background
A private developer is redeveloping an office building located along Whitehaven Road, N.W. into a
multifamily residential building with some ground-floor educational uses. The approval resolution
approves the transfer of jurisdiction over a small, 709 square foot sliver of land located in the public
right-of-way from the National Park Service to the District.1 The land will be greenspace and provide
the necessary ingress and egress from the development.
Financial Plan Impact
Funds are sufficient in the fiscal year 2026 through fiscal year 2029 budget and financial plan to
implement the proposed resolution. The transfer of jurisdiction will add this land to the District’s
public space inventory. Like most public space located between the sidewalk and a private building,
