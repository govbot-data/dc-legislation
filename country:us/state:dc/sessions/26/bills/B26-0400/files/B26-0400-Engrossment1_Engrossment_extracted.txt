11 To amend section 2301 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia Official Code to consolidate, and
12 standardize the statutory definition of neglect, to clarify that neither a parent, guardian, or
13 custodian’s eligibility for or use of public benefits, nor homelessness or lack of stable
14 housing, alone shall not be sufficient to establish that the parent, guardian, or custodian’s
15 failure to provide adequate care is due to the lack of financial means.
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
18 act may be cited as the “Statutory Neglect Amendment Act of 2026”.
19 Sec. 2. Section 2301 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia Official Code is amended as
20 follows:
21 (a) Paragraph (9)(A) is amended to read as follows:
22 “(A) The term “neglected child” means a child:
23 “(i) who has been abused by their parent, guardian, or custodian, or
24 whose parent, guardian, or custodian has failed to take actions that are reasonable under the
25 circumstances to protect the child from abuse;
26 “(ii) who is suffering or is at substantial risk of suffering serious
27 physical, mental, or emotional harm, due to the parent, guardian, or custodian’s failure to provide

28 adequate care, including minimally necessary supervision, subsistence, and education as required
29 by law based on a consideration of a totality of the circumstances, and the failure is not solely
30 due to the lack of financial means of his or her parent, guardian, or custodian; provided, that
31 eligibility for or receipt of public benefits alone shall not be sufficient to establish that the failure
32 is not due solely to the lack of financial means;
33 “(iii) who is suffering or is at substantial risk of suffering serious
34 physical, mental, or emotional harm, because the parent, guardian, or custodian is unable to
35 discharge their responsibilities to the child due to mental incapacity;
36 “(iv) whose parent, guardian, or custodian refuses, is unavailable,
37 or states that they are unable to continue providing for or assume the child’s care, control, or
38 subsistence;
39 “(v) who is in imminent danger of being abused and another child
40 living in the same household or under the care of the same parent, guardian, or custodian has
41 been abused;
42 “(vi) [Repealed];
43 “(vii) who has been abandoned;
44 “(viii) who is born dependent on a controlled substance or has a
45 significant presence of a controlled substance in their system at birth;
46 “(ix) in whose body there is a controlled substance as a direct and
47 foreseeable consequence of the acts or omissions of the child’s parent, guardian, or custodian; or

50 “(xi) [Repealed].
51 (b) A new subparagraph (9)(D) is added to read as follows:
52 “(D) Homelessness or lack of stable housing alone shall not be sufficient to establish that
53 a child is a neglected child.”
54 (c) Paragraph (23) is amended as follows:
55 (1) Subparagraph (A) is amended to read as follows:
56 “(A) The term “abused” when used with reference to a child, includes:
57 “(i) infliction of physical injury;
58 “(ii) infliction of mental injury;
59 “(iii) sexual abuse or exploitation;
60 “(iv) mistreatment of the child by withholding adequate food,
61 clothing, shelter, or medical care;
62 “(v) interfering with a child's breathing; or
63 “(vi) threatening a child with a firearm, a knife, or any prohibited
64 weapon described in section 14 of An Act To control the possession, sale, transfer, and use of
65 pistols and other dangerous weapons in the District of Columbia, to provide penalties, to
66 prescribe rules of evidence, and for other purposes, approved July 8, 1932 (47 Stat. 654; D.C.
67 Official Code § 22-4514), or using such a weapon on a child.”.

69 “(B) The term “abused” does not include physical
70 discipline administered by a parent, guardian, or custodian to their child; provided, that the child
71 is at least 3 years of age, and the physical discipline is reasonable in manner and moderate in

73 (c) Paragraph (24) is repealed.
74 (d) A new paragraph (52) is added to read as follows:
75 “(52)(A) The term “abandoned” means a child:
76 “(I) whose parent, guardian, or legal custodian has not made
77 reasonable efforts to maintain a parental relationship with the child for a period of at least 4
78 months; or
79 “(II) who has resided in a hospital located in the District of
80 Columbia for at least 10 days following the birth of the child and the parent of the child did not
81 undertake any action or make any effort to maintain a parental relationship or contact with the
82 child or hospital.
83 “(B) The term “abandoned” does not include a child who is surrendered in
84 accordance with the Newborn Safe Haven Amendment Act of 2010, effective May 27, 2010
85 (D.C. Law 18-158; D.C. Official Code § 4-1451.01 et seq.).”.
