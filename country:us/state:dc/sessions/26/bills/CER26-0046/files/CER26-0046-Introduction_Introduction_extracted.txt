28 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
29
30 ______________
31
32 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
33
34 __________________
35
36 To declare the month of June, 2025 as “Alzheimer’s and Brain Awareness Month” in the District
37 of Columbia and to recognize the importance of raising awareness of Alzheimer’s disease
38 and related dementias as a critical public health issue impacting millions of Americans.
39
40 WHEREAS, June is designated as Alzheimer’s and Brain Awareness Month by the U.S.
41 Department of Health and Human Services Office of Disease Prevention and Health Promotion;

42 WHEREAS, DC Mortality Data 2010-2020 found that Alzheimer’s disease and related
43 dementias ranked as the 6th leading cause of death in the District, with approximately 13% of
44 residents 65 and older living with the disease;
45 WHEREAS, the Alzheimer’s Association estimates that over 15,000 District residents
46 live with Alzheimer's disease, supported by 15,000 family caregivers;
47 WHEREAS, over half of District residents have at least one modifiable risk factor for
48 cognitive decline, with higher rates among Black residents;
49 WHEREAS, Medicaid costs for Alzheimer's care in DC are estimated to total $146
50 million in 2025;
51 WHEREAS, advancements in Alzheimer’s research have led to evidence-based testing to
52 aid in diagnosis of Alzheimer’s disease;
53 WHEREAS, there are now multiple treatments approved by the U.S. Food and Drug
54 Administration to slow the progression of Alzheimer’s disease, but they require a diagnosis in
55 the mild cognitive impairment or early dementia stages;
56 WHEREAS, access to new diagnostic tools and treatments is critical to ensure DC
57 residents can benefit from these advancements;
58 WHEREAS, the District of Columbia Plan on Alzheimer’s Disease and Related
59 Dementias 2024-2028 identified as its top two goals: (1) Increase the proportion of older adults
60 with subjective cognitive decline who have discussed their symptoms with a provider; and (2)
61 Increase the proportion of District residents diagnosed with Alzheimer’s Disease and Related
62 Dementias receiving social and other support services; and
63 WHEREAS, the District has invested in the development of BrainHealth.DC.gov, a
64 comprehensive resource of educational materials, services, and supports for residents
65 experiencing Alzheimer’s Disease and Related Dementia and their caregivers.

66 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
67 resolution may be cited as the “Alzheimer’s and Brain Awareness Month Ceremonial Resolution
68 of 2025.”
69 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes the importance of addressing
70 Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias as a public health issue and pledges to support efforts
71 to prevent and treat Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias within the District and declares
72 June, 2025 as “Alzheimer’s and Brain Awareness Month” in the District of Columbia.
73 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
74 the District of Columbia Register.
