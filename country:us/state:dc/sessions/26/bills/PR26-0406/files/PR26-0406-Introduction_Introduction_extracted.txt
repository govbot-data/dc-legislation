Lauren McHale
Ms. Lauren McHale has served as President and CEO of The L’Enfant
Trust since 2017, following her prior roles as Executive Director and
Director of Preservation. An experienced and visionary leader in
the preservation field, she spearheaded the Trust’s Historic Properties
Redevelopment Program in 2012, which revitalized historic buildings and
enriched Washington, DC’s architectural landscape.
She currently serves as President of the Latrobe Chapter of the Society of
Architectural Historians and is a past chair of the Preservation Action
Foundation. She also sits on the board of the Architecture, Construction,
and Engineering DC Mentor Program and contributes her expertise as
a citizen member of the Planning, Zoning, and Economic Development
Committee of DC’s Advisory Neighborhood Commission 6C.
A Ward 6 resident, Ms. McHale earned her Bachelor of Arts in Art History and Historic
Preservation and Community Planning from the College of Charleston and a Master's in Historic
Preservation from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago.
