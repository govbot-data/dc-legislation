15 To amend, on an emergency basis, due to congressional review, Title 6-A of the District of
16 Columbia Municipal Regulations to make conforming changes; to amend the Omnibus
17 Police Reform Amendment Act of 2000 to allow applicants for sworn officers of the
18 Metropolitan Police Department to count certain credit hours for completion of the initial
19 training program towards the requirement that certain applicants complete 60-hours of
20 post-secondary education at an accredited college or university; to amend section 23-581
21 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify the authority of Metropolitan Police
22 Department warrantless arrest requirements; to amend the Act to Regulate Public
23 Conduct on Public Passenger Vehicles to provide that the adjudication of fare evasion
24 citations shall be pursuant to the Department of Consumer and Regulatory Affairs Civil
25 Infractions Act of 1985; to amend Chapter 8 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia
26 Official Code to clarify that offenses eligible in the future for automatic sealing are
27 currently eligible for sealing by motion; and to amend the Secure DC Omnibus
28 Amendment Act of 2024 to amend the sunset date of certain provisions regarding pretrial
29 detention.
30
31 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
32 act may be cited as the “Peace DC Omnibus Congressional Review Emergency Amendment Act
33 of 2025”.
