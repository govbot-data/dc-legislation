Appointments, should the Council require additional information.

Chris Morrison
, FAIA, NCARB, LEED® AP BD+C
Managing Principal, Principal
After serving as the Managing Director for the Perkins&Will Washington, DC
studio for a decade, Chris Morrison returns to his role as Managing Principal
to continue fulfilling his passion for practicing architecture. He has more than
30 years of experience in architecture, historic preservation, resilience/living
design, adaptive reuse, and planning. His projects have received numerous
design awards, including, The Catalyst Award and The Award of Excellence
from AIA Washington Chapter, the D.C. Mayor’s Award for Excellence
in Historic Preservation, and a Merit Award from the General Services
Administration’s Design Excellence Program. He serves on the boards of the
National Building Museum, the Young Concert Artists, Community Forklift,
and the Watergate West Residences. He continues to be deeply motivated by
design excellence, Living Design, the development of young architects, and
advocacy for LGBTQ+ rights.

Chris Morrison
Mr. Chris Morrison currently serves as the Managing Principal at
Perkins&Will. Throughout his career, he has worked on work on the
preservation, adaptive re-use, and repositioning of many historic buildings
at Shalom Baranes Associates and as a Partner at Cunningham|Quill.
Mr. Morrison has more than 30 years of experience in architecture,
historic preservation, resilience/living design, adaptive reuse, and
planning. His projects have received numerous design awards, including,
The Catalyst Award and The Award of Excellence from AIA Washington
Chapter: the D.C. Mayor’s Award for Excellence in Historic Preservation,
and a Merit Award from the General Services Administration’s Design
Excellence Program.
A Ward 2 resident, Mr. Morrison earned his Bachelor of Arts in Physics and Chemistry from St.
Louis University and his Master of Architecture from the University of Maryland.
