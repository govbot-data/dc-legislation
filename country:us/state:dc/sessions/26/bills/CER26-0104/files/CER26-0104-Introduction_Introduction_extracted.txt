12 To honor and celebrate the life of Ms. Alberta Landra Paul, a fifth-generation Washingtonian,
13 dedicated public servant, educator, and community advocate, whose over four decades of service
14 to the District of Columbia left an indelible mark on the city and the residents she loved.
15 WHEREAS, Ms. Paul was educated in the District of Columbia public schools,
16 graduating from McKinley Technology High School, and went on to earn a Batchelor of Science
17 degree in Education from Howard University and Master’s degree in Special Education from
18 Antioch College;
19 WHEREAS, Ms. Paul began her professional career at Control Data Corporation on N
20 Street NE, where she managed and pioneered computer manufacturing operations and led
21 workforce training programs that helped District residents gain critical technology and trade
22 skills;
23 WHEREAS, Her leadership helped establish one of the largest regional utility training
24 centers on Eckington Place, in partnership with Pepco, Verizon, Comcast, and Washington Gas,
25 providing literacy-to-employment pipelines that resulted in more than 130 full-time job
26 placements for D.C. residents;
27 WHEREAS, For nearly two decades, Ms. Paul served as a Communications Specialist in
28 the Infrastructure Program Management Administration (IPMA) at the District Department of
29 Transportation (DDOT), where she drafted technical responses, developed safety protocols, and
30 enhanced public communication across major infrastructure projects;

31 WHEREAS, Ms. Paul played a vital role in securing and implementing federal funding
32 for several September 11 Memorial Parks across the District, including one in Ward 7’s Penn
33 Branch community, where she remained an advocate for its upkeep and dignity for more than 14
34 years;
35 WHEREAS, Known as a mentor, innovator, and compassionate leader, Ms. Paul guided
36 countless engineers, colleagues, and residents through the complexities of District infrastructure
37 projects ensuring community engagement, safety, and transparency in government service;
38 WHEREAS, Ms. Paul was a devoted mother, grandmother, sister, and friend who
39 cherished her family deeply, particularly her son, James Roland Story, and grandson, Jamael
40 Story, and was known for her hospitality, passion for gardening, love of reading, and dedication
41 to her faith and community;
42 WHEREAS, Her 40 years of public service exemplified the highest ideals of commitment
43 to the people of the District of Columbia, especially her beloved Ward 7, where her professional
44 and personal legacies continue to inspire others; and
45 WHEREAS, The Council of the District of Columbia wishes to honor and remember Ms.
46 Alberta Landra Paul for her extraordinary service, lifelong dedication to her community, and
47 unwavering examples of grace, excellence, and public devotion:
48 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
49 resolution may be cited as the “Alberta Landra Paul Recognition Resolution of 2025.”
50 RESOLVED FURTHER, that this resolution shall be transmitted to the family of Alberta
51 Landra Paul as an expression of the Council’s profound respect and appreciation.
52 Sec.2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes, honors and celebrates the life
53 of Ms. Alberta Landra Paul, a fifth-generation Washingtonian, dedicated public servant,
54 educator, and community advocate, whose over four decades of service to the District of
55 Columbia left an indelible mark on the city and the residents she loved.
56 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
