Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is a bill entitled
“Transfer and Recordation Tax Appeals Amendment Act of 2025”.
This bill will amend the District’s deed transfer and recordation tax laws to allow transferors and
transferees to appeal the fair market value used for calculation of the transfer and recordation taxes.
Adoption of the bill will give transferors and transferees an avenue to contest transfer and recordation
taxes that are higher than warranted based on the transaction and encourages turnover of underutilized
property.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed bill.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

15 To amend the District of Columbia Real Estate Deed Recordation Tax Act to authorize
16 transferors or transferees of real property to petition for review of the fair market value
17 used in calculating taxes imposed on the transfer of the property and to amend Title 4 7 of
18 the District of Columbia Official Code to allow transferors or transferees of real property
19 to appeal final determinations of a real property's fair market value to the Real Property
20 Tax Appeals Commission for the District of Columbia or the Superior Court as
21 appropriate under the law.

23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, This
24 subtitle may be cited as the "Transfer and Recordation Tax Appeals Amendment Act of 2025".
25 Sec. 2. Section 304 of the District of Columbia Real Estate Deed Recordation Tax Act,
26 approved March 2, 1962 (76 Stat. 12; D.C. Official Code§ D.C. Code§ 42-1104), is amended
27 by adding a new subsection (d) to read as follows:
28 "(d )( 1) The transferor or transferee of the real property or economic interest in real
29 property may, pursuant to D.C. Official Code§ 47-825.0la(d), file a petition for review of the
30 determination of fair market value made under subsection ( c) of this section or under section
31 303(a). The notice of final determination issued by the Office of Tax and Revenue on the petition
32 for review may be appealed to the Real Property Tax Appeals Commission for the District of
33 Columbia as provided in D.C. Official Code§ 47-825.0la(e).

34 “(2) A transferor or transferee aggrieved by a fair market value determination
35 under subsection (c) of this section or under section 303(a) may appeal the determination to the
36 Superior Court as provided in D.C. Official Code § 47-825.01a(g).
37 “(3) For the purposes of this subsection, the terms “transferor” and “transferee”
38 shall have the same meaning as provided in D.C. Official Code § 47-901(10) and (11),
39 respectively.”.
