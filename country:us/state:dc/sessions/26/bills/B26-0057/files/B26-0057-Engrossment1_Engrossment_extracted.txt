11 To amend the Compulsory/No-Fault Motor Vehicle Insurance Act of 1982 to increase the
12 mandatory minimum motor vehicle insurance requirements for property damage liability
13 and third-party liability coverage, to require insurers to provide enhanced underinsured
14 motor vehicle coverage under a passenger vehicle liability insurance policy, and to
15 increase the mandatory minimum motor vehicle insurance requirements for property
16 damage liability third-party liability, underinsured motor vehicle, and enhanced
17 underinsured motor vehicle coverage by a set increment beginning on January 1, 2037
18 and on January 1 every 10 years after January 1, 2037.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COUMBIA, that this act
21 may be cited as the “Motor Vehicle Insurance Modernization Amendment Act of 2026”.
22 Sec. 2. Section 7 of the Compulsory/No-Fault Motor Vehicle Insurance Act of 1982,
23 effective September 18, 1982 (D.C. Law 4-155; D.C. Official Code § 31-2406), is amended as
24 follows:
25 (a) Subsection (a)(1)(D) is amended by striking the phrase “and (iii) uninsured motorist
26 protection.” and inserting the phrase “(iii) enhanced underinsured motorist protection or
27 underinsured motorist protection; and (iv) uninsured motorist protection.” in its place.

49 (e) A new subsection (f-1) is added to read as follows:
50 “(f-1) Enhanced underinsured motorist protection. -- Enhanced underinsured motorist
51 protection is for the protection of an insured who is legally entitled to recover damages from the
52 owner or operator of an underinsured motor vehicle.
53 “(1)(A) The limit of liability for an insurer that provides enhanced underinsured
54 motor vehicle coverage is the amount of that coverage without any reduction for the amount paid
55 to the insured, that exhausts any applicable liability insurance policies, bonds, and securities on
56 behalf of any person that may be held liable for the bodily injuries or death of the insured; and
57 “(B) A policy providing excess coverage may include enhanced
58 underinsured motor vehicle coverage, in an amount equal to but not greater than the excess
59 coverage in the policy. A policy that, as its primary purpose, provides coverage in excess of
60 other valid and collectible insurance or qualified self-insurance may include the enhanced
61 underinsured motor vehicle coverage.
62 “(2) Unless waived in accordance with this subsection, each insurer selling motor
63 vehicle insurance in the District with respect to any motor vehicle registered or principally
64 garaged in the District shall include coverage for enhanced underinsured motor vehicle
65 insurance.

66 “(A) The amount of enhanced underinsured motorist coverage provided
67 under a private passenger motor vehicle liability insurance policy shall not exceed the amount of
68 uninsured motorist coverage provided under the policy.
69 “(B) Such policy shall contain coverage for damages, subject to the policy
70 limits, that:
71 “(i) The insured is entitled to recover from the owner or operator of
72 an underinsured motor vehicle because of bodily injuries sustained in a motor vehicle accident
73 arising out of the ownership, maintenance, or use of the underinsured motor vehicle; and
74 “(ii) A survivor of the insured is entitled to recover from the owner
75 or operator of an underinsured motor vehicle because the insured died as the result of a motor
76 vehicle accident arising out of the ownership, maintenance, or use of the underinsured motor
77 vehicle.
78 “(C) An insurer may exclude the following from the enhanced
79 underinsured motor vehicle coverage benefits:
80 “(i) The named insured or a family member of the named insured
81 who resides in the named insured’s household for an injury that occurs when the named insured
82 or family member is occupying or is struck as a pedestrian by an underinsured motor vehicle that
83 is owned by the named insured or an immediate family member of the named insured who
84 resides in the named insured’s household; and
