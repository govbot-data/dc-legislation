5 A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
6 _____________________
7
8 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
9 _____________________
10
11 To amend, on an emergency basis, Section 8a of the District of Columbia Election Code of 1955
12 to provide for the implementation of ranked choice voting beginning with the 2027
13 election cycle.
14
15 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
16 resolution may be cited as “Ranked Choice Voting Implementation Clarification Emergency
17 Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
18 Sec. 2. (a) The purpose of this emergency is to provide the Board of Elections with
19 additional time to properly and successfully implement ranked choice voting in the District of
20 Columbia. This emergency speaks to the preparedness of the District to implement, for the first
21 time, the ranked choice voting model in the June 2026 election.
22 (b) This emergency is necessary to mitigate legal risks for the District. A
23 comprehensive plan is imperative in implementing any new system. This minimizes harm and
24 ensures that vulnerable District communities are protected and are not disenfranchised by a new
25 voting system.
1

26 (c) There is not enough funding available or resources specifically dedicated to
27 educating populations about the complexities of this new voting process. Voters must understand
28 the ranking process, how to mark ballots correctly, and how their votes will be tabulated.
29 (d) It is particularly concerning that the Board of Elections, during a November 24, 2025
30 roundtable, could not definitely commit to conducting sufficient outreach to District residents
31 before the June 2026 primary. On multiple occasions, Executive Director Monica Evans and
32 General Counsel Terri Stroud compared their implementation of ranked choice voting in June
33 2026 as working “a miracle” and stated that it would not be the first time the Board has “been
34 asked to the impossible.”
35 (e) It is clear that six months is not enough time to prepare to implement and execute any
36 new large-scale process that will have an impact on thousands of District residents. Thorough
37 preparation ensures equitable treatment across all affected individuals. Inadequate implementation
38 can subject District residents to inconsistent outcomes, unequal access, and potentially
39 discriminatory effects.
40 (e) Moreover, the lack of a concrete outreach plan from the Board has created an
41 extremely dangerous situation leading to the rise of purported ranked choice voting “training
42 sessions” for residents conducted by unvetted third parties. This means that potentially biased
43 groups are setting up events to train the public on how to fill our ranked choice voting ballots
44 instead of the official entity responsible for conducting the elections. If implementation is flawed,
45 structural issues and inequities will likely be imbedded in the District’s voting process, creating
46 negative precedents impacting District residents. The prioritization of speed over preparedness
47 undermines trust and fairness in the elections process.
2

48 (f) Ultimately, the District will benefit from having sufficient time and resources to
49 educate the voters and implement ranked choice voting. Proper preparation and execution are
50 necessary to safeguard voters, uphold fairness, maintain legitimacy, mitigate legal risks, and
51 prevent harmful precedents that can have lasting impact.
52 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
53 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making in necessary that the “Ranked Choice
54 Voting Implementation Clarification Declaration Resolution of 2025” be adopted on an
55 emergency basis.
56 Sec. 4. The resolution shall take effect immediately.
