ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
26-177
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
June 30, 2026
To recognize honoree Kimberley Bush during the 2026 celebration of DC Black Pride.
WHEREAS, Kimberley Bush is the Executive Director of The DC LGBTQ+ Community
Center, where she leads strategic initiatives that advance the health, wellness, creativity, and
empowerment of LGBTQIA+ siblings and communities across Washington, DC;
WHEREAS, with more than 25 years of nonprofit leadership experience, Kimberley
Bush has built sustainable programs, cultivated meaningful partnerships, and championed work
rooted in advocacy, service, creativity, and community care;
WHEREAS, since 2014, Kimberley Bush has led the DC LGBTQ+ Community Center’s
arts and cultural programming, creating platforms that amplify LGBTQIA2S+ voices through
film, literature, theater, visual art, and community-centered cultural experiences;
WHEREAS, in 2017, Kimberley Bush founded the DC LGBTQ+ Community Center’s
CenterArts Gallery and has curated award-winning festivals, exhibitions, and programs that
create space for storytelling, healing, joy, and belonging;
WHEREAS, during her tenure as Interim Executive Director from 2019 to 2022, while
also leading arts and cultural programming, Kimberley Bush helped guide the organization
through the COVID-19 crisis by moving vital supportive services online and deepening
community outreach;
WHEREAS, Kimberley Bush was appointed Executive Director in February 2022 and
has since led a tiny-yet-mighty team, in partnership with the community, in the advocacy,
fundraising, and visioning that helped bring the new DC LGBTQ+ Community Center to life;
WHEREAS, the new DC LGBTQ+ Community Center opened in April 2025, a 7,000-
square-foot home that has allowed the DC Center to meaningfully expand its supportive life and

human services while creating shared space for LGBTQIA+ sibling organizations under one
vibrant roof;
WHEREAS, Kimberley Bush leads with light, love, service, empathy, gratitude, joy, and
intention, and remains deeply committed to building affirming, community-rooted, and resilient
systems of support for our LGBTQIA+ siblings and communities;
WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is an annual event to increase awareness of and showcase
pride in the diversity of the LGBTQIA+ populations in the African American community, and to
support organizations that focus on health disparities, education, youth, and families;
WHEREAS, as the very first Black Pride festival in the world, DC Black Pride fostered
the beginning of the Center for Black Equity and the “Black Pride Movement”;
WHEREAS, since the birth of DC Black Pride, more than 50 other Black Pride
celebrations now take place throughout the world and on 4 continents, with many using DC
Black Pride as a model;
WHEREAS, May 22, 2026, through May 25, 2026, marks the 35th annual DC Black
Pride celebration;
WHEREAS, the theme for this year’s celebration is “New Black Renaissance,” which
centers Black LGBTQIA+ “artists, leaders, healers, and organizers while confronting what still
makes Black Pride necessary, including HIV, inequity, homophobia, and transphobia;”
WHEREAS, for more than 30 years, DC Black Pride has been an opportunity for the
Black LGBTQIA+ community to gather and celebrate being both African American and
members of LGBTQIA+ communities;
WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is the oldest and one of the largest Black Pride events in the
world, drawing thousands of residents and visitors from around the globe; and
WHEREAS, DC Black Pride brings more than 500,000 people to the nation’s capital
from across the United States, Canada, the Caribbean, South Africa, Great Britain, France,
Germany, and the Netherlands and will carry that tradition this year and into the future to
celebrate their shared community, raise awareness and funding for HIV/AIDS prevention, and
stand against homophobia inspired by violence and bigotry.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That
this resolution be cited as the “Kimberley Bush Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
