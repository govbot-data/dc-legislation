22 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Department of Transportation Establishment Act of 2002
23 to reduce the fees for participating in the Streatery Program, to establish a start date for
24 enforcement of the Streatery Guidelines or applicable rules, to declare the District’s policy
25 of promoting streateries, to authorize the Public Space Committee to grant waivers of
26 certain program requirements, if such waiver would not create an undue risk, and to require
27 the Public Space Committee to give great weight to recommendations issued by the Old
28 Georgetown Board.
29
30 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
31 act may be cited as the “Streatery Program Emergency Amendment Act of 2025”.
32 Sec. 2. The Department of Transportation Establishment Act of 2002, effective May 21,
33 2002 (D.C. Law 14-137; D.C. Official Code § 50-921.01 et seq.), is amended by adding a new
34 section 9v to read as follows:
35 “Sec. 9v. Streatery program.
36 “(a)(1) The annual public space rental fee for a streatery permit shall be assessed at no more
37 than $15 per square foot.
1

38 “(2) District agencies shall begin enforcement activities relating to noncompliance
39 with the Streatery Guidelines or applicable rules no sooner than January 15, 2026.
40 “(b)(1) It is the policy of the District to promote streateries, as streateries are a beneficial
41 use of public space that create a more vibrant dining scene and support local businesses.
42 “(2) In furtherance of the policy described in paragraph (1) of this subsection, the
43 Public Space Committee may waive the following program requirements; provided, that the waiver
44 would not create an undue risk to pedestrians or other roadway users:
45 “(A) The minimum required distance between streateries and curb cuts,
46 driveways, alleys, and trees;
47 “(B) The prohibition on streatery enclosures or vertical support structures
48 that exceed 42 inches in height; and
49 “(C) The maximum seating capacity of a streatery.
50 “(3) Recommendations by the Old Georgetown Board on the design of streateries
51 in Georgetown shall be given great weight by the Public Space Committee.”.
52 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
53 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
54 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
55 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
56 Sec. 4. Effective date.
57 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
58 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto) and shall remain in effect for no longer than 90
59 days, as provided for emergency acts of the Council of the District of Columbia in section 412(a)
2

60 of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 788; D.C.
