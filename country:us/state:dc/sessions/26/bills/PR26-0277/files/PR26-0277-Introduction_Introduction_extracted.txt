15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Personal Delivery
16 Device Act of 2018 to increase the weight limit for personal delivery devices.
17
18 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
19 resolution may be cited as the “Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Emergency Declaration
20 Resolution of 2025”.
21 Sec. 2. (a) The Personal Delivery Device Act of 2018, effective July 17, 2018 (D.C. Law
22 22-137; D.C. Code § 50-1101 et seq.) (“PDD Act”), created a permitting process for eligible
23 entities to operate personal delivery devices (“PDDs”) that did not exceed 90 pounds in weight.
24 (b) Since the adoption of the PDD Act, several companies have developed new PDD
25 models that exceed the existing weight limit. The weight limit originally adopted 7 years ago now
26 presents a barrier to the introduction and use of newer PPD models, some of which possess
27 enhanced hardware features.
28 (c) Many states already allow for the operating of PDDs exceeding 90 pounds in weight.
29 At least 12 states allow PDDs to exceed 500 pounds in weight, and another 7 states that have
30 approved PDDs have not established a weight limit in statute.

31 (d) On May 6, 2025, Councilmembers Charles Allen, Zachary Parker, and Christina
32 Henderson introduced the Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Amendment Act of 2025, as
33 introduced on May 6, 2025 (Bill 25-245) (“permanent bill”). The permanent bill would allow the
34 operation of PDDs weighing up to 275 pounds. The Committee expects to hold a hearing on that
35 bill in fall 2025.
36 (e) As of April 2025, no permits authorizing the use of PDDs within the District were in
37 effect.
38 (f) Without an immediate change to the PDD weight limit, the District Department of
39 Transportation is precluded from issuing permits to eligible entities that seek to operate PDDs that
40 exceed the 90-pound weight limit. Consequently, District residents are deprived of an innovative
41 and convenient solution for last-mile deliveries, which takes cars off the roads, reduces carbon
42 emissions, and supports local businesses.
43 Sec. 3. The Council finds the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency
44 circumstances making it necessary that the Personal Delivery Device Weight Limit Emergency
45 Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
46 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
