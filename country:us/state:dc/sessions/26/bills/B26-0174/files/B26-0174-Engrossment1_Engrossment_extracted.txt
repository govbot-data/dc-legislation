11 To amend the Attorney General for the District of Columbia Clarification and Elected Term
12 Amendment Act of 2010 to authorize the use of interrogatories in investigations; to
13 amend the Criminal Abuse and Neglect of Vulnerable Adults Act of 2000 to clarify that
14 injunctive relief may include affirmative action; to amend Chapter 39 of Title 28 of the
15 District of Columbia Official Code to clarify the application of the Consumer Protection
16 Procedures Act and provide enhanced remedies to secure compliance, to authorize fines,
17 costs, and fees for failure to fully comply with an investigative subpoena, to clarify that
18 that Federal Trade Commission precedents should be interpreted in a manner most
19 favorable to consumers, to grant additional consumer protection enforcement authority to
20 the Department of Licensing and Consumer Protection, to clarify that the Office of
21 Administrative Hearings has jurisdiction over appeals of action taken by the Department
22 of Licensing and Consumer Protection, to clarify that unlawful and abusive trade
23 practices are prohibited, to provide that overcharging consumers for goods and services,
24 failure to post the price of goods, services and merchandise, and the failure to comply
25 with construction industry standards are unfair and deceptive trade practices, to prohibit
26 retaliation for consumer complaints and aiding and abetting prohibited trade practices, to
27 clarify complaint procedures, to expressly authorize asset freezes as injunctive relief, to
28 set mandatory minimum penalties for violations and recovery of costs and fees for
29 enforcement and investigative measures in Attorney General actions, to require
30 confidentiality agreements and protective orders entered into by the Attorney General to
31 permit sharing confidential documents with law enforcement agencies abiding by the
32 agreement or protective order, to authorize the use of interrogatories in investigations, to
33 specify that service may be made by any method reasonably calculated to give actual
34 notice, to allow use of sworn investigative statements for perjury prosecutions, and to
35 allow the Mayor to issue rules concerning the Department of Licensing and Consumer
36 Protection and the Attorney General to issue any other rules necessary to carry out the
37 chapter.
