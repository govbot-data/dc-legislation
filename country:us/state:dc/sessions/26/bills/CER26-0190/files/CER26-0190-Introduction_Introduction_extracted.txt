23 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
24
25 _________________________
26
27 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
28
29 _________________________
30
31
32 To recognize the importance of kinship families in the District of Columbia and the many
33 benefits kinship caregivers bestow on the children in their care as well as the many
34 challenges they face when raising these children, and to declare the month of September
35 2026 as “Kinship Care Month” in the District of Columbia.
36
37 WHEREAS, the D.C. Council seeks to proclaim September 2026 as Kinship Care Month
38 in the District of Columbia; and
39
40 WHEREAS, Kinship Care Month is observed to recognize children in kinship families,
41 and the grandparents, other relatives, and family friends who raise them, ensuring their safety,
42 promoting their well-being, and establishing a safe and stable home for these young people; and
43
1

44 WHEREAS, in kinship families, a caregiver cares for a child or children who were
45 biologically born to another family member or close friend because the biological parents are
46 unable to raise them; and
47
48 WHEREAS, nationally, approximately 2.7 million children are living with grandparents
49 and other relatives or fictive kin in kinship care, and more than 18,000 of these young people
50 reside in the District of Columbia, with fewer than 120 of them living in kinship foster care; and
51
52 WHEREAS, one in 11 of all children, and one in five Black children, will live within a
53 kinship family sometime during their childhood; and
54
55 WHEREAS, kinship caregivers report tremendous satisfaction in caring for and raising
56 children, but face substantial challenges in accessing the resources they need to care for their
57 families; and
58
59 WHEREAS, many kinship caregivers live at or below the margins of financial stability
60 themselves even before they agree to take a child into their homes, while others may give up
61 their retirement years to assume parenting duties for these children; and
62
63 WHEREAS, many children come to live with kinship caregivers after allegations of
64 parental abuse or neglect; and
65
66 WHEREAS, children in kinship care frequently have mental health conditions from
67 trauma experienced in their homes and communities; and
68
69 WHEREAS, kinship caregivers offer a refuge, as well as love, nurturing and care, for
70 these children; and
71
72 WHEREAS, research shows that kinship care helps maintain family, sibling and
73 community ties, improves behavioral and mental health outcomes, and promotes permanency for
74 children who cannot live with their parents; and
75
76 WHEREAS, it is vitally important for the public to grow in awareness of the challenges
77 faced by children, grandparents, and other relatives in kinship families; so as to ensure there are
78 services and supports available and accessible to enable kinship youth to grow and thrive; and
79
80 WHEREAS, the D.C. Council expresses its wholehearted thanks and support for kinship
81 caregivers for stepping up in times of crisis to provide loving homes for D.C. children; and
82
83 WHEREAS, Kinship Care Month honors the priceless role these grandparents and other
84 kinship caregivers play in the lives of the children they love.
85
86 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
87 resolution may be cited as the “Kinship Care Month Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
88
2

89 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes the contributions of kinship
90 families raising children in the District of Columbia, and declares the month of September 2026
91 as “Kinship Care Month”, as a confirmation of the District’s continued support of kinship
92 caregivers for stepping up in times of crisis to provide loving homes for children in the District.
93
94 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
