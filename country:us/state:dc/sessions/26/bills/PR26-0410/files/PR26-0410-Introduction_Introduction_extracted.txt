10 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
11 _________________

14 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the District of
15 Columbia Election Code of 1955 to require the Board of Elections to conduct a
16 comprehensive needs assessment on the District’s institutional readiness to implement
17 ranked choice voting, including an evaluation of voter awareness and public perception,
18 an assessment of equity and access impacts on historically marginalized communities, a
19 review of best practices from other jurisdictions, and the submission of a report to the
20 Mayor and the Council containing an implementation plan, full cost model, operational
21 timeline with critical milestones, and a comprehensive voter outreach and education plan
22
23 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 resolution may be cited as the “Board of Elections Ranked Choice Voting Needs Assessment
25 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
26 Sec. 2. (a) The District of Columbia Election Code of 1955 establishes the legal
27 framework governing elections in the District, including the responsibilities and administrative
28 duties of the Board of Elections (“Board”). The Ranked Choice Voting and Open Primary
29 Elections to Independent Voters Act of 2024 (D.C. Law 25-295) was approved December 2,
30 2024 and became effective March 7, 2025, but the law states that implementation of ranked
31 choice voting (RCV) is contingent upon appropriation of funds and related preparations made by
32 the Board.
1

33 (b) The successful implementation of RCV requires substantial administrative
34 preparation, including but not limited to redesigning ballots, upgrading tabulation systems,
35 training staff, preparing regulations, conducting community education, and ensuring compliance
36 with federal and local accessibility, translation, and cybersecurity requirements.
37 (c) Other jurisdictions that have implemented RCV such as New York City, Maine,
38 Alaska, and several U.S. municipalities used multi-year preparation timelines, readiness
39 assessments, cost modeling, pilot programs, and community-education planning before full
40 deployment of RCV; these assessments were critical in identifying technology, staffing, public-
41 understanding, and regulatory gaps.
42 (d) The District’s current statute does not require the Board to complete a mandatory pre-
43 implementation needs assessment, nor does it mandate an operational readiness analysis, full-
44 cost model, or detailed implementation plan prior to deploying RCV. Without these safeguards,
45 the District risks launching a voting system without sufficient preparation, public awareness, or
46 administrative capacity.
47 (e) A comprehensive needs assessment is essential to determine the Board of Elections’
48 operational readiness for ranked choice voting, including analysis of capacity limitations,
49 staffing and fiscal needs, required technology enhancements, and the development of an
50 appropriate implementation schedule.
51 (f) A comprehensive needs assessment is necessary to ensure that RCV is implemented
52 effectively, equitably, and without disenfranchising historically marginalized communities
53 including seniors, people with limited English proficiency, returning citizens, and residents of
54 Wards 5, 7, and 8 who already face systemic barriers to participation.
2

55 (g) Emergency legislation is required to mandate that the Board undertake such an
56 assessment including institutional readiness, equity/access impacts, review of other
57 jurisdictions’ best practices, and the creation of a detailed implementation plan outlining costs,
58 timelines, milestones, and a comprehensive voter-education strategy.
59 Therefore, immediate action is required to ensure that the District does not proceed with
60 deployment of ranked choice voting absent the critical assessments needed to safeguard voter
61 access, prevent administrative failures, and ensure a smooth and equitable transition to the RCV
62 system.
63 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
64 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Board of Elections
65 Board of Elections Ranked Choice Voting Needs Assessment Emergency Act of 2025 be
66 adopted after a single reading.
67 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
