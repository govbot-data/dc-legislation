Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is a proposed
resolution titled “Cesar Chavez Public Charter Schools for Public Policy Revenue Bonds Project
Emergency Approval Resolution of 2025”, and the accompanying emergency declaration resolution.
The resolution authorizes the issuance, sale, and delivery of tax-exempt revenue bonds, notes, or other
obligations in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed $25,000,000. The bonds will be used to
refinance existing debt of the Cesar Chavez Public Charter Schools for Public Policy and fund building
renovations and facility improvements at its campus located at 3701 Hayes Street NE in Ward 7. Planned
renovations include modernization of classrooms and science laboratories, upgrades to the gymnasium,
and improvements to ancillary facilities.
In accordance with section 490 of the Home Rule Act, it has been determined that the bonds, when, as,
and if issued, shall be without recourse to the District. The bonds shall not be general obligations of the
District; shall not be a pledge of or involve the full faith and credit or the taxing power of the District;
shall not constitute a debt of the District; and shall not constitute a lending of public credit for a private
undertaking as prohibited in section 602(a)(2) of the Home Rule Act. The bonds shall not give rise to any
pecuniary liability of the District, and the District shall have no obligation with respect to the purchase of
the bonds.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed measure.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

Cesar Chavez Public Charter Schools for Public Policy
Revenue Bond
FACT SHEET
The Cesar Chavez Public Charter Schools for Public Policy has requested that the District issue
up to $25,000,000 of revenue bonds to refinance outstanding debt and finance renovations and
modernizations at their campus located at 3701 Hayes Street NE in Ward 7.
The Applicant
Cesar Chavez Public Charter Schools for Public Policy (“Chavez Schools”) is a 501(c)(3) non-
profit organization founded in the District of Columbia in 1998. For almost 30 years, Chavez
Schools has served students in grades 6–12 through a network of three academic campuses,
currently enrolling approximately 370 students. Serving a predominantly low-income student
population, Chavez Schools integrates public policy themes throughout its curriculum, providing
students with hands-on experiences that prepare them to become informed citizens and
community leaders capable of shaping public policy.
Chavez Schools has consistently demonstrated strong academic outcomes and student success:
The school continues its 5-year tradition of 100% college acceptance for graduating seniors, with
students collectively earning approximately $2.9 million in college scholarships in 2024.
Proposed Project
Chavez Schools will use the proposed bond financing to refinance existing debt and fund
building renovations and facility improvements at its campus located at 3701 Hayes Street NE in
Ward 7. Planned renovations include modernization of classrooms and science laboratories,
upgrades to the gymnasium, and improvements to ancillary facilities. These updates are part of a
long-term modernization plan initiated in 2018.
Financing Plan
A summary of the proposed sources and uses of funds is attached (see Table 1).
Feasibility/Structure/Security of the Bonds
Municipal Resource Advisors, LLC (MRA), the Chavez Schools’ financial advisor, has reviewed
the financial feasibility of the Chavez Schools in connection with the proposed $25,000,000
revenue bond application. Based on the assessment of the school’s financial position and
financing needs, MRA has determined this financing to be feasible.
MRA reviewed Chavez Schools’ current debt structure and evaluated its financial capacity to
support future debt issuance(s). Based on MRA’s evaluation of the Chavez Schools’ current debt
obligations and pro forma projections of new debt in the current market conditions, MRA
determined the Chavez Schools can support anticipated future debt service related to the

Cesar Chavez Public Charter School
proposed financing, pending final terms to be determined by the ultimate financing instrument
selected.
Public Purpose Benefits
Chavez Schools is a non-profit organization organized to provide public education, to foster
educational innovation and reform, to inspire young people to become involved in public policy,
and to develop and improve the quality of schools and curricula. The issuance of these bonds
will continue to support the mission and development of the Chavez Schools’ campus located in
Ward 7. This will enhance career, contracting, and educational opportunities for District
residents and businesses. Chavez Schools has maintained steady enrollment for the past several
years and has an upward trending attendance over the same time period, currently around 88%.
Legal and Regulatory Affairs
The law office of Squire Patton Boggs is assigned as bond counsel to the Revenue Bond Program
and has preliminarily determined that Chavez Schools is a 501(c)(3) organization, and the project
constitutes a permissible undertaking in Section 490(a)(1) of the District of Columbia Home
Rule Act.
Based upon the information outlined in the application, the Revenue Bond Program staff has
determined that the proposed project complies with the criteria for approval of a proposed
financing transaction through the District’s Revenue Bond Program and will assist the Chavez
Schools in furthering its organizational mission.
