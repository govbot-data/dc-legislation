Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is the
“Transfer of Jurisdiction Over the Robert F. Kennedy Memorial Stadium Campus Emergency
Approval Resolution of 2025”, along with an accompanying emergency declaration resolution.
This emergency legislation is technical in nature and helps execute the D.C. Robert F. Kennedy
Memorial Stadium Campus Revitalization Act, approved January 6, 2025 (Pub. L. No. 118-274;
138 Stat. 3234). The federal legislation effectuates the transfer of jurisdiction from the National
Park Service to the District of Columbia of the Robert F. Kennedy Memorial Stadium Campus,
comprising over seven million square feet of waterfront and development property. This historic
transfer will allow the District to put the RFK Campus back into productive use for our city and
DC residents. The new potential for redevelopment will enable us to deliver housing, jobs,
amenities, sports and recreation, green space and parks, and more, all along the Anacostia
riverfront.
Emergency approval of this transfer will allow the District to meet the timeline established in the
federal law. Section 2(a)(1) of that law requires the transfer of jurisdiction over the RFK Campus
to be completed within 180 days after the date of enactment of the law.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser
