12 To declare the existence of an emergency, due to congressional review, with respect to the need
13 to amend Residential Tranquility Act of 2010 to prohibit persons targeting a residence for
14 purposes of a demonstration from using sound amplifying devices in a residential zone
15 between 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m., and to prohibit a person from launching or throwing a
16 projectile onto the residential property of another with the intent to cause fear, intimidate,
17 or retaliate against any person living or working at that residence; and to amend the
18 National Capital Revitalization and Self-Government Improvement Act of 1997 to
19 provide that the term expiration date for a Council appointment to the Corrections
20 Information Council shall be December 7th of the year in which the term expires.
21 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 resolution may be cited as the “Residential Tranquility Second Congressional Review
23 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
24 Sec. 2. (a) On July 1, 2025, the Council adopted the Residential Tranquility Emergency
25 Amendment Act of 2025, effective July 21, 2025 (D.C. Act 26-119; 72 DCR 8178) (“emergency
26 act”), which expires on October 19, 2025.
27 (b) On July 1, 2025, the Council adopted the Residential Tranquility Amendment Act of
28 2025, enacted on July 21, 2025 (D.C. Act 26-115; 72 DCR 8167) (“permanent act”), which has a
29 projected law date of November 8, 2025.
1

30 (c) This congressional review emergency legislation is necessary to prevent a gap in the
31 law between the expiration of the emergency act and the projected law date of the permanent act.
32 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
33 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making it necessary that the Residential
34 Tranquility Second Congressional Review Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after
35 a single reading.
36 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
