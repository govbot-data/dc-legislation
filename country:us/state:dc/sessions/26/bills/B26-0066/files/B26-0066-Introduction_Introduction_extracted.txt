Secretary Howard:
Today, along with Councilmembers Bonds, Lewis George, Nadeau, Pinto, and Robert White, I am
introducing the “Youth Advisory Council on Climate Change and Environmental Conservation
Establishment Act of 2025”. Please find enclosed a signed copy of the legislation.
Young people are some of the most passionate and informed advocates for climate action. Youth-
led movements—like the Fridays for Future campaign started by Greta Thunberg—have
transformed public discourse while pushing for urgent policy changes to combat climate change.
Youth voices have also taken center stage in forums like the United Nations’s Framework
Convention on Climate Change Conference of the Parties (COP). Youth-led climate movements and
action campaigns have been steadily growing over the last decade, bringing much-needed attention
to attention to the urgency of the climate crisis.
Despite these significant contributions, youth voices remain underrepresented in formal decision-
making spaces, including those in the District. Establishing a Youth Advisory Council dedicated to
climate change and environmental conservation is a simple step to empower those most acutely
impacted by the long-term effects of climate change. This bill recognizes the value of
intergenerational collaboration and ensures that the perspectives of those most invested in the
future are not only heard, but actively integrated into our climate strategies.
During the Committee on Transportation and the Environment’s performance oversight hearings in
early 2024, youth advocates made clear that they want to participate in climate change governance
here in the District and engage more meaningfully with the Council, the Executive, and other
stakeholders to ensure that climate action policies are inclusive and reflect the needs of
marginalized communities. This legislation will achieve that goal, and I look forward to working with

my colleagues to pass it so that young environmental activists have a seat at the table and know
that their voices are heard.
Sincerely,
Councilmember Charles Allen, Ward 6
Chairperson, Committee on Transportation & the Environment

26 To establish the Youth Advisory Council on Climate Change and Environmental Conservation to
27 provide an organized youth perspective on various issues, including the impacts of the
28 changing climate, adverse weather events, and ways the District can mitigate, prepare, and
29 adapt to climate change and create and sustain environmental educational opportunities for
30 youth.
31
32 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
33 act may be cited as the “Youth Advisory Council on Climate Change and Environmental
34 Conservation Establishment Act of 2025”.
35 Sec. 2. Youth Advisory Council on Climate Change and Environmental Conservation.
36 (a) There is established the Youth Advisory Council on Climate Change and
37 Environmental Conservation (“Youth Climate Council”), whose mission is to:
38 (1) Consult with and advise the Mayor, the Council of the District of Columbia,
39 including the committees with jurisdiction over the environment, natural resources, and energy,
1

40 the Department of Energy and Environment, the Commission on Climate Change and Resiliency,
41 and other relevant District agencies;
42 (2) Comment on legislation, laws, policies, and practices that impact youth and the
43 environment;
44 (3) Identify climate issues that disproportionately affect youth and provide
45 recommendations on how to mitigate and adapt to these issues and present methods to combat
46 climate change;
47 (4) Monitor climate programs and policies that affect youth and ensure they are
48 achieving the intended outcomes, including making recommendations on environmental education
49 and outdoor recreation programs in schools and those directed at youth and their families; and
50 (5) Collaborate with other youth organizations and entities in the District, including
51 the Youth Advisory Council (D.C. Official Code § 2–1562).
52 (b) The Youth Climate Council shall have 17 voting members, who shall be appointed as
53 follows:
54 (1) Nine members appointed by the Mayor for a term of 3 years, with initial
55 staggered appointments of 5 voting members appointed for 2 years and 4 voting members
56 appointed for 1 year, and shall be:
57 (A) Three middle school students;
58 (B) Three high school students; and
59 (C) Three college or university students from District-area colleges and
60 universities.
2

61 (2)(A) Eight voting members appointed by the Chairman of the Council, which
62 shall reflect each of the District’s 8 wards in consultation with each Ward Councilmember who
63 shall make recommendations from among the applicants to represent his or her respective ward;
64 (B) The voting members appointed by the Chairman of the Council shall
65 be appointed for a term of 2 years, with initial staggered appointments of 4 voting members
66 appointed for 2 years and 4 voting members appointed for 1 year.
67 (C) A member shall serve for a term of 2 years. A member may be
68 reappointed, but may not serve more than two full terms.
69 (c)(1) A member of the Youth Climate Council shall have been a District resident for at
70 least two years, not including time residing in a college dormitory, prior to their application to
71 serve on the Youth Climate Council.
72 (2) College students shall be eligible to participate in the Youth Climate Council if
73 they have been a District resident living outside a college dormitory for at least two years prior to
74 their application to serve on the Youth Climate Council.
75 (d) Youth applying to serve on the Youth Climate Council shall have:
76 (1) An interest in and motivation for climate change and resiliency;
77 (2) A background in climate-based initiatives;
78 (3) A general knowledge of how the District is affected by climate change;
79 (4) An ability to bring creative perspectives to the issue of climate change and how
80 to engage youth in mitigating and adapting to climate change risks;
81 (5) The ability to collaborate and work both patiently and constructively in a group
82 setting;
83 (6) The ability to responsibly fulfill commitments; and
3

84 (7) An interest in the development of leadership skills.
85 (e) Vacancies shall be filled in the same manner as the original appointment to the position
86 that became vacant. Voting members appointed to fill vacancies before the expiration of a voting
87 member's full term shall serve only the unexpired portion of the voting member's term.
88 (f) All initial appointments to the Youth Climate Council shall be made within 180 days
89 after the effective date of this act.
90 Sec. 3. Functions and procedures of the Youth Climate Council.
91 (a)(1) The Youth Climate Council shall elect a chairperson, vice-chairperson, and secretary
92 for a term of one year.
93 (2) These officers may be re-elected but shall serve no more than two (2) terms in
94 any combination of offices.
95 (3) The Youth Climate Council may vote to create other leadership positions from
96 within its membership.
97 (b) The Youth Climate Council shall meet at least once per quarter.
98 (c) The chairperson of the Commission, or vice-chairperson in their absence, shall convene
99 all Youth Climate Council meetings.
100 (d)(1) The first Youth Climate Council shall create bylaws and rules to which all future
101 Youth Climate Councils shall adhere.
102 (2) Where not otherwise provided in this act or the bylaws and rules, the procedures of
103 the Youth Climate Council shall be governed by Robert’s Rules of Order.
104 (d) A majority of the voting members appointed to the Youth Climate Council at any given
105 time shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of official business.
4

106 (e) All actions of the Youth Climate Council shall be considered advisory and shall be
107 taken by majority vote of the voting members present and voting at a meeting.
108 (f) The Youth Climate Council may form committees or working groups from its
109 membership to consider specific subtopics and invite participation from outside individuals and
110 entities, including the public and subject-matter experts.
111 (g) The Department of Energy and Environment shall provide principal staff and
112 equipment support for the activities of the Youth Climate Council and assist with the development,
113 publication, and submission of all reports to the Council and Mayor.
114 (h) The Council may provide staff assistance to the Youth Climate Council for the purposes
115 of assistance with comments on all proposed legislation pending before the Council.
116 (i) The chairpersons of the committees with jurisdiction over the environment, energy and
117 natural resources at the Council shall provide an opportunity for the Youth Climate Council to
118 report in person on its findings and recommendations at least once each year.
119 (j) The Youth Climate Council may use space and supplies owned or rented by the District
120 government for purposes consistent with this chapter.
