30 To require that all lead service lines in the District on both public and private property be
31 replaced with non-lead lines by December 31, 2030, to prohibit the practice of partial
32 lead service line replacements, to prohibit the installation of new lead, brass, or
33 galvanized steel service lines, to require, by October 1, 2024, that owners of private
34 property serviced by a lead service line enroll in the Lead Service Line Replacement
35 Program or replace the lead service line themselves, to prescribe how owners of private
36 property may provide proof to DC Water that their service line does not contain lead, to
37 prescribe how DC Water is to treat properties with service lines of unknown composition,
38 to prescribe acceptable methods of testing service lines for lead, to establish robust notice
39 and public education requirements, to establish the Lead Service Line Replacement
40 Program to provide lead service line replacements at District properties at no cost to the
41 property owner, to establish a schedule by which DC Water is to prioritize properties for
42 lead service line replacements, to establish a high-priority lead service line replacement
43 program to prioritize replacement of lead service lines at child development facilities,
44 multi-unit residential properties, properties that previously underwent a partial lead
45 service line replacement, and homes with emergency service line repairs, to establish
46 plumbing system flushing protocols following a lead service line replacement, to require
47 DC Water to maintain a service line inventory and prescribe the contents of that

48 inventory, to mandate that DC Water provide water filtration devices and replacement
49 cartridges to all properties serviced by a service line made of lead or of unknown
50 composition until at least 6 months after the property’s lead service line is replaced, to
51 require that, as a condition of a real property sale or transfer, a purchaser enroll in the
52 Lead Service Line Replacement Program or replace the lead service line, to authorize
53 occupants, including a tenant, to enroll in the Lead Service Line Replacement Program
54 on behalf of a property owner, to prescribe certain rights and protections for occupants
55 residing at a property serviced by a lead service line, to require that the Department of
56 Energy and Environment engage community-based organizations (“CBO”) to review
57 contractor lead service line replacements, to set deadlines for residents to comply with the
58 requirements of this act, to prescribe notice and DC Water and CBO outreach
59 requirements where a property owner has not complied with the requirements of this act
60 by a date certain, to prescribe penalties, including fines and restrictions on access to
61 District-issued permits and licenses where a property owner has not complied with the
62 requirements of this act by a date certain, to prescribe the manner in which information
63 about the Lead Service Line Replacement Program, lead exposure risks, and other
64 information is to be shared with the public via a public education campaign, to set data
65 reporting requirements on spending, program administration, and property owner
66 compliance with this act, to clarify that District and federal funding for lead service line
67 replacements shall not be used for water main replacements unless DC Water determines
68 the lead service line replacement would cause the main to fail, and to require the Mayor
69 to issue regulations to implement this act; and, to amend the Fiscal Year 2005 Budget
70 Support Act of 2004 to remove language establishing the Lead Pipe Replacement
71 Assistance Program and permitting, in certain instances, DC Water from completing
72 partial lead service line replacements, and to amend permissible uses of the Lead Service
73 Line Priority Replacement Assistance Fund.
74
75 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
76 act may be cited as the “Lead-Free DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2025”.

78 Sec. 101. Definitions.
79 (a) “Adequate proof” means test-pitting or inspection of a service line:
80 (1) At a minimum of one location on the service line on public property and one
81 location on private property, each excavation covering at least two feet of the service line; and

85 services for infants, toddlers, and preschoolers on a regular basis. The term “child development

87 public or private school.

89 data from the American Community Survey, consisting of 600 to 3,000 individuals on average.
90 (d) “Community-based organization” means an organization operated by a nonprofit
91 entity or faith-based organization that provides services to residents of the District.
92 (e) “Historic brass service line” means those service lines that DC Water had identified as
93 brass based on existing service line data, plumbers’ records, home build dates, information on
94 known and likely locations of brass service lines, or combined with tap cards and other
95 engineering project data but have not been field-verified;
96 (f) “Historic copper service line” means those service lines that DC Water had identified
97 as copper based on existing service line data, plumbers’ records, home build dates, information
98 on known and likely locations of copper service lines, or combined with tap cards, and other
99 engineering project data but have not been field-verified;
100 (g) “Lead service line” means a water service line containing any lead, and shall include
101 a:
102 (1) Brass service line; or
103 (2) Galvanized service line.
104 (h) “Multi-unit residential property” means a residential building with 2 or more dwelling
105 units.
106 (i) “Public property” means all lands, properties, and holdings owned or leased by or
107 under the control of the District, and shall include properties under the control of the District of
108 Columbia Housing Authority.
3

109 (j) “Public space” means all the publicly owned property between the property lines on a
110 street, as such property lines are shown in the records of the District, and includes any roadway,
111 tree space, sidewalk, or parking between such property lines.
112 (k) “Private property” means all lands, properties, and holdings owned by private entities
113 in the District, and shall include federal properties on District land.
114 (l) “Private space” means the bounds of all privately owned property as demarcated by
115 the property lines on a street, as such property lines are shown in the records of the District.
116 (m) “Property line” means the line of demarcation between privately owned property
117 fronting or abutting a street and the publicly owned property in the line of such street.
118 (n) “Replacement of a lead service line” means:
119 (1) Replacement of all portions of a service line with a non-lead material where
120 the service line is a lead service line;
121 (2) Replacement and leveling of dirt and soil displaced during excavation and
122 replacement landscaping as required under this act;
123 (3) Replacement or restoration of interior and exterior walls and floors damaged
124 or demolished during replacement of a lead service line to a safe and habitable condition;
125 (4) Flushing household plumbing according to AWWA C810-17, or any standard
126 that revises or replaces this standard deemed to be equally or more protective, prior to returning
127 property to water service after the lead service line replacement; and
128 (5) Provision of filters in accordance with section 108 of this act.
129 (o) “Service line” means all portions of a water service line in both public and private
130 space, including the discharge of the corporation fitting to customer site piping, to the building
131 plumbing at the first shut-off valve inside the building, or 18 inches inside the building,
132 whichever is shorter, and including goosenecks and pigtails.
4

133 (p) “Service line of unknown composition” means a service line that:
134 (1) Is made of an unknown material, unidentified material, or materials
135 categorized as historic brass or historic copper;
136 (1) Has not been replaced by an approved contractor; and
137 (2) Has not had the service line composition assessed using testing pitting.
138 (q) “Test pitting” means excavation to expose a section of water service to determine the
139 location, size, and material type of existing water services.
140 Sec. 102. Lead service line mandates.
141 (a) The District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority shall, by December 31, 2030:
142 (1) Replace all lead service lines in public and private space servicing private
143 properties;
144 (2) Be in receipt of written declaration from a licensed and approved contractor
145 that the property is not serviced by a lead service line; or
146 (3) Have a verified inventory record, including photographic evidence, for every
147 service line in the district documenting that no portion of the service line contains lead, brass, or
148 galvanized steel piping components.
149 (b) The Mayor shall replace all lead services line in public and private space on public
150 property, including property under the control of the District of Columbia Housing Authority, by
151 December 31, 2030.
152 (c) As of the effective date of this act, no public or private entity or person in the District
153 shall:
154 (1) Replace the portion of a service line that is in the public space at a property
155 unless it also replaces the portion of the lead service line that is in private space; or
5

156 (1) Replace the portion of a service line that is in private space at a property
157 unless it also replaces the portion of the lead service line that is in the public space; or
158 (d) No service line shall be installed in the District that contains lead, brass, or galvanized
159 steel piping components.

455 Sec. 108. Water filters.
456 (a) Within 14 days after providing notice to an owner of a residential property pursuant to
457 Section 103(e) of this act, the District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority (“DC Water”)
458 shall provide at no cost:
459 (1) Pitcher-style filters as follows:
460 (A) For single family properties, one pitcher and at least a 6 month supply
461 of filter cartridges; and
462 (B) For multi-unit residential properties, at least one filter unit per unit; or
463 (2) Point-of-use filters for all drinking water sources; and
464 (b)(1)(A) Filters provided pursuant to this section shall meet the current NSF/ANSI 42
465 standard for particulate Class I reduction and the NSF/ANSI 53 standard for lead reduction, or
466 any standard that revises or replaces this standard deemed to be equally or more effective at lead
467 reduction.
468 (B) The Department of Energy and Environment may issue regulations to
469 adopt different standards for filters from those established by this section, where the different
470 standard meets or exceeds the NSF/ANSI 42 standard for particulate Class I reduction and the
471 NSF/ANSI 53 standard for lead reduction.
472 (2) DC Water shall replace a broken filter, upon notice from the property owner
473 or occupant. DC Water may require a property owner to return the broken filter or provide other
474 evidence that the filter is broken to the agency.
19

475 (c)(1) DC Water shall provide a property owner or occupant eligible to receive filters
476 under subsection (a) of this section with filter cartridge replacements in a volume or frequency
477 such that the property owner or occupant can replace the filter cartridge in accordance with
478 manufacturer’s recommendations until the lead service line is replaced and for 6 months
479 afterwards; or
480 (2) DC Water shall prepare an automated customer alert system that reminds
