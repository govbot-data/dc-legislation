25 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
26
27
28 To authorize the Mayor to execute and enter, on behalf of the District, the Nurse Licensure
29 Compact to permit licensed registered and licensed practical/vocational nurses in party
30 states to practice in the District and for multistate licensed nurses in the District to
31 practice in other party states; to increase the availability of licensed nurses; to standardize
32 minimum requirements for education and training for participating compact nurses; to
33 establish requirements for the administration of interstate licenses; to join the compact
34 licensure information system; to require all compact states to share licensee information
35 with other compact states; to establish the Interstate Commission of Nurse Licensure
36 Compact Administrators; to allow the Board of Nursing to charge an additional fee for
37 the issuance of a multistate license; and to require individuals or hospitals that employ
38 nurses to report the number of multistate license holding nurses to the Board of Nursing
39 and to prepare and provide each nurse with a copy of laws and rules specific to the
40 practice of nursing in the District.

1

42 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
43 act may be cited as the “District of Columbia Nurse Licensure Compact Authorization Act of
44 2025”.
45 Sec. 2. Findings and declaration of purpose.
46 The nurse licensure compact is enacted into law and entered into by the District, with all
47 other party states legally joining therein in the form substantially as follows:
48 “Sec. 1. Findings and Declaration of Purpose:
49 (a) The party states find that:
50 (1) The health and safety of the public are affected by the degree of compliance
51 with, and the effectiveness of enforcement activities related to state nurse licensure laws;
52 (2) Violations of nurse licensure and other laws regulating the practice of nursing
53 may result in injury or harm to the public;
54 (3) The expanded mobility of nurses and the use of advanced communication
55 technologies as part of our nation’s health care delivery system require greater coordination and
56 cooperation among states in the areas of nurse licensure and regulation;
57 (4) New practice modalities and technology make compliance with individual
58 state nurse licensure laws difficult and complex;
59 (5) The current system of duplicative licensure for nurses practicing in multiple
60 states is cumbersome and redundant for both nurses and states; and
61 (6) Uniformity of nurse licensure requirements throughout the states promotes
62 public safety and public health benefits.
63 (b) The general purposes of this Compact are to:
64 (1) Facilitate the states’ responsibility to protect the public’s health and safety;
2

65 (2) Ensure and encourage the cooperation of party states in the areas of nurse
66 licensure and regulation;
67 (3) Facilitate the exchange of information between party states in the areas of
68 nurse regulation, investigation, and adverse actions;
69 (4) Promote compliance with the laws governing the practice of nursing in each
70 jurisdiction;
71 (5) Invest all party states with the authority to hold a nurse accountable for
72 meeting all state practice laws in the state in which the patient is located at the time care is
73 rendered through the mutual recognition of party state licenses;
74 (6) Decrease redundancies in the consideration and issuance of nurse licenses; and
75 (7) Provide opportunities for interstate practice by nurses who meet uniform
76 licensure requirements.
77 “Sec. 2. Definitions
78 As used in this Compact:
79 (1) “Adverse action” means any administrative, civil, equitable, or criminal action
80 permitted by a state’s laws which is imposed by a licensing board or other authority against a
81 nurse, including actions against an individual’s license or multistate licensure privilege such as
82 revocation, suspension, probation, monitoring of the licensee, limitation on the licensee’s
83 practice, or any other encumbrance on licensure affecting a nurse’s authorization to practice,
84 including issuance of a cease and desist action.
85 (2) “Alternative program” means a non-disciplinary monitoring program
86 approved by a licensing board.
3

87 (3) “Coordinated licensure information system” means an integrated process for
88 collecting, storing, and sharing information on nurse licensure and enforcement activities related
89 to nurse licensure laws, which is administered by a nonprofit organization composed of and
90 controlled by licensing boards.
91 (4) “Current significant investigative information” means:
92 (A) Investigative information that a licensing board, after a preliminary
93 inquiry that includes notification and an opportunity for the nurse to respond, if required by state
94 law, has reason to believe is not groundless and, if proved true, would indicate more than a minor
95 infraction; or
96 (B) Investigative information that indicates that the nurse represents an
97 immediate threat to public health and safety regardless of whether the nurse has been notified
98 and had an opportunity to respond
99 (5) “Encumbrance” means a revocation or suspension of, or any limitation on, the
100 full and unrestricted practice of nursing imposed by a licensing board.
101 (6) “Home state” means the party state that is the nurse’s primary state of
102 residence.
103 (7) “Licensing board” means a party state’s regulatory body responsible for
104 issuing nurse licenses.
105 (8) “Multistate license” means a license to practice as a registered nurse (RN) or a
106 licensed practical/vocational nurse (LPN/VN) issued by a home state licensing board that
107 authorizes the licensed nurse to practice in all party states under a multistate licensure privilege.
4

108 (9) “Multistate licensure privilege” means a legal authorization associated with a
109 multistate license permitting the practice of nursing as either a registered nurse (RN) or a
110 licensed practical/vocational nurse (LPN/VN) in a remote state.
111 (10) “Nurse” means RN or LPN/VN, as those terms are defined by each party
112 state’s practice laws.
113 (11) “Party state” means any state that has adopted this Compact.
114 (12) “Remote state” means a party state, other than the home state.
115 (13) “Single-state license” means a nurse license issued by a party state that
116 authorizes practice only within the issuing state and does not include a multistate licensure
117 privilege to practice in any other party state.
118 (14) “State” means a state, commonwealth, territory, or possession of the United
119 States, and the District of Columbia.
120 (15) “State practice laws” means a party state’s laws, rules, and regulations that
121 govern the practice of nursing, define the scope of nursing practice, and create the methods and
122 grounds for imposing discipline. “State practice laws” do not include requirements necessary to
123 obtain and retain a license, except for qualifications or requirements of the home state.
124 “Sec. 3. General provisions and jurisdiction.
125 (a) A multistate license to practice registered or licensed practical/vocational nursing
126 issued by a home state to a resident in that state will be recognized by each party state as
127 authorizing a nurse to practice as a registered nurse (RN) or as a licensed practical/vocational
128 nurse (LPN/VN), under a multistate licensure privilege, in each party state.
129 (b) A state must implement procedures for considering the criminal history records of
130 applicants for initial multistate license or licensure by endorsement. Such procedures shall
5

131 include the submission of fingerprints or other biometric-based information by applicants for the
132 purpose of obtaining an applicant’s criminal history record information from the Federal Bureau
133 of Investigation and the agency responsible for retaining that state’s criminal records.
134 (c) Each party state shall require the following for an applicant to obtain or retain a
135 multistate license in the home state:
136 (1) Meets the home state’s qualifications for licensure or renewal of licensure, as
137 well as, all other applicable state laws;
138 (2)(A) Has graduated or is eligible to graduate from a licensing board-approved
139 RN or LPN/VN prelicensure education program; or
140 (B) Has graduated from a foreign RN or LPN/VN prelicensure education
141 program that:
142 (i) has been approved by the authorized accrediting body in the
143 applicable country, and:
144 (ii) has been verified by an independent credentials review agency
145 to be comparable to a licensing board-approved prelicensure education program.
146 (3) Has, if a graduate of a foreign prelicensure education program not taught in
147 English or if English is not the individual’s native language, successfully passed an English
148 proficiency examination that includes the components of reading, speaking, writing and
149 listening;
150 (4) Has successfully passed an NCLEX-RN® or NCLEX-PN® Examination or
151 recognized predecessor, as applicable;
152 (5) Is eligible for or holds an active, unencumbered license;
6

153 (6) Has submitted, in connection with an application for initial licensure or
154 licensure by endorsement, fingerprints or other biometric data for the purpose of obtaining
155 criminal history record information from the Federal Bureau of Investigation and the agency
156 responsible for retaining that state’s criminal records;
157 (7) Has not been convicted or found guilty, or has entered into an agreed
158 disposition, of a felony offense under applicable state or federal criminal law;
159 (8) Has not been convicted or found guilty, or has entered into an agreed
160 disposition, of a misdemeanor offense related to the practice of nursing as determined on a case-
161 by-case basis;
162 (9) Is not currently enrolled in an alternative program;
163 (10) Is subject to self-disclosure requirements regarding current participation in an
164 alternative program; and
165 (11) Has a valid United States Social Security number.
166 (d) All party states shall be authorized, in accordance with existing state due process law,
167 to take adverse action against a nurse’s multistate licensure privilege such as revocation,
168 suspension, probation or any other action that affects a nurse’s authorization to practice under a
169 multistate licensure privilege, including cease and desist actions. If a party state takes such
170 action, it shall promptly notify the administrator of the coordinated licensure information system.
171 The administrator of the coordinated licensure information system shall promptly notify the
172 home state of any such actions by remote states.
173 (e) A nurse practicing in a party state must comply with the state practice laws of the
174 state in which the client is located at the time service is provided.
7

175 (1) The practice of nursing is not limited to patient care, but shall include all
176 nursing practice as defined by the state practice laws of the party state in which the client is
177 located.
178 (2) The practice of nursing in a party state under a multistate licensure privilege
179 will subject a nurse to the jurisdiction of the licensing board, the courts and the laws of the party
180 state in which the client is located at the time service is provided.
181 (f) Individuals not residing in a party state shall continue to be able to apply for a party
182 state’s single- state license as provided under the laws of each party state. However, the single-
183 state license granted to these individuals will not be recognized as granting the privilege to
184 practice nursing in any other party state. Nothing in this Compact shall affect the requirements
185 established by a party state for the issuance of a single-state license.
186 (g) Any nurse holding a home state multistate license, on the effective date of this
187 Compact, may retain and renew the multistate license issued by the nurse’s then-current home
188 state, provided that:
189 (1) A nurse, who changes primary state of residence after this Compact’s effective
190 date, must meet all applicable Section 3(c) requirements to obtain a multistate license from a new
191 home state.
192 (2) A nurse who fails to satisfy the multistate licensure requirements in Section
193 3(c) due to a disqualifying event occurring after this Compact’s effective date shall be ineligible
194 to retain or renew a multistate license, and the nurse’s multistate license shall be revoked or
195 deactivated in accordance with applicable rules adopted by the Interstate Commission of Nurse
196 Licensure Compact Administrators (“Commission”).
197 “Sec. 4. Applications for licensure in a party state.
8

198 (a) Upon application for a multistate license, the licensing board in the issuing party state
199 shall ascertain, through the coordinated licensure information system, whether the applicant has
200 ever held, or is the holder of, a license issued by any other state, whether there are any
201 encumbrances on any license or multistate licensure privilege held by the applicant, whether any
202 adverse action has been taken against any license or multistate licensure privilege held by the
203 applicant and whether the applicant is currently participating in an alternative program.
204 (b) A nurse may hold a multistate license, issued by the home state, in only one party
205 state at a time.
206 (c) If a nurse changes primary state of residence by moving between two party states, the
207 nurse must apply for licensure in the new home state, and the multistate license issued by the
208 prior home state will be deactivated in accordance with applicable rules adopted by the
209 Commission.
210 (A) The nurse may apply for licensure in advance of a change in primary state of
211 residence.
212 (B) A multistate license shall not be issued by the new home state until the nurse
213 provides satisfactory evidence of a change in primary state of residence to the new home state
214 and satisfies all applicable requirements to obtain a multistate license from the new home state.
215 (d) If a nurse changes primary state of residence by moving from a party state to a non-
216 party state, the multistate license issued by the prior home state will convert to a single-state
217 license, valid only in the former home state.
218 “Sec. 5. Additional authorities invested in party state licensing boards.
219 (a) In addition to the other powers conferred by state law, a licensing board shall have the
220 authority to:
9

221 (1) Take adverse action against a nurse’s multistate licensure privilege to practice
222 within that party state.
223 (A) Only the home state shall have the power to take adverse action
224 against a nurse’s license issued by the home state.
225 (B) For purposes of taking adverse action, the home state licensing board
226 shall give the same priority and effect to reported conduct received from a remote state as it
227 would if such conduct had occurred within the home state. In so doing, the home state shall
228 apply its own state laws to determine appropriate action.
229 (2) Issue cease and desist orders or impose an encumbrance on a nurse’s authority
230 to practice within that party state.
231 (3) Complete any pending investigations of a nurse who changes primary state of
232 residence during the course of such investigations. The licensing board shall also have the
233 authority to take appropriate action(s) and shall promptly report the conclusions of such
234 investigations to the administrator of the coordinated licensure information system. The
235 administrator of the coordinated licensure information system shall promptly notify the new
236 home state of any such actions.
237 (4) Issue subpoenas for both hearings and investigations that require the
238 attendance and testimony of witnesses, as well as, the production of evidence. Subpoenas issued
239 by a licensing board in a party state for the attendance and testimony of witnesses or the
240 production of evidence from another party state shall be enforced in the latter state by any court
241 of competent jurisdiction, according to the practice and procedure of that court applicable to
242 subpoenas issued in proceedings pending before it. The issuing authority shall pay any witness
10

243 fees, travel expenses, mileage and other fees required by the service statutes of the state in which
244 the witnesses or evidence are located.
245 (5) Obtain and submit, for each nurse licensure applicant, fingerprint or other
246 biometric-based information to the Federal Bureau of Investigation for criminal background
247 checks, receive the results of the Federal Bureau of Investigation record search on criminal
248 background checks and use the results in making licensure decisions.
249 (6) If otherwise permitted by state law, recover from the affected nurse the costs
250 of investigations and disposition of cases resulting from any adverse action taken against that
251 nurse.
252 (7) Take adverse action based on the factual findings of the remote state, provided
253 that the licensing board follows its own procedures for taking such adverse action.
254 (b) If adverse action is taken by the home state against a nurse’s multistate license, the
255 nurse’s multistate licensure privilege to practice in all other party states shall be deactivated until
256 all encumbrances have been removed from the multistate license. All home state disciplinary
257 orders that impose adverse action against a nurse’s multistate license shall include a statement
258 that the nurse’s multistate licensure privilege is deactivated in all party states during the
259 pendency of the order.
260 (c) Nothing in this Compact shall override a party state’s decision that participation in an
261 alternative program may be used in lieu of adverse action. The home state licensing board shall
262 deactivate the multistate licensure privilege under the multistate license of any nurse for the
263 duration of the nurse’s participation in an alternative program.
264 “Sec. 6. Coordinated licensure information system and exchange of information.
11

265 (a) All party states shall participate in a coordinated licensure information system of all
266 licensed registered nurses (RNs) and licensed practical/vocational nurses (LPNs/VNs). This
267 system will include information on the licensure and disciplinary history of each nurse, as
268 submitted by party states, to assist in the coordination of nurse licensure and enforcement efforts.
269 (b) The Commission, in consultation with the administrator of the coordinated licensure
270 information system, shall formulate necessary and proper procedures for the identification,
271 collection and exchange of information under this Compact.
272 (c) All licensing boards shall promptly report to the coordinated licensure information
273 system any adverse action, any current significant investigative information, denials of
274 applications (with the reasons for such denials) and nurse participation in alternative programs
275 known to the licensing board regardless of whether such participation is deemed nonpublic or
276 confidential under state law.
277 (d) Current significant investigative information and participation in nonpublic or
278 confidential alternative programs shall be transmitted through the coordinated licensure
279 information system only to party state licensing boards.
280 (e) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, all party state licensing boards
281 contributing information to the coordinated licensure information system may designate
282 information that may not be shared with non-party states or disclosed to other entities or
283 individuals without the express permission of the contributing state.
284 (f) Any personally identifiable information obtained from the coordinated licensure
285 information system by a party state licensing board shall not be shared with non-party states or
286 disclosed to other entities or individuals except to the extent permitted by the laws of the party
287 state contributing the information.
12

288 (g) Any information contributed to the coordinated licensure information system that is
289 subsequently required to be expunged by the laws of the party state contributing that information
290 shall also be expunged from the coordinated licensure information system.
291 (h) The Compact administrator of each party state shall furnish a uniform data set to the
292 Compact administrator of each other party state, which shall include, at a minimum:
293 (1) Identifying information;
294 (2) Licensure data;
295 (3) Information related to alternative program participation; and
296 (4) Other information that may facilitate the administration of this Compact, as
297 determined by Commission rules.
298 (i) The Compact administrator of a party state shall provide all investigative documents
299 and information requested by another party state.
300 “Sec. 7. Establishment of the interstate commission of nurse licensure compact
301 administrators.
302 (a) The party states hereby create and establish a joint public entity known as the
303 Interstate Commission of Nurse Licensure Compact Administrators:
304 (1) The Commission is an instrumentality of the party states.
305 (2) Venue is proper and judicial proceedings by or against the Commission shall
306 be brought solely and exclusively in a court of competent jurisdiction where the principal office
307 of the Commission is located. The Commission may waive venue and jurisdictional defenses to
308 the extent it adopts or consents to participate in alternative dispute resolution proceedings.
309 (3) Nothing in this Compact shall be construed to be a waiver of sovereign
310 immunity.

465 “Sec. 8. Commission rulemaking.
466 (a) The Commission shall exercise its rulemaking powers pursuant to the criteria set forth
467 in this Section and the rules adopted thereunder. Rules and amendments shall become binding as
20

468 of the date specified in each rule or amendment and shall have the same force and effect as
469 provisions of this Compact.
470 (b) Rules or amendments to the rules shall be adopted at a regular or special meeting of
471 the Commission.
472 (c) Prior to promulgation and adoption of a final rule or rules by the Commission, and at
473 least sixty (60) days in advance of the meeting at which the rule will be considered and voted
474 upon, the Commission shall file a notice of proposed rulemaking:
475 (1) On the website of the Commission, and;
476 (2) On the website of each licensing board or the publication in which each state
477 would otherwise publish proposed rules.
478 (d) The notice of proposed rulemaking shall include:
479 (1) The proposed time, date and location of the meeting in which the rule will be
480 considered and voted upon;
481 (2) The text of the proposed rule or amendment, and the reason for the proposed
482 rule;
483 (3) A request for comments on the proposed rule from any interested person; and
484 (4) The manner in which interested persons may submit notice to the Commission

526 (a) Oversight:
527 (1) Each party state shall enforce this Compact and take all actions necessary and
528 appropriate to effectuate this Compact’s purposes and intent.
529 (2) The Commission shall be entitled to receive service of process in any
530 proceeding that may affect the powers, responsibilities or actions of the Commission, and shall
531 have standing to intervene in such a proceeding for all purposes. Failure to provide service of
532 process in such proceeding to the Commission shall render a judgment or order void as to the
533 Commission, this Compact or promulgated rules.
534 (b) Default, technical assistance and termination
23

535 (1) If the Commission determines that a party state has defaulted in the
536 performance of its obligations or responsibilities under this Compact or the promulgated rules,
537 the Commission shall:
538 (A) Provide written notice to the defaulting state and other party states of
539 the nature of the default, the proposed means of curing the default, or any other action to be
540 taken by the Commission; and
541 (B) Provide remedial training and specific technical assistance regarding
542 the default.
543 (2) If a state in default fails to cure the default, the defaulting state’s membership
544 in this Compact may be terminated upon an affirmative vote of a majority of the administrators,
545 and all rights, privileges, and benefits conferred by this Compact may be terminated on the
546 effective date of termination. A cure of the default does not relieve the offending state of
547 obligations or liabilities incurred during the period of default.
548 (3) Termination of membership in this Compact shall be imposed only after all
549 other means of securing compliance have been exhausted. Notice of intent to suspend or
550 terminate shall be given by the Commission to the governor of the defaulting state and to the
551 executive officer of the defaulting state’s licensing board and each of the party states.
552 (4) A state whose membership in this Compact has been terminated is responsible
553 for all assessments, obligations and liabilities incurred through the effective date of termination,
554 including obligations that extend beyond the effective date of termination.
555 (5) The Commission shall not bear any costs related to a state that is found to be
556 in default or whose membership in this Compact has been terminated unless agreed upon in
557 writing between the Commission and the defaulting state.

558 (6) The defaulting state may appeal the action of the Commission by petitioning
559 the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia or the federal district in which the
560 Commission has its principal offices. The prevailing party shall be awarded all costs of such
561 litigation, including reasonable attorneys’ fees.
562 (c) Dispute resolution:
563 (1) Upon request by a party state, the Commission shall attempt to resolve
564 disputes related to the Compact that arise among party states and between party and non-party
565 states.

567 binding dispute resolution for disputes, as appropriate.
568 (3) In the event the Commission cannot resolve disputes among party states
569 arising under this Compact:

572 the affected party states and an individual mutually agreed upon by the Compact administrators
573 of all the party states involved in the dispute.
574 (B) The decision of a majority of the arbitrators shall be final and binding.
575 (d) Enforcement:
576 (1) The Commission, in the reasonable exercise of its discretion, shall enforce the
577 provisions and rules of this Compact.
578 (2) By majority vote, the Commission may initiate legal action in the U.S. District
579 Court for the District of Columbia or the federal district in which the Commission has its
580 principal offices against a party state that is in default to enforce compliance with the provisions

581 of this Compact and its promulgated rules and bylaws. The relief sought may include both

583 shall be awarded all costs of such litigation, including reasonable attorneys’ fees.
