14 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to extend the deadline for DC
15 housing providers to register their respective rental properties in the new DC
16 RentRegistry, and to require owners of rental accommodations that are exempt from rent
17 stabilization to report current and prior rent amounts for the previous two calendar years
18 at the time of registration.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 resolution may be cited as the “Rental Housing Registration Extension Emergency Declaration
22 Resolution of 2025”.
23 Sec. 2. (a) On June 2nd, 2025, the new DC RentRegistry will go live, and housing
24 providers will be required to submit all documentation under § 42–3502.03c of the Code of the
25 District of Columbia within 90 days of the database becoming accessible to housing providers.
26 Whereas housing providers are required to enter additional information into DHCD’s online
27 database within 90 days, more time is needed to mitigate administrative burdens and ensure that
28 housing providers can provide this documentation in compliance with statute.
29 (b)Within 90 days after completion of the publicly accessible rent control housing
30 database created pursuant to §42-3502.03c, section (f)(1) of §42–3502.05 requires housing
31 providers to file a new registration statement and, if applicable, a new claim of exemption for
32 housing accommodation for which the housing provider is receiving rent or is entitled to receive
33 rent via the online housing provider portal developed pursuant to §42-3502.03c(e-1);

34 (c) The Rental Accommodations Division has been informed by housing industry
35 stakeholders that housing providers do not understand the new requirements and need additional
36 time to ensure compliance;
37 (d) Some housing providers of exempt rental accommodations have expressed concern
38 that the Rent Administrator lacks the authority to require submission of current rent and rent
39 amounts from the previous two calendar years, and argue that this requirement is burdensome;
40 however, this rent history data is essential to providing a comprehensive snapshot of the
41 District’s rental market and will only be required at the time of registration—not on a recurring
42 basis. Clarifying this requirement on an emergency basis will reduce confusion, strengthen legal
43 authority for its collection, and support full functionality of the RentRegistry system at launch;
44 (e) With the amount of information required for entry into the RentRegistry, housing
45 providers will need more than 90 days to adjust their business practices and staffing needs to
46 ensure all requirements under §42–3502.03c are met;
47 (f) Given that the RentRegistry will not contain any information previously submitted to
48 the Rental Accommodations Division prior to June 2, 2025, housing providers, especially those
49 operating larger or multiple properties, will need additional time to compile and enter all the
50 information required under information §42–3502.03c.
51 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
52 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
53 Housing Provider RentRegistry Registration Deadline Extension Emergency Declaration
54 Resolution of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
55 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
