Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration by the Council of the District of Columbia is the "Cybersecurity and
Accountability Act of 2025" ("Bill"). The purpose of the Bill is to establish standards for data
security and standards for investigating and notifying the Commissioner of the Department of
Insurance, Securities and Banking of cybersecurity events affecting insurance licensees.
The proposed legislation would establish standards for data security for licensees of the
Department of Insurance, Securities and Banking; establish standards for investigating and
notifying the Commissioner of cybersecurity events affecting these licensees; and establish
standards for security measures to protect all nonpublic information being transmitted over an
external network and or stored on a laptop computer or other portable computing or storage
device or media.
Adopting these nationally accepted data standards for cybersecurity will serve the goals of
maintaining transparency among insurance licensees in the District and providing robust
consumer protection.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the Bill.

28 (4 ) "Cybersecurity event" means an event resulting in unauthorized access to or
29 disruption or misuse of an information system or nonpublic information stored on the
30 Information System. "Cybersecurity event" does not include:

31 (A) The unauthorized acquisition of encrypted nonpublic information if
32 the encryption, process, or key is not also acquired, released, or used without authorization; or
33 (B) An event where the licensee has determined that the nonpublic
34 information accessed by an unauthorized person has not been used or released and has been

37 (6) “Encrypted” means the transformation of data into a form which results in a
38 low probability of assigning meaning to the data without the use of a protective process or key.
39 (7) “Information security program” means the administrative, technical, and
40 physical safeguards that a licensee uses to access, collect, distribute, process, protect, store, use,

101 Sec. 3. Information security program.
102 (a) Commensurate with the size and complexity of the licensee, the nature and scope of
103 the licensee’s activities, including its use of third-party service providers, and the sensitivity of
104 the nonpublic information used by the licensee or in the licensee’s possession, custody, or
105 control, each licensee shall develop, implement, and maintain a comprehensive written
106 information security program based on the licensee’s risk assessment. The information security
107 program shall contain administrative, technical, and physical safeguards for the protection of
108 nonpublic information and the licensee’s information system.
109 (b) A licensee’s information security program shall be designed to:
110 (1) Protect the security and confidentiality of nonpublic information and the
111 security of the information system;
112 (2) Protect against any threats or hazards to the security or integrity of nonpublic
113 information and the information system;
114 (3) Protect against unauthorized access to or use of nonpublic information, and
115 minimize the likelihood of harm to any consumer; and
116 (4) Define and periodically reevaluate a schedule for retention of nonpublic
117 information and a mechanism for its destruction when no longer needed.
118 (c) The Commissioner, by rule, shall establish guidance setting forth the standards and
119 practices that must be incorporated in a licensee’s information security program, including risk
120 assessments, the role of the board of directors, oversight of third-party service providers, and the
121 requirement for written incident response plans.
5

122 (1) For risk assessment, notwithstanding the Commissioner’s guidance setting
123 forth standards and practices, the licensee shall:
124 (A) Designate one or more employees, an affiliate, or an outside vendor
125 designated to act on behalf of the licensee who is responsible for the information security
126 Program;
127 (B) Identify reasonably foreseeable internal or external threats that could
128 result in unauthorized access, transmission, disclosure, misuse, alteration, or destruction of
129 nonpublic information, including the security of information systems and nonpublic information
130 that are accessible to, or held by, third-party service providers;
131 (C) Assess the likelihood and potential damage of these threats, taking into
132 consideration the sensitivity of the nonpublic information;
133 (D) Assess the sufficiency of policies, procedures, information systems,
134 and other safeguards in place to manage these threats, including consideration of threats in each
135 relevant area of the licensee’s operations, including:
136 (i) Employee training and management;
137 (ii) Information systems, including network and software design,
138 information classification, governance, processing, storage, transmission, and disposal; and
139 (iii) Detecting, preventing, and responding to attacks, intrusions, or
140 other systems failures; and
141 (E) Implement information safeguards to manage the threats identified in
142 ongoing assessment, and, no less than annually, assess the effectiveness of the safeguards’ key
143 controls, systems, and procedures.
6

144 (2) For risk management, notwithstanding the Commissioner’s guidance setting
145 forth standards and practices, the licensee shall:
146 (A) Design its information security program to mitigate the identified
147 risks, commensurate with the size and complexity of the licensee’s activities, including it use of
148 third-party service providers, and the sensitivity of the nonpublic information used by the
149 licensee or in the licensee’s possession, custody, or control.
150 (B) Determine which of the following security measures are appropriate
151 and implement such security measures:
152 (i) Place access controls on information systems, including
153 controls to authenticate and permit access only by authorized individuals to protect against the
154 unauthorized acquisition of nonpublic information;
155 (ii) Identify and manage the data, personnel, devices, systems, and
156 facilities that enable the organization to achieve business purposes in accordance with their
157 relative importance to business objectives and the organization’s risk strategy;
158 (iii) Restrict access at physical locations containing nonpublic
159 information only to authorized individuals;
160 (iv) Protect by encryption or other appropriate means all nonpublic
161 information while being transmitted over an external network and all nonpublic information
162 stored on a laptop computer or other portable computing or storage device or media;
163 (v) Adopt secure development practices for in-house developed
164 applications utilized by the licensee and procedures for evaluating, assessing, or testing the
165 security of externally developed applications utilized by the licensee;
7

166 (vi) Modify the information system in accordance with the
167 licensee’s information security program;
168 (vii) Utilize effective controls which may include multi-factor
169 authentication procedures for any individual accessing nonpublic information;
170 (viii) Regularly test and monitor systems and procedures to detect
171 actual and attempted attacks on or intrusion into information systems;
172 (ix) Include audit trails within the information security program
173 designed to detect and respond to cybersecurity events and designed to reconstruct material
174 financial transactions sufficient to support normal operations and obligations of the licensee;
175 (x) Implement measures to protect against destruction, loss, or
176 damage of nonpublic information due to environmental hazards, such as fire and water damage
177 or other catastrophes or technological failures; and
178 (xi) Develop, implement, and maintain procedures for the secure
179 disposal of nonpublic information in any format.
180 (C) Include cybersecurity risks in the licensee’s enterprise risk
181 management process.
182 (D) Stay informed regarding emerging threats or vulnerabilities and utilize
183 reasonable security measures when sharing information relative to the character of the sharing
184 and the type of information shared; and
185 (E) Provide personnel with cybersecurity awareness training that is
186 updated as necessary to reflect risks identified by the licensee in the risk assessment.
187 (d) Annually, each insurer domiciled in the District shall, by February 15, submit a
188 written statement to the Commissioner certifying that the insurer is in compliance with the
8

189 requirements of this section. Each insurer shall maintain for examination by the Department all
190 records, schedules, and data supporting this certificate for a period of 5 years. To the extent an
191 insurer has identified areas, systems, or processes that require material improvement, updating,
192 or redesign, the insurer shall document the identification and the remedial efforts planned and
193 underway to address those areas, systems, or processes. The documentation shall be available for
194 inspection by the Commissioner.
195 Sec. 4. Investigation of a cybersecurity event.
196 (a) If the licensee learns that a cybersecurity event has or may have occurred, the licensee
197 or an outside vendor or service provider designated to act on behalf of the licensee shall conduct
198 a prompt investigation.
199 (b) During the investigation, the licensee or the outside vendor or service provider shall,
200 at a minimum and to the extent possible:
201 (1) Determine whether a cybersecurity event has occurred;
202 (2) Assess the nature and scope of the cybersecurity event;
203 (3) Identify any nonpublic information that may have been involved in the
204 cybersecurity event; and
205 (4) Perform or oversee reasonable measures to restore the security of the
206 information systems compromised in the cybersecurity event to prevent further unauthorized
207 acquisition, release, or use of nonpublic information in the licensee’s possession, custody, or
208 control.
209 (c) If the licensee learns that a cybersecurity event has or may have occurred in a system
210 maintained by a third-party service provider, the licensee shall complete the steps listed in
9

211 subsection (b) of this section or confirm and document that the third-party service provider has
212 completed those steps.
213 (d)The licensee shall maintain records concerning all cybersecurity events for a period of
214 at least 5 years from the date of the cybersecurity event and shall produce those records upon the
215 Commissioner’s request.
216 Sec. 5. Notification of a cybersecurity event.
217 (a)Each licensee shall notify the Commissioner as promptly as possible, but in no event
218 later than 3 business days after a determination that a cybersecurity event has occurred, when
219 either of the following criteria has been met:
220 (1)The District is the licensee’s jurisdiction of domicile in the case of an insurer
221 or the District is the licensee’s home jurisdiction in the case of a producer as those terms are
222 defined in the Producer Licensing Act of 2002, effective March 27, 2003 (D.C. Law 14-264;
223 D.C. Official Code § 31-1131 et seq.); or
224 (2)The licensee reasonably believes that the nonpublic information involves 250
225 or more consumers residing in the District and the cybersecurity event is either:
226 (A)A cybersecurity event that requires notice to any government body,
227 self-regulatory agency, or any other supervisory body pursuant to any state or federal law; or
228 (B)A cybersecurity event that has a reasonable likelihood of materially
229 harming:
230 (i)Any consumer residing in the District; or
231 (ii)Any material part of the normal operations of the licensee.
10

232 (b) The Commissioner, by rule, shall establish guidance addressing the form and contents
233 of the information that shall be included in any initial, updated, or supplemental notification to
234 the Commissioner concerning the cybersecurity event.
235 (c) A Licensee shall comply with the Consumer Personal Information Security Breach
236 Notification Act of 2006, effective March 8, 2007 (D.C. Law 16-237; D.C. Official Code § 28-
237 3852), as applicable, and, when a licensee is required to notify the Commissioner under
238 subsection (a) of this section, the licensee shall provide a copy of the notice sent to consumers
239 under that statute to the Commissioner.
240 (d) When a cybersecurity event occurs in a system maintained by a third-party service
241 provider:
242 (1) The licensee shall treat the event as it would under subsection (a) of this
243 section;
244 (2) The computation of the licensee’s deadlines shall begin on the day after the
245 third-party service provider notifies the licensee of the cybersecurity event or the licensee
246 otherwise has actual knowledge of the cybersecurity event, whichever is sooner; and
247 (3) Nothing in this act shall prevent or abrogate an agreement between a licensee
248 and another licensee, a third-party service provider, or any other party to fulfill any of the
249 investigation requirements imposed under section 4 of this act or the notice requirements
250 imposed under this section.
251 (e) With respect to reinsurers’ notice regarding cybersecurity events to insurers:
252 (1)(A) In the case of a cybersecurity event involving nonpublic information that is
253 used by or is in the possession, custody, or control of a licensee that is acting as an assuming
254 insurer and that does not have a direct contractual relationship with the affected consumers, the
11

255 assuming insurer shall notify its affected ceding insurers and the Commissioner of its jurisdiction
256 of domicile within 3 business days of determining that a cybersecurity event has occurred; and
257 (B) The ceding insurers that have a direct contractual relationship with
258 affected Consumers shall fulfill the consumer notification requirements imposed under the
259 Consumer Personal Information Security Breach Notification Act of 2006, effective March 8,
260 2007 (D.C. Law 16-237; D.C. Official Code § 28-3852), and any other notification requirements
261 relating to a cybersecurity event imposed under this section.
262 (2)(A) In the case of a cybersecurity event involving nonpublic information that is
263 in the possession, custody, or control of a third-party service provider of a licensee that is an
264 assuming insurer, the assuming insurer shall notify its affected ceding insurers and the
265 Commissioner of its jurisdiction of domicile within three 3 business days of receiving notice
266 from its third-party service provider that a cybersecurity event has occurred;
267 (B) The ceding insurers that have a direct contractual relationship with
268 affected consumers shall fulfill the consumer notification requirements imposed under the
269 Consumer Personal Information Security Breach Notification Act of 2006, effective March 8,
270 2007 (D.C. Law 16-237; D.C. Official Code § 28-3852), and any other notification requirements
271 relating to a cybersecurity event imposed under this section.
272 (f) In the case of a cybersecurity event involving nonpublic information that is in the
273 possession, custody, or control of a licensee that is an insurer or its third-party service provider
274 for which a consumer accessed the insurer’s services through an independent insurance producer,
275 the insurer shall notify the producers of record of all affected consumers as soon as practicable as
276 directed by the Commissioner. The licensee is excused from the obligation to provide notice to
12

277 individual consumers where the licensee does not have the current producer of record
278 information for those consumers.

331 Sec. 8. Exemptions.
332 (a) The following individuals and entities shall be exempt from the requirements of
333 section 3 of this Act as follows:
334 (1) A licensee with fewer than 10 employees, including any independent
335 contractors, shall be exempt from section 3;
336 (2) A licensee subject to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act,
337 approved August 21, 1996 (Pub. L. 104-191; 110 Stat. 1936), that has established and maintains
338 an information security program pursuant to that statute and the rules, regulations, procedures, or
339 guidelines established thereunder shall be considered to meet the requirements of section 3,
340 provided that the licensee is compliant with and submits a written statement certifying its
341 compliance with those requirements;
342 (3) A licensee having less than $5,000,000 in annual written premiums in each of
343 the last 3 calendar years from District of Columbia business operations shall be exempt from
344 section 3;
15

345 (4) A licensee having less than $10,000,000 in year-end admitted assets,
346 calculated in accordance with statutory accounting principles, including admitted assets of all
347 affiliates shall be exempt from section 3;
348 (5) An employee, agent, representative, or designee of a licensee, who is also a
349 licensee, is exempt from section 3 and need not develop its own information security program to
350 the extent that the employee, agent, representative, or designee is covered by the information
351 security program of the other licensee.
352 (b) A company claiming an exemption under subsection (a) of this section shall file a
353 request for an exemption in accordance with the rules prescribed by the Commissioner.
354 (c) In the event that a licensee ceases to qualify for an exception, the licensee shall have
355 180 days to comply with this act.
356 Sec. 9. Penalties.
357 Any insurer found, without just cause as defined by the Commissioner by rule, to be in
358 violation of this act, after notice and hearing conducted according to the rules for contested cases
359 set forth in Chapter 38 of Title 26A of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations, shall pay
360 a penalty in an amount not to exceed $1,000 per day. The maximum penalty assessed under this
361 section shall be $25,000. The Commissioner shall recover this penalty.
