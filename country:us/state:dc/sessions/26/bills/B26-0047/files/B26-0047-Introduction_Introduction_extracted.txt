1

35 (1) Paragraph (2) is repealed.
36 (2) New paragraphs (7A) and (7B) are added to read as follows:
37 “(7A) “Embalmer” means any person engaged in the practice of embalming.
38 “(7B) “Embalming” means the process of chemically treating the dead human
39 body by arterial injection and cavity treatment or, when necessary, hypodermic tissue injection to
40 reduce the presence and growth of microorganisms to temporarily retard organic
41 decomposition.”.
42 (3) A new paragraph (8A) is added to read as follows:
43 “(8A) “Funeral directing” means the for-profit profession of directing or
44 supervising funerals, preparing human dead for burial by means other than embalming, or
45 making arrangements for funeral services or the financing of funeral services.”.
46 (4) Paragraph (9) is amended to read as follows:
47 “(9) “Funeral director” means any person licensed by the District to perform the
48 practice of funeral directing.”.
49 (5) Paragraph (14) is amended to read as follows:
50 “(14) “License” means an authority from the District which entitles the holder to
51 practice in the District as a funeral director or an embalmer, or an authority from the District
52 which entitles the holder to own and operate a funeral services establishment.”.
53 (6) Paragraph (18) is amended to read as follows:
54 “(18) “Practice of funeral services” means engaging in the care and disposition of
55 the human dead, the preparation of the human dead for the funeral service, burial or cremation,
56 the making of arrangements for the funeral service or for the financing of the funeral service and
57 the selling or making of financial arrangements for the sale of funeral supplies to the public.”.

136 (g) Section 8 (D.C. Official Code § 3–407) is amended to read as follows:
137 “§ 3–407. Internships.
138 “(a)(1) A person desiring to become a funeral director or embalmer shall serve as an
139 intern under the direct supervision of a funeral director or embalmer licensed in the District.
140 “(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1) of this subsection, an intern who is required to
141 serve a 2-year minimum internship may serve up to 1 year of the internship outside of the
142 District, if the period of internship served outside of the District is served under the supervision
143 of an individual who has passed a national approved examination for the internship in question.
144 The Mayor shall issue rules and regulations to implement this subsection pursuant to § 3-420.
145 “(b) The applicant shall furnish proof that he or she is:
146 “(1) Is at least 18 years of age;
147 “(2) Holds a high school diploma or its equivalent, or higher.
6

148 “(c) An internship for funeral directing or embalming shall consist of at least 2,000 hours
149 of training to be completed in no more than 2 years; provided, that an internship may be
150 extended, for good cause, to a total period of no more than 5 years.
151 “(d) A funeral directing intern shall receive training in all areas of funeral directing,
152 including assisting at least 25 funerals, 25 arrangement conferences, as well as visitations and
153 financing of funeral services.
154 “(e) An embalming intern shall receive training in all aspects of embalming practice,
155 including assisting in at least 25 embalmings, as well as treatment, restorative art, safety and
156 sanitation, and organ, tissue, or anatomical donation.
157 “(f)(1) The intern and the licensee supervising the intern shall submit a written report at
158 the end of every 1,000 hours of training that specifies the period of time in which the 1,000 hours
159 has been completed and verify that the intern has actually served in the required capacity during
160 the preceding period. The report shall be submitted no more than 30 days following the
161 completion of 1,000 hours.
162 “(2) An intern shall not receive credit for training hours on a new 1,000-hour
163 report until the previous 1,000-hour report has been approved.
164 “(g) Every intern employed pursuant to this section shall, within 5 days after terminating
165 his or her employment, notify the Mayor of the termination, indicating the date on which
166 employment ceased.
167 “(h) Every intern whose employment under the supervision of a funeral director or
168 embalmer is terminated shall, immediately upon being employed to work under the supervision
169 of another funeral director or embalmer, notify the Mayor of the change of employment,
7

170 indicating the name, address, and license number of the funeral director or embalmer under
171 whose supervision the intern is continuing his or her internship.
172 “(i) A funeral director or embalmer shall, upon employing an intern or terminating the
173 employment of an intern, notify the Mayor in writing accordingly. The notification shall contain
174 the name, address, and license number of the intern, as well as the date on which the intern was
175 employed or terminated.
176 “(j) The Mayor shall issue rules and regulations, including the establishment of fees for
177 internship applications, to implement this section pursuant to § 3-420.”.
