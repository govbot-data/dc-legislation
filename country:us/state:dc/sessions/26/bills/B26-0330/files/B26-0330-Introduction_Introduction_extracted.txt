Dear Secretary Howard,
Today, I am introducing the “Green’s Court Park Designation Act of 2025.” Please find enclosed
a signed copy of the legislation.
This legislation would symbolically designate the playground and dog park at 26th Street NW and
I Street NW, in Ward 2, as “Green’s Court Park.” This park makes up a portion within Square 5
in the Foggy Bottom neighborhood, which is bounded by 26th Street NW to the east, 27th Street
NW to the west, K Street NW to north, and I Street NW to the south.1 The Foggy Bottom
Association (FBA) voted on various naming options for the park with “Green’s Court Park” being
the overwhelming favorite, winning 68.8% of the 94 votes. This name was chosen by the FBA in
recognition of the contributions of Foggy Bottom’s working-class residents and businesses to the
District and to dedicate the park in honor of the historic Green’s Court that was previously located
in this area.
The land where the renovated playground and dog park now stand and the surrounding area was,
in the late 19th century until the middle of the 20th century, an alley dwelling known as “Green’s
Court.”2 Green’s Court was home to individuals such as Thomas Green.3 Thomas Green was an
Irish-born laborer with a wife and three sons who owned property within Green’s Court in the
1880s and sold it before the 1890 census.4 In the 19th century, Green’s Court was used by a
multitude of people with professions such as laborers, shoemakers, whitewashers, and teamsters.5
Green’s Court was primarily home to African American families as well as some Irish American
families.6 During the 1960s, homes within the court and in Square 5 were demolished for the
Potomac Freeway construction, leaving only 3 homes remaining in Square 5.7 Within the Foggy
