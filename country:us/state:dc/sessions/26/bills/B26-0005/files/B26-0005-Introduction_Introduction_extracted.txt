8 To amend, on an emergency basis due to congressional review, the Residential Tranquility Act of
9 2010 to prohibit persons targeting a residence for purposes of a demonstration from using
10 sound amplifying devices in a residential zone between 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m., and to
11 prohibit a person from launching or throwing a projectile onto the residential property of
12 another with the intent to cause fear, intimidate, or retaliate against any person living or
13 working at that residence; and to amend the National Capital Revitalization and Self-
14 Government Improvement Act of 1997 to provide that the term expiration date for a
15 Council appointment to the Corrections Information Council shall be December 7th of
16 the year in which the term expires.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
19 be cited as the “Residential Tranquility Congressional Review Emergency Amendment
20 Act of 2025”.
21
22 Sec. 2. The Residential Tranquility Act of 2010, effective May 26, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-
23 374; D.C. Official Code § 22-2751 et seq.), is amended as follows:
24 (a) Section 2 (D.C. Official Code § 22-2751) is amended by adding a new paragraph (4)
25 to read as follows:
26 “(4) “Sound amplifying device” means any machine, device, or loudspeaker that
27 amplifies sound of the human voice, music, or any other sound. The term “sound amplifying
28 device” shall not include automobile audio systems when used and heard only by the occupants
29 of the vehicle in which the automobile audio system is installed, personal hearing aids, or
30 headphones.”.
31 (b) Section 3 (D.C. Official Code § 22-2752) is amended by adding new subsections (a-1)
32 and (a-2) to read as follows:

33 “(a-1) It is unlawful for a person to use a sound amplifying device to target a residence
34 for purposes of a demonstration between 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m. in Residential Zones,
35 Residential Flat Zones, or Residential Apartment Zones, as those terms are defined in the District
36 of Columbia Zoning Regulations.
37 “(a-2) It is unlawful to continue or resume targeting a residence in violation of this law
38 after being instructed by a law enforcement officer to cease targeting a residence in violation of
39 this law.”.
40 (c) A new section 3a is added to read as follows:
41 “Sec. 3a. Throwing projectiles at residential property.
42 “(a) It is unlawful for any person to launch or throw a projectile onto the residential
43 property of another with the intent to cause fear, intimidate, or retaliate against any person living
44 or working at that residence.
45 “(b) A person who violates this section, upon conviction, shall be fined no more than the
46 amount set forth in section 101 of the Criminal Fine Proportionality Amendment Act of 2012,
47 effective June 11, 2013 (D.C. Law 19-317; D.C. Official Code § 22-3571.01), or incarcerated for
48 no more than 90 days, or both.”.
49 Sec. 3. Section 11201a(b)(2) of the National Capital Revitalization and Self-Government
50 Improvement Act of 1997, effective October 2, 2010 (D.C. Law 18-233; D.C. Official Code §
51 24-101.01(b)(2)), is amended by adding a new subparagraph (B-1) to read as follows:
52 “(B-1) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the term expiration date for a Council
53 appointment to the Corrections Information Council shall be December 7th of the year in which
54 the term expires.”.
55 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.

56 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
57 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
58 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
59 Sec. 5. Effective date.
60 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
61 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), and shall remain in effect for no longer than
62 90 days, as provided for emergency acts of the Council of the District of Columbia in section
63 412(a) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 788;
64 D.C. Official Code § 1-204.12(a)).
