11 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
12
13 _________
14
15 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
16
17 ____________
18
19 To recognize, honor, and celebrate the life of Marvin “Slush” Gross for his contributions to the
20 Barry Farm community, the District of Columbia, Go-Go culture, and the global dance
21 scene.
22
23 WHEREAS, Marvin “Slush” Gross was a native Washingtonian whose life embodied the
24 strength, spirit, and cultural fabric of the District;
25
26 WHEREAS, Mr. Gross was not only a beloved neighbor and friend, but also a cultural
27 cornerstone in his community, known for his generous spirit, his sharp wit, and his unwavering
28 dedication to the people and neighborhoods that shaped him;
29
30 WHEREAS, Mr. Gross was born on June 13, 1982, and grew up in the Barry Farm
31 community in Southeast DC;
32
33 WHEREAS, Mr. Gross danced from the day he was born, catalyzing a new era of dance
34 within the Go-Go world that resonated within his neighborhood and beyond and teaching his
35 brothers, sisters, friends, and classmates his signature moves;
36
37 WHEREAS, Mr. Gross attended Anacostia High School, and during his time there, other
38 students dance battled across the school imitating Mr. Gross’s moves and saying they were
39 “doing the Slush”;
40
41 WHEREAS, Mr. Gross was a visionary artist and founder of Beat Ya Feet, a uniquely DC
42 dance style that evolved out of a DC dance called the Hee Haw that quickly became a defining
43 expression of DC’s Go-Go music culture starting in the late 1990s;
44

45 WHEREAS, Beat Ya Feet gained national recognition across platforms like the America’s
46 Best Dance Crew show and continues to influence people overseas, through crews and artists
47 like the Beat Ya Feet Kings and Crazy Legz;
48
49 WHEREAS, through his love of dance, Mr. Gross created space for joy, movement, and
50 connection, helping young people find identity and pride in the District’s rhythm and style and
51 lifting up a grassroots art form that continues to resonate across generations and on the
52 international stage;
53
54 WHEREAS, Mr. Gross lost his life on his way home from a Go-Go on Saturday, July 27,
55 2002, but his legacy lives on through the Beat Ya Feet dance and his contributions to the culture.
56
57 WHEREAS, to honor Mr. Gross’s legacy, the Council will review legislation that would
58 symbolically designate a portion of Sumner Road, S.E., as “Marvin “Slush” Gross Way”.
59
60 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
61 resolution may be cited as the “Marvin “Slush” Gross Posthumous Recognition Resolution of
62 2025”.
63
64 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes Marvin “Slush” Gross for his
65 contributions to the global dance scene and lasting legacy on the District of Columbia.
66
67 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication.
