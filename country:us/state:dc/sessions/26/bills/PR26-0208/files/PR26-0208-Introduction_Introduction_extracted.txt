Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is the
Subcontracting Requirements Exemption for Contracts for Health Benefits for District Employees
Emergency Amendment Act of 2025, along with the accompanying emergency declaration
resolution, the Subcontracting Requirements Exemption for Contracts for Health Benefits for
District Employees Temporary Amendment Act of 2025, and the Subcontracting Requirements
Exemption for Contracts for Health Benefits for District Employees Amendment Act of 2025.
The legislation adds a new subsection (d)(4) to D.C. Official Code§ 2-218.46 to extend the time
in which compliant subcontracting plans for contracts for health benefits to District employees and
their families shall be provided.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action at your earliest convenience.

15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Small and
16 Certified Business Enterprise Development and Assistance Act of 2005 to extend the
17 time in which compliant subcontracting plans for contracts for health benefits for District
18 employees and their families shall be provided.
19
20 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 resolution may be cited as the "Subcontracting Requirements Exemption for Contracts for Health
22 Benefits for District Employees Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025".
23 Sec. 2. (a) There exists an immediate need to amend the Small and Certified Business
24 Enterprise Development and Assistance Act of 2005, effective October 20, 2005 (D.C. Law 16-
25 33; D.C. Official Code§ 2-218.01 et seq.) (the "CBE Act"), to extend the deadline for provision
26 of a subcontracting plan for contracts for health benefits for District employees and their families
27 pursuant to section 2346(d) of the CBE Act.
28 (b) Section 2346 of the CBE Act (D.C. Official Code§ 2-218.46) requires that, for all
29 District contracts in excess of $250,000, a bid or proposal responding to a solicitation include a
30 subcontracting plan detailing how the prime contracting will subcontract at least 35% of the
31 dollar value of the contract to small or certified business enterprises unless the Director of the
32 Department of Small and Local Business Development grants a waiver of those requirements.
1

33 (c) The Contract Appeal Board’s decision in Conduent State Healthcare, LLC, CAB No.
34 P-1120 (Aug. 20, 2020) (“Conduent”), held that contract proposals shall be considered non-
35 responsive and must be rejected if the vendor fails to submit a compliant subcontracting plan.
36 The CAB’s approach was later codified by Council at D.C. Official Code § 2-218.46(d)(1).
37 (d) The Department of Human Resources (“DCHR”) administers the Employees Health
38 Benefits Program (“EHBP”), which provides District employees and their families with a
39 comprehensive selection of healthcare options.
40 (e) EHBP procurements are unlike other District procurements. The solicitation’s price
41 schedule is based on enrollment estimates for all District employees for each plan type. After
42 proposals are submitted, DCHR adjusts the estimates downward to reflect DCHR’s estimates of
43 enrollment for each proposed contractor, but these are only estimates. Each contractor’s actual
44 enrollment numbers will not be known until after open enrollment closes in December of each
45 year. Proposed premium rates may also change during discussions with the offerors before final
46 award. This process makes it impossible for offerors to submit with their initial proposal
47 compliant subcontracting plans based on an amount that accurately reflects the eventual award
48 amount, making each of their proposals subject to rejection under the CBE Act, as interpreted by
49 the Contract Appeals Board in Conduent.
50 (f) Efforts to comply with existing mandatory subcontracting requirements have delayed
51 procurements for new contracts for these vital services.
52 (g) Emergency legislation is necessary to amend the CBE Act to extend the deadline for
53 bidders to submit subcontracting plans for contracts for health benefits for District employees
54 and their families shall be provided to more accurately reflect the contracting process for these
2

55 contract types while retaining the requirement for these contractors to subcontract out work
56 under these contracts pursuant to the CBE Act .
57 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
58 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
59 Subcontracting Requirements Exemption for Contracts for Health Benefits for District
60 Employees Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
61 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
