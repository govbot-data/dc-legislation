IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
_________________________
1 To amend Chapter 39 of Title 28 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify the
2 application of the Consumer Protection Procedures Act and provide enhanced remedies
3 to secure compliance, to authorize fines, costs, and fees for failure to fully comply with
4 an investigative subpoena, to clarify that that Federal Trade Commission precedents
5 should be interpreted in a manner most favorable to consumers, to grant additional
6 consumer protection enforcement authority to the Department of Licensing and
7 Consumer Protection, to clarify that unlawful and abusive trade practices are prohibited,
8 to clarify complaint procedures, to prohibit retaliation for consumer complaints and
9 aiding and abetting prohibited trade practices, to expressly authorize asset freezes as
10 injunctive relief, to set mandatory minimum penalties for violations and recovery of costs
11 and fees for enforcement and investigative actions in Attorney General actions, to require
12 confidentiality agreements and protective orders entered into by the Attorney General to
13 permit sharing confidential documents with law enforcement agencies abiding by the
14 agreement or protective order, to authorize the use of interrogatories in investigations, to
15 specify that service may be made by any method reasonably calculated to give actual
16 notice, to allow use of sworn investigative statements for perjury prosecutions, and to
17 allow the Mayor to issue rules concerning the Department of Consumer and Regulatory
18 Affairs and the Attorney General to issue any other rules necessary to carry out the
19 chapter; to amend the Attorney General for the District of Columbia Clarification and
20 Elected Term Amendment Act of 2010 to authorize the use of interrogatories in
21 investigations; and to amend the Criminal Abuse and Neglect of Vulnerable Adults Act
22 of 2000 to clarify that injunctive relief may include affirmative action.
23
24
25 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
26 act may be cited as the “Enhancing Consumer Protection Procedures Amendment Act of 2025”.
