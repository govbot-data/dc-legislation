9 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
10 ____________________
11
12 To declare an emergency with respect to the need to amend, on an emergency basis, the the
13 District of Columbia Housing Authority Act of 1999 to revise the structure of the board
14 of the Authority, provide flexibility for board and executive director training, and update
15 the public housing resident bill of rights; to amend the District of Columbia Government
16 Comprehensive Merit Personnel Act of 1978 to adjust Housing Authority board stipends;
17 to amend the Confirmation Act of 1978 to conform terminology regarding the Housing
18 Authority board; and to repeal superseded emergency and temporary legislation.
19
20 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 resolution may be cited as the “Housing Authority Resident Empowerment Emergency
22 Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
23 Sec. 2. (a) The District of Columbia Housing Authority (DCHA) helps tens of thousands
24 of District residents with low incomes afford housing through supports such as Local Rent
25 Supplement Program vouchers, federal Housing Choice Vouchers, and public housing.
26 (b) In December 2022, in response to a crisis of operational and organizational
27 deficiencies, the Council and Mayor collaborated on legislation to establish a temporary
28 Stabilization and Reform Board (“STAR Board”) of professional subject matter experts and
29 housing benefit recipient lived experience experts to guide the agency in the initial stages of its
30 recovery.
31 (c)The STAR Board held its first meeting on January 25, 2023. It has since overseen
32 major DCHA policy overhauls, the hiring of an experienced new Executive Director and
33 executive leadership team, the development of a formal recovery plan, and other initiatives.

34 (d) The initial District of Columba Housing Authority Stabilization and Reform
35 Emergency Amendment Act of 2022, effective December 22, 2022 (D.C. Act 24-702),
36 contemplated that the STAR Board’s members would “serve for one term of 2 years or until the
37 Board sunsets; provided, that each such member may continue to serve until a successor board
38 assumes the responsibilities of” the STAR Board.
39 (e) The 2022 legislation also instructed the STAR Board to “[p]rovide recommendations
40 to the Mayor and the Council… for the structure of a successor Board of Directors.”
41 Accordingly, the STAR Board filed a report, the “District of Columbia Housing Authority
42 (DCHA) Stabilization and Reform (S.T.A.R.) Board of Commissioners Recommendation for the
43 DCHA successor Board,” available in the Council’s Legislative Information Management
44 System as RC25-207 (the “STAR Board Report”). Among other recommendations, the STAR
45 Board Report requested that the Council “[e]xtend the existing STAR Board through December
46 2025” to allow it to continue its reform and oversight work. Accordingly, the Council has
47 continued to maintain the STAR Board legislation in effect through a series of substantially
48 similar emergency and temporary bills, including most recently the District of Columbia
49 Housing Authority Stabilization and Reform Temporary Amendment Act of 2025, enacted July
50 7, 2025 (D.C. Act 26-100; 72 DCR 7676).
51 (f) Meanwhile, on March 3, 2025, Chairman Phil Mendelson introduced on behalf of
52 Mayor Muriel Bowser the Rebalancing Expectations for Neighbors, Tenants, and Landlords
53 (RENTAL) Act of 2025. Title XI of the RENTAL Act included a new permanent board structure
54 and other reforms for DCHA.
55 (g) On July 9, 2025, the Committee on Housing voted in favor of an updated version of
56 the RENTAL Act. The committee print reflects a carefully considered compromise based on

57 extensive input from the executive, DCHA leadership, residents, and legal experts. However, the
58 Committee of the Whole has elected to postpone first reading of the RENTAL Act such that the
59 Council will not have an opportunity to finalize the permanent legislation before the 2025
60 summer recess.
61 (h) Emergency legislation is necessary to ensure that DCHA and the executive have a
62 clear mandate to begin preparations for formal nominations and elections to the newly
63 constituted Board of Commissioners beginning this autumn.
64 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
65 enumerated in section 2 constitute an emergency making it necessary that the Housing Authority
66 Resident Empowerment Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
67 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
