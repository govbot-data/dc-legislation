(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The Government of the District of Columbia, Office of Contracting and Procurement, on behalf of the
Department of Human Services, is seeking to exercise the remaining option in year three for DC
Doors, Inc., to continue to provide Permanent Supportive Housing III (PSH3) program case
management services, utility assistance, and financial assistance to chronically homeless and other
highly vulnerable individuals and families experiencing homelessness.
The purpose of this program is to assist residents with histories of chronic homelessness in
transitioning from the street or temporary living situation into stable and permanent housing,
maintaining long-term housing, and achieving self-sufficiency. The success of the aforementioned
initiative will be achieved by referring and connecting participants to supportive services that will
address their barriers to obtaining and maintaining permanent housing and achieving an optimum level
of self-sufficiency.
(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:
The advertisement was issued in the Washington Times on April 8, 2022. The RFQ Doc575024 was
issued in the open market utilizing the Office of Contracting and Procurement’s Procurement
Automated Support System on April 11, 2022, with a closing date of May 17, 2022. The District
received a total of 28 submissions. Twenty-three of the 28 responses were deemed qualified, and the
participants were notified on June 23, 2022.
The responses were evaluated in accordance with the qualification criteria outlined in the solicitation.
The qualification evaluation factors were PSH3 design, case management, organizational capacity, and
past performance. DC Doors, Inc., met the qualifications indicated in the RFQ and has a satisfactory
rating, providing similar services through current and prior HCAs with the District.
The contracting officer awarded an HCA on July 1, 2022, after determining that DC Doors, Inc., was
qualified and responsible, and the proposed price was determined to be fair and reasonable.

14. Description of Amendment/Modification (Organized by UCF Section headings, including solicitation/contract subject matter where feasible.)
The purpose of this modification to the subject Human Care Agreement (HCA) identified in block 10A is as
follows:
1. Exercise the contract terms for the remainder of option period three. The total not-to-exceed amount of
option period three is hereby increased from $493,329.62 by $550,624.94 to $1,043,954.56. The period
of performance is from July 1, 2025 through June 30, 2026.
2. Delete in its entirety 29 DCMR Chapter 25 and 77 and Replace with 29 DCMR Chapters 25 and 74.
3. In accordance with HCA Section F.8 “Department of Labor Wage Determinations” the US
Department of Labor Wage Determination No. 2015-4281, Revision No. 34, dated July 8, 2025, is
incorporated by reference and available at: https://sam.gov/wage-determination/2015-4281/34.
4. In accordance with Section F.10 “Way to Work Amendment Act of 2006”, the District of Columbia
2024 Living Wage rate is adjusted to $17.50 per hour effective January 1, 2025 until June 30, 2025.
Effective July 1, 2025, the District’s Minimum Wage and Living Wage will increase to $17.95 per
hour. The 2025 Living Wage Notice and Fact Sheet are hereby incorporated by reference:2025
Living Wage Notice | (dc.gov) and 2025 Living Wage Fact Sheet | (dc.gov).

5. In accordance with Section J.9, Attachments, Mayor’s Order 2023-131; Notice of Prohibition of
Sexual Harassment in the Workplace: https://ocp.dc.gov/modules/file/icons/application-pdf.png.
