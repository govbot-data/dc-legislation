42 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
43 ______________
44
45
46 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
47
48 ____________________________
49
50 To honor the District’s Fire and Emergency Medical Services Department for its dedication to
51 and protection of the citizens and visitors of Washington, D.C., and for their valiant and
52 courageous service to the metropolitan area after the attacks of September 11, 2001
53
54 WHEREAS, on the occasion of the 25th Anniversary of the September 11, 2001 terrorist
55 attacks on United States, the Council of the District of Columbia honors the memory of the
56 nearly 3,000 people who perished from the attack, including twelve victims from the District;
57
58 WHEREAS, an exceptionally courageous force of first responders—including more than
59 100,000 firefighters, paramedics, rescue and recovery workers, and police officers across the
60 country—risked their lives that day to save the lives of others;
61
62 WHEREAS, the brave District Fire and EMS Department which, along with the
63 Arlington County Fire Department and other local fire agencies, helped with the Pentagon rescue
64 and recovery efforts on September 11, 2001;
65
66 WHEREAS, nationally, thousands of first responders have suffered adverse physical and
67 emotional effects in the twenty-five years since 2001 and, even now, are at significantly greater
68 risk for developing occupational cancers due to their exposure to chemicals and debris from the
69 attacks;
70
71 WHEREAS, the men and women who serve as first responders in the District of
72 Columbia carry out the extraordinary responsibility of protecting not only District residents, but
73 also all who visit and work here, and have always done so with tremendous dedication and care;
74
75 WHEREAS, the men and women of the District’s emergency services have fulfilled
76 every duty to the District and their country in an honorable, courageous, and timely fashion, and
77 have demonstrated immense compassion for those who have suffered unforeseeable tragedies,
78 while routinely considering the safety and well-being of others before their own; and
79
80 WHEREAS, it is fully right and just to honor the memory of those who lost their lives in
81 the terrorist attacks more than two decades ago, and it is equally compelling to observe and pay

82 our respects to the first responders of the District and their bravery and selflessness in the face of
83 extraordinarily trying circumstances.
84
85 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
86 resolution may be cited as the “September 11th Emergency and First Responders Remembrance
87 and Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
88
89 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia honors its first responders for their
90 tremendous commitment to serving the District and remembers emergency workers who
91 dutifully served their country in the face of danger on September 11, 2001.
92
93 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
