Dear Secretary Howard,
Today, along with Councilmembers Anita Bonds, Brianne Nadeau, Christina Henderson, Wendell
Felder, and Zachary Parker, I am introducing the “Teen Centers Establishment Amendment Act
of 2025.” This bill marks the creation of a critical initiative aimed at providing youth across the
District with safe and creative outlets that will also help them meet graduation and career-
preparation milestones by linking engagement in the centers to high-school graduation credits
when appropriate. This legislation establishes structured, expanded, teen-centered recreation
spaces that provide evidence-backed programming that supports youth development.
Across the District, many teens, particularly those from under-resourced communities, struggle to
find safe, enriching spaces after school and during weekends, holidays, and school breaks.
Research consistently shows that youth who lack access to structured recreational and mentoring
programs are more likely to experience negative academic and behavioral outcomes, including
disengagement from school and increased risk of justice system involvement.1
The Teen Center Establishment Amendment Act of 2025 seeks to directly address this gap by
requiring the Department of Parks and Recreation (“DPR”) to open and operate at least four
comprehensive teen centers—one in each quadrant of the District.
Each Teen Center established in the District will provide:
• Recreation and health services, including healthy refreshments, fitness and wellness
activities, meditation, and recreation spaces;
• Arts and culture programming, including access to visual, digital, and performing arts,
creative writing, journalism, and media production;
• Academic and career support, including tutoring, mentoring, financial literacy, college
prep, and workforce development linkages;
• Character-building opportunities, including leadership development, self-esteem, service-
learning, and conflict resolution activities; and
1 Mentoring Programs for Youth: A Promising Intervention for Delinquency Prevention, National Institute of
Justice, (May 26, 2021), available here.

• Special events, including regular community activities such as art shows, open mics,
game tournaments, and youth-led events.
Importantly, this bill also seeks to prioritize youth voices by creating Youth Advisory Councils at
each site. Youth Advisory Council members will shape programming and operations and have
direct input on the creation of these centers, ensuring the spaces are designed by and with young
people.
Should you have any questions about this legislation, please contact my Senior Legislative Policy
Advisor, Anaiah Mitchell, at amitchell@dccouncil.gov.
Thank you,

26 To amend Title 10, Chapter 3 of the District of Columbia Official Code to establish the D.C.
27 Teen Center Fund; to amend Section 6 of The Recreation Act of 1994 to require the
28 Department of Parks and Recreation to establish and operate a minimum of four
29 comprehensive teen centers; and to require the creation of Youth Advisory Councils for
30 each center to ensure youth participation in governance, programming, and operations.
31
32 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
33 act may be cited as the “Teen Center Establishment Amendment Act of 2025”.
34 Sec. 2. Section 3c of the “Recreation Act of 1994,” effective March 23, 1995 (D.C. Law
35 10-246; D.C. Official Code §10-303) is amended as follows:
36 (a) A new section 4a is added to read as follows:
37 “(1) There is established the “D.C. Teen Center Fund” (“Fund”), which the

38 Department shall administer in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles.
39 Revenue deposited into the Fund account shall be expended by the Department for the
40 administration, improvement, and maintenance of property and programs managed by the
41 Department and shall supplement, but not replace, services provided by the Department.
42 “(2) The Fund shall be nonlapsing and may receive:
43 (A) Local appropriations;
44 (B) Federal grants; and
45 (C) Private donations, gifts, and other contributions.
46 “(c) Proceeds and money deposited into the Fund may be used solely for the purposes of
47 this section, including operations, staffing, maintenance, programming, and capital
48 improvements for the teen centers.
49 “(d) All fees collected and interest earned on funds shall not revert to the unrestricted
50 fund balance of the General Fund of the District of Columbia at the end of a fiscal year, or at any
51 other time.”.
52 (c) Section 5 is amended as follows:
53 “§10-305. Mega recreation centers and Teen centers.
54 “(a) The Mayor shall develop, construct, and implement mega recreation centers in
55 strategic locations throughout the District of Columbia. Such centers shall be spacious enough to
56 accommodate several indoor activities simultaneously and contain state of the art equipment and
57 apparatus.
58 “(b) The Department shall establish and operate a minimum of 4 comprehensive teen
59 centers, one located in each Quadrant across the District of Columbia.
60 “(c) Program Standards.

61 “Each teen center shall provide programming that incorporates evidence-based
62 prevention and intervention strategies, connects recreation to educational achievement, and
63 supports holistic youth development, including:
64 “(1) Recreation and Health (including a snack area or café offering healthy
65 refreshments, arcade, open recreational area, meditation, yoga, fitness, and wellness
66 programming);
67 “(2) Arts and Culture (including visual, digital, and performing arts, journalism
68 and creative writing, access to recording and production equipment);
69 “(3) Academic and Career Support (including tutoring, mentoring, study support,
70 financial literacy and career readiness, college preparation, and internship or apprenticeship
71 linkages);
72 “(4) Character Building (including leadership and development opportunities,
73 team-building and service-learning projects, programming supporting self-esteem, conflict
74 resolution, and positive relationships); and
75 “(5) Special Events (including regular themed activities and community events
76 such as art shows, open mic nights, video game tournaments, dance lessons, karaoke nights, and
77 other youth-led initiatives).
78 “(d) Notwithstanding any other provision of the law, the services under this section shall
79 be applicable to count toward graduation requirements in accordance with section 2203.3(f) of
80 Title 5-A of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations (5-A DCMR § 2203.3(f)).
81 “(e) Centers shall be open during after-school hours, weekends, and school breaks
82 throughout the calendar year, or additional times as determined by the Youth Advisory Council
83 established in section 4 of this act.

84 “(f) All staff and volunteers shall undergo a mandatory minimum background check and
85 complete youth engagement and trauma-informed training.
86 “(g) The Department shall establish and maintain a minimum staff-to-teen supervision
87 ratio for safety and program quality. Each center shall not operate below the minimum staffing
88 level.”.
89 “(h)(1)(A) The Department shall establish a Youth Advisory Council for each center
90 composed primarily of D.C. youth and community stakeholders, to provide recommendations on
91 programming, policy, and operations.
92 “(B) Councils shall meet at least quarterly and provide recommendations
93 to the Director of the Department and to the Council Committee with jurisdiction over the
94 Department.
95 “(2) Youth Advisory Councils shall have decision-making authority regarding
96 center activities, events, art installations, and general facility rules as established in subsection
97 2(b) of section 3 of this act.”.
98 “(i) The Department shall submit an annual report to the Council and the Mayor
99 detailing:
100 “(1) The number and location of centers established;
101 “(2) Program participation rates and outcomes;
102 “(3) Financial statements for the Teen Center Fund; and
103 “(4) Recommendations for program expansion and improvement.”.
