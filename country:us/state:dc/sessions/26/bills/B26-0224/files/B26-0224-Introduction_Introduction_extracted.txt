Dear Secretary Howard:
Today, I am introducing the “Restricting Egregious Scalping Against Live Entertainment (RESALE)
Amendment Act of 2025”, along with Councilmembers Brianne K. Nadeau, Kenyan R. McDuffie, Matthew
Frumin, Christina Henderson, Janeese Lewis George, Robert C. White, Jr., and Zachary Parker. Please find
enclosed a signed copy of the legislation.
The District is home to world-class arts and live entertainment venues. Unfortunately, the experience of
buying a ticket to see an artist or musician at a concert here in the District is unfair, deceptive, and
frustrating for concertgoers. Once a show is announced and tickets are released, scalpers, using bots and
other technology, will typically flood the venue’s ticketing platform and buy up most of the tickets to
immediately resell them on secondary ticketing platforms like Stubhub, Vivid Seats, and SeatGeek, at an
egregiously higher price. As a result, not only are concertgoers fleeced and price gouged, but they are
denied a fair price and a fair opportunity to get initial tickets at face value. The higher prices that
concertgoers pay for a ticket on the secondary market never go to the artist or venue, and instead
exclusively benefit the scalper. Additionally, when a concertgoer buys a ticket on the secondary market,
the exact seat number or section of the venue is typically not disclosed until the ticket has been
transferred. A ticket for the same or similar seat can also have its price vary dramatically across different
platforms.
Another opaque practice used by scalpers that harms concertgoers is the offer of “speculative tickets” –
i.e., tickets that are not yet in their physical possession. For example, on March 28, 2025, the Sex Pistols
announced a show in the District this coming September. On the very same day, the secondary market
was offering $335 tickets for the show, even though tickets had not been released. When tickets are
released, the initial price will be $60. Not only is this practice deceptive, as scalpers are offering to sell
something that they do not actually have, it denies musicians the opportunity to set prices for their own
shows.
The RESALE Amendment Act of 2025 will protect artists, venues, and consumers in the District from unfair
and deceptive practices in the live event ticket market by:
1

• Capping the price of tickets sold on a secondary ticket platform at the total price of the initial
ticket plus up to 10% of the total price of the initial ticket;
• Banning the sale of “speculative tickets”;
• Requiring a reseller that sells or offers to sell 50 or more tickets a year to register annually with
the Department of Licensing and Consumer Protection and to require that registered resellers
provide certain information to the Department and maintain a surety bond of no less than
$10,000;
• Requiring “all-in” pricing and clarifying the existing Consumer Protection Procedures Act
obligation with language specifying that ticket issuers, resellers, and secondary ticket exchanges
must clearly and conspicuously disclose upfront certain information regarding ticket pricing;
• Laying out when refunds must be provided to consumers;
• Prohibiting a ticket issuer from restricting the transferability of a ticket unless the terms and
conditions on transferability are clearly disclosed to the consumer prior to purchase;
• Allowing ticket issuers to maintain and enforce policies and conditions or requirements for ticket
purchase with respect to conduct, behavior, public health and safety, or age at the venue or event,
and establish limits on the amount of tickets that can be purchased;
• Requiring a ticket issuer or secondary ticket platform to maintain reasonable technology to
prevent any machine, device, computer program, or computer software, with or without human
assistance, from bypassing security measures or access control systems designed to limit the
number of tickets that can be purchased by a person on a marketplace for initial purchase or
secondary ticket exchange;
• Imposing responsibilities on secondary ticket exchanges to ensure fair practices in the secondary
market;
• Requiring the Mayor to issue rules to implement provisions governing the registration of resellers;
and
• Providing for civil penalties of $5,000 for the first violation and $10,000 for the second and
subsequent violations.
This legislation is supported by I.M.P., which owns the 9:30 Club, The Anthem, The Atlantis, and manages
the Lincoln Theatre, 30 local venues and music organizations, including the National Independent Venue
Association, and the Office of the Attorney General.

29 To amend Title 28 of the District of Columbia Official Code to protect artists, venues, and
30 consumers in the District from unfair and deceptive practices in live event ticket
31 purchases by clarifying a ticket issuer, reseller, or secondary ticket exchange’s existing
32 obligation under the Consumer Protection Procedures Act, like in all industries, to clearly
33 and conspicuously disclose upfront certain information regarding ticket pricing, to
34 prescribe procedures governing the provision of refunds, to prohibit the sale of
35 speculative tickets, to prohibit a ticket issuer from restricting the transferability of a ticket
36 unless the terms and conditions on transferability are clearly disclosed to the consumer
37 prior to purchase, to permit ticket issuers to maintain and enforce policies and conditions
38 or requirements for ticket purchase with respect to conduct, behavior, public health and
39 safety, or age at the venue or event, and establish limits on the amount of tickets that can
40 be purchased, to require a ticket issuer or secondary ticket exchange to maintain
41 reasonable technology to prevent any machine, device, computer program, or computer
42 software, with or without human assistance, from bypassing security measures or access
43 control systems designed to limit the number of tickets that can be purchased by a person
44 on a marketplace for initial purchase or secondary ticket exchange, to require a reseller
45 that sells or offers to sell 50 or more tickets a year to register annually with the
46 Department of Licensing and Consumer Protection and to require that registered resellers
47 provide certain information to the Department and maintain a surety bond of no less than
48 $10,000, to impose responsibilities on secondary ticket exchanges, to prohibit the price of
1

49 tickets sold on a secondary ticket exchange from exceeding the total price of the initial
50 ticket plus up to 10% of the total price of the initial ticket, to require the Mayor to issue
51 rules to implement provisions governing the registration of resellers, and to provide for
52 civil penalties of $5,000 for the first violation and $10,000 for the second and subsequent
53 violations of this chapter.

55 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this

58 Sec. 2. Title 28 of the District of Columbia Official Code is amended as follows:
59 (a) The table of contents is amended by adding a new chapter designation to read as
60 follows:
61 “55. Sale and Resale of Event Tickets.”.
62 (b) A new Chapter 55 is added to read as follows:
63 “CHAPTER 55. SALE AND RESALE OF EVENT TICKETS.
64 “28-5501. Definitions.
65 “28-5502. Ticket disclosure and refund requirements.
66 “28-5503. Prohibition on the sale or offer to sell of speculative tickets.
67 “28-5504. Transferability of tickets.
68 “28-5505. Requirement of reasonable technology for a ticket issuer and secondary ticket
69 exchange.
70 “28-5506. Reseller registration and reporting requirements.
71 “28-5507. Secondary ticket exchange responsibilities.
72 “28-5508. Price cap for tickets on secondary ticket exchange.
73 “28-5509. Rules.
74 “28-5510. Penalties and enforcement.
75 “§ 28-5501. Definitions.
2

76 “For the purposes of this chapter, the term:
77 “(1) “Department” means the Department of Licensing and Consumer Protection.
78 “(2) "Resale" means the second or subsequent sale of a ticket by any method,
79 including in-person transactions, telephone, mail, e-mail, facsimile, or electronic means through
80 a website or mobile application.
81 “(3) "Reseller" means a person engaged in the resale of tickets.
82 “(4) "Secondary ticket exchange" means an electronic marketplace that enables a
83 person to sell, purchase, and resell tickets.
84 “(5) "Speculative ticket" means a ticket that is not in the actual or constructive
85 possession of a reseller at the time of listing, sale, or advertisement, including tickets that are not
86 owned by the reseller or not under contract to be transferred to the reseller.
87 “(6) "Ticket" means any form of physical, electronic, or other evidence that grants
88 the possessor of the evidence license to enter a place of entertainment for one or more events at a
89 specified date and time; provided, that the event is not organized by a professional or
90 intercollegiate sports team located in the District or professional athletic association with an
91 event located in the District.
92 “(7) "Ticket issuer" means a person or their agent who issues tickets for initial
93 sale, including musicians, venues, promoters, theater companies, and marketplaces for initial
94 purchases.
95 “§ 28-5502. Ticket disclosure and refund requirements.
96 “(a) The listing for a ticket and each step of a transaction to purchase a ticket shall clearly
97 and conspicuously disclose:
98 “(1) The total price of the ticket, including all fees and taxes other than shipping
99 costs that are not determinable at a step in the transaction;
3

100 “(2) An itemized listing of all charges that comprise the total price of the ticket,
101 including all fees and taxes; and
102 “(3) The seat number, zone, and section, to the extent applicable to the seat and
103 venue.
104 “(b)(1) The total price of a ticket disclosed pursuant to subsection (a) of this section may
105 be increased in a noninitial step of a transaction by the amount of reasonable shipping costs for
106 physically delivered tickets.
107 “(2) The shipping costs allowed under paragraph (1) of this subsection may vary
108 with the consumer’s location relative to the shipment’s location of origin and the delivery
109 method selected by the consumer.
110 “(3) The total price of the ticket, including all fees, taxes, and shipping costs, shall
111 be clearly and conspicuously disclosed prior to final purchase of the ticket.
112 “(c)(1) A ticket issuer, secondary ticket exchange, or reseller directly engaged in a
113 transaction with a consumer shall provide a full refund, including all fees and taxes, if:
114 “(A) The ticket is counterfeit;
115 “(B) The event is canceled; or
116 “(C) The ticket does not conform to the description provided at the time of
117 purchase.
118 “(2) Refunds shall be issued within 10 days of the event’s cancellation or
119 discovery of non-conformance.
120 “§ 28-5503. Prohibition on the sale or offer to sell of speculative tickets.
121 “A reseller or a secondary ticket exchange shall not sell or offer to sell speculative
122 tickets, as defined in § 28-5501(4).
123 “§ 28-5504. Transferability of tickets.
4

124 “(a) A ticket issuer shall not restrict the transferability of a ticket sold unless the terms
125 and conditions on transferability are clearly and conspicuously disclosed to the consumer prior to
126 purchase and the consumer acknowledges receipt of such disclosure prior to purchase.
127 “(b) A ticket issuer may, subject to applicable District or federal law:
128 “(1) Maintain and enforce policies and conditions or requirements for ticket
129 purchase with respect to conduct, behavior, public health and safety, or age at the venue or event;
130 and
131 “(2) Establish limits on the quantity of tickets that may be purchased.
132 “§ 28-5505. Requirement of reasonable technology for a ticket issuer and secondary
133 ticket exchange.
134 “A ticket issuer, to the extent applicable, and secondary ticket exchange shall implement
135 and maintain reasonable technology to prevent any machine, device, computer program, or
136 computer software, with or without human assistance, from bypassing security measures or
137 access control systems designed to limit the number of tickets that can be purchased by a person
138 on a primary or secondary ticket exchange.
139 “§ 28-5506. Reseller registration and reporting requirements.
140 “(a)(1) A reseller that sells or offers to sell 50 or more tickets in a year shall register
141 annually with the Department before conducting any ticket resale activity.
142 “(2) If a reseller lists a single ticket across multiple platforms, the total number of
143 listings for the ticket shall count toward the registration threshold provided in paragraph (1) of
144 this subsection.
145 “(b) A reseller subject to the requirement imposed in subsection (a) of this section shall
146 provide the following information to the Department at the time of registration:
147 “(1) Full legal name and any aliases or usernames used in the resale of tickets;
5

148 “(2) Contact information, including a physical street address, email address, and
149 phone number;
150 “(3) Social security number and employer identification number (“EIN”) and
151 contact information for any employer, if applicable;
152 “(4) All associated entities involved in the resale of tickets; and
153 “(5) Any additional information required by the Department.
154 “(c) All entities under common ownership or control of a person shall be considered one
155 reseller for the purposes of the registration requirements imposed under this section.
156 “(d) A registered reseller shall submit a report every 6 months to the Department
157 containing:
158 “(1) The total number of tickets sold by the reseller;
159 “(2) The original purchase price of each ticket purchased by the reseller; and
160 “(3) The final resale price of each ticket sold by the reseller.
161 “(e)(1) A registered reseller shall maintain a surety bond of no less than $10,000 to
162 ensure compensation to consumers in cases where the reseller fails to deliver purchased tickets or
163 engages in fraudulent activities.
164 “(2) The surety bond shall cover compensation for lost funds by the consumer,
165 including travel expenses incurred by the consumer if the reseller fails to deliver the purchased
166 tickets.
167 “(3) The Department may adjust the surety bond amount for a registered reseller
168 based on the reseller’s sales volume and consumer complaint history.
169 “(4) A registered reseller shall submit evidence of the surety bond to a secondary
170 ticket exchange prior to listing any ticket for resale.
6

171 “(f) The Department may revoke the registration of any registered reseller found to be in
172 violation of this chapter for a period of at least 1 year and not more than 5 years.
173 “(g)(1) The Department shall maintain a list of registered resellers and make the list
174 available to the public via the Department’s website.
175 “(2) The list required pursuant to paragraph (1) of this subsection shall be updated
176 at least once every 30 days and include information on any violations of this chapter within the
177 past 5 years.
178 “(3) The Department shall not publicly disclose information provided by a
179 registered reseller pursuant to subsection (b)(3) of this section.
180 “§ 28-5507. Secondary ticket exchange responsibilities.
181 “(a) A secondary ticket exchange shall maintain records of all registered resellers in the
182 District using its platform and verify that each reseller meets the registration and bonding
183 requirements laid out in § 28-5506.
184 “(b) A secondary ticket exchange shall report any unregistered or non-compliant resellers
185 selling or offering to sell tickets to an event being held in the District to the Department.
186 “(c) A secondary ticket exchange shall:
187 “(1) Be responsible for maintaining records of ticket sales on its platform,
188 including the number tickets sold and resale prices; and
189 “(2) Submit a report to the Department every 6 months containing:
190 “(A) The total number of tickets sold by a registered reseller on its
191 platform;
192 “(B) The original purchase price of each ticket purchased by a registered
193 reseller; and
194 “(C) The final resale price of each ticket sold by a registered reseller.
7

195 “§ 28-5508. Price cap for tickets on secondary ticket exchange.
196 “(a) A reseller or secondary ticket exchange shall not sell or offer to sell a ticket that
197 exceeds the total price of the initial ticket, including all fees and taxes in connection with the
198 initial ticket, plus up to 10% of the total price of the initial ticket.
199 “(b) A secondary ticket exchange may charge a fee for the service of providing a
200 marketplace for the resale of a ticket; provided, that the fee shall not exceed 10% of the total
201 price of the initial ticket.
202 “§ 28-5509. Rules.
203 “The Mayor, pursuant to Title I of the District of Columbia Administrative Procedure
204 Act, approved October 21, 1968 (82 Stat. 1204; D.C. Official Code § 2-501 et seq.), shall issue
205 rules to implement § 28-5506.
206 “§ 28-5510. Penalties and enforcement.
207 “(a)(1) A reseller or secondary ticket exchange that violates § 28-5503 or § 28-5508 shall
208 be assessed a civil penalty of:
209 “(A) $5,000 per ticket for the first violation; and
210 “(B) $10,000 per ticket for the second and any subsequent violations.
211 “(2) Any person, where applicable, who violates §§ 28-5502, 28-5504, 28-5505,
212 28-5506, 28-5507, shall be assessed a civil penalty of:
213 “(A) $5,000 for the first violation; and
214 “(B) $10,000 for the second and any subsequent violations.
215 “(b) The civil penalty imposed pursuant to subsection (a) of this section shall be in
216 addition to the remedies available to the Attorney General pursuant to § 28-3909.”.
