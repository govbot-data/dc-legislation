16 To amend the District of Columbia Comprehensive Bicycle Transportation and Safety Act of
17 1984 to provide a process for community engagement and public comment on proposed
18 protected bike lane projects prior to beginning construction.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 act may be cited as the “Public Infrastructure Notice Modernization Amendment Act of 2026”.
22 Sec. 2. The District of Columbia Comprehensive Bicycle Transportation and Safety Act
23 of 1984, effective March 16, 1985 (D.C. Law 5-179; D.C. Official Code § 50-1601 et seq.), is
24 amended by adding a new section 4a to read as follows:
25 “Sec. 4a. Community engagement and public comment.
26 “(a)(1) At least 60 days before construction begins on a protected bicycle lane, cycle
27 track, transit lane, or multimodal priority street (“project”), the Department shall provide notice
28 that shall include:
29 “(A) A statement of intent to construct a new project, including the
30 proposed design;

31 “(B) A statement describing a 60-day period for public comment on the
32 proposed project, and how affected parties can comment and Advisory Neighborhood
33 Commissions can submit resolutions on the potential impact of the proposed project;
34 “(C) An expected construction schedule; and
35 “(D) Notice of the public hearing required by subsection (b)(3) of this
36 section.
37 “(2) The notice required by paragraph (1) of this subsection shall be transmitted
38 via:
39 “(A) Electronic mail to the Advisory Neighborhood Commissions
40 (“ANCs”), all councilmembers, and impacted groups affected by the project; and
41 “(B) Certified mail or hand-delivery on the proposed route to impacted
42 neighbors.
43 “(3)(A) At least 30 days before construction begins on a project, and within the
44 60-day public comment period, the Department shall hold a public hearing at an accessible
45 evening or weekend time and in an accessible location in the vicinity of the proposed route.
46 “(B) At least 15 days before the public hearing, the Department shall
47 provide notice of this hearing by posting written notice in public buildings including libraries,
48 recreation centers, senior centers, and along the proposed route, and by placing a notice in the
49 District of Columbia Register.
50 “(b) A resolution of an affected ANC shall be given great weight, as described in section
51 13(d)(3)(A) of the Advisory Neighborhood Commissions Act of 1975, effective March 26, 1976
52 (D.C. Law 1-58; D.C. Official Code § 1-309.10(d)(3)(A)).

53 “(c) The Mayor shall maintain for public review all records, including the notice provided
54 to impacted neighbors and groups, notice of the public hearing, records from the public hearing,
55 comments from affected parties received pursuant to sub-paragraph (a)(1)(B) of this section and
56 responses thereto, and any other information about the project.
57 “(d) The Department shall make a determination of whether design changes are required
58 within 21 days of the closure of the public comment period and provide notice, which shall be
59 communicated in accordance with the requirements of subsection (a)(2) of this section.
60 “(e) If the Department determines that the design of the project should be changed
61 significantly after the public comment period, the Department shall initiate a second period of
62 public comment prior to beginning construction.
63 “(f) For the purposes of this section, the term:
64 “(1) “Department” means the District Department of Transportation.
65 “(2) “Impacted neighbors” means residents whose street address is either on the
66 street or on a street directly perpendicular to the street on which construction will take place.
67 “(3) “Impacted groups” means a school, place of worship, business, nonprofit
68 organization, or other group whose street address is either on the street or on a street directly
69 perpendicular to the street on which construction will take place.
70 “(4) “Changed significantly” means a material modification to the design of a
71 project that alters the project’s function, use, or impacts, and that was not subject to public
72 comment during the initial public comment period.”.
73 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.

74 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
75 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
76 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
77 Sec. 4. Effective date.
78 This act shall take effect after approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
79 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto) and a 30-day period of congressional review
80 as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
81 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)).
