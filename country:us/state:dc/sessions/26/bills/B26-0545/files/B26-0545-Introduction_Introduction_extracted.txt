Dear Secretary Howard,
Today, with Councilmembers Janeese Lewis George and Trayon White, I am introducing the
“River East at Grandview Homeownership Relief and Restoration Amendment Act of 2025.” This
legislation will help restore dignity, opportunity, and stability to families who have endured years
of displacement and uncertainty.
Between 2017 and 2019, 46 first-time homebuyers, many of them Black residents, purchased
homes at River East at Grandview in Ward 8. These homes represented more than shelter; they
were the promise of equity, stability, and generational wealth. However, structural issues forced
families to evacuate in 2021. Since then, many have remained in limbo, unable to afford new
homes, living with their belongings in storage, and struggling to rebuild what was lost.
This bill acknowledges the emotional and financial toll that displacement has had on these families
and takes meaningful steps to help them move forward. It recognizes that homeownership is not
just a transaction; it is a foundation for life, and when that foundation is shaken, the consequences
ripple for years.
The legislation provides targeted relief through existing District programs to former River East
homeowners, tailored to their current housing status. It offers HPAP grants to those who have not
yet repurchased, converts HPAP loans to grants for those who have, and adjusts Inclusionary
Zoning affordability covenants to help owners access the equity they would have built in their
Talbert Street homes. It also ensures that relief is not taxed as income and requires annual reporting
to track progress and challenges. These are actionable measures the District can implement using
existing, budgeted programs.
This bill is designed to help families reclaim the stability and opportunity they once had and still
deserve. We urge our colleagues to support this legislation and stand with the residents of River
East as they continue their journey toward healing and home.
Please contact the Committee on Housing staff at housing@dccouncil.gov with any questions.
Sincerely,

17 To amend the Relief for River East at Grandview Condominium Owners Act of 2024 to provide
18 housing relief to certain owners and former owners of the River East at Grandview
19 Condominiums by establishing eligibility for Home Purchase Assistance Program grants,
20 converting existing Home Purchase Assistance Program loans to grants, and limiting the
21 affordability covenant period for former owners Inclusionary Zoning ownership units to
22 15 years; to require coordination between the Department of Housing and Community
23 Development and the District of Columbia Housing Finance Agency; and to address
24 related tax considerations and reporting requirements.
25
26 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA,
27 That this act may be cited as the “River East at Grandview Homeownership Relief and
28 Restoration Amendment Act of 2025”.
29 Sec. 2. The Relief for River East at Grandview Condominium Owners Act of 2024,
30 effective September 18, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-217; D.C. Official Code § 42–2081 et seq.) is
31 amended as follows:
32 (a) Section 2132 (D.C. Official Code § 42–2081) is amended as follows:
33 (1) Paragraph (l) is amended by striking the period and inserting the phrase
34 “between July 2017 and March 2019.” in its place.

35 (2) A new paragraph (m) is added to read as follows:
36 “(m) ‘Former Property Owner’ means an individual who purchased a condominium unit
37 at the River East at Grandview Condominium between July 2017 and March 2019 and who no
38 longer owns that unit.”
39 (b) A new Section 2135 is added to read as follows:
40 “Sec. 2135 HPAP loan conversion to grant and shortening the length of IZ affordability
41 covenant for Property Owners and Former Property Owners
42 “(a) The Department of Housing and Community Development (‘DHCD’), in
43 coordination with the District of Columbia Housing Finance Agency (‘DCHFA’), shall provide
44 relief under this section to individuals defined as Property Owners or Former Property Owners
45 based on their homeownership status, as follows:
46 (1) A property owner or former property owner who has not purchased another
47 primary residence since leaving the Property shall be eligible for assistance through the Home
48 Purchase Assistance Program (‘HPAP’), established under the Home Purchase Assistance Fund
49 Act of 1978, effective September 20, 1978 (D.C. Law 2-135; D.C. Official Code § 42–2601 et
50 seq.), in the form of a grant, toward the purchase of a primary residence in the District of
51 Columbia. The applicant shall be excluded from credit requirements for the HPAP program.
52 (2) Property owners or former property owners who previously received HPAP
53 assistance to purchase a primary residence since leaving the Property in the District of Columbia
54 shall have their outstanding HPAP loan converted to a grant. This provision shall not apply to
55 any subsequent purchase beyond the first purchased primary residence.
56 (A) DHCD and The Office of Tax and Revenue shall record any release,
57 modification, or other instrument necessary to reflect the conversion in the land records.

58 (3) Property Owners or Former Property Owners who purchased or will purchase
59 as their next primary residence following leaving the Property an IZ unit pursuant to 14 DCMR §
60 2200 et seq. shall be given prioritization when applying for a IZ ownership unit and have any
61 affordability covenant recorded on that unit terminate 15 years after the date that the Property
62 Owner or Former Property Owner closed on a condominium unit at the Property. This provision
63 shall not apply to any subsequent purchase beyond the first qualifying unit.
64 (A) DHCD shall ensure that the termination of a covenant under this
65 paragraph is recorded and publicly available.
66 “(b) Notwithstanding any other provision of law:
67 (1) Any amount received under this section shall be excluded from District gross
68 income for purposes of Title 47 of the D.C. Official Code; and
69 (2) Any amount realized by reason of the conversion or forgiveness of an HPAP
70 loan under subsection (a)(2) shall also be excluded from District gross income for purposes of
71 Title 47 of the D.C. Official Code. The Office of Tax and Revenue shall issue guidance to
72 implement this subsection.
73 “(c) By September, 1 2027, and annually thereafter through Fiscal Year 2030, DHCD and
74 DCHFA shall jointly submit to the Council a report identifying:
75 (1) The number of individuals receiving relief and the total amount of relief
76 received under each paragraph of subsection (b);
77 (2) The ward in which each grant recipient purchased a property; and
78 (3) Any administrative or fiscal challenges in implementing this section.
79 “(d) Nothing in this section shall be construed to create an entitlement or to require the
80 District to make payments in excess of funds appropriated for the purpose of this section.”.
