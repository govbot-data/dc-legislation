Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is a proposed
resolution titled “The Field School, Inc. Revenue Bonds Project Approval Resolution of 2025”.
The resolution authorizes the issuance, sale, and delivery of tax-exempt revenue bonds, notes, or other
obligations in aggregate principal amount not to exceed $25,000,000. The bonds will be used to build a
new 15,000 square foot Innovation Center and to renovate the Wonder Building and athletic fields on the
school’s campus located at 2301 Foxhall Road NW in Ward 3.
In accordance with section 490 of the Home Rule Act, it has been determined that the bonds, when, as,
and if issued, shall be without recourse to the District. The bonds shall not be general obligations of the
District; shall not be a pledge of or involve the full faith and credit or the taxing power of the District;
shall not constitute a debt of the District; and shall not constitute a lending of public credit for a private
undertaking as prohibited in section 602(a)(2) of the Home Rule Act. The bonds shall not give rise to any
pecuniary liability of the District, and the District shall have no obligation with respect to the purchase of
the bonds.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed measure.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

Field School Inc. Revenue Bond
FACT SHEET
The Field School Inc. has requested that the District issue up to $25,000,000 in tax-exempt bonds
to build a new 15,000 square foot Innovation Center and to renovate the Wonder Building and
athletic fields on the school’s campus located at 2301 Foxhall Road NW in Ward 3.
The Applicant
Field School is a private college preparatory school serving children from grades 6 through 12.
In 1972, the Field School was founded with 44 students, on the belief that learning occurs best in
an informal, personal, and non-institutional environment. The Field School’s student body also
includes special needs students, such as students with attention deficit syndrome and behavioral
disorders. The Field School is one of the few schools operating in the District that are responsive
to meeting the unique needs of such students. Currently, they have approximately 370 students.
The Field School’s minority student enrollment is 23.9%, and the school has a student-teacher
ratio of 8:1.
Proposed Project
The proposed project is to build a new 15,000 square foot Innovation Center and renovate the
Wonder Building and athletic fields on the school’s campus located at 2301 Foxhall Road NW in
Ward 3.
Financing Plan
A summary of the proposed sources and uses of funds is attached (see Table 1).
Feasibility/Structure/Security of the Bonds
First Tryon Advisors, financial advisor for Field School, has determined this bond transaction to
be financially feasible.
The bonds, if issued, will be secured by a general obligation pledge of the Field School and will
be without recourse to the District of Columbia.
Public Purpose Benefits
As a private school in Washington, D.C., the Field School provides public purpose benefits by
offering high-quality education that promotes civic engagement, leadership, and community
service. Through student volunteerism, partnerships with local organizations, and cultural
programming, the school contributes meaningfully to the social, educational, and cultural fabric
of the city. The proposed bond financing will support the Field School in furthering its mission

The Field School, Inc.
by enabling facility upgrades and expansions in a more financially sustainable way, thus
allowing for more scholarship and financial assistance opportunities.
Legal and Regulatory Affairs
The law firm of Orrick, bond counsel to the Revenue Bond Program, has preliminarily
determined that the applicant is a 501(c)(3) organization and that this project constitutes a
permissible undertaking under to Section 490(a)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act.
Based on the information set forth in the application, the Revenue Bond Program staff has
determined that the proposed project complies with criteria for approval of this proposed
financing through the District’s Industrial Revenue Bond Program and will assist the Borrower
in furthering its organizational mission.
