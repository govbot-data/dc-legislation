12 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA

14 _________________________
15
16
17 To recognize, honor, and celebrate the 50th anniversary of the Mu Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma
18 Theta Sorority, Incorporated, at The George Washington University.
19
20 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, founded on January 13, 1913, at
21 Howard University, is an esteemed organization of college-educated women committed to the
22 constructive development of its members and to public service, with a primary focus on the
23 Black community;
24 WHEREAS, on April 20, 1975, nine dynamic women chartered the Mu Beta Chapter of
25 Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, at The George Washington University, making history
26 as the first Black Greek-lettered organization on campus;
27 WHEREAS, the visionary charter members—Francine Burnett, Linda Finch, Tanya
28 Goodman, Monica Jordan, Carolyn Marks, Helen Quander, Deborah Randolph, Ellen Wells, and
29 Theresa Weston—established a legacy of excellence that has flourished for five decades;
30 WHEREAS, for 50 years, the Mu Beta Chapter has exemplified Delta Sigma Theta's
31 commitment to sisterhood, scholarship, and service through numerous community initiatives,
32 including the Walk to End HIV with the Whitman-Walker Foundation, Voter Registration
1

33 Tabling with GWVotes, and participation in The George Washington University's Martin Luther
34 King, Jr., Day of Service;
35 WHEREAS, the Mu Beta Chapter has served as a beacon of leadership, academic
36 excellence, and civic engagement, fostering the development of women leaders who have made
37 significant contributions to the District of Columbia, the nation, and communities around the
38 world;
39 WHEREAS, the Mu Beta Chapter continues to embody the sorority's mission through
40 dedicated service to The George Washington University campus and the greater Washington
41 Metropolitan area, creating meaningful impact through thoughtful programming and community
42 outreach; and
43 WHEREAS, in April 2025, the Mu Beta Chapter will commemorate its 50th Charter Day
44 with a week-long celebration, bringing together current members, alumnae, and supporters to
45 honor this historic milestone and reaffirm their commitment to continued service.
46 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
47 resolution may be cited as the "Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated - Mu Beta Chapter 50th
48 Anniversary Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
49 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates the 50th
50 anniversary of the Mu Beta Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, at The George
51 Washington University for five decades of exemplary service, leadership, and dedication to the
52 advancement of the District of Columbia community.
53 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
54 the District of Columbia Register.
