15 To celebrate the 75th anniversary of the American Fazl Mosque and recognize it for its
16 contributions to the District of Columbia and Greater Washington Metropolitan Area.
17
18 WHEREAS, the building on 2141 Leroy Place was purchased by the Ahmadiyya Muslim
19 Community USA on April 6, 1950, making the American Fazl Mosque, Fazl meaning “Grace”,
20 the first permanent mosque in Washington, D.C., and the longest serving mosque in the nation's
21 capital;
22 WHEREAS, The Ahmadiyya Muslim Community, founded in 1889 by Hazrat Mirza
23 Ghulam Ahmad of Qadian (India), and presently led by his fifth successor, Hazrat Mirza
24 Masroor Ahmad, is a global Islamic community dedicated to the promotion of peace, justice,
25 interfaith harmony, and service to humanity;
26 WHEREAS, the Ahmadiyya Muslim Community USA, established in 1920, has been an
27 integral part of American society, contributing to civic engagement, humanitarian aid, and efforts
28 to foster religious freedom and understanding;
29 WHEREAS, historian Richard Brent Turner has described the Ahmadiyya Muslim
30 Community as unquestionably “one of the most significant movements in the history of Islam in
1

31 the United States in the twentieth century, providing as it did the first multiracial model for
32 American Islam”;
33 WHEREAS, the Ahmadiyya Muslim Community has a long history of supporting local
34 food banks and conducting nationwide blood drives in partnership with local organizations;
35 WHEREAS, the American Fazl Mosque serves the public today as a place of worship,
36 interfaith dialogue and social service; and
37 WHEREAS, over the past 75 years the congregation of the American Fazl Mosque has
38 grown throughout the DMV and has established 4 more mosques in the area.
39 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
40 resolution may be cited as the “75th Anniversary of the American Fazl Mosque Ceremonial
41 Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
42 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates the American
43 Fazl Mosque for its 75 years of commitment to peace, education, and public service, as well as
44 its commitment to practicing the principles of its motto “Love for All, Hatred for None”, and
45 welcomes its continued presence and impact in Washington, D.C.
46 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
47 the District of Columbia Register.
