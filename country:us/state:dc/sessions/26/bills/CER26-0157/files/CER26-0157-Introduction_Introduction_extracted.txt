14
15 To honor and celebrate the life and legacy of Romaine B. Thomas and recognize her lifelong
16 contributions to the District of Columbia.
17
18 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas, a native Washingtonian, was born on June 5, 1929,
19 and passed away on April 27, 2026, at the age of 96, leaving behind a lasting legacy of service,
20 leadership, and devotion to the District of Columbia;
21 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas was a devoted mother, grandmother, and great-
22 grandmother, whose commitment to family remained central throughout her life;
23 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas dedicated her professional life to education, serving as
24 a teacher and later as principal of John Henry Ketchum Elementary School, where she shaped the
25 lives of generations of District students and families;
26 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas was awarded the National Distinguished Principal
27 Award by the United States Secretary of Education in recognition of her outstanding leadership
28 and commitment to excellence in education;
29 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas earned her Bachelor of Science degree from Miners
30 Teachers College and a Master of Arts degree from The George Washington University, and
31 pursued additional advanced studies, including at Vanderbilt University and in London, England;
1

32 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas was a dedicated civic leader, serving as President of
33 the Woodridge Civic Association and as Second Vice President of the District of Columbia
34 Federation of Civic Associations;
35 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas was a tireless advocate for seniors and residents with
36 disabilities, serving as President of the Executive Council of D.C.’s AARP and as Chairperson of
37 the D.C. Commission on Aging;
38 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas contributed to the District’s civic and political life as
39 Chairwoman of the D.C. Democratic Convention and as a delegate to the 2012 Democratic
40 National Convention; and
41 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas was a pillar of the Ward 5 community, known for her
42 longstanding advocacy, leadership, and commitment to improving the lives of District residents;
43 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas will be remembered for her lifelong dedication to
44 education, civic engagement, and public service in the District of Columbia.
45 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
46 resolution may be cited as the “Romaine B. Thomas Posthumous Recognition Resolution of
47 2026”.
48 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the life and legacy
49 of Romaine B. Thomas and expresses its deepest condolences to her family, including former
50 Councilmember Harry Thomas Jr., and all who were touched by her life.
51 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
