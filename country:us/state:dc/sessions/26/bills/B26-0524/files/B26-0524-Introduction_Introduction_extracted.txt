Statement of Introduction
Distribution of False Sexual Imagery Prohibition Amendment Act of 2025
December 1, 2025
Today, I am introducing the Distribution of False Sexual Imagery Prohibition Amendment Act of
2025, along with Chairman Phil Mendelson and Councilmembers Brianne K. Nadeau, Matthew
Frumin, Anita Bonds, Janeese Lewis George, and Charles Allen. This bill would establish new
criminal penalties for the distribution of sexually explicit images, videos, and audio created
through artificial intelligence (AI), directly addressing the emerging digital-age harm of creating
and spreading non-consensual AI-generated sexual content depicting real individuals.
Current District of Columbia law does not adequately protect residents against the dangers posed
by distribution of false sexual imagery nor hold perpetrators accountable. D.C. Code §§ 22-
3052-54 prohibit the unauthorized sharing of authentic sexual images, videos, and recordings.
The statute does not directly address AI-generated depictions or manipulated images that were
initially non-sexual but were edited to include nudity or sexual conduct. D.C. Code § 22-2201
prohibits the distribution of obscene images and videos, but sexually explicit content is not
necessarily obscene. Additionally, while D.C. Code § 7-2161 et seq. provides for civil remedies
for the unauthorized disclosure of intimate images, I believe that disseminating both real and
false sexual images is not only a matter of dispute between two parties, but also an offense
against society, and that the District’s criminal laws should acknowledge the harms and dangers
of such behavior.
The federal government has already recognized the urgency of this issue. Congress recently
passed, and the President signed, the Take it Down Act, establishing federal criminal penalties
for the nonconsensual online publication of sexually explicit media, whether depicting real
people or AI-generated. The Distribution of False Sexual Imagery Prohibition Amendment Act
of 2025 would better align District of Columbia law with the federal standard and demonstrates
the urgent need for comprehensive protections at all levels of government. I look forward to
working with my colleagues to strengthen local safeguards for residents against these forms of
digital abuse.

27 To amend the Criminalization of Non-Consensual Pornography Act of 2014 to create a new
28 misdemeanor criminal offense and a new felony criminal offense for nonconsensual
29 distribution of sexually explicit images, video, or audio of an individual created or altered
30 through use of digital technology, including by the use of artificial intelligence methods.
31
32 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
33 act may be cited as the “Distribution of False Sexual Imagery Prohibition Amendment Act of
34 2025.”.
35 Sec. 2. The Criminalization of Non-Consensual Pornography Act of 2014, effective May
36 7, 2015 (D.C. Law 20-275; D.C. Official Code § 22-3051 et seq.), is amended as follows:
37 (a) Section 2 (D.C. Official Code § 22-3051) is amended by adding new paragraphs (1A)
38 and (1B) to read as follows:
1

39 “(1A) “Distribute” means granting another person the ability to exercise dominion
40 and control over the image.
41 “(1B) “False sexual image” means a sexual image or audio recording created or
42 altered through the use of digital technology that a reasonable person would believe it depicts
43 speech or conduct of the person depicted and the production of which was substantially
44 dependent upon technical means, rather than the ability of another person to physically or
45 verbally impersonate such individual.”.
46 (b) A new section 5a is added to read as follows:
47 “Sec. 5a. Unlawful distribution of false sexual imagery.
48 “(a)(1) First-degree unlawful distribution of false sexual imagery — It shall be unlawful
49 in the District of Columbia for a person to knowingly distribute or disclose one or more false
50 sexual images of another identified or identifiable person when, in addition to satisfying the
51 elements described in subsection (b)(1) of this section, the person commits the violation:
52 “(A) Within 5 years of one or more prior convictions of unlawful
53 distribution of false sexual imagery under this section;
54 “(B) With the intent to cause violence or bodily harm; or
55 “(C) Is reckless as to the fact that the person depicted is:
56 “(i) Under 16 years of age and the defendant is at least 4 years
57 older than the person depicted; or
58 “(ii) Under 18 years of age and the defendant is at least 4 years
59 older than the complainant and in a position of trust with or authority over the person depicted.
60 “(2) A person who violates this subsection shall be guilty of a felony and, upon
61 conviction, shall be fined not more than the amount set forth in section 101 of the Criminal Fine
2

62 Proportionality Amendment Act of 2012, effective June 11, 2013 (D.C. Law 19-317; D.C.
63 Official Code § 22-3571.01), imprisoned for not more than 2 years, or both.
64 “(b)(1) Second-degree unlawful distribution of false sexual imagery — It shall be
65 unlawful in the District of Columbia for a person to knowingly distribute or disclose one or more
66 false sexual images of another identified or identifiable person when:
67 “(A) It is to a person other than the person depicted, including making the
68 image accessible on the internet to a person other than the person being depicted;
69 “(B) The person depicted did not consent to the disclosure or distribution
70 of the false sexual image; and
71 “(C) The person discloses or distributed the false sexual image with the
72 intent to;
73 “(i) Alarm, sexually abuse, humiliate, harass, or degrade the person
74 depicted;
75 “(ii) Sexually arouse or gratify a person; or
76 “(iii) Receive financial gain as a result of the distribution or
77 display.
78 “(2) A person who violates this subsection shall guilty of a misdemeanor and,
79 upon conviction, shall be fined not more than the amount set forth in section 101 of the Criminal
80 Fine Proportionality Amendment Act of 2012, effective June 11, 2013 (D.C. Law 19-317; D.C.
81 Official Code § 22- 3571.01), imprisoned for not more than 180 days in jail, or both.”.
