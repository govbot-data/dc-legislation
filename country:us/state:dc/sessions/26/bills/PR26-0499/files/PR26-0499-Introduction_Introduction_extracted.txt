5 A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
6 _____________________
7 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
8 _____________________
9
10 To declare the need to amend, on an emergency basis, the District of Columbia Election Code of
11 1955 to require candidate nomination petition affidavits to state that the circulator has not
12 altered signer information on a petition after a signer has signed the petition; to prohibit
13 anyone from altering signer information provided by the petition signer, except in cases
14 where the signer requests assistance in signing the petition before signing the petition; to
15 amend the Initiative, Referendum and Recall Procedures Act of 1979 to require initiative,
16 referendum and recall petition affidavits to state that the circulator has not altered signer
17 information on a petition after a signer has signed the petition; and, to prohibit anyone
18 from altering signer information provided by the petition signer, except in cases where
19 the signer requests assistance in signing the petition.
20
21 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 resolution may be cited as “Petition Administration Clarification Emergency Declaration
23 Resolution of 2026”.
24 Sec. 2. (a) The purpose of the underlying emergency measure is to clarify the law
25 governing ballot access in the District before the Board of Elections releases candidate
26 nomination petitions for the 2026 primary election cycle. This emergency bill is identical to B26-
27 233, which unanimously passed first reading by the Committee of the Whole on December 16,
28 2025. This bill would require petition circulator affidavits to state that the circulator has not
29 altered signer information on a nomination petition after a signer has signed the petition. It would
30 also prohibit anyone from altering signer information provided by the petition signer, unless the
1

31 signer requests assistance altering their information. The bill also clearly bans the use of whiteout
32 or other correctional fluid.
33 (b) The Board of Elections will release nomination petitions for the June 2026 primary on
34 January 23, 2026. Circulators will immediately begin collecting the required signatures for the
35 candidates for the offices on the 2026 primary ballot. Although B26-233 is on track for Council
36 approval, it will be months before that bill becomes law due to mayoral and congressional review
37 periods.
38 (c) B26-233 has widespread support because it provides clarity to circulators and the
39 Board of Elections by banning changes to petitions after a signer affixes their signature. The 2026
40 election cycle will include many high-profile, contested races – including for Delegate, Mayor,
41 Attorney General, and Chairman of the Council – and it is crucial that we begin the petition
42 period with the provisions of this law in effect. The underlying emergency legislation will allow
43 the public to collect and review candidate petition submissions in accordance with this bill.
44 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
45 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making in necessary that the “Petition
46 Administration Clarification Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026” be adopted after a
47 single reading.
48 Sec. 4. The resolution shall take effect immediately.
