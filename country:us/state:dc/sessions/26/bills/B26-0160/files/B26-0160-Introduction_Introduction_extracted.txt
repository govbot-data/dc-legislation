Dear Secretary Smith,
Today, I am introducing the “Closing of a Cul-de-Sac and Dedication of Land for Alley Purposes
in Square 4350, S.O. 22-05506, Act of 2025”. Please see enclosed signed copy of the legislation.
First built in 1962, the Bladensburg Bus Garage sits on 17.59 acres bounded by Bladensburg
Road NE, 25th Place NE, Douglas Street NE, and a CSX railroad Right of Way. The
Bladensburg Bus Garage Property (Square 4350 Lot 0006) began as a series of
unconsolidated lots and squares. Beginning in the 1960s, the Washington Metropolitan Area
Transit Authority (WMATA) consolidated these into a single Property. In support of this
consolidation, the District of Columbia Office of the Surveyor closed those portions of 26th
Street NE and Douglas Street NE that were contained within the larger Property. Douglas Street
NE was redesigned to terminate in a cul-de-sac that would allow for U-turns.
Today, the Bladensburg Bus Garage is one of Metro’s oldest assets and home to some 260 buses
and support vehicles. Nearly 500 employees on-site oversee bus repairs, maintenance, and
inspections at this location. WMATA is currently engaged in the redesign and reconstruction of
the existing Bus Garage. This will serve to modernize the facility and improve essential bus
operations and maintenance. This redesign will achieve a number of goals. The new garage will
continue to provide important services like maintenance space, parts storage, and employee
amenities. It will also incorporate environmental and infrastructure improvements necessary to
support additional buses. It will dramatically improve parking accommodations by allowing the
creation of 200 40-foot-long bus spaces, 100 articulated bus spaces, and 560 employee spaces.
Finally, the new garage will allocate space for electric bus infrastructure in the future (i.e.
charging locations), consistent with Metro’s zero-emission bus plan.

In order to maximize bus parking, WMATA has worked closely with the District Department of
Transportation (DDOT) and the Office of Planning (OP) to shift the facility’s main entrance on
Bladensburg Road to the north, in alignment with Channing Street NE. This will allow for a
drive aisle to be relocated along the facility’s northeast property line. This relocation of the drive
aisle will require the closure of the portion of the Douglas Street cul-de-sac that encroaches upon
the Property’s NE boundary. A new alley, which will be maintained and utilized through a public
easement, will be created at the end of Douglas Street in its place.
Please feel free to reach out to me or my Legislative Director, Neferteria Brown, with any
questions or for additional information.
Sincerely,
Zachary Parker
Ward 5 Councilmember

15 To approve the closing of a cul-de-sac in Square 4350, abutting Lot 0006, and bounded by
16 Douglas Street, N.E., and the dedication of a portion of Lot 0006 for public alley
17 purposes in Ward 5.
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
20 be cited as the “Closing of a Cul-de-Sac and Dedication of Land for Alley Purposes in
21 Square 4350, S.O. 22-05506, Act of 2025”.
22 Sec. 2. (a) Pursuant to Section 201 of the Street and Alley Closing and Acquisition
23 Procedures Act of 1982 (“Act”), effective March 10, 1983 (D.C. Law 4-201; D.C. Official Code
24 § 9-201.01 et seq.), the Council finds the cul-de-sac in Square 4350, as shown on the Surveyor’s
25 plat filed under S.O. 22-05506, is unnecessary for alley purposes and orders it closed, with title
26 to the land to vest as shown on the Surveyor’s plat.
27 (b) Pursuant to section 302 of the Act (D.C. Official Code § 9-203.02), the Council
28 accepts the dedication of a portion of Lot 0006 in Square 4350 for public alley purposes, as
29 shown on the Surveyor’s plat filed under S.O. 22-05506.
30 Sec. 3. The closing of this cul-de-sac and dedication of a portion of Lot 0006 for public
31 alley purposes, pursuant to section 2, is contingent upon the satisfaction of all conditions in the
32 official file for S.O. 22-05506.

33 Sec. 4. Transmittal.
34 The Secretary of the Council shall transmit a copy of this act, upon its effective date, to
35 the District of Columbia Office of the Surveyor and the District of Columbia Recorder of Deeds
36 Office.
37 Sec. 5. Fiscal impact statement.
38 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
39 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
40 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
41 Sec. 6. Effective date.
42 This act shall take effect after approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
43 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of congressional review as
44 provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
45 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)), and publication in the District of
46 Columbia Register.
