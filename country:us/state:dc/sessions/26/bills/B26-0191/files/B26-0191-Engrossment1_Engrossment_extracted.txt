12 To amend, on an temporary basis, the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016 to prohibit
13 private disability insurance providers from reducing short-term disability benefits based
14 on actual or estimated paid leave benefits to which an eligible individual may be entitled
15 to from the District, regardless of the jurisdiction in which the insurance policy was
16 issued or written; and to amend Title I of the Insurance Trade and Economic
17 Development Amendment Act of 2000 to make the prohibition on offsetting or reducing
18 benefits under a private market short-term disability insurance policy based on estimated
19 or actual benefits received under the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016
20 enforceable under that law, regardless of the jurisdiction in which the insurance policy
21 was issued or written.
22
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 act may be cited as the “Short-Term Disability Insurance Benefit Protection Clarification
25 Temporary Amendment Act of 2025”.
26 Sec. 2. Section 107(j)(1) of the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016, effective
27 April 7, 2017 (D.C. Law 21-264; D.C. Official Code § 32-541.07(j)(1)), is amended to read as
28 follows:
29 “(1) No insurer may offset or reduce benefits or income available to an eligible
30 individual under an individual or group policy for temporary or short-term disability insurance

33 Sec. 3. Section 120a the Insurance Trade and Economic Development Amendment Act of
34 2000, effective April 2, 2001 (D.C. Law 13-265; D.C. Official Code § 31-2231.20a), is amended
35 as follows:
36 (a) Subsection (a) is amended to read as follows:
37 “(a) No insurer may offset or reduce benefits or income available to an eligible individual
38 under a temporary or short-term disability insurance policy, based on estimated or actual benefits
39 the eligible individual may or does receive under the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of
40 2016, effective April 7, 2017 (D.C. Law 21-264; D.C. Official Code § 32-541.01 et seq.),
41 regardless of in which jurisdiction such policy was executed, written, or delivered.”.
42 (b) Subsection (c) is amended to read as follows:
43 “(c) For the purposes of this section, the term:
44 “(1) “Eligible individual” shall have the same meaning as provided in section
45 101(6) of the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016, effective April 7, 2017 (D.C. Law
46 21-264; D.C. Official Code § 32-541.01(6)).
47 “(2) “Self-insured employer” shall have the same meaning as provided in section
48 101(19A) of the Universal Paid Leave Amendment Act of 2016, effective April 7, 2017 (D.C.
49 Law 21-264; D.C. Official Code § 32-541.01(19A)).”.
