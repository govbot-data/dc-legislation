8 A BILL
9
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
11
12 To amend the Public Education Reform Amendment Act of2007 to provide the D.C. State
13 Board of Education with the authority to require the initiation of education policy actions
14 under its approval authority, to amend education policy actions requested by the Office of
15 the State Superintendent of Education by a two-thirds majority vote, to require the
16 submission of education-related data, and to provide great weight to recommendations of
17 the State Board adopted by resolution.
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
20 be cited as the "D.C. State Board of Education Expanded Authority Amendment Act of 2025".
21 Sec. 2. Section 403 of the Public Education Reform Amendment Act of2007 (D.C. Law
22 17-9, D.C. Official Code § 38-2652) is amended as follows:
23 (a) Paragraph (c)(l) is amended by inserting the phrase "by majority vote, or amendment
24 by two-thirds majority vote" after the word "approval";
25 (b) A new subsection (c-1) is inserted to read as follows:
26 "(c-1)(1) The State Board may initiate the submission of a policy action concerning a
27 matter enumerated in subsection (a) of this section as follows:
28 "(A)(i) The State Board shall formally request the submission of a
29 specified policy action concerning a matter enumerated in subsection (a) of this section by
30 transmitting a letter signed by five or more board members as signatories.
1

31 “(ii) The DC government entity from which the policy
32 action was requested has 90 days to respond to the written request from the State Board, and
33 shall inform the State Board whether they will commence the requested policy action within six
34 months, and to provide a written work plan detailing the timeline for submission of the requested
35 policy action.
36 “(B) If the relevant DC government entity does not respond within 90
37 days, declines the request of the State Board, does not initiate the process within six months, then
38 the State Board has the authority to require that the government entity initiate the policy through
39 a resolution, approved by a supermajority of two-thirds of members.
40 “(C) The resolution may require that the relevant DC government entity
41 initiate the policy within six months of transmission to the entity, and may require the relevant
42 DC government entity to submit the policy to the State Board for approval within one year of the
43 adoption of the resolution.”.
44 (2) The State Board may require education-related data from a DC government
45 entity, within the Executive Branch, to be submitted to the State Board as follows:
46 “(A) The State Board shall request the submission of education-related
47 data by transmitting a letter signed by five or more board members as signatories.
48 “(B) If the relevant DC government entity does not provide the data within
49 15 business days, or if the education agency declines the request, then the State Board has the
50 authority to require that the data be submitted to the Board, by a state resolution of the Board,
51 which requires a supermajority of members, within at least 15 days of transmission to the
52 agency.
2

53 “(C) This subsection shall not be interpreted to preclude a DC government
54 entity from voluntary submitting education-related data requested by the State Board of
55 Education or its individual members.”.
56 (b) A new subsection (f) is inserted to read as follows:
57 “(f)(1) The issues and concerns raised in the recommendations of a state resolution
58 adopted by the State Board of Education shall be given great weight during the deliberations by
59 the government entity. Great weight requires acknowledgement of the State Board as the source
60 of the recommendations and explicit reference to each of the State Board’s issues and concerns.
61 “(2) In all cases the government entity is required to articulate its decision in
62 writing. The written rationale of the decision shall articulate with particularity and precision the
63 reasons why the State Board does or does not offer persuasive advice under the circumstances. In
64 so doing, the government entity must articulate specific findings and conclusions with respect to
65 each issue and concern raised by the State Board. Further, the government entity is required to
66 support its position on the record.
67 “(3) The government entity shall promptly send to the State Board a copy of its
68 written decision within 60 days.
