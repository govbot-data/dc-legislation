27 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
28 ______________
29
30
31 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
32
33 ____________________________
34
35 To honor the District’s Fire and Emergency Medical Services Department for its dedication to and
36 protection of the citizens and visitors of Washington, D.C., and for their valiant and
37 courageous service to the metropolitan area after the attacks of September 11, 2001.
38
39 WHEREAS, on the occasion of the 24th Anniversary of the September 11, 2001 terrorist
40 attacks on the United States, the Council of the District of Columbia honors the memory of the
41 nearly 3,000 people who perished from the attack, including twelve victims from the District;
42

43 WHEREAS, an exceptionally courageous force of first responders—including more than
44 100,000 firefighters, paramedics, rescue and recovery workers, and police officers across the
45 country—risked their lives that day to save the lives of others;
46
47 WHEREAS, the brave District Fire and EMS Department which, along with the Arlington
48 County Fire Department and other local fire agencies, helped with the Pentagon rescue and
49 recovery efforts on September 11, 2001;
50
51 WHEREAS, nationally, thousands of first responders have suffered adverse physical and
52 emotional effects in the twenty-four years since 2001 and, even now, are at significantly greater
53 risk for developing occupational cancers due to their exposure to chemicals and debris from the
54 attacks;
55
56 WHEREAS, the men and women who serve as first responders in the District of Columbia
57 carry out the extraordinary responsibility of protecting not only District residents, but also all who
58 visit and work here, and have always done so with tremendous dedication and care;
59
60 WHEREAS, the men and women of the District’s emergency services have fulfilled every
61 duty to the District and their country in an honorable, courageous, and timely fashion, and have
62 demonstrated immense compassion for those who have suffered unforeseeable tragedies, while
63 routinely considering the safety and well-being of others before their own; and
64
65 WHEREAS, it is fully right and just to honor the memory of those who lost their lives in
66 the terrorist attacks more than two decades ago, and it is equally compelling to observe and pay
67 our respects to the first responders of the District and their bravery and selflessness in the face of
68 extraordinarily trying circumstances.
69
70 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
71 resolution may be cited as the “September 11th Emergency and First Responders Remembrance
72 and Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
73
74 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia honors its first responders for their
75 tremendous commitment to serving the District and remembers emergency workers who dutifully
76 served their country in the face of danger on September 11, 2001.
77
78 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
