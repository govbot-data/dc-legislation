ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
26-10
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
February 4, 2025
To honor and recognize the life of Derrick Ward and celebrate his profound contributions to
journalism, his unwavering dedication to the Washington, D.C., community, and his
enduring legacy as a loving father and inspiring role model.
WHEREAS, Derrick Ward was born and raised in Washington, D.C. and grew up in the
Marshall Heights and H Street Corridor neighborhoods, attended H.D. Woodson High School,
and graduated from the University of Maryland, reflecting his deep-rooted connection to the
community he served;
WHEREAS, Derrick began his illustrious journalism career in radio, contributing to
esteemed stations such as WPFW, WAMU, and WTOP, where he covered significant events
including the Iran-Contra hearings, September 11th, and the Washington-area sniper shootings;
WHEREAS, throughout his career, Ward served as a mentor to many aspiring journalists,
offering guidance and support to those entering the field. His leadership and dedication to
journalism left a lasting impact on his colleagues and the broader media community;
WHEREAS, in 2006, Derrick joined News4 Washington as a reporter, bringing his
profound knowledge of the city’s history and his exceptional storytelling skills to television
audiences, earning the reputation of News4’s “resident D.C. historian and poet”;
WHEREAS, beyond his journalistic endeavors, Derrick was a talented musician, known
for his proficiency with the guitar, and was celebrated for his warmth, humor, and unwavering
dedication to his craft;
WHEREAS, Derrick Ward passed away on January 7, 2025, at the age of 62;
WHEREAS, Derrick is survived by his 3 children—Derrick Jr., Ian, and Marisa—who
continue to honor his legacy.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this resolution
may be cited as the “Derrick Ward Posthumous Recognition Resolution of 2025”.

ENROLLED ORIGINAL
Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the
accomplishments of Derrick Ward and congratulates him on a great season.
Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
