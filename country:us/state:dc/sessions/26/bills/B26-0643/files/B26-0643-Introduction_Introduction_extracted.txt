6 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
7 _____
8 To amend the Traffic and Parking Ticket Penalty Amendment Act of 2018 and the District of
9 Columbia Traffic Adjudication Act of 1978 to require the Mayor to establish a Community
10 Service Debt Repayment Program to allow residents to perform community service in
11 order to repay debts related to certain unpaid notices of infraction.
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
14 act may be cited as the “Community Service Debt Repayment Program Amendment Act of 2026”.
15 Sec. 2. Section 2(i) of the Traffic and Parking Ticket Penalty Amendment Act of 2018,
16 effective October 30, 2018 (D.C. Law 22-175; D.C. Official Code § 50-2303.21) is revived as of
17 the effective date of this act and amended to read as follows:
18 “Sec. 321. Community Service Debt Repayment Program.
19 “(a) For the purposes of this section, the term:
20 “(1) “Gross income” shall have the same meaning as provided in section 505(3)
21 of the District of Columbia Public Assistance Act of 1982, effective April 6, 1982 (D.C. Law 4-
22 101; D.C. Official Code § 4-205.05(3)).
1

23 “(2) “Low-income resident” means a District resident with a gross income at or
24 below 250% of the federal poverty level.
25 “(3) “Program” means the Community Service Debt Repayment Program
26 established pursuant to subsection (b) of this section.
27 “(b) The Mayor shall establish a Community Service Debt Repayment Program to reduce
28 the amount of civil fines or penalties owed by low-income residents resulting from the issuance
29 of:
30 “(1) Those notices of infraction that would be processed and adjudicated pursuant
31 to the provisions of Title II that were detected by an automated traffic enforcement system
32 pursuant to section 901 of the Fiscal Year 1997 Budget Support Act of 1996, effective April 9,
33 1997 (D.C. Law 11-198; D.C. Official Code § 50-2209.01); or
34 “(2) Notices of infraction that would be processed and adjudicated pursuant to the
35 provisions of Title III.
36 “(c)(1) Under the Program, a low-income resident may, pursuant to rules issued by the
37 Mayor, for each hour of community service performed, reduce the amount he or she owes by an
38 amount equivalent to the minimum hourly wage described in section 4(a)(5) of the Minimum
39 Wage Act Revision Act of 1992, effective March 25, 1993 (D.C. Law 9-248; D.C. Official Code
40 § 32-1003(a)(5)).
41 “(2) The Mayor shall not impose on a low-income resident who participates in the
42 Program any enrollment fee nor any other additional fees associated with participation in the
43 Program.
2

44 “(3) A low-income resident may choose to reduce any portion of the amount the
45 low-income resident owes through participation in the Program, and participation in the Program
46 shall not be predicated on a commitment by a low-income resident to reduce any specified
47 amount owed.
48 “(d) The Mayor, pursuant to Title I of the District of Columbia Administrative Procedure
49 Act, approved October 21, 1968 (82 Stat. 1204; D.C. Official Code § 2-501 et seq.), shall issue
50 rules to implement the provisions of this section.”.
51 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
52 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
53 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
54 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
55 Sec. 4. Effective date.
56 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
57 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto) and a 30-day period of congressional review
58 as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
59 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)).
