17 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
18 _______________
19
20 To amend the Advisory Neighborhood Councils Act of 1975 to require the Board of Elections to
21 oversee the filling of a vacancy in an Advisory Neighborhood Commission, to clarify
22 when the Board shall deem a vacancy to have occurred, to clarify the term of a
23 Commissioner filling a vacancy, to prohibit the circulator of a candidate petition form
24 from altering signer information, to require the Board to publish a list a candidates who
25 qualify to fill a vacancy, to require the Board to issue a final determination of vacancy
26 upon review of a request by the Advisory Neighborhood Commission or Office of
27 Advisory Neighborhood Commissions that a vacancy exists or that a Commissioner no
28 longer meets the qualifications for office, and to allow an Advisory Neighborhood to
29 meet to fill a vacancy without meeting quorum; to repeal the Supplementary
30 Neighborhood Commissions Act; and to amend the District of Columbia Election Code
31 of 1955 to require the Board of Elections to oversee the filling of vacancies in Advisory
32 Neighborhood Commissions.
33
34 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
35 act may be cited as the “Board of Elections Certification of ANC Vacancies Amendment Act of
36 2025”.

38 (D.C. Law 1-21; D.C. Official Code § 1-309.01 et seq.), is amended as follows:
39 (a) Section 8 is amended as follows:
40 (1) Subsection (a) is amended to read as follows:

43 year.”.

45 (A) Paragraph (1) is amended to read as follows:

49 Commission filling a vacancy shall serve the remainder of the unexpired term beginning at noon

52 (3) Subsection (d) is amended as follows:

56 “(2) For the purposes of this section, a vacancy is deemed to occur upon the
57 effective date of a notice of resignation or when the Board receives a notice of resignation,
58 whichever is later, or when the Board issues a final determination that a Commissioner no longer

61 resignation or issues a final determination that a Commissioner no longer meets the
62 qualifications set forth in section 6(a).

64 election, within 60 business days after the date that the Board publishes notice of a vacancy, the

70 (D) Paragraph (6) is amended to read as follows:
71 “(6)(A) Within 5 business days after the date that the Board publishes the notice
72 of vacancy in the District of Columbia Register, the Board shall make available petitions for the
73 purpose of obtaining the signatures of registered qualified electors within the affected single-
74 member district.
75 “(B) The petition form shall:
76 “(i) Be made available electronically to any person who files a
77 Declaration of Candidacy with the Board and is confirmed by the Board to be a registered
78 qualified elector residing in the affected single-member district.
79 “(ii) Include an affidavit to be signed and dated by the circulator of
80 the petition indicating that they witnessed each signature and did not write, alter, correct, clarify,
81 or obscure any information about the signer of the petition after the signer affixed their signature
82 to the petition.
83 “(iii) Include a notice that the signature line must be completed in
84 full by a registered qualified elector residing in the affected single-member district and any
85 misrepresentation of name or address may result in a civil fine or criminal prosecution.
3

86 “(C) Within 20 business days after the date that the Board makes the
87 petitions available, persons interested in filling the vacancy shall submit a petition to the Board
88 that contains the signatures of at least 25 registered qualified electors within the affected single-
89 member district. Within one business day after petitions are due, the Board shall publish on its
90 website a list of names of persons who submitted petitions and instructions for challenging a
91 petition. After a 5-business-day challenge period, the Board shall publish on its website a list of
92 the names of persons who submitted petitions who qualify for membership on the affected
93 Advisory Neighborhood Commission.
94 “(D) If there is only one person qualified to fill the vacancy within the
95 affected single-member district, the vacancy shall be deemed filled by the qualified person and
96 the Board shall certify the filling of the vacancy by publication in the District of Columbia
97 Register.
98 “(E)(i) If more than one candidate qualifies to fill the vacancy, the affected
99 Advisory Neighborhood Commission shall hold a public meeting to fill the vacancy. The
100 meeting may be held as a special meeting or as a regularly scheduled meeting, provided at least 2
101 Commissioners are present. The affected Advisory Neighborhood Commission shall notify all
102 qualified candidates at least 15 business days in advance of the meeting and shall post notice of
103 the meeting on their website.
104 “(ii) Each candidate qualified to fill the vacancy shall be afforded
105 at least 10 minutes to address the Commission, after which the Commissioners shall vote in open
106 session to select a candidate. A representative of the Board of Elections shall oversee voting and
107 declare the winner. In the event of a tie vote, the representative shall select a winner by casting
4

108 lots. The Board shall certify the filling of a vacancy pursuant to this subparagraph by publishing
109 notice in the District of Columbia Register.
110 “(iii) Notwithstanding sub-subparagraph (i) of this subparagraph,
111 if the affected single-member district contains the Central Detention Facility and Correctional
112 Treatment Facility, the Board, in consultation with the OANC and the affected Advisory
113 Neighborhood Commission, shall make in-person voting available to qualified registered electors
114 within the single-member district who are not in the care and custody of the Department of
115 Corrections, and the Department of Corrections, in consultation with the Board, shall make
116 voting available to registered qualified electors in its care and custody, including by distributing
117 ballots to registered qualified electors listed as voters in the single-member district on the voter
118 registration list provided by the Board, collecting the ballots, and transmitting the ballots to the
119 Board.”.

135 Board declare a vacancy based on its own determination that a vacancy exists or that a

140 “(3)(A)(i) Within 3 business days after receiving a request filed pursuant to
141 paragraph (2) of this subsection, the Board shall notify the Commissioner by certified mail of the
142 request for a vacancy determination. The notification shall include a copy of the request, the
143 deadline for challenging the request, and the date of a public meeting at which the Board intends
144 to issue a final determination. The Board shall also publish notice of the challenge period and
145 date of the public meeting on its website.

147 pursuant to paragraph (2) of this subsection may challenge the request by submitting a written

150 “(iii) The Board shall send a copy of any challenge received to the
151 OANC and the affected Advisory Neighborhood Commission.
152 “(B) The Board shall issue a final determination at a public meeting held

160 “(D) The District of Columbia Court of Appeals shall expedite
161 consideration of the determination.
