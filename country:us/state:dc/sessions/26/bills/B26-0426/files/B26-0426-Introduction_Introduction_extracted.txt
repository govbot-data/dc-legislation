Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration by the Council of the District of Columbia is the “Holding Company System
Amendment Act of 2025” (“Bill”). The purpose of the Bill is to amend the Holding Company System Act
of 1993 to update the group capital calculation for the purpose of insurance holding company system
group solvency supervision, add a liquidity stress test for macroprudential surveillance and provide the
Commissioner with additional authority over third parties in the event an insurer is in hazardous financial
condition or placed in receivership.
These amendments will enhance the Department of Insurance, Securities and Banking’s (DISB) ability to
oversee and regulate insurance companies in the District and ensure that insurers remain solvent and able
to fulfill their consumer protection obligations. The Bill is also crucial to DISB retaining its accreditation
status as the District’s insurance regulator.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the Bill.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser
