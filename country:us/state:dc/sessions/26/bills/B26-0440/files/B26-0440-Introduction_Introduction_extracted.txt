Dear Secretary Smith,
I am introducing the “Care for Animals Amendment Act of 2025”. Please see enclosed signed
copy of the legislation.
This legislation, which is being aptly introduced during National Pet Wellness Month, will
support the health and wellbeing of pets in the District. This legislation is the result of
meaningful collaboration with the District’s partner in animal care and control services - the
Brandywine Valley SPCA, and the District Department of Health. I am proud to introduce this
legislation with my colleagues Councilmember Charles Allen, Councilmember Janeese Lewis
George, Councilmember Robert C. White, Jr., and Councilmember Brooke Pinto.
The District is full of pet owners, including myself, and on any given day, you are likely to see
residents out walking their dogs or utilizing a dog park, and free-roaming cats exploring their
neighborhood. While there is limited data on the number of dogs in the District, a 2021
preliminary estimate placed the number of cats at approximately 200,000, with about 3,000 of
these designated as feral cats.1 Between January and May 2025, the Committee on Health
reported that the number of animals housed at a District shelter exceeded 2,000.2
This legislation expands the newly-renamed Animal Population Health and Education Program
to include preventive and emergency veterinary services for pet owners in addition to existing
low-cost spay and neuter clinic services, and an educational program for pet owners regarding

Health.

25 To amend the Animal Control Act of 1979 to create an Animal Population Health and Education
26 Program to support veterinary services, sterilization, and pet ownership education, to
27 clarify funding sources and uses for the Animal Education and Outreach Fund, and to
28 establish pet food registration fees; and to amend the Animal Protection Amendment Act
29 of 2008 and the Department of Health Functions Clarification Act of 2001 to create a
30 mechanism for the enforcement of standards for pet care facilities.
31
32 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
33 be cited as the “Care for Animals Amendment Act of 2025”.
34 Sec. 2. The Animal Control Act of 1979, effective October 18, 1979 (D.C. Law 3-30;
35 D.C. Official Code § 8-1801 et seq.) is amended as follows:
36 (a) Section 2 (D.C. Official Code § 8-1801) is amended as follows:
37 (1) New paragraphs 16A, 16B, and 16C are inserted to read as follows:
38 “(16A) “Pet food” means any commercial feed prepared and distributed for
1

39 consumption by dogs and cats.
40 “(16B) “Program” means the Animal Population Health and Education Program
41 established in § 8-1810.
42 “(16C) “Spay or Neuter” means sterilization of a dog or cat by surgical means or
43 FDA approved pharmaceutical methods that remove or alter reproductive function.”.
44 (2) New paragraph 19 is inserted to read as follows:
45 “(19) “Veterinarian” shall have the same meaning as provided in § 3-
46 1201.01(16).”.
47 (b) Section 11 (D.C. Official Code § 8-1810) is amended to read as follows:
48 “Sec. 11. Animal Population Health and Education Program.
49 “(a) The Department of Health shall establish the Animal Population Health and
50 Education Program to support residents with veterinary services and animal control needs. The
51 Program’s goals include:
52 “(1) providing preventive and emergency veterinary services for cats and dogs;
53 “(2) reducing population growth among stray and unwanted cats and dogs;
54 “(3) reducing stray and unwanted cats and dogs entering animal shelters;
55 “(4) reducing cat and dog euthanasia rates;
56 “(5) reducing animal-inflicted injuries to humans (e.g. bites); and
57 “(6) reducing threats to public health and safety (e.g. from rabies and vehicular
58 accidents).
59 “(b) The Program shall provide animal care and education services, including:
60 “(1) Providing preventive and emergency veterinary services;
61 “(2) Providing low-cost spay and neuter clinic services; and
2

62 “(3) Implementing an educational program for animal owners regarding pet care
63 and safety, specifically in extreme weather conditions or emergencies, and on the laws related to
64 pet ownership.”
65 “(c) An individual may qualify to participate in the Program if the individual meets all of
66 the following:
67 “(1) Is 18 years of age or older; and
68 “(2) Is a resident of the District.
69 “(d) The Mayor shall adopt regulations pursuant to this section relative to:
70 “(1) Format and content of all forms required under this section;
71 “(2) Proof of eligibility under subsection (c);
72 “(3) Administration of the Fund established pursuant to section 11a; and
73 “(4) Any other matter necessary for the administration of the Fund under section
74 11a.
75 “(e) The Department of Health shall administer the Program and shall be responsible for:
76 “(1) distributing, collecting and compiling all forms, including but not limited to,
77 veterinarian participation agreements, sterilization and immunization certifications, and creating
78 a database therefrom for enforcement and accountability purposes;
79 “(2) maintaining a list of participating organizations;
80 “(3) determining eligibility;
81 “(4) obtaining the maximum number of spay, neuter and inoculation procedures
82 available to the Program’s financial parameters per calendar year;
83 “(5) and establishing rules and financial parameters per calendar year for the
84 implementation of preventive and emergency veterinarian services.
