16 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
17
18 ______________
19
20 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
21
22 __________________
23
24 To declare the month of February 2026 as “Children’s Dental Health Month” in the District of
25 Columbia, and to recognize the importance of oral health for children and the dedicated
26 work of pediatric dentists and other oral health practitioners, educators, and caregivers
27 across the District.
28
29 WHEREAS, tooth decay is one of the most common chronic conditions of childhood and
30 can cause pain, infection, and missed school when left untreated, yet is largely preventable
31 through regular dental visits, daily oral hygiene, and evidence-based preventive services;
32
33 WHEREAS, children under the age of six are at elevated risk for early childhood caries,
34 and early prevention and timely treatment help protect children’s overall health and readiness to
35 learn;
36
37 WHEREAS, oral health outcomes and access to care reflect longstanding inequities, and
38 Black and African American District residents and communities with lower income experience
39 disproportionate burdens of untreated decay, tooth loss, and dental pain;
40
41 WHEREAS, ensuring that children enrolled in public coverage can receive preventive
42 care is critical, and in FY 2024 approximately 41 percent of District children under age 21
43 enrolled in DC Medicaid received a preventive service from a dentist;
44

45 WHEREAS, preventive interventions such as dental sealants can reduce tooth decay, and
46 strengthening outreach and access to preventive services is an important opportunity to improve
47 children’s oral health outcomes in the District;
48
49 WHEREAS, pediatric dentists and pediatric oral health teams provide specialized, child-
50 centered care, including preventive screenings and sealants and restorative treatment as they help
51 children and families build healthy habits, reduce dental anxiety, and establish lifelong oral
52 health routines;
53
54 WHEREAS, the District is strengthened by an oral health workforce that includes 1,083
55 licensed dentists, with pediatric dentistry among the top reported specialties among dentists
56 practicing in the District, representing 4.9 percent of dentists practicing in DC;
57
58 WHEREAS, DC Health’s School-Based Oral Health Program increases access to clinical
59 and non-clinical dental services, links children to dental homes, and helps reduce the negative
60 impact of poor oral health on student preparedness and readiness to learn;
61
62 WHEREAS, each February, the American Dental Association sponsors National
63 Children’s Dental Health Month to promote good oral hygiene, encourage early preventive care,
64 and support lifelong healthy habits;
65
66 WHEREAS, recognizing Children’s Dental Health Month provides an opportunity to
67 celebrate District providers, schools, and families, and to reaffirm the District’s commitment to
68 ensuring every child can grow up healthy and thrive with a bright smile;
69
70 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
71 resolution may be cited as the “Children’s Dental Health Month Recognition Resolution of
72 2026”.
73
74 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia declares the month of February 2026 as
75 “Children’s Dental Health Month” in the District of Columbia and commends pediatric dentists,
76 oral health practitioners, public health professionals, educators, community providers, and
77 families for their continued efforts to promote prevention, expand access to care, and ensure that
78 every child in the District has the opportunity to achieve lifelong oral health and well-being.
79
80 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
