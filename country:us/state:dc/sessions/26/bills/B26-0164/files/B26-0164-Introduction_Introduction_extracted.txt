MAYOR

The Honorable Phil Mendelson
Chairman

Washington, DC 20004
Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is the
“Rebalancing Expectations for Neighbors, Tenants, and Landlords (RENTAL) Amendment Act
of 2025.” The RENTAL Act will protect the District’s existing affordable housing and
strengthen the local housing ecosystem so that the District can continue to create new affordable
housing, and preserve existing affordable housing, for its residents.
The affordable housing market in Washington, DC is currently facing severe financial distress
and existing affordable units are at risk of foreclosure. This is due in large part to COVID-era
legislative provisions that were made permanent, even though circumstances had changed
significantly, and to procedural inefficiencies and obstacles in pre-existing laws.
The RENTAL Act addresses these issues by rebalancing the COVID-era laws and modernizing
and restructuring previously enacted laws to reduce barriers to affordable housing production and
preservation.
The legislation includes provisions to preserve and produce affordable units, protect tenant
safety, and improve the housing ecosystem to work better for tenants and housing providers. In
these areas, the RENTAL Act will do the following:
• Increase Local Rent Supplement Program (LRSP) Availability – Allows more
housing units to be eligible for the Local Rent Supplement Program support by
expanding eligibility to households with incomes up to 50% of the area median income
(versus the current limit of 30% of area median income).
• Reform the Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERAP) – Makes the Emergency
Rental Assistance Program more sustainable and able to help those it was designed for by
removing the required stay of eviction proceedings in ERAP cases and modifying the
self-attestation provisions.
• Strengthen Tenant Safety Protections – Authorizes eviction if a tenant or other
occupant of a rental unit is arrested or charged with a violent offense that occurred in or
adjacent to the housing accommodation.

• Streamline Court Processes – Modifies eviction proceeding timelines and standards to
be more consistent with pre-pandemic timelines and standards and with neighboring
jurisdictions, while maintaining a fair and robust process for tenants.
• Protect the District's Ability to Support Affordable Housing Projects – Makes
permanent current emergency and temporary legislation clarifying that the District is not
liable for damages as a lender when supporting affordable housing projects.
• Preserve the DC Housing Authority’s Stabilization and Reform (STAR) Board –
Keeps the DC Housing Authority’s temporary STAR Board in effect, and creates a
permanent reform board structure, to provide consistent and sustained leadership in order
to deliver quality, safe, and affordable public housing for District residents.
The RENTAL Act also addresses the investment uncertainty the District is facing and that risks
the future production of housing, including affordable housing. In this area, the RENTAL Act
legislation includes provisions that:
• Modernize the Tenants Opportunity to Purchase Act (TOPA) -- Focuses TOPA on
preventing displacement by protecting naturally occurring affordable housing and
modernizes TOPA by recognizing the current array of complex financing and refinancing
structures.
• Reposition Vacant Properties into Affordable Housing – Gives the Department of
Housing and Community Development the authority to acquire and reposition vacant
properties to increase investment in communities and create more opportunities for
affordable housing developments.
• Maximize Impact of Affordable Housing Funding Programs – Implements technical
measures to allow DHCD to better utilize the District’s low-income housing tax credit for
affordable housing projects.
We must act swiftly to enact these provisions to protect the affordable housing we have worked
diligently to create and to support the production of even more affordable housing for District
residents.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser
