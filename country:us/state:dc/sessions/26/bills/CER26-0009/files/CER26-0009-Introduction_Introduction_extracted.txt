25 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
26 __________
27
28
29 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
30
31 ____________________
32
33 To honor and recognize the Washington Commanders on their remarkable 2024-2025
34 season in the National Football League.
35
36 WHEREAS, the Washington Commanders have experienced a remarkable resurgence in
37 the 2024-2025 NFL season, finishing the regular season with a 12-5 record and securing the sixth
38 seed in the National Football Conference playoffs, a testament to the collective effort and
39 determination of the players, coaches, and staff;
40
41 WHEREAS, Coach Quinn's strategic acumen led the Commanders to their first playoff
42 appearance since 2020 and their first playoff victory since 2005, with a 23–20 win over the
43 Tampa Bay Buccaneers in the Wild Card round;
44
45 WHEREAS, the Commanders continued their remarkable postseason run by defeating
46 the top-seeded Detroit Lions 45–31 in the Divisional Round;

47
48 WHEREAS, rookie quarterback Jayden Daniels has emerged as a transformative leader
49 for the Washington Commanders, completing 69% of his passes for 3,568 yards, throwing 25
50 touchdowns, and rushing for 891 yards and 6 touchdowns during the regular season, showcasing
51 his dual-threat capabilities;
52
53 WHEREAS, the Washington Commanders offensive line has demonstrated remarkable
54 resilience and strength, allowing the fewest sacks in the NFC and providing crucial protection for
55 the quarterback, as well as paving the way for a balanced and dynamic running game;
56
57 WHEREAS, the Commanders' offense tied for the second-most total points in a season in
58 franchise history, scoring 485 points, and ranked among the top three in rushing and total yards;
59
60 WHEREAS, the Commanders’ receiving corps, led by Terry McLaurin, Jahan Dotson,
61 and Curtis Samuel, has consistently delivered explosive plays and key contributions, helping
62 propel the offense to one of the best in the league;
63
64 WHEREAS, the Commanders have shown a strong commitment to community
65 engagement, exemplified by initiatives such as the "Commanders Day of Service," which
66 includes youth football clinics, partnerships with local museums, and support for public charter
67 schools;
68
69 WHEREAS, wide receiver Terry McLaurin set a franchise record with 13 touchdown
70 receptions in a single season, earning him a second-team All-Pro selection;
71
72 WHEREAS, under Head Coach Dan Quinn’s leadership, the Washington Commanders
73 advanced to the NFC Championship Game for the first time since 1991, where their season
74 concluded;
75
76 WHEREAS, on January 30, 1983, the Commanders defeated the Miami Dolphins with a
77 score of 27-17, marking their first Super Bowl victory;
78
79 WHEREAS, on January 31, 1988, the Commanders triumphed over the Denver Broncos
80 with a decisive 42-10 win, showcasing a record-breaking performance, marking their second
81 Super Bowl victory;
82
83 WHEREAS, on January 26, 1992, the Washington Commanders secured their third
84 Super Bowl title by defeating the Buffalo Bills 37-24, demonstrating exceptional skill and
85 teamwork;
86
87 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, this resolution
88 may be cited as the “Washington Commanders Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
89

90 The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the accomplishments of
91 the Washington Commanders and congratulates them on their great season.
92
93 This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
94 District of Columbia Register.
