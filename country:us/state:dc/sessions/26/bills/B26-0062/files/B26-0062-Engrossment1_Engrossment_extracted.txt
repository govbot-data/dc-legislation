12 To amend the District of Columbia School Reform Act of 1995 to require members of a Board of
13 Trustees of a public charter school to complete training programs on best practices for
14 school board governance.
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
17 act may be cited as the “Board of Trustees Training Amendment Act of 2025”.
18 Sec. 2. The District of Columbia School Reform Act of 1995, approved April 26, 1996
19 (110 Stat. 1321-107; D.C. Official Code § 38-1802.01 et seq.), is amended as follows:
20 (a) Section 2205 (D.C. Official Code § 38-1802.05) is amended by adding a new
21 subsection (f) to read as follows:
22 “(f) The Board of Trustees of a public charter school shall complete school governance
23 training consistent with the requirements of section 2214(k).”.
24 (b) Section 2214 (DC Official Code § 38-1802.14) is amended by adding a new
25 subsection (k) to read as follows:
26 “(k) School governance mandatory training. –
1

27 “(1) Beginning in School Year 2026-2027, the Board shall, in coordination with
28 the Office of the State Superintendent as appropriate, offer governance training to all public
29 charter school Boards of Trustees at no cost to a public charter school or its Board of Trustees
30 members. The Board shall require each member of a public charter school Board of Trustees to
31 successfully complete an approved structured training offered pursuant to this subsection
32 annually, and shall require a newly elected or appointed member of a public charter school Board
33 of Trustees to complete an initial training within 90 days after a member’s election or
34 appointment to the Board of Trustees. The Board shall designate which topics identified in
35 paragraph (2) of this subsection are required for a member’s initial training and which are
36 required for the refresher training.
37 “(2) At a minimum, the training offered pursuant to this subsection shall include
38 training on:
39 “(A) The State and Authorizer Academic Accountability System;
40 “(B) Hiring, management, and evaluation of public charter school
41 leadership;
42 “(C) Organizational fiscal management;
43 “(D) Nonprofit and public charter school governance, including the role of
44 a fiduciary;
45 “(E) Obligations of a charter school and the responsibilities of a school
46 governing body under federal and District law and regulation; and

47 “(F) Updates to relevant federal and District laws and regulations.
48 “(4) The training offered pursuant to this subsection may be administered through
49 any modality that ensures accessibility and effective participation, as determined by the Public
50 Charter School Board.”.
51 Sec. 3. Applicability.
52 (a) This act shall apply upon the date of inclusion of its fiscal effect in an approved
53 budget and financial plan.
54 (b) The Chief Financial Officer shall certify the date of the inclusion of the fiscal effect
55 in an approved budget and financial plan and provide notice to the Budget Director of the
56 Council of the certification.
57 (c)(1) The Budget Director shall cause the notice of the certification to be published in
58 the District of Columbia Register.
59 (2) The date of publication of the notice of the certification shall not affect the
60 applicability of this act.
61 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
62 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
63 impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
64 approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
