AMES URNER
EXECUTIVE RESUME
James A. Turner is a seasoned executive and public-service leader with cross-sector
experience spanning health care, health IT, telecommunications, communications,
property development, and nonprofit governance. He is known for delivering measurable
outcomes—leading enterprise risk and privacy initiatives, steering large-scale technology
transformation, and building partnerships across government, community, and industry.
Public Service
Turner is a seven-term elected Advisory Neighborhood Commissioner (ANC) in
Washington, DC, representing a single-member district of approximately 2,000 residents
and advancing a broad portfolio of community priorities.
• Policy focus areas: transportation and parking; public safety; sanitation services;
zoning and liquor licensing; neighborhood infrastructure; economic development;
and District budget oversight.
• Leadership: four terms as Treasurer and seven terms as Chair of ANC 1B (12-
member commission representing 24,000 residents); principal spokesperson to the
Mayor, Council, District agencies, federal partners, and community stakeholders.
• Selected by Councilmember Brianne Nadeau to co-chair the Ward 1 redistricting
committee; helped restructure four ANCs into five to better align representation
with core neighborhoods.
• Twice appointed by Mayor Muriel Bowser to three-year terms on the District of
Columbia Health Information Exchange Advisory Board.
Leadership Experience
• Verizon — Executive Advisor, Government Relations & Corporate Strategy:
Advanced business opportunities tied to federal and state Health Information
Technology (HIT) initiatives and emerging market trends; represented Verizon at
congressional briefings and before the Office of the National Coordinator for
Health IT and the Federal Communications Commission.
• National coalitions: Served on health care coalitions and the Chronic Care
Workgroup of the American Health Information Community (AHIC), contributing to
efforts that helped establish the National eHealth Collaborative (NeHC), AHIC’s
successor organization.
• Verizon — Senior Leadership Roles (including Chief of Staff to the CIO): Led
portfolio responsibilities spanning Corporate Strategy, Corporate Security, Public
Relations, Media and Public Affairs, and the Verizon Foundation.

• Enterprise privacy & information management: Led a privacy and sensitive-data
protection initiative—assessing enterprise risk and implementing an expanded
information management program.
• Technology transformation: Program-managed a $250M strategic transformation,
delivering more than $53M in annualized savings across software, hardware, and
energy spend.
• Small Business: Investor and advisory board of three small business enterprises in
retail, real estate development, and technology services.
• Corporate strategy & growth: Represented the CIO organization in corporate
strategy, business development, and acquisition initiatives.
Community & Board Service
• Hope and a Home (Board Member): Supports a local housing, education, and food
security organization helping families move from homelessness to long-term
stability within three to five years.
• African American Civil War Museum Committee of 100: Helps guide the
museum’s reopening celebration in 2025.
• Whitman-Walker Health (Former Board Director): Spearheaded implementation
of an electronic health record and practice management system.
• Advisory roles: Served as an advisory board member for two community health
clinics, the DC Primary Care Association, and the DC Health Information
Exchange—strengthening governance and cross-organizational collaboration.
• Civic and political engagement: Advised local, state, and federal campaigns,
including as a campaign advisor to a DC mayoral candidate.
Education & Recognition
• George Washington University: Through the George Washington Society,
endowed a graduate scholarship to advance nonprofit leadership development.
• Honors: Twice named a Kentucky Colonel by the Governor of Kentucky in
recognition of philanthropic leadership and sustained community service.
• Education: Master of Jurisprudence (Health Law and Policy), Loyola University
Chicago School of Law; Master of General Administration (Management
Information Systems), University of Maryland; Bachelor of Business Administration
(Data Processing, Finance, and Banking), Morehead State University.
• Additional study: Completed two professional programs in international business
and development at Georgetown University.

James Turner
Mr. James Turner is a former seven-term elected Advisory Neighborhood
Commissioner of ANC1A09. He served as Treasurer for four terms and
Chair for seven terms on ANC 1B, a twelve-member Commission, and
the Commission's spokesperson to the Mayor, Council, and Independent
Agencies of the District of Columbia and the Federal Government.
Councilmember Nadeau selected him to co-chair the Ward One
redistricting committee, which resulted in the redistricting of four ANCs
into five to align and focus on core neighborhoods. Turner was twice
appointed to a three-year term by Mayor Muriel Bowser to serve on the
District of Columbia Health Information Exchange Advisory Board.
Mr. Turner served as Executive Advisor assigned to the Verizon
Government Relations and the Verizon Corporate Strategy organizations to identify the business
opportunities related to federal and state Health Information Technology (HIT) legislative
initiatives and future market trends. He has represented Verizon at Congressional briefings, on
Health Care Coalitions, at the Office of the National Coordinator for Health Information
Technology, before the Federal Communications Commission, providing input and monitoring
evolving HIT policies and standards, and by serving on the Chronic Care Workgroup of the
American Health Information Committee (AHIC).
Mr. Turner is actively involved in the community and with political organizations in the
Washington, DC area. He served on the board of Hope and a Home, a local housing, education,
and food security provider, moving families from homelessness to sustainability within three to
five years, and on the Board of Directors of Whitman Walker Health, where he spearheaded an
electronic health record and practice management system. Mr. Turner also served as an advisory
board member of two Community Health Clinics, and the DC Primary Care Association.
A Ward 1 resident, Mr. Turner earned his Bachelor of Business Administration from Morehead
State University, his Master of Business Administration from the University of Maryland, and his
Master of Laws from Loyola University.
