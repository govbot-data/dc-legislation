12 To amend the Health Services Planning Program Re-establishment Act of 1996 to exempt from
13 the certificate of need process digital-only telehealth platforms and providers, federally
14 qualified health centers, outpatient or residential behavioral health services, and primary
15 care, dental care, and specialty care providers, to define group practice and nonpatient
16 care project, to remove the 3 year maximum on certificate of need applications for active
17 projects, to increase the capital expenditure thresholds, and to require the State Health
18 Planning and Development Agency to update the capital expenditure and medical
19 equipment spending thresholds every 2 years, to increase the number of beds that must be
20 added or removed at a health care facility in order to trigger a certificate of need, to
21 require the Department of Health to create a registration process for entities offering
22 primary, dental, and specialty care, virtual provider networks, virtual telehealth
23 platforms, and federally qualified health centers, and to require the Department of Health
24 to post information about certificate of need applications for the previous 3 years on its
25 website; to amend the District of Columbia Health Occupations Revision Act of 1985 to
26 rename the Board of Integrative Healthcare to the Board of Acupuncture, Chiropractic,
27 and Naturopathic Physicians, to decrease the size of the Board of Acupuncture,
28 Chiropractic, and Naturopathic Physicians from 9 to 7 members, to create a pathway for
29 graduate social workers to become licensed without passing an examination, and to
30 clarify that cottage food businesses can sell products wholesale; and to make other
31 technical amendments.
32
33 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
34 act may be cited as the “Certificate of Need Improvement Amendment Act of 2025.”
