13 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
14 __________
15
16 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
17 ____________________
18
19
20 To recognize and honor Laurice Gadson Redhead on the occasion of her eightieth birthday for
21 her lifelong contributions to education, faith, civic engagement, and community service
22 in the District of Columbia and beyond.
23
24 WHEREAS, Laurice Gadson Redhead was born on July 2, 1946, in Washington, DC, and
25 will celebrate her eightieth birthday in 2026;
26
27 WHEREAS, Ms. Redhead is a proud alumna of Holy Comforter School and a graduate of
28 Howard University, institutions that have long served as pillars of education and leadership in the
29 District;
30
31 WHEREAS, Ms. Redhead has been a devoted and respected member of the St. Augustine
32 Catholic Church, and has served as a member of the Cemetery Rededication Committees for the
33 Epiphany, Holy Rood, and Holy Trinity Churches in Georgetown;
34
35 WHEREAS, Laurice Gadson Redhead has been a longstanding member of the
36 Association for the Study of African American Life and History and the DC History Center, and
37 has generously volunteered her time by serving as a Docent at the National Museum of African
38 American History and Culture, helping to spread awareness of the history and contributions of
39 African Americans;
40
41 WHEREAS, Ms. Redhead has made significant contributions to education in the District
42 by mentoring and teaching District of Columbia Public School students and by mentoring
43 Howard University students through the University’s former Center for Academic
44 Reinforcement;
1

45
46 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this resolution
47 may be cited as the “Laurice Gadson Redhead Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
48
49 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia celebrates the milestone of Ms.
50 Redhead’s eightieth birthday and recognizes her lifelong commitment to education, community
51 service, and civic engagement in the District of Columbia.
52
53 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
54 the District of Columbia Register.
