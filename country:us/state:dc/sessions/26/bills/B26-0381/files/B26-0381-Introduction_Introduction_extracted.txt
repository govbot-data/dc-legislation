17 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Office of Citizen Complaint Review Establishment Act of
18 1998 to expand the membership of the Police Complaints Board, and to allow the Office
19 of Police Complaints’ Executive Director to initiate their own complaint if they discover
20 evidence of abuse or misuse of police powers that was not alleged in the original complaint,
21 including the failure to intervene or report to a supervisor when another officer used
22 excessive force, engaged in other forms of misconduct, or violated a rule or regulation; and
23 to amend Chapter 3 of Title 14 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify that
24 certain provisions only apply to a victim who is under the age of 18.
25
26 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
27 act may be cited as the “Comprehensive Policing and Justice Reform Technical Emergency
28 Amendment Act of 2025”.
29 Sec. 2. The Office of Citizen Complaint Review Establishment Act of 1998, effective
30 March 26, 1999 (D.C. Law 12-208; D.C. Official Code § 5-1101 et seq.), is amended as follows:
31 (a) Section 4 (D.C. Official Code § 5-1103) is amended by adding a new paragraph (3B)
32 to read as follows:
33 “(3B) “MPD” means the Metropolitan Police Department.”.
1

34 (b) Section 5(a) (D.C. Official Code § 5-1104(a)) is amended by striking the phrase
35 “There is established a Police Complaints Board (“Board”). The Board shall be composed of 5
36 members, one of whom shall be a member of the MPD, and 4 of whom shall have no current
37 affiliation with any law enforcement agency.” and inserting the phrase “There is established a
38 Police Complaints Board. The Board shall be composed of 9 members, which shall include one
39 member from each Ward and one at-large member, none of whom, after the expiration of the
40 term of the currently serving member of the MPD, shall be affiliated with any law enforcement
41 agency.” in its place.
42 (c) Section 8 (D.C. Official Code § 5-1107) is amended as follows:
43 (1) A new subsection (g-1) is added to read as follows:
44 “(g-1)(1) If the Executive Director discovers evidence of abuse or misuse of police
45 powers that was not alleged by the complainant in the complaint, the Executive Director may:
46 “(A) Initiate the Executive Director’s own complaint against the subject
47 police officer; and
48 “(B) Take any of the actions described in subsection (g)(2) through (6) of
49 this section.
50 “(2) The authority granted pursuant to paragraph (1) of this subsection shall
51 include circumstances in which the subject police officer failed to:
52 “(A) Intervene in or subsequently report any use of force incident in which
53 the subject police officer observed another law enforcement officer, including an MPD officer,
54 utilizing excessive force or engaging in any type of misconduct, pursuant to MPD General Order
55 901.07, its successor directive, or a similar local or federal directive; or
2

56 “(B) Immediately report to their supervisor any violations of the rules and
57 regulations of the MPD committed by any other MPD officer, and each instance of their use of
58 force or a use of force committed by another MPD officer, pursuant to MPD General Order
59 201.26, or any successor directive.”.
60 (2) Subsection (h) is amended by striking the phrase “subsection (g)” and
61 inserting the phrase “subsection (g) or (g-1)” in its place.
62 Sec. 3. Chapter 3 of Title 14 of the District of Columbia Official Code is amended as
63 follows:
64 (a) Section 14-310(b)(4) is amended as follows:
65 (1) Subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase “whom the victim has”
66 and inserting the phrase “whom a victim under 18 years of age has” in its place.
67 (2) Subparagraph (C) is amended by striking the phrase “the victim” and inserting
68 the phrase “a victim who is under 18 years of age” in its place.
69 (b) Section 14-311(b)(4) is amended as follows:
70 (1) Subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase “with whom the victim

72 (2) Subparagraph (C) is amended by striking the phrase “the victim” and inserting
73 the phrase “a victim who is under 18 years of age” in its place.
74 (c) Section 14-312(b)(4) is amended as follows:
75 (1) Subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase “with whom the victim
76 has” and inserting the phrase “with whom a sexual assault victim under 18 years of age has” in
77 its place.
