Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is an
emergency bill, the “Modification Nos. M0013 and M0014 to Contract No. CW100309 with The
National Center for Children and Families, Inc. Approval and Payment Authorization Emergency
Act of 2025,” and the accompanying emergency declaration resolution in the amount of not to
exceed $1,035,556.24.
The legislation will approve Modification Nos. M0013 and M0014 to Contract CW100309 with
The National Center for Children and Families, Inc. to provide Permanent Supportive Housing III
program case management services, and to fully exercise option year three of the contract. In
addition, the legislation will approve payment for the goods and services received and to be
received under the modifications.
My administration is available to discuss any questions you may have regarding the proposed
contract modification. In order to facilitate a response to any questions you may have, please have
your staff contact Marc Scott, Chief Operating Officer, Office of Contracting and Procurement, at
(202) 724-8759.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.

(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The Government of the District of Columbia, Office of Contracting and Procurement, on behalf of
the Department of Human Services, is seeking to exercise the remaining option in year three for
The National Center for Children and Families, Inc., to continue to provide Permanent Supportive
Housing III (PSH3) program case management services, utility assistance, and financial assistance
to chronically homeless and other highly vulnerable individuals and families experiencing
homelessness.
The purpose of this program is to assist residents with histories of chronic homelessness in
transitioning from the street or temporary living situation into stable and permanent housing,
maintaining long-term housing, and achieving self-sufficiency. The success of the aforementioned
initiative will be achieved by referring and connecting participants to supportive services that will
address their barriers to obtaining and maintaining permanent housing and achieving an optimal
level of self-sufficiency.
(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:
The solicitation was advertised in the Washington Times on April 8, 2022. RFQ Doc575024 was
issued in the open market on April 11, 2022, with a closing date of May 17, 2022. The District
received a total of 28 submissions. Of the 28 responses, 23 were deemed qualified and the vendors
were notified on June 23, 2022.
The responses were evaluated in accordance with the qualification criteria outlined in the
solicitation. The qualification evaluation factors were PSH3 design, case management,
organizational capacity, and past performance. The National Center for Children and Families,
Inc., met the qualifications indicated in the RFQ and has a satisfactory rating, providing similar
services through current and prior HCAs with the District.
The contracting officer awarded an HCA on July 1, 2022, after determining The National Center for
Children and Families, Inc. was qualified and responsible, and the proposed price was determined
to be fair and reasonable.
