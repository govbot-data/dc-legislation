18 To declare an emergency with respect to the need to amend Chapter 3 of Title 20 of the District of
19 Columbia Official Code to amend the definition of abbreviated probate, to authorize the
20 Register of Wills to refer proceedings to the Court to determine if one or more personal
21 representatives should be appointed and the will be admitted to probate, and to strike the
22 reference to will admission in transfers by affidavit; and to amend the Strengthening
23 Probate Amendment Act of 2024 to clarify that the act applies to estates for whom the
24 decedent died on or after March 21, 2025.
25
26 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
27 resolution may be cited as the “Strengthening Probate Administration Emergency Declaration
28 Resolution of 2025”.
29 Sec. 2. (a) The District’s probate system has long been criticized for being outmoded,
30 having not been updated since 2001. Economically-vulnerable residents are encumbered by an
31 outdated and antiquated system while enduring the loss of a loved one. The D.C. Estate
32 Administration Working Group (“Working Group”) produced an extensive joint report in
33 February 2022 outlining the need to modernize the District’s probate system. In addition to
34 District residents having to face an arcane and outdated system, the Working Group observed
35 that 97% of small estates (those valued at $40,000 or less) are pursued by pro se litigants, that is,
36 those who are not represented by legal counsel. Last, the District’s outdated and complex District
1

37 probate system has caused assets and property to languish and remain irretrievable to
38 beneficiaries due to system inefficiencies.
39 (b) Because an inefficient probate system contributes to economic uncertainty and wealth
40 loss for District residents, on December 17, 2024, the Council passed on final reading the
41 Strengthening Probate Administration Amendment Act of 2024, effective March 21, 2005 (D.C.
42 Law 25-302; 72 DCR 780) (“Strengthening Probate Act”), which proposed to modernize and
43 update the legal and administrative processes and procedures around probate for District
44 residents and estate lawyers consistent with the recommendations of the Working Group’s 2022
45 report. These important updates included:
46 (1) Allowing certain letters of appointment designating personal representatives to
47 be issued by the Register of Wills, instead of relying only on judges to issue these letters;
48 (2) Streamlining the publication notice requirement;
49 (3) Raising the maximum threshold for “small estates”;
50 (4) Increasing allowable reimbursement rates for funeral expenses;
51 (5) Allowing for transfers of small estates by affidavit;
52 (6) Increasing homestead and family exemption allowances; and
53 (7) Adding a fee waiver provision for personal representatives.
54 (c) The Strengthening Probate Act requires technical amendments in order to be
55 implemented. The current statutory language has proven inadministrable and unclear, leading to
56 delays in appointing personal representatives and precluding access to decedents’ estate assets.
57 Important pieces of the law are, practically speaking, suspended.
58 (d) In particular, the Strengthening Probate Act needs to be amended to establish
59 administrability in probate processes and obviate delays in the timely appointment of personal
60 representatives. This emergency legislation would:
2

61 (1) Restore language that was unintentionally removed regarding the nature of
62 probate proceedings;
63 (2) Authorize the Register of Wills or the Court to issue letters of administration
64 and appoint personal representatives, so that if a petition is deemed insufficient by the Register
65 of Wills, it may be submitted to a judge for review and appointment;
66 (3) Clarify that the Strengthening Probate Act applies to estates for whom the
67 decedent died on or after March 21, 2025; and
68 (4) Strike a reference to a will being “duly admitted to probate” in the transfer by
69 affidavit section, in order to allow individuals to transfer by affidavit in the event that the probate
70 process does not need to be initiated, which aligns with the law’s purpose of allowing for
71 property transfer external to the probate process.
72 (e) Given that the Council is due to recess in the coming month, the legislative process
73 will take further time to move permanent technical fixes for this law. Emergency legislation is
74 necessary to allow the Strengthening Probate Act to actually be implementable, preventing
75 delays and access to estate access issues for a law already in effect. These changes represent an
76 urgent and necessary correction and clarification, and the emergency nature of this legislation is
77 justified by the immediate need for the courts to administer probate and to ensure that District
78 residents can pursue probate and benefit from an updated and modernized system.
79 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
80 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
81 Strengthening Probate Administration Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a
82 single reading.
83 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
