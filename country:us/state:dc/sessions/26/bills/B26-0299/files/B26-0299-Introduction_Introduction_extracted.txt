16 To amend, on an emergency basis, section 25-113 of the District of Columbia Official Code to
17 continue the extension of the Streatery Program endorsements through December 31,
18 2025, and to clarify endorsement deadlines for certain classes of licensees; and to amend
19 the Legalization of Marijuana for Medical Treatment Initiative of 1999 to make a
20 clarifying amendment.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
23 act may be cited as the “Streatery Program Endorsement Extension and Medical Cannabis
24 Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2025”.
25
26 Sec. 2. Section 25-113(a)(6) of the District of Columbia Code is amended as follows:
27 (a) Strike the phrase “2022 and 2023” wherever it appears and insert the phrase “2022,
28 2023, 2024, and through December 31, 2025” in its place.
29 (b) Subparagraph (A)(i)(II) is amended by striking the semicolon and inserting the
30 phrase “annually with the fee for 2025 due by April 1, 2025; except, that an on-premises retailer
31 licensee, class C/T, D/T, C/N, or D/N, or a manufacturer licensee class B, shall pay the fee for
32 2025 by September 30, 2025;” in its place.
1

33 Sec. 3. Section 7(q)(1) the Legalization of Marijuana for Medical Treatment Initiative of
34 1999, effective July 27, 2010 (D.C. Law 18-210; D.C. Official Code § 7-1671.06(q)(1)), is
35 amended to read as follows:
36 “(q)(1)(A) A retailer or internet retailer shall not locate within any residential district or
37 within 400 feet of a preschool, primary or secondary school, or recreation center; except, that a
38 license holder or an applicant who has applied prior to the effective date of the Medical Cannabis
39 Clarification and Program Enforcement Amendment Act of 2024, passed on 2nd reading on
40 October 1, 2024 (Enrolled version of Bill 25-581), shall not be permitted to locate within 300
41 feet of a preschool, primary or secondary school, or recreation center.
42 “(B) Notwithstanding paragraph (A) of this paragraph, an unlicensed
43 establishment license holder or applicant that applied for a retailer or internet retailer license
44 during the 90-calendar day open application period set forth in section 7a(a)(1) D.C. Official
45 Code § 7-1671.06a(a)(1)) shall be permitted to locate within 300 feet of a preschool, primary or
46 secondary school, or recreation center where the main entrance to the preschool, primary school
47 or secondary school, or recreation center is actually on or occupies ground zoned commercial or
48 industrial according to the official atlases of the Zoning Commission of the District of
49 Columbia.”.
50 Sec. 4. Repealers.
51 (a) Sections 2 and 3 of the Streatery Program Endorsement Extension Emergency
52 Amendment Act of 2024, effective December 19, 2025 (D.C. Act 25-669; 71 DCR 16311), are
53 repealed.
2

54 (b) Sections 2 and 3 of the Streatery Program Endorsement Extension Temporary
55 Amendment Act of 2024, effective December 19, 2025 (D.C. Law 25-294; 72 DCR 3105), are
56 repealed.
57 Sec. 5. Fiscal impact statement.
58 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
59 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
60 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
61 Sec. 6. Effective date.
62 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
63 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), and shall remain in effect for no longer than
64 90 days, as provided for emergency acts of the Council of the District of Columbia in section
65 412(a) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 788;
66 D.C. Official Code § 1-204.12(a)).
