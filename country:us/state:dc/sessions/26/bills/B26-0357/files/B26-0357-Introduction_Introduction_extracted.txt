(K) The reason why the proposed change was sent to Council for approval after it is
intended to take effect:
When it was announced that Cedar Hill’s opening would be delayed by fifteen days, the
Not-for-Profit Hospital Corporation prepared Modification 2 for $53,618.00, and sought to
extend the contract from April 1, 2025 to April 15, 2025. This proposed extension
necessitated Council approval, because when combined with Modification 1 partially
exercising Option Year 1 of this contract ($997,649.00; August 1, 2024 – March 31, 2025)
the total retroactive value of Option Year 1 would be $1,051,267.00. Unfortunately, United
Medical Center was unable to seek Council approval prior to recess because the vendor did
not obtain its updated Clean Hands Certification until July 8, 2025.
As United Medical Center continued its closure and dissolution preparation activities during
recess, Contract NFPHC-OPS-23-C-00059 was found to be trending over budget due to the
impact of inflation on the cost of food and higher than estimated utilization. These higher
costs, which totaled $93,466.00, are captured in Proposed Modification 3 and if approved
along with Proposed Modification 2, would bring the total Option Year 1 contract amount to
$1,144,733.00.
United Medical Center is submitting this legislation to Council for approval at its first
legislative meeting after its recess. Emergency approval is required to approve these
modifications at Council’s September 17, 2025 meeting, because the Not-for-Profit Hospital
Corporation will statutorily dissolve at the end of the day on September 30, 2025, which is
prior to the next legislative meeting of the Council. Emergency approval is required to
approve these modifications at Council’s September 17, 2025 meeting, because the Not-for-
Profit Hospital Corporation will statutorily dissolve at the end of the day on September 30,
2025, which is prior to the next legislative meeting of the Council of the District of
Columbia.
