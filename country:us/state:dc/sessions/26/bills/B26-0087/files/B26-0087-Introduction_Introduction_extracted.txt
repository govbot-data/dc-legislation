13 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Open Meetings Act of 2010 to provide that a meeting
14 shall be deemed open to the public if the public body takes steps reasonably calculated to
15 allow the public to view or hear the meeting while the meeting is taking place, or, if
16 doing so is not technologically feasible, as soon thereafter as reasonably practicable.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
19 act may be cited as the “Virtual Open Meetings Authority Extension Emergency Amendment Act
20 of 2025”.
21 Sec. 2. The Open Meetings Act of 2010, effective March 31, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-350;
22 D.C. Official Code § 2-571 et seq.), is amended as follows:
23 (a) Section 405(a) (D.C. Official Code § 2-575(a)) is amended as follows:
24 (1) Paragraph (2) is amended by striking the phrase “; or” and inserting a
25 semicolon in its place.
26 (2) Paragraph (3) is amended by striking the period and inserting the phrase “; or”
27 in its place.
28 (3) A new paragraph (4) is added to read as follows:
29 “(4) The public body takes steps reasonably calculated to allow the public to view
30 or hear the meeting while the meeting is taking place, or, if doing so is not technologically
31 feasible, as soon thereafter as reasonably practicable.”.
32 (b) Section 407(a)(1) (D.C. Official Code § 2-577(a)(1)) is amended by striking the
1

33 phrase “attend the meeting;” and inserting the phrase “attend the meeting, or steps are taken that
34 are reasonably calculated to allow the public to view or hear the meeting while the meeting is
35 taking place, or, if doing so is not technologically feasible, as soon thereafter as reasonably
36 practicable;” in its place.
37 Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
38 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
39 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
40 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
41 Sec. 4. Effective date.
42 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
43 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), and shall remain in effect for no longer than
44 90 days, as provided for emergency acts of the Council of the District of Columbia in section
45 412(a) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 788;
46 D.C. Official Code § 1-204.12(a)).
