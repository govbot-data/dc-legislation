15 To establish as an independent agency the Office of Neighborhood Engagement and Safety
16 Agency to coordinate the District’s violence prevention efforts; to amend the
17 Neighborhood Engagement Achieves Results Amendment Act of 2016 and the Attorney
18 General for the District of Columbia Clarification and Elected Term Amendment Act of
19 2010 to make conforming amendments; to amend the Office of the Deputy Mayor for
20 Public Safety and Justice Establishment Act of 2011 to study on how to enhance
21 recruitment tactics for youth prospects for the Metropolitan Police Departments and
22 enhance retention of senior officers; to amend the Omnibus Police Reform Amendment
23 Act of 2000 to clarify the standards of eligibility to be a member of the Metropolitan
24 Police Department; and to amend the Government Employer-Assisted Housing
25 Amendment Act of 1999 to enhance the First Responder benefits for the Employee-
26 Assisted Housing Program.

28 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
29 act may be cited as the “Safe Neighborhoods Amendment Act of 2025”.
30 TITLE I. THE OFFICE OF NEIGHBORHOOD ENGAGEMENT AND SAFETY
31 AGENCY.
32 Sec. 101. Office of Neighborhood Engagement and Safety Agency; establishment.
33 There is established as an independent government agency, the Office of Neighborhood
34 Engagement and Safety Agency (“ONES”), which shall be headed by a Director. Within ONES
35 shall be Office of the Office of Violence Prevention and Health Equity and the Office of
36 Neighborhood Safety and Engagement (“NEAR Offices”), established by the Neighborhood
1

37 Engagement Achieves Results Amendment Act of 2016, effective June 30, 2016 (D.C. Law 21-
38 125; D.C. Official Code § 7–2411 et seq.), (“NEAR Act”).
39 Sec. 102. Purpose, duties.
40 (a) The purpose of ONES shall include to coordinate and lead and direct the NEAR
41 Offices.
42 Sec. 103. Director; appointment, term.
43 The Director shall:
44 (1) Have at least 5 years of relevant experience in criminal justice and public
45 health-based approaches to violence, including matters affecting the deterrence of violent
46 criminal behavior;
47 (2) Be appointed by the Mayor with the advice and consent of the Council,
48 pursuant to section 2(a) of the Confirmation Act of 1978, effective March 3, 19790 (D.C. Law 2-
49 142; D.C. Official Code § 1-523.01(a)); and
50 (3) Serve a 6-year term.
51 Sec. 104. Director, duties.
52 The Director shall:
53 (1) Also be the director of the NEAR Offices;
54 (2) Administer the Office of Neighborhood Engagement and Safety
55 Administration Fund, established by section 106; and
56 (3) Administer the Cure the Streets Program, established pursuant to section 108f
57 of the Omnibus Public Safety and Justice Amendment Act of 2018, effective May 10, 2019
58 (D.C. Law 25-98, D.C. Official Code § 1–301.88f).
2

59 Sec 105. Office of Neighborhood Engagement and Safety Administration Fund;
60 establishment.
61 (a) There is established as a special fund the Office of Neighborhood Engagement and
62 Safety Administration Fund ("Fund"), which shall be administered by the Director of the Office
63 of Neighborhood Engagement and Safety Agency (“ONES”) in accordance with subsections (c)
64 and (d) of this section.
65 (b) Revenue from the following sources shall be deposited in the Fund:
66 (1) Funds appropriated by the District;
67 (2) Grants;
68 (3) Donations from the public; and
69 (4) Donations from private entities.
70 (c) Money in the Fund shall be used to fund the activities of the ONES, including:
71 (1) Providing stipends to eligible participants; and
72 (2) Appropriate overhead or administrative expenses related to the administration
73 ONES and the Fund.
74 (d)(1) The money deposited into the Fund, and interest earned, shall not revert to the
75 unrestricted fund balance of the General Fund of the District of Columbia at the end of a fiscal
76 year, or at any other time.
77 (2) Subject to authorization in an approved budget and financial plan, any funds
78 appropriated in the Fund shall be continually available without regard to fiscal year limitation.
