10 To declare the existence of an emergency, due to congressional review, with respect to the need
11 to amend the Residential Tranquility Act of 2010 to prohibit persons targeting a residence
12 for purposes of a demonstration from using sound amplifying devices in a residential
13 zone between 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m., and to prohibit a person from launching or
14 throwing a projectile onto the residential property of another with the intent to cause fear,
15 intimidate, or retaliate against any person living or working at that residence; and to
16 amend the National Capital Revitalization and Self-Government Improvement Act of
17 1997 to provide that the term expiration date for a Council appointment to the
18 Corrections Information Council shall be December 7th of the year in which the term
19 expires.
20 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
21 resolution may be cited as the “Residential Tranquility Congressional Review Emergency
22 Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
23 Sec. 2. (a) On October 29, 2024, the Council adopted the Residential Tranquility
24 Emergency Amendment Act of 2024, effective November 4, 2024 (D.C. Act 25-622; 71 DCR
25 13558) (“emergency act”), which expires on February 2, 2025.
26 (b) On November 12, 2024, the Council adopted the Residential Tranquility Temporary
27 Amendment Act of 2024, enacted on November 22, 2024 (D.C. Act 25-640, 71 DCR 14480)
28 (“temporary act”), which has not yet completed congressional review.
29 (c) This congressional review emergency legislation is necessary to prevent a gap in the
30 law between the expiration of the emergency act and the effective date of the temporary act.
1

31 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
32 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
33 Residential Tranquility Congressional Review Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted
34 after a single reading.
35 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
