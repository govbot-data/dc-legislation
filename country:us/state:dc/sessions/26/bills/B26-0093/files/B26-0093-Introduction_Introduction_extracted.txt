19 To amend the Security Alarms Systems Regulations Act of 1980 to prohibit, as of July 1, 2025,
20 the sale of smoke detectors that are not tamper-resistant in the District of Columbia, and
21 to establish a process by which a person may file a complaint alleging a violation of this
22 subsection.
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
25 act may be cited as the “Smoke Detector Safety Amendment Act of 2025”.
26 Sec. 2. Section 9 of the Security Alarms Systems Regulations Act of 1980, effective Sept.
27 26, 1980 (D.C. Law 3-107, D.C. Official Code § 7-2808), is amended as follows:
28 (a) A new subsection (d) is added to read as follows:
29 “(d)(1) Effective July 1, 2025, no person shall sell, offer for sale, install for a fee, or offer
30 to install for a fee a solely battery-operated smoke detector that is not tamper-resistant in the
31 District of Columbia.
32 “(2) A civil fine not to exceed $500, penalties, and fees may be imposed as a
33 sanction for any infraction of this subsection, pursuant to Titles I through III of the Department
34 of Consumer and Regulatory Affairs Civil Infractions Act of 1985, effective October 5, 1985
35 (D.C. Law 6-42; D.C. Official Code § 2-1801.01 et seq.).
1

36 “(3)(A) Any person may file with the Mayor a complaint alleging an individual or
37 company sold, offered for sale, installed for a fee, or offered to install for a fee a smoke detector
38 product in violation of this subsection.
39 “(B) A complaint filed pursuant to sub-subparagraph (i) of this
40 subparagraph shall be submitted in writing on a form prescribed by the Mayor and made
41 available on the District of Columbia website. The complaint shall include:
42 “(i) The name of the individual or company alleged to have
43 sold, offered for sale, installed for a fee, or offered to install for a fee a smoke detector product in
44 violation of this subsection.
45 “(ii) The location of the alleged violation;
46 “(iii) The date and time of the alleged violation; and
47 “(iv) Any additional identifying information regarding the
48 violation.
49 “(C) A District inspector need not witness a violation for a complaint to be
50 valid.
51 “(D) A complainant under paragraph this subparagraph may appear and
52 give testimony at any administrative hearing or administrative review of the complaint, or any
53 other judicial or quasi-judicial action that may result from the complaint.
54 “(E)(i) If the Mayor determines that the complaint has merit, the
55 Mayor shall file a Notice of Infraction and proceed pursuant to the Department of Consumer and
56 Regulatory Affairs Civil Infractions Act of 1985, effective October 5, 1985 (D.C. Law 6-42;
57 D.C. Official Code § 2-1801.01 et seq.).
58 “(ii) The Mayor shall provide a copy of the Notice of Infraction to
59 the Office of Administrative Hearings.
2

60 “(4) For the purposes of this subsection, the term:
61 “(A) “Smoke detector” means a device of the ionization or photoelectric
62 type used to assess high levels of combustion nearby and shall include fire alarms and smart
63 home products that provide this functionality.
64 “(B) “Tamper-resistant” means a smoke detector designed such that the
65 battery powering the smoke detector cannot be manually removed, replaced, or disabled by the
66 consumer.”.
