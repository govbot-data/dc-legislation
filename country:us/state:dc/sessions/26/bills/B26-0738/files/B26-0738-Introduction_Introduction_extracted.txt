(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The contractor provided the heavy equipment, management, personnel, and transportation needed to
remove heavy accumulations of snow and ice across primary and secondary roadways, bridges,
intersections, and critical access routes within the District.
(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:

The District solicited quotes from multiple firms that demonstrated experience in the required
services and the ability to perform the work immediately. The District received a quote from
Strittmatter. Strittmatter has the availability and capacity to begin the work immediately. Therefore,
Strittmatter’s quote was determined to be the most advantageous to the District, given the nature of
the emergency.

For over 17 years, Strittmatter has provided excavation, demolition, and equipment rental services
for government, commercial, and residential projects in the District of Columbia. Furthermore,
Strittmatter has the capacity, personnel, and equipment necessary to begin the work immediately.

(L) The reason why the proposed change was sent to Council for approval after it is intended to
take effect:
The major winter storm that began on January 25, 2026, followed by frigid temperatures, created
hardened, compacted ice that obstructed transportation corridors, emergency access, and public
operations in the District. The snowy and icy conditions posed an imminent threat to public health
and safety by restricting and delaying access to essential services.
(M) The reason for the proposed change:
A letter contract was awarded to begin the work immediately to restore access to the District’s
transportation infrastructure.
