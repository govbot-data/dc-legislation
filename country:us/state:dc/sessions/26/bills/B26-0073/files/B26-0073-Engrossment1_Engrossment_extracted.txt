12 To direct local education agencies to adopt and implement a personal wireless communication
13 device policy that prohibits a student from using a personal wireless communication
14 device during the full school day.
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
17 act may be cited as the “Disconnect Act of 2025”.
18 Sec. 2. Definitions.
19 For the purposes of this act, the term:
20 (1) “Full School Day” shall have the same meaning as provided in section 1(2B)
21 of an Act To provide for compulsory school attendance, for the taking of a school census in the
22 District of Columbia, and for other purposes, approved February 4, 1925 (43 Stat. 806; D.C.
23 Code § 38-201(2B)).
24 (2) “Health care needs” means specific medical requirements or disability- or
25 health-related supports that are documented by a licensed medical provider with the LEA and
26 that require the use of a personal wireless communication device for monitoring a medical
27 condition, administering medication, receiving health-related alerts, receiving disability-related

28 supports, or communicating with medical personnel or caregivers regarding the student’s health
29 during the school day.
30 (3) “LEA” means local education agency, which is the District of Columbia
31 Public Schools system or any individual public charter school or group of public charter schools
32 operating under a single charter.
33 (4) “Personal wireless communication device” means a cellular phone, tablet
34 computer, laptop computer, or gaming device that is not owned or provided by the school.
35 (5) “Possessing” means carrying or holding an item on one’s person but does not
36 include carrying or holding an item in a backpack, purse, handbag, or other personal carryall.
37 Sec. 3. LEA requirements for a personal wireless communication device policy.
38 (a) No later than 15 days before the start of the 2026-2027 School Year, each LEA
39 serving students enrolled in grades K-12 shall adopt and implement a personal wireless
40 communication device policy that prohibits K-12 students from, at a minimum, possessing a
41 personal wireless communication device on school premises at any time during the full school
42 day.
43 (b) An LEA’s personal wireless communication device policy may include exemptions
44 for a student possessing a personal wireless communication device for specific educational
45 purposes and to manage a student’s health care needs.
46 (c) Each LEA’s personal wireless communication device policy shall:

47 (1) Establish consequences if a student violates the policy; provided that
48 consequences shall be consistent with section 204 of the Student Fair Access to School
49 Amendment Act, approved August 25, 2018 (D.C. Law 22-157; DC Official Code § 38-236.04);
50 (2) Establish a process and timeline for the LEA to assess whether the policy is
51 working and how to refine it;
52 (3) Be published on the LEA’s website no later than 15 days before the start of
53 each school year for which the policy is in effect; mid-year modifications shall be published no
54 later than 15 days before being effective;
55 (4) Be communicated to all enrolled families before the first day of school; and
56 (5) Be communicated to all LEA employees, contractors, and volunteers who
57 have contact with students during school hours.
58 (d) Each LEA’s personal wireless communication device policy shall require each school
59 in the LEA to:
60 (1) Identify how a parent or guardian of an enrolled student should communicate
61 urgent information to their child during the full school day;
62 (2) Identify how the school will communicate with families in cases of
63 emergency; and
64 (3) Identify appropriate storage for students’ personal wireless communication
65 devices during the full school day.
66 Sec. 4. State educational agency responsibilities.

67 (a) By March 1, 2026, the Office of the State Superintendent for Education (“OSSE”)
68 shall develop and share with LEAs a model policy that meets the requirements prescribed in
69 section 3.
70 (b) OSSE shall monitor LEA compliance with the requirements of this act.
71 (c) OSSE shall share research concerning the effect of the use of personal wireless
72 communication devices by students in school settings with LEAs.
73 (d) OSSE may issue a waiver to a school or LEA, as requested by an LEA, if the school
74 or LEA can demonstrate appropriate storage constraints or an existing personal wireless
75 communication device policy that effectively limits distractions caused by personal wireless
76 communication devices throughout the full school day.
77 Sec. 5. Applicability.
78 (a) Section 4 of this act shall apply upon the date of inclusion of its fiscal effect in an
79 approved budget and financial plan.
80 (b) The Chief Financial Officer shall certify the date of the inclusion of the fiscal effect
81 in an approved budget and financial plan and provide notice to the Budget Director of the
82 Council of the certification.
83 (c)(1) The Budget Director shall cause the notice of the certification to be published in
84 the District of Columbia Register.
85 (2) The date of publication of the notice of the certification shall not affect the
86 applicability of this act.
