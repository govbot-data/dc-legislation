15 To recognize and celebrate Friendship Baptist Church in Southwest, DC, on the occasion of its
16 150th anniversary, for its service, faith, mission, and dedication to the community.
17
18 WHEREAS, Friendship Baptist Church was first established as the Virginia Avenue Baptist
19 Church under the vision of Reverend R.S. Laws in 1875, bringing 54 members from the Reverend’s
20 previous tenure at the Mount Olivet Baptist Church of Arlington, Virginia;
21
22 WHEREAS, in 1886, the congregation funded the church’s move to a new building at 734
23 1st Street, SW;
24
25 WHEREAS, in 1905, the Virginia Avenue Baptist Church was renamed the Friendship
26 Baptist Church under Reverend Alexander Wilbanks’ leadership;
27
28 WHEREAS, the Friendship Baptist Church congregation came together to support the
29 relocation of the church again in 1965 to its present location at 900 Delaware Avenue, SW;
30
31 WHEREAS, Friendship Baptist Church, then under the leadership of Reverend B.H.
32 Whiting, purchased an entire city block to support its growing congregation, taking out a
33 substantial loan in the process, which the community came together to pay off quickly;
34
35 WHEREAS, a focus on supporting the community through spiritual guidance and
36 education has been a cornerstone of Friendship Baptist Church, one of the District of Columbia’s
37 oldest African American congregations;
38
39 WHEREAS, Friendship Baptist Church offers a vast array of community engagement
40 activities, hosting events for all ages;
41
42 WHEREAS, Friendship Baptist Church encourages its youth members to expand their
43 horizons, from joining the youth choir to pursuing higher education with the help of the Benjamin
44 H. Whiting Scholarship Fund;
45
1

46 WHEREAS, Friendship Baptist Church endeavors to allow anyone to join in worship,
47 offering live streaming for those unable to attend in person;
48
49 WHEREAS, Friendship Baptist Church and its congregation have withstood many
50 hardships, remaining a pillar of the Southwest community through gentrification and
51 redevelopment;
52
53 WHEREAS, Friendship Baptist Church has long served as a hub of civic life in Southwest,
54 including by opening its doors as a polling place and for community meetings;
55
56 WHEREAS, Friendship Baptist Church has been blessed with many outstanding leaders,
57 including the current leadership of Reverend Dr. J. Michael Little; and
58
59 WHEREAS, Friendship Baptist Church has always been, and remains, a cornerstone of the
60 Southwest community and the District’s faith community.
61
62 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
63 resolution may be cited as the “Friendship Baptist Church 150th Anniversary Recognition
64 Resolution of 2025”.
65
66 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates Friendship
67 Baptist Church in Southwest, DC, on the occasion of its 150th anniversary, for its service, faith,
68 mission, and dedication to the community.
69
70 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
71 the District of Columbia Register.
