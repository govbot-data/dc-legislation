Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for the consideration of the Council of the District of Columbia is legislation entitled "Closing
of a Portion of a Public Alley in Square 785, S.O. 21-03369, Act of 2026".
The legislation will authorize the closure of an alley abutting Lots 844, 27, 28, 29, and 30 in Square 785
to allow for the narrow pedestrian alley (7 -8 feet wide) to be incorporated into two of the abutting
residential properties in Ward 6.
Pursuant to D.C. Official Code §9-202-02, the Surveyor has drawn a draft plat showing the portion of the
alley proposed to be closed, the location of a new building restriction line to be established on the closed
alley , and the lot to which title of the proposed alley closing would revert. The Surveyor also referred the
application for comment to the following parties:
o Affected District agencies and public utilities, all of which responded either in support of or not
objecting to the proposed alley closing, with two exceptions:
o The Solid Waste Management Administration (SWMA) objected based on concern that
the proposed alley closing would impact SMW A's trash collection, although the District
Department of Transportation (DDOT)'s report stated that the proposed alley closing
would not affect trash collection. The Surveyor and applicant requesting SWMA to revise
or confirm its objection, but SWMA has not responded.
o Pepco' s letter of support referred to the wrong square (844 instead of 785) and type of
application ( dedication instead of closing). The Surveyor notified Pepco of these errors
but Pepco has not responded.
o The National Capital Planning Commission (NCPC), which submitted a December 27, 2023,
letter stating that the proposed alley closing was exempt from NCPC review;
o Advisory Neighborhood Commission 5F, which submitted a July 15, 2024, resolution in support
of this application; and
o The owners of each property abutting the portion of the alley proposed to be closed that are not
under the control of the applicant (Lot 54 in Square 3524 and Lot 892 in Square 3530), which
have not responded to the February 14, 2025, notice.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.

Sincerely,

SUBJECT: S.O. No. 21-03369 – Alley Closure in Square 785
The District Department of Transportation (DDOT) has reviewed the submitted materials for the proposed alley
closure. This memorandum provides DDOT’s assessment of the proposal.
OVERVIEW
Donna McLean (“the Applicant”) seeks approval to remove a 257-square foot walking alley segment (7 feet x 27
feet and 8 feet x 85 feet) within an existing public alley system. The subject alley is located in Square 0785
adjacent to Lots 844 and 28 (Property) owned by the Applicant and the neighbor at 308 A Street NE. The
walking alley proposed to close is part of a larger alley network connecting A Street NE, Frederick Douglass Court
NE, and Constitution Avenue NE. The alley is located west of part of the alley network, south of garages, west of
Frederick Douglass Court NE, and east of existing townhomes. It is DDOT’s practice to anticipate possible future
uses of the right-of-way during the review of closure requests. Given the walking alley is surrounded by existing,
built alley connections, DDOT does not find this portion of the alley is necessary for trash collection or vehicle
access to the adjacent or surrounding lots.
OPERATIONS
The proposed alley closure is located in the rear of lots 844 and 28 and abuts lots 29 and 30, as shown in Figure
2. Lot 844 is owned by the owner of 308 A Street NE and Lot 38 is owned by 310 A Street NE. The surrounding
public alley network will maintain connectivity and is not negatively impacted with the closure. Currently, the
alley segment is paved, but inaccessible to vehicles due to the narrow width. Trash collection is located off of
Frederick Douglas Court to the east of the proposed closure and will not change with the closure.

DDOT aims to provide a safe and efficient roadway network that provides for the timely movement of people,
goods and services. This includes short blocks, transportation connections, and a healthy tree canopy. DDOT has
reviewed the site plan and does not find a future connection likely given the area has existing, easier navigable
access points within the existing alley network.
DDOT ASSETS
DDOT requires the Applicant to compensate the District for the removal of existing DDOT assets in the area of
the proposed alley closure. DDOT’s Streetlight Division reviewed the submitted materials and has noted that
there is an underground electrical conduit powering alley pole number 65012, a pole and light that is outside of
the proposed alley closure, in the proposed alley closure area, as shown in Figure 2. If approved, the Applicant
will be required to relocate the conduits to alternative feed points within public space. The Applicant will need
to apply for a public space permit, compensate DDOT for any lost conduit, and complete the relocation prior to
closing the alley.

SUBJECT: Legal sufficiency review of a draft legislation entitled “Closing of a portion of a
public alley in Square 785, S.O. 21-03369, Act of 2025”
DATE: May 13 2025
This Office has reviewed the attached proposed legislation and found it to be legally sufficient for review
by the Mayor and submission to the Council.
On February 19, 2021, an owner of Lot 844 in Square 785 filed an application to the Office of the
Surveyor of the District of Columbia (the “Surveyor”) proposing to:
• Close a portion of a public alley in Square 785 abutting Lots 27-30 and 844.
The applicant included a statement of support signed by the owners of all lots abutting the proposed alley
closing (Lots 27-30 and 844 in Square 785) and a statement that the proposed alley closing would not
displace retail tenants as required by Section 2 of the Street and Alley Closing and Acquisition
Procedures Act of 1982 Relocation Assistance Amendment Act of 1986, effective August 7, 1986 (D.C.
Law 6-133; D.C. Official Code § 9-202.09(b)(5)).
Pursuant to Section 202 of the Street and Alley Closing and Acquisition Procedures Act of 1982,
effective March 10, 1983 (D.C. Law 4-201; D.C. Official Code § 9-202.02), the Surveyor drew a plat
depicting the proposed alley closing, which stated that title would revert to the owner of Lots 28 & 844
in Square 785. The Surveyor created a file (S.O. 21-03369) for all documents relevant to the application
and provided notice of the alley closing proposal, to:
• Public agencies and utilities; and
• The National Capital Planning Commission (“NCPC”) on April 29, 2021; and
• Advisory Neighborhood Commission (“ANC”) 6C, in which district the proposed alley closure is
located.
Public agency responses
The District Department of Transportation (“DDOT”) submitted an April 30, 2024, letter in support of
the proposed alley closing that stated that the applicant had resolved DDOT’s initial June 5, 2021,
objections by paying to relocate the DDOT facilities.

May 1, 2025, request to Pepco to revise the letter to refer to the correct square and type of application
but Pepco has not yet responded. The applicant separately contacted SWMA to confirm that the
application should not have an impact on trash collection as it would close only a narrow portion of the
alley that is inaccessible to vehicles, but SWMA refused to change its recommendation.

The Department of Housing and Community Development submitted a May 5, 2021, letter to the

federal interest.

budget and the FY 2025 through FY 2028 budget and financial plan.
Public utility responses

facilities in the portion of the alley proposed to be closed and therefore approves the proposed alley
closing.
