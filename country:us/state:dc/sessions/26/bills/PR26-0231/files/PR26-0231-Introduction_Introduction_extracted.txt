Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is the Juvenile Curfew
Emergency Amendment Act of 2025. This emergency legislation will strengthen and enhance enforcement and
accountability tools for juveniles, with a specific focus on a stronger and more flexible curfew program. The
legislation seeks flexibility in the District's juvenile curfew to allow the Metropolitan Police Department (MPD) to
ensure the safety of juveniles and the public.
While MPD has used various methods to address past conduct of juveniles, including enhanced enforcement of the
disturbance of the peace offenses, working with local businesses on signage related to trespassing by
unaccompanied minors, and requests for a restricted curfew in the Wharf, the tools available are limited.
The legislation will ensure the safety of juveniles and the public by:
• Changing the juvenile curfew hours to 11 p.m. every day in June, July, and August 2025, and to extend the
applicability of the juvenile curfew to 17-year-olds.
• Authorizing the Chief of Police of MPD to establish an extended juvenile curfew zone(s). This proposal
mirrors the parameters of the popular drug free zones from the Secure DC Omnibus bill signed into law by
Mayor Bowser in March 2024. For these extended juvenile curfew zones, the curfew will begin at 7:00
p.m. and remain in effect for 15 consecutive days, with a possibility of extension based on public safety
considerations for up to 30 days. An Advisory Neighborhood Commission, business improvement district,
or Main Street organization can also petition the Chief of Police to establish an extended juvenile
curfew hours zone.
• Allowing the Mayor to authorize an emergency juvenile curfew.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

15 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the the Juvenile
16 Curfew Act of 1995 to authorize the Mayor to extend the juvenile curfew hours in the
17 District when appropriate to protect public safety or public or private property, to
18 authorize the Chief of Police to establish extended juvenile curfew hour zones in
19 specified circumstances, to start the juvenile curfew hours at 11 p.m. every day in June,
20 July, and August 2025, and to extend the applicability of the juvenile curfew to 17-year
21 olds.
22
23 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 resolution may be cited as the "Juvenile Curfew Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025".
25 Sec. 2. ( a) the Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995 was enacted for the protection of minors to
26 prevent violence and crime in the interest of public health, safety, and general welfare.
27 (b) Juvenile Curfew has subsequently been amended multiple times to extend the curfew
28 period in the Enhanced Crime Prevention and Abatement Emergency Amendment Act of 2006
29 D.C. Act 16-446, the Fiscal Year 2003 Budget Support Emergency Act of 2002 (D.C. Act 14-
30 453), and the Enhanced Crime Prevention and Abatement Emergency Amendment Act of2006
31 (D.C. Act 16-446).
32 (c) Since 2023, the District has seen a rise in unruly behavior by some juveniles. Already
33 this spring, the District has had several instances of large groups of juveniles engaging in
34 harmful, and often times, criminal conduct in multiple areas around the city, including the

35 Wharf, Navy Yard, and U Street. Multiple fights have broken out, robberies, assaults, and
36 shootings have occurred. This past week, multiple youth were injured during melees in Dupont
37 Circle and U Street. While MPD has used various methods to address this conduct, including
38 enhanced enforcement of the disturbance of the peace offenses, working with local businesses on
39 signage related to trespassing by unaccompanied minors, and requests for a restricted curfew in
40 the Wharf, the tools available are limited. With summer, a time when we know that incidents of
41 violence tend to tick upward citywide particularly amongst juveniles, the District is seeking
42 flexibility in our juvenile curfew to allow MPD to ensure the safety of both juveniles and the
43 public in order to more nimbly address significant safety concerns.
44 (d) Therefore, emergency legislation is needed to address the safety of minors in the
45 District.
46 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
47 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Juvenile Curfew
48 Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
49 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
