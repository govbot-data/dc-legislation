16
17 To recognize and honor the Burleith Citizens Association on the occasion of its 100th
18 anniversary and to celebrate its century of dedicated service to the Burleith community
19 and the District of Columbia.
20
21 WHEREAS, the Burleith Citizens Association was established in January 1925 by the
22 first residents of the Burleith neighborhood to serve and represent their newly developed
23 community;
24 WHEREAS, in its earliest years, the Burleith Citizens Association successfully advocated
25 for essential infrastructure improvements, including exceptional streets, street lights, sidewalks,
26 and improved bus service, laying the foundation for a thriving residential community;
27 WHEREAS, the Burleith Citizens Association has consistently championed educational
28 excellence, supporting the establishment and enhancement of local schools, including a
29 community center at Gordon Junior High School, night classes at Western High School (now
30 Duke Ellington School of the Arts), and the creation of the Georgetown branch of the DC Public
31 Library in 1935;
32 WHEREAS, during the Great Depression, the Burleith Citizens Association
33 demonstrated extraordinary compassion by preparing Christmas baskets for those in need and
1

34 allowing unemployed residents to use vacant lots for growing food––establishing a legacy of
35 community service that continues to this day;
36 WHEREAS, the Burleith Citizens Association has been instrumental in preserving and
37 enhancing the neighborhood's natural environment, supporting the development of Glover
38 Archbold Park, maintaining the community's cherished tree canopy, and promoting
39 environmental stewardship;
40 WHEREAS, in 2008, the Burleith Citizens Association established the Burleith
41 Community Fund, a 501(c)(3) organization that has funded numerous community improvement
42 projects, including the restoration of historic call boxes, landscaping of Ellington Field, and
43 preservation of neighborhood landmarks;
44 WHEREAS, the Burleith Citizens Association continues to foster community
45 engagement through special interest groups, seasonal events, and its long-running newsletter, the
46 Burleith Bell, first published in 1926;
47 WHEREAS, throughout its history, the Burleith Citizens Association has served as an
48 exemplary model of civic engagement, democratic participation, and community service,
49 enriching both the Burleith neighborhood and the District of Columbia as a whole; and
50 WHEREAS, in 2025, the Burleith Citizens Association celebrates its centennial
51 anniversary, marking 100 years of dedicated service to its community, responsible development,
52 and civic leadership.
53 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
54 resolution may be cited as the "Burleith Citizens Association Centennial Recognition Resolution
55 of 2025".
56 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates the Burleith
57 Citizens Association's century of outstanding service to the District of Columbia and commends
2

58 its continued dedication to fostering community engagement, civic participation, and
59 neighborhood improvement in the Burleith community.
60 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
61 the District of Columbia Register.
