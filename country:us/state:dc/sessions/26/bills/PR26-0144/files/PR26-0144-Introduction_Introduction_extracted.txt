Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is a proposed
resolution titled “Georgetown University Revenue Bonds Project Emergency Approval
Resolution of 2025”, and the accompanying emergency declaration resolution.
The resolution authorizes the issuance, sale, and delivery of tax-exempt revenue bonds, notes, or
other obligations in aggregate principal amount not to exceed $675,000,000. The bonds will be
used for the financing, refinancing, or reimbursing of costs associated with an authorized project
of the Borrower’s properties located at 3700 O Street, N.W., in Ward 2, and 111 Massachusetts
Avenue, N.W., 120 F Street, N.W., and 600 New Jersey Avenue, N.W. in Ward 6.
In accordance with section 490 of the Home Rule Act, it has been determined that the bonds,
when, as, and if issued, shall be without recourse to the District. The bonds shall not be general
obligations of the District; shall not be a pledge of or involve the full faith and credit or the
taxing power of the District; shall not constitute a debt of the District; and shall not constitute a
lending of public credit for a private undertaking as prohibited in section 602(a)(2) of the Home
Rule Act. The bonds shall not give rise to any pecuniary liability of the District, and the District
shall have no obligation with respect to the purchase of the bonds.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed measure.
Sincerely,
Muriel E. Bowser

205 Sec. 3. Findings.
206 The Council finds that:
207 (a) Section 490 of the Home Rule Act provides that the Council may by resolution authorize
208 the issuance of District revenue bonds, notes, or other obligations (including refunding bonds, notes,
209 or other obligations) to borrow money to finance, refinance, or reimburse costs, and to assist in the
210 financing, refinancing, or reimbursing of the costs of undertakings in certain areas designated in
211 section 490 and may effect the financing, refinancing, or reimbursement by loans made directly or
212 indirectly to any individual or legal entity, by the purchase of any mortgage, note, or other security,
213 or by the purchase, lease, or sale of any property.
9

214 (b) The Borrower has requested the District to issue, sell, and deliver revenue bonds, in one
215 or more series, in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed $675,000,000 and to make the Loan
216 for the purpose of financing, refinancing, or reimbursing costs of the Project.
217 (c) The Project is located in the District and will contribute to the health, education, safety,
218 or welfare of, or the creation or preservation of jobs for, residents of the District, or to economic
219 development of the District.
220 (d) The Project is an undertaking in the area of college and university facilities within the
221 meaning of section 490 of the Home Rule Act.
222 (e) The authorization, issuance, sale, and delivery of the Bonds and the Loan to the
223 Borrower are desirable, are in the public interest, will promote the purpose and intent of section
224 490 of the Home Rule Act, and will assist the Project.

GEORGETOWN UNIVERSITY
Revenue Bond Project
FACT SHEET
Georgetown University has requested that the District issue up to $675,000,000 of tax-exempt
and taxable revenue bonds to authorize and provide for the loan of the proceeds of the bonds to
assist Georgetown University, in financing, refinancing, or reimbursing of costs associated with
an authorized project on properties located at 3700 O Street, N.W., in Ward 2, and 111
Massachusetts Avenue, N.W., 120 F Street, N.W., and 600 New Jersey Avenue, N.W. in Ward 6.
The Applicant
Georgetown University (Georgetown), a 501(c)(3) corporation, is a private, non-profit,
comprehensive, coeducational university founded in 1789, affiliated with the Society of Jesus,
and open to students of every religious faith. In 1815, Georgetown became the first college to
receive a federal charter. The main campus is located at 37th and O St., N.W. in Ward 2, and has
a law school located at 600 New Jersey Avenue, N.W., in Ward 6.
Georgetown offers a wide array of graduate and undergraduate degree programs and non-degree
courses of study. Georgetown consists of three graduate and professional schools: Law,
Medicine, and the Graduate School; four undergraduate schools: Georgetown College and the
schools of Foreign Service, Nursing, and Business; and the School for Summer and Continuing
Education, which offers year-round academic programs. Georgetown enrolls more than 11,500
students in undergraduate, master’s, doctoral, and professional degree programs, attracting
students from the District of Columbia, all 50 states, and numerous foreign countries. The faculty
of Georgetown University includes many experts and scholars of national and international
prominence. To complement its full-time faculty of 1,394 and 311 part-time members, the
University employs part-time teachers drawn from Washington’s professional community.
Approximately 1,982 voluntary faculty members assist in the clinical education of students,
principally in the Medical Center.
Proposed Project
The bonds will be used for the financing, refinancing, or reimbursing of costs associated with an
authorized project of Georgetown’s properties located at 3700 O Street, N.W., in Ward 2, and
111 Massachusetts Avenue, N.W., 120 F Street, N.W., and 600 New Jersey Avenue, N.W. in
Ward 6.

Feasibility/Structure/Security of the Bonds
Barclays, serving as the financial advisor to Georgetown, has determined this deal to be
financially feasible after reviewing the Universities’ financial statements and status.
The bonds, if issued, will be a general obligation of the University and will be without recourse
to the District.
Public Purpose Benefits
Georgetown University’s facilities offer housing, business and educational programs to District
residents, while generating revenue and other direct benefits to citizens and residents of the
District.
Legal and Regulatory Affairs
The law firm of Squire Patton Boggs, bond counsel to the Revenue Bond Program, has
preliminarily determined that DC Prep is a 501(c)(3) organization and that the project constitutes
a permissible undertaking within the meaning of Section 490(a)(1) of the District of Columbia
Home Rule Act.

approval of proposed financing through the District’s Revenue Bond Program and that the
proposed project will enhance employment opportunities and contribute to community
betterment.
