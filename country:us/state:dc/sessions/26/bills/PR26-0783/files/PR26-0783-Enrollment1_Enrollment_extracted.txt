ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A RESOLUTION
26-491
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
July 14, 2026
To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Green Building
Act of 2006 to exempt certain District-owned and -funded residential and nonresidential
projects from complying with net-zero-energy standards and to modify net-zero-energy
standard compliance requirements for nonresidential and residential projects; to amend
section 2 of the Clean Energy DC Building Code Amendment Act of 2022 to modify the
definition of the net-zero-energy standard and the types of projects to which the net-zero-
energy standard applies; and for other purposes.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
resolution may be cited as the “Net Zero Continuity Emergency Declaration Resolution of
2026”.
Sec. 2. (a) On January 12, 2023, Mayor Bowser signed into law the Greener Government
Buildings Amendment Act of 2022, effective March 10, 2023 (D.C. Law 24-306; 70 DCR 658)
(“GGBA”), which amended the Green Building Act of 2006 to require construction of new or
substantially improved projects owned or financed by the District to adhere to a net-zero-energy
(“NZE”) standard.
(b) Since passage of the GGBA, the Council has enacted several pieces of non-permanent
legislation exempting District-financed affordable housing projects from the requirements of the
GGBA in order to ensure predictability in project design and development.
(c) On November 4, 2025, the Council passed the most recent iteration of the Green
Housing Coordination Emergency Amendment Act of 2025, effective November 24, 2025 (D.C.
Act 26-0207; 72 DCR 13506), which removed the NZE provisions established by the GGBA that
applied to residential and mixed-use projects and created separate green building requirements
for certain residential and mixed-use projects in the Housing Production Trust Fund Act of 1988,
effective March 16, 1989 (D.C. Law 7-202; D.C. Official Code § 42-2801 et seq.). The
accompanying temporary bill, the Green Housing Coordination Temporary Amendment Act of
2025, effective February 12, 2026 (D.C. Law 26-90; 73 DCR 3), expires on September 25, 2026.
(d) On February 3, 2026, the Council considered and passed the Net Zero Modification
and Preservation Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, effective March 6, 2026 (D.C. Act 26-
275; 73 DCR 3779), which, as introduced, updated the NZE standard, including the definition of
this standard, and how it is applied to both residential and nonresidential projects covered by the

GGBA. The accompanying temporary bill, the Net Zero Modification and Preservation
Temporary Amendment Act of 2026, effective May 21, 2026 (D.C. Law 26-123; 73 DCR 5075),
expires on January 01, 2027.
(e) As the provisions related to the GGBA-covered residential projects were removed
through an amendment at the February 03, 2026, Legislative Meeting, there is a significant gap
between the expiration of the NZE standard definition that was created by the Net Zero
Modification and Preservation Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, effective March 6, 2026
(D.C. Act 26-275; 73 DCR 3779), and the expiration of the green housing provisions for
residential projects as codified in the Green Housing Coordination Temporary Amendment Act
of 2025, effective February 12, 2026 (D.C. Law 26-90; 73 DCR 3). As a result, when the
residential provisions expire, GGBA-covered residential projects will revert to the permanent
NZE requirements in the Green Building Act of 2006, which rely on the definition of the NZE
standard in section 2 of the Clean Energy DC Building Code Amendment Act of 2022, effective
September 21, 2022 (D.C. Law 24-177; D.C. Official Code § 6-1453.01). The permanent
residential requirements will not align with that definition, which will remain in its temporarily
modified form until January 1, 2027, and the standard would change again when the temporary
definition expires.
(f) Therefore, the Council must act now to provide continuity and advanced certainty of
NZE standards for all projects subject to the GGBA and align these standards so they operate on
a unified timeline. If the Council does not act at the July 14, 2026, Legislative Meeting, the
Council would not have opportunity again until the next scheduled Legislative Meeting on
September 22, 2026—just 3 days before the expiration of the Green Housing Coordination
Temporary Amendment Act of 2025, effective February 12, 2026 (D.C. Law 26-90; 73 DCR 3),
thus creating uncertainty for affordable housing developers applying for District financing. In
addition, without passage of the accompanying emergency and temporary bills, the Council
would again need to consider the NZE standard definition and provisions for GGBA non-
residential projects later in the fall to ensure continuity for non-residential projects given the
limitations on passage of permanent legislation at the end of Council Period 26. By repealing
both temporary legislations now, the Council will align the various provisions impacting the
NZE standard so all considerations can be made and adjusted on one timeline in the new Council
Period.
Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making it necessary that the Net
Zero Continuity Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted after a single reading.
Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
