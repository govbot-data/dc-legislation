Martin A. Skolnik
MartinA. Skolnik is currently the GSE Compliance Officer with BBG,
Inc., where he is responsible for the company’s compliance with the
multifamily appraisal requirements ofFreddie Mac and Fannie Mae. He
is a current member of the District of Columbia Board of Real Estate
Appraisers.
Mr. Skolnik is an experienced executive real estate appraiser who has
focused his career on commercial, multifamily, industrial, retail, and
residential properties inthe DistrictofColumbia, Maryland,Virginia, and
Pennsylvaniamarkets. Prior tojoining BBG in 2024, he held the position
ofDirector/Lecturer ofthe undergraduate real estate program at the
University of Baltimore and, before that, was the Chief Appraiser of
Freddie Mac’s multifamily line-of-business in McLean, VA. He also served as the Mid-Atlantic
Value Group Regional Appraisal Manager in York, Pennsylvania, and was the District of
Columbia’s DirectorofReal PropertyTax Administration. Mr. Skolnik is a former instructorwith
the Johns Hopkins University’s MasterofScience in Real Estate Development, where he taught
coursework in real estate evaluation, and wasan instructorwithAnneArundelCommunity College
for real estate appraiser licensure coursework.
Mr. Skolnik earned the prestigious MAI appraisal designation from theAppraisal Institute and has
published and authored several real estate articles and provided testimony on real estate appraisal
and tax assessment valuation in numerous states, most recently publishing “A Proposed
Methodology to Explore Racial and Ethnic Bias in Multifamily Appraisals” in the Counselors of
Real Estate’s peer-reviewedjournal Real Estate Issues.
‘A Ward 3 resident, Mr. Skolnik earned his Bachelor of Arts in Urban Geography from the
University ofMaryland, Baltimore County, and his Master of Business Administration from the
University ofBaltimore.
