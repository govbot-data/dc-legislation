Councilmember Brooke Pinto
A BILL
________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
________________
1 To amend, on an emergency basis, the Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995 to authorize the Mayor to
2 extend the juvenile curfew hours in the District when appropriate to protect public safety
3 or public or private property, to authorize the Chief of Police to establish extended
4 juvenile curfew hour zones in specified circumstances, to start the juvenile curfew hours
5 at 11 p.m. every day, and to extend the applicability of the juvenile curfew to 17-year
6 olds.
7
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
9 act may be cited as the “Juvenile Curfew Congressional Review Emergency Amendment Act of
10 2025”.
11 Sec. 2. The Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995, effective September 20, 1995 (D.C. Law 11-48;
12 D.C. Official Code § 2-1541 et seq.), is amended as follows:
13 (a) Section 3 (D.C. Official Code § 2-1542) is amended as follows:
14 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended to read as follows:
15 “(1) “Chief of Police” means the Chief of the Metropolitan Police Department.”.
16 (2) A new paragraph (1A) is added to read as follows:
17 “(1A) “Curfew hours” means:
18 “(A) During every month except July and August:
19 “(i) From 11:00 p.m. on every Sunday, Monday, Tuesday,
20 Wednesday, and Thursday, until 6:00 a.m. on the following day; and
1

21 “(ii) From 12:01 a.m. until 6:00 a.m. on every Saturday and
22 Sunday;
23 “(B) During the months of July and August, from 12:01 a.m. until 6:00
24 a.m. every day;
25 “(C) Notwithstanding subparagraphs (A) and (B) of this paragraph, during
26 the months of September, October, and November 2025, from 11:00 p.m. on every day until 6:00
27 a.m. on the following day;
28 “(D) Extended curfew hours established by the Mayor pursuant to section
29 3a; and
30 “(E) Extended curfew hours in extended juvenile curfew zones established
31 by the Chief of Police pursuant to section 3b.”.
32 (3) Paragraph (5) is amended by striking the phrase “under the age of 17 years”
33 and inserting the phrase “under the age of 18 years” in its place.
34 (4) A new paragraph (6A) is added to read as follows:
35 “(6A) “Notice of violation of curfew” means a verbal warning, authorized by an official
36 that informs a groups of juveniles that they are violating the terms of a curfew, that they may be
37 arrested if they continue to violate the curfew, and identifies reasonable steps that they can take
38 to leave the area to avoid arrest.”.
39 (b) New sections 3a and 3b are added to read as follows:
40 “Sec. 3a. Temporary extensions of juvenile curfew hours.
41 “(a) The Mayor may, by issuance of an administrative order pursuant to section 422(12)
42 of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, effective December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 890; D.C.
43 Official Code § 1-204.22(12), extend the juvenile curfew hours in the District, or any part of the
2

44 District, beyond the hours established in section 3(1A)(A), (B), and (C), if the Mayor determines
45 that such an extension is appropriate to protect public safety or public or private property.
46 “(b) An administrative order issued by the Mayor under subsection (a) of this section
47 shall:
48 “(1) State the basis for the extension of the juvenile curfew hours;
49 “(2) State the locations in the District where the extended juvenile curfew hours
50 shall apply or state that the extended juvenile curfew hours shall apply throughout the District;
51 and
52 “(3) State the days and hours during which the extended juvenile curfew hours
53 shall be in effect; provided, that the extended juvenile curfew hours:
54 “(A) Shall not begin earlier than 8:00 p.m., nor extend past 6:00 a.m., on
55 any day;
56 “(B) Shall not remain in effect for more than 4 consecutive days, except as
57 provided in subsection (e) of this section; and
58 “(C) Shall not apply to groups of 8 or fewer individuals.
59 “(c) Except where emergency circumstances, as determined by the Mayor and described
60 in the administrative order issued under subsection (a) of this section, warrant otherwise,
61 extended juvenile curfew hours imposed under this section shall not take effect until at least 24
62 hours after the issuance of the administrative order.
63 “(d) Notice of the issuance of each administrative order issued under this section shall be
64 promptly posted on the main website of the District government and on the website of the
65 Metropolitan Police Department.
3

66 “(e) The Mayor may extend the time period during which extended juvenile curfew hours
67 imposed by this section shall remain in effect by issuing subsequent Mayor’s orders extending
68 the time period; provided, that each such extension of the time period shall not exceed 30 days.
69 “(f) If members of the Metropolitan Police Department discover juveniles in the
70 designated extended juvenile curfew hours zone during the hours of curfew, the Metropolitan
71 Police Department shall:
72 “(1) Where there:
73 “(A) Is not an imminent danger of bodily injury of significant damage to
74 property, issue at lest 2 clearly audible and understandable notices of violation of curfew; or
75 “(B) Is imminent danger of bodily injury or significant damage to
76 property, issue at least one clearly audible and understandable notice of violation of curfew;
77 “(2) Provide juveniles a reasonable and adequate time to disperse; and
78 “(3) Capture on body-worn camera each component of the order to return home
79 described in paragraph (1) of this subsection.
80 “Sec. 3b. Temporary designations of extended juvenile curfew hour zones.
81 “(a) The Chief of Police may designate any area of the District as an extended juvenile
82 curfew hours zone, in which extended juvenile curfew hours determined by the Chief of Police
83 shall apply, if the Chief of Police determines that the designation of the extended juvenile curfew
84 hours zone is appropriate to protect public safety or public or private property in the extended
85 juvenile curfew hours zone.
86 “(b) To designate an extended juvenile curfew hours zone, the Chief of Police shall issue
87 an order that includes:
88 “(1) The basis for the designation of the extended juvenile curfew hours zone;
89 “(2) The boundaries of the extended juvenile curfew hours zone; and
4

90 “(3) The days and hours of the extended juvenile curfew hours in the extended
91 juvenile curfew hours zone; provided, that the extended juvenile curfew hours in the extended
92 juvenile curfew hours zone:
93 “(A) Shall not begin earlier than 8:00 p.m., nor extend past 6:00 a.m., on
94 any day;
95 “(B) Shall not remain in effect for more than 4 consecutive days, except as
96 provided in subsection (e) of this section; and
97 “(C) Shall not apply to groups of 8 or fewer individuals.
98 “(c) In determining whether to designate an extended juvenile curfew hours zone, the
99 Chief of Police shall consider the following:
100 “(1) Within the preceding 30 days, the occurrence of:
101 “(A) Juvenile curfew violations in the proposed extended juvenile curfew
102 hours zone;
103 “(B) Police reports of disturbances of the public peace described in section
104 1 of An Act To confer concurrent jurisdiction on the police court of the District of Columbia in
105 certain cases, approved July 16, 1912 (37 Stat. 192; D.C. Official Code§ 22-1301), sections 6,
106 3,9, or 16of An act for the preservation of the public peace and the protection of property within
107 the District of Columbia, approved July 29, 1892 (27 Stat. 323; D.C. Official Code § 22-1307, §
108 22-1309, § 22-1312, or § 22-1318), section 211 of the District of Columbia Law Enforcement
109 Act of 1953, approved June 29, 1953 (67 Stat. 98; D.C. Official Code § 22-1321), or section 901
110 of An Act relating to crime and criminal procedure in the District of Columbia, approved
111 December27, 1967 (81 Stat. 742; D.C. Official Code § 22-1322), in the proposed extended
112 juvenile curfew hours zone;
5

113 “(C) Police reports of dangerous crimes, as that term is defined in D.C.
114 Official Code § 23-1331(3), in the proposed extended juvenile curfew hours zone;
115 “(D) Police reports of crimes of violence, as that term is defined in D.C.
116 Official Code § 23-1331(4), in the proposed extended juvenile curfew hours zone; or
117 “(E) Police reports of destruction of or damage to public or private
118 property in the proposed extended juvenile curfew hours zone;
119 “(2) Intelligence or verifiable information indicative of an intent of 9 or more
120 juveniles to gather in the proposed extended juvenile curfew hours zone in such a manner that is
121 likely to result in substantial harm to the public safety, or the safety of those gathering, or
122 substantial damage to public or private property; and
123 “(3) Any other verifiable information from which the Chief of Police may
124 ascertain that public safety or public or private property in the proposed extended juvenile
125 curfew hours zone is likely to be endangered by the presence of unaccompanied juveniles in the
126 extended juvenile curfew hours zones.
127 “(d) Upon the designation of an extended juvenile curfew hours zone the Chief of Police
128 shall post notice of the designation on the website of the Metropolitan Police Department and
129 shall post signs in the immediate area of the extended juvenile curfew hours zone.
130 “(e) The Chief of Police may extend the duration of the designation of an extended
131 juvenile curfew hours zone to 30 days by issuing a subsequent order extending the duration of
132 the designation.
133 “(f) If members of the Metropolitan Police Department discover juveniles in the
134 designated extended juvenile curfew zone during the hours of curfew, the Metropolitan Police
135 Department shall:
136 “(1) Where there:

137 “(A) Is not an imminent danger of bodily injury or significant damage to
138 property, issue at least 2 clearly audible and understandable notices of violation of curfew; or
139 “(B) Is imminent danger of bodily injury or significant damage to
140 property, issue at least one clearly audible and understandable notice of violation of curfew;
141 “(2) Provide juveniles a reasonable and adequate time to disperse; and
142 “(3) Capture on body-worn camera each component of the order to return home
143 described in paragraph (1) of this subsection.
144 “(g)(1) An Advisory Neighborhood Commission, business improvement district, or Main
145 Street organization may petition the Chief of Police to establish an extended juvenile curfew
146 hours zone in an area covered by, or adjacent to the area covered by, such entity. Such petition
147 shall be approved by a formal vote of the members of the Advisory Neighborhood Commission,
148 the members of the board of the business improvement district, or the members of the board of
149 the Main Street organization.
150 “(2) A petition submitted pursuant to this subsection shall include the following
151 information:
152 “(A) The proposed boundaries of the extended juvenile curfew hours zone;
153 “(B) The proposed hours of the extended juvenile curfew;
154 “(C) The proposed duration, in days, of the proposed extended juvenile
155 curfew hours;
156 “(D) The reasons why the Advisory Neighborhood Commission, business
157 improvement district, or Main Street organization is requesting the designation of an extended
158 juvenile curfew hours zone; and
159 “(E) Such other information as the Chief of Police may require.
