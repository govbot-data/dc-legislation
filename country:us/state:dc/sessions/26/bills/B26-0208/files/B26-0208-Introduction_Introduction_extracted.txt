32 To amend the Open Meetings Act to clarify the definition of “meeting”; to provide for a public
33 body’s ability to be briefed about potential terrorist or public health threats so long as no
34 official action is taken; to exempt from the act meetings between the Council and the
35 Mayor provided that no official action is taken at such meetings; and to provide that a
36 meeting shall be deemed open to the public if the public body takes steps reasonably
37 calculated to allow the public to view or hear the meeting while the meeting is taking
38 place, or, if doing so is not technologically feasible, as soon thereafter as reasonably
39 practicable.
40
41 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
42 act may be cited as the “Open Meetings Clarification Amendment Act of 2025”.

43 Sec. 2. The Open Meetings Act, effective March 31, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-350; D.C.
44 Official Code § 2-571 et seq.), is amended as follows:
45 (a)(1) Section 404(1) (D.C. Official Code § 2-574(1)) is amended to read as follows:
46 “(1)(A) “Meeting” means any gathering of a quorum of the members of a public
47 body, including hearings and roundtables, whether formal or informal, regular, special, or
48 emergency, at which the members consider, conduct, or advise on public business, including
49 gathering information, taking testimony, discussing, deliberating, recommending, and voting,
50 regardless whether held in person, by telephone, electronically, or by other means of
51 communication.
52 “(B) The term “meeting” shall not include:
53 “(i) A chance or social gathering; provided, that it is not held to avoid the
54 provisions of this paragraph;
55 “(ii) A press conference;
56 “(iii) Field trips, site visits, or similar activities to gather information; or
57 “(iv) A retreat of the public body where there are discussions and briefings (but
58 no action is taken) about policy, issues or organizational matters.
59 “(C) For the purposes of the Council of the District of Columbia, the term
60 “meeting” means a regular or additional legislative meeting, and committee meetings where
61 votes are taken.”
