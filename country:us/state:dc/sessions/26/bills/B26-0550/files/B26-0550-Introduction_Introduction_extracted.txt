Dear Secretary Smith,
I am introducing the “Ralph J. Bunche Way Designation Act of 2025”. Please see enclosed
signed copy of the legislation.
This legislation will symbolically designate the 1500 block of Jackson Street, N.E., in Ward 5, as
Ralph J. Bunche Way. I am proud to introduce this legislation with the support of ANC 5B
Commissioner Jingwen Sun and the Brookland Neighborhood Civic Association.
Dr. Ralph J. Bunche was a distinguished African American political scientist, diplomat, and
scholar.1 Dr. Bunche was born in Detroit, Micihigan, in 1904. He began his professional career in
Washington, D.C., where he was a part of the Brookland community, residing at 1510 Jackson
Street, N.E. His residence is now one of the District’s historic sites.2 Dr. Bunche spent more than
two decades serving as professor and the founder of the Political Science Department at Howard
University from 1928 to 1941.3
Dr. Bunche is widely regarded for his work as the acting United Nations mediator who
negotiated agreements in the Arab-Israeli conflict in 1949.4 He received the Nobel Peace Prize in
1950 for his efforts. Dr. Bunche was an integral part of the U.S. Delegation that drafted the
charter of the then new United Nations Organization in the 1940s. He tackled conflict at home as
