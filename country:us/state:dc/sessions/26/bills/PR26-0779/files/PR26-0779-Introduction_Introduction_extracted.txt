14 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Legalization of
15 Marijuana for Medical Treatment Initiative of 1999 to amend the definition of an
16 unlicensed establishment to include establishments that sell Schedule I substances or
17 products that contain Schedule I substances, to extend the expiration dates of conditional
18 licenses for medical cannabis cultivation centers, retailers, internet retailers,
19 manufacturers, couriers, and testing laboratories for an additional year, to clarify that a
20 conditional license applicant may submit a zoning certificate instead of a certificate of
21 occupancy with their permanent license application, to clarify the distance requirements
22 between retailers and internet retailers and schools and recreation centers, to authorize
23 enforcement actions to be taken against all unlicensed establishments beginning on April
24 1, 2025, regardless of whether the unlicensed establishments have an application pending
25 application before the Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis Board, to authorize certain
26 enforcement actions before April 1, 2025, against an unlicensed establishment that has an
27 application before the Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis Board if the unlicensed
28 establishment sells a Schedule I substance or a product that contains a Schedule I
29 substance, to authorize summary closures and related enforcement actions to continue for
30 as long as the imminent danger to the public persists and is likely to recur, and to allow
31 the Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis Administration and Metropolitan Police
32 Department to take related enforcement actions against a licensed medical cannabis
33 establishment that engages in certain unlawful activity.
34
35 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
36 resolution may be cited as the “Medical Cannabis Licensing and Unlicensed Establishment
37 Enforcement Clarification Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
38 Sec. 2. (a) On October 7, 2025, the Council approved the Medical Cannabis Conditional
39 Licensure Extension Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 (D.C. Act 26-163), followed by
40 approval of the Medical Cannabis Conditional Licensure Extension Temporary Amendment Act
1

41 of 2025 (D.C. Law 26-71; 73 DCR 604) on January 21, 2026. These laws extended the
42 expiration dates for conditional licenses for medical cannabis cultivation centers, retailers,
43 internet retailers, manufacturers, couriers, and testing laboratories for an additional year. Act 26-
44 163 expired on January 21, 2026, and Law 26-71 will expire on August 13, 2026, while the
45 Council is on recess.
46 (b) On July 1, 2025, the Council approved the Streatery Program Endorsement Extension
47 and Medical Cannabis Clarification Emergency Amendment of 2025 (D.C. Act 26-117),
48 followed by approval of the Streatery Program Endorsement Extension and Medical Cannabis
49 Clarification Temporary Amendment of 2025 (D.C. Law 26-58; 72 DCR 13999) on October 7,
50 2025. These laws extended certain licensees’ ability to serve alcohol in outdoor public and
51 private spaces through December 2025 and clarified the distance from certain locations that
52 cannabis retailers and internet retailers must maintain. D.C. Act 26-117 expired on October 19,
53 2025 and D.C. Law 26-58 will expire on July 24, 2026, while the Council is on recess.
54 (c) On March 4, 2025, the Council approved the Unlicensed Establishment Enforcement
55 Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 (D.C. Act 26-38) followed by approval of the
56 Unlicensed Establishment Enforcement Clarification Temporary Amendment Act of 2025 (D.C.
57 Law 26-10; 72 DCR 6764) on April 1, 2025. These acts gave greater enforcement authority to
58 the Alcohol Beverage and Cannabis Administration over licensed and unlicensed establishments.
59 D.C. Act 26-38 expired on June 19, 2025, and D.C. Law 26-10 expired on January 21, 2026.
60 (c) The expiration of the foregoing measures necessitates a second round of emergency
61 legislation. The Committee of the Whole intends to move permanent legislation in the fall that
62 will include these provisions.
2

63 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
64 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
65 Medical Cannabis Licensing Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be adopted after
66 a single reading.
67 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
