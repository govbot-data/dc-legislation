10 To amend, on a temporary basis, the District of Columbia Public Works Act of 1954 to clarify that
11 an owner or occupant of property in the District shall pay an impervious area charge that
12 the District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority d/b/a DC Water (“DC Water”)
13 assesses; and to amend the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of
14 Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996 to clarify that DC Water may assess an
15 impervious area charge on any property in the District.
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
18 act may be cited as the “CRIAC Clarification Temporary Amendment Act of 2025”.
19 Sec. 2. The District of Columbia Public Works Act of 1954, approved May 18, 1954 (68
20 Stat. 104; D.C. Official Code § 34-2101 et seq.), is amended as follows:
21 (a) Section 207 (D.C. Official Code § 34-2107) is amended as follows:
22 (1) Subsection (a)(1) is amended to read as follows:
23 “(1) A billing methodology that takes into account both the water consumption of,
24 and water service to, a property where water is supplied from the District water supply system.”.
25 (2) Subsection (c) is repealed.
26 (b) Section 208 (D.C. Official Code § 34-2108) is amended by adding a new subsection
27 (d) to read as follows:

28 “(d) The owner or occupant of each property in the District shall pay any impervious area
29 charge that the District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority establishes pursuant to section
30 216(c-1) of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of Public Works
31 Reorganization Act of 1996, effective April 18, 1996 (D.C. Law 11-111; D.C. Official Code § 34-
32 2202.16(c-1)).”.
33 Sec. 3. Section 216 of the Water and Sewer Authority Establishment and Department of
34 Public Works Reorganization Act of 1996, effective April 18, 1996 (D.C. Law 11-111; D.C.
35 Official Code § 34-2202.16), is amended as follows:
36 (a) A new subsection (c-1) is added to read as follows:
37 “(c-1)(1)(A) The Authority shall assess an impervious area charge on any property in the
38 District based on a billing methodology that takes into account the amount of impervious surface
39 on a property that either prevents or retards the entry of water into the ground as occurring under
40 natural conditions, or that causes water to run off the surface in greater quantities or at an increased
41 rate of flow, relative to the flow present under natural conditions.
42 “(B) For the purposes of this paragraph, the term “surface” includes
43 rooftops, footprints of patios, driveways, private streets, other paved areas, athletic courts and
44 swimming pools, and any path or walkway that is covered by impervious material.
45 “(2) The impervious area charge shall be the obligation of the property owner.
46 Failure to pay the impervious area charge shall result in a lien being placed upon the property
47 without further notice to the owner. The Mayor may enforce the lien in the same manner as in
