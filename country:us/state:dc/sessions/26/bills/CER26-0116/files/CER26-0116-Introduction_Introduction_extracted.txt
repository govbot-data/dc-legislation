6 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
7 __________
8
9
10 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
11
12 ____________________
13
14 To recognize and honor the life, service, and legacy of Joan Adams Thomas Kelley and to
15 commend her for her decades of dedication to education, faith, leadership, and family in the
16 District of Columbia.
17
18 WHEREAS, Joan Adams Thomas Kelley was born on November 8, 1938, in
19 Washington, D.C., to the late Bennie Frank and Thomasine Scott Adams;
20
21 WHEREAS, Joan received her early education in the District of Columbia Public Schools
22 before graduating from Paul Laurence Dunbar High School and later earning degrees from D.C.
23 Teachers College and Georgetown University, reflecting her lifelong commitment to learning
24 and excellence;
25
26 WHEREAS, Joan devoted 43 years to the District of Columbia Public Schools, beginning
27 her teaching career in 1961 and later serving in staff development and leadership roles,
28 ultimately spending 17 years as Principal of John Tyler Elementary School, where her
29 dedication, professionalism, and compassion shaped the lives of generations of students and
30 educators;
31
32 WHEREAS, Joan was an active member of several professional educational
33 organizations and, even after retiring in 2004, remained engaged in supporting the educational
34 community through the D.C. Retired Educators Association and regular fellowship with fellow
35 retired principals;
36
37 WHEREAS, Joan was a proud member of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc. for nearly
38 five decades, exemplifying its values of sisterhood, scholarship, and service throughout her
39 personal and professional life;
40
41 WHEREAS, Joan was a deeply committed church leader who served faithfully in
42 multiple ministries, including the Lay Council, Christian Education, music, youth programming,
43 missionary work, and community outreach, and also contributed her leadership to Church
44 Women United at both the District and regional levels;

45 WHEREAS, Joan cherished her family, raising her daughters Kathy and Dawn, and
46 embraced her extended family of grandchildren, siblings, and loved ones who were central to her
47 life;
48
49 WHEREAS, Joan will be remembered for her warmth, generosity, and radiant
50 personality, as well as for her love of dancing, travel, and the joy she brought to every
51 community she touched;
52
53 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
54 resolution may be cited as the “Joan Adams Thomas Kelley Posthumous Recognition Resolution
55 of 2025”.
56
57 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the life of Joan
58 Adams Thomas Kelley for her extraordinary contributions to public education, her steadfast
59 commitment to faith and service, and the enduring love and inspiration she shared with her
60 family, students, colleagues, and community.
61
62 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
63 the District of Columbia Register.
