Dear Secretary Smith,
Today, I am introducing the Interstate Social Work Licensure Compact Act of 2025. Please find
enclosed a signed copy of the legislation, which is co-introduced by Councilmembers Anita
Bonds, Brooke Pinto, and Trayon White, Sr.
The importance of our social work workforce cannot be overstated. Social workers are essential
partners in our schools, hospitals, housing programs, and public assistance systems. They help
children, families, and individuals navigate major life transitions - no segment of our school or
human services systems could function effectively without their contributions.
The Interstate Social Work Licensure Compact Act will help alleviate the administrative burden
associated with licensure and strengthen our ability to attract and retain qualified professionals.
The legislation would authorize the District of Columbia to join the national Social Work
Compact, an interstate agreement that allows licensed social workers in participating states and
territories to practice across state lines, either in person or via telehealth, without obtaining
multiple state licenses.
Under current law, D.C. licensed social workers cannot provide services to clients located in
another state, even temporarily, unless they hold a license in that jurisdiction. This creates
barriers to continuity of care for clients who move or travel, including military families, college
students, and federal employees. The Compact, endorsed by the National Association of Social
Workers, addresses this issue by promoting mobility and flexibility within the profession while
maintaining high standards of practice.
This bill adopts the standardized compact language to formally establish D.C. as a “member
state” of the Social Work Licensure Compact. It reduces redundant licensure barriers, supports
workforce mobility for social workers, and enables cross-state telehealth social work, all while
preserving the District’s authority to regulate practice and protect clients. Importantly, the
legislation also preserves the District's recently established alternative licensure pathway, which
allows graduate-level social workers to obtain a local license through supervised practice hours
rather than the national exam. That pathway will still exist for graduate-level students seeking to
practice solely in the District.
I have long been a champion of our social work workforce, and this legislation continues that
commitment. Joining the Social Work Licensure Compact is a common-sense step toward
increasing flexibility and strengthening support for social workers across the District. Please
contact Legislative Assistant Kate Conquest (kconquest@ddccouncil.gov) if you have any
questions about the legislation.

Sincerely,
Robert C. White, Jr.

24 To enter the District of Columbia into the interstate Social Work Licensure Compact; to authorize
25 the Department of Health to issue multistate licenses; to clarify that the District retains the
26 authority to enact and enforce certain laws, regulations, or other rules related to the practice
27 of social work; to clarify that the District retains the authority to take adverse action against
28 a multistate license holder; to establish the Social Work Licensure Compact Commission;

30 coordinated data system and to promulgate rules for implementation; to require the
31 executive and judicial branches of District government to enforce and implement the Social

33 are severable; and to require licensees providing services in other jurisdictions adhere to
34 the laws and regulations of the jurisdiction where the client is located.

36 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
37 act may be cited as the “Interstate Social Work Licensure Compact Act of 2025”.
38 Sec. 2. Purpose
39 (a) The purpose of this Compact is to facilitate interstate practice of Regulated Social
40 Workers by improving public access to competent Social Work Services. The Compact preserves
1

41 the regulatory authority of States to protect public health and safety through the current system of
42 State licensure. This Compact is designed to achieve the following objectives:
43 (1) Increase public access to Social Work Services;
44 (2) Reduce overly burdensome and duplicative requirements associated with
45 holding multiple licenses;
46 (3) Enhance the Member States’ ability to protect the public’s health and safety;
47 (4) Encourage the cooperation of Member States in regulating multistate practice;
48 (5) Promote mobility and address workforce shortages by eliminating the necessity
49 for licenses in multiple States by providing for the mutual recognition of other Member State
50 licenses;
51 (6) Support military families;
52 (7) Facilitate the exchange of licensure and disciplinary information among
53 Member States;
54 (8) Authorize all Member States to hold a Regulated Social Worker accountable for
55 abiding by a Member State’s laws, regulations, and applicable professional standards in the
56 Member State in which the client is located at the time care is rendered; and
57 (9) Allow for the use of telehealth to facilitate increased access to regulated Social
58 Work Services.

277 Sec. 6. Issuance of a multistate license
278 (a) Upon receipt of an application for Multistate License, the Home State Licensing
279 Authority shall determine the applicant’s eligibility for a Multistate License in accordance with
280 Section 4 of this Compact.
281 (b) If such applicant is eligible pursuant to Section 4 of this Compact, the Home State
282 Licensing Authority shall issue a Multistate License that authorizes the applicant or Regulated
283 Social Worker to practice in all Member States under a Multistate Authorization to Practice.
284 (c) Upon issuance of a Multistate License, the Home State Licensing Authority shall
285 designate whether the Regulated Social Worker holds a Multistate License in the Bachelors,
286 Masters, or Clinical category of Social Work.
287 (d) A Multistate License issued by a Home State to a resident in that State shall be
288 recognized by all Compact Member States as authorizing Social Work Practice under a Multistate
289 Authorization to Practice corresponding to each category of licensure regulated in each Member
290 State.

291 Sec. 7. Authority of interstate compact commission and member state licensing authorities
292 (a) Nothing in this Compact, nor any Rule of the Commission, shall be construed to limit,
293 restrict, or in any way reduce the ability of a Member State to enact and enforce laws, regulations,
294 or other rules related to the practice of Social Work in that State, where those laws, regulations, or
295 other rules are not inconsistent with the provisions of this Compact.
296 (b) Nothing in this Compact shall affect the requirements established by a Member State
297 for the issuance of a Single State License.
298 (c) Nothing in this Compact, nor any Rule of the Commission, shall be construed to limit,
299 restrict, or in any way reduce the ability of a Member State to take Adverse Action against a
300 Licensee’s Single State License to practice Social Work in that State.
301 (d) Nothing in this Compact, nor any Rule of the Commission, shall be construed to limit,
302 restrict, or in any way reduce the ability of a Remote State to take Adverse Action against a
303 Licensee’s Multistate Authorization to Practice in that State.
304 (e) Nothing in this Compact, nor any Rule of the Commission, shall be construed to limit,
305 restrict, or in any way reduce the ability of a Licensee’s Home State to take Adverse Action against
306 a Licensee’s Multistate License based upon information provided by a Remote State.
307 Sec. 8. Reissuance of a multistate license by a new home state
308 (a) A Licensee can hold a Multistate License, issued by their Home State, in only one
309 Member State at any given time.
310 (b) If a Licensee changes their Home State by moving between two Member States:
311 (1) The Licensee shall immediately apply for the reissuance of their Multistate
312 License in their new Home State. The Licensee shall pay all applicable fees and notify the prior
313 Home State in accordance with the Rules of the Commission.
13

314 (2) Upon receipt of an application to reissue a Multistate License, the new Home
315 State shall verify that the Multistate License is active, unencumbered and eligible for reissuance
316 under the terms of the Compact and the Rules of the Commission. The Multistate License issued
317 by the prior Home State will be deactivated and all Member States notified in accordance with the
318 applicable Rules adopted by the Commission.
319 (3) Prior to the reissuance of the Multistate License, the new Home State shall
320 conduct procedures for considering the criminal history records of the Licensee. Such procedures
321 shall include the submission of fingerprints or other biometric-based information by applicants for
322 the purpose of obtaining an applicant’s criminal history record information from the Federal
323 Bureau of Investigation and the agency responsible for retaining that State’s criminal records.
324 (4) If required for initial licensure, the new Home State may require completion of
325 jurisprudence requirements in the new Home State.
326 (5) Notwithstanding any other provision of this Compact, if a Licensee does not
327 meet the requirements set forth in this Compact for the reissuance of a Multistate License by the
328 new Home State, then the Licensee shall be subject to the new Home State requirements for the
329 issuance of a Single State License in that State.
330 (c) If a Licensee changes their primary State of residence by moving from a Member State
331 to a non-Member State, or from a non-Member State to a Member State, then the Licensee shall
332 be subject to the State requirements for the issuance of a Single State License in the new Home
333 State.
334 (d) Nothing in this Compact shall interfere with a Licensee’s ability to hold a Single State
335 License in multiple States; however, for the purposes of this Compact, a Licensee shall have only
336 one Home State, and only one Multistate License.
14

337 (e) Nothing in this Compact shall interfere with the requirements established by a Member
338 State for the issuance of a Single State License.

343 Sec. 10. Adverse actions
344 (a) In addition to the other powers conferred by State law, a Remote State shall have the
345 authority, in accordance with existing State due process law, to:
346 (1) Take Adverse Action against a Regulated Social Worker’s Multistate
347 Authorization to Practice only within that Member State, and issue subpoenas for both hearings
348 and investigations that require the attendance and testimony of witnesses as well as the production
349 of evidence. Subpoenas issued by a Licensing Authority in a Member State for the attendance and
350 testimony of witnesses or the production of evidence from another Member State shall be enforced
351 in the latter State by any court of competent jurisdiction, according to the practice and procedure
352 of that court applicable to subpoenas issued in proceedings pending before it. The issuing
353 Licensing Authority shall pay any witness fees, travel expenses, mileage, and other fees required
354 by the service statutes of the State in which the witnesses or evidence are located.
355 (2) Only the Home State shall have the power to take Adverse Action against a
356 Regulated Social Worker’s Multistate License.
357 (b) For purposes of taking Adverse Action, the Home State shall give the same priority and
358 effect to reported conduct received from a Member State as it would if the conduct had occurred
15

359 within the Home State. In so doing, the Home State shall apply its own State laws to determine
360 appropriate action.
361 (c) The Home State shall complete any pending investigations of a Regulated Social
362 Worker who changes their Home State during the course of the investigations. The Home State
363 shall also have the authority to take appropriate action(s) and shall promptly report the conclusions
364 of the investigations to the administrator of the Data System. The administrator of the Data System
365 shall promptly notify the new Home State of any Adverse Actions.
366 (d) A Member State, if otherwise permitted by State law, may recover from the affected
367 Regulated Social Worker the costs of investigations and dispositions of cases resulting from any
368 Adverse Action taken against that Regulated Social Worker.
369 (e) A Member State may take Adverse Action based on the factual findings of another
370 Member State, provided that the Member State follows its own procedures for taking the Adverse
371 Action.
372 (f) Joint Investigations:
373 (1) In addition to the authority granted to a Member State by its respective Social
374 Work practice act or other applicable State law, any Member State may participate with other
375 Member States in joint investigations of Licensees.
376 (2) Member States shall share any investigative, litigation, or compliance materials
377 in furtherance of any joint or individual investigation initiated under the Compact.
378 (g) If Adverse Action is taken by the Home State against the Multistate License of a
379 Regulated Social Worker, the Regulated Social Worker’s Multistate Authorization to Practice in
380 all other Member States shall be deactivated until all Encumbrances have been removed from the
381 Multistate License. All Home State disciplinary orders that impose Adverse Action against the
16

382 license of a Regulated Social Worker shall include a statement that the Regulated Social Worker’s
383 Multistate Authorization to Practice is deactivated in all Member States until all conditions of the
384 decision, order or agreement are satisfied.
385 (h) If a Member State takes Adverse Action, it shall promptly notify the administrator of
386 the Data System. The administrator of the Data System shall promptly notify the Home State and
387 all other Member State’s of any Adverse Actions by Remote States.
388 (i) Nothing in this Compact shall override a Member State’s decision that participation in
389 an Alternative Program may be used in lieu of Adverse Action.
390 (j) Nothing in this Compact shall authorize a Member State to demand the issuance of
391 subpoenas for attendance and testimony of witnesses or the production of evidence from another
392 Member State for lawful actions within that Member State.
393 (k) Nothing in this Compact shall authorize a Member State to impose discipline against a
394 Regulated Social Worker who holds a Multistate Authorization to Practice for lawful actions
395 within another Member State.
396 Sec. 11. Establishment of a social work licensure compact commission
397 (a) The Compact Member States hereby create and establish a joint government agency
398 whose membership consists of all Member States that have enacted the compact known as the
399 Social Work Licensure Compact Commission. The Commission is an instrumentality of the
400 Compact States acting jointly and not an instrumentality of any one State. The Commission shall
401 come into existence on or after the effective date of the Compact as set forth in Section 14.
402 (b) Membership, Voting, and Meetings
403 (1) Each Member State shall have and be limited to one (1) delegate selected by
404 that Member State’s State Licensing Authority.
17

405 (2) The delegate shall be either:
406 (A) A current member of the State Licensing Authority at the time of
407 appointment, who is a Regulated Social Worker or public member of the State Licensing
408 Authority; or
409 (B) An administrator of the State Licensing Authority or their designee.
410 (3) The Commission shall by Rule or bylaw establish a term of office for delegates
411 and may by Rule or bylaw establish term limits.
412 (4) The Commission may recommend removal or suspension any delegate from
413 office.
414 (5) A Member State’s State Licensing Authority shall fill any vacancy of its
415 delegate occurring on the Commission within 60 days of the vacancy.
416 (6) Each delegate shall be entitled to one vote on all matters before the Commission
417 requiring a vote by Commission delegates.
418 (7) A delegate shall vote in person or by such other means as provided in the
419 bylaws. The bylaws may provide for delegates to meet by telecommunication, videoconference,
420 or other means of communication.
421 (8) The Commission shall meet at least once during each calendar year. Additional
422 meetings may be held as set forth in the bylaws. The Commission may meet by telecommunication,
423 video conference or other similar electronic means.
424 (c) The Commission shall have the following powers:
425 (1) Establish the fiscal year of the Commission;
426 (2) Establish code of conduct and conflict of interest policies;
427 (3) Establish and amend Rules and bylaws;
18

428 (4) Maintain its financial records in accordance with the bylaws;
429 (5) Meet and take such actions as are consistent with the provisions of this Compact,
430 the Commission’s Rules, and the bylaws;
431 (6) Initiate and conclude legal proceedings or actions in the name of the
432 Commission, provided that the standing of any State Licensing Board to sue or be sued under

664 allow persons to provide oral and written comments, data, facts, opinions, and arguments.

666 in advance of the meeting at which the Commission will hold a public hearing on the proposed
667 Rule, the Commission shall provide a Notice of Proposed Rulemaking:
668 (1) On the website of the Commission or other publicly accessible platform;
669 (2) To persons who have requested notice of the Commission’s notices of proposed
670 rulemaking, and
671 (3) In such other way(s) as the Commission may by Rule specify.
672 (h) The Notice of Proposed Rulemaking shall include:
673 (1) The time, date, and location of the public hearing at which the Commission will
674 hear public comments on the proposed Rule and, if different, the time, date, and location of the
675 meeting where the Commission will consider and vote on the proposed Rule;
29

676 (2) If the hearing is held via telecommunication, video conference, or other
677 electronic means, the Commission shall include the mechanism for access to the hearing in the
678 Notice of Proposed Rulemaking;
679 (3) The text of the proposed Rule and the reason therefor;
680 (4) A request for comments on the proposed Rule from any interested person; and
681 (5) The manner in which interested persons may submit written comments.
682 (i) All hearings will be recorded. A copy of the recording and all written comments and
683 documents received by the Commission in response to the proposed Rule shall be available to the

692 changes made to the proposed Rule as well as reasons for substantive changes not made that were

696 sooner than 30 days after issuing the notice that it adopted or amended the Rule.

30

699 Rulemaking procedures provided in the Compact and in this section shall be retroactively applied

705 (3) Meet a deadline for the promulgation of a Rule that is established by federal law
