21 To recognize, honor, and remember the life of Mathew McCollough and his exceptional
22 advocacy for people with disabilities and devoted service to the United States and District
23 of Columbia.
24
25 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was born with cerebral palsy and was fourteen years old
26 when the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) was signed into federal law by President
27 George H. Bush in 1990;
28 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was a resident of the District of Columbia for 25 years,
29 and a Ward 6 resident for much of that time;
30 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough had a long and distinguished career working in disability
31 advocacy and public service;
32 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough, began his career serving in U.S. Department of Justice;
33 Office of Justice Programs (OJP) from 2000 to 2002, and left because he observed there were
34 few employees like him who were from diverse, ethnic backgrounds and/or had a disability;
1

35 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as a grants manager and trainer at the Association
36 of University Centers on Disabilities and National Service Inclusion Project, a training and
37 technical assistance provider that advocates on behalf of individuals with disabilities to fully
38 participate in service and civic-minded programs within their communities, from 2002 to 2008;
39 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was recognized for his communication and training
40 expertise in the areas of education, diversity and sensitivity, cultural competency, and disability
41 issues;
42 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served in the Office of Disability Rights (ODR) since the
43 agency was established;
44 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as ODR’s first Communications Manager for 2
45 years; from 2008 to 2010;
46 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough, as Communications Manager, was lead coordinator to
47 enforce Section 508 web accessibility compliance of the DC.gov portal and to collect and
48 monitor the effectiveness of 19 agencies' Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) compliance
49 plans and 108 facility assessments;
50 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as the Executive Director of the DC Developmental
51 Disabilities Council (DC DDC), which promotes independence and equal opportunity for District
52 residents with intellectual and developmental disabilities, for 7 years, from 2010 to 2017;
53 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was the only the Executive Director of a state
54 developmental disabilities council with developmental disabilities in the nation;
55 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough, as Executive Director of DC DDC, supervised two staff
56 personnel and collaboratively worked with fifteen District resident volunteers charged with
2

57 strengthening the voices of people with developmental disabilities and their families in support
58 of greater independence, empowerment, and the pursuit of life as they choose;
59 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as the ODR Director for eight years, after being
60 nominated by Mayor Muriel Bowser and confirmed unanimously by the Council of the District
61 of Columbia in 2017;
62 WHEREAS, during his confirmation hearing, Mr. McCollough’s record of providing
63 excellent advocacy, especially within the deaf community and his high degree understanding the
64 requirements and accommodations of people with disabilities were commended by Jarvis
65 Grindstaff, a Commissioner on the DC Commission on Persons with Disabilities;
66 WHEREAS, during his confirmation hearing, Mr. McCollough’s assistance setting up the
67 Hearing Loss Association of America’s DC Chapter and continued support was recognized by
68 Lon Rosenman, Vice President, Hearing Loss Association of America - DC Chapter;
69 WHEREAS, during his confirmation hearing, Mr. McCollough committed that his one
70 mission as ODR director is to “ensure that The District of Columbia serves as a model city of
71 accessibility for all who live and work in this great city and across the United States”;
72 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough, as ODR Director, led the development and
73 implementation of the 3 most recent three-year District Olmstead Plans; 2017 – 2020; 2021 –
74 2024; and 2025 – 2027; which provide a comprehensive, effective working plan to demonstrate
75 the District’s compliance with the ADA’s integration mandate, as clarified in the 1999 U.S.
76 Supreme Court Case Olmstead v. L.C., in which the Court ruled people with disabilities have the
77 right to live and receive care in the community rather than in an institutional setting;
3

78 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough offered detailed and thoughtful testimony during the
79 agency’s performance and budget oversight hearings over 5 Council Periods and before 2
80 Committee Chairs;
81 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough oversaw ODR’s partnership with the Department of
82 General Services to create a new category of ADA-related work orders to better and more
83 quickly resolve facilities issues that create accessibility barriers in District government spaces in
84 Fiscal Year 2025;
85 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough oversaw the transition of the Office of Deaf, DeafBlind,
86 and Hard of Hearing into the Division of Deaf, DeafBlind, & Hard of Hearing within ODR in
87 Fiscal Year 2026;
88 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough closed ODR staff meetings by saying, “Take care of each
89 other, and love each other”;
90 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough held memberships and positions of leadership on
91 numerous national and District boards and commissions;
92 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as the Mayoral-appointed Government
93 Representative to the DC State Rehabilitation Council; from 2009 to 2025;
94 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as the Chair of the DC State Rehabilitation
95 Council; from 2016 to 2017;
96 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was appointed by President Barack Obama to serve on the
97 United States Access Board, an independent federal agency devoted to establishing accessibility
98 standards that promote the full integration and participation of people with disabilities, in 2011;
99 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was reappointed by President Barack Obama to serve a
100 second term on the United States Access Board in 2015;
4

101 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough, nominated by the US. Department of Health and Human
102 Services Office of Minority Health to serve as a member of the Department’s Region III Health
103 Equity Council, from 2011 to 2016;
104 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as a Commissioner on Human Rights; from 2012 to
105 2017;
106 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as a Commissioner of the Serve DC Commission in
107 the Mayor’s Office of Volunteerism; from 2012 to 2018;
108 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as a board member of the TASH- Equity,
109 Opportunity & Inclusion for People with Disabilities, which internationally advocates for human
110 rights and inclusion for people with significant disabilities and support, from 2012 to 2015;
111 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was a member of the Georgetown-Howard Universities
112 Center for Clinical and Translational Science Community Advisory Board; from 2012 to 2026;
113 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served as the Board President of the National Association
114 of Councils on Developmental Disabilities (NACDD), from 2016 to 2017;
115 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough made history as the first NACDD Board President with
116 developmental disabilities;
117 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough served on the District of Columbia's 44th, 45th, and 46th,
118 Presidential Inaugural Committee, reviewed and offered accessibility recommendations on the
119 Presidential Inaugural's Evacuation Walk-out Concept of Operations Plan, and provided ADA
120 compliance guidance to representatives from the National Presidential Inaugural Committee;
121 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough achieved an impressive academic career, during which he
122 earned multiple awards and recognitions;
123 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was received the Eagle Scout Award in 1991;
5

124 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough graduated from James Madison University with a
125 Batchelor of Arts in Political Science and a minor in Middle School Education in 1999;
126 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was inducted into the Alpha Phi Omega National Service
127 Fraternity in 1998;
128 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough graduated from American University in 2004 with a
129 Master’s in Public Administration (MPA) with a concentration in Management Consulting in
130 2004, and noted during his confirmation hearing this degree “solidified] my commitment and
131 purpose to serving all people with and without disabilities as a public servant”;
132 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was inducted into the Pi Alpha Alpha, Public
133 Administration National Honors Society in 2003;
134 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was the Director and Chapter Founder of the Best Buddies
135 International, James Madison University from 1997 to 1999;
136 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough held a staff faculty position at the Leadership Institute for
137 Cultural Diversity & Linguistic and Cultural Competence, from 2015 to 2017;
138 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was inducted into the Susan M Daniels Mentoring Hall of
139 Fame Class of 2016;
140 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough’s life and legacy were remembered and honored
141 throughout the District of Columbia upon the announcement of his passing;
142 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was cherished and will be deeply missed by his wife,
143 Melissa McCollough, family, friends, and all those who loved him.
144 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was admired by previous and current ODR employees and
145 leaves a great absence within the agency that has never existed without him;
6

146 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough’s ODR colleagues reflected he “truly loved his work and
147 believed in the power of community” and that “[h]is heart was with the community first as he
148 prioritized programs and events that connected people with disabilities to important resources or
149 empowered them in their own advocacy journeys”;
150 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough’s ODR colleagues shared his love for “engaging with
151 young people and made the most of every chance to show them that they can, and should, take
152 on leadership and decision-making roles”;
153 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was remembered by Mayor Muriel Bowser, under whom
154 he served for 11 years, for serving “with extraordinary dedication and passion in public service”
155 and for a career that “reflected his deep belief in dignity, opportunity, and respect for every
156 individual”;
157 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was remembered by Councilmember Janeese Lewis
158 George, who chaired the committee that held oversight jurisdiction over ODR for the past 3
159 years as a “fierce advocate for and recognized champion of people with disabilities” and for his
160 belief “legal compliance is the minimum — not maximum — standard for accessibility”;
161 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was remembered by Councilmember Charles Allen as a
162 fierce advocate, who spoke truth to power, and for his kindness and compassion when working
163 with elected officials and constituents;
164 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was remembered by Councilmember Brianne Nadeau,
165 who chaired the committee that previously held oversight jurisdiction over ODR, as “kind,
166 funny, incredibly smart and deeply dedicated to the critical role of protecting the rights of people
167 with disabilities” and a “collaborative and transparent leader”;
7

168 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough was remembered by Councilmember Matthew Frumin as
169 “widely respected for his expertise, leadership, and commitment to public service”;
170 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough’s legacy presses governments at all levels to reduce and
171 eliminate discrimination and barriers to access within the areas of employment, housing,
172 transportation, healthcare, and public services and spaces;
173 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough’s legacy encourages citizens of all abilities to identify and
174 remove ableism through intentional examination of individual and institutional values, attitudes,
175 beliefs, and actions; and
176 WHEREAS, Mr. McCollough’s decades of advocacy and service will continue to impact
177 and improve the lives of individuals with disabilities in the District of Columbia and United
178 States of America for decades to come;
179 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
180 resolution may be cited as the “Mathew McCollough Memorial Recognition Resolution of
181 2026”.
182 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes, honors, and remembers the life
183 of Mathew McCollough and his exceptional advocacy for people with disabilities and devoted
184 service to the United States and District of Columbia.
185 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
