117 Sec. 3. Findings.
118 (a) The RFK Campus consists of approximately 180 acres of waterfront property located
119 in the District within Reservation 343F, as shown on the transfer of jurisdiction plat recorded in
120 the Surveyor’s Office on February 5, 2025, on page 13 of subdivision book 223. The District has
121 been unable to redevelop the site over the last decade due to federal restrictions on development
122 at the site, and the site has remained vacant, blighted, and fallow. Over the course of the last
123 decade, the District has worked to gain broader control of the site and to remove the federal
124 development restrictions so that a new, multi-use future for the area could be realized.
125 (b) With bipartisan support from Congress, the President signed the D.C. Robert F.
126 Kennedy Memorial Stadium Campus Revitalization Act, approved on January 6, 2025 (Pub. L.
127 No. 118-274; 138 Stat. 3234), into law. Pursuant to this law, the National Park Service
128 transferred administrative jurisdiction over the RFK Campus to the District. The Council
129 approved the transfer of jurisdiction pursuant to the Transfer of Jurisdiction Over the Robert F.
130 Kennedy Memorial Stadium Campus Emergency Approval Resolution of 2025, effective
131 February 4, 2025 (Res. 26-16; 72 DCR 1436).
132 (c) The District now has a once-in-a-generation opportunity to continue to revitalize the
133 Anacostia Waterfront, catalyze growth, and develop a vibrant, mixed-use development featuring
134 community sports, professional sports, public recreation, public park space, and cultural
7

135 amenities. The development will provide the District with significant economic, cultural, social,
136 and financial benefits.
137 (d) Since gaining administrative jurisdiction over the RFK Campus, the District
138 government has undertaken a comprehensive public engagement process, considering the
139 specific interests of communities near the RFK Campus; economic development opportunities;
140 the capacity of the campus to generate jobs for District residents and opportunities for District
141 businesses; park, open space, and recreational options; river access options; potential community
142 amenities; opportunities to create new housing units; and environmental and historical factors,
143 among other considerations, to determine the best uses for the site.
144 (e) Considering the public input received, the existing infrastructure of the campus, and
145 the possibility of a catalytic investment in the District, it was determined that the construction
146 and operation of a new first-class, state-of-the-art, sports stadium with supporting facilities,
147 including parking, and infrastructure, for use by the professional football franchise owned by
148 Pro-Football LLC, and an adjacent Sportsplex would be of considerable public benefit to the
149 District as the first stage of the redevelopment and revitalization of the entire campus.
150 (f) To ensure delivery of the Stadium, supporting facilities, and Sportsplex by 2030, a
151 limited zoning exemption is necessary for portions of the RFK Campus.
152 (g) Planning for other residential and commercial development at the RFK Campus, in
153 addition to other uses permitted by the transfer of jurisdiction and the Declaration of Covenants,
8

154 will continue as the Stadium and Sportsplex projects move forward, and will inform the
155 Comprehensive Plan rewrite (DC 2050).
156 (h) The District intends for the comprehensive redevelopment of the RFK Campus to
157 create a vibrant, multi-use community and destination that will create new jobs, business
158 opportunities, housing, community amenities, and neighborhood-serving retail and achieve the 5
159 Anacostia Waterfront Framework Plan themes: (1) a clean and active river, (2) gaining access to,
160 along, and across the river, (3) a great riverfront park system, (4) a riverfront of distinct places
161 and cultural destinations, and (5) building and sustaining strong waterfront neighborhoods.
162 (i) The District and Developer have entered into the Term Sheet for the development of
163 the Stadium and supporting facilities and the redevelopment of a portion of the RFK Campus
164 that sets forth the terms and conditions upon which the parties are willing to undertake the
165 projects.
166 (j) The Commanders have made additional commitments regarding the redevelopment of
167 the RFK Campus in a July 29, 2025, letter to the Council.
168 (k) The construction, development, and revitalization of the RFK Campus and the public
169 ownership of the Stadium, for use for athletic and entertainment events, is a municipal use that is
170 in the interest of, and for the benefit of, District residents because such a publicly-owned
171 Stadium will contribute to the social and economic well-being of District residents and
172 significantly enhance economic development and employment opportunities in the District.
9

173 (l) To further that interest, it is appropriate for the District to take certain actions with
174 respect to the redevelopment of the RFK Campus, as further set forth in this act.
