5 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
6 ______________
7
8
9 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
10
11 ____________________________
12
13 To recognize Jonathan R. Alger for his inauguration as the 16th President of American
14 University.
15
16 WHEREAS, Mr. Alger officially began his service to American University as its 16th
17 president on July 1, 2024;
18
19 WHEREAS, President Alger presides over an institution with eight schools and colleges,
20 over 170 programs, and more than 13,000 students, that has for 132 years fostered the next
21 generation of change makers;
22
23 WHEREAS, President Alger previously served as president of James Madison University
24 for 12 years, where throughout his tenure the university advanced academic achievement, a sense
25 of community, civic activism by the students and community engagement by the University;
26
27 WHEREAS, President Alger serves as the chair of the national board of directors for the
28 American Association of Colleges and Universities, chair of the Association of Governing
29 Boards’ Council of Presidents, and on the Knight Commission on Intercollegiate Athletics;
30
31 WHEREAS, President Alger has already demonstrated his desire and commitment to the
32 American University community through over 30 listening sessions during the fall semester and
33 the launch of The Civic Life initiative; and
34
35 WHEREAS, the Council shares the excitement of the American University community as
36 it embarks on its next chapter under President Alger’s leadership.
37
38 IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF
39 COLUMBIA, that this resolution may be cited as the “American University President Jonathan
40 R. Alger Inaugural Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
41

42 Sec. 2. The Council and District of Columbia welcome President Alger and his family
43 and embrace his desire to contribute to American University and the D.C. metro region
44 community.
45
46 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
47 the District of Columbia Register.
