19 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend, on an emergency
20 basis, the District of Columbia Official Code to allow retail establishments with valid on-
21 premises Class C and Class D retailer license to refuse cash as a form of payment.
22 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
23 resolution may be cited as the “Entertainment Establishment Employee Safety Extension
24 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025.”.
25 Sec. 2. (a) This amendment would permit a retail establishment with a valid on-premises
26 Class C or Class D retailer license or a restaurant location that operates 24 hours a day or
27 operates at 3:00 am at least 2 days a week would be able to refuse cash as a form of payment,
28 provided that such establishments display written, conspicuous notice to the public that cash
29 payments are not accepted on the door of the establishment or menu.
30 (b) This measure was introduced and approved in Council Period 25 in recognition of
31 the changes in consumer use of payment forms post-pandemic and to balance unbanked
32 individuals’ reliance on cash as a form of payment to obtain necessary goods and services with
33 the safety concerns of particularly vulnerable businesses.

34 The emergency and temporary measures are identical to those approved by the Council
35 on December 17, 2024. It is necessary to extend this measure’s applicability while the permanent
36 measure is pending in committee.
37 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
