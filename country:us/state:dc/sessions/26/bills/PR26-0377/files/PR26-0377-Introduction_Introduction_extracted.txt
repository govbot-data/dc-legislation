A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
______________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
__________________
1 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Department of
2 For-Hire Vehicles Establishment Act of 1985 to clarify the definition of a private vehicle-
3 for-hire operator..
4
5 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
6 resolution may be cited as the “Private Vehicle-for-Hire Operator Clarification Emergency
7 Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
8 Sec. 2. (a) The District’s for-hire vehicle laws are designed to authorize only properly
9 licensed, insured, and identifiable vehicles and operators to transport passengers for
10 compensation. Specifically, private vehicle-for-hire operators are required to be registered with a
11 private vehicle-for-hire company that is registered to operate in the District and must provide
12 service only through the registered digital dispatch of such a company. These legal requirements
13 are carefully tailored to safeguard public safety, accountability, and passenger trust.
14 (b) For several years, the Department of For-Hire Vehicles (DFHV) and the Metropolitan
15 Police Department (MPD) have identified an increase in illegal vehicle-for-hire services wherein
16 certain operators are not registered with a private vehicle-for-hire company that is legally
1

17 authorized to operate in the District. In these instances, vehicle-for-hire service is being provided
18 without confirmation that the operator holds required commercial insurance, without internal
19 processes to screen out dangerous or predatory drivers, and without proper trade dress as
20 required by District law.
21 (c) Failure of such vehicle-for-hire services to comply with insurance and registration
22 requirements poses an immediate and irreparable risk of harm to passengers, operators, and the
23 public. In the event of a crash, unregistered or uninsured operators cannot provide coverage or
24 recourse for injuries or damages.
25 (d) Further, for illegal operations, there is no guarantee of processes to appropriately
26 identify and screen out dangerous operators, both in terms of operators who have demonstrated a
27 record of unsafe driving or those who pose a meaningful risk of harm to passengers. Illegal
28 operations may also lack a process for passengers to file complaints or otherwise seek reasonable
29 redress following an incident involving operator misconduct.
30 (e) Where vehicles used to provide illegal service lack proper trade dress, passengers are
31 prevented from verifying the vehicles as legitimate. This practice impedes enforcement officers’
32 efforts to determine whether a particular operator is providing service legally.
33 (f) This illegal activity has a harmful effect on the broader for-hire vehicle industry,
34 eroding public confidence in legitimate for-hire services and undermining safety, fair
35 competition, and the District’s consumer protection framework.
36 (g) Recent proceedings at D.C. Superior Court have challenged DFHV’s authority to
37 enforce illegal for-hire-vehicle operations, as described in subsections (a) through (f), with an
38 unregistered private vehicle-for-hire company attempting to take advantage of ambiguity in the
39 Department of For-Hire Vehicles Establishment Act of 1985.
2

40 (h) Emergency legislation is therefore necessary to clarify that DFHV has had and
41 continues be empowered with the authority to address the range of threats to passenger and
42 driver safety caused by illegal for-hire vehicle operators and companies, empowering DFHV to
43 continue enforcing registration, insurance, and trade dress requirements.
44 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
45 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making it necessary that the Private Vehicle-for-
46 Hire Operator Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single
47 reading.
48 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
