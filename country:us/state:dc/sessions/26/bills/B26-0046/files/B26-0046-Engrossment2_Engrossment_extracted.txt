12 To amend section 8f of the Homeless Services Reform Act of 2005 to require specific
13 documentation from tenants establishing eligibility for Emergency Rental Assistance
14 Funds, to clarify the definition of a qualifying emergency situation, and to require the
15 Mayor to issue regulations implementing the amended provisions of that section; and to
16 amend section 501 of the Rental Housing Act of 1985 to permit a court to enter a stay,
17 rather than require a court to enter a stay, when a tenant submits documentation to the court
18 demonstrating that the tenant has a pending Emergency Rental Assistance Program
19 application, to require housing providers to reschedule evictions in certain circumstances
20 involving pending and approved Emergency Rental Assistance Program applications, and
21 to create an affirmative defense in eviction cases on the basis of housing providers’
22 noncompliance with the Emergency Rental Assistance Program application process.
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
25 act may be cited as the “Emergency Rental Assistance Program Reform Amendment Act of 2025”.

54 Sec. 3. Section 501 of the Rental Housing Act of 1985, effective July 17, 1985 (D.C. Law
55 6-10; D.C. Official Code § 42-3505.01), is amended as follows:
56 (a) Subsection (r) is amended to read as follows:
57 “(r)(1) The court may stay any proceeding adjudicating a claim brought by a housing
58 provider to recover possession of a rental unit for nonpayment of rent if a tenant submits
59 documentation to the court demonstrating that he or she has a pending Emergency Rental
60 Assistance Program (“ERAP”) application; provided, that the court may stay a proceeding under
61 this subsection only once during the pendency of the case.
62 “(2) Paragraph (1) of this subsection shall not be construed to limit a court’s
63 discretion to extend or continue a stay.
64 “(3) When an eviction based solely on a claim for nonpayment of rent has been
65 authorized by the court and the tenant notifies the housing provider, no later than 48 hours prior to
66 the scheduled date and time of the eviction, that the tenant has an approved ERAP application that
67 would pay the full amount indicated in the Notice to Tenant of Payment Required to Avoid
68 Eviction, the housing provider shall reschedule the eviction for a date no earlier than 3 weeks from
69 the scheduled eviction date.
