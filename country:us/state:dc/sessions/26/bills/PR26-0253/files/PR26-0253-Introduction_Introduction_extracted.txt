79 Sec. 3. Findings.
80 The Council finds that:
81 (1) Section 490 of the Home Rule Act provides that the Council may by resolution
82 authorize the issuance of District revenue bonds, notes, or other obligations (including refunding
83 bonds, notes, or other obligations) to borrow money to finance, refinance, or reimburse costs, and to
84 assist in the financing, refinancing, or reimbursing of the costs of undertakings in certain areas
85 designated in section 490 and may effect the financing, refinancing, or reimbursement by loans
86 made directly or indirectly to any individual or legal entity, by the purchase of any mortgage, note,
87 or other security, or by the purchase, lease, or sale of any property.
88 (2) The Borrower has requested the District to issue, sell, and deliver revenue bonds,
89 in one or more series, in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed $37.5 million and to make the
90 Loan for the purpose of financing, refinancing, or reimbursing costs of the Project.
91 (3) The Project is located in the District and will contribute to the health, education,
92 safety, or welfare of, or the creation or preservation of jobs for, residents of the District, or to
93 economic development of the District.
94 (4) The Project is an undertaking in the area of commercial development within the
95 meaning of section 490 of the Home Rule Act.
96 (5) The authorization, issuance, sale, and delivery of the Bonds and the Loan to
97 the Borrower are desirable, are in the public interest, will promote the purpose and intent of
98 section 490 of the Home Rule Act, and will assist the Project.

THE USBC ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT CORPORATION
Revenue Bond Project
FACT SHEET
The USBC Economic Development Corporation has requested that the “District” issue up to
$37,500,000 of revenue bonds for financing and refinancing costs accrued for the acquisition and
transformation of the former Black Entertainment Television (“BET”) Networks campus located
at 1900 W Place, N.E., Washington, D.C. 20018 in Ward 5 along with the acquisition and
transformation of 1301 W Street, N.E., 1235 W Street, N.E., and Parcel C a 1.73 acre parking lot
(Square 142, Lots 133, 134, 125, and 123).
The Applicant
The USBC Economic Development Corporation (USBC EDC) is a non-profit development
corporation created to support financial education, training needs, CARES ACT pandemic relief
resources, and programs of the U.S. Black Chambers, Inc. USBC EDC is a national network of
Black Chambers of Commerce committed to providing visionary leadership and advocacy in the
realization of economic empowerment. Through the creation of resources and initiatives, USBC
EDC supports African American Chambers of Commerce and business organizations in their work
of developing and growing Black enterprises across the nation.
Proposed Project
The proposed bond proceeds will be used to finance the purchase and transformation of space
located at the former home of BET studios located at 1900 W Place NE, in Ward 5, along with the
acquisition and transformation of 1301 W Street, N.E., 1235 W Street, N.E., and Parcel C a 1.73
acre parking lot (Square 142, Lots 133, 134, 125, and 123). The renovated campus (including
additional properties) will become USBC EDC’s headquarters, and a dynamic ecosystem
dedicated to the growth of DC small business markets and a catalyst for development throughout
the surrounding area.
Financing Plan
A summary of the proposed sources and uses of funds is attached (see Table 1).
Feasibility/Structure/Security of the Bonds
USBC EDC has reviewed the financial feasibility of the USBC Economic Development
Corporation in connection with the $37,500,000 Revenue Bond application. Based on the
assessment of the USBC EDC finances and financing needs, USBC has been advised to pursue
public offering of tax-exempt bonds and believes that such financing is feasible under current
market conditions.
Public Purpose Benefits

U.S. Black Chambers Economic Development Corporation
The issuance of these bonds will create and further economic development in the Ward 5
Brentwood neighborhood of D.C. This will enhance career and contracting opportunities for
District residents and business.
Legal and Regulatory Affairs
Graves & Horton, LLC is assigned as bond counsel to the Revenue Bond Program and has
preliminarily determined that the applicant is a 501(c)(3) organization, and the project constitutes
a permissible undertaking in Section 490(a)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act.
Based upon the information outlined in the application, the Revenue Bond Program staff has
determined that the proposed project complies with the criteria for approval of a proposed
financing transaction through the District’s Revenue Bond Program and will assist USBC EDC in
furthering its organizational mission.
