12 To require institutions of higher education in the District to retain at least two confidential
13 resource advisors, to require amnesty protections for student code of conduct violations
14 under certain circumstances, to require institutions make reasonable efforts to require
15 every undergraduate student to complete sexual misconduct trainings that conform with
16 minimum criteria, to require institution personnel involved in an institution’s sexual
17 misconduct investigative and disciplinary processes to receive annual training in handling
18 sexual misconduct complaints and in the operations of the institution's disciplinary
19 process, to require confidential resource advisors, Title IX coordinators, and institution
20 public safety personnel to be educated in trauma-informed responses, to establish
21 minimum criteria for institutions’ sexual misconduct policies, to require institutions to
22 prepare and submit to the Higher Education Licensure Commission and publish on their
23 websites a report with data about confidential resource advisor and Title IX office
24 outcomes at the institution, and to establish penalties for institutions that do not comply
25 with this act; and to make conforming amendments.
26
27 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
28 act may be cited as the “Institution of Higher Education Sexual Misconduct Reporting and
29 Resource Accessibility Act of 2025”.
30 Sec. 2. Definitions.
31 For the purposes of this act, the term:
32 (1) “Assisted party” means a student at or an employee of an institution who seeks
33 the services of a confidential resource advisor.

67 Sec. 3. Confidential resource advisor role and responsibilities.
68 (a)(1) Each institution shall employ or contract for at least 2 confidential resource
69 advisors to serve as confidential resources for students and employees seeking resources
70 pertaining to sexual misconduct.
71 (2) An institution shall notify all students and employees, in writing, of the
72 identities, contact information for, and availability of its confidential resource advisors.

74 with resources related to sexual misconduct in written or electronic format, which may include
75 website links, pamphlets, handouts, and information packets. An institution may authorize its
76 Title IX Coordinator to approve the resources to be provided by confidential resource providers
77 before dissemination to ensure accuracy and institutional consistency.
78 (c) Confidential resource advisors may provide resources and information to assisted
79 parties in addition to those provided pursuant to subsection (b) of this section, including:

87 orders, or similar lawful orders issued by a criminal or civil court;

227 Sec. 5. Amnesty protections.
228 (a) A student or a witness to an incident of sexual misconduct who reports an incident of
229 sexual misconduct to an institution or to local or federal law enforcement shall not be subject to a
230 disciplinary proceeding or sanction for a violation of the institution's policies governing students
231 or the student code of conduct for voluntary use or possession of illegal drugs or alcohol or for
11

ENGROSSED ORIGINAL
232 consensual sexual activity that occurred immediately preceding, during, or immediately
233 following the incident of sexual misconduct unless the institution determines that the report was
234 not made in good faith or that the violation put at immediate risk the health and safety of other
235 individuals.
236 (b) Notwithstanding an institution’s policies governing students or a student code of
237 conduct, an institution shall not take disciplinary action against any student for consensual sexual
238 activity related to an incident or allegation of sexual misconduct or for other consensual sexual
239 activity revealed during an investigation of a report of sexual misconduct.
240 (c) This section shall not apply to an institution if the application would be inconsistent
241 with a sincerely held religious belief of the institution.
242 Sec. 6. Sexual misconduct awareness and prevention trainings.
243 (a) An institution that provides undergraduate education shall make reasonable efforts to
244 require every undergraduate student to complete two sexual misconduct primary prevention and
245 awareness trainings that are trauma-informed and gender-inclusive, one of which shall occur
246 before the beginning of the 2nd year of a student’s undergraduate education, and the 2nd before
247 the beginning of the 4th year of a student’s undergraduate education; provided that an institution
248 will be deemed to have complied with this section if a student enrolled in a 2-year degree
249 program receives one training before the beginning of the student’s 2nd year. The training shall
250 include:

251 (1) An explanation of consent in reference to sexual activity and sexual
252 relationships;
253 (2) Examples of physical and mental incapacitation, such as when an individual is
254 asleep, unresponsive, unconscious, vomiting, unable to walk unassisted or dress or undress
255 without assistance, incoherently communicating, or displaying a lack of awareness;
256 (3) The role that drugs and alcohol play in an individual's ability to consent;
257 (4) Information in writing about:
258 (A) The importance of preserving evidence to document sexual
259 misconduct;
260 (B) Procedures to report an incident of sexual misconduct, including
261 confidential and anonymous disclosure, and a statement that the institution’s ability to respond to
262 confidential or anonymous disclosure may be limited; and
263 (C) Information on the institution's procedures for resolving sexual
264 misconduct complaints, including informal resolution options, and the range of sanctions the
265 institution may impose on students and employees found responsible for sexual misconduct;
266 (5) The name and contact information of the institution’s confidential resource
267 advisors and the limitations of the confidentiality of this role;
268 (6) Safe and positive strategies for bystander intervention that may be carried out
269 by an individual to prevent harm or reduce risk of sexual misconduct;

282 Sec. 7. Training for individuals involved in sexual misconduct response processes.
283 (a) An individual who participates in the implementation of an institution's sexual
284 misconduct investigative and disciplinary processes shall receive annual training in handling
285 sexual misconduct complaints and in the operations of the institution's disciplinary process,
286 which shall include:
287 (1) Information about working with and interviewing individuals involved in an

296 (6) Techniques to communicate sensitively and compassionately with individuals

298 (b) An institution shall ensure that its confidential resource advisor, Title IX office

382 Sec. 10. Enforcement.
383 (a) Upon determination, after reasonable notice and opportunity for a hearing, that an
384 institution has materially violated or failed to carry out any provision of this act, the Higher
385 Education Licensure Commission may impose a civil fine upon an institution for each violation
386 not to exceed $59,000, or one percent of an institution’s annual operating budget, whichever is
387 lower. Revenues generated from collecting civil penalty fines shall be directed to the Office of
388 Victims Services and Justice Grants to allocate to sexual misconduct resource providers.
