Rachel Pierre
Ms. Rachel Pierre currently serves as Interim Director of the Department
of Human Services. She previously served as the Administrator of the
Family Services Administration (FSA) within the Department of Human
Services.
While serving as Administrator, she oversaw the District’s continuum of
care of homeless services. Her homeless services portfolio ranged from
homeless prevention services, diversion, street outreach, emergency
shelter, and the end of the spectrum of permanent housing, for families,
single adults, and youth. She also had oversight of juvenile justice
diversion and community services through FSA’s Youth Services
Division, Block Grants, and the newly formed Office of Migrant
Services.
Ms. Pierre has been working on behalf of District residents for nearly 20 years. She’s served as a
child welfare social worker, senior management at Lutheran Social Services, and immediately
prior to her current position, the chief operating officer for Pathways to Housing DC.
Ms. Pierre is originally from Haiti and moved to the District at the age of 13 where she discovered
her passion for serving the people of DC, her adopted home, and the people of Haiti her native
land.
A Ward 4 resident Ms. Pierre earned her Bachelor of Science in Finance from James Madison
University, her Master of Social Work from Howard University, and her Master of Business
Administration from the University of Maryland Smith School of Business.
