______________________________
Councilmember Trayon White, Sr.
A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
_____________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
_____________
1 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Green Building
2 Act of 2006 to exempt natatoriums built by the District in Ward 8 from being required to
3 maintain net zero energy compliance standards and to require that Ward 8 natatoriums be
4 built to at least LEED Silver standards.
5
6 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
7 resolution may be cited as the “Sensible Pool Building Emergency Declaration Resolution of
8 2025”.
9 Sec. 2. (a) There exists an immediate need to amend the Green Building Act of 2006 to
10 exempt indoor pools built in Ward 8 from the requirement to build to net zero energy standards,
11 the adherence to which will mean that residents in Ward 8 will not have an indoor pool built with
12 the modernization of the Congress Heights Recreation Center.
13 (b) The Congress Heights Recreation Center in Ward 8 is in the process of being
14 modernized.
1

15 (c) For years, community members have been firm in meetings with the Department of
16 Parks and Recreation (DPR) and the Department of General Services (DGS) that the modernized
17 Congress Heights Recreation Center must include an indoor pool.
18 (d) In 2023 Council passed the Greener Government Buildings Amendment Act which
19 amended the Green Building Act of 2006 to require buildings built with District funds be built to
20 net zero standards, meaning that the facility creates as much energy as is needed for operations
21 or more.
22 (e) The law became effective while DGS was in the last stages of design for the Congress
23 Heights Recreation Center, and DGS had created a design working with the community that
24 included an indoor pool.
25 (f) For projects that do not meet the Net Zero standard, design plans must be submitted to
26 the Green Building Advisory Committee (GBAC) for their review and recommendation to
27 determine if an exemption should be granted by the Department of Energy and Environment
28 (DOEE).
29 (g) DGS sought a recommendation for an exemption to the net zero requirements from
30 GBAC and was denied. This recommendation was adopted by DOEE.
31 (h) DGS redesigned the Recreation Center without a pool however, when the community
32 learned of the new design, they insisted on a pool which will require a revised design and
33 resubmission to GBAC.
34 (i) There are two net zero pools in the country. The indoor pool in Los Angeles has the
35 benefit of temperate weather and a large campus. The other pool is in the Plainfield Park
2

36 District, Illinois, a suburban/rural community with more land for solar and geothermal wells for
37 heating the water and running the high energy components of an indoor pool and aquatic facility.
38 (j) There is no net zero pool built in the country facing the space constraints of a dense
39 urban setting and with the need to heat the pool due our zone 7 temperature parameters.
40 (k) The site for the Congress Heights Recreation Center is very challenging because a
41 large portion of it is covered by heritage trees which cannot be disturbed to install the geothermal
42 wells underneath them to support heating the building in accordance with net zero standards.
43 (l) While the GBAC required the design plans to include the installation of geothermal
44 wells, the available land at the site not covered by heritage trees is insufficient space for the
45 geothermal wells required to heat the indoor pool and support the energy demands of an aquatic
46 center according to net zero standards, particularly since a portion of the available land will be
47 used to support the net zero modernization efforts at the adjacent Martin Luther King Elementary
48 School.
49 (m) DGS asserts that the installation costs of the geothermal wells are beyond the budget
50 provided for this project and the agency cannot provide a pool to satisfy the requirements of the
51 GBAC without further delays to this project.
52 (n) For years, the Congress Heights community has worked with DPR and DGS to design
53 a modernized recreation center with an indoor pool, and any further delay will cause significant
54 disruption to the government’s ability to deliver a modernized Congress Heights Recreation
55 Center with an indoor pool and negatively impact the health and wellbeing of residents who seek
56 to use the modernized recreation center and pool.
3

57 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
58 enumerated in Section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
59 “Sensible Pool Building Emergency Amendment Act of 2025” be adopted after a single reading.
60 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
