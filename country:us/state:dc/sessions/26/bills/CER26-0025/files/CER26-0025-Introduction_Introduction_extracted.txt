21 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
22
23 ______________
24
25 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
26
27 __________________
28
29 To declare the month of March, 2025 as “Problem Gambling Awareness Month” in the District of
30 Columbia and to recognize the importance of raising awareness of problem gambling as a
31 critical public health issue impacting millions of Americans of all ages, races, and ethnic
32 backgrounds.
33
34 WHEREAS, March is designated as Problem Gambling Awareness Month by the
35 National Council on Problem Gambling;
36 WHEREAS, problem gambling has significant societal and economic costs for
37 individuals, families, businesses, and communities;

38 WHEREAS, the National Council on Problem Gambling estimates that between 12,000
39 and 15,000 District residents are affected by gambling addiction, with limited resources available
40 for those seeking help;
41 WHEREAS, in 2024, the National Problem Gambling Helpline (1-800 GAMBLER)
42 received 5,494 calls, texts, and chats from the District;
43 WHEREAS, individuals with gambling problems face increased risks of suicide, drug or
44 alcohol dependence, and bankruptcy; and
45 WHEREAS, problem gambling is treatable, and effective treatment can significantly
46 reduce harm to both individuals and society as a whole.
47 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
48 resolution may be cited as the “Problem Gambling Awareness Month Recognition Resolution of
49 2025.”
50 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes the importance of addressing
51 problem gambling as a public health issue and pledges to support efforts to prevent, intervene,
52 and treat problem gambling within the District and declares March, 2025 as “Problem Gambling
53 Awareness Month” in the District of Columbia.
54 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
55 the District of Columbia Register.
