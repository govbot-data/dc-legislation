Dear Secretary Howard,
Today, along with Councilmembers Matthew Frumin, Anita Bonds, and Brianne K. Nadeau, I am
introducing the “Project Homecoming Amendment Act of 2025.” This bill marks the creation of
a critical initiative aimed at providing stable housing and comprehensive support for individuals
returning home after incarceration. This legislation establishes a structured, community-based,
reentry housing program that connects returning citizens with family, friends, or vetted
independent homeowners, offering financial assistance, training, and case management services to
support successful reintegration.
Housing insecurity remains one of the greatest barriers to successful reentry. Each year thousands
of DC residents are released from DC Department of Corrections1 and over 2,000 individuals
return to the District from federal custody.2 Too many find themselves without a safe place to live.
Individuals released from incarceration are 10 times more likely to experience homelessness than
the general population.3 Without stable housing, returning citizens face increased risks of
unemployment, instability, and recidivism—challenges that impact not only those individuals but
also their families and communities and the District as a whole.
Jurisdictions across the country are demonstrating that innovative, community-driven housing
models work. Specifically, Impact Justice’s Homecoming Project, piloted in California, has
demonstrated that pairing returning citizens with vetted hosts—while providing financial
assistance and support services—leads to stable housing, increased employment opportunities, and
a dramatically reduced risk of recidivism.4 By integrating these best practices, this bill ensures that
returning citizens in D.C. have access to a supportive, structured housing program tailored to their
needs.
The Project Homecoming Act will:

• Provide direct rental stipends of $1,500 per month to family members, friends, or
individuals willing to house a returning citizen;
• Ensure returning citizens have access to wraparound reentry services, including
counseling, job training, and financial literacy support;
• Require mandatory training for hosts, equipping them with the knowledge and skills
needed to provide a stable, supportive environment;
• Establish structured rental agreements, ensuring clear expectations and dispute resolution
mechanisms; and
• Ensure strong oversight and accountability, with the Commission on Reentry and
Returning Citizen Affairs monitoring implementation and outcomes.
A stable home is the foundation for successful reentry. When returning citizens have safe housing,
they are more likely to secure employment, contribute to their communities, and avoid
reoffending.5 By investing in housing solutions that strengthen families and expand community
support networks, we reduce the strain on emergency shelters, lower incarceration costs, and foster
safer neighborhoods.

19 To establish a program to support citizens who have recently returned from incarceration by
20 facilitating access to affordable housing, economic stability, and community connections,
21 thereby promoting successful reentry and reducing recidivism, to require the Mayor’s
22 Office of Returning Citizen’s Affairs to administer and implement the program, and to
23 require the Commission on Reentry and Returning Citizen’s Affairs to oversee the
24 implementation of the program and ensure compliance with the provisions established
25 under this act.

27 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
28 act may be cited as the “Project Homecoming Amendment Act of 2025”.
29 Sec. 2. The Office on Ex-Offender Affairs and the Commission on Re-Entry and Ex-
30 Offender Affairs Establishment Act of 2006, effective March 8, 2007 (D.C. Official Code § 24-
31 1302), is amended as follows:
32 (a) Section 2 is amended by adding new paragraphs (1), (2), (3), (4), and (5) to read as
33 follows:
34 (1) “Program participant” means a returning citizen participating in the Returning
35 Citizens Family and Community Reunification Support Program.
36 (2) "Program" means the Returning Citizens Family and Community Reunification

37 Support Program established by this act.
38 (3) "Eligible household" means an individual who meets the eligibility requirements
39 established in section 3a(c) of this act.
40 (4) “Eligible program participant” means a returning citizen who meets the eligibility
41 requirements established in section 3a(d) of this act.
42 (5) "Rental Agreement" means a structured, time-limited agreement between an Eligible
43 Household and a Program participant, facilitated through the Program, that sets expectations for
44 housing arrangements, financial assistance, and support services.
45 (b) A new section 3a is added to read as follows:
46 “Sec. 3a. “(a) The Office shall establish a Returning Citizens Family and Community
47 Reunification and Support Program ("Program") to assist eligible households with housing and
48 support returning citizens who are recently returned in finding high quality, supportive living
49 arrangements.
50 “(b)(1) The Program shall:
51 “(A) Support returning citizens who are recently returned in finding high quality, supportive
52 living arrangements;
53 “(B) Provide direct financial assistance to eligible households to subsidize
54 housing and support costs associated with housing returning citizens;
55 “(C) Train eligible households on typical challenges facing returning citizens,
56 available support resources across the District, and program expectations of eligible households;
57 “(D) Connect program participants to wraparound services, including counseling,
58 employment support, and access to mental health services; and
59 “(E) Monitor and evaluate households and individuals participating in the
60 program quarterly for compliance to program requirements and to understand outcomes.

61 (2) In Fiscal Year 2026, the Office may use up to 5% of funds allocated for this
62 Program for administrative costs associated with implementation.
63 “(c) For a household to be eligible, the individual representing the household shall:
64 “(1) Reside in the District of Columbia;
65 “(2) Have a lease or deed with their name on it at the location where they reside;
66 “(3) Successfully complete a program screening and approval process;
67 (4) Participate in mandatory training as described in subsection (e)(3) of this act;
68 “(5) Provide a single, enclosed, non-shared space of at least 70 square feet at the
69 address listed in the lease or deed where they reside to an eligible program participant for at least
70 6 months;
71 “(6) Demonstrate financial need for support to maintain stable living
72 arrangements;
73 “(7) Sign a housing agreement, meeting the requirements of subsection (e) of
74 this section, with an eligible program participant; and
75 (8) Agree to participate in Program evaluations, including periodic check-ins, and
76 service utilization reviews.
77 “(d) To be eligible, a program participant must:
78 (1) Have been incarcerated for at least 365 days consecutively, with a release date
79 within the past 24 months;
80 (2) Remain compliant with applicable parole agreements, maintain contact with
81 parole agent, including confirmation of new address and all other conditions
82 of parole; and
83 (3) Sign a housing agreement, meeting the requirements of subsection (e) of this
84 section, with an eligible household.

85 “(e) The Office shall:
86 “(1) Establish and facilitate housing agreements between Program Participants
87 and Participating Households that:
88 “(A) Establishes clear expectations, rights, and responsibilities for
89 Program Participants and Participating Households;
90 “(B) Outlines mutual expectations regarding housing conditions and
91 financial assistance;
92 “(C) Includes a dispute resolution mechanism to address conflicts between
93 Program Participants and Participating Households;
94 “(D) Establishes a right for the Participating Household to immediately
95 terminate the lease agreement and for the Program Participant to vacate the property if the
96 Program Participant brings a weapon or drug onto the property or commits a physical assault on
97 the property; and
98 “(E) Establishes a right for the Participating Household to provide 30 days
99 notice to terminate the lease agreement and for the Program Participant to vacate the property for
100 any reason;
101 “(2) Administer the program, including ensuring program compliance, supporting
102 participant success, and evaluating outcomes;
103 “(3) Partner with community-based reentry service providers to provide training
104 for Participating Households, which shall be completed prior to approval of an
105 application and shall include:
106 “(A) Understanding reentry challenges, including employment barriers,
107 legal restrictions, and stigmas faced;
108 “(B) Trauma-informed support, including strategies for assisting

109 individuals transitioning from incarceration;
110 “(C) Conflict resolution to foster positive household dynamics;
111 “(D) Housing stability best practices and setting household expectations;
112 and
113 “(E) Available community resources, including mental health and
114 employment services for returning citizens;
115 “(3) Maintain a database of approved eligible households willing to participate in
116 the program and match those households with eligible participants;
117 “(4) Establish an application process for eligible program participants and eligible
118 households and issue a request for applications for this Program no later than January 31,
119 2026, and no later than January 31 annually thereafter;
120 “(5) Allocate funds to Participating Households in the form of monthly stipends
121 $1,500 per month per Program Participant and notify approved applicants no later than October
122 31, 2026;
123 “(6) Partner with community-based organizations and other District agencies,
124 including the Department of Housing and Community Development, the Department of
125 Employment Services, The Department of Human Services, and the Department of Behavioral
126 Health, to provide reentry services and case management to program participants; and
127 “(7) Conduct annual reviews to assess the Program’s impact on recidivism,
128 employment, and housing stability among returning citizens and submit an annual report to the
129 Council detailing Program operations, outcomes, and recommendations for improvement.
130 “(f) The Commission on Reentry and Returning Citizen Affairs, as established by section
131 4a of this act, shall oversee the implementation of this Program and ensure compliance with its
132 provisions.

133 “(1) The Commission shall:
134 “(A) Monitor program outcomes, including housing stability, employment rates,
135 and recidivism reduction among Program Participants;
136 “(B) Provide policy recommendations to the Mayor and the Council regarding
137 improvements to the Program;
138 “(C) Conduct an annual review of financial expenditures, participation rates, and
139 service utilization to assess the Program’s effectiveness;
140 “(D) Hold quarterly public meetings to solicit feedback from Program
141 Participants, Participating Households, and community stakeholders; and
142 “(E) Submit an annual report to the Director and the Council detailing the
143 Program’s impact, challenges, and recommendations for expansion or modification.
144 “(2) MORCA shall provide all necessary data, reports, and administrative support to the
145 Commission to facilitate oversight and ensure transparency.”
