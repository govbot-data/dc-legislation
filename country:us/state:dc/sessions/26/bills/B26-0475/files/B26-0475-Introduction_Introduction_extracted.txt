Dear Secretary Howard,
Today I am introducing the “Park Concessions Amendment Act of 2025.” This bill marks the
creation of a targeted initiative aimed at revitalizing underutilized recreation centers and parks
across the District. This legislation establishes a seasonal grant program, administered by the
Department of Parks and Recreation (“DPR”), to support local food and drink vendors in operating
at recreation sites during peak months. Through this bill, DPR would provide approved vendors a
grant between $8,000 to $12,000 to operate and sell food and beverages between April 1st and
October 31st at recreation centers, parks, and other DPR facilities in all eight wards.
By incentivizing vendor presence, this program would create more vibrant and welcoming public
spaces that attract residents and foster greater community connections. Many recreation centers
and adjacent parks across the District remain underused,1 despite their potential to serve as hubs
for neighborhood and community activity. Barriers such as limited on-site amenities and lack of
consistent foot traffic often discourage community members from making full use of these spaces.2
By activating these areas with food and drink options, the District can promote healthier, more
engaged neighborhoods, while also creating economic opportunities for small vendors and
potentially provide apprenticeship and paid employment opportunities in the hospitality industry,
especially for immigrants, veterans, disconnected youth, people of color, and returning citizens.
Recreational centers and parks function as the heart of community life in residential spaces. They
provide a space where residents can come together, participate in activities, and improve their
overall health and well-being. By investing in local vendors, this legislation would ensure that
District parks and recreation centers are utilized to their full potential, increasing foot traffic while
maintaining the cleanliness our public spaces.
Should you have any questions about this legislation, please contact my Legislative Counsel, Isaiah
Boyd, at iboyd@dccouncil.gov.

18 To amend the Recreation Act of 1994 to direct the Department of Parks and Recreation to issue annual
19 grants to vendors to provide concessions at Department recreation centers on a seasonal basis.
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
22 be cited as the “Park Concessions Amendment Act of 2025”.
23 Sec. 2. Section 3 of the Recreation Act of 1994, effective March 23, 1995 (D.C. Law 10-246;
24 D.C. Official Code § 10-302), is amended by adding a new subsection (g) to read as follows:
25 “(g)(1) Beginning in Fiscal Year 2026, and on an annual basis thereafter, in accordance with the
26 Grant Administration Act of 2013, effective December 24, 2013 (D.C. Law 20-61; D.C. Official Code §
27 1-328.11 et seq.), the Department shall issue at least 8 grants, of value greater than $8,000 but less than
28 $12,000 each, to individual vendors to provide beverages or light fare at Department facilities, including
29 recreation centers and parks, on a seasonal basis. The grantees shall demonstrate previous experience in
30 providing similar food and beverage services.
31 “(2) A vendor receiving a grant under this subsection shall:
32 “(A) Obtain the required permits to operate and sell food and beverages at
33 Department parks and facilities;
1

34 “(B) Operate from April 1st to October 31st during the facility hours set by the
35 Department, for a minimum of 50% of the facility hours set by the Department for that facility;
36 “(C) Accept payment via cash, credit, and debit transactions;
37 “(D) Not sell alcoholic beverages or cannabis products;
38 “(E) Continually ensure that the physical space where vending service is
39 provided and the immediate surroundings are clean and neat;
40 “(F) Handle all trash in a safe and controlled manner to avoid spills and leaking
41 containers; and
42 “(G) Partner with the Department to ensure that their vending services are a true
43 complement to park services and do not hinder park visitors from accessing any Department equipment or
44 amenities.
45 “(3) The Department shall provide to grantees:
46 “(A) Access to a secure, moderate storage space on site;
47 “(B) Access to trash containers used by the Department on site; and
48 “(C) First priority in re-applying for the seasonal grant in subsequent years.
49 “(4) The Department shall:
50 “(A) Select at least one Department recreation center per ward at which the
51 vendors issued grants will operate; and
52 “(B) Work in consultation with ward councilmembers when selecting the
53 Department recreation center in their ward.”.
