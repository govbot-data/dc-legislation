15 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
16 __________
17
18 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
19 ____________________
20
21
22 To honor and celebrate the extraordinary life and legacy of Sister Juanita Mary Tillman White, a
23 beloved community leader, advocate, and pillar of Ward 8, who passed away on Sunday,
24 June 21, 2026.
25
26 WHEREAS, Sister Juanita Mary Tillman White, affectionately known as Mother, Sister,
27 Friend, Bahka, Advocate, Dr. White, and Ward 8 Mayor, was born in Foggy Bottom, Northwest
28 Washington, D.C., and later made her home in Barry Farms;
29
30 WHEREAS, for more than seven decades, Sister Juanita White selflessly dedicated her
31 life to uplifting the most vulnerable residents of the District of Columbia, particularly in Wards 6
32 and 8, guided by her core principle “To love one another,” and her deep faith that “My life has no
33 meaning unless I am serving God”;
34
35 WHEREAS, Sister Juanita White served as a tireless community organizer, hosting
36 parent workshops on health and wellness, organizing neighborhood watch programs, block
37 clean-up campaigns, voter registration and get out the vote drives, and serving as an Advisory
38 Neighborhood Commissioner, block captain, poll worker, and precinct captain;
39
40 WHEREAS, she provided critical support to countless individuals and families by
41 recruiting participants into programs such as Pride, Inc. under Mayor Marion Barry, Covenant
42 House, Sasha Bruce House, Harambee House, Hanna’s House, and the Far Southeast
43 Collaborative, while offering meals, transportation to recovery meetings, empowerment training,
44 sewing classes, and acting as “Grandma/Bahka” to generations of children in her community;
45
1

46 WHEREAS, Sister Juanita White championed civic engagement by organizing support
47 for the March on Washington, the Million-Man March, and many local and national candidates,
48 including multiple campaigns for District leaders, while earning recognition as a Ward 8 super
49 voter;
50
51 WHEREAS, her international travels to Haiti, Senegal, Ghana, Ivory Coast, and Cuba
52 were marked by extraordinary generosity as she consistently gave away clothing, food, and
53 personal belongings to support those in need and build meaningful cross-cultural relationships;
54
55 WHEREAS, throughout her life, Sister Juanita White received numerous honors for her
56 service, including the WHUR-FM 96.3 “Goldie Award” for Best Community Leader (2021), the
57 Ben’s Chili Bowl Award for Best Community Leader and Activist (2023), the Legend and
58 Legacy Award, Outstanding Service to Families Award, and many others, and was recently
59 featured in exhibitions such as “Food for the People” at the Anacostia Museum and “The
60 Prescription Is HOME” at Mason Exhibitions;
61
62 WHEREAS, on May 30, 2026, she was honored at the opening of the Frederick Douglass
63 Food Pantry, continuing her lifelong commitment to service even in her 90th year;
64
65 WHEREAS, Sister Juanita White’s passing on Sunday, June 21, 2026, leaves a profound
66 void in Ward 8 and across the District of Columbia, yet her legacy of compassion, leadership,
67 and unwavering service will continue to inspire future generations;
68
69 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
70 resolution may be cited as the “Sister Juanita Mary Tillman White Remembrance Recognition
71 Resolution of 2026.”
72
73 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia honors and celebrates the remarkable life
74 of Sister Juanita Mary Tillman White, expresses its deepest condolences to her family, friends,
75 and the entire Ward 8 community, and extends profound gratitude for her more than seventy
76 years of transformative service to the District of Columbia.
77
78 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
79 the District of Columbia Register.
