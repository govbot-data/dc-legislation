15 child development facilities must pay assistant and lead teachers beginning in January 2026

17 Childhood Educator Equitable Compensation Task Force and the Early Childhood
18 Educator Pay Equity Fund into the act, and to make conforming amendments.

22 2025”.

161 “Sec. 15c. Early Childhood Educator Equitable Compensation Task Force Establishment.
162 “(a)(1) There is established by the Council an Early Childhood Educator Equitable
163 Compensation Task Force (“Task Force”), which shall have, as of the effective date of the Early
8

164 Childhood Educator Pay Scales Amendment Act of 2025, passed by the Committee of the Whole
165 on October ___, 2025 (Committee print of Bill 26-176) (“ECE Pay Scales Amendment Act”), the
166 same members as the Early Childhood Educator Equitable Compensation Task Force established
167 pursuant to the Early Childhood Educator Equitable Compensation Task Force Act of 2021,
168 effective November 13, 2021 (D.C. Law 24-45, D.C. Official Code § 38-2241 et seq.), until their
169 successors are appointed.
170 “(b)(1) The Task Force shall be composed of the Chairman of the Council, or his or her
171 designee, the State Superintendent of Education, or his or her designee, and 12 District residents,
172 appointed by the Chairman, representing the following entities or groups:
173 “(A) Families whose children are receiving or have received early
174 childhood education or childcare services from early childhood development facilities in the
175 District;
176 “(B) Community-based organizations;
177 “(C) Early childhood education associations, advocacy or research
178 organizations;
179 “(D) Operators of child development facilities who participate in the
180 childcare subsidy programs;
181 “(E) Operators of child development facilities who do not currently
182 participate in the childcare subsidy program;
183 “(F) Operators of home-based child development facilities;
184 “(G) Employees of child development facilities; and
9

185 “(H) An individual with an expertise in economics or policy, who has an
186 understanding of the District’s early childhood development and education sector.
187 “(3) At least two members of the Task Force shall be employees of child
188 development facilities, one of whom shall be a participant in the Early Childhood Educator Pay
189 Equity Program.
190 “(4) The Chairman, or his or her designee, shall serve as the Chairperson of the
191 Task Force.
192 “(c) Following the effective date of the ECE Pay Scales Amendment Act, the Task Force
193 shall convene:
194 (1) At least every 3rd calendar year starting in 2027; or
195 (2) When deemed necessary by the Chairman of the Council.
196 “(d) Within 60 days of the publication of the report pursuant to section 11b(c), the Task
197 Force shall meet and submit a response to the Council that:
198 “(1) Reviews the Department’s report, the recommended salary scales, the Early
199 Childhood Educator Pay Equity Program funding formula, and the state of the early childhood
200 education workforce; and
201 (2) Makes recommendations to improve systemic supports.
202 “(e) Should a vacancy occur on the Task Force after the effective date of the ECE Pay
203 Scales Amendment Act the Chairman, in consultation with the State Superintendent of
204 Education, shall appoint an individual to fill the vacancy whose qualifications and experience are
