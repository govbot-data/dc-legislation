11 To amend the Health Services Planning Program Re-establishment Act of 1996 to require the
12 Department of Health to collect certain data, and to require certain health care facilities
13 to offer financial assistance and payment plans to eligible patients; to amend section 28-
14 3814 of the District of Columbia Official Code to prohibit the reporting of medical debt
15 to a credit reporting agency, and to prohibit wage garnishments and property liens to
16 collect on a medical debt; to add a new section 28-3820 of the District of Columbia
17 Official Code to prohibit health care providers from assisting patients with completing
18 applications for, or promoting, medical lending products to patients under certain
19 conditions, and to prohibit health care providers from requiring credit card authorization
20 before the date that health services are provided or costs are incurred; to make a
21 violation of the restrictions on medical lending products an unfair or deceptive trade
22 practice; to amend Chapter 2 of Title 40 of the District of Columbia Official Code to
23 limit liens for emergency health services provided by a health care facility to the lesser
24 of either the amount the patient owes after insurance claims or 33% of the total amount
25 awarded to the patient; and to amend Chapter 1 of Title 15 of the District of Columbia
26 Official Code to prevent the revival of judgments against a debtor for medical debt.
27
28 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
29 act may be cited as the “Medical Debt Mitigation Amendment Act of 2026”.
