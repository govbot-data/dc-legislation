12 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA

15
16
17 To recognize and celebrate the history, legacy, and significant contributions of the DACOR Bacon
18 House to the District of Columbia.
19
20 WHEREAS, the DACOR Bacon House rests upon land originally belonging to the
21 Anacostan people, who lived in the area between the Potomac and Anacostia Rivers for
22 generations before European settlers arrived on their shores;
23 WHEREAS, the House is built upon land previously owned by David Burnes, a
24 Revolutionary War veteran who negotiated with President George Washington for the sale of his
25 property to the Federal Government, which included the land upon which the White House and
26 much of the National Mall are built;
27 WHEREAS, the property was acquired in 1815 by Tobias Lear V, George Washington’s
28 personal secretary, who was the last person to whom President Washington spoke before passing
29 and who was instrumental in ending the First Barbary War during his service as Consul General
30 to Algiers;
31 WHEREAS, Tench Ringgold, United States Marshal for the District of Columbia and
32 Keeper of the Keys of the Capitol, who personally led President James Madison and several
1

33 members of his Cabinet to safety during the 1814 British burning of the White House, completed
34 building the present-day DACOR Bacon House in 1825, through the use of enslaved people;
35 WHEREAS, Chief Justice John Marshall, a previous Secretary of State, boarded at the
36 House and was joined at times by Justices Joseph Story, Gabriel Duval, Smith Thompson, and
37 Henry Baldwin;
38 WHEREAS, former Maryland Governor Samuel Sprigg, who was instrumental in the
39 completion of the historic Chesapeake and Ohio Canal, purchased the House as a gift for his
40 daughter Sally Sprigg and nephew William Thomas Carroll, Clerk of the Supreme Court of the
41 United States and a member of the prominent Carroll family, which includes signers of the
42 Declaration of Independence, Articles of Confederation, United States Constitution, as well as
43 numerous elected officials and the founder of Georgetown University;
44 WHEREAS, William Thomas Carroll was also known for providing President Abraham
45 Lincoln with the Bible that was used for his first inauguration, which has since become known as
46 the ‘‘Lincoln Bible’’ and has been utilized by other presidents, including President Barack Obama
47 and President Donald Trump, for their oaths of office;
48 WHEREAS, the DACOR Bacon House served as the official residence of Russian Imperial
49 Minister Nikolai Shishkin, the Russian Federation’s top diplomatic representative to the United
50 States, from 1875 to 1878 while he was dispatched to Washington, D.C.;
51 WHEREAS, Chief Justice Melville Fuller and his family resided at the House from 1896
52 to 1910, during which time the House remained a fixture in Washington, D.C., high society and
53 hosted President Grover Cleveland, President Theodore Roosevelt, and Secretary of War William
54 Howard Taft;
2

55 WHEREAS, from 1911 to 1925, Alice Cornelia Copley Thaw owned the House and
56 modernized its inner workings;
57 WHEREAS, Illinois Senator Medill McCormick and his wife, the future Congresswoman
58 Ruth Hanna McCormick, resided in the House during the 1920s when they entertained lawmakers
59 and their spouses;
60 WHEREAS, socialite Virginia Murray Bacon and her husband, 8-term New York
61 Congressman Robert Low Bacon, owned the House from 1925 to 1980, during which period
62 Virginia Murray Bacon’s hosting abilities earned her the reputation of being one of Washington,
63 D.C.’s last grandes dames;
64 WHEREAS, the House was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1973,
65 paying homage to its nearly 150 years of contributions to American history and society;
66 WHEREAS, Virginia Murray Bacon established the Bacon House Foundation, which
67 worked hand in hand with the DACOR Education and Welfare Foundation to establish the
68 DACOR Bacon House Foundation, which was charged with caring for the historic House in
69 perpetuity;
70 WHEREAS, on May 23, 1986, Secretary of State George P. Shultz attended the dedication
71 ceremony that marked the DACOR Bacon House Foundation’s new ownership and charged those
72 present to ‘‘protect and advance the interests and ideals of America’’;
73 WHEREAS, since 1986, the House has served as the headquarters of the Diplomatic and
74 Consular Officers, Retired (DACOR) organization, a premier organization of foreign affairs
75 professionals, whose members include ambassadors, foreign service officers, military and
76 intelligence officials, political appointees from both major parties, Members of Congress and their
3

77 staffs, Peace Corps and International Monetary Fund officials, global business leaders, and other
78 professionals dedicated to promoting American interests and diplomatic relations;
79 WHEREAS, through DACOR and the DACOR Bacon House Foundation, which host
80 regular educational discussions, policy programs, leadership development opportunities, and
81 musical events, the House continues to be a vibrant center of cultural and diplomatic exchange, a
82 museum of American life, and the home of the foreign affairs community; and
83 WHEREAS, 200 years after its construction, the DACOR Bacon House remains a pillar of
84 strength in the President’s neighborhood, pays tribute to the legacy of its prior owners, enslaved
85 workers, and distinguished guests, and serves as a unique prism for understanding the history of
86 both the District of Columbia and the United States of America.
87 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
88 resolution may be cited as the “DACOR Bacon House Bicentennial Recognition Resolution of
89 2025”.
90 Sec. 2. The Council hereby recognizes the unique contributions of the DACOR Bacon
91 House to the people of the District of Columbia on the occasion of its bicentennial.
92 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
93 the District of Columbia Register.
