12 To amend the School Proximity Traffic Calming Act of 2000 to require mandatory bi-annual
13 meeting between Safe Passage and Safe Blocks personnel with the school administrators
14 whose school routes are served by Safe Passage Ambassadors, and to clarify the training
15 requirements for Safe Passage personnel hired by community-based organizations.
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
18 act may be cited as the “Safe Passage Training and School Engagement Amendment Act of
19 2025”.
20 Sec. 2. Section 2b(d) of the School Proximity Traffic Calming Act of 2000, effective
21 March 10, 2023 (D.C. Law 24-285; D.C. Official Code § 38-3103), is amended as follows:
22 (a) Paragraph (1)(D) is amended to read as follows:
23 “(D) Building relationships with school administrators, police personnel,
24 parents, and community residents by increasing their awareness of the Safe Blocks Program
25 whenever possible, including a mandatory bi-annual meeting between Safe Passage and Safe
26 Blocks personnel with the school administrators whose school routes are served by Safe Passage
27 Ambassadors; and”.

28 (b) Paragraph (2) is amended to read as follows:
29 “(2) Receive at least the following trainings, developed in consultation with OSSE
30 and school administrators, to be administered by the DMPSJ:
31 “(A) Quarterly bystander intervention training, including conflict

38 “(D) Annual training on recognizing signs of abuse, neglect, and domestic,

40 “(E) Any other trainings required by the DMPSJ.”.
