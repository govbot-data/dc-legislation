ENROLLED ORIGINAL
AN ACT
____________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
_________________
To amend the Open Meetings Act to clarify the definition of meeting, to provide for a public
body’s ability to be briefed about potential terrorist or public health threats so long as no
official action is taken, to exempt from the act meetings between the Council and the
Mayor provided that no official action is taken at such meetings, and to provide that a
meeting shall be deemed open to the public if the public body takes steps reasonably
calculated to allow the public to view or hear the meeting while the meeting is taking
place, or, if doing so is not technologically feasible, as soon thereafter as reasonably
practicable.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
act may be cited as the “Open Meetings Clarification Amendment Act of 2026”.
Sec. 2. The Open Meetings Act, effective March 31, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-350; D.C.
Official Code § 2-571 et seq.), is amended as follows:
(a) Section 404 (D.C. Official Code § 2-574) is amended as follows:
(1) Paragraph (1) is amended to read as follows:
“(1)(A) “Meeting” means any gathering of a quorum of the members of a public
body, including hearings and roundtables, whether formal or informal, regular, special, or
emergency, at which the members consider, conduct, or advise on public business, including
gathering information, taking testimony, discussing, deliberating, recommending, and voting,
regardless whether held in person, by telephone, electronically, or by other means of
communication.
“(B) The term “meeting” shall not include:
“(i) A chance or social gathering; provided, that it is not held to
avoid the provisions of this paragraph; or
“(ii) A press conference.
“(C) For the purposes of the Council of the District of Columbia
(“Council”), the term “meeting” means a regular or additional legislative meeting, and
committee meetings where votes are taken.”.
(2) Paragraph (3) is amended as follows:

(2) Subsection (b)(8) is amended to read as follows:
“(8) To discuss and take action regarding specific methods and procedures to
protect the public or a public body from existing or potential terrorist activity or other substantial
dangers to public health and safety, or to receive briefings by staff members, legal counsel, law

provided, that disclosure could endanger the public or the public body;”.
(3) Subsection (f) is amended to read as follows:
“(f) Notwithstanding any provision of this act, the Council may adopt its own rules to

“(h) This title shall not apply to a meeting between members of the Council and the
Mayor; provided, that no official action is decided at the meeting.”.

(1) The existing text is designated as subsection (a).

notice requirement of paragraph (2) of this subsection, except” and inserting the word “Except”

(C) Paragraph (4) is amended by striking the phrase “urgent matter,” and
inserting the phrase “urgent matter, and notwithstanding paragraphs (1) and (3) of this
subsection,” in its place.
(3) A new subsection (b) is added to read as follows:
“(b)(1) For the purposes of the Council, the appropriate officer of the Council shall
provide notice pursuant to paragraph (3) of this subsection when a gathering of a quorum of the
Council that is not otherwise required by law or rule to be noticed in advance took place, at
which Councilmembers considered public business, regardless whether the gathering was held in
person, by telephone, or by video conference, and the gathering of Councilmembers was not
noticed in advance.
“(2) The convener of a gathering of Councilmembers that meets the notice
requirement in paragraph (1) of this subsection shall notify the appropriate Council officer within
24 business hours after the gathering and provide the information required by paragraph (3)(B)
of this subsection.
“(3) Notice provided pursuant to paragraph (1) of this subsection shall:
“(A) Be posted by the Council on the website of the Council no later than
24 business hours after receiving notice that a gathering of Councilmembers took place; and
“(B) Include the date and approximate time the gathering of
Councilmembers took place, and the topic of the gathering.”.
(d) Section 407 (D.C. Official Code § 2-577) is amended as follows:
(1) Subsection (a) is amended as follows:
(A) Paragraph (1) is amended to read as follows:
“(1) Reasonable arrangements are made to accommodate the public’s right to
attend the meeting, or steps are taken that are reasonably calculated to allow the public to view or
hear the meeting while the meeting is taking place, or if doing so is not technologically feasible,
as soon thereafter as reasonably practicable;”.
(B) Paragraph (3) is amended by striking the phrase “taken by roll call”
and inserting the word “recorded” in its place.
(2) Subsection (c) is amended by striking the phrase “E-mail exchanges” and
inserting the phrase “E-mail exchanges and text messages” in its place.
(e) Section 409(a) (D.C. Official Code § 2-579(a)) is amended by striking the phrase
“Council meetings” and inserting the phrase “meetings and gatherings of councilmembers” in its
place.
Sec. 3. Fiscal impact statement.
The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement in the committee report as the fiscal
impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975,
approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
