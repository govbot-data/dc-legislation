Prechelle Shannon
Dr. Prechelle Shannon is an accomplished mental health professional with
over 20 years of experience at the intersection of mental health and
criminal justice. As a Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC), Master
Addictions Counselor (MAC), and Certified Clinical Trauma
Professional (CCTP), Dr. Shannon has devoted her career to empowering
system-impacted individuals and communities. She is the founder of
4TRUTH, a trauma-informed consulting firm that partners with
correctional institutions to advance humane, culturally responsive, and
evidence-based practices. Her work focuses on improving conditions of
confinement, promoting gender-responsive housing and classification,
strengthening reentry planning, and embedding trauma-informed care
within institutional and community systems. A passionate advocate for restorative justice, Dr.
Shannon builds coalitions that elevate the voices of survivors and promote healing and
accountability for victims on both sides of the crime dynamic. She also serves as the Chief
Wellness Officer of the Mindflower Group, a virtual therapy organization based in Washington,
D.C., that offers structurally competent, culturally sensitive, and holistic mental health services to
under-resourced communities.
As part of her commitment to equitable mental health and recovery care, Dr. Shannon serves with
the NAADAC Critical Issues in the Black Community (CIBC) initiative, collaborating with
addiction professionals nationwide to address systemic barriers in recovery and behavioral health
services. Her work has focused on advancing culturally competent treatment models and ensuring
communities of color have access to healing pathways that reflect their cultural values and lived
experiences.
A Ward 6 resident, Dr. Shannon earned her Bachelor of Science in Behavioral Science, Master of
Science in Criminal Behavior, and Master of Administration of Justice from Wilmington
University. Additionally, she earned her Doctor of Criminal Justice from Kaiser University.
