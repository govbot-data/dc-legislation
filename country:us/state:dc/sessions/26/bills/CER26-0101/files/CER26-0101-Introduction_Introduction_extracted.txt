18 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
19
20 _________________________
21
22 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
23
24 _________________________
25
26
27 To recognize and honor Gerard Brown for his twenty-five years of dedicated public service to
28 the District of Columbia.
29
30 WHEREAS, Mr. Brown, as a lifelong Washingtonian, has served as a public servant for
31 the District of Columbia improving the community health and quality of life as a code and rodent
32 inspector with the District of Columbia Department of Public Works before transitioning to
33 District of Columbia Health;
34 WHEREAS, as a graduate from Purdue University's Center for Urban and Industrial Pest
35 Management and Technology, Mr. Brown’s commitment to the health and quality of life of the
36 District of Columbia community is a larger reflection of his dedication to the science of public
37 health pest management;
1

38 WHEREAS, Mr. Brown has built strategic partnerships with local and federal
39 organizations, including the Environmental Protection Agency and Beyond Pesticides, to launch
40 Washington, D.C.’s first Rodent and Vector Control Academy; and
41 WHEREAS, Mr. Brown’s service to the District of Columbia community has gained
42 recognition from Purdue University’s Center for Urban and Industrial Pest Management as well
43 as one of the city’s highest honors, the Morris and Gwendolyn Cafritz Award for Distinguished
44 District of Columbia Government Employees.
45 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
46 resolution may be cited as the "Gerard Brown 25 Years of Service Recognition Resolution of
47 2025".
48 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates Gerard Brown
49 for his outstanding service to the District of Columbia and commends Mr. Brown for his
50 dedication to protecting communities, strengthening neighborhoods, and improving the daily
51 lives of residents across the District.
52 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
53 the District of Columbia Register.
