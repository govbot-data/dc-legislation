21 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Volunteers
22 Services Act of 1977 to clarify that the Attorney General for the District of Columbia has
23 the authority to promulgate regulations governing the Attorney General’s use of volunteers.
24
25 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
26 resolution may be cited as the “Volunteer Services Clarification Emergency Declaration
27 Resolution of 2025”.
28 Sec. 2. (a) There exists an immediate need to amend the Volunteers Services Act of 1977
29 to clarify that the Attorney General for the District of Columbia (“Attorney General”) has the
30 authority to promulgate regulations governing the Office of the Attorney General’s use of
31 volunteers.
32 (b) Section 2 of the Volunteers Services Act of 1977, effective June 28, 1977 (D.C. Law
33 2-12; D.C. Official Code § 1-319.01), establishes a policy of the District government to utilize
34 volunteer citizens in as many governmental programs as is practicable to serve the interests of the
35 community.
1

36 (c) Section 4(a) of the Volunteers Services Act of 1977, effective June 28, 1977 (D.C. Law
37 2-12; D.C. Official Code § 1-319.03(a)), requires that regulations governing the use of volunteers
38 by the District government be promulgated before District agencies accept volunteer services.
39 (d) Section 3 of the Volunteers Services Act of 1977, effective June 28, 1977 (D.C. Law
40 2-12; D.C. Official Code § 1-319.02), enacted before the Attorney General became an
41 independently elected office, does not explicitly authorize the Attorney General to promulgate
42 regulations governing the Attorney General’s use of volunteers.
43 (e) Rules governing the use volunteers by the District government were published in the
44 D.C. Register on December 10, 1982 (29 DCR 5405). These rules covered the Corporation
45 Counsel and later, the Office of the Attorney General (“OAG”).
46 (f) Section 108a of the Attorney General for the District of Columbia Clarification and
47 Elected Term Amendment Act of 2010, effective October 22, 2015 (D.C. Law 21-36; D.C. Official
48 Code § 1-301.88a), generally provided the Attorney General with independent personnel authority
49 for OAG. However, it did not amend the Volunteers Services Act to explicitly authorize the
50 Attorney General to promulgate separate, independent regulations governing OAG’s use of
51 volunteers.
52 (g) Amending the Volunteers Services Act to explicitly authorize OAG to promulgate its
53 own rules would align with the Attorney General’s status as an independently elected official and
54 the Attorney General’s independent personnel authority.
55 (h) This amendment is needed immediately so that OAG can continue to use volunteers to
56 fulfill its mission to enforce the District’s laws and protect and defend District residents.
57 Volunteers, including attorneys and interns, contribute important legal, governmental, policy,
58 investigatory, research, mediation, and constituent services to the agency and to the District. For
2

59 example, volunteer work constitutes crucial support to the litigative and other work across the
60 Office of the Attorney General. The emergency nature of this legislation is justified in order to
61 prevent deleterious impacts on operations and delays to the agency’s various programs, divisions,
62 and cases supported by volunteer work.
63 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances
64 enumerated in section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the
65 Volunteer Services Clarification Emergency Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
66 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
