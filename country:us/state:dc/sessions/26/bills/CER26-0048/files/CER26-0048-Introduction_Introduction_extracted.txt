12 To remember, honor, and celebrate the life of Maurice Sykes, a beloved early childhood educator
13 and an iconic figure in the field of early childhood education.
14
15 WHEREAS, Mr. Sykes, a graduate of Mont Pleasant High School in Schenectady, New
16 York, Wilberforce University, and Antioch University graduate school, and a longtime Ward 1
17 resident, began his career as a teacher, a role that ignited his passion for early education; and
18 WHEREAS, Mr. Sykes served as the Director of the Tufts University Educational Day
19 Care Center and as a Lecturer in the Elliot-Pearson Department of Child Study and Human
20 Development; and
21 WHEREAS, throughout his career, Mr. Sykes held significant roles within the District of
22 Columbia Public School System, including Deputy Superintendent for the Center for Systemic
23 Educational Change and Director of Early Childhood Programs, served as an advisor to the U.S.
24 Education Department on policies related to urban school improvement, and ultimately served as
25 Director of the Early Childhood Leadership Institute at the University of the District of Columbia;
26 and
27 WHEREAS, Mr. Sykes was selected as a National Policy Fellow by the Institute for
28 Educational Leadership, and later coordinated its D.C. Education Policy Fellowship program; and
1

29 WHEREAS, Mr. Sykes was profiled as an Early Childhood Champion in a national study
30 by the National Association of State Boards of Education and is a former governing board member
31 of the National Association for the Education of Young Children which recently recognized him
32 for his impact on early childhood education; and
33 WHEREAS, Mr. Sykes was awarded the Dumbarton Arts & Education’s Charles A. Miller
34 Distinguished Service Award, and he is a proud recipient of the Marjorie Holloman Parker
35 Distinguished Educator Award; and
36 WHEREAS, Mr. Sykes’ influential writings, Doing the Right Thing for Children: Eight
37 Qualities of Leadership, and the co-edited volume Child Care Justice: Transforming the System
38 of Care for Young Children, reflect his unwavering commitment to advocating for high-quality
39 early childhood education for all children; and
40 WHEREAS, Mr. Sykes’ contributions to the field were recognized with the Marjorie
41 Holloman Parker Distinguished Educator Award from the University of the District of Columbia,
42 and he was proudly inducted into the D.C. Hall of Fame Society; and
43 WHEREAS, Mr. Sykes played an instrumental, hands-on role in the creation and
44 development of DC Men in ECE—a visionary initiative dedicated to empowering men of color in
45 the early education workforce; and
46 WHEREAS, he fought tirelessly against inequality, championing access to quality
47 programs for every child, regardless of background, and his work inspired countless individuals to
48 become change agents within the early childhood education system and will leave a lasting impact
49 on future generations.
50 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
51 resolution may be cited as the “Maurice Sykes Memorial Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
2

52 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes, honors, and remembers and
53 the life of Mr. Maurice Sykes, his significant contributions to the field of early childhood
54 education, and his impact on the lives of countless children in D.C. and across the country.
55 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
56 the District of Columbia Register.
