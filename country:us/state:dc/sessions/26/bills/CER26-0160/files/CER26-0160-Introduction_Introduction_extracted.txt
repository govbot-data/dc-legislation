20 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA

22 _________________________

25 To honor and celebrate the life, legacy, and extraordinary public service of Mrs. Romaine B.
26 Thomas, a distinguished educator, civic leader, and lifelong Washingtonian whose
27 contributions strengthened the fabric of the District of Columbia, particularly the Ward 5
28 community.
29
30 WHEREAS, Romaine B. Thomas (June 5, 1929 – April 27, 2026) was a proud second-
31 generation Washingtonian born in historic Ivy City, a community whose history, culture, and
32 resilience shaped her life’s work and values;
33 WHEREAS, Mrs. Thomas received her early education in the District of Columbia
34 Public Schools, attending Crummell Elementary School, Brown Junior High School, and
35 Cardozo High School, later earning her Bachelor of Science degree from Miners Teachers
36 College (now the University of the District of Columbia) and her Master of Arts degree from The
37 George Washington University;
1

38 WHEREAS, she further pursued advanced studies at Vanderbilt University and in
39 London, England, where she participated in a pioneering program on open-space education;
40 WHEREAS, Mrs. Thomas began her professional career at Crummell Elementary
41 School—the same school she attended as a child—and went on to serve more than twenty-five
42 years as Principal of John Henry Ketchum Elementary School, where she was selected as one of
43 only fifty-four principals nationwide to receive the National Distinguished Principal Award from
44 the U.S. Secretary of Education;
45 WHEREAS, Mrs. Thomas demonstrated an enduring commitment to community service,
46 senior advocacy, youth empowerment, and historic preservation, focusing on “what really
47 matters in seniors’ lives—community, safety, financial security, aging in place, and supporting
48 independent living”;
49 WHEREAS, her civic leadership was extensive and influential, including service as:
50 • President, Woodridge Civic Association
51 • Second Vice President, DC Federation of Civic Associations
52 • DC Democratic Convention Chairwoman
53 • Delegate, 2012 Democratic National Convention
54 • President, Executive Council of AARP DC
55 • Chairperson, DC Commission on Aging
56 • Member, Friends of Ivy City Task Force
57 • Member, DC Historic Preservation Board
58 • Board Member, DC Historic Preservation Board
59 • Member, Bethesda Baptist Church
60 • Member, Michigan Park Christian Church
2

61 • Life Member, Girl Friends, Inc.
62 • Member, The Paragons Club
63 • Member, Phi Beta Kappa Honor Society;
64 WHEREAS, her service and impact were recognized through numerous honors, including
65 the Romaine B. Thomas Campus Dedication at Rocketship Public Charter School (Ward 5),
66 National Mother of the Year, the AARP Andrus Award, the Thurgood Marshall Phenomenal
67 Community Service Award, the Seabury Community Service Award, and her appointment to the
68 Age-Friendly DC Task Force;
69 WHEREAS, Mrs. Thomas was a devoted wife to the late Councilmember Harry L.
70 Thomas Sr., a loving mother to Debra Truhart (Spillman K. Truhart Jr.) and former
71 Councilmember Harry L. Thomas Jr. (Diane Romo Thomas);
72 WHEREAS, she was the grandmother of eight grandchildren — Anthony Gause,
73 Spillman K. Truhart Jr., Danae L. Truhart, Kendrick Thomas, Stephan K. Truhart (Abigail
74 Truhart), Harry L. Thomas III, Raye L. Thomas, and Kai L. Thomas — and the great-
75 grandmother of six great-grandchildren;
76 WHEREAS, she also leaves a loving extended family including her sister-in-law Joyce
77 Bell, her nieces Joy Lynn Bell and Lynette Bell Kirk, and her nephew Anthony Eldrey Bell,
78 along with a host of additional relatives and loved ones; and
79 WHEREAS, her life’s work embodied excellence, service, dignity, and an unwavering
80 commitment to the people of the District of Columbia.
81 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
82 resolution may be cited as the “Romaine B. Thomas Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
3

83 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia honors the life and legacy of Mrs.
84 Romaine B. Thomas, expresses its profound gratitude for her decades of service to the District,
85 and extends heartfelt condolences to her family, friends, and the Ward 5 community she so
86 deeply loved.
87 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
