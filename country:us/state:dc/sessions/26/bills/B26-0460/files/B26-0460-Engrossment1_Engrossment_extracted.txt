ENGROSSED ORIGINAL
A BILL
26-460
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
________________
1 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995 to authorize the Mayor to
2 extend the juvenile curfew hours in the District when appropriate to protect public safety
3 or public or private property, to authorize the Chief of Police to establish extended
4 juvenile curfew hour zones in specified circumstances, to start the juvenile curfew hours
5 at 11 p.m. every day, and to extend the applicability of the juvenile curfew to 17-year
6 olds; and to amend the Volunteer Services Clarification Temporary Amendment Act of
7 2025 to make conforming changes.
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
10 act may be cited as the “Juvenile Curfew Second Temporary Amendment Act of 2025”.
11 Sec. 2. The Juvenile Curfew Act of 1995, effective September 20, 1995 (D.C. Law 11-48;
12 D.C. Official Code § 2-1541 et seq.), is amended as follows:
13 (a) Section 3 (D.C. Official Code § 2-1542) is amended as follows:
14 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended to read as follows:
15 “(1) “Chief of Police” means the Chief of the Metropolitan Police Department.”.
16 (2) A new paragraph (1A) is added to read as follows:
17 “(1A) “Curfew hours” means:
18 “(A) From 11:00 p.m. on every dayuntil 6:00 a.m. on the following day;

31 “Sec. 3a. Temporary extensions of juvenile curfew hours.
32 “(a) The Mayor may, by issuance of an administrative order pursuant to section 422(12)
33 of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, effective December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 890; D.C.
34 Official Code § 1-204.22(12), extend the juvenile curfew hours in the District, or any part of the
35 District, beyond the hours established in section 3(1A)(A), (B), and (C), if the Mayor determines
36 that such an extension is appropriate to protect public safety or public or private property.
37 “(b) An administrative order issued by the Mayor under subsection (a) of this section
38 shall:
