18 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Act to Regulate
19 Public Conduct on Public Passenger Vehicles to provide that the adjudication of fare evasion
20 citations shall be pursuant to the Department of Consumer, and Regulatory Affairs Civil
21 Infractions Act of 1985; to amend the Omnibus Police Reform Amendment Act of 2000 to allow
22 applicants for sworn officers of the Metropolitan Police Department to count certain credit hours
23 for completion of the initial training program towards the requirement that certain applicants
24 complete 60-hours of post-secondary education at an accredited college or university; to amend
25 Title 23 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify the authority of Metropolitan Police
26 Department warrantless arrest requirements, and to create an exception to the rebuttable
27 presumption for pretrial detention for robbery where the victim does not sustain a physical injury
28 and burglary in the second degree; to amend Chapter 8 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia
29 Official Code to clarify that offenses eligible in the future for automatic sealing are currently
30 eligible for sealing by motion; and to amend the Secure DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2024 to
31 repeal the sunset date of certain provisions regarding pretrial detention.
32
33 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
34 resolution may be cited as the “Peace DC Omnibus Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
35 Sec. 2. (a) Emergency legislation is necessary to implement several provisions of the
36 Peace DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2025. The Peace DC Omnibus Amendment Act
37 advances several key components of Councilmember Pinto’s Peace DC plan by supporting our
38 public safety workforce, reducing recidivism, strengthens neighborhood harmony, and
39 preventing violence.

40 (b) The provisions to amend Chapter 8 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia Official
41 Code to clarify that offenses eligible in the future for automatic sealing are currently eligible for
42 sealing by motion are necessary to implement on an emergency basis because the existing gap is
43 creating confusion and inefficiencies in our court system.
44 (c) The provisions to create an exception to the rebuttable presumption for pretrial
45 detention for robbery where the victim does not sustain a physical injury and burglary in the
46 second degree and to amend the Secure DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2024 to repeal the
47 sunset date of certain provisions regarding pretrial detention are necessary to avoid a gap in the
48 law.
49 (d) The provisions to amend the Act to Regulate Public Conduct on Public Passenger
50 Vehicles to provide that the adjudication of fare evasion citations shall be pursuant to the
51 Department of Consumer, and Regulatory Affairs Civil Infractions Act of 1985 should be
52 implemented immediately to allow for improvements in the adjudication system to begin to be
53 implemented expeditiously.
54 (e) The provisions to amend the Omnibus Police Reform Amendment Act of 2000 to
55 allow applicants for sworn officers of the Metropolitan Police Department (“MPD”) to count
56 certain credit hours for completion of the initial training program towards the requirement that
57 certain applicants complete 60-hours of post-secondary education at an accredited college or
58 university is necessary to implement immediately so that MPD may begin in the fall with a
59 partnership with an accredited institution.
60 (f) The provisions to amend Title 23 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify
61 the authority of Metropolitan Police Department warrantless arrest requirements are necessary to
62 provide technical corrections in existing law.
2

63 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
64 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances, making it necessary that the Secure DC Omnibus
65 Emergency Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
66 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
