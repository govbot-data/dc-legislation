12 To amend Chapter 46 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code to abate the real property
13 taxes on the real property known as Lot 2, Square 1048, Suffix-S, or 1333 M Street, SE, in
14 Ward 6.
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
17 act may be cited as the “1333 M Street, SE Tax Abatement Amendment Act of 2025”.
18 Sec. 2. Chapter 46 of Title 47 of the District of Columbia Official Code is amended as
19 follows:
20 (a) The table of contents is amended by adding a new section designation to read as follows:
21 “§ 47-4682. 1333 M Street, SE development project tax abatement.”.
22 (b) A new section 47-4682 is added to read as follows:
23 “§ 47-4682. 1333 M Street, SE development project tax abatement.
24 “(a) For the purpose of this section, the term:
25 “(1) “Developer” means FRF Land Owner LLC, its successors, affiliates, and
26 assigns.

27 “(2) “Inclusionary Units” means a unit set aside for sale or rental to eligible
28 households as required by the Inclusionary Zoning Program or established by an order approving
29 a Planned Unit Development pursuant to Chapter 3 of Title 11-X of the District of Columbia
30 Municipal Regulations.
31 “(3) “Orders” means Z.C. Order No. 20-06, effective April 23, 2021, as amended
32 and extended pursuant to Z.C. Order No. 20-06A, effective September 8, 2022, Z.C. Order No.
33 20-06B, effective April 7, 2023, and Z.C. Order No. 20-06C, effective April 28, 2023.
34 “(4) “Project” means the Planned Unit Development (“PUD”) approved by the
35 Zoning Commission of the District of Columbia pursuant to the Orders.
36 “(5) “Property” means the real property, including any improvements thereon,
37 located at 1333 M Street, SE, known for tax assessment purposes as Lot 2, Square 1048, Suffix-S,
38 or as the land for such lots may be subdivided into record lots, assessment and taxation lots,
39 condominium lots, air rights lots, or any combination in the future.
40 “(b) Subject to subsections (c) and (d) of this section, for the 15 consecutive tax years
41 beginning on October 1, 2029, the Property shall be allowed an annual real property tax abatement
42 equal to the amount of the real property taxes assessed and imposed by Chapter 8 of this title, in
43 excess of any amount over $150,000 per year.
44 “(c) The Property shall be eligible for the abatement authorized in subsection (b) of this
45 section in each tax year that Mayor determines that the Developer has met the following conditions,
46 consistent, as applicable, with the terms established under the Orders:

47 “(1) To the extent constructed, the Developer has reserved a minimum of 12% of
48 the residential gross floor area of the Project as inclusionary units for households earning equal to
49 or less than 60% of the median family income (“MFI”), including 3 of the 6 two-bedroom
50 inclusionary units in the East Tower for households earning equal to or less than 50% of the MFI.
51 “(2) The Developer has constructed or is in the process of constructing each of the
52 following public space improvements that provide benefits of special value to the neighborhood:
53 “(A) A neighborhood greenway on Water Street, SE, between 12th

55 “(B) The Arrival Plaza, Waterfront Plaza, 14th Street Corridor Plaza,

57 “(C) A reconstructed Anacostia Bicycle Trail on M Street, SE;
58 “(D) A reconstructed Virginia Avenue, SE with bioretention
59 facilities; and
60 “(E) The installation of 52 short-term bicycle spaces in public space
61 on M Street and Virginia Avenue, SE.
62 “(3) The Developer has provided a one-time monetary contribution in the amount
63 of $25,000 to the Department of Parks and Recreation to support the programming for seniors at
64 the Arthur Capper Recreation Center.

66 of $25,000 to the Seafarer’s Yacht Club, located in Washington, DC to support the operations and

68 “(d)(1) By October 31 of each tax year commencing on or after October 1, 2029, the
69 Developer shall provide to the Mayor information with respect to each of the conditions provided
70 in subsection (c) of this section sufficient to enable the Mayor to determine whether each such
71 condition has been satisfied.
72 “(2) By December 31 of the tax year, the Mayor shall determine whether the
73 Property is eligible for the abatement provided in subsection (b) of this section for that tax year
74 and shall so certify to the Office of Tax and Revenue (“OTR”). The Mayor’s certification shall
75 include:
76 “(A) The Developer’s taxpayer identification number;
77 “(B) A description of the eligible Property, by street address and square, lot,
78 parcel, or reservation number, including the number of floors, location, and square footage;
79 “(C) A statement as to whether the Developer has satisfied the conditions
80 enumerated in subsection (c) of this section; and
81 “(D) Any other information that OTR considers necessary or appropriate.”
82 “(3) If at any time the Mayor determines that the Property has become ineligible
83 for the abatement provided pursuant to this section, the Mayor shall notify OTR of the Property’s
84 ineligibility and shall specify the date that the Property became ineligible.

85 “(e) If the Property is eligible for the tax abatement provided pursuant to subsection (b) of
86 this section, OTR shall process the abatement before the first semi-annual billing of the tax year.”.
87 “(f) The real property tax abatement provided pursuant to subsection (b) of this section
