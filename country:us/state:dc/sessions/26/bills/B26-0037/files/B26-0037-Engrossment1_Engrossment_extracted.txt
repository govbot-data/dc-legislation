12 To amend, on a temporary basis, section 205 of the Clemency Board Establishment Act of 2018
13 to authorize the Clemency Board of the District of Columbia to, for applicants seeking a
14 pardon, grant a waiver of the five-year waiting period.
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
17 act may be cited as the “Clemency Board Waiver Authority Temporary Amendment Act of
18 2025”.
19 Sec. 2. Section 205 of the Clemency Board Establishment Act of 2018, effective
20 December 13, 2018 (D.C. Law 22-197; D.C. Official Code § 24-481.05), is amended by adding a
21 new subsection (f) to read as follows:
22 “(f) The Board may, upon written request of the applicant, grant a waiver of the
23 requirements of subsection (c)(1) of this section; provided, that:
24 “(1) The Board shall only grant a waiver under this subsection where the Board is
25 in receipt of notice from the Office of the Pardon Attorney that the Pardon Attorney or the
26 President of the United States has provided the applicant with a waiver of the five-year
27 requirement pursuant to § 9-140.112 of the Department of Justice Manual;

28 “(2) The Board may decline to grant a waiver under this subsection even when the
29 Board is in receipt of the notice described in paragraph (1) of this subsection;
30 “(3) The Board shall not grant a waiver under this subsection when the applicant
31 is on probation, parole, or supervised release;
32 “(4) The Board may request additional information from the applicant to support
33 their request for a waiver of subsection (c)(1) of this section, which may include a request to
34 meet with the applicant to discuss their waiver request;
35 “(5) The Board shall provide the applicant with written notice of the Board’s
36 decision to grant or deny a waiver request pursuant to this subsection; and
37 “(6) Prior to the Board making a determination whether to grant a request for a
38 waiver pursuant to this subsection, the applicant’s application shall be deferred until a
39 determination is made.”
