contract modification. In order to facilitate a response to any questions you may have, please
have your staff contact Marc Scott, Chief Operating Officer, Office of Contracting and
Procurement, at (202) 724-8759.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.

(C) The goods or services to be provided, the methods of delivering goods or services, and any
significant program changes reflected in the proposed contract:
The Government of the District of Columbia, Office of Contracting and Procurement, on behalf of the
Department of Human Services, is seeking to exercise the remaining option in year three for
Transitional Housing Corporation dba Housing Up, to continue to provide Permanent Supportive
Housing III (PSH3) program case management services, utility assistance, and financial assistance to
chronically homeless and other highly vulnerable individuals and families experiencing homelessness.
The purpose of this program is to assist residents with histories of chronic homelessness in
transitioning from the street or temporary living situation into stable and permanent housing,
maintaining long-term housing, and achieving self-sufficiency. The success of the aforementioned
initiative will be achieved by referring and connecting participants to supportive services that will
address their barriers to obtaining and maintaining permanent housing and achieving an optimum level
of self-sufficiency.
(D) The selection process, including the number of offerors, the evaluation criteria, and the
evaluation results, including price, technical or quality, and past performance components:
The solicitation was issued in the Washington Times on April 8, 2022. An electronic RFQ
DOC575024 was issued in the open on April 11, 2022, with a closing date of May 17, 2022. The
District received a total of 28 submissions. Of the 28 responses, 23 were deemed qualified and the
vendors were notified on June 23, 2022.
The responses were evaluated in accordance with the qualification criteria outlined in the solicitation.
The qualification evaluation factors were PSH3 design, case management, organizational capacity, and
past performance. Transitional Housing Corporation dba Housing Up met the qualifications indicated
in the RFQ and has a satisfactory rating providing similar services through current and prior HCAs
with the District.
The contracting officer awarded an HCA after determining that Transitional Housing Corporation dba
Housing Up, was qualified and responsible, and the proposed price was determined to be fair and
reasonable. A definitive HCA was awarded to Transitional Housing Corporation dba Housing Up
