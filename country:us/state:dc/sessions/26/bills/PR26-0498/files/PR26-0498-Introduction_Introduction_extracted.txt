14 To declare the existence of an emergency, due to congressional review, with respect to the need
15 to amend section 1507 of the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council for the District of
16 Columbia Establishment Act of 2001 to change the date by which the Prearrest Diversion
17 Task Force shall issue initial recommendations for prearrest diversion of certain
18 misdemeanor offenses and categories of persons to July 2026; and to amend Title 20 of
19 the District of Columbia Official Code to allow for notice of request for formal probate,
20 notice from a foreign personal representative of a decedent who owned any property
21 located in the District of Columbia, and notice of appointment to interested persons,
22 creditors and unknown heirs in a legal periodical or newspaper of general circulation in
23 the District.
24
25 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
26 resolution may be cited as the “Prearrest Diversion Task Force Recommendations Congressional
27 Review Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026”.
28 Sec. 2. (a) Section 1507 of the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council for the District of
29 Columbia Establishment Act of 2001, effective June 8, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-175; D.C. Official
30 Code § 22-4237) (“Secure DC”), requires the Criminal Justice Coordinating Council (“CJCC”)
31 to establish a Prearrest Diversion Task Force (“Task Force”) to review best practices and make
32 recommendations to implement prearrest diversion of certain misdemeanor offenses and for
33 certain categories of persons identified by the Task Force as being appropriate for diversion. The
34 Task Force was initially required to begin meeting within 3 months after the applicability date of
35 Secure DC and issue its initial recommendations within one year after the applicability date of

36 Secure DC. However, due to CJCC staffing constraints, the Task Force was not able to begin
37 meeting until June 2025.
38 (b) On December 17, 2024, the Council passed on final reading the Strengthening
39 Probate Administration Amendment Act of 2024, effective March 21, 2025 (D.C. Law 25-302;
40 72 DCR 780) (“Strengthening Probate Act”), which proposed to modernize and update the legal
41 and administrative processes and procedures around probate for District residents and estate
42 lawyers, consistent with the recommendations of the Working Group’s 2022 report.
43 Subsequently, the Council adopted emergency legislation on July 14, 2025, to make technical
44 amendments as some of the language had proven unadministrable and unclear, leading to delays
45 in appointing personal representatives and precluding access to decedents’ estate assets. Probate
46 proceedings require a publication of a notice. Currently the law says that various notices must be
47 published in a legal periodical, which can limit access to residents and has also interfered with
48 the law’s implementation.
49 (c) Emergency legislation is necessary to update the deadlines in Secure DC to accurately
50 reflect feasible timelines for the Task Force and to amend our probate laws to expand
51 permissible publications to include newspapers in order to ensure access to publications and
52 implementability of the original law.
53 (d) This congressional review emergency legislation is necessary to prevent a gap in the
54 law between the expiration of the emergency act and the effective date of the temporary act.
55 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
56 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Prearrest Diversion
57 Task Force Recommendations Congressional Review Emergency Amendment Act of 2026 be
58 adopted after a single reading.

59 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
