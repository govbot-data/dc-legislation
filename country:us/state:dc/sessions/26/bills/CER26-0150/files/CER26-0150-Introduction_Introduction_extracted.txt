27 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
28 __________
29
30 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
31 __________
32
33
34 To recognize and honor the Freedom Riders, civil rights activists who risked their lives to travel
35 to Southern states in protest of racial segregation.
36
37 WHEREAS, the Congress of Racial Equality (CORE) was founded in 1942 by James Farmer to
38 combat racial injustice and end discriminatory laws throughout the United States;
39
40 WHEREAS, the mission of CORE was to bring equality for all, regardless of race, sex, age,
41 disability, sexual orientation, or religious or ethnic background;
42
43 WHEREAS, Supreme Court rulings in Morgan v. Virginia in 1946, Gayle v. Browder in 1956,
44 and Boynton v. Virginia in 1960 overturned state laws permitting segregation on interstate buses and in
45 bus terminals;
46
47 WHEREAS, these rulings proceeded to be openly and brazenly violated, with opponents
48 coordinating political strategies of massive resistance, enforcing Jim Crow laws, and attacking and
49 murdering those who resisted;

50
51 WHEREAS, from April 9th to April 23rd, 1947, sixteen members of CORE participated in the
52 Journey of Reconciliation, embarking on buses from Washington, D.C. and traveling through Virginia,
53 North Carolina, Tennessee, and Kentucky in open defiance of state laws requiring passengers to sit in
54 designated areas according to their race;
55
56 WHEREAS, Black and white participants who refused to move faced assaults and arrests, with
57 three participants in the Journey of Reconciliation ultimately being sentenced to work in segregated chain
58 gangs;
59
60 WHEREAS, in response to continued defiance of Supreme Court rulings, CORE organized the
61 Freedom Rides in 1961, with the first bus departing from Washington D.C. on May 4th and additional
62 trips planned through the end of the year;
63
64 WHEREAS, the Freedom Riders faced violent mob attacks, often supported by the police,
65 including several attacks in which assailants threw firebombs into the buses in an attempt to burn
66 occupants alive;
67
68 WHEREAS, international press coverage of the Freedom Rides led to criticism and
69 condemnation of the U.S., with countries including France, the Soviet Union, the People’s Republic of
70 China, and Mexico arguing that racism belied American claims of a free and democratic society;
71
72 WHEREAS, organizers rejected the federal administration’s request for a “cooling off period,”
73 instead escalating the campaign with more than sixty rides organized throughout the summer months of
74 1961;
75
76 WHEREAS, in the month of July, 1961, more than 300 Freedom Riders were incarcerated
77 simultaneously;
78
79 WHEREAS, 75% of Freedom Riders ultimately were arrested for refusing to comply with
80 segregation laws;
81
82 WHEREAS, amongst others, DC-area natives Joan Trumpauer Mulholland, Genevieve Hughes
83 Houghton, and Gwendolyn T. Britt participated in the Freedom Rides;
84
85 WHEREAS, amongst others, Howard University students Hank Thomas, John Moody, Kwame
86 Ture, and Dion Diamond participated in the Freedom Rides;
87
88 WHEREAS, the Freedom Singers amplified the messages of the civil rights movement through
89 gospel music and call-and-response spirituals, performing across the country and before a crowd of more
90 than 250,000 at the March on Washington in August 1963;
91
92 WHEREAS, throughout the civil rights movement, CORE and other organizations, including the
93 Southern Christian Leadership Conference, the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee, and the
94 National Association for the Advancement of Colored People, organized acts of civil disobedience in the
95 form of sit-ins, bus boycotts, and public marches and demonstrations;
96
97 WHEREAS, Black Americans have continued to fight for racial equality, opposing state-imposed
98 voting laws, voter suppression efforts, segregation in public schools, educational discrimination, housing
99 discrimination, employment discrimination, and other forms of legal and illegal injustice;
100

101 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this resolution
102 may be cited as the “Freedom Riders Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
103
104 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia hereby recognizes all of the Freedom Riders who
105 fought against racism and segregation, courageously facing violence and arrests, and risking their lives to
106 ensure a better, more equitable future.
107
108 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
109 District of Columbia Register.
