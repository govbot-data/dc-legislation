TO: Nyasha Howard, Secretary of the Council
FROM: Councilmember Brianne Nadeau
Chairperson, Committee on Public Works & Operations
DATE: March 13, 2025
SUBJECT: Report on Committee Action: Unauthorized Private For-Hire Vehicle
Investigation Reauthorization Resolution of 2025
Pursuant to Council Rule 601(b)1, the Committee on Public Works & Operations has
adopted the “Unauthorized Private For-Hire Vehicle Investigation Reauthorization
Resolution of 2025” authorizing the use of subpoenas in furtherance of an investigation
conducted by the Committee on Public Works & Operations.
On January 28, 2025, the Committee on Public Works & Operations met to consider
the “Unauthorized Private For-Hire Vehicle Investigation Reauthorization Resolution of
2025” The meeting was called to order at 10:05 a.m. After ascertaining a quorum consisting
of Councilmembers Robert C. White, Sr. and Janeese Lewis George, Chairperson Nadeau
gave a brief description the resolution.
Chairperson Nadeau then moved for approval of the resolution; the Committee voted
3-0 to approve the resolution, with the members voting as follows:
YES: Nadeau, R. White, Lewis George
NO:
PRESENT:
The meeting adjourned at 10:12 a.m.
To ensure a complete legislative record, the “Unauthorized Private For-Hire Vehicle
Investigation Authorization Resolution of 2024”, adopted by the Committee on September
25, 2024, is attached in addition to the resolution approved at the Committee’s January 28,
2025 meeting.

Councilmember Brianne K. Nadeau
A RESOLUTION
_________________________
IN THE COMMITTEE ON PUBLIC WORKS AND OPERATIONS
_________________________
1 To authorize an investigation by the Committee on Public Works and Operations into the
2 unauthorized private for-hire-vehicle operations of Yazam, Inc., d/b/a Empower, in the
3 District of Columbia.
4
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COMMITTEE ON PUBLIC WORKS AND OPERATIONS,
6 That this resolution may be cited as the “Unauthorized Private For-Hire Vehicle Investigation
7 Reauthorization Resolution of 2025”.
8 Sec. 2. Findings.
9 (a) The Committee on Public Works & Operations finds that:
10 (1) The unauthorized operation of Yazam, Inc., d/b/a Empower, a company that
11 operates a mobile device application (“app”) offering private for-hire vehicle services in the
12 District, constitutes a potentially imminent threat to the safety and security of residents and
13 visitors in the District, and warrants an investigation led by the Chairperson of the Committee on
14 Public Works and Operations (“the Committee”).
15 (2) Empower is not authorized to operate in the District, nor are the drivers who
16 use their app. Empower has not registered as a private vehicle-for-hire company with the
17 Department of For-Hire Vehicles and is not in compliance – or has not shown proof of
18 compliance – with numerous laws and regulations to which private vehicle-for-hire companies
1

19 are subject, including those covering consumer protection, discrimination, safety inspections,
20 data disclosure, background checks, and insurance coverage.
21 (3) The Department of For-Hire Vehicles issued two cease-and-desist orders and a
22 compliance order requiring Empower to remedy its violation of the law and agency regulations
23 before April 4, 2024. The company has yet to comply with any order or pay the fines issued for
24 ongoing violations.
25 (4) The Committee has received reports of health and safety risks posed by
26 operators using the Empower app, including reports of stalking, harassment, and impaired
27 driving. Because of Empower’s unauthorized status, there is currently no way for the Committee
28 or the public to understand the frequency of these problems, how many instances are reported,
29 and whether drivers who engaged in these activities are still permitted to use the platform.
30 (5) Additionally, Empower’s failure to comply with laws pertaining to insurance
31 requirements for private for-hire-vehicle companies may expose riders to considerable financial
32 risk in case of injury.
33 (b) Pursuant to Rule 601(b) of the Rules of Organization and Procedure for the Council
34 of the District of Columbia, Council Period 26, the Committee authorizes the continuation of the
35 investigation begun in Council Period 25, in accordance with subsection (c) of this section.
36 (c) The scope of the investigation authorized by subsection (b) of this section shall be:
37 (1) To investigate the extent of violations of District laws and regulations
38 governing private for-hire vehicles due to Empower’s continued unauthorized operations and the
39 resulting lack of oversight;
40 (2) To ascertain the total amount owed to the District by Empower resulting from
41 unpaid taxes, civil penalties, and other fees;
2

42 (3) To determine the impact of Empower’s continued operations on riders,
43 operators, and the for-hire vehicle industry in the District as a whole;
44 (4) To consider whether updates to District laws are needed to ensure the
45 protection of riders; and
46 (5) To assess effective enforcement mechanisms to prevent future unauthorized
47 entry into the private for-hire-vehicle market.
48 (d) Pursuant to Council Rule 601, the Committee authorizes the use of subpoenas in
49 furtherance of the investigation authorized by subsection (c) of this section to compel the
50 attendance of witnesses, to obtain testimony, or to produce documents or other information or
51 tangible items.
52 (e) In conducting the investigation, the Committee may take testimony of witnesses by
53 oral, written, or videotaped depositions.
54 Sec. 3. Effective date.
55 This resolution shall take effect immediately.
