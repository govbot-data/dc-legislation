4 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
5 ______________
6
7 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
8 _________________
9
10
11 To recognize and acknowledge the work and accomplishments of Chris Wheatley Figueras on
12 the occasion of her retirement as Legislative Director of the Trial Lawyers Association of
13 Metropolitan Washington, DC.
14
15 WHEREAS, Chris Wheatley Figueras is a member of the bar in both the District of
16 Columbia and the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.
17
18 WHEREAS, Ms. Figueras earned her Juris Doctor degree from Suffolk University School
19 of Law and holds a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration from Mary Washington
20 College (now the University of Mary Washington);
21
22 WHEREAS, Ms. Figueras began her career serving in several legal support and public
23 service roles with the United States Department of Justice and the United States Attorney’s Office
24 in Washington, DC. At the Department of Justice, she assisted in researching and drafting appellate
25 briefs filed on behalf of the United States before the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals and supported
26 Supreme Court certiorari opposition practice. While serving on special detail with the United
27 States Attorney’s Office, she helped prepare domestic violence cases for trial and worked closely
28 with victims, witnesses, law enforcement officers, and prosecutors;
29
30 WHEREAS, prior to her legislative work, Ms. Figueras practiced as a trial attorney with
31 the Law Offices of Cohen & Cohen, P.C., representing plaintiffs in personal injury matters. She
32 regularly appeared in court proceedings, mediations, hearings, and conferences, and also assisted
33 with legal training initiatives;
34
35 WHEREAS, throughout her career, and particularly during her tenure with the Trial
36 Lawyers Association, Ms. Figueras has led legislative and policy efforts on issues impacting
37 consumer rights, victims’ rights, court procedure, public safety, and access to justice. Her work
38 has included lobbying elected officials and government agencies, building coalitions with
39 community organizations and advocacy groups, and organizing attorneys and experts to advance
40 meaningful legislative reform. Ms. Figueras also has been a frequent speaker at seminars and
41 conferences on topics related to civil justice, legislative advocacy, and emerging legal issues
42 affecting injured individuals and their families;
43
44 WHEREAS, in addition to her professional work, Ms. Figueras is deeply committed to
45 community service and faith-based outreach. Through her church parish, she has participated in
46 numerous service trips supporting communities in need around the world and has devoted

47 significant time to youth mentoring. She also has been a longtime volunteer with Giving Back –
48 Linda’s Legacy, helping provide warm clothing, winter supplies, and other essential items to
49 homeless men and women; and
50
51 WHEREAS, after 24 years of service as Legislative Director for the Trial Lawyers
52 Association of Metropolitan Washington, DC, Ms. Figueras is retiring from her role with the
53 organization, where she has been a leading voice for protecting access to justice and advancing the
54 rights of injured individuals and consumers in the District of Columbia.
55
56 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
57 resolution may be cited as the “Chris Wheatley Figueras Ceremonial Recognition Resolution of
58 2026”.
59 Sec. 2. The Council recognizes and applauds the diligent efforts of Chris Wheatley
60 Figueras in the areas of public policy, litigation, and protecting the civil justice system and the
61 right to trial by jury through her work with the Trial Lawyers Association of Metropolitan
62 Washington, DC, and wishes her the best in her future professional endeavors.
63 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
