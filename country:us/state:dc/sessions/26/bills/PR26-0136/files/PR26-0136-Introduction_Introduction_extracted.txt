Catherine Daily
Ms. Catherine Daily is a nurse midwife who lives in Washington DC.
She practices primarily at a federally qualified health center, Mary’s
Center, and as a per diem hospitalist midwife in Virginia. Her role at
Mary’s Center includes primary women’s health and providing prenatal,
postpartum, and family planning services to a diverse, majority Spanish-
speaking population. She is the clinical lead for the Healthy Start home
visiting program where she trains staff and reviews cases. She is adjunct
faculty at Georgetown in the WHNP/CNM program, teaching courses in
complex pregnancies and intrapartum care as well as on campus skills
trainings.
Ms. Daily spent several years working in international health policy before joining AmeriCorps
and shifting gears to direct health and social services. She provided doula birth services and trained
community birth doulas and student nurses in doula skills at Johns Hopkins University. She joined
the George Washington University Medical Faculty Associates for a yearlong midwifery
fellowship, then joined the team at Mary’s Center. She has spent her entire professional life
learning and working in the DC metro area.
Ms. Daily completed a nurse leadership fellowship training program with Duke University to
prepare to implement innovative projects in clinical care settings. She served on the board of the
DC Chapter of the American College of Nurse Midwives.
Ms. Daily is pursuing her Doctorate in Nursing Practice at Georgetown University to expand her
role teaching the next generation of midwives and nurse practitioners, incorporating the model of
social change and concepts of health equity into teaching.
A Ward 1 resident, Ms. Daily earned her Bachelor of Arts in Spanish International Studies from
American University, her Bachelor of Science in Nursing from Georgetown University, and her
Master of Science in Nurse Midwifery from Frontier University.
