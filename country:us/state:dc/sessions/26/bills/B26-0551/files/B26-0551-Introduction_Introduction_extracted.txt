Dear Secretary Smith,
I am introducing the “Union Wesley Way Designation Act of 2025”. Please see enclosed signed
copy of the legislation.
This legislation will symbolically designate the alleyway behind Union Wesley African
Methodist Episcopal (AME) Zion Church that runs parallel to 1860 Michigan Avenue, N.E., in
Ward 5, as Union Wesley Way. I am proud to introduce this legislation with the support of Pastor
Dr. Brian S. Relford, the Board of Trustees, and the congregation of Union Wesley AME Zion
Church, who received the blessing of the Queens Chapel Civic Association in initiating this
street designation.
The Union Wesley AME Zion Church had its humble beginnings in Georgetown in 1844. Due to
racial tensions and a changing landscape in the District, the Church relocated several times,
before establishing its current home at 1860 Michigan Avenue, NE. Since 1967, Union Wesley
has been a pillar of support to the community, with outreach and programs to support individual
and community needs. As the central meeting center for the Queens Chapel Civic Association,
Union Wesley has hosted District leadership and community leaders, and seen the birth of
numerous programs and projects that contributed significantly to the community, Ward 5, and the
District as a whole.
In recognition of six decades of service, Union Wesley AME Zion Church was nominated by the
Queens Chapel Civic Association and selected by the DC Federation of Civic Associations for its
grassroots award in 2023. In 2024, I had the distinct honor of introducing a ceremonial resolution
with the DC Council, to recognize and celebrate Union Wesley AME Zion Church, on the

occasion of the Church’s 140th anniversary, for its outstanding commitment, dedication, and
service to its members and the community.1
Please feel free to reach out to me or my Legislative Director, Neferteria Brown, with any
questions or for additional information. She can be reached at nbrown@dccouncil.gov or by
phone at (771) 333-9734.
Sincerely,
Zachary Parker
Ward 5 Councilmember
