Statement of Introduction
Community Health Amendment Act of 2025
October 6, 2025
Today, I am pleased to introduce the Community Health Amendment Act of 2025, along
with Chairman Mendelson and Councilmembers Charles Allen, Brooke Pinto, Matthew Frumin,
Zachary Parker, Janeese Lewis George, Anita Bonds, and Robert C. White, Jr. This legislation
would ensure that pharmacists can order and administer, and pharmacy technicians can administer,
without a prescription, the immunizations needed to protect the public health of District of
Columbia residents, visitors, and workers. It would permanently codify the changes approved by
the Council on an emergency and temporary basis through the Community Health Emergency
Amendment Act of 2025 and Community Health Temporary Amendment Act of 2025, both of
which were approved without dissent at the September 17, 2025 Legislative Meeting.
Vaccines are evidence-based, safe, and extremely effective tools to protect the public from
severe illness and death from infectious diseases. Even though certain populations, like seniors,
children, and those who are immunocompromised, are more likely to become severely ill, a basic
tenet of public health science is that vaccines work best when everyone, including healthy
individuals, are vaccinated.
Unfortunately, in recent months, federal actions have created uncertainty and confusion
about the future of evidence-based federal immunization recommendations. This has led to several
large pharmacy chains announcing that they would not offer certain vaccines in some states,
including the District, where local laws tie pharmacists’ ability to independently administer a
vaccine to federal immunization recommendations. Although the emergency and temporary
legislation passed by the Council ensures continued vaccine access in the short term, permanent
legislation is needed for a longer term solution.
The legislation I am introducing today will permanently allow pharmacists and pharmacy
technicians to administer vaccines recommended by a competent medical or public health
organization as designated by DC Health. This could include, for example, the American Academy
of Pediatrics, American Academy of Family Physicians, or American College of Obstetricians and
Gynecologists, all of which have issued their own scientifically valid vaccine recommendations
for this year. It also aligns language in our consent for vaccines for minors law with this new
standard.
Although this emergency does not address insurance coverage of vaccines, the national
trade group for health insurance companies (AHIP) announced last month that they will continue
