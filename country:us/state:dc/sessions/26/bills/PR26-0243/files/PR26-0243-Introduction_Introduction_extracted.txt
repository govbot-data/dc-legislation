Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is a proposed
resolution titled "DC Housing Solutions, Inc. Revenue Bonds Project Emergency Approval Resolution of
2025".
The resolution authorizes the issuance, sale, and delivery of tax-exempt revenue bonds, notes, or other
obligations in aggregate principal amount not to exceed $700,000,000. The bonds will be used for the
financing, refinancing, or reimbursing of costs associated with authorized projects that include the
acquisition and renovation of 19 buildings consisting of approximately 3500 units across seven of the eight
Wards in the District. The project will cover 417 units in Ward 1, 589 units in Ward 2, 260 units in Ward
3, 189 units in Ward 5, 1,096 units in Ward 6, 565 units in Ward 7, and 474 units in Ward 8.
In accordance with section 490 of the Home Rule Act, it has been determined that the bonds, when, as,
and if issued, shall be without recourse to the District. The bonds shall not be general obligations of the
District; shall not be a pledge of or involve the full faith and credit or the taxing power of the District;
shall not constitute a debt of the District; and shall not constitute a lending of public credit for a private
undertaking as prohibited in section 602(a)(2) of the Home Rule Act. The bonds shall not give rise to any
pecuniary liability of the District, and the District shall have no obligation with respect to the purchase of
the bonds.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed measure.
Sincerely,

DC HOUSING SOLUTIONS, INC.
Revenue Bond Project
FACT SHEET
DC Housing Solutions, Inc. (DCHS) has requested that the District issue up to $700,000,000 of
tax-exempt revenue bonds and authorize and provide for the loan of the proceeds of the bonds to
assist DCHS in financing, refinancing, or reimbursing costs associated with affordable housing
projects in the District.
The Applicant
DC Housing Solutions, Inc., is a District-based 501(c)(3) non-profit corporation that provides
safe, quality, and affordable housing to residents with extremely low to moderate incomes.
DCHS focuses on building sustainable communities and partners with local organizations to
create opportunities that help residents thrive. Guided by strategic goals, DCHS works to expand
housing access and support healthy living environments.
Proposed Project
The proceeds of the bonds will be used to finance, refinance, or reimburse costs associated with
the renovation of 19 buildings consisting of approximately 3,500 housing units in seven of the
District’s eight wards: 417 units in Ward 1; 589 units in Ward 2; 260 units in Ward 3; 189 units
in Ward 5; 1,096 units in Ward 6; 565 units in Ward 7; and 474 units in Ward 8.
Financing Plan
A summary of the proposed sources and uses of funds is attached (see Table 1).
Feasibility/Structure/Security of the Bonds
KeyBanc Capital Markets, serving as underwriter to DCHS, has determined the bond transaction
to be financially feasible after reviewing DCHS’s financial statements and status.
The bonds, if issued, will be a general obligation of DCHS and will be without recourse to the
District.
Public Purpose Benefits
DCHS will renovate affordable housing in the District, providing improved housing options for
District residents.

DC Housing Solutions, Inc.
DCHS intends to reposition the current operating subsidies of the properties away from public
housing subsidies to a voucher system through Rental Assistance Demonstration conversions
and/or project-based vouchers.
DCHS will preserve the mixed-income properties that the District of Columbia Housing
Authority previously developed by working with DCHS’s development partners to recapitalize
the properties by re-syndicating the projects with low-income housing tax credits.
Legal and Regulatory Affairs
Orrick, bond counsel to the Revenue Bond Program, has preliminarily determined that DCHS is
a 501(c)(3) organization and that the project constitutes a permissible undertaking within the
meaning of Section 490(a)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act.
Based on the foregoing, the Revenue Bond Program staff has determined that the proposed
project complies with criteria for approval of proposed financing through the District’s Revenue
Bond Program and that the proposed project will enhance affordable housing and contribute to
community betterment.
