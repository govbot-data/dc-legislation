Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia is an emergency
bill, the “Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051 with the Hospital for Sick Children Pediatric Center (HSC)
and Payment Authorization Emergency Act of 2025,” and the accompanying emergency declaration
resolution.
The legislation will approve definitive Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051 with HSC to provide in-
patient care services for medically fragile CFSA youth, issued August 20, 2024, in the amount of
$3,663,625.74.
A letter contract was executed on August 20, 2024, in the not-to-exceed amount of $900,277.20.
Subsequently, Modification M001 was executed on November 15, 2024, to reduce the letter contract
by $7,504.62. Thereafter, Modification M002 was executed on December 4, 2024, to increase the letter
contract value by $62,519.25, resulting in revised letter contract amount not- to-exceed $955,291.83.
On December 10, 2024, a letter contract (DCRL-2025-C-0022) was issued for a period of seventy-
nine (79) days in the amount of $987,804.15. Subsequently, on February 28, 2025, a letter contract
(DCRL-2025-C-0040) was executed for a period of ninety-nine (99) days, in the not-to-exceed amount
of $990,304.92. Furthermore, the legislation will approve payment for the goods and services received
and to be received under this contract in a not-to-exceed amount of $3,663,625.74.
My administration is available to discuss any questions you may have regarding the proposed contract
modification. In order to facilitate a response to any questions you may have, please have your staff
contact Ebony Terrell, Agency Chief Contracting Officer, Child and Family Services Agency (CFSA),
at (202) 724-7415.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed legislation.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

A PROPOSED RESOLUTION
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to approve Contract No.
DCRL-2024-C-0051, and to authorize payment for the goods and services received and
to be received under the contract.
RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
resolution may be cited as the "Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051 with The Hospital for Sick
Children Pediatric Center Approval and Payment Authorization Emergency Declaration
Resolution of 2025".
Sec. 2. (a) There exists an immediate need to approve Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051
and to authorize payment for the goods and services received and to be received under the
contract.
(b) On August 20, 2024, the Child and Family Services Agency ("CFSA"), executed an
emergency Letter Contract with The Hospital for Sick Children Pediatric Center to provide in
patient respite care services for medically fragile CFSA youth in the not-to-exceed amount of
$900,277.20.
(c) Modification No. M00l extended the period of performance through November 16,
2024 and decreased the not-to-exceed amount of the contract by $7,504.62 to a total not-to
exceed amount of $892,772.58.
1

(d) Modification No. M002 extended the period of performance through December 9,
2024 and increased the contract by $69,519.25 to a total not-to-exceed amount of 955,291.83.
(e) On December 9, 2024, Letter Contract DCRL-2025-H-0022 was issued in a total not-
to-exceed amount of $987,804.15.
(f) On February 26, 2025, Letter Contract DCRL-2025-H-0040 was issued in a total not-
to-exceed amount of $990,304.92.
(g) Contract Nos. DCRL-2024-C-0051, DCRL-2025-H-0022, and DCRL-2025-C-0040
were combined into a single definitized contract designated Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051,
which incorporated and superseded the previous letter contracts, extended the period of
performance through August 19, 2025, and increased the not-to-exceed amount by $730,224.84
to a total not-to-exceed amount of $3,663,625.74.
(h) Under section 451(b)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved
December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 803; D.C. Official Code § 1-204.51(b)(1)), Council approval is
necessary since the actions will increase the value of the contract to more than $1 million during
a 12-month period.
(i) Approval is necessary to allow the continuation of these vital services. Without this
approval, The Hospital for Sick Children Pediatric Center cannot be paid for goods and services
provided in excess of $1 million for the period August 20, 2024 through August 19, 2025.
Sec. 3. The Council determines that the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute
emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Contract No. DCRL-2024-C-0051 with
The Hospital for Sick Children Pediatric Center Approval and Payment Authorization
Emergency Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
