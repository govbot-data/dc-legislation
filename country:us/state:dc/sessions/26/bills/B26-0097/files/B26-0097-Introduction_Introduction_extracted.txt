Dear Chairman Mendelson:
The “Universal Public School Nurses for Safer Students Amendment Act of 2025” is enclosed for
consideration and enactment by the Council of the District of Columbia.
The State Board of Education unanimously approved this proposed bill for transmittal to the
District of Columbia Council by a vote of 9-0 on January 15, 2025.
The D.C. State Board of Education believes that every DCPS and public charter school should
have a minimum of 40 hours per week of school nurse coverage during each week school is open
to students for instruction and that the current lack of nurse coverage poses a danger to our DC
students. This legislation amends the D.C. Code to require universal nursing coverage by August
1, 2028. The legislation also establishes a new DC Health program to provide school nurses with
up to $10,000 per year for use on student loan repayment and other eligible educational
expenses.
If you or your staff have any questions about this legislation, please contact me at (202) 741-
0888 or bernice.butler@dc.gov.
On behalf of the State Board of Education, I respectfully request prompt and favorable Council
action on the enclosed legislation.

39 TITLE II. SCHOOL NURSE RECRUITMENT AND RETENTION LOAN
40 REPAYMENT PROGRAM.
41 Sec. 201. Short title.
42 This title may be cited as the “School Nurse Recruitment and Retention Loan Repayment
43 Program Establishment Act of 2025”.
44 Sec. 202. Definitions.
45 For the purposes of this chapter, the term:
46 (1) “Commercial loans” means loans made by banks, credit unions, savings and
47 loan associations, insurance companies, schools, and either financial or credit institutions that are
48 subject to examination and supervision in their capacity as lenders by an agency of the United
49 States or of the State or District in which the lender has its principal place of business.
50 (2) “Director” means Director of the Department of Health or his or her designee.
51 (3) “Reasonable educational expenses” means the costs of education, exclusive of
52 tuition, which are considered to be required by the school’s degree program or an eligible
2

53 program of study, such as fees for room, board, transportation and commuting costs, books,
54 supplies, educational equipment and materials, or clinical travel, which were part of the
55 estimated student budget of the school in which the participant was enrolled.
56 Sec. 203. Establishment of Program.
57 (a) There is hereby established the School Nurse Recruitment and Retention Loan
58 Repayment Program (“Program”) to serve as a recruitment and retention tool for school nurses
59 within District of Columbia public and public charter schools.
60 (b) The Program shall pay for the cost of undergraduate and graduate education necessary
61 to obtain a health professional degree. The Program may pay toward the outstanding principal,
62 interest, and related expense of federal, state, or local government loans and commercial loans
63 obtained by the participant for:
64 (1) School tuition and required fees incurred by the participant; and
65 (2) Reasonable educational expenses.
66 Sec. 204. Eligibility requirements.
67 (a) Individuals eligible for the Program must:
68 (1) Be a registered nurse working at a DC Public School or a public charter
69 school;
70 (2) Be licensed or eligible to practice in the District of Columbia; and
71 (3) Submit a completed application to participate in the Program;
72 (b) Participants in the Program must submit the following to DC Health by July 31 of
73 each year:
74 (1) A form approved by DC Health where they confirm their continued eligibility
75 and intent to remain in the program; and
3

76 (2) Documentation demonstrating that the individual spent funds on eligible uses
77 under Section 203(b) between August 1 to July 31. Examples of eligible uses include:
78 (A) Student loan payments the individual had made over the course of the
79 year;
80 (B) A lump-sum payment to pay down principal owed on student loans
81 after receiving the funds on June 30.
82 Sec. 205. Administration of the Program.
83 (a) The Department of Health shall administer the Program and shall:
84 (1) Establish an application process;
85 (2) Create a form for individuals enrolled in the program to renew enrollment;
86 (3) Conduct regular surveys to ensure participant compliance with the Program;
87 (4) Disburse all awarded funds by June 30 of each year; and
88 (5) Administer any other functions necessary to the Program.
89 (b) Participants shall not be disenrolled from the Program unless they do not meet the
90 eligibility requirements of Section 204.
91 (c) By August 15 of each year, DC Health shall notify each approved Program participant
92 of the amount of funds they will receive on June 30 of the next calendar year.
93 (d) In determining disbursement amounts DC Health shall:
94 (1) Avoid reducing awards paid in the prior year, unless available funds for the
95 Program have been reduced in the annual appropriations process.
96 (2) Avoid approving new applications for the Program if it would necessitate a
97 reduction in awards to current Program participants.
98 Sec. 206. Release of information.
4

99 (a) Any applicant to the Program shall agree to execute a release to allow the Department
100 access to loan records, credit information, and information from lenders necessary to verify
101 eligibility and to determine loan repayments. The applicant is required to submit all requested
102 loan documentation prior to approval by the Program.
103 (b) It is the responsibility of the participant to negotiate with each lending institution for
104 the terms and conditions of the educational loan repayments. Any penalties associated with early
105 repayment shall be the responsibility of the participant.
106 Sec. 207. Selection criteria.
107 (a) Applicants shall be reviewed and selected for participation in the Program based upon
108 professional qualifications and relevant experience.
109 (b) Preferential consideration, in order of priority, shall be given to:
110 (1) Residents of the District of Columbia;
111 (2) Applicants’ years of service as a school nurse in DC; and
112 (3) Graduates of accredited District of Columbia health profession schools or
113 programs.
114 Sec. 208. Loan repayment.
115 (a) School nurses who are full-time participants in the Program shall be eligible to have
116 100% of their total debt, repaid by the Program over 20 years of service, in an amount not to
117 exceed $10,000 per year, or $200,000 in total.
118 (b) The Director is permitted to increase the dollar amount of the total loan repayment
119 annually to adjust for inflation. All disbursements shall be adjusted accordingly.
120 Sec. 209. Disbursement procedure.
5

121 The Department of Health shall disburse loan repayment funds, in annual lump sum
122 payments by June 30 of each year, to Program participants who served the entire year of school.
123 Sec. 210. Tax implications.
124 For purposes of the District of Columbia Office of Tax and Revenue, all loan repayment
125 awards shall not be considered income and are therefore not taxable, provided that they are used
126 for eligible expenses.
127 Sec. 211. Annual Reporting.
128 By October 1 of each year, DC Health shall provide the Council of the District of
129 Columbia and the State Board of Education a report on the Program that includes the following
130 information:
131 (a) The number of Program participants who received funds by June 30;
132 (b) The amount of funds paid by the Program;
133 (c) The number of applications received for the Program;
134 (d) The number of applications approved by August 15; and
135 (e) The estimated amount of funds required next fiscal year to award all eligible
136 applicants the maximum $10,000 annual amount.
137 Sec. 212. Rulemaking.
138 The Mayor is authorized to promulgate rules necessary to implement this chapter.
