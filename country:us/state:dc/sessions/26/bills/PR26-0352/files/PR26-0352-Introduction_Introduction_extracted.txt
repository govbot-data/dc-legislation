Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Pursuant to D.C. Official Code § 2-352.02 and 6-227(f)(2), I have enclosed, for consideration by the Council of the
District of Columbia, the (a) Council Contract Summary for the Assignment, Assumption and Amendment of Long
Term Subsidy Contract (“Agreement”) by and among the District of Columbia Housing Authority, Jubilee Housing
Limited Partnership II, and Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP, and (b) proposed Assignment, Assumption and Amendment
of Long Term Contract Subsidy Contract for the Sorrento Apartments by and among the District of Columbia Housing
Authority Jubilee Housing Limited Partnership II, and Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP Approval Resolution of 2025.
The District of Columbia Housing Authority proposes to consent to an assignment, assumption and amendment of
the current Long Term Subsidy Contract between DCHA and Jubilee Housing Limited Partnership II (LTSC Contract
Number 0076-2007-0022L) (the "LTSC"). The LTSC will be assigned by Jubilee Housing Limited Partnership II to
Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP and will be assumed by Jubilee ADMO. Apartments LP. The LTSC will be extended for
an additional twenty (20) years. With the LTSC extension, Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP will obtain long term
financing to conduct moderate rehabilitation on the Sorrento Apartments.
As always, I am available to discuss any questions you may have regarding the proposed Agreement. In order to
facilitate a response to any questions concerning this Agreement, please have your staff contact Hammere Gebreyes,
Director of External Affairs and Senior Advisor, at (202) 207-4694.
I look forward to your favorable consideration of this proposed Agreement.
Sincerely,
Keith Pettigrew
Executive Director, District of Columbia Housing Authority

31 Sec. 2. (a) In 2007, the Council of the District of Columbia ("Council") passed Title II of
32 the Fiscal Year 2007 Budget Support Act of 2006 ("BSA") to provide funding for affordable
33 housing for extremely low-income households in the District of Columbia ("District"). The
34 passage of the BSA created the Local Rent Supplement Program ("LRSP"), a program designed
35 to provide affordable housing and supportive services to extremely low-income District
36 residents, including those who are homeless or in need of supportive services, such as elderly
{0030893 -} 1

1 individuals or those with disabilities, through project-based, tenant-based, and sponsored-based
2 LRSP affordable housing units. The BSA provided for the District of Columbia Housing
3 Authority (“DCHA”) to administer the LRSP on behalf of the District.
4 (b) On February 5, 2007, DCHA issued a “Request for Proposals Number 0076A-
5 2007, Local Rent Supplement Program – Project-Based Program (“2007 RFP”)” seeking
6 proposals from property owners, developers, and service providers for participation in DCHA’s
7 LRSP. In the 2007 RFP, DCHA sought to provide LRSP project-based and sponsor-based
8 funding for units that would combine any LRSP award with public housing subsidy received by
9 DCHA from the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development. The LRSP
10 would be funded with District governmental funds. The LRSP housing units were for extremely
11 low-income families making zero to thirty percent (30%) of the area’s median income, as well as
12 the chronically homeless and individuals with mental or physical disabilities.
13 (c) Under the 2007 RFP, DCHA chose Jubilee Housing Limited Partnership II
14 (“Jubilee Housing”), a non-profit housing provider, to receive sponsor-based LRSP funds to
15 subsidize seventeen (17) units at the Sorrento Apartments, an affordable housing project located at
16 2233 18th Street, NW, Washington, D.C. On February 28, 2011, DCHA and Jubilee Housing
17 entered into a sponsor-based LTSC for the seventeen (17) LRSP units at the Sorrento
18 Apartments.
19 (d) Jubilee Housing desires to obtain long term financing to conduct a
20 rehabilitation of the Sorrento Apartments. Two of the conditions to obtain the long-term
21 financing are to transfer the Sorrento Apartments to Jubilee ADMO Apartments LP (“Jubilee
22 ADMO”) and to extend the term of the LTSC for the Sorrento Apartments by twenty (20) years
23 to February 28, 2046.

1 (e) Under D.C. Code §6-227(f)(2), an existing LTSC may be extended without the
2 need for DCHA to issue another RFP for the LTSC if the owner, when the LTSC was executed,
3 or the owner’s successor-in-interest, will be the owner under the extended LTSC. The current
4 owner, Jubilee Housing, will assign the LTSC to Jubilee ADMO, an affiliate of Jubilee Housing,
5 who will assume the LTSC as the successor-in-interest to Jubilee Housing.
6 (f) After approval by the Council, DCHA will enter into the Assignment,
7 Assumption, and Amendment of Long-Term Subsidy Contract by and among DCHA, Jubilee
8 Housing, and Jubilee ADMO (the “Agreement”) which extends the LTSC for twenty (20) years
9 and assigns the LTSC from Jubilee Housing to Jubilee ADMO.
10 (g) There exists an immediate need to approve the Agreement in order to continue to
11 provide long-term affordable housing units for extremely low-income households for the
12 Sorrento Apartments LRSP units located at 2233 18th Street, NW.
13 (h) The legislation to approve the Agreement will authorize the assignment of the
14 Sorrento LTSC from Jubilee Housing to Jubilee ADMO, the assumption by Jubilee ADMO of
15 the Sorrento Apartments LTSC, and the extension of the LTSC for twenty (20) years.
16 Sec. 3. Pursuant to section 451 of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved
17 December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 803; D.C. Official Code § 1-204.51), and section 202 of the
18 Procurement Practices Reform Act of 2010, effective April 8, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-371; D.C.
19 Official Code § 2-352.02), the Council approves the Agreement by and among Jubilee
20 Housing, Jubilee ADMO and DCHA to assign the Sorrento Apartments LTSC to Jubilee
21 ADMO, to have Jubilee ADMO assume the Sorrento Apartments LTSC, and to extend the
22 Sorrento Apartments LTSC for twenty (20) years to continue to provide an operating subsidy
23 in support of eighteen (18) affordable housing units at Sorrento Apartments.
