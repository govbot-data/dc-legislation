Albert Cacozza
Mr. Albert Cacozza is a retired partner at Ropes & Gray LLP, where he
was an active partner for over 20 years. During over three decades in
private legal practice, he represented Fortune 50 global companies,
entrepreneurial start-ups, national not-for-profit organizations, research
institutions, and academic medical centers. He also has extensive pro
bono and community service experience, including mayoral appointment
to a government-run at-risk youth foundation, working on the board of an
organization combating homelessness and poverty, consulting public
officials on DC statehood issues, and serving as an elected parent
representative on a local public school restructuring team.
Earlier in his career, Mr. Cacozza served as a law clerk to then–Judge
Ruth Bader Ginsburg on the United States Court of the Appeals for the District of Columbia. He
also was committee counsel to the Labor Subcommittee of the Senate Labor and Human Resources
Committee, chaired by Senator Howard Metzenbaum (D-OH). In addition to his government
service, he developed and taught several courses related to legislation and employment law at
George Washington University School of Law.
A Ward 3 resident, Mr. Cacozza earned his Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from Brown
University and his Juris Doctor from the University of Chicago.
