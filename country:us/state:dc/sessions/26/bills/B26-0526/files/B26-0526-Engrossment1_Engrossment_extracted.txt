11 To amend An Act To create a Department of Corrections in the District of Columbia to designate
12 the Department of Corrections (“DOC”) as the District agency to provide a free
13 appropriate public education under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act and
14 District law to certain individuals in DOC custody and detained in its secure facilities; to
15 expand eligibility for special education assessments for individuals in DOC custody; to
16 require that the Department of Corrections establish an online portal and tracking system
17 for grievances and short-term and long-term service requests; to repeal section 3001.16
18 and 3001.17 of Chapter 30 of Subtitle A of Title 5 of the District of Columbia Municipal
19 Regulations; to amend Chapter 3 of Title 14 of the District of Columbia Official Code to
20 clarify that certain provisions only apply to a victim who is under the age of 18; to amend
21 Title 20 of the District of Columbia Official Code to amend the definition of abbreviated
22 probate, to authorize the Register of Wills to refer proceedings to the Court to determine
23 if one or more personal representatives should be appointed and the will be admitted to
24 probate, to strike the reference to will admission in transfers by affidavit, to allow for
25 notice of request for formal probate, notice from a foreign personal representative of a
26 decedent who owned any property located in the District of Columbia, and notice of
27 appointment to interested persons, creditors, and unknown heirs in a legal periodical or
28 newspaper of general circulation in the District; to amend the Strengthening Probate
29 Amendment Act of 2024 to clarify that the act applies to estates for whom the decedent
30 died on or after March 21, 2025; to amend the Volunteers Services Act of 1977 to clarify
31 that the Attorney General for the District of Columbia has the authority to promulgate
32 regulations governing the Attorney General’s use of volunteers; to amend the Criminal
33 Justice Coordinating Council for the District of Columbia Establishment Act of 2001 to
34 clarify certain reporting requirements, and to change the date by which the Prearrest
35 Diversion Task Force shall issue initial recommendations for prearrest diversion of
36 certain misdemeanor offenses and categories of persons to July 2026; and to repeal
37 section 3 of the Safe Passage Training and School Engagement Amendment Act of 2025.

182 Court or the Register of Wills shall appoint one or more personal representatives, except as
183 provided in § 20-322. The appointment of a personal representative shall constitute an Order for
184 unsupervised administration, unless the Order specifically provides for supervised administration
