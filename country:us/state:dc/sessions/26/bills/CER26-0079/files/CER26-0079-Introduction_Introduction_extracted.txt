_____________________________
Councilmember Brianne K. Nadeau
A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
_________________________
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
_________________________
1 To remember, honor, and celebrate the life of Luis Eduardo Rumbaut.
2 WHEREAS, Mr. Luis Eduardo Rumbaut was born in Havana, Cuba, and chose
3 Washington, D.C. as his beloved permanent home in 1972, where he earned a J.D. from Antioch
4 Law School, and;
5 WHEREAS, while actively pursuing his law degree, Mr. Rumbaut began his career at
6 Ayuda, a newly formed organization, with a mission to provide legal, social and language access
7 services to low-income immigrants in the DC region, serving as one of its founding directors
8 until 1980, and;
9 WHEREAS, Mr. Rumbaut worked for two decades in the DC Office of Corporation
10 Counsel, from Senior Counsel to Director, Civil Branch, Enforcement Division, and;
11 WHEREAS, following his retirement from government service, he worked creatively as
12 the Director of the Latin American Folk Institute in DC, assuming a wide range of roles, and;
13 WHEREAS, in 2004, Mr. Rumbaut founded, edited, and wrote the editorial content for a
14 bilingual online newsletter, “La Alborada,” which garnered an international audience,
1

15 distributing five issues weekly for more than a dozen years that included curated political,
16 economic and cultural news from across Latin America, with a focus on Cuba, and;
17 WHEREAS, beginning in his college years, he created and performed in several bands,
18 playing chiefly Latin music with the tres (cubano) and occasionally the cuatro (puertorriqueño),
19 at regional events and locales, but most predominantly in the Adams Morgan and Columbia
20 Heights neighborhoods, and;
21 WHEREAS, decade after decade, he was recognized for his outstanding and selfless
22 service, promoting the welfare of the Spanish-speaking communities of DC, as well as Public
23 Service Awards for excellence in government service and individual superior achievements, and;
24 WHEREAS, Mr. Rumbaut left an indelible imprint and impact upon the Latino
25 communities of Washington, DC, and was a beloved father, brother and friend.
26 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this
27 resolution may be cited as the “Luis Eduardo Rumbaut Recognition Resolution of 2025”.
28 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes, honors, and remembers the life
29 and contributions of Mr. Luis Eduardo Rumbaut.
30 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
31 the District of Columbia Register.
