18 To declare the existence of an emergency, due to congressional review, with respect to the need
19 to amend Title 6-A of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations to make
20 conforming changes; to amend the Omnibus Police Reform Amendment Act of 2000 to
21 allow applicants for sworn officers of the Metropolitan Police Department to count
22 certain credit hours for completion of the initial training program towards the requirement
23 that certain applicants complete 60-hours of post-secondary education at an accredited
24 college or university; to amend section 23-581 of the District of Columbia Official Code
25 to clarify the authority of Metropolitan Police Department warrantless arrest
26 requirements; to amend the Act to Regulate Public Conduct on Public Passenger Vehicles
27 to provide that the adjudication of fare evasion citations shall be pursuant to the
28 Department of Consumer and Regulatory Affairs Civil Infractions Act of 1985; to amend
29 Chapter 8 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia Official Code to clarify that offenses
30 eligible in the future for automatic sealing are currently eligible for sealing by motion;
31 and to amend the Secure DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2024 to amend the sunset date
32 of certain provisions regarding pretrial detention.
33
34 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
35 resolution may be cited as the “Peace DC Omnibus Congressional Review Emergency
36 Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
37 Sec. 2. (a) Emergency legislation is necessary to implement several provisions of the
38 Peace DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2025, enacted on July 21, 2025 (D.C. Act 26-114; 72
39 DCR 8162) (“Peace DC Omnibus”). The Peace DC Omnibus advances several key components

40 of Councilmember Pinto’s Peace DC plan by supporting our public safety workforce, reducing
41 recidivism, strengthening neighborhood harmony, and preventing violence.
42 (b) The provisions to amend Chapter 8 of Title 16 of the District of Columbia Official
43 Code to clarify that offenses eligible in the future for automatic sealing are currently eligible for
44 sealing by motion are necessary to implement on an emergency basis because the existing gap is
45 creating confusion and inefficiencies in our court system.
46 (c) The provisions to amend the Secure DC Omnibus Amendment Act of 2024, effective
47 June 8, 2024 (D.C. Law 25-175; 71 DCR 2732), to amend the sunset date of certain provisions
48 regarding pretrial detention are necessary to avoid a gap in the law.
49 (d) The provisions to amend the Act to Regulate Public Conduct on Public Passenger
50 Vehicles, effective September 23, 1975 (D.C. Law 1-18; D.C. Official Code § 32-251 et seq.), to
51 provide that the adjudication of fare evasion citations shall be pursuant to the Department of
52 Consumer, and Regulatory Affairs Civil Infractions Act of 1985, effective October 5, 1985 (D.C.
53 Law 6-42; D.C. Official Code § 2-1801.01 et seq.), should be implemented immediately to allow
54 for improvements in the adjudication system to begin to be implemented expeditiously.
55 (e) The provisions to amend the Omnibus Police Reform Amendment Act of 2000,
56 effective October 4, 2000 (D.C. Law 13-160; 47 DCR 4619), to allow applicants for sworn
57 officers of the Metropolitan Police Department (“MPD”) to count certain credit hours for
58 completion of the initial training program towards the requirement that certain applicants
59 complete 60-hours of post-secondary education at an accredited college or university are
60 necessary to implement immediately so that MPD may begin in the fall with a partnership with
61 an accredited institution.
2

62 (f) The provisions to amend section 23-581 of the District of Columbia Official Code to
63 clarify the authority of Metropolitan Police Department warrantless arrest requirements are
64 necessary to provide technical corrections in existing law.
65 Sec. 3. The Council of the District of Columbia determines that the circumstances in
66 section 2 constitute emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Peace DC Omnibus
67 Congressional Review Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
68 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
