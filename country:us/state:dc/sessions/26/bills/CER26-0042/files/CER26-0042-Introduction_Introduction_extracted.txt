8 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
9 __________
10
11 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
12 __________
13
14
15 To officially recognize the 50th anniversary of the mainstream breakthrough of the musical
16 ensemble, The Blackbyrds, which formed in the District and helped shape our city's cultural identity.
17
18 WHEREAS, the Blackbyrds, a groundbreaking ensemble formed under the visionary leadership
19 of the late Dr. Donald Byrd – celebrated jazz trumpeter, composer, bandleader, and the professor at
20 Howard University – celebrate the 50th anniversary of their mainstream breakthrough in 2025;
21
22 WHEREAS, the Blackbyrds were conceived as a bold experiment in musical mentorship, uniting
23 some of the most talented students at Howard University to create a sound that was both deeply rooted in
24 the traditions of jazz and boldly contemporary in its embrace of funk, soul, and rhythm and blues;
25
26 WHEREAS, their song “Happy Music” was issued as their first club mix release by Fantasy
27 Records in November 1975;
28
29 WHEREAS, the Blackbyrds hit the big time with their 1975 hit “Walking in Rhythm”, which
30 received a Grammy nomination and sold over one million copies by May 1975;
31
32 WHEREAS, they followed up “Walking in Rhythm” with their 1975 song “Rock Creek Park”,
33 which became an anthem of the District of Columbia and a charted on the U.S. R&B chart;
34
35 WHEREAS, the band’s chart-topping hits such as “Do It, Fluid” captured the energy and essence
36 of urban life while establishing the Blackbyrds as pioneers of the jazz-funk genre, all the while
37 influencing the hip-hop generation, with artists like Tupac Shakur, Gang Starr, and Full Force sampling
38 their music;
39
40 WHEREAS, “Rock Creek Park” alone has been sampled by hip-hop artists such as MF Doom,
41 De La Soul, Eric B. & Rakim, Massive Attack, Heavy D, Nas, Grandmaster Flash & the Furious Five,
42 Tone Lōc, Mac Dre, and Wiz Khalifa, bringing national attention to Washington, DC’s thriving Black arts
43 and music scene, and played a vital role in shaping the city’s cultural identity;
44
45 WHEREAS, the influence of the Blackbyrds transcends decades with their recordings and their
46 contribution to hip-hop, affirming their lasting musical legacy as a pioneering music group;
47
48 WHEREAS, fifty years after the release of their first major hit, the music of the Blackbyrds
49 continues to represent a significant cultural cornerstone of Washington, DC’s rich musical heritage;
50

51 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, that this resolution
52 may be cited as the “The Blackbyrds 50th Anniversary Recognition Resolution of 2025.”
53
54 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia hereby congratulates the living members of The
55 Blackbyrds, Keith Killgo and Joe Hall, as treasured DC natives whose hit “Rock Creek Park” celebrates
56 one of DC’s most cherished public spaces. .
57
58 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in the
59 District of Columbia Register.
