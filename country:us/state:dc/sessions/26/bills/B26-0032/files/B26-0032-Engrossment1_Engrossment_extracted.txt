11 To amend Title 28 of the District of Columbia Official Code to enact the Uniform Special Deposits
12 Act to establish clear criteria for what constitutes a special deposit, to permit parties to opt-
13 in to the protections of the act by agreement, to establish the conditions under which a bank
14 is obligated to pay a special deposit to a beneficiary, to provide that neither the depositor
15 nor the beneficiary have a property interest in a special deposit, to clarify the applicability
16 of the creditor process on a special deposit, to clarify the legality of a bank’s right to
17 exercise a setoff or right to recoupment against a special deposit that its unrelated to the
18 special deposit, to establish a 5-year default term for special deposits, to provide that this
19 act shall be applied and construed in a manner that promotes uniformity among the
20 jurisdictions that adopt it, and to provide for its application to existing and future special
21 deposits.
22
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 act may be cited as the “Uniform Special Deposits Act of 2025”.

122 “§ 28-2952. Scope; choice of law; forum.
123 “(a) This chapter applies to a special deposit under an account agreement that states the
124 intention of the parties to establish a special deposit governed by this chapter, regardless of
125 whether a party to the account agreement or a transaction related to the special deposit, or the
126 special deposit itself, has a reasonable relation to the District.

127 “(b) The parties to an account agreement may choose a forum in the District for settling a
128 dispute arising out of the special deposit, regardless of whether a party to the account agreement

154 “§ 28-2954. Requirements for special deposit.
155 “(a) A deposit is a special deposit if it is:
156 “(1) A deposit of funds in a bank under an account agreement;
157 “(2) For the benefit of at least 2 beneficiaries, one or more of which may be a
158 depositor;
159 “(3) Denominated in a medium of exchange that is currently authorized or
160 adopted by a domestic or foreign government;
161 “(4) For a permissible purpose stated in the account agreement; and
162 “(5) Subject to a contingency.
163 “§ 28-2955. Permissible purpose.
164 “(a) A special deposit must serve at least one permissible purpose stated in the account
165 agreement from the time the special deposit is created in the account agreement until termination

166 of the special deposit.
167 “(b) If, before termination of the special deposit, the bank or a court determines the
168 special deposit no longer satisfies subsection (a) of this section, §§ 28-2957 through 2960 of this
169 chapter shall cease to apply to any funds deposited in the special deposit after the special deposit
170 ceases to satisfy subsection (a) of this section.
171 “(c) If, before termination of a special deposit, the bank determines the special deposit no
172 longer satisfies subsection (a) of this section, the bank may take action it believes is necessary
173 under the circumstances, including terminating the special deposit.
174 “§ 28-2956. Payment to beneficiary by bank.
175 “(a) Unless the account agreement provides otherwise, the bank is obligated to pay a
176 beneficiary if there are sufficient actually and finally collected funds in the balance of the special
177 deposit.
178 “(b) Except as provided in subsection (c) of this section, the obligation to pay the
179 beneficiary is excused if the funds available in the special deposit are insufficient to cover such
180 payment.
181 “(c) Unless the account agreement provides otherwise, if the funds available in the
182 special deposit are insufficient to cover an obligation to pay a beneficiary, a beneficiary may
183 elect to be paid the funds that are available or, if there is more than one beneficiary, a pro rata
184 share of the funds available. Payment to the beneficiary making the election under this
185 subsection discharges the bank’s obligation to pay a beneficiary and does not constitute an

186 accord and satisfaction with respect to another person obligated to the beneficiary.
187 “(d) Unless the account agreement provides otherwise, the obligation of the bank
188 obligated to pay a beneficiary is immediately due and payable.
189 “(e) The bank may discharge its obligation under this section by:
190 “(1) Crediting another transaction account of the beneficiary; or
191 “(2) Taking other action that:
192 “(i) Is permitted under the account agreement for the bank to obtain a
193 discharge; or

199 “§ 28-2957. Property interest of depositor or beneficiary.
200 “(a) Neither a depositor nor a beneficiary has a property interest in a special deposit.
201 “(b) Any property interest with respect to a special deposit is only in the right to receive
202 payment if the bank is obligated to pay a beneficiary and not in the special deposit itself. Any
203 property interest under this subsection is determined under other law.
204 “§ 28-2958. When creditor process enforceable against bank.

205 “(a) Subject to subsection (b) of this section, creditor process with respect to a special
206 deposit is not enforceable against the bank holding the special deposit.
207 “(b) Creditor process is enforceable against the bank holding a special deposit with
208 respect to an amount the bank is obligated to pay a beneficiary or a depositor if the process:
209 “(1) Is served on the bank;
210 “(2) Provides sufficient information to permit the bank to identify the depositor or
211 the beneficiary from the bank’s books and records; and
212 “(3) Gives the bank a reasonable opportunity to act on the process.
213 “(c) Creditor process served on a bank before it is enforceable against the bank under
214 subsection (b) of this section does not create a right of the creditor against the bank or a duty of
215 the bank to the creditor. Other law determines whether creditor process creates a lien enforceable
216 against the beneficiary on a contingent interest of a beneficiary, including a depositor as a
217 beneficiary, even if not enforceable against the bank.
218 “§ 28-2959. Injunction or similar relief.
219 “A court may enjoin, or grant similar relief that would have the effect of enjoining, a
220 bank from paying a depositor or beneficiary only if payment would constitute a material fraud or
221 facilitate a material fraud with respect to a special deposit.
222 “§ 28-2960. Recoupment or setoff.
223 “(a) Except as provided in subsection (b) or (c) of this section, a bank may not exercise a
11

224 right of recoupment or setoff against a special deposit.
225 “(b) An account agreement may authorize the bank to debit the special deposit:
226 “(1) When the bank becomes obligated to pay a beneficiary, in an amount that
227 does not exceed the amount necessary to discharge the obligation;
228 “(2) For a fee assessed by the bank that relates to an overdraft in the special

230 “(3) For costs incurred by the bank that relate directly to the special deposit; or

234 “(c) The bank holding a special deposit may exercise a right of recoupment or setoff
235 against an obligation to pay a beneficiary, even if the bank funds payment from the special
236 deposit.
237 “§ 28-2961. Duties and liability of bank.
238 “(a) A bank does not have a fiduciary duty to any person with respect to a special deposit.
239 “(b) When the bank holding a special deposit becomes obligated to pay a beneficiary, a
240 debtor-creditor relationship arises between the bank and beneficiary.
241 “(c) The bank holding a special deposit has a duty to a beneficiary to comply with the
242 account agreement and this chapter.

243 “(d) If the bank holding a special deposit does not comply with the account agreement or
244 this chapter, the bank is liable to a depositor or beneficiary only for damages proximately caused
245 by the noncompliance. Except as provided by other law of the District, the bank is not liable for
246 consequential, special, or punitive damages.
247 “(e) The bank holding a special deposit may rely on records presented in compliance with
