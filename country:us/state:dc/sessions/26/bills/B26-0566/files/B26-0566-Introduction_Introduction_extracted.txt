13 To amend, on an emergency basis, the District of Columbia Election Code of 1955 to require
14 candidate nomination petition affidavits to state that the circulator has not altered signer
15 information on a petition after a signer has signed the petition; to prohibit anyone from
16 altering signer information provided by the petition signer, except in cases where the
17 signer requests assistance in signing the petition before signing the petition; to amend the
18 Initiative, Referendum and Recall Procedures Act of 1979 to require initiative,
19 referendum and recall petition affidavits to state that the circulator has not altered signer
20 information on a petition after a signer has signed the petition; and, to prohibit anyone
21 from altering signer information provided by the petition signer, except in cases where
22 the signer requests assistance in signing the petition.
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may be
25 cited as the “Petition Administration Clarification Emergency Amendment Act of 2026”.
26 Sec. 2. The District of Columbia Election Code of 1955, approved August 12, 1955 (69
27 Stat. 701; D.C. Official Code § 1-1001.01 et seq.) is amended as follows:
28 (a) Section 8 (D.C. Official Code 1-1001.08) is amended as follows:
29 (1) Subsection (b)(3) is amended as follows:
30 (A) Subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase “; and” and
31 inserting a semicolon in its place.
32 (B) Subparagraph (C) is amended by striking the period and inserting the
33 phrase “; and” in its place.
1

34 (C) A new subparagraph (D) is added to read as follows:
35 “(D) Not written, altered, corrected, clarified, or obscured information on the
36 petition about the signer of the petition after the signer affixed their signature to the petition.”.
37 (2) A new subsection (p-1) is added to read as follows:
38 “(p-1) No person other than the petition signer may write, alter, correct, or clarify on a
39 nominating petition any information about the petition signer unless the signer requests assistance
40 in writing, altering, correcting, or clarifying on the petition any information about the signer before
41 the signer affixes their signature. The use of correction tape or fluid is prohibited.”.
42 (b) Section 16 (D.C. Official Code § 1-1001.16) is amended as follows:
43 (1) Subsection (h) is amended by adding a new paragraph (3A) is added to read as
44 follows:
45 “(3A) That the circulator of the petition sheet has not written, altered,
46 corrected, clarified, or obscured information on the petition about the signer of the petition after the
47 signer affixed their signature to the petition.”.
48 (2) A new subsection (h-1) is added to read as follows:
49 “(h-1) No person other than the petition signer may write, alter, correct, or clarify on a
50 petition sheet for an initiative or referendum unless the signer requests assistance in writing,
51 altering, correcting, or clarifying on the petition any information about the petition signer before
52 the signer affixes their signature. The use of correction fluid or tape is prohibited.”.
53 (c) Section 17 (D.C. Official Code § 1-1001.17) is amended as follows:
54 (1) Subsection (f) is amended by adding a new paragraph (3A) to read as follows:
55 “(3A) That the circulator of the petition sheet has not written, altered,
2

56 corrected, clarified, or obscured information on the petition about the signer of the petition after the
57 signer affixed their signature to the petition.”.
58 (2) A new subsection (f-1) is added to read as follows:
59 “(f-1) No person other than the petition signer may write, alter, correct, or clarify on a
60 petition sheet for a recall petition unless the signer requests assistance in writing, altering,
61 correcting of clarifying on the petition any information about the petition signer before the signer
62 affixes their signature. The use of correction fluid or tape is prohibited.”.
63 Sec. 4. Effective date.
64 This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the
65 Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), and shall remain in effect no longer than 90-
66 days, as provided for emergency acts of the Council of the District of Columbia in section 412(a)
67 of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 788; D.C.
68 Official Code § § 1-204.12(a)).
