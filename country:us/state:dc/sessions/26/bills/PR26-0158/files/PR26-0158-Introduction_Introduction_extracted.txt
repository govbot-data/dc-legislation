Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is a proposed
resolution titled “Maret School, Inc. Revenue Bonds Project Emergency Approval Resolution of
2025”, and the accompanying emergency declaration resolution.
The resolution authorizes the issuance, sale, and delivery of tax-exempt revenue bonds, notes, or
other obligations in aggregate principal amount not to exceed $37,000,000. The bonds will be
used for the financing and refinancing of the acquisition, improvement, expansion, equipping,
furnishing, and development of Series 1998, 2003, and 2004 bonds and the new facility located
at 5901 Utah Avenue, NW, in Ward 4.
In accordance with section 490 of the Home Rule Act, it has been determined that the bonds,
when, as, and if issued, shall be without recourse to the District. The bonds shall not be general
obligations of the District; shall not be a pledge of or involve the full faith and credit or the
taxing power of the District; shall not constitute a debt of the District; and shall not constitute a
lending of public credit for a private undertaking as prohibited in section 602(a)(2) of the Home
Rule Act. The bonds shall not give rise to any pecuniary liability of the District, and the District
shall have no obligation with respect to the purchase of the bonds.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed measure.
Sincerely,
Muriel E. Bowser

MARET SCHOOL
Revenue Bond Project
FACT SHEET
Maret School, Incorporated has requested that the District issue up to $37 million of tax-exempt
revenue bonds to finance, refund and refinance Series 1998, 2003, and 2004 bonds along with
the cost related to their new facilities located at 5901 Utah Avenue, NW in Ward 4.
The Applicant
Maret School, Incorporated, is a non-profit corporation incorporated in the District of Columbia
in 1937. The school is a nonsectarian, independent, coeducational, college preparatory day
school for children from kindergarten through twelfth grade. Maret School has been educating
families of the District since 1911.
Maret School attracts a committed, highly responsive faculty of lifelong learners. Most Maret
teachers hold master’s degrees, and many have more than one advanced degree. Several of the
teachers at Maret have won national fellowships and awards. All middle and upper-level teachers
are advisors who link home with school and provide enormous personal and academic support
for students. Maret’s teachers volunteer numerous evening and weekend hours to supervise
extracurricular and community service programs.
Proposed Project
The proposed bond proceeds will be used to support the purchase and renovations of a new
building located at 5901 Utah Avenue, NW and the refunding of bond series 1998, 2003, and
2004.
Financing Plan
A summary of the proposed sources and uses of funds is attached (see Table 1).
Feasibility/Structure/Security of the Bonds
PFM, serving as the financial advisor to the Maret School, has determined that this bond
transaction is financially feasible for this organization. Repayment of the bonds will be the total
responsibility of the borrower with no recourse to the District.
.
Public Purpose Benefits
Maret School has a current enrollment of 600 students, 125 employees and boasts approximately
2,312 past students. Approximately 55% of Maret’s students live in the District of Columbia and

Maret School, Inc.
nearly half of its faculty and staff are District residents. Maret grants over $1 million dollars in
financial aid each year, of which approximately 34% is awarded to District residents.
Maret has been actively involved in community service work benefiting organizations such as:
• Tutoring at H.D. Cooke and Oyster Bilingual Elementary Schools
• Volunteering Students at So Others Might Eat (S.O.M.E.), homeless shelters,
• AIDS clinics and Martha’s Table
• Community Service Day
In addition, Maret also donates the use of its facilities to local youth sports and other local
organizations.
Legal and Regulatory Review
The law office of Squire, Patton, Boggs is assigned as bond counsel to the Revenue Bond
Program and has preliminarily determined that the Maret School is a 501(c)(3) organization, and
the project constitutes a permissible undertaking in Section 490(a)(1) of the District of Columbia
Home Rule Act.
Based on the foregoing, we have determined that the proposed project complies with criteria for
approval of proposed financing through the District’s Revenue Bond Program and that the
proposed project will enhance employment opportunities and contribute to community
betterment.
