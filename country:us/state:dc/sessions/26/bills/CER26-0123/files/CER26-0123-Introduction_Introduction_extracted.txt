21 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
22
23 _______
24
25 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
26
27 _______
28
29 To celebrate the ceremony of the District of Columbia’s famous groundhog weatherman and
30 political prognostician, Potomac Phil, as he delivers his highly anticipated annual forecast
31 on February 2, 2026, and to declare that date “D.C. Groundhog Day.”
32
33 WHEREAS, On February 2, 2026, residents and visitors of the District of Columbia will
34 emerge to be educated on the American folklore tradition of Groundhog Day by Dupont Festival;
35 WHEREAS, on that morning, District residents will be reminded that if Potomac Phil
36 emerges from his burrow and sees his shadow due to clear weather, he will retreat to his den and
37 winter will persist for 6 more weeks and if he does not see his shadow because of cloudiness,
38 spring will arrive early;

39 WHEREAS, District residents and visitors will be enlightened with the knowledge of
40 Potomac Phil’s uncanny power of weather and political predictions; and
41 WHEREAS, Potomac Phil will elucidate the seasonal forecast for hundreds of individuals
42 in and around Dupont Circle during the morning of February 2, 2026.
43 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
44 resolution may be cited as the “D.C. Groundhog Day Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
45 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia hereby declares February 2, 2026, as
46 “D.C. Groundhog Day” in the Nation’s Capital and celebrates the District of Columbia’s famous
47 groundhog weatherman and political prognostician, Potomac Phil, as he delivers his
48 highly anticipated annual forecast on February 2, 2026.
49 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately upon the first date of publication in
50 the District of Columbia Register.
