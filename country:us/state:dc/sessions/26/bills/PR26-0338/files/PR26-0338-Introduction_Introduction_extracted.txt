14 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend the Office of District
15 Waterways Management Establishment Act of 2022 to make the process for Mayoral
16 appointment of members of the District Waterways Advisory Commission consistent with
17 that of similar commissions, to add the Department of Parks and Recreation as an ex-officio
18 non-voting member of the District Waterways Advisory Commission, to adjust the District
19 Waterways Advisory Commission meeting schedule, to allow sufficient time for
20 engagement with stakeholders and the District Waterways Advisory Commission in
21 development of the Advisory Plan, and to provide the District Waterways Advisory
22 Commission with authority to determine whether the Advisory Plan needs to be updated
23 and extend the update period to at least every 3 years; and to amend the Confirmation Act
24 of 1978 to make a conforming amendment.
25
26 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
27 resolution may be cited as the “Office of District Waterways Management Establishment
28 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2025”.
29 Sec. 2. (a) Section 2(a) of the Office of District Waterways Management Establishment
30 Act of 2022, effective March 22, 2023 (D.C. Law 24-336; D.C. Official Code § 8-191.01(a))
31 (“Act”), created the Office of District Waterways Management (“ODWM”) with a mandate to
32 plan, promote, advocate for, and facilitate stakeholder cooperation for the diverse uses of and
33 access to the Anacostia and Potomac Rivers, the Washington Channel, and adjacent property. The
34 Act also established a District Waterways Advisory Commission (“Commission”), composed of
35 various stakeholders and subject matter experts, which will advise ODWM and develop and adopt

36 a District Waterways Advisory Plan (“Advisory Plan”) to create a strategic vision for District
37 waterways and adjacent property.
38 (b) ODWM plans to engage a consultant to co-facilitate meetings of the Commission and
39 assist with public outreach efforts and the drafting of the Advisory Plan. To do so, ODWM must
40 issue a request for proposals (“RFP”) that adheres to the Act, but important adjustments to the Act
41 are needed to increase flexibility and streamline implementation.
42 (c) The Act outlines a timeframe of one year for the Commission to adopt an Advisory
43 Plan and requires the plan to be updated at least every 2 years. ODWM’s extensive market research
44 shows that 2 years is a more realistic timeframe for developing the initial Advisory Plan given the
45 broad scope of topics that the Advisory Plan must address and the extensive stakeholder and public
46 outreach and engagement that will be necessary. A 3-year frequency for updating the plan is more
47 reasonable than 2 years so that ODWM can have more time to implement the plan before working
48 with the Commission to update it.
49 (d) The Act dictates that the Commission meet at least once a month. Current and potential
50 ex-officio Commission members have consistently told ODWM that they prefer bi-monthly
51 meetings, especially as these are voluntary roles being taken on by working professionals.
52 Additionally, given the Commission's large size, providing for bi-monthly meetings with more
53 frequent engagement as needed will allow greater flexibility to coordinate schedules and thus
54 ensure more robust engagement with Commission members.
55 (e) The Act further requires specific procedures to be followed for Council approval of
56 mayoral appointees, with a 90-day active approval period. However, the Commission is similar to
57 other bodies whose Council approval procedures involve a 45-day passive approval period. The
2

58 Commission could begin its work more quickly if the mayoral appointees could be approved under
59 the alternative Council approval procedures.
60 (f) Emergency legislation is necessary to permit ODWM staff to issue an RFP with more
61 realistic parameters in a timely fashion and to allow mayoral appointees to join the Commission
62 more expeditiously.
63 Sec. 3. The Council determines that the circumstances enumerated in section 2 constitute
64 emergency circumstances making it necessary that the Office of District Waterways Management
65 Establishment Emergency Amendment Act of 2025 be adopted after a single reading.
66 Sec. 4. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
