28 A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
29
30 _________________________
31
32 IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
33
34 _________________________
35
36
37 To recognize the history and the importance of DC Black Pride, including the 35th annual
38 celebration from May 22-25, 2026, and to celebrate the contributions of the first Black
39 Pride celebration in the District of Columbia to the national and international recognition
40 of the Black LGBTQIA+ community, including the establishment of the Center for Black
41 Equity.
42
1

43 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is an annual event to increase awareness of and showcase
44 pride in the diversity of the LGBTQIA+ populations in the African American community, and to
45 support organizations that focus on health disparities, education, youth, and families;
46
47 WHEREAS, from 1975 until 1990, the Club House was a premiere community gathering
48 space for the African-American lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender community of
49 Washington, DC, and was notable for its annual Memorial Day weekend celebration, called the
50 Children’s Hour;
51
52 WHEREAS, Wilmore Cook, Theodore Kirkland, and Ernest Hopkins envisioned creating
53 an event that would continue the tradition of the Children’s Hour as well as raise much-needed
54 funds for the HIV/AIDS organizations that served the African American community after the
55 Club House closed its doors in 1990;
56
57 WHEREAS, with the Children’s Hour as inspiration, the inaugural DC Black Pride was
58 held on May 25, 1991, in cooperation with the DC Coalition of Black Lesbians and Gay Men
59 and the Inner-City AIDS Network on the grounds of Banneker Field;
60
61 WHEREAS, as the very first Black Pride festival in the world, DC Black Pride fostered
62 the beginning of the Center for Black Equity and the “Black Pride Movement”;
63
64 WHEREAS, as DC Black Pride grew in popularity and in programmatic offerings,
65 organizers created a board of directors, created a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization, and officially
66 established the Black Lesbian & Gay Pride Day, Inc.;
67
68 WHEREAS, during the 1998 festival, Black Lesbian & Gay Pride Day, Inc., and
69 organizers of Black Prides from New York City, Detroit, Atlanta and several other cities formed
70 the International Federation of Black Prides, Inc.;
71
72 WHEREAS, the International Federation of Black Prides later became the Center for
73 Black Equity, and today the organization has more than 50 members and serves as the principal
74 body through which organizers find support, raise funds, and share best practices;
75
76 WHEREAS, since the birth of DC Black Pride, more than 50 other Black Pride
77 celebrations now take place throughout the world and on 4 continents, with many using DC
78 Black Pride as a model;
79
80 WHEREAS, May 22, 2026, through May 25, 2026, marks the 35th annual DC Black
81 Pride celebration;
82
83 WHEREAS, the theme for this year’s celebration is “New Black Renaissance,” which
84 centers Black LGBTQIA+ “artists, leaders, healers, and organizers while confronting what still
85 makes Black Pride necessary, including HIV, inequity, homophobia, and transphobia;”
86
2

87 WHEREAS, for more than 30 years, DC Black Pride has been an opportunity for the
88 Black LGBTQIA+ community to gather and celebrate being both African American and
89 members of LGBTQIA+ communities;
90
91 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is the oldest and one of the largest Black Pride events in the
92 world, drawing thousands of residents and visitors from around the globe; and
93
94 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride brings more than 500,000 people to the nation’s capital
95 from across the United States, Canada, the Caribbean, South Africa, Great Britain, France,
96 Germany, and the Netherlands and will carry that tradition this year and into the future to
97 celebrate their shared community, raise awareness and funding for HIV/AIDS prevention, and
98 stand against homophobia inspired by violence and bigotry.
99
100 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That
101 this resolution be cited as the “DC Black Pride Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
102
103 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and honors the hard work of
104 everyone involved in organizing the 35th annual DC Black Pride and the opportunity to celebrate
105 the Black LGBTQIA+ community.
106
107 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
