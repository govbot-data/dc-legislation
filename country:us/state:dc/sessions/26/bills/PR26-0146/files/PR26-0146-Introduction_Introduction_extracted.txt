Dear Chairman Mendelson:
Enclosed for consideration and adoption by the Council of the District of Columbia is a proposed
resolution titled “Society for Science Public Revenue Bonds Project Emergency Approval
Resolution of 2025”, and the accompanying emergency declaration resolution.
The proposed resolution authorizes the issuance, sale, and delivery of tax-exempt revenue bonds,
notes, or other obligations in aggregate principal amount not to exceed $25,000,000. The bonds
will be used for the financing and refinancing of the acquisition, improvement, expansion,
equipping, furnishing, and development of the real estate located at 1776 Massachusetts Avenue,
NW, in Ward 2.
In accordance with section 490 of the Home Rule Act, it has been determined that the bonds,
when, as, and if issued, shall be without recourse to the District. The bonds shall not be general
obligations of the District; shall not be a pledge of or involve the full faith and credit or the
taxing power of the District; shall not constitute a debt of the District; and shall not constitute a
lending of public credit for a private undertaking as prohibited in section 602(a)(2) of the Home
Rule Act. The bonds shall not give rise to any pecuniary liability of the District, and the District
shall have no obligation with respect to the purchase of the bonds.
I urge the Council to take prompt and favorable action on the enclosed measure.
Sincerely,
Muriel E. Bowser

SOCIETY FOR SCIENCE
Revenue Bond Project
FACT SHEET
The Society for Science (SFS) has requested that the District issue up to $25,000,000 in revenue
bonds to support the acquisition cost and cost of issuance of a new Ward 2 location at 1776
Massachusetts Ave., NW, Washington, D.C. 20036.
The Applicant
Society for Science (SFS) is a nonprofit 501(c)(3) membership organization focused on
promoting the understanding and appreciation of science and the vital role it plays in human
advancement: to inform, educate, and inspire. SFS is a champion for science, dedicated to
expanding scientific literacy, effective STEM education, and scientific research. SFS was
originally founded as Science Service in 1921.
Proposed Project
The proposed bond proceeds will be used to support the purchase and renovations of a new
building located at 1776 Massachusetts Avenue, NW, Washington, D.C. 20036, in Ward 2. All
bond proceeds will be used to cover costs related to the purchase, renovations, equipping, and
furnishings of the building, as well as cost of issuance, reserves, and capitalized interest. SFS plans
to begin work on this location in the summer of 2025 and is expected to be completed by Winter
2026 with the building being operable by January 2027.
Financing Plan
A summary of the proposed sources and uses of funds is attached (see Table 1).
Feasibility/Structure/Security of the Bonds
GME Investments, serving as the financial advisor to SFS, has determined that this bond
transaction is financially feasible for this organization. Repayment of the bonds will be the total
responsibility of the borrower with no recourse to the District.
Public Purpose Benefits
SFS is dedicated to supporting teachers and community-driven nonprofits through their STEM
Research Grant and STEM Action Grant programs. Over four years, the Society distributed
$440,000 in STEM Research Grants to 188 middle and high school teachers, supporting students
in low-income communities and underrepresented demographics in STEM fields.

Society for Science
SFS also publishes Science News, a magazine that provides independent, unbiased, fact-based
coverage of science to over 23 million readers in print and online. The magazine’s sister
publication, Science News Explores, makes science accessible both in a print magazine and online
for people ages 9 and up.
Legal and Regulatory Affairs
The law office of Squire, Patton, Boggs is assigned as bond counsel to the Revenue Bond Program
and has preliminarily determined that the Society for Science is a 501(c)(3) organization, and the
project constitutes a permissible undertaking in Section 490(a)(1) of the District of Columbia
Home Rule Act.
Based on the foregoing, we have determined that the proposed project complies with criteria for
approval of proposed financing through the District’s Revenue Bond Program and that the
proposed project will enhance employment opportunities and contribute to community
betterment.
