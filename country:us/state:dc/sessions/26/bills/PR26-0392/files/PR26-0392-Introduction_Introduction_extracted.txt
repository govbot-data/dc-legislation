Ms. Jalelah Ahmed
14th Street, NW
Washington, DC 20011
(Ward 4)
for appointment as a public member of the Commission on African-American Affairs, filling a vacant
seat formerly held by Sondra Phillips-Gilbert, for a term to end July 8, 2028.
Enclosed, you will find biographical information detailing the experience of the above-mentioned
nominee, together with a proposed resolution to assist the Council during the confirmation process.
I would appreciate the Council’s earliest consideration of this nomination for confirmation. Please
do not hesitate to contact me, or Steven Walker, Director, Mayor’s Office of Talent and
Appointments, should the Council require additional information.
Sincerely,
Muriel Bowser

Jalelah Sofia Ahmed
Ms. Jalelah Sofia Ahmed is an accomplished communications
professional with a robust background in both government and private
sector roles. Currently serving as the Deputy Communications Director
for the Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee,
Jalelah manages high-level press requests, prepares Chairman Peters for
interviews, and coordinates the rollout of press materials.
Previously, Ms. Ahmed was a Biden-Harris political appointee at the
Export-Import Bank of the United States, where she served as Acting
Senior Vice President of Communications and Speechwriter. Her
responsibilities included managing the Bank's communications portfolio,
crafting messaging for high-ranking officials, and coordinating
international visits.
Ms. Ahmed has also served as Communications Director and Digital Director for several members
of the U.S. House of Representatives, including Congresswoman Sheila Jackson Lee,
Congresswoman Lucille Roybal-Allard, and Congressman Donald Payne Jr. In these roles, she
developed strategic communication plans, secured media coverage, and managed crisis
communication strategies.
Her career in government began as a Legislative Aide for Councilman Mohamed E. Seifeldein’s
and includes experience as a Freelance Journalist, Photographer, and Producer for the Center for
Popular Democracy, as well as roles with FOX Television Stations and Al Jazeera America.
A Ward 4 resident, Ms. Ahmed earned dual Bachelor of Arts degrees in Broadcast Journalism and
International Relations and a Master of International Affairs from Pennsylvania State University.
