19 To declare the existence of an emergency with respect to the need to amend, on an emergency
20 basis, the District of Columbia Official Code to allow retail establishments with valid on-
21 premises Class C and Class D retailer license to refuse cash as a form of payment in
22 certain circumstances.
23 RESOLVED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
24 resolution may be cited as the “Entertainment Establishment Employee Safety Extension
25 Emergency Declaration Resolution of 2026.”.
26 Sec. 2. (a) This emergency measure would permit a retail establishment with a valid on-
27 premises Class C or Class D retailer license or a restaurant location that operates 24 hours a day
28 or operates at 3:00 am at least 2 days a week to refuse cash as a form of payment, provided that
29 such establishments display written, conspicuous notice to the public on the door of the
30 establishment or on its menu that cash payments are not accepted.
31 (b) This emergency measure and its accompanying temporary measure were initially
32 introduced and approved in Council Period 25 in recognition of the changes in consumer use of
33 payment forms post-pandemic, and to balance unbanked individuals’ reliance on cash as a form
34 of payment to obtain necessary goods and services with the safety concerns of particularly

35 vulnerable businesses. A permanent version of this measure was introduced on January 17, 2025,
36 and was referred to the Committee on Public Works and Operations. The committee held a
37 hearing on the permanent measure on June 9, 2025. The applicability of the emergency and
38 temporary measures was extended by additional emergency and temporary measures on October
39 7, 2025.
40 These emergency and temporary measures are identical to those approved by the
41 Council on December 17, 2024, and on October 7, 2025. Law 26-77, the Entertainment
42 Establishment Employee Safety Extension Temporary Amendment Act of 2025, will expire on
43 August 13, 2026. It is necessary to extend these measures’ applicability while the permanent bill
44 is pending in committee.
45 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
