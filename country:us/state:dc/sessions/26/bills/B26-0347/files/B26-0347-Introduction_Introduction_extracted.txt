10 To amend, on an emergency basis, due to congressional review, the Residential Tranquility Act
11 of 2010 to prohibit persons targeting a residence for purposes of demonstration from
12 using sound amplifying devices in a residential zone between 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m., to
13 prohibit the use of unattended sound amplifying devices emitting sound while targeting a
14 residence, and to prohibit a person from launching or throwing an object onto the
15 residential property of another with the intent to cause fear to, intimidate, or harass any
16 person living or working at that residence; and to amend the National Capital
17 Revitalization and Self-Government Improvement Act of 1997 to provide that the term
18 expiration date for a Council appointment to the Corrections Information Council shall be
19 December 7th of the year in which the term expires.
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
22 act may be cited as the “Residential Tranquility Second Congressional Review Emergency
23 Amendment Act of 2025”.
24 Sec. 2. The Residential Tranquility Act of 2010, effective May 26, 2011 (D.C. Law 18-
25 374; D.C. Official Code § 22-2751 et seq.), is amended as follows:
26 (a) Section 2 (D.C. Official Code § 22-2751) is amended as follows:
27 (1) Paragraph (1) is amended by striking the phrase “, for the purpose of
28 persuading one or more individuals, or the public, or to protest some action, attitude, or belief.”
29 and inserting a period in its place.
1

30 (2) Paragraph (3) is amended by striking the phrase “human beings” and inserting
31 the word “individuals” in its place.
32 (3) A new paragraph (4) is added to read as follows:
33 “(4) “Sound amplifying device” means any machine, device, or loudspeaker that
34 amplifies sound of the human voice, music, or any other sound. The term “sound amplifying
35 device” shall not include automobile audio systems when used and heard only by the occupants
36 of the vehicle in which the automobile audio system is installed.”.
37 (b) Section 3 (D.C. Official Code § 22-2752) is amended by adding new subsections (a-
38 1), (a-2), (a-3), and (a-4) to read as follows:
39 “(a-1) It is unlawful for a person to target a residence for purposes of a demonstration by
40 using a sound amplifying device between 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m. where the targeted residence
41 is, in fact, located in a Residential Zone, Residential Flat Zone, or Residential Apartment Zone,
42 as those terms and Zones are defined at the time of the of the conduct giving rise to the violation
43 in the District of Columbia Zoning Regulations.
44 “(a-2) It is unlawful to leave an unattended sound amplifying device emitting sound to
45 target a residence for purposes of a demonstration where the targeted residence is, in fact, located
46 in a Residential Zone, Residential Flat Zone, or Residential Apartment Zone, as those terms and
47 Zones are defined at the time of the of the conduct giving rise to the violation in the District of
48 Columbia Zoning Regulations.
49 “(a-3) It is unlawful to continue or resume targeting a residence in violation of subsection
50 (a-1) or (a-2) of this section after being instructed by a law enforcement officer to cease targeting
51 a residence in violation of this law.
52 “(a-4) The Mayor and Metropolitan Police Department may seize and impound any
2

53 unattended sound amplifying devices targeting a residence for purposes of a demonstration in
54 violation of subsection (a-2) of this section.”.
55 (c) A new section 3a is added to read as follows:
56 “Sec. 3a. Throwing objects at residential property.
57 “(a) It is unlawful for any person to knowingly launch or throw an object onto the
58 residential property of another with the intent to cause fear to, intimidate, or harass any person
59 living or working at that residence.
60 “(b) A person who violates this section, upon conviction, shall be fined no more than the
61 amount set forth in section 101 of the Criminal Fine Proportionality Amendment Act of 2012,
62 effective June 11, 2013 (D.C. Law 19-317; D.C. Official Code § 22-3571.01), or incarcerated for
63 no more than 90 days, or both.”.
64 Sec. 3. Section 11201a(b)(2) of the National Capital Revitalization and Self-Government
65 Improvement Act of 1997, effective October 2, 2010 (D.C. Law 18-233; D.C. Official Code §
66 24-101.01(b)(2)), is amended by adding a new subparagraph (B-1) to read as follows:
67 “(B-1) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the term expiration
68 date for a Council appointment to the Corrections Information Council shall be December 7th of
69 the year in which the term expires.”.
70 Sec. 4. The Residential Tranquility Temporary Amendment Act of 2024, effective April
71 18, 2025 (D.C. Law 25-323; 71 DCR 14480), is repealed.
72 Sec. 5. Fiscal impact statement.
73 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
74 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
75 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
