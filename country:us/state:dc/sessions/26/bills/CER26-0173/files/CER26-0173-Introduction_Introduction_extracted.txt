17 To recognize honoree Charity Blackwell during the 2026 celebration of DC Black Pride.
18
19 WHEREAS, Charity Blackwell is an award-winning spoken word artist, creative
20 storyteller, and cultural curator whose work lives at the intersection of sports, music, advocacy,
21 and community;
22
23 WHEREAS, Charity Blackwell currently serves as the Chief of Culture & Arts at DC
24 SCORES and Director of Poetry Events at Busboys and Poets;
25
26 WHEREAS, known for her emotionally charged performances and cinematic storytelling
27 style, Charity Blackwell has collaborated with organizations and platforms connected to the
28 National Women’s Soccer League, National Basketball Association, Major League Baseball,
29 Major League Soccer, TEDx, BBC News, and the Washington Spirit, most recently contributing
30 to a campaign alongside Wale and Magic Johnson celebrating women’s sports;
31
32 WHEREAS, Charity Blackwell’s work has also been featured on many stages and
33 platforms including The Lincoln Theatre, The Kennedy Center, and The Hirshhorn Museum;
34
35 WHEREAS, through poetry, live performance, and cultural programming, Charity
36 Blackwell creates spaces rooted in healing, visibility, authenticity, and human connection;
37
38 WHEREAS, Charity Blackwell’s work is deeply grounded in LGBTQIA+ advocacy and
39 creating environments where Black queer communities feel seen, celebrated, protected, and
40 empowered;
41
42 WHEREAS, whether on stage, in community, or through leadership, Charity Blackwell
43 uses storytelling as a tool to amplify marginalized voices, spark conversation, and build spaces
44 where people can show up fully as themselves;
1

45
46 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is an annual event to increase awareness of and showcase
47 pride in the diversity of the LGBTQIA+ populations in the African American community, and to
48 support organizations that focus on health disparities, education, youth, and families;
49
50 WHEREAS, as the very first Black Pride festival in the world, DC Black Pride fostered
51 the beginning of the Center for Black Equity and the “Black Pride Movement”;
52
53 WHEREAS, since the birth of DC Black Pride, more than 50 other Black Pride
54 celebrations now take place throughout the world and on 4 continents, with many using DC
55 Black Pride as a model;
56
57 WHEREAS, May 22, 2026, through May 25, 2026, marks the 35th annual DC Black
58 Pride celebration;
59
60 WHEREAS, the theme for this year’s celebration is “New Black Renaissance,” which
61 centers Black LGBTQIA+ “artists, leaders, healers, and organizers while confronting what still
62 makes Black Pride necessary, including HIV, inequity, homophobia, and transphobia;”
63
64 WHEREAS, for more than 30 years, DC Black Pride has been an opportunity for the
65 Black LGBTQIA+ community to gather and celebrate being both African American and
66 members of LGBTQIA+ communities;
67
68 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride is the oldest and one of the largest Black Pride events in the
69 world, drawing thousands of residents and visitors from around the globe; and
70
71 WHEREAS, DC Black Pride brings more than 500,000 people to the nation’s capital
72 from across the United States, Canada, the Caribbean, South Africa, Great Britain, France,
73 Germany, and the Netherlands and will carry that tradition this year and into the future to
74 celebrate their shared community, raise awareness and funding for HIV/AIDS prevention, and
75 stand against homophobia inspired by violence and bigotry.
76
77 RESOLVED, BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That
78 this resolution be cited as the “Charity Blackwell Recognition Resolution of 2026”.
79
80 Sec. 2. The Council of the District of Columbia recognizes and celebrates Charity
81 Blackwell during the 2026 celebration of DC Black Pride.
82
83 Sec. 3. This resolution shall take effect immediately.
