ENROLLED ORIGINAL
A CEREMONIAL RESOLUTION
26-156
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
May 5, 2026
To recognize, honor, and express gratitude to Diji Omisore for his years of service to the District
of Columbia on the occasion of his retirement from the Office of the Chief Financial
Officer.
WHEREAS, Diji Omisore is a Certified Public Accountant and a Chartered Global
Management Accountant. Mr. Omisore holds a Bachelor’s Degree in Accounting from Towson
University in Towson, Maryland, from which he graduated magna cum laude;
WHEREAS, Prior to joining the District of Columbia government, Mr. Omisore managed
the tax, accounting, and auditing practice of Linwood Jennings and Associates, PA, a Maryland-
Certified Public Accounting firm specializing in small- and medium-sized businesses;
WHEREAS, Mr. Omisore was first employed by the District of Columbia government as
a Group Accountant in the Office of the Chief Financial Officer’s Office of Financial Operations
and Systems (“OFOS”) in June 2001. In September 2005, he was promoted to the position of
Financial Reporting and Policy Manager. In that position, he oversaw the compilation of sections
of the District’s Annual Comprehensive Financial Report (“ACFR”) related to capital projects
expenditures, construction in progress, and capital assets; developed strategies for implementing
new accounting standards; and provided guidance to agencies on technical accounting and
financial reporting issues;
WHEREAS, From 2008 to 2014, Mr. Omisore served as the Director for Financial
Control and Reporting Division within OFOS. In that capacity, he managed the following
functions: compilation of the ACFR in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting
Principles; implementation of newly-issued accounting and financial reporting standards; and
provision of professional advisory services to District agencies on the proper application of
accounting principles. He also researched and wrote position papers on the proper accounting
treatment of new or unusual business events; oversaw remediation of internal control
deficiencies noted in the ACFR; and provided accounting systems training to District employees;
WHEREAS, Mr. Omisore is the Deputy Controller for the District of Columbia. During
his tenure, he has ensured that the District’s general ledger was properly maintained, the ACFR

District of Columbia government.
