ENGROSSED ORIGINAL
A BILL
26-354
IN THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA
________________
1 To amend, on a temporary basis, the Retired Police Officer Redeployment Amendment Act of
2 1992 to expand the Metropolitan Police Department Senior Officer Program to
3 Metropolitan Police Department Lieutenants, and to amend, on an emergency basis, the
4 Policemen and Firemen's Retirement and Disability Act to remove the Metropolitan
5 Police Department and Fire and Emergency Medical Services mandatory retirement age.
6
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may
8 be cited as the “First Responder Retention Efforts Temporary Amendment Act of 2025”.
9 Sec. 2. The Retired Police Officer Redeployment Amendment Act of 1992, effective
10 September 29, 1992 (D.C. Law 9-163; D.C. Official Code § 5-761(d)), is amended to read as
11 follows:
12 “(d) A retired police officer who is rehired under subsection (a0 of this section as a:
13 “(1) Senior police officer shall be paid a salary of no more than that equal to the
14 salary paid a Class 1, Step 5 Officer and shall not be eligible for longevity pay; and
15 “(2) Senior police lieutenant shall be paid a salary of no more than that equal to
16 the salary paid a Class 5, Step 3 lieutenant and shall not be eligible for longevity pay.”.
17 Sec. 3 Section 12(h)(2) of the Policemen and Firemen's Retirement and Disability Act,
18 approved September 1, 1916 (39 Stat 718; D.C Official Code § 5-712 (b)), is amended to read as
19 follows:

20 “(b) Any member of the United States Secret Service Uniformed Division or of the
21 United States Park Police force or of the United States Secret Service Division, having reached
22 the age of 60 years, to whom this subchapter apply shall, in the discretion of the head of the
23 member’s department, be retired from the service and shall be entitled to receive an annuity as

25 Sec. 4. Fiscal impact statement.
26 The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact
27 statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved
28 October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).
29 Sec. 5. Effective date.
30 (a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by
31 the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto), a 30-day period of congressional review
32 as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December
33 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)).
34 (b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.
