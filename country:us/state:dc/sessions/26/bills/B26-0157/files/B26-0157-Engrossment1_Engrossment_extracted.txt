9 To declare as surplus and approve the disposition of District-owned real property known as the
10 Langston and Slater Schools, located at 33-45 P Street, NW, and known for taxation and
11 assessment purposes as Lot 0827 in Square 0615.
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this
14 act may be cited as the “Langston Slater Surplus Declaration and Disposition Approval Act of
15 2026”.
16 Sec. 2. Definitions.
17 For the purposes of this act, the term:
18 (1) “Act” means An Act Authorizing the sale of certain real estate in the District
19 of Columbia no longer required for public purposes, approved August 5, 1939 (53 Stat. 1211;
20 D.C. Official Code § 10-801 et seq.).
21 (2) “CBE Act” means the Small and Certified Business Enterprise Development
22 and Assistance Act of 2005, effective October 20, 2005 (D.C. Law 16-33; D.C. Official Code §
23 2-218.01 et seq.).
24 (3) “Certified Business Enterprise” means a business enterprise or joint venture
25 certified pursuant to the CBE Act.

26 (4) “Developer” means Lebanon Village at Langston Slater MD LLC, with a
27 business address of 875 N Street, NW, Suite 202, Washington, DC 20001, a District of Columbia
28 joint venture by and among special purpose entities affiliated with CSG Urban Partners, LLC,
29 with a business address of 875 N Street, NW, Suite 202, Washington, DC 20001, and special

36 employment generated as a result of the construction on the Property.
37 (6) “LDDA” means the draft Land Disposition and Development Agreement
38 transmitted by the Mayor to the Council on February 28, 2025, with the introduced version of
39 this act.
40 (7) “Property” means the real property located at the current address of 33-45 P
41 Street, NW, and known for tax and assessment purposes as Lot 0827 in Square 0615, which is
42 improved with 2 former elementary schools—John Fox Slater Elementary School and John
43 Mercer Langston Elementary School.
44 Sec. 3. Findings.
45 (a) The District of Columbia is the owner of the Property.
2

46 (b) The Property consists of approximately 30,000 square feet of land.
47 (c) The Property is no longer required for public purposes.
48 (d) The District government and the public will benefit substantially from the disposition
49 of the Property for private development.
50 (e) The Mayor, through the Office of the Deputy Mayor for Planning and Economic
51 Development (“DMPED”), satisfied the public hearing requirement of section 1(a-1)(4) of the
52 Act (D.C. Official Code § 10-801(a-1)(4)) by holding a virtual public hearing on February 11,
53 2021, to obtain community input on potential public uses of the real property to inform the
54 Mayor’s determination whether the real property is no longer required for public purposes.
55 (f) The Mayor, through DMPED, has selected the Developer to develop the Property.
56 (g) The intended development of the Property is mixed-income, rental, and for sale
57 residential development, as further described in the term sheet (“Project”).
58 (h) The Mayor has proposed that the terms of the disposition of the Property include the
59 following:
60 (1) The Developer shall comply with the requirements of section 1(b-3) of the Act
61 (D.C. Official Code § 10-801(b-3)), by dedicating at least 30% of the residential units in the
62 Project as affordable housing units;
63 (2) The Developer shall enter into an agreement that shall require the Developer
64 to, at a minimum, contract with Certified Business Enterprises for at least 35% of the contract
65 dollar volume of the Project and shall require at least 20% equity and 20% development

66 participation of Certified Business Enterprises in the Project, in accordance with section 2349a of
67 the CBE Act (D.C. Official Code § 2-218.49a) and section 1(b)(6) of the Act (D.C. Official Code
68 § 10-801(b)(6)); and
69 (3) The Developer shall enter into a First Source Agreement.
70 (i) The method of disposition shall be a ground lease of greater than 15 years for that
71 portion of the Property where the Developer intends to develop rental residential units and a fee
72 simple private sale for that portion of the Property where the Developer intends to develop for
73 sale residential units, as further described in the LDDA documents transmitted by the Mayor to
74 the Council on February 28, 2025, with the introduced version of this act and the amended term
75 sheet transmitted on November 21, 2025.
76 (j) The LDDA for the disposition of the Property shall not be inconsistent with the
77 substantive business terms of the transaction transmitted by the Mayor to the Council on
78 February 28, 2025, with the introduced version of this act and the amended term transmitted on
79 November 21, 2025, in accordance with section 1(b-1)(2) of the Act (D.C. Official Code § 10-
80 801(b-1)(2)), unless revisions to those substantive business terms are approved by the Council.
81 Sec. 4. Surplus declaration and disposition approval.
82 (a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the Council determines that the Property
83 is no longer required for public purposes and authorizes the Mayor to dispose of the Property.
84 (b) The authority of the Mayor to dispose of the Property pursuant to this act shall expire
85 3 years after the effective date of this act.
