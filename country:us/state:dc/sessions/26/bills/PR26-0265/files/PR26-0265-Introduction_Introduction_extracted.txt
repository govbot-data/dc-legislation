Alexis Gardner
Ms. Alexis Gardner currently serves as an Assistant Federal Public
Defender for the District of Columbia. After law school she joined the
Bronx Defenders and moved with them to Tulsa, Oklahoma, starting the
first ever public defender’s office dedicated solely to representing
women.
The Bronx Defenders opened the sister firm, Still She Rises, to combat
Oklahoma’s increasing incarceration rate for women, the highest in the
nation. Later, Ms. Gardner launched her own private practice where she
maintained a 100% jury trial success rate while still dedicating substantial
efforts to indigent defense through accepting conflict appointments in
both federal and state court.
Ms. Gardner joined the Federal Public Defender in 2020. When she’s not advocating for the
marginalized or traveling the world, she teaches aspiring lawyers at various universities in addition
to raising her young son. Ms. Gardner also serves District residents as a Court-Appointed Special
Advocate (CASA) for Children of DC as well as a mentor with Spark the Journey.
A Ward 6 resident, Ms. Gardner earned both her Bachelor of Arts in Interpersonal
Communications and her Master of Arts in Rhetoric for Social Change from California State
University, and both her Master of Arts in Afro-American Studies and her Juris Doctor from the
University of California Los Angeles.
